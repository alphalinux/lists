>
> Is working on MILO the best route? Maybe we can convince Compaq to let
> us get SRM going for older machines. Then we could standardize on SRM
> for firmware, and aboot for the boot loader. The bigger problem (IMHO)
> is that the various alpha platforms have such specialized needs. I am
> such a geek that I want to support them all :) but from the perspective
> of a distro builder, supporting SRM/alphabios/milo/aboot/etc/etc are a
> real pain.

There's a couple of issues here....first is SRM availibility. SRM is
available for every Alpha platform out there except the XL series
(XL233,266,300,366) and the UX boards. However I think the bigger issue is
unfortunatly hardware support. Take for instance Adaptecs....only LX,SX
and EV6 machine can boot from adaptecs. Or graphics cards....TNT2 is
useless for the PC164 users...the only real solution would be something
that could replace both SRM and AlphaBIOS+MILO, IMHO that is.
