on Wed, Jan 26, 2000 at 08:58:50PM -0500, Chris Kloiber <ckloiber@rochester.rr.com> wrote:
> You have cleverly avoided telling us what kind of scsi adapter you are
> trying to use. If it is not one of the models that milo can see, but it
> is one the kernel can see, then that is your problem right there. Is
> there a good reason to use such an old kernel on a new install?

Yes that was clever of me. The scsi controller is on the motherboard and
is an NCR53C810. When I first turn the machine one the arc console says
"initalizing NCRC810 ok". At the arc consoles supplementrary menu/
Display hardware configuration the scsi disk is recognized.

I do a show dev at the milo prompt I get:

Device drivers:
    SCSI (NCR (ncr53c8xx), Qlogic ISP, Buslogic, AHA2940UW)
    IDE
    Floppy

and

Please wait while setting up devices..
Major devices are: fd (0002)
Devices:
    fd (0200)

File Systems:
    ext2 iso9660 msdos

The milo is
Linux/Alpha Miniloader (Milo) v2.0.35-c5.4 (Fri Mar 19 20:52:30 GMT
1999)

A pci command a the milo prompt shows the scsi controller as

NCR 53c810 (rev2)
medium devsel. IRQ11. Master capable. Latency=32
IO at 0x8800
Non-prefectable 32 bit memory at 0x4000000.

So all the drivers seem to match.
