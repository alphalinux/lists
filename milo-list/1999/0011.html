I have been maintaining an unofficial MILO branch for some time. I
don't know how appropriate it is to call it "The MILO": here is a brief
summary.

- Based on the latest official sources from gatekeeper.
- Plenty of bug fixes and new features.
- Does not require RH4.2 to build.
- Distributed with a set of kernel patches for the 2.0.35 kernel against
  which MILO is built.
- No official support.
- Used by Debian and Red Hat (don't know about SuSE and others).
- Available at ftp://genie.ucd.ie/pub/alpha/milo

I am still maintaining it, although quite busy these days and can't
guarantee quick patch inclusion.

What other alternatives are there?

The official DEC MILO does not seem to be actively maintained...
When I started my activity, I tried to propagate my patches into the
official source. I had some useful and entertaining correspondence with
Jay Estabrook, but my changes did not make their way onto gatekeeper,
so I just forked my own branch. Jay even sent me a new Digital BIOS
emulation library (binary, of course) that no longer needs RH4.2 to
successfully link with MILO (I hope he doesn't mind my disclosing this
information). Interestingly, Jay did not include this library into his
official MILO distribution, although it works perfectly.

I would be willing to act as a central MILO maintainer if my branch is
considered qualifying to represent the "current" or "latest" MILO and
if developers don't mind using it in their work.

Nikita
