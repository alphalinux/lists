Hi.

I am currently thinking about releasing my MILO tree now rather than later
to get it heavily tested. It should with very little changes be at least
at the same point as milo linked against 2.0 kernels, but of course the
source didn't really get easier and there's no real advantage except you
can use the new drivers and you don't need an old 2.0 source tree on your
hard disk.

Whether I do this depends mainly on your arguements and wishes. The
average user hasn't been compiling his own milo until now and I guess this
won't change in near future.

Should I announce it on the redhat-axp list? (I fear a bit that everyone
expects this to be much better than the old milo, but it's currently not
more than working about as good as the old one)
