Claus Denk wrote:
>
> A very poor result for g77 can be obtained by comparing the original
> Netlib BLAS routines SGEMM and DGEMM (matrix matrix multiplication)
> routines (rates in MFLOPS, mean values over matrix sizes 32x32 up to
> 128x128 in steps of 32):
>
> g77 0.5.21 DU
> SGEMM 27 144
> DGEMM 26 115

Hm, using libraries written specifically for Linux/Alpha I measured g77
(egcs) compiled SGEMM/DGEMM on UX in 700 and over MFLOPS range. No,
this is not a typo. "Raw" stuff from netlib, which is definitely
not written for alpha or other RISC, was also much higher than numbers
qouted above.

If you would rummage a bit in this list archives you would find
out how to get these results as well.
