At the moment it seems, at least to me, particularly difficult to pin down
exactly how good or bad the free compilers (egcs/gcc) are against the DEC
compilers unless one actually has access to the various machines. One can
glean snippets here and there (from Dejanews say) but it isn't always
clear what was done and the comparisons are but for a single program.

What I would like to see established is an ongoing comparison of the
compilers using some agreed 'trivial' or real world benchmarks in C and
Fortran. I'm thinking of something along the lines of running say five
programs in the following combinations on the same architecture;

o Digital Unix & DEC C (Fortran)
o Digital Unix & egcs (g77)
o Linux & egcs (g77) with compiled libraries
o Linux & egcs (g77) with hand-coded libraries

I'd do this myself if I had access to such machines but I don't (at least
not yet) but perhaps the list members could provide suitable programs and
patch the information together ? The only catch is this would have to be
repeated where any component, particularly egcs, is subsequently updated.
The influence of compiler options is also useful information.

I'm sure some people must be already be performing tests of this kind.

John P.
