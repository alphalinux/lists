On Wed, 20 Jan 1999, Dr J Pelan wrote:

> At the moment it seems, at least to me, particularly difficult to pin down
> exactly how good or bad the free compilers (egcs/gcc) are against the DEC

As a novice in the alphalinux world, I must say that it is really
difficult for the begginer to obtain a valuable information, specyfying
the differences between several compilers and their relative power.

We bought at the Observatory our first alpha station (based on the
Microway PC164LX board (21164A/533MHz, 512 MB RAM) planning to use in
under Linux. We hawe a lot of experience with i386 Linux machines so we
hoped it will be easy to switch to alpha. After a month of reading and
experimenting, I still don't know how good is egcs when compiling for
alpha (-m21164a). I read hundrets of pages on alpha architecture, on
optimizing for alpha , on NDP optimizing compilers etc. Finally I came to
the conclusion, that Microway NDP Fortran seems to be the only tool,
dedicated for this processor. They say in Microway, that they do not ofer
a C compiler for alphalinux becouse egcs is preety good but from my
investigations it seems that the best way is to produce .o files with egcs
and then let NDP optimize it for particular architecture.

All above is a theoretical result, I am serching for arguments whether to
buy NDP Fortran compiler from Microway or not. My work will be in C, so I
will not use their product as a whole.
Any comments from experienced users?

> What I would like to see established is an ongoing comparison of the
> compilers using some agreed 'trivial' or real world benchmarks in C and
> Fortran. I'm thinking of something along the lines of running say five
> programs in the following combinations on the same architecture;
>
> o Digital Unix & DEC C (Fortran)
> o Digital Unix & egcs (g77)
> o Linux & egcs (g77) with compiled libraries
> o Linux & egcs (g77) with hand-coded libraries

The above should be extended to NDP compilers!

> I'd do this myself if I had access to such machines but I don't (at least
> not yet) but perhaps the list members could provide suitable programs and
> patch the information together ?

It might be very helpful and informative both for beginers and experienced
users. Please accept me as a volunteer for any tests on my AlphaLinux
machine (21164A/533MHz/512MB RAM/no graphics/3,4 GB SCSI HD).
I have some experience in Fortran (IBM360 etc) but strongly prefer to work
in C.

> I'm sure some people must be already be performing tests of this kind.

I am not ready but I hope to be in a week or two.
