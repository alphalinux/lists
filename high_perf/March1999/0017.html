Yes, you are right, rational is known to be MUCH better, but you need
pretty SLOW div !!

I checked several combinations (with div) considering full range, full
*REL* precision and decided it would very likely be slower than the
Mosberger sqrt.

However, what I suggest for 1e-10 precision (including 1 Newton step)
might probably work (mean, be a bit faster, but compared to libcpml ???)

And yes we probably could replace the rather small table in the
libffm sqrt by another (nonrational) polynomial, but for .25 to 1 we
need at least 8th to better 9th order for 1e-5 (here my software is
pretty dependable) to be on the sure side after 3 Newton steps, and it
will be DEFINITELY slower than the actual version. You see, sqrt is
"§$%&/@ as long as you don't go for rational approx.

YES, in case cache misses occur, this would be different, but I'm still
waiting for the results what the real-world impact on that seems to be.
