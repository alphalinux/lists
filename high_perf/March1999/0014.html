Further addition to my last post:

*Only* for the problem the original poster mentioned, you might consider
using max. *absolute* error here, this would be a very special routine.

However, when fitting the *full* range, the problem is the infinite
derivative at zero, that fools all polynomial approx. schemes. Maybe my
approximation software fails here a bit, but, even at 10 th order, I
can't get better 2e-3 *absolute* precision. sqrt is known to be bad in
function approx. theory. Müller in his recent book cites that you need
at 25th degree polynomials (!!) only to get to 1e-15 absolute error in
0.25..1
