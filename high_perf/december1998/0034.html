I know it's offtopic, sorry,
but I also know that there is a number of people having quite some knowledge
about High Perf. Computing and I guess some of them are physicists like I am.

I happen to have written yet another numerical library in C++ [Vectors,
Matrices, Optimized version of Matrices (Band, Compressed ...), Tensors,
lots of solvers, etc.] within the frame of my diploma work. I am thinking
that it's done quite well, cause it uses such things as parallelization
(pthreads) and the Temporary Base Class Idiom to prevent those nasty
redundant Copy Constructors when giving back the results of Operators which
normally prevent C++ code to perform well.
IMHO, it is worth a publication.

Most probably the code of the library will also be made freely available,
but the Professor has to give his final statement on it.

Of course, there's also an application: We used it to implement our FD
algorithm to solve Maxwell's equations in arbitrary coordinates.

So now my question is: How should this be published? Two articles,
one for computer scientists and one for physicists? Or rather one made of
two parts: Efficient C++ class design for num. apps and applic. of
coordinate trafos to solve FD problems?

The related question is: Where to publish?
I have no great overview of the journals dealing with such kind of problems.
I know that there is the Journ. for Computational Physics. I also found
references to Dr. Dobbs for questions about numerical design. (Is the latter
an online journal, only, BTW?)
I think there are some more journals which might have the audience to read
and profit from such an article. Maybe you can tell me ... ?

Thanks for your help!
