I see all the time these square root routines with big lookup
tables and impressive cycle counts. I cannot help not to wonder
what will be impact of such tables in a "real life". While
benchmarking one is performing square root operations in a tight
loop; everything fits in a cache and a performance is impressive.
But in reality these things are not used that way and it will
be, usually, lots of other code around. If one starts taking
cache, or even worse - page, hits because of lookups then a performance
can worsen considerably.

I still remember a paper by Dave Mosberger-Tang, from a few years
ago, about optimizing on Alpha where he showed that local calculations
may be much prefererable to distant lookups. I do not have measurements
for a different computation strategies of sqrt in real code as opposed
to small loops. But does anybody attempted some investigations of
that kind?
