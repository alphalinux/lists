"Andrei A. Dergatchev" wrote:
> I counted (well, after I've fallen into this myself) a few dozens of
> posts
> on dejanews in comp.os.linux.alpha saying basically the same thing -
> problems booting Mandrake install CD on systems under AlphaBIOS.
> It really seems to me that Stefan van der Eijk is the only person who
> got it running. I'm currently in the situation when everything is
> installed,
> but even after fiddling with MILO's and boot options for a few hours I
> couldn't get /boot/vmlinuz running - I'm always getting:
>
> request_module[block-major-8]: Root fs not mounted
> VFS: Cannot open root device 08:02
> Kernel panic: VFS: Unable to mount root fs on 08:02

Maybe it's not loading initrd image like it should. block-major-8 is
the SCSI disk major, and SCSI modules are typically put into initrd
rather than built into the kernel.

FWIW, Mandrake-Alpha 7.0 works on my UP1000 w/ AlphaBIOS... no boot
loader at all.

AlphaBIOS loads my [custom] kernel from /dev/hda1, which is an MSDOS
partition. Then the kernel boots, and everything is fine...
