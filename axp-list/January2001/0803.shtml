On Wed, Feb 28, 2001 at 09:53:53AM -0500, Mohan wrote:
>
>
> e.g:
> /etc/aboot.conf:
> 0:1/boot/vmlinux-2.2.14-6.0 root=/dev/sda3
>
> AT SRM:
> boot -file "1/boot/vmlinux-2.2.14-6.0" -flag "root=/dev/sda3"
>
> will hang at aboot. And then if I enter 0 at the aboot prompt
> it will continue?????

What about 'boot -fl 0' in that situation? This, obviously, assumes
that your bootdef_dev was set correctly in SRM.

Also for those who are trying to boot 2.4 kernels (NOT 2.2.14-6.0) -
is CONFIG_ALPHA_LEGACY_START_ADDRESS turned in these kernels or not?

BTW - 2.2.14-6.0 from RH7 was replaced by another kernel from updates
quite a while ago.
