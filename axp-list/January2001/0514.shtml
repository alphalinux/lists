On Sun, 4 Feb 2001, Matt Wilson wrote:

> http://boudicca.tux.org/hypermail/linux-kernel/2000week52/0742.html

> Note that despite my public comments about it beign a bad idea to ship
> extremely untested compilers in a major release, I actually think that
> it would be wonderful to have people who are ready to face the
> consequences to try the new 2.96.

I note that he did not retract his opinion of RH shipping it, and I would
question as to whether or not 'face the consequences' should be expected
in a vendor release.

> It's not been all that widely tested, but if you kno a bit about
> what you're doing (or want to learn), gcc-2.96 _does_ potentially
> create better code, and if nobody is willing to test it, any
> potential bugs (be they in the kernel sources and triggered by a
> smarter compiler, or in the compiler itself) won't be found.

Indeed ... I was reading with interest the alpha optimisations.

> Sending a "I tried to compile foo and it didn't go" is better than
> just saying the compiler doesn't work. You have enough time to type
> these exchanges, but not enough time to tell me *which* applications
> you had trouble building?

kernel 2.4.0-test9 to 2.4.0-prerelease
devfsd
modutils
util-linux

Those come immediately to mind.
