On a similar thread, I have seen this strange behaviour
on a AS600 with RH6.2. I give the exact partition and root
device to the -file and -flags, but aboot would hang.
And the only way I can continue to boot, is give "0" or
"1" aboot argument. I know, this is whacky, I put in
*exactly* the same info on the SRM command line, but it
will not boot, hang at aboot prompt.

e.g:
/etc/aboot.conf:
0:1/boot/vmlinux-2.2.14-6.0 root=/dev/sda3

AT SRM:
boot -file "1/boot/vmlinux-2.2.14-6.0" -flag "root=/dev/sda3"

will hang at aboot. And then if I enter 0 at the aboot prompt
it will continue?????

Any ideas? Thanks

Cheers,
Mohan
