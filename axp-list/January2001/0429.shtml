Hi Eugene.
Actually the largest 1" now available are the IBM and Seagate 73GB in 1" form.
2GB is maximum RAM. For over that a board needs dual memory controllers. An
example of this is the DP264 planar board ( same as used in our Hard Data
DP264 and Compaq DS20 machines) but these are far from 1U.
6U is the typical rack form factor of those.
We find no problems on a _properly_ configured Alpha system with 4GB. The
term properly does not mean dead stock Red Hat 6.2 or 7, btw.

Of course you could also buy Compaq ES40 machines and get over 4GB. And for
the cost of those machines you could also buy a Rolls Royce and have the
data driven to Florida..
But they aren't 1U either.
In terms of rackspace versus power the CS-20 is about as dense as one can
buy nowadays,
