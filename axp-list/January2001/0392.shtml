On Fri, Jan 26, 2001 at 05:16:00PM +0000, Nelson Brito wrote:
>
> > On Fri, Jan 26, 2001 at 10:27:35AM +0000, Nelson Brito wrote:
> > > > make zImage (or make bzImage)

> > > make vmlinux
> > > it will work :-)
> >
> > Indeed, but it will not do really what you want. 'make boot' does a few
> > important things more.
>
> can you give more details about it?
> i compiled the kernel a few days ago for a machine, and i did it with
> make vmlinux!

Oh, sure. But you really want arch/alpha/boot/vmlinux.gz and its
System.map as well. The later is not essential until you will get
an oops (se a file with a somewhat telling name REPORTING-BUGS in
a top directory of your kernel sources) but 'depmod -a' may want
to consult it.

> does make boot do some special
> optimization for the architecture??

Compressed kernels do load a bit faster then uncompressed ones
and take less space on your hard drive - if you can call that an
optimization. But the second point can be vital if you would like
to use that kernel on a floppy. :-)

Also you do not really know if in some future Makefile 'make boot'
will not do few other, possibly important, actions.

('make vmlinux' and simple 'make' do not differ that much with this
exception that the _later_ will run for you 'make dep' automatically
if you forgot that step, or even 'make config', and the former will
just fail if prerequisites are missing; look into the top Makefile).
