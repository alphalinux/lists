On Sun, 21 Jan 2001, Andrew A. Gill wrote:

As i understand it:

> I get the following output:
>
> --- This has been edited, but it looks like the info is correct. ---
> (null)

you ran it with no arguments, right? that's the terminator for
*argv[].

then you don't check that i<argc and you continue on and start
accessing memory beyond *argv[]:

> PWD=
> HOSTNAME=
> QTDIR=
> LESSOPEN=
> KDEDIR=
> USER=
> LS_COLORS=
> MACHTYPE=
> MAIL=
> INPUTRC=
> BASH_ENV=
> LOGNAME=
> SHLVL=
> SHELL=
> USERNAME=
> HOSTTYPE=
> OSTYPE=
> HISTSIZE=

this means your environment variables are stored in the memory
directly after *argv[].

Eventually *argv[i] ends up referencing memory that has not been
allocated to you, and so kernel issues your programme a SIGSEGV when
your printf() tries to deference it.

> It seems like this might provide too much information to a possibly
> malicious user.

your test programme is setuid? :)
