        Ross A Knepper wrote:
> personally, i think that exporting a hwrpb_struct to userland is both ugly
> and unnecessary. the rpb is intended to be hidden to upper levels and
> certainly to userland.

I fully agree with you.

> whereas finding the MHz of the cpu is conceptually
> the same from the user's point of view on any architecture,
> parsing a hwrpb_struct is clearly alpha-specific.

AFAIK, this is the only place that the processor frequency is
recorded in the kernel. Not knowing how else to get the value
(other than parsing /proc/cpuinfo and hence relying on the string
"cycle frequency [Hz]" not changing in the sprintf() in
get_cpuinfo()), I enquired.

As you say, since processor frequency is an architecture
independent characteristic, may be it should be part of
information returned in struct sysinfo structure by or sysconf().
getsysinfo() on Tru64 lets you pick that information with
GSI_CPU_INFO operation.
