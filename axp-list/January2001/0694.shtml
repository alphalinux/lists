I have an AlphaPC 164 (Ruffian) and I have the desire to drop a sound
card into it. My problem is that the Hardware Compatibility Lists
are not so helpful for sound cards because the model names change so
often. So far I have tried a newer "Creative Ensoniq AudioPCI" which
has a CT5880 chip on it and an "Asound Express 32-bit PnP" with an
Avance ALS4000 chip on it. I have tried the default RedHat modules
and I have tried the OSS demo modules with no luck. The CT5880 simply
does not work (/dev/dsp: Device not configured") and the ALS4000
spits back errors from any application that tries to use it.

For example, mpg123(1) says the following when trying an MP3:
> Playing MPEG stream from 03-What_Else_Is_New.mp3 ...
> MPEG 1.0 layer III, 192 kbit/s, 44100 Hz stereo
> mpg123: Can't rewind stream by 23 bits!
> mpg123: Can't rewind stream by 13 bits!
> mpg123: Can't rewind stream by 10 bits!
> mpg123: Can't rewind stream by 21 bits!
> mpg123: Can't rewind stream by 36 bits!
> [until termination]

and kmedia(1) spews the following on a WAV file:
> maudio OSS Error: 5
> maudio: warning (please ignore)
> maudio OSS Error: 5
> maudio: warning (please ignore)
> maudio OSS Error: 5
> maudio: warning (please ignore)
> [until termination]

Any suggestions would be seriously apprechiated! If you can recommend
a card that I have a hope in finding, please let me know. I saw a few
folks recommend an ISA SoundBlaster in the archieve but my 1 ISA slot
is in use.
