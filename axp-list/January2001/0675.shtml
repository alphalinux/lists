Carl,

>OKay, so after much studying of SCSI arch. I came to the conclusion that
>the problem was my SCSI controller which has two physical busses (68pin
>and 50pin) shared on the same SCSI bus.

Sounds like either the IQ 10 PCI (1020 or 1020A) or the QLA 1040B. These
have internal 68 and 50 pin connectors as well as a standard 68 pin external
connector. The QLA1040B is a Fast-20 (Ultra) controller while the IQ 1020
is a Fast-10 (fast wide) controller.

>So, I went down the street and got a dual channel QLogic ISP board.

I assume this is a QLogic QLA 1240 (Fast-20 Ultra) or a QLA 1280 (Fast-40
Ultra 2)?

MILO supported it so I figured I was cool. Come to find out - AlphaBIOS
doesn't like it and I can't get SRM on
my box for the life of me.

AlphaBIOS "doesn't like it" in what way? Does it report the controller in
the device list and can you see the devices attached to it?

>I guess SRM doesn't like any of the video cards I have lying around. Tried
an ATI Rage IIc, and a Matrox Mystyque -
>all I get is a blue screen with a flashing cursor in the upper left corner.

The Matrox Mystique should work fine on SRM 5.x and up. The SRM 5.3 list is
a bit shorter for bootable video controllers (I think it is a limitation of
the x86 VGA BIOS emulation that was later improved).

You may kill two birds with one stone (if video perf is not too important)
with an Intranserver combined video and single or dual Fast Ethernet
controller. These are Tru64 and OpenVMS compatible. Remember to hit return
a few times on the keyboard to get the >>> prompt. If you don't, you may be
in serial console mode. There are some good references to backing out to
graphical console on the AlphaLinux.org site. Go to the search engine on
the top right hand corner and type in "console mode" you should get a hit.

Good luck,
