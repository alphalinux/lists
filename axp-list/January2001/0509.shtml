> > As horrifically broken as gcc-2.96 is, it and above do have better

> Pardon? We put a lot of work into the GCC shipped in Red Hat Linux 7.
> If you've found some way that makes it "horrifically broken", I've yet

I am not going to get into a fight with you about this.

gcc-2.96 is not suited for kernel, nor development work. The C++ APIs will
not be forward or backward compatible.

It's a development snapshot.

The GCC people and the linux kernel development people have both come out
strongly against RH shipping 7.0 with gcc-2.96 ...

I've had about 1 in 4 luck compiling things with it, and out of that, a 1
in 4 of them running.

The good news is that gcc-3.0 has entered a feature freeze, where they're
just concentrating on bug fixes for it.
