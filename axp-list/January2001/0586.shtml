> > The QL ISP1020 is an Ultra Wide SCSI adapter, which means you can get a
>> maximum transfer rate of 40 MB/s. The disks Compaq shipped with my
XP-1000
>> are all Ultra3 SCSI, which have a max. rate of 160 MB/s. This fabulous

Its actually a QLA 1040B, not the IQ 1020/1020A. The 1020/1020A are
fast/wide SCSI 2 (Fast-10). 8 bit transfers on the 1020x are 10MBps in
synchronous mode while 16bit transfers are 20MBps in synchronous mode. The
QLA 1040B is a SCSI 3 controller (Fast-20), sometimes called "Ultra" SCSI
with 40MBps 16bit synchronous transfers and 20MBps 8bit synchronous
transfers.

>The max I've seen out of disks is about 33 MB/s.

33MBps is VERY good, but probably only reflects buffered sequential reads
with the transfer blocks fitting neatly into the disk drive's buffer cache
(2MB out to 16MB in some cases). The random, uncached read of 15K 36GB
drives is about 10MBps, or about 4 times faster than most 18.2 7200 RPM
drives.
