On Wed, Jan 17, 2001 at 01:30:38PM +0300, Ivan Kokshaysky wrote:
> On Tue, Jan 16, 2001 at 10:20:52AM -0800, Ron Farrer wrote:
> > Anyone know why a PCI sound card would get assigned an IRQ of 255? All
> > I can think of is that the kernel was unable to assign it properly...
>
> Due to the lack of tech docs we do not assign IRQs on ruffian
> and rely on the firmware doing that properly.

It appears to always have been so; I just looked at old (2.0.35) sources
for both kernel and MILO, and that's the way it's always been done.

> If someone has knowledge of ruffian's PCI IRQ routing, please share it.

A possible way to figure it out would be to put a different card in
the same slot, and see what IRQ is assigned to *it*. That would most
likely be the same that the PCI sound card *should* have been given by
the firmware. Then you have to find a way to force that card to be
given the desired IRQ - that is tougher...

It'd be interesting to know why the firmware has problems with that
card...
