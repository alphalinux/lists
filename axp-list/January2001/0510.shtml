On Sun, Feb 04, 2001 at 09:00:56PM -0500, John Jasen wrote:
>
> I am not going to get into a fight with you about this.

It is worth discussing so that people know all the issues involved,
not just the sensationalist hipe posted on slashdot.

> gcc-2.96 is not suited for kernel, nor development work. The C++ APIs will
> not be forward or backward compatible.

And C++ ABIs (not APIs) have *nothing* to do with kernel development.
Further, all Linux development recommendations require that C++
applications link all C++ symbols statically (in both the LDPS (Linux
Development Platform Specification) and soon to be in the LSB (Linux
Standard Base) drafts by consensus of the LSB meeting in NYC on
Tuesday). C++ won't be covered until later.

> It's a development snapshot.

That has had considerable work done on it to make it stable. In fact,
GCC 3.0 will be in much better shape sooner because of the development
during Red Hat Linux 7.

> The GCC people and the linux kernel development people have both come out
> strongly against RH shipping 7.0 with gcc-2.96 ...

The GCC people were upset about the naming. Linus followed up saying
that while he wasn't happy about it appearing in a major distribution,
the GCC snapshot may well produce the best code of any other released
GCC. In fact, he encouraged people to give it a try, but to qualify
bug reports with gcc version (which is good practice anyway.) I can
dig up his post if you would like to see it.

> I've had about 1 in 4 luck compiling things with it, and out of that, a 1
> in 4 of them running.

I've put your email address in the "Reporter" field of the bugzilla
query page and got zero (0) matches.

> The good news is that gcc-3.0 has entered a feature freeze, where they're
> just concentrating on bug fixes for it.

Which is what we've been doing for our compiler snapshot for months.
In fact, almost every bug we've fixed needed to be applied to CVS HEAD
GCC (which we do, along with the appropriate regression test cases for
the test suite)

In short, we are dedicated to providing support for this compiler. It
bootstraps the entire distribution (except the kernel, for the kernel
makefile and inline assumptions in 2.2.x, and qt 1.2 apps as we wanted
to keep the ancient egcs 1.1.2 ABI for them). This is no small feat.

I won't promise that there are no bugs, but people who say that it is
broken without reporting any do no good for either 2.96 or 3.0
development.

Reporting a bug against the 2.96 snapshot in Red Hat Linux 7 will be a
sure-fire way to make sure that it is fixed in 3.0 as well.

Cheers,

Matt
