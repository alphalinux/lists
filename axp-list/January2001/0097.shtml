Alan Young wrote:
>
> Hi all,
>
> Attached is a patch to aboot 0.7a that will give it the capability
> to recognize a DOS style primary (not extended) partition table
> on a hard disk.
>
> After applying the patch and recompiling aboot, you can then boot
> linux from SRM like so:
>
> >>>boot dva0,dkb100 -fi vmlinux.gz -fl "root=/dev/sda1"
>

Just a thought on your syntax. Maybe something like:

boot dva0 -fi dkb100:vmlinux.gz -fl "root=/dev/sda1"

might be a little more indicative of what you're doing.

You only have one boot device, it's just the partition/file you
want to load the kernel from isn't the same disk. You could
allow the same syntax for BSD labeled disks and have only
one disk with aboot access whatever other disks/partitions
you like without having to put aboot on all of the disks.
