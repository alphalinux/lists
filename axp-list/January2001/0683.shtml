Don't forget Apple and the PowerPC vs 68K.

Then there was some outfit in Blue that was emulating the living daylights
out of things on something called an SP2.

I've been pushing the prediction that by about 2003-2005, we are going to
see the "end" of the "midrange" computer... boxes that TODAY cost around
$100 or $200K and look like EV7 based machines. Those EV7 class machines
will be "shrunk" to under $10K and have EV9 performace... and we're going
to call them "appliances." Supporting those boxes (ie as aplication
servers, etc) will be Wildefire class machines... priced down to the $250K
level.

Once you get "real" performance down to the silicon level, "emulation"
becomes a much more intellegent solution.

Then there is the thing called Transmeta.... it emulates everything and
supposedly everything.

The technologies and capabilities of emulation have changed pretty
radically in recent years and I would expect them to continue changing at
much the same pace.

A few years back Soft-PC ran on DEC. But Soft-PC only ever ran a pretended
version of Windows... Unlike Connectix Virtual PC which emulates the X86
and can run Windows/NT and Linux on top of the Mac OS. (And remember,
Connectix also has the Virtual Game Station.)

Now if the Mac OS ran on Alpha.... but then something called OSX (aka
Darwin) DOES... and, I've been told by reasonably reliable rumor mongers,
that OSX AND Mac OS 9.1 are up and running on ALPHAs in Cupertino. Hmmm...

So maybe we should see about geting Connectix to port to Alpha. (If they
haven't already done so.)

We're wating to see how Jobs deals with the 1ghz barrier that Motorola
has slammed into... maybe at the Developer Conference coming up.
