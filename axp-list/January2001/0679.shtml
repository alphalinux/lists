On Wed, 14 Feb 2001, Carl Perry wrote:

> OKay, so after much studying of SCSI arch. I came to the conclusion that
> the problem was my SCSI controller which has two physical busses (68pin
> and 50pin) shared on the same SCSI bus. So, I went down the street and
> got a dual channel QLogic ISP board. MILO supported it so I figured I was
> cool. Come to find out - AlphaBIOS doesn't like it and I can't get SRM on
> my box for the life of me. I guess SRM doesn't like any of the video
> cards I have lying around. Tried an ATI Rage IIc, and a Matrox Mystyque -
> all I get is a blue screen with a flashing cursor in the upper left
> corner.

If you see the blue screen and the flashing cursor, that's good. That
(usually) means that card made it through the x86 emulator. Chances are it's just
set to serial console. Wait a minute or so and press enter several times,
then do a:
set console graphics

you should be in good shape after that.

>I have a FireGL 1000 at home which I will try tomorrow. Anyone
> have any idea how I can make this configuration work?

That card should work as well.
