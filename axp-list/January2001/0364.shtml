On Wed, Jan 24, 2001 at 01:56:29PM -0700, James Fowler wrote:
> I just booted the new kernel. An 'lsmod' originally showed no modules
> loaded. I did an 'insmod' on the binfmt_foo modules and now they work.
> [santiago@george /root]$ lsmod
> Module Size Used by
> binfmt_em86 1336 0
> binfmt_aout 5088 1
> binfmt_misc 4992 0

These modules will be loaded automatically "on demand" if you put
in your /etc/modules.conf lines like that:

alias binfmt-17791 binfmt_em86
alias binfmt-464c binfmt_em86
alias binfmt-0008 binfmt_aout

"Magic signatures" needed here (they may be different depending on
versions) can be found in your /var/log/messages after a failed attempt
to use a facility of one of these modules.

If you are not going to load them "on demand" ('insmod' without any
flags has this effect as modules will be not unloaded when not used)
then it is better to configure and compile then as an integral part of a
kernel and not as modules.
