With regards to your message at 09:45 AM 2/19/01, Jeff Sturm. Where you stated:
>Maurice Hilarius wrote:
> > If I look at the various documents (mainly Powerpoint slides) of DEC,
> > Compaq, and Samsung/API conferences and releases of 4 years ago until last
> > year, I see a common thread in that we should already be running EV7, newer
> > Alpha chipsets, and well over 1GHz.
>
>Probably. But what's the hangup on clock speed? If Intel/AMD have everyone
>thinking their slower Alpha/PPC/SPARC/whatever is inferior, they've won a
>major
>marketing battle.
>
>1GHz isn't a real "barrier" in any engineering sense. The memory wall is very
>real in comparison, and has a far more profound impact on system performance
>nowadays than clock speed. But nobody is interested in that :(
It is a marketing barrier and a perception barrier though..
Intel HAD to have a faster clockrate chip than the Athlon, and as they
couldn't push the P3 beyond 1GHz they released the P4 early with piddly 8K
L1 cache, a crippled floating point engine, and a pre-release scheduler.
So, in most software the P3 at 1GHz kicks the P4 - 1.5GHz in the butt.

However, Alpha has "stalled at the jump" for 667 to 833 taking 2 years, and
the jump to 1GHz not yet even at a real ship date yet. Even the 833 are
still mainly vapourware. We were promised CPUs in December, then January,
then February.. You guessed it.
 From what I can tell so far Samsung has shipped a couple of hundred, API
none, and Compaq perhaps some in ES40's although I get conflicting reports
on that, whether they are actually a "production" product, or a
pre-production sampling for PR purposes..
Anyone I know who tried to order them has been told "April" unless they
happened to be a high enough PR profile for Compaq to get them some
"special" product.
Apparently from what I hear the problem is not so much in the CPU but in
some components that go with it on the processor daughtercard modules, such
as the DDR cache RAM chips..

>Gimmee faster cache, and lots of it...
Exactly, and there is the problem.. <grin>
