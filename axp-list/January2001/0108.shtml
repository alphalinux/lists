On Mon, Jan 08, 2001 at 06:29:59PM -0500, Matt Wilson wrote:
> On Mon, Jan 08, 2001 at 04:10:25PM -0700, Michal Jaegermann wrote:
> >
> > If you have a working milo on your machine, from the current, old, or
> > no distribution at all, then you may not want to replace it with another
> > one in any case. Usually there is no good reason for that. These
> > things have _nothing_ to do with libraries in the current distro.
> > They will not even see those.
>
> A good reason to upgrade MILO would include support for ext2
> filesystems with features that older MILO didn't support, such as
> sparse-superblocks or filetype-in-dirent.

Well, yes, maybe; but this is not critical. Only a partition on which
your boot images reside has to be formatted without these features and
all other can be done in any way you want. With a sane installation
where a root partition is quite small this comes naturally and at least
older version of an installer were aware about that requirement. Also a
file system block size has to be 1k. I have to admit that I do not know
if Anaconda from RH 7 still keeps that in mind. OTOH if you would like
to boot with root on a RAID device, which sounds to me like a doubtful
proposition but some do it, then I do not know if any of available milos
would support that. You may need a custom one.
