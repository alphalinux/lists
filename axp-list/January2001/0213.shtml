> The kernel is taking up nearly 50% of the total cycles!!

Numbers you show are pretty interesting but if you omit an ouput from
your 'uname -a' for the kernel on which they were obtained their value
is greatly diminished.

> (Note that these timings basically the same between the 2.2.17
> and 2.4.0 kernels.)

There is a number of virtual memory fixes for 2.2.17 and 2.2.18,
plus assorted Alpha specific patches. You can find all this material
in ftp://ftp.us.kernel.org/pub/linux/kernel/people/andrea/
(for those not in USofA substitute "us" above with your country code).
Virtual memory handling changes are incorporated now in 2.2.19pre
kernels and they will show up eventually in 2.2.19 when it will be
released. It would be quite important if you could re-run your
timings but using the latests 2.2.19pre and compare results.
I think that Andrea is watching this list but just in case send what
you will get also to andrea@suse.de. He may have some insightful
comments and advice how to tune your machine via /proc interface.
