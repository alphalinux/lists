On Sat, Feb 10, 2001 at 06:07:07PM -0600, Carl Perry wrote:

> and I have recently added a 18g
> WD Enterprise for more space and an OS upgrade. However, the WD drive
> does not like my alpha.

Maybe this is rather termination and/or cables?

> There is an active
> terminator on the bus.

OK.

> After MILO boots Linux, the kernel just keeps
> resetting the SCSI controller until the end of time. Never getting past
> READ CAPACITY.

What about versions of your kernel, a driver, which driver that is and
small details like that? In any case in drivers/scsi/README.ncr53c8xx
there is a description of various parameters which may affect this
driver behaviour. Assuming that you are using ncr53c8xx driver you may
try to boot with a kernel parameter like 'ncr53c8xx=safe:y'. If this
boots then you may work down from here to find more optimal settings.
BTW - I would not turn off in BIOS a tagged queue on a drive.
