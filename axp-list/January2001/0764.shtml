"S.D. Brown" wrote:

> On Wed, Sep 27, 2000 at 07:34:49PM -0400, S.D. Brown came forth with:
> > I have an AlphaPC 164 (Ruffian) and I have the desire to drop a sound
> > card into it. My problem is that the Hardware Compatibility Lists
> > are not so helpful for sound cards because the model names change so
> > often. So far I have tried a newer "Creative Ensoniq AudioPCI" which
> > has a CT5880 chip on it and an "Asound Express 32-bit PnP" with an
> > Avance ALS4000 chip on it. I have tried the default RedHat modules
> > and I have tried the OSS demo modules with no luck. The CT5880 simply
> > does not work (/dev/dsp: Device not configured") and the ALS4000
> > spits back errors from any application that tries to use it.
> >
> I submitted this message back in September and it just showed up on the
> list this week. In the mean time, let me fill you all in.
>
> The short summary of my solution was this: fall back to a good old
> ISA SoundBlaster 16 card that I picked up from a used computer store
> for $10. Kudzu found it on boot and grabbed the right drivers, etc.
> All my apps (including mpg123) worked just fine for all the sources
> I had (MP3, wav, etc.).
>
> I had been exchanging mail with the folks at OSS about the CT5880 and
> ALS4000 based boards on Alpha and about a week after I was cruising
> along with my ISA card they sent me some new modules to try. Unfortunatly,
> I had returned the PCI cards (minus the 10% restocking fee) so I didn't
> get a chance to try them. I have heard from various folks that the ALS4000
> does indeed work these days.
>
