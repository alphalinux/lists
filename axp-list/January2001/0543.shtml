Matt Wilson wrote:

> There are many cases where the new compiler is a bit more strict
> and/or pedantic. Why are you including varargs.h?

Well, I didn't write that program myself; I downloaded it from some
FTP server. The #include statements are in some complicated *.h files
that are partially configured by the GNU configure script and
partially hand crafted by the author of the program. Include level 3
or 4 will include stdarg.h and later on varargs.h. I tried to comment
out the "#include varargs.h" line, but then gcc will complain heavily
at another point. Alas, it's not trivial to change that program
without deeper knowledge of it's internals.
