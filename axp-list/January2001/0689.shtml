Hmmm... had to go dig out some files.

Compaq is now (as of 14 Feb) shipping the EV68 - 883mhz, and the 1.1 Ghz
EV7 is expected to ship in May (which May depends upon the meaning of the
word "next") [Hey, if you can't believe the Register, who can you
believe?] and Apple's ADC is in May...

So depending upon the way I read the graphs, 1.1 ghz EV7 was due on the
last day of 2000... but then those graphs I have all refer to first
tape-out, not shipping-to-end-customer production.

Also, when mine were done, IBM wasn't in the picture, they were strictly
the initial Samsung projections. And according to Shannon (SKC), IBM is now
clocking the EV68 at 1.1 GHZ - faster than Samsung's EV7!

My guess is that the pressure on EV7 is from Tandem, not High-Performance
Computing. And that IS an interesting implication.

Call it wishful thinking, but Apple sized production volumes would do
wonders for the cost of the Alpha Chip! It would be kind of ironic if the
first EV7 chips were to be available in your Titanium.
