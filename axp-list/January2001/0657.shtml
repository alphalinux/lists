>Oh, yeah, also I find that when you first enter fdisk on alpha, change
'units'
>to sectors- then you shift to a BSD style label ('b')- things seem to work
>easier then as well.

I wish I knew this earlier, the Disk Druid bundled with Red Hat 7.0 Alpha
edition was pretty useless, I finally had to go into FDISK and toggle BSD
disk label options, changed cylinders/blocks to sectors and partition IDs.
What a mess for a first time install. Thankfully FDISK is very powerful
once you get over the hurdle of understanding how it works and learning to
calculate partition size in sectors/blocks and cylinders.
