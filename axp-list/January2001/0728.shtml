James,

Do you still have a useable kernel somewhere? You can change your boot time
parameters for SRM. Do a shot boot* to show what all of the pathes devices
files and flags are listed as. If you have a good kernel, this shouldn't be
too rough since you can direct the SRM to use whatever kernel you want, so
long as you didn't overwrite the one and only kernel.

You may also be able to boot from floppy (there is a good bit of info on
building a bootable floppy on ALO, FYI) to get back into your system and
either complete your noodling with the kernel resident on disk or nuke it
and replace with the working prebuilt kernel from CD or archive.

Good luck,

--KE
