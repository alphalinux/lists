> Samsung fabs Alpha's in Korea and has for a number
> of years.
>
> You'd have to ask Intel if they are still fabbing Alpha's in the
> old DEC plant in Hudson, but I don't think they are. Primarily
> StrongARM and PCI chips I think. (Just a guess)
>
It was pretty well known that the PCI chips were what kept the Hudson fab
from loosing its shirt, the Alpha chips were "lost leaders." And I don't
believe that StrongARM ever achieved money-making quanities like the PCI
chips did either. I was once told that the Hudson fab provided something
like 60% of the PCI chips used in the industry! (No idea how true that
statement might have been.)

Intel actually got a turkey when they bought Hudson. About the only thing
they got was the site. Which for fabs is important. So assuming that Hudson
is "rock solid," the contents of the plant might not have been important.
Especially with the other assorted "technology licensing" stuff that went
along with the settlement. Also, buying the fab was probably a cheap "out
of court settlement" for Intel.

Dec's switch to Samsung resulted in a 20% performance jump simply by
switching fab technologies. Some have said a 10-15 year difference in fab
technology. DS20Es were DRAMATICALLY faster than everyone (in the field at
least) expected compared to the DS20s -- and at the same clock rates.

The IBM copper fab jump is rumored to produce a similar jump in
performance. I guess we'll see at Wednesday's announcements. (I assume they
are still on for the 24th.)
