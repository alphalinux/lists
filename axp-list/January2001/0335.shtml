On Wed, Jan 24, 2001 at 12:26:50AM +0300, Ivan Kokshaysky wrote:
>
> Secondary bus
> IdSel INTA INTB INTC INTD
> Slot 0 0x08 ? ? ? ?
> Slot 1 0x09 ? ? ? ?
> Slot 2 0x0a 27 26 ? ?
> Slot 3 0x0b ? ? ? ?
> Slot 4 0x0c 35 ? ? ?
> 53c875 0x0d 20 - - -
>
> These IRQ values were found in the logs and lspci outputs that I got
> from Michal 2 months ago. The interesting one is INTB for slot 2 -
> it was IRQ assigned to ethernet on a scsi+ethernet combo card. It
> allows me to guess that IRQs for slot 2 are 27, 26, 25, 24, for
> slot 4 - 35, 34, 33, 32 etc.

Well, that was the reason I expected to find 31 on INTA of slot 3. :-)
OTOH this may be not the case as IRQ 20 is already taken and not on
INTD of slot 1. Still can be shared.

To get values for all interrupt pins one would have to use something
like a dual SCSI controller with two ethernet interfaces on the board.
Yes, such cards do exist. You do not even have to run it. Just put
in a slot, boot, and see what lspci has to say. If we will have to
resign ourself to only INTA values for all slots so be it for a time
beeing.
