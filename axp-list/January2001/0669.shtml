>
> I used SWRITEBOOT repeatedly, but apparently the partition was hosed and it
> refused to write anything.

'swriteboot' does not care about partitions at all. It writes on a raw
disk and that is why it can be dangerous. Something does not compute
here.

> Did you file a detailed report on http://bugzilla.redhat.com/bugzilla?
>
> At the time, I didn't know if that would do any good.

Filing something on bugzilla does not necessary will bring you an
immediate solution, although it might as a side-effect, but bugs not
reported are bugs likely not fixed in future releases.

>
> FDISK -L did what again?

'man fdisk' is not a bad idea. Anyway, this lists your partition
table on stdout.

> What is the best method for setting the mount point from FDISK?

'fdisk' has nothing do do with mount points. It just creates a
partition table for your other software to use. Mount points
are simply directories and any one will do.
