On Wed, Jan 17, 2001 at 01:06:45PM -0500, Jay Estabrook wrote:
> A possible way to figure it out would be to put a different card in
> the same slot, and see what IRQ is assigned to *it*.

Yes. Actually it is possible to find out the entire IRQ routing
table using some PCI card which has all 4 interrupt pins connected
(4-port ethernet card, for example). Just to write down IRQs
assigned to that card for every PCI slot.
I have such card, but don't have a ruffian. :-(

> It'd be interesting to know why the firmware has problems with that
> card...

Perhaps that card reports bogus interrupt pin and the firmware gets
confused. BTW, the kernel would assume INTA in this case.
