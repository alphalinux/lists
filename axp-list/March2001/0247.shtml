Daniel,

Thanks to the previous poster for identifying the source of confusion with
the driver identification printout.

>I very happily stand correct here. My card is indeed a 1040C = 1020
>revision 6, according to the logs. Very good to know. What does the "C"
>part indicate? I checked the Qlogic site, but found nothing.

The IC rev 6 and version C just means its the 6th revision of a minor core
modification in the 1040, the "C" being some feature enhancement. If I'm
not mistaken, they added a few bytes of depth to the FIFOs and improved some
auto-negotiation and active-negation issues. You should be able to pick up
more details here:

http://www.qlogic.com/products/isp1040c.html get the PDF for technical
details
Compare to:
http://www.qlogic.com/products/isp1040b.html get the PDF and do the same.

Note Qlogic has posted a new product, the QLA 12320 (Ultra SCSI 4, Fast
160=320MBps 16bit transfers), this is the first PCI X (64 bit, 133 MHz,
backward compatible to 66MHz PCI 2.2) component and offers two channels of
U4/320 (640 MBps) over a 1.06GBps PCI bus implementation.
