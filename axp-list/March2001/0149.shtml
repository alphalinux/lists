> Yup, your thinking is flawed Maurice. :) Open Sourcing

> Linux booting Linux, I think, makes alot more sense. A
> Linux kernel booting directly out of flash offers you

>
> We'll (API) have more to talk about regarding this in

So Mike, does this address booting Tru64 and VMS on the same system? I
know this is a Linux list, but not thinking of the big picure makes very
little sense when talking about something like this. Everyone wants one
bootloader for all their Alpha systems that supports all the gadgets they
want it to. Are you saying that API has a version of Linux to flash into
the SROM that will be able to boot Linux, Tru64, VMS, and *BSD? If not,
that just makes things more of a mess than they already are.

Don't get me wrong - I think Linux as a boot loader could be an admirable
goal, but only if it's taken to its fullest extreme and can be used for
all Alphas and all OSes that could be booted on them. Otherwise it's a
source of confusion and even more widely dispersed engineering effort and
a new source of interesting bugs. I'd love a Linux in SROM that can boot
everything from the Multia down the hall to the 256P EV7 systems that
we'll be building, and boot Linux, Tru64, VMS and *BSD. _That_ would be
cool - _if_ it doesn't cost too much in terms of performance vs. the SRM
that has been hand-tweaked for years here. Like I said, I like the idea,
I just want it to work "big picture", rather than be a point solution.
MHO...
