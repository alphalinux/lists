On Fri, Apr 06, 2001 at 11:45:25AM -0700, KirkE@paccessglobal.com wrote:

> I'm curious as to whether the rumored Caspian and Titan II chipsets will
> have support for the ECC DDR SDRAM in the larger 512MB or 1GB DIMM

I believe that information about these chipsets is only available
under non-disclosure. If you want to take Compaq's past history into
account, then you can guess that they will certainly support what's on
the market now, and 1 GB DIMMs have been available (although rare) for
2 years.

> Purportedly, the Titan chipset will be the new chipset that Compaq settles
> on, where Caspian seems to be the AMD/API project. Given that both of these
> are still vaporware,

Actually API has formally announced one piece of a chipset, it's a
chip that interfaces between HypedTransport (er, HyperTransport) and
PCI. So it's not totally vapor.

> At the very least, new Alpha platforms may serve to reduce the entry cost
> into EV6x systems, given that the current entry point is the Compaq DS10

API already has the UP1100, which uses AMD's chipset. I haven't asked,
but one would presume that they will continue to have a low-end
machine using the least expensive motherboard possible. If you really
care, you could ask.
