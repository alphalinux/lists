Peter,

>I wondered if we are missing the point here. With the introduction of 3G
mobile
>devices I wondered if we'll be moving away from the traditional desktop
machines
>to something smaller and more portable.

You raise an interesting point, I think there is a lot of validity to this.
On the other hand, I don't see desktop systems or servers going away. The
line will probably be drawn in a different place for determining what
ultimately justifies having a powerful system that is fettered by its own
bulk to wherever it is semi-permanently installed. Really powerful systems
(leave this to your own interpretation of what that will be in 7-10 years)
will probably be like something of a "personal server" while the primary
computing device will be some combination of portable computer (wearable?).
I have doubts that given the current environment if Alpha will see much use
in these "portable" roles unless they consistently keep the processor
simpler and the die shrinks ahead of the competition (lending nicely to
portable use). The only portable Alpha was the Alphabook 1 from Tadpole
(based on the 233MHz 21066A, which was already technically obsolete at the
time it was released). There was a point at which the 21264 was to be
adapted to portable/low cost use in the form of the 21264PC, but like the
21164PCA, it never appeared after the Digital acquisition by Compaq.

Digital's inspired StrongARM is seeing remarkable success, now in the hands
of Intel (with a new generation dubbed Xscale) and will debut at 1GHz before
the end of the year yet consume something between 1-3 watts. But this is no
Alpha processor, its based on the ARM core.

I see three major issues with the widescale adoption of truly handheld or
wearable PCs: 1) interfaces are still fairly crude, including miniaturized
displays, speech recognition of commands (you can train them only so far),
2) Lack of a broader range of applications (more geared towards
organizers/schedules, messaging and simple word processing than say
programming, layout or graphics manipulation) 3) Overall price/performance
is still less than a desktop machine in most comparisons.

It will still be years before the major issues of system power, battery
life, display and speech recognition are resolved. There is also the issue
of having some reduction in privacy of you are having to chatter verbally to
your portable/handheld/wearable computer in public.
      
Thanks for that interesting perspective Peter.
