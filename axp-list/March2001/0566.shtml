Peter,

>- EV6 is way too expensive.

You've got that right. Like you, I'm waiting to see the results of the IBM
fabbing of the Alpha. They are a good source for silicon, Sun Ecache debacle
aside.

>Alternatives? 21164A and 21164PC fabbed at todays more
>efficient micron yields and clocked higher. I suspect
>a 700MHz 21164PC would be more than adaquate for a LCA.

Who would fab the 21164A/21164PCx parts? Mitsubishi fabbed 21164PC for a
time, they could pick it up again if they could make some profit off of it.

>- Features?
>If you're starting from scratch , sticking with a bus
>entirley made up of PCI may be your best bet cost & time to market wise.
>You wouldnt have to deviate "that" much from the LX/SX reference board
>designs. If you want to build a board
>with AGP that could take alittle longer and cost more money.

If you want AGP, you can't use Tsunami/Typhoon (a pricey chipset that's
great for servers), leaving the AMD and VIA chipsets as the only options.

>For the folks claiming without AGP you wont have any video cards. You
>can develop this board with say openfirmware and have access to all the
>vid cards MACs have access to. Or develop an emulator that will execute
>their forth based bios.

If not OpenBoot firmware, some free implementation thereof, like FICL.

>- USB should be included and functional at the firmware level. So no
>more PS/2 ports. I can see atleast 4 USB ports...
>- Ethernet could be included. insert the cheapest chipset here.
>- firewire, include if cheap enough to implement.
>- Sound, maybe, maybe not.

IEEE 1394 chipsets from TI and IBM are quite cheap, not much more expensive
than the USB 2.0 chipsets at much higher frequency.

>- OS support?

>Decisions made here will directly influnece what chipsets are used. If
>you want Tru64/VMS support then you must go with tsunami. Which is
>expensive.

OpenVMS support is probably not going to happen, nice as it would be. Ditto
for Tru64. Compaq is putting the screws to the whole non-Compaq
Tru64/OpenVMS biz, a lack of hardware will make it all but impossible to run
these OSs as a home hobbyist on anything but old gear.

>dont expect any LCA to approach the price of a rock bottom priced PC. A
reasonable expectation is 30%-40% above
>what a mid-range PC costs and no they wont have matching bells and
whistles.

Given the lower production numbers, I'd be happy to see one of these in the
$1,500 to $1,900 range. A .18 micron 21164A with an 8MB L3 should do nicely
at 1GHz. : )

>-Conclusion
>- Folks get together and start your own company and develop such
>products.
>- VARs looking to expand their offerings and become an OEM
>- Wait and see how the UP1500 does and go from there.

Good stuff Peter.
