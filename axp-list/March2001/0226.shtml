On Fri, Mar 16, 2001 at 01:53:17AM -0900, bryan@ak.net wrote:
> Do the Tru64 compatibility libraries for alphalinux depend
> on anything specific to redhat?

No, they cannot. Red Hat did not buy Compaq yet. :-)

All this "Tru64" stuff depends only on a support in your kernel.
Newer kernels may have emulation of some system calls which did
not exist in earlier versions but that is about it and may, or
may not, be irrelevant to your specific binaries. Even if your
kernel does not have this support turned on then nothing prevents
you from recompiling your own version.

Distribution dependencies (even if they exist, as often they are
bogus) are really dependencies on specific library versions plus
some really minor configuration stuff. Libraries you are interested
in this specific case are NOT Linux libraries.

> I'm considering switching
> to another distribution, and I want to make sure I don't
> lose functionality.

You can move your Netscape between different Red Hat, and non-Red Hat
distributions even if nominally it comes from some specific one. :-)
