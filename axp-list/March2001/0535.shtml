With regards to your message at 01:29 PM 4/9/01, KirkE@paccessglobal.com.
Where you stated:
>Chris,
>
> >I think the real issue with VMS and Tru64 support is a matter of
> >the operating systems don't support most of the inexpensive hardware or
> >much hardware without the compaq firmware.
>
>Yes indeed, you are pretty limited on your bootable/supported hardware for
>these two OSs. One big loss is Intraserver which used to provide a much
>cheaper alternative to Compaq OEM SCSI/Network/Video controllers (including
>combo cards). Compaq is no longer going to produce drivers or SRM updates
>the recognize the Intraserver controllers properly, making them effectively
>"unsupported" which puts your support contract at risk (as well as making it
>necessary to hack the SRM and driver in order to get it to work, if your not
>under a support contract but want to run Tru64 or OVMS at home as a
>hobbyst).
>
>--KE
Intraserver is still around, and their cards are still available.
What HAS changed is that for U160 SCSI3 support Compaq opted to go with
Adaptec.
Good news is availability, bad news ( in my opinion) is that Adaptec sucks..
You have the scenario above backwards. It was not that Compaq isn't
supporting Intraserver, rather that they are supporting Adaptec, and
Intraserver uses LSI/Symbios chips.
Frankly, unless you are running a lot of disks it is not relevant, as the
existing LVD Ultra80 stuff (SCSI2) is still faster than a couple of hard
disks on a SCSI bus, so there is no advantage to SCSI3 for most applications..
