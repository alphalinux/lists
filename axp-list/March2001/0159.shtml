Peter,

>How about relaxing the the scope of which SRM decides to support a given
type of card,
>for example symbios based cards. I never understood why SRM needed to KNOW
the PCI vendor
>ID of every card it wanted to support.

A good point. I suppose the justification is that the size of the driver
that is used for a bootable device must be a very small footprint,
increasing the generality (wrapping a range of chipsets across SCSI 1
through SCSI 3 for example) would no doubt increase the size and complexity
of the driver, which would also increase regression testing and further
delay releases (the Compaq Party line). Given that 4, 8 and 16MB flash
EEPROM parts have gotten quite cheap (mirroring the trend in more
conventional SDRAM), it would at least be practical to increase the
footprint of the console footprint from the 1MB or less to something with a
larger device support. Lengthened development time (assuming no significant
increase in the number of engineers allocated) would of course apply.

API has started placing Linux in "ROM," but I have not played with this to
see how flexible the bootable configurations can be made as opposed to
traditional console firmware. Its a good concept in any case. Other options
exist as well (OpenBoot in particular) and are open source.

PALcode is the biggest sticking point all the way around. Alpha architecture
requires it, so he who controls the PALcode and SRM licenses controls all.
I have never been able to get a good answer out of Samsung/API whether their
free reign over Alpha architecture permits them similar access to SRM and
PALcode, I would only assume so.
