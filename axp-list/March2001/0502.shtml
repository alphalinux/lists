Greetings all,

I'm curious as to whether the rumored Caspian and Titan II chipsets will
have support for the ECC DDR SDRAM in the larger 512MB or 1GB DIMM
capacities given that other RISC vendors (Sun in particular) are investing
heavily in large memory configurations and VLM support in their respective
OS offerings. The Tsunami/Typhoon platform is almost 4 years old now with
the only other EV6x chipset available has been the diminutive and under
performing AMD Iron Gate 751.

Purportedly, the Titan chipset will be the new chipset that Compaq settles
on, where Caspian seems to be the AMD/API project. Given that both of these
are still vaporware, I don't suppose we'll be able to get a timeframe on the
release of these chipsets or the new platforms they will support. It's a
shame that we can't be teased by a tidbit of what's coming up the pipe.

At the very least, new Alpha platforms may serve to reduce the entry cost
into EV6x systems, given that the current entry point is the Compaq DS10
466MHz at around 1/2 of the cost of a single 667MHz/4MB UP2000+ system
(minus most of the additional cost associated with disk and display). Were
we not promised EV56 and PC57 price equivalent EV6x systems in this
timeframe by Digital and later the Q?

Hint, hint.

--KE
