> excellent engineers have available to them. And finally, I've been told there
> are some things in the PAL that Compaq will never allow to be seen because they
> are _that_ much of a competitive advantage (this could be wrong, I don't know).

One would guess that those items relate to the Wildfire/Galaxy boxes...
namely the "console system" -- which only exists on the high-end boxes.
And the serious competitive advantage which it gave Digital was lost when
Alpha NT was dropped. Who cares about running VMS, Tru64 and NT (now Linux)
SIMULTANEOUSLY on your DS10, or even XP1000? Only a couple of existing VMS
customers did - and they wanted to do it on a GS box anyway. Now they want
to do it on Wildfire adding in Tandem's NSK.

If you don't know what I'm babbling about, chalk it up to "stealth
marketing." So much for a "competitive advantage" that is so well known
and in such demand that everybody else is trying to emulate it!

[From an engineering point of view, it is a jaw-dropping thing to watch.]
