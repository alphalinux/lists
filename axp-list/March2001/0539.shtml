> >I'd substitute "Any GOOD 3d AGP chipset that has at least 24mb of on board

> Probably a good point. GeForce may be a good choice, but not to the
> exclusion of all others. As far as I know, neither the ATI Radeon nor the
> GeForce have bootable support thanks to their 32 bit extended VGA BIOS
> routines and lack of support in most OSes that Alpha supports.

SRM 5.9 has an updated BIOS emulator that can handle Radeon, TNT2 (5.8 can
can that one), don't know about GeForce.

> >Couldn't we use LINUX in flash the way API is now? This may/would save
> >development time, and I'm quite sure you could teach linux to load Tru 64
> >unix.
[snip]
>
> Absolutely, the only other alternative is to do an OpenBoot-like console
> based on the free implementations, like FICL. Since Linux is the primary
> business driver of Alpha at the low end to mid-tier, it makes sense to have
> a Linux-centric system design (especially on a Linux list). The adapted
> LinuxROM project that API is using is good evidence

Ummm...not quite. API's Linux in ROM is not based on the OpenBIOS
project.

>FSB is not present on all Alpha systems though, even some of the
> newer boards.

Boards that don't have FSB usually have DBM (debug Monitor) which can be
used to repair flash images, not as easy to use as FSB though.
