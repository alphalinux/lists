Of all the talks about the fabled low cost Alpha I
thought I should offer my spin on things :-) From a manufacturing POV.

- EV6 is way too expensive.

You all remember the talk when EV6 was first released that EV6 was going
to be soooooo cheap that everyone will buy one and things will get cheap
etc etc. That never happened. So just cross off your list of low cost ==
EV6.

Alternatives? 21164A and 21164PC fabbed at todays more
efficient micron yields and clocked higher. I suspect
a 700MHz 21164PC would be more than adaquate for a LCA.

- Features?
If you're starting from scratch , sticking with a bus
entirley made up of PCI may be your best bet cost & time to market wise.
You wouldnt have to deviate "that" much from the LX/SX reference board
designs. If you want to build a board
with AGP that could take alittle longer and cost more money.

For the folks claiming without AGP you wont have any video cards. You
can develop this board with say openfirmware and have access to all the
vid cards MACs have access to. Or develop an emulator that will execute
their forth based bios.

- USB should be included and functional at the firmware level. So no
more PS/2 ports. I can see atleast 4 USB ports...
- Ethernet could be included. insert the cheapest chipset here.
- firewire, include if cheap enough to implement.
- Sound, maybe, maybe not.

- OS support?

Decisions made here will directly influnece what chipsets are used. If
you want Tru64/VMS support then you must go with tsunami. Which is
expensive.

So you wanna run just Linux? Irongate and IG-4 are good options. Maybe
some VIA chipsets too. Yes these are lower performing chipsets and yes
PC parts arent as reliable as DEC
parts. It's a comprimise.

Your IDE support and such depend on the chipset you use.

- chips and such.
for as many toys you want built into the board it's still
cheaper to add more PCI slots than to imbed something. I personally like
the LX board that netapp has designed for use of their products. lots of
PCI slots. It may be wise to start with this board as a reference and
reduce it until you reach what you want.

- Pricing.

Begin flame war :-)

PC's are cheap for a reason. Sure they have
mass production on their side but their quality and
reliablilty doesnt hold a candle to anything DEC/Q, SUN,
or IBM makes. So dont expect any LCA to approach the price
of a rock bottom priced PC. A reasonable expectation is 30%-40% above
what a mid-range PC costs and no they wont have
matching bells and whistles.

-Conclusion

Well, I ask the question to you business folks out there.
Can I make money off this? You will have to invest money
and resources into building your own boards and cpus. Now can you get a
return? What markets are there that will buy these units complete for
say $1200-$1500 (total guestimate)?

I know I'm going to mangle this old addage but if you want somethign
done right you have to do it yourself. Whatever your definition of right
may be :-)

So in closing.

- Folks get together and start your own company and develop such
products.
- VARs looking to expand their offerings and become an OEM
- Wait and see how the UP1500 does and go from there.
