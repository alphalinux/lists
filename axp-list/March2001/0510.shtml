I apologize if my comment about the 2U case was considered by some as spam.
I was only trying to point out that a rack case for Alpha need be no more
expensive than X86.
Frankly, with regards to the other posting I think it missed the point that
Kirk was making is very valid. While there will always be older models
available, either through EBay or dealers of refurbs and used computers,
most users who want an Alpha for development want a machine that is vaguely
"state of the art". Spending money on a machine that does not perform well
by modern standards and has had no BIOS updates for 2 years is not an
adventure for most to embark on.

And that is somewhat lacking in todays market.
UP1100 is still available for now, and is not a bad machine, other than
lack of memory bandwidth. In the 700MHz variant it is actually fairly speedy.
XP1000 and DS10 are decent machines, and reasonably modern. They have good
memory bandwidth, and give up a bit on CPU speed, but not that much when
you realize the fastest clockspeed on a 21264 is still at 833MHz.

But if we go back further than that it makes no sense to me. Of course it
all depends on one's expectations, budget and goals..

An Athlon at 1.2GHz is a pretty fast machine, and costs around $1500 in
full trim.
It will certainly kick an LX or earlier machine in most significant
benchmarks.
The Sun 1105 and Blade were never designed to be speed demons. Just a
decent cheap architecture to run Solaris, with modern hardware support.
Ultimately it is often the hardware support list that is the desirable
feature. And on the Alphas to date it has been somewhat problematic. Recent
versions of SRM which added support for TNT2 and 3DLABS Oxygen VX1 cards
have helped enormously in this respect. The prospect of going on another
Easter egg hunt for Elsa Gloria Synergy cards makes me shudder in pain!
At least the UP1x00 have supported AGP video for over a year now, and that
helps plenty. As does the onboard ATA66 chip.

Anyway, just my $0.01 worth ( that's $0.02 Canadian)
