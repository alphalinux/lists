I wondered if we are missing the point here. With the introduction of 3G mobile
devices I wondered if we'll be moving away from the traditional desktop machines
to something smaller and more portable. Just as we laugh at the brick like
mobile phones of a few years ago within 5-10 years or even less people may laugh
at the clumsy desk tops we use today. With new technologies such as Transmeta
Crusoe, cheaper smaller and solid state disks, and more powerful bus
technologies we may find that we have a smaller hand held device that is always
connected to the internet and which we carry from the office, home, college and
then plug into monitor/keyboard/mouse . The need for more and more powerful
desktop processors will be limited except for unique applications such as games
(which will be replaced by cheaper consoles). Thus I can see why Compaq is
erring towards the Server end for the Alpha where there will still be for a
years a healthy market for vms/tru64 and possibly Linux (maybe a market for
handhelds here).
