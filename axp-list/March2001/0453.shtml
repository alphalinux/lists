On Tue, Apr 03, 2001 at 01:51:52PM -0400, Greg Johnson wrote:
>
> I've been having trouble getting the 2.4.x series kernels working
> on my Sable (2100). The kernel boots, but when it's detecting the
> SCSI goes into an endless loop resetting the SCSI bus. It claims
> to be timeing out. The system works fine with kernel 2.2.x.
>
> I believe the SCSI controller is a ncr53c810, so I can't use the
> sym53c8xx driver.

The ncr53c8xx driver is the correct one for 810; do NOT use the 53c7,8xx
one any longer, it's not maintained AFAIK.

As to the problem, no clue... :-\

The 810 in question is the built-in one, right? Not behind a bridge?

I'm afraid the 2.4.x kernels haven't been well-tested (if at all) on
those old SABLEs, and a *lot* of changes were made in PCI probing and
SCSI drivers.

Does the log indicate what IRQ is being used for the 810?
