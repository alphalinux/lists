Personally, I'd like to see support for contemporary, 3rd-party, hardware
before it becomes antique.

I don't think OUR "older hardware" is half the problem -- it's Compaq's
"old PCIhardware" that is -- continuing support for incredibly proprietarty
antiques like the PowerStorm cards is a good example.

Aside from a very small 3D market, the PowerStorm cards are out-performed
at radically cheaper prices by almost every other video card available
today. ATI Radeon and nVeida Gforce immediately spring to mind. There was a
time when the price premium might have been justifiable for support of
"high-end" graphics, but not any more. You get conned into it because your
only alternative is the Elsa Gloria junk. Then you are stuck running
Tru64 or having lousy graphics. (I still want to be able to run my PS350
under Linux.)

Philosophically within Compaq, the problem also comes from the fact that
the Alpha Product line does NOT embrace "Professional Workstations."
Those are Wintel Boxes. Alpha workstations are a separate category and
exist primarily because the VMS crowd within DECUS yelled and screamed
sufficiently at the loss of the old single user VAX station that they got
one created... then we (the Unix sig) had to yell and scream for another
year because that Alpha was announced as a VMS product only, with no
plans for (then) Digital Unix to support it! The XP900 was the result.

Linux within Compaq is now an unknown quantity... mainly because it appears
to have been taken over by the "Industry Standard Server" (ie Intel/Amd not
Alpha) folks. But then again that could just be the effect of the standard
unix "stealth marketing" approach.

I'm quite eager to find out what and if any changes are going to happen
with the new head of the BSG... but so far I haven't seen any signs of a
change in direction. ESPECIALLY on the marketing side. Stealth Marketing
still rules... it gets really, really depressing.
