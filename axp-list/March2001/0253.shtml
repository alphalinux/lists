On Fri, 16 Mar 2001, Peter Watkinson wrote:

> Hi,
>
> I've been thinking about doing the RedHat Certified Engineer Course. I was wondering
> If anyone had done the course and whether they thought it was useful?
>

I took the course and passed the exam about 2 years ago. It was very
worth it to me. I don't know if the course has changed.

Will it be worth it to you? Depends.

I had several years experience as a UNIX (Ultrix/DU) system manager and I
knew very little about Linux specifically. I didn't need much to bring me
up to speed. I think it helped my confidence as much as anything.

The exam is different than any of the other certification exams around,
since it consists of one section of multiple choice questions, and two
sections of hands-on exam: one troubleshooting, one complete system
installation/configuration implementing an assigned list of services.
Both exercises were very realistic and useful.

Now, in the class that I was in, a large percentage of people were there
to get a feel for what Linux was all about. They were Window NT or
Netware system managers and/or consultants, and boy were they lost! If
you have trouble with the Gnome GUI or if you are lost without a GUI, then
you'd have a hard time here!

The class goes over material very quickly, assuming that you do have some
foundation. While you don't have to take EVERY listed prerequisite
seriously, you should have a good foundation with UNIX system programming
if not with Linux. While the instructors were very good, in general, the
time frame is too short to take the time for too much hand-holding, so
fasten your seatbelt if you decide to go. On the other hand, there were
experienced Linux system and application programmers in the course that
just breezed through the course - it is not an ADVANCED RedHat Cert Exam.
As I said, it demonstrates basic, useful system config/management.
Also, you are coming from the AXP Linux world. The course is an I386
course, mostly. The instructor may have experience with AXP and can also
pass on your questions to other instructors, but not much time is given to
other versions of RHL than I386.

So, is it worth it? For me it was.
