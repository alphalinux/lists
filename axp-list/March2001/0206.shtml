alvin wrote:
> For what It's worth I am not sure that the linuxthreads implemtation is
> all that solid.

Well, linuxthreads is a little naive but I see that as a good thing,
since it is less likely to have bugs than a complex m x n
implementation.

glibc 2.2 will fix some outstanding issues as well.

> I found a number of places where MySQL would break that
> seem to be realated to thread creation and mmap. But I have been assuerd
> by the linuxthreads maintainers that I am stupid and this could not be
> the case ;)

Was that with SMP, by any chance? Alpha is probably the most
challenging platform for threaded apps. If a package works correctly on
Alpha, it is likely to be correct everywhere else. That's yet another
good excuse to develop and test on Alpha hardware ;)

Besides that, there are many corner cases in pthreads that are
underspecified (the "p" in pthreads surely doesn't stand for
"portable").
