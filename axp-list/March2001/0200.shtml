Alan Young wrote:
>
> Ron Farrer wrote:
> >
> > Jeff Sturm (jeff.sturm@appnet.com) wrote:
> >
> > > I don't believe so. Linus had SRM because he did his early development
> > > on OSF/1, before Alpha/Linux became self-hosting. As a nice
> > > side-effect, Linux and OSF/1 (now Tru64) became compatible at the system
> > > call layer.
> >
> > Not comletely, remember Linux cannot handle Tru64 threads...
>
> I believe that's due to Linux not supporting certain kernel functions
> that the Tru64 kernel has and the threads library is looking for them.

Right. The point I didn't make is that Linus really only implemented
the syscalls he needed to get a self-hosting system, and Linux/OSF
syscalls have probably diverged since anyway. You can bet that Linux
also has syscalls that Tru64 does not.

I don't use Tru64, but I'd wager that its thread APIs are based on Mach
tasks, which could be very difficult to emulate (feel free to correct me
here). Although Linux can run with Mach (as in MkLinux for PPC) to my
knowledge nobody has attempted that on Alpha.
