With regards to your message at 01:58 PM 7/1/01, Peter Watkinson. Where you
stated:
>Hi,
>
>I'm considering or was considering purchasing a UP1500. Now that the Alpha
>processor is going the way of the fishes I'm concerned about the future of
>Alpha Linux. Ideally I'd like support of Alpha Linux way into the future but
>being a pragmatist I'd be happy with support say for the next 3 years.
>Seeing is this is probably the best place to ask can anyone tell me for how
>long into the future willl Redhat be supporting Alpha Linux. Failing that
>can anyone tell me where to purchase an Itanium system and how much are they
>likely to cost.
I am sure that Red Hat will be supporting it for a long time. There is a
big installed base.

Itaniums as it sits right now are not very fast, and cost a LOT of money.
IT appears that Intel is pretty well stuck on development, probably why
they want the Alpha teams so badly.. And that presumes that these people
will work for Intel, something that is far from a "done deal" yet..

As for Alpha being dead, we will see new machine through EV7, and into EV8
before Compaq is able to move to Itanium.

In the meantime Alpha is a long way from dead yet. Just because Compaq is
quitting does not mean the platform is going away.

Stay tuned for more developments on this front over the next couple of weeks..
