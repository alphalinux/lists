With regards to your message at 11:19 PM 6/11/01, Emir Alikadic (ADNOC
IS&T). Where you stated:
>I've posted this message on comp.os.linux.alpha so forgive me if you're
>reading it for the second time...
>
>I have a chance to convert a couple of my company's DS7300's (21164@400MHz)
>running WinNT 4.0 to Linux but unfortunately haven't had a chance to work
>with Linux on Alpha before (using Linux on x86 for couple years now,
>though).
>
>Could anyone give me some quick pointers on how to approach this,
>especially:
>1. Good *Alpha* distro (I prefer RedHat and derivatives on x86 but I don't
>know how good they are on Alpha - I need Alpha-specific experience, not a
>distro flamewar)

Red Hat and SuSE dominate in the commercial distribution market for Alpha.
As such they are good starting points, but it is never a bad thing to see
what the major updates are for this, as with any platform.
There are quite a few other smaller and derivative distributions, ranging
from Debian to other commercial endeavors.

>2. How compatible are 7300's? I'd especially like to hear from someone who's
>already installed Linux on a 7300 (4100 with DEC marketroid braindamage, as
>Paul from COLA put it).

Many people have successfully installed on that platform.

>3. Good apps for Linux on Alpha (e.g. web, mail, file/print - not scientific
>stuff)

An Alpha running Linux is as capable as an x86 in the typical server
functions. i.e.: Samba, Sendmail, and so on. As such the Alpha platforms
have a good history of reliability, and excellent bandwidth and
characteristics under load.

>4. Online resources for Linux on Alpha (aside from
>linuxalpha.org/alphalinux.org)

Alphalinux.org is the traditional main point, having taken over from the
predecessor aztarnet site, which still exists, but is no longer
significantly updated.
For example:
ftp://ftp.azstarnet.com/pub/linux/axp/docs/FAQ.html#toc5

There are a few other good points, and they are more than adequately
referenced at :
http://www.alphalinux.org/otherpages/
