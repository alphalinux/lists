Yes, it *was* nice to see Alpha mentioned, even though he didn't test
it... ;-}

Regardless, the notable things related to Alpha in 4.1.0 are:

1. 3DFx card 3D support is *much* quicker than before. I tested Banshee
   and Voodoo3 3000 PCI cards on both EV56/PYXIS and EV6 machines
   and had good results.

2. ATI Rage 128 PCI and Radeon PCI now support 3D operation via PCI-GART
   hardware on the cards. Again, tested on both EV56/PYXIS and EV6 based
   machines, with very nice results.

3. support for Matrox Millenniums and Permedia 2/2v should now be working
   again on the older (non-EV6) machines.

4. TGA support now includes TGA2 support, both 8 and 24 bit cards.

5. Permedia 3 (VX1) PCI 2D-only support, seems to work on most boxes.

Note that other/older machines may have significant problems trying
with 3DFx/Rage128/Radeon, related to inability of the consoles' BIOS
emulation to work on the card, or the requirement for SPARSE access.

Unfortunately, there were some of my patches that got left out due to
time or testing constraints, among them being reduction of the
memory/swap requirements for the Xserver process on Alpha machines
requiring SPARSE access. This is especially important for those older
machines with small memory configurations.

These patches will be available soon; I'll post an announcement on
this list (among other places).

Enjoy! We now have 3 cards that'll do 3D stuff on our PCI Alphas... :-)
