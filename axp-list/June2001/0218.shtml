Well, without going on at length, only 2 things:
1) I too have not heard of anyone having much success with these.
2) All of the "cheap" cards I have seen are substantially slower than the
"expensive" ones.
3) Software transports are often as much of a problem as the hardware itself.
Wes mentioned ftp limitations. I have also seen similar limits on NFS2, but
many people report better results under NFS3.
4) We have many customers who started with "cheap" cards and then migrated
to "expensive" cards and were much happier.

So, have I run comparative benchmarking between them? No.

But a lot of anecdotal evidence from customers and other users suggest that
in these, as in so many things , you get what you pay for..

BTW, sorry, I apologize if my previous message sounded insulting to you. I
admit to being a bit bitter on the subject of NICs.

I hate ethernet cards.
I just wasted 3 hours today arranging to return some Intel 8460C NICs that
were supposed to be compatible with the 8460B. And then finding a
distributor who still had stock of the 8460B's.
They aren't compatible, to say the least.
