People often forget it IS Linux, and that distributions are just
"snapshots" of what a company decided to package at some given point in
time. As progress continues, and nothing stops when a distro is made, it is
best to consider these as simply "starting points".
Naturally if you are a fan of "Distribution X", stick to that, as it is
going to give you the shortest learning curve to get comfortable.
