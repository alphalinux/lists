> I think the main thing to consider in all of this is that Compaq has a

existing
> and future generations.
>
> Here is the quote from the Compaq press release that is both relevant, and
> gives a bit of an idea about what lies ahead:
>
> "Compaq will immediately begin to port Tru64 UNIX, OpenVMS and NonStop
> Kernel operating systems and development tools to the Itanium processor
> family. Operating system and application development tools compatibility
> protects customers' long-term investments in Tru64 UNIX, OpenVMS, and
> NonStop Kernel, as well as advancing the capabilities for Windows 2000 and
> Linux on ProLiant."
>
> In other words at least a year, probably 2 or 3 to move the OS over.
>
>

> Under the multi-year technology agreement, Compaq is transferring

> granting licenses to Compaq's Alpha microprocessor technology and

>
> Intel now has access to the technologies and people who can make Itanium
> faster.
>
>
> "AlphaServer, NonStop, ProLiant Roadmaps
