Compaq dropped NT support for Alpha circa RC2 for Win2k. MS refused to "go it
alone" on Alpha/NT without Compaq's (financial) support. MS never released Win2k
on Alpha. However, MS used Alphas to build Win2k/64 in-house to prove that the
code remained "portable" and did not contain any processor-specific type bugs. It
also helped during the period when IA64 was in short supply and buggier than a
discovery-channel "All bugs, all weekend" special. As of about 6 months ago MS
had 1500+ IA64 systems, so hardware availability it's not such a big issue.

I think it would be interesting to see the on-die waste-of-space x86 emulation
being replaced by various incarnations of FX!32. Forget the Alpha chip -- the
real jewels Intel has acquired is the killer back-end GEM compiler, and the FX!32
translator.
