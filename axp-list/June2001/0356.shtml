With regards to your message at 09:15 AM 6/27/01, Peter Watkinson. Where
you stated:
>Well Transmeta developed the Crusoe how much did that cost?
>They must have got funding from somewhere.

Yes, they did, as did many people in the last few years.
To say that the economic and venture cap market has changed would be an
understatement.
Then, of course, one has to ask who is going to fund it if it is "open source"?

>For me it's a real shame Intel buying the Alpha I'm amazed that the US
>Government doesn't intervene.
>I'm sure I read on the Alpha Powered website that Alpha development was
>protected by Law for the next 20 years. It's like Coke buying Pepsi or
>Mcdonalds
>buying Burger King I 'm sure they'd take some action then.

Really? Why?
If you own something, and can't sell it, then is probably has less value.
The U.S. government seems unable to police Microsoft on what are really
fairly clear and straightforward abuses, so why should this be different?
Whether you like Intel or not, they are successful, and no government wants
to curb a local industry that is employing lots of people and making money.
If you want real grounds for monopoly abuse against Intel contemplate the
fact that it is in a battle with AMD, and by all reports selling it's P4
CPUs below cost. Usually that abuse is called dumping, among other things.

>For my part I'd like to see another Risc processor. Not sure how high end it
>would need to be.
We already have enough of those.
What we don't have is an economy where Compaq feels they can win a long
term battle with Intel and Sun, and make enough off of it to satisfy
shareholders.
