There are a couple of cooling methods which cool to temperatures below
ambient that a person could do in his own home. The simplest being a
peltier cooler--a pad of two different conducting materials sandwiched
together, which when a current is run through them, one side gets hot and
the other cold. There's a good description of this at
http://www.arstechnica.com/guide/cooling/peltier-1.html.

The other way is via a small cooling tower. I've seen descriptions of
setups using 4 or 6 inch diameter PVC pipe with a small shower head at the
top, or a coil of soft copper tubing drilled with small holes every few
inches. The idea is to sprinkle water down the tube while forcing air up
it (with a 80mm or 92mm fan). The water evaporates and dissipates the
heat. There are a few examples of this at http://www.overclockers.com.

The cooling tower seems a little rickety to me, although technically
fascinating. A peltier along with a standard closed watercooling system
seems far more practical. Any of these can be done for far less than
US$24K--more like $50 to $200.
