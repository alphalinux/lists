I think the main thing to consider in all of this is that Compaq has a need
to continue to support TRU64 and VMS on their hardware. It will certainly
take some time to port the OS to Itanium, and it will also take time for
Itanium to achieve anything resembling Alpha performance, both for existing
and future generations.

Here is the quote from the Compaq press release that is both relevant, and
gives a bit of an idea about what lies ahead:

"Compaq will immediately begin to port Tru64 UNIX, OpenVMS and NonStop
Kernel operating systems and development tools to the Itanium processor
family. Operating system and application development tools compatibility
protects customers' long-term investments in Tru64 UNIX, OpenVMS, and
NonStop Kernel, as well as advancing the capabilities for Windows 2000 and
Linux on ProLiant."

In other words at least a year, probably 2 or 3 to move the OS over.

"Technology Transfer
Under the multi-year technology agreement, Compaq is transferring
significant Alpha tools and engineering resources to Intel, as well as
granting licenses to Compaq's Alpha microprocessor technology and compilers. "

Intel now has access to the technologies and people who can make Itanium
faster.

"AlphaServer, NonStop, ProLiant Roadmaps
Compaq said it will continue to design and build new AlphaServer™ systems
based on current and upcoming Alpha processor technology through 2003. The
company plans to upgrade the current high-end AlphaServer GS Series with a
1 GHz Alpha processor this summer. The next-generation EV7 Alpha processor,
which is currently under development, will power a new AlphaServer system
planned for introduction late next year. "

In other words it will take at least 2 more years to phase out Alpha.

Yes, this is the end of Alpha, but not for at least a couple of years.

Really I think this will be very good for Alpha products to migrate in to
the "low cost market" that people have been asking for.
With the existing cost investment in Alpha, I think Compaq will want to
squeeze every penny it can on what it has already paid for..

And for us Linux users the good news is that we will finally reach a point
where we don't have the endless situation where the updates to kernel and
libs lag between x86 and Alpha..
