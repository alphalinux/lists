On Sun, 10 Jun 2001, Andri Saar wrote:

> Hi,
>
> So happened that I dug out a old 164LX board that had been discarded a
> while back, and thinking why, plugged a vid card in and much to my
> surprise the board booted.
>
> This board seems to have some strange bugs. As I am no Alpha expert (in
> fact, it's my first non-x86 comp I have mettled with to such a large
> extent), I cannot tell if those problems can be fixed with a simple
> patch or flash or must I forget the idea of using a 64-bit computer...
>
> First, booting a floppy from SRM sometimes succeeds, sometimes fails
> crashing after telling me that it's bootstrapping. No ideas why.

Bad floppy disk(s), drive. and/or cable?
 
> Second, I tried to use MILO by flashing AlphaBIOS, but the comp seems to
> 'forget' it and next time it boots SRM is back. Might it be the failsafe
> as, honestly, I haven't plugged the speaker in? And if it is, how to fix
> it?

The Failsafe boot only loads the firmware from floppy, it does not flash
it in for you as well. So after AlphaBIOS started you would need to choose
the "upgrade AlphaBios" uption and actually do the update.

> Somehow, I got Red Hat 7.0 installed, and stupid as I am on DOS
> partitions. Can't RH be booted from DOS partitions at all?

Well, via floppy or network. Or boot, do a
dd if=/dev/sda of=/dev/null bs=512 count=4

(this zeros out the partition table) and re-install, the RH installer will
then see no disk label and partition using BSD.
