Back on that Alpha/Intel thing. It's pretty well understood that VLIW
architectures require intensive compiler work. So my idea of Intel's main
job in all of this . . .
One: An attempt to weaken and get rid of a competitor. (obvious)
and
Two: Get a compiler to produce code that makes the (t)itanic perform closer
to the theory of the design. (which *in theory* isn't a bad design)

The Digital people do have a history of making great compilers and tools for
advanced processors (FX32, ccc, spike, etc). Which leads to the next
statistics question . .
What percentage of engineers jumping ship is required to make Intel's
investment in orginal Digital code go to crap? :)

I still can't get my head around what the hell Compaq gets out of this. It
can not be humanly possible to port their tools, OpenVMS, AND True64 to an
ia-64 type system that doesn't even exist yet in only 3 years. And how much
will that cost? The entire distribution of RedHat 7.1 is estimated to be
over 1 Billion dollars. Porting two operating systems and all of the tools
can not be any cheaper than that.

crawl around on the floor with my kids. I don't care how much money Intel
pumps into the ia-64, it will never be as fast as my 10 month old son
crawling for a toy he can destroy. :)
