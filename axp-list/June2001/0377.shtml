I do not believe this Identity statement. Anyone who has
studied the details of design of the Alpha's
(according to regularly published white papers
by Dec/Compaq designers), the pipelining,
out of order execution, and branch prediction
hardware, along with the number of parallel
execution units is a big distinction in what was
planned for the early IA-64 Intel machines and
even the EV67. We can only hope that Intel
will take these ideas and be able to capitalize
them, find enough market share to make them
affordable which Dec/Compaq could never do.
I hate ideas, ones they know are "right" and spreads
them out over generations in order to maximize
cash flow. However, we see who is left
standing and who isn't.
