On Sat, Jun 23, 2001 at 09:20:41AM -0700, John E. Augustine wrote:
>
> I am trying to install an ATI XPert 98 video card (PCI) that is
> listed in the redhat.com alpha hardware compatibility list in my
> digital workstation 500 au. SRM complains when I do this, and it
> says...
>
> "Illegal device detected on primary bus in physical slot 4 Power
> down the system and remove the unsupported device from slot 4"
>
> I tried to see if alphabios would recognize it. It did not like the
> card either. I pretty recently upgraded my bios to the latest
> available i.e., SRM firmware V7.2-1. Please let me know how I can
> work around this.

To get that card working in a 64-bit slot, try telling SRM:

>>> set pci_override -1

It should work in a 32-bit slot, however performance will be affected.

> Also, I previously had a powerstorm series video card that I could
> not get Xconfigurator to recognize. Has anybody been successful with
> those cards?

IIRC, the older Powerstorm cards were numbered thus:

3D10 S3 Trio64 VGA
3D30 TGA2 8-plane
4D10 Permedia-2 VGA
4D20 TGA2 24-plane

Full support exists for TGA2 now in 4.1.0.
