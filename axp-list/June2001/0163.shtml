On Mon, Jun 18, 2001 at 09:43:05AM +0100, David Woodhouse wrote:
>
> There's no code in XFree86 to deal with the second head of the G400. If it
> claims to be trying to do so, that's a bug.

To be more precise I was experimenting with various "rawhide" rpms
for XFree86-4.0.3 (Mike Harris from Red Hat); in particular 4.0.3-15
and 4.0.3-16. These sources included somethings which seemed to me
as a dual head support for mga but I may as well be mistaken. It seems
to be working, at least to some extent, for G450 and this HALlib
requirement for G400 is mentioned by Matrox in READMEs which come
with their driver; but they may mean the current state of affairs.

OTOH these srpms include things like 'XFree86-4.0.3-mga-dualhead-dpms.patch'
so I am not sure what this is fixing? Do you mean that G400 requires
more model specific spells?

> The second head of the G400 is reasonably well-documented, and example code
> exists in the Linux matroxfb driver - the author of which has given
> permission for his code to be reused under the XFree86 licence. All that's
> missing is a volunteer with sufficient motivation and time to port it.

Some understanding what is going on would undoubtely help as well. :-)

> I've heard reports of strangeness with some modelines. It's working fine
> here, albeit on i386, with 1600x1200 on the second head.

G450 or G400? Could you mail me out-of-list your XF86Config file?
Modelines I tried were mostly defaults (or maybe slightly tuned up
for my monitor).
