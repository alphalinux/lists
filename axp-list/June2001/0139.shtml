> On Mon, Jun 18, 2001 at 12:56:08AM +0200, Jonas Gustavsson wrote:

> > I was thinking about the new PCI model of the G450(even though it´s a slap
> > on PCI bridge thingy on those)

> Well, Michal knows best about those things.

I know only what I have seen on a small sample of cards with
which I tried to bring a server up. :-)

> I know there's been problems with making both heads work, in the past,
> under XFree86. 2D, single head is fine, IIRC.
>
> There's a small binary library (x86 only, of course) from Matrox that
> is needed to make dual-head (among other things) work best.

This HALlib module from Matrox is needed to get a picture on the
second screen of a G400 card. Without it everything seems to be just
fine if you look at log files but there is this small detail that
you cannot see anything on the second screen (the first one is fine).
Matrox provides this binary-only module only for x86 - at least
so far. They make noises about other architectures but this remains
to be seen.

OTOH with G450 a dual screen works without an extra HALlib and this
means that it works on Alpha as well. You have to explicitly specify
PCI BusID for the second screen; the first one will be found by
your server on a startup if not given in your XF86Config-4 file, but
this is the only onerous requirement as far as I can tell.

With the card I was experimenting I had various "nasties" in a picture
on the second screen in any resolution above 1024x768, IIRC. No
problems on the first screen or when resolution was low enough. No idea
what was the trouble here. Could be that my hardware has
"imperfections" or something is funny in software but the later would be
a bit strange, I think.
