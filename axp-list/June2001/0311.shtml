> I still can't get my head around what the hell Compaq gets out of this. It
> can not be humanly possible to port their tools, OpenVMS, AND True64 to an
> ia-64 type system that doesn't even exist yet in only 3 years. And how much
> will that cost? The entire distribution of RedHat 7.1 is estimated to be
> over 1 Billion dollars. Porting two operating systems and all of the tools
> can not be any cheaper than that.

Tru64 already has a head start, as there was a project to port Tru64
to the IA64 earlier; they can pick up where they left off.

Himalaya NSK was being ported to the IA64 when Pfieffer (sp?) killed
the port in favor of a port to Alpha. Again, they can pick up where
they left off.

The port for OpenVMS is expected to take the longest time, at 18 months,
but a lot of the porting work should be easier as they went through
most of the problems during the VAX->Alpha port. The biggest roadblock
should be getting the GEM compilers ready.

Most of this information is from the announcement and discussions in
comp.os.vms.
