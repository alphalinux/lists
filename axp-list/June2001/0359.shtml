Another set of questions;

1 - Since the deal is 'non-exclusive', why not also provide similar deal to
AMD so they also get full access to EV-7/EV-8 etc stuff and integrate them
with X86-64? I'm sure AMD could make a pure 64-bit CPU that could run both
Alpha and X86-64 binaries...

2 - Are we going to see another press release in say June 2003, saying "Due
to increased cost and diminishing returns as it now has no distinctive
hardware compared to the others, Compaq will transfer its Tru64 and OVMS OS
platforms, engineers etc. to Micro$oft, and will only use Windows XPGT
Enterprise in its future Itanium servers, of course enhanced with Tru64 &
OVMS features given to M$'

3 - An Intel exec told me today that Intel will not necessarily stay with
the present Itanium instr set and that, if the performance demands it, they
may combine Alpha and Itanium arch and instr set features into the 'Itanium
Procesor Family 2 - IPF2' starting in 2004, after Madison CPU. He says that
even providing hardware assists for direct Alpha binary execution is
considered. I also heard their very negative opinion about Compaq PR and
customer handling, saying Intel is aware about many customers' being pissed
off...
