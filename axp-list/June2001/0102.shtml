Mike Foley wrote:

>The "marketroid" comment was directed at DEC, not
>you Maurice. As you know, the PC Group at DEC came
>out with "white" Alpha's that would only run NT and
>not Tru64 and VMS. This was done for marketing reasons,
>or so I was told when I joined that group in 1997. Being
>a new person in that group, I held my tongue, but
>privately, I thought it was a silly idea. However, if
>you knew the politics of DEC at the time, there was a
>push from the PC group to lower the cost of Alpha. The
>"enterprise servers" group said "only if they don't run
>VMS or Tru64, our cash cows" (that's paraphrased) The
>PC group was kind of forced into it.

This is a rant over spilled milk, but I have some time to burn
right now:

I'm sure we all have a lot to say about marketing decisions made by
DEC, as far back as the VAX-750. Talk about shooting themselves in
the foot competing in the industry. While SUN was trying to be as
aggressive as possible in delivering their latest and greatest, to
the point of delivering stuf that often doesn't quite work yet,
DEC would actually intentionally slow down their processors, or hold
back delivery of faster processors, just so they don't compete with
their own existing systems. It was definitely the wrong business
model throttling some excellent technical development.

>So, the reference was directed at DEC, circa 1997,
>NOT you.

Ditto, circa 1982.
