On Wed, 20 Jun 2001 jsturm1@home.com wrote:

> This is getting off topic, but...
>
> I fail to understand Linus's stubborn refusal to adopt a version system
> like CVS. That's how distributed development is done.

But, you forget, the kernel is NOT a project that's a wide-scale
development project. Sure, many people submit patches and blah, blah,
blah, but, for the most part, only two people control what goes into the
kernel tree (Alan and Linus)...and that's the way they want to keep it.

I don't object to having a few folks reviewing changes, especially since
the volume of changes submitted daily could easily pollute/break kernel
operation possibly every minute of each day, but the case could be made
also that the sheer volume is more than two people can handle.

I dunno the solution, personally. My feeling is that they should allow
the ports to maintain themselves by using a person/group in charge of each
port who can have total control over the changes that go in for that port.
I don't see how either Alan or Linus (neither of which, IIRC, has powered
up their Alphas in over a year) know what's best for Alpha.

I know I'm coming off a bit harsh against Alan and Linus...I don't mean
to, but frustration has set in within my brain. I have had pleasant
conversations with both and think that they both do a damn good job under
the circumstances, but I can't help but feel like i386 and powerpc get
more attention from them than any other port out there (making the rest of
us feel like our architectures are "second-class citizens").
