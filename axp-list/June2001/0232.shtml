Uncle George wrote:
>
> Then 2 FTP's ( simultaneously run ) at the same time would aggregate to ~80mb.sec ?
>

Not necessarily. By FTP, I mean the whole process. On the
write side, you have a read() from cache, and a write() to
the socket. On the receive side, a read() from the socket,
and a write() to /dev/null. The other issue is how FTP sets
it's socket state and buffer sizes. At least with only
sockets code, you eliminate a lot of variables. If it's not
FTP, then it well could be a less than optimal driver. I didn't
look at the source closely so I don't know if it has sufficient
internal buffers. Then the other thing is can you use jumbo
frames. When I used the x-over wire, I could and it made
about a 30% difference, 1500 vs 4000. I just reran the test
and I get 45MB/sec now, slightly better than the last time.
All that I know of different is one of the systems was rebooted
a week or so ago. Anyway, it's a little better now.

These were the Asante cards on an Athlon 1.2Ghz and PIII 933.

Wes
