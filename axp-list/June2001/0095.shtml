I guess your experience is heavy-duty network jobs and related
programs - for such applications I can see your point.

However, my experience during last months was very different from yours:
(Running RH/Debian/Slack at home and Suse on a DS10 I got an access to,
and compiling mostly office-related stuff on them)
1) g++ 2.96 - you will be surprised how many packages are fine with 2.95.2
but can't be compiled with RH g++;
2) glibc variations hell - somehow I stepped most often on it with Debian -
glibc report 2.1.3 or 2.2 yet the program links fine on one system and barfs
on another;
3) PAM (security) - Slack is without and others are with;
etc etc etc ...
So, I agree with Chris here - if someone has at least some experience on how
to make stuff working on his favorite distro on x86, it can help very well on
Alpha too.
