On Wed, Jun 20, 2001 at 05:33:04PM +0200, Rick van der Linde wrote:
> Newer versions of Linux won't support ARC boots.
> Correct me if I'm wrong. This is what I interpreted from the AlphaLinux
> documentation.

"Won't support" really means that:
  - if you bought a Red Hat boxed set
  - you have troubles booting with milo because you did not read
    writeups on www.alphalinux.org
  - you called Red Hat phone line support for help
then they will tell you "go away" as they likely have no idea what all
of this about. Provided documentation also explains only what to do
with SRM.

OTOH likely you will find on the first distribution disk a directory
strangely named 'milo', with some 18 MB of unsupported software, and it
is up to you to figure out what to do with it. The answer really is
"boot CD and load ramdisk from a floppy". If you have an already
working milo and you are doing an upgrade then nothing really compels
you to even look into 'milo' subdirectory. Your old milo is fine. You
just need to make a floppy with a ramdisk image and boot with
'load_ramdisk=1`.
