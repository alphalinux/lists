On Tue, 12 Jun 2001, Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> Red Hat and SuSE dominate in the commercial distribution market for Alpha.
> As such they are good starting points, but it is never a bad thing to see
> what the major updates are for this, as with any platform.
> There are quite a few other smaller and derivative distributions, ranging
> from Debian to other commercial endeavors.

I'm not going to start a flamewar here, although it would be easy for me
to do so :-) To answer the question more directly, RedHat does have an
Alpha distribution, as do SuSE, Debian, and Slackware. My advice: use the
distribution that you're most familiar with.

> >2. How compatible are 7300's? I'd especially like to hear from someone who's
> >already installed Linux on a 7300 (4100 with DEC marketroid braindamage, as
> >Paul from COLA put it).
>
> Many people have successfully installed on that platform.

This is very true. The Rawhide family is supported by the kernel and has
been for some time. BTW, I like the "marketroid braindamage" snippet
above:-P

> An Alpha running Linux is as capable as an x86 in the typical server
> functions. i.e.: Samba, Sendmail, and so on. As such the Alpha platforms
> have a good history of reliability, and excellent bandwidth and
> characteristics under load.

I've seen quite a few older Alpha systems perform extremely well under
heavier web service loads (an old Miata that I know of actually withstood
the Slashdot effect quite handily). Newer Alphas obviously tend to handle
the load even better. Mail handling performance is quite
good as well, in my experience (I've used exim and qmail, but sendmail
should perform well also).

> Alphalinux.org is the traditional main point, having taken over from the
> predecessor aztarnet site, which still exists, but is no longer
> significantly updated.
> For example:
> ftp://ftp.azstarnet.com/pub/linux/axp/docs/FAQ.html#toc5
>
> There are a few other good points, and they are more than adequately
> referenced at :
> http://www.alphalinux.org/otherpages/

The above is definitely the best sites to hit. If you want to know more
about what other Alpha systems exist (for the curious), you can also hit:
  http://www.debian.org/ports/alpha/sys_types

That link lists every Alpha systype that I could find and whether or not
it's supported by Linux.
