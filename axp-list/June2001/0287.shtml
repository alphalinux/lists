With regards to your message at 10:58 AM 6/25/01, Mark Arrasmith. Where you
stated:
>I just want to understand some of this Compaq/Intel crap.
>
>1) Compaq is selling all of their Alpha tech to Intel and then promising to
>use Intel's future 64bit designs (whatever that may be) for Compaq hardware.
>
>My problems with this are:
>Is the govt going to allow this?

Has the government actually STOPPED anything? Microsoft case seems to have
ground to a halt, and they are already convicted. Now the appeals have
follow-up just "melted away"

>What happens to purely Compaq products? (SRM, OpenVMS, True64, Compaq's
>compilers, etc)

Compaq claims that they will port those to the merged IA64/Alpha tech.

>How freaking stupid is Compaq's management? Intel can't even get a first
>generation ia-64 product out the door. Does anyone really think they will
>have a next generation processor available by 2004. Talk about being sold on
>marketing.

Can't afford to compete as a box-pusher against Dell. And they have to cut
costs to make bottom line in short term look nicer to shareholders. Poor
planning, but shareholder driven.

>2) How much of future Alpha design is inside of API now?

Really none. API makes some boards that they designed/contracted out.
Chipsets are all Compaq/DEC products. CPUs are all Samsung. Single CPU
board designs are all Samsung. Fab is all foundry/contracted stuff.

>API seems to have been forced to be an all Linux hardware company with this
>announcement. Which makes their work for gcc 3.0 improvements very
>important. Personally I'm only buying Linux stuff, so Compaq leaving isn't
>that big of a deal. UNLESS this really going to affect future design
>improvements by API.

Chipsets are the issue.

Current (EV68)CPUs:
Samsung and IBM both make Alpha CPUs, as long as they have a customer base
to sell to.
Right now the Samsung customers are Compaq (diminishing) and API, and other
OEMs.
IBM customer is Compaq, which will shrink as Intel eventually takes over.
This will be quite a while to reach, as I see it..

Future(EV7 and on) CPUs:
As I understand it the agreement with Compaq due to the terms of licensing
grants Samsung the access to technology at least through EV7 designs.
Question is if they are motivated to produce these in the future. Compaq
has started buying CPUs from IBM, and that cuts away their biggest customer.

Board chipsets for bridges, memory controllers, etc.
There have been promises of new chipsets floating about for quite a while,
but little has come of it except in the larger multi (over 4) CPU designs.
Samsung has been forced to use chipsets from AMD to make the new DDR UP1500
board.
Compaq's new designs are now basically 2 years late already. We are still
using the same chipset for dual boards for the last 3 years.

API have announced they are doing something with Lightning Data Transport
chips, and AMD have announced that this is the path they are on now, and
have abandoned the successors to Tsunami, probably mostly because they are
still not available (see above comment).
How much is this API technology and how much Samsung remains to be seen. It
is rumoured that Samsung have been grooming API to go IPO this year..
Adding some small "crown jewels" adds to the viability of this move. API
have not yet announced any customers or board designs utilizing this yet,
but perhaps we will see some announcements in the future?

The feeling we are getting from Samsung is that they are hoping to find OEM
customers to manufacture the UP1x00 boards for their own use, and Samsung
simply wants to keep manufacturing CPUs and simplifying their work. I think
they are taking a long hard look at whether or not there is really a market
for them.

Bottom line is that there is still a healthy Alpha market, even with the
delays we have seen. I hope that one of the players is sufficiently well
enough funded to continue to make headway.

Time will tell..
