On Fri, Jun 15, 2001 at 10:58:49PM +0200, Sebastian Moeller wrote:
>
> I used to run XFree86 3.3.5 on my trusty old jensen. Later I upgraded
> everything to 3.3.6 except the server binary for my S3-928 elsa winner
> 1000, as the 3.3.5 binaries just locked the graphics card for me.

IIRC, the 928 cards were the ONLY cards ever supported on JENSEN, and
that was done with a bunch of patches (part of the sources, but only
active on Alpha and JENSEN).

Yes, there was code for the old COMPAQ QVision EISA adapters in the
XFree86 sources, but they were orphaned at some point, subsequently
dropped from the source tree for 4.0 (IIRC). Regardless, they weren't
ever ported to Alpha (as the 928 support was), AFAIK. Too bad, too;
they were capable of multi-head operation - I once had a 6-head setup
under Digital OSF1, had to take out the Ethernet controller to get to
6 heads, as there were only 7 slots and one was needed for SCSI... ;-}

> The was some traffic on the debian alpha list about enabling
> jensen support or something in their 4.0.? port.

I think the comment was, that you have to explicitly go into the sources
for 4.x.x to turn on JENSEN support, and there's no guarantee that it'll
work even then. AFAICT, there's been no testing of that code since the
3.3 releases, and the code has just been carried forward with no attempt
(so far) to see if it will still work.
