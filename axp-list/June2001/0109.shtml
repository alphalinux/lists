My fears were justified, then: the discussion turned to flamewar.

I honestly didn't want that, I just need some help to get those 7300's
running a real OS. What I was really looking for is a distro that packs a
good selection of software suitable for an Alpha in an enterprise business
environment with timely updates (to Alpha version, not just x86). I was
also hoping someone would point out a few "gotchas" that are Alpha-centric,
or maybe even 7300-centric. Perhaps a few pointers on kernel recompilation
(I'm obsessed with running lean, mean, custom kernels on ALL of my Linux
machines)... Maybe some usefull "I remember..." stories on
compilation/installation of Apache, Samba, etc.

I like this list, though; it reminds me a lot of the Linux lists back in the
day, when you could only get Linux off an FTP server or through mail order
and not on your local supermarket's software shelf. Perhaps it's because
we're once again a fringe movement despite such a runaway
hype^H^H^H^Hsuccess of Linux lately: we're running Linux on a non-x86
platform!

Thank you all for your help and please do keep it coming.
