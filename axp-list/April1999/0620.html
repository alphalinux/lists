With regards to your message at 07:54 AM 04-23-99 -0400, Brian Rerecich.
Where you stated:
>I had similar memory problems with my LX board with the Pyxsis? chipset.
>Being that I work for Compaq's Alpha division, I went and asked about the
>issue. The problem is loading on the memory wires. The chipset doesn't
>drive the JEDEC standard and that is why when you put 2 in its ok, but with
>4 its shaky. I would say the less chips the better, but it will be fun
>trying to get 1GB of RAM in that board. Ask the manufacturer what memory
>sticks are verified to work and go buy those.
>
>-Brian
In general we have found it to be sensitive, but we have been shipping LX
and UX machines for nearly 2 years with 1GB of RAM, and recently with 1.5GB
on the UX and they work fine.
If you install Digital UNIX on an LX it is the most sensitive situation,
and there we recommend the memory from Corsair. In general this is now
needed, and we mostly use RAM from Daines (AMG), Ventura, and Virtium. They
work, they are companies which strongly support their customers, and are
Linux friendly.
