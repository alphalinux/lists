With regards to your message at 10:36 AM 04-15-99 -0500, Todd Henderson.
Where you stated:
>I was one of the ones that purchased the $250 164SX boards and now I'm
looking
>for a decent, but cheap video card. I know the Matrox Mill II were
popular, but
>I don't see them listed forsale anymore. Is one of their other cards the
same
>now, or as good?
>
>I'll use it for Linux most, maybe some NT.
>
The Matrox Millenium is end-of-life.
The replacement is the G200-PCI, which works nicely.
There is also the MArvel G100 which also has onboard TV/Video/Tuner.
Matrox promises Linux sources tidbit for drivers "by the end of May"
(quoted from a Matrox tech. supervisor).
The Elsa Gloria Synergy is a nice Permedia2 with OpenGL capability,a nd
works in Tru64 V 4.0E and later.
The Diamond FireGLPro1000 works, and is also a Permedia2 variant, but is
also end-of-life.
The whole ATI family that use Rage II, Rage IIc, and best of all Rage PRO
chips work, and are pretty nice, and cheap. In all cases use the latest
XFree servers. The XFree included on 5.2 does not work with the Permedia2,
but the updates do. This is the 3dLabs server.
A Rage IIc, such as the ATI 3D Charger4MB, is available in OEM whitebox for
under $50!

Avoid the AccellGraphics so-called Permedia2 based cards, such as the
AccelSTAR, which actually uses the Permedia2V, which , outside of a XiG
server, is non-functioning.
