"Eric Brown" <ericbr@gte.net> writes:

> 1) These motherboards do NOT use ECC memory, correct? The documentation
> for the motherboard certainly implies this.

As far as I can tell, it does not *require* ECC, as some other alpha
boards do. I am certainly using mine with non-ECC and it works fine.

> 2) Several memory vendors mention EPROM on the DIMM. Do I, as a system
> builder, really care about this? Or does the system take care of it?

I bought a pair of EPROM DIMMs for my SX and they were not recognized.
I contacted DCG, where I had originally bought the computer, and they
explained that sometimes the information in the EPROM can confuse an
alpha, so the EPROM would have to be blanked. The memory vendor said
that they could blank the EPROM, but this would void the warranty,
fortunately though they were willing to take the memory back. I ended
up buying correct memory from DCG. Remember, it takes DIMMS in pairs.

So, I would suggest getting your memory from a place who know that it
will work on the SX, such as http://www.dcginc.com/ or one of the
other alpha vendors.

> 3) Does RedHat support IDE disks > 8 GB? I'm thinking about buying a 13.5
> GB disk, as they're not that much more expensive than the 6 GB disks that
> seem to be the bottom end disks.

I do not see why it would not. The alpha is not hampered by the PC
BIOS limitations on disk size, boot partitions, and such.
