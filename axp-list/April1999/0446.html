Hi Y'll

It works. On the LX164, at least.

Here is what I did:

Read, carefully, and (eventually) followed, to the letter,
/usr/src/linux/Documentation/serial-console.txt.

Since I am using aboot, here is /etc/aboot.conf:

0:5/boot/kernel-2.2.5-lx.gz ro root=/dev/sda5 console=ttyS0,9600
1:5/boot/kernel-2.2.5-lx.gz ro root=/dev/sda5 console=ttyS0,9600 single
2:5/boot/kernel-2.2.5-lx.gz ro root=/dev/sda5 console=tty0
3:5/boot/kernel-2.2.5-lx.gz ro root=/dev/sda5 console=tty0 single

As you can see, I choose to have separate options for serial console
than VGA, rather than combine them into a single entry; this way I can
have a VGA card install that has nothing to do with the console. I
prefer, in my development environment, to have all consoles go to
serial ports. this asslows me to capture the console output of all the
systems, regardless of their VGAness.

I received some other options, for which I am thankful. while they
work, they bypass the concept behind having an independant /dev/console
that can be switched, at will from one device to another.
