On Sat, 17 Apr 1999, William wrote:

> I have looked at that page.....it helped a lot. One more question if I may,
> I have a USR currier v. everything X2 internal modem I wuld like to
> install. It has jumpers, so I can set the comm and irq, but how do I know
> what to set it at for an alpha? Its not like an X86 with com1, com2, com3
> is it?

Yes and no. Your AlphaStation 200 has two serial ports. In Windows NT they
will be shown as com1 & com2. Linux assigns things as cua0 (com1), cua1
(com2), etc. So yes they are the same as on an x86.

You will probably want to get Linux up and going on your system and see
what's free as far as I/O & IRQs go before you install the modem.

For the *most* part, drivers that are available within the kernel on x86
systems are also available (perhaps needing a little bit of tweaking in
some cases) for Alpha.
