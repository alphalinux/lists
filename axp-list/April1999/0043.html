On 01-Apr-99 Ken Hansen wrote:

[on solutions to the need to run intel/windows programs which cost less than
the "unix price" of Softwindows]

> Or one of those "spiffy" http://www.microworks.com $299 PC (K6-300, 32 Meg
> RAM, *some* HD (2+ gig) and a modem - note: NO CD-ROM, no floppy) -

$15 for a floppy drive - enough to jumpstart an FTP or NFS install. Besides,
it's good to backup your Quicken data to a floppy now and then. :)

> take that machine, throw in a tulip PCI ethernet card ($40?) and a copy of
> WABI ($79?)

$50 when I bought it, or $0 for wine. I'm not sure how good wine's gotten
recently (the past year). The reason I got WABI in the first place was that the
only program I really *needed* to run from my old install of Windows was
Quicken, and wine wasn't quite up to the task yet (no printing, and a little too
unstable for my tastes). You also need a copy of Windows 3.1 for WABI, but who
*doesn't* have one of those CDs kicking around? WABI ran Quicken 4 well, and
also runs Quicken 98.

> and you are still under $499, even after S/H for all the above!
> And you can have this solution in place by the end of the month!

Might be heretical for this list, but I remote display Quicken running on WABI
to the Sparcstation and not the Alpha. ;)

> HTH (sorry in advance for the inevitable storm of interest in the $299 PC!),

Well, you could always pick up a cheap Multia as a complementary machine.
Anyone else have a Multia in their stereo system? ;)
