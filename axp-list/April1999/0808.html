On Thu, Apr 29, 1999 at 12:34:02AM -0400, Izaak Bozof wrote:
> This is from the UDB service manual. Anyone tried o/c your 21066?

I have a 21068-A, a VX42, which would normally be a 233 MHz
processor (it's marked so on the side of the heat sink) but
clocked to 266 MHz. I received this machine by way of Brain E. W.
Wood, who informed me it's been running this way for a few years now.
Personally, I've had no problems with it--certainly none that
would point to heat as a cause. Works fine at 266 MHz, and I've
even got the original fan in it.

Being that the power supply in these boxes is not the mightiest
thing, one could probably safely replace the fan with a stronger
version if needed. I'm not using the 528 MB internal micro-IDE
drive in the machine, and I guess a fan above the installed 12V
DC, 0.18A fan would probably do well.

By the way, in case Brian is reading this post, I actually DID plug
in and boot the Alpha with the IDE drive enabled. It made the
most horrendous noise I've ever heard from a computer. It was
somewhere between car tires on a gravel road and white noise from
an un-tuned TV. It was near 50 dB. I turned the machine off and
went back to the external SCSI drive. :)
