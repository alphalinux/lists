These get hot in any case.
I have used a number with the 233 chip, and clocked to 266. In general it
is smart to ensure the enclosure has lots of airflow. A second case fan
blowing over the CPU and voltage regulator is strongly recommended.
In general overclocking by 1 step ( 166 to 200, or 233 to 266) is workable.
More is sometimes effectgive, but often not. For example none of my 233 ran
happily at 300.
