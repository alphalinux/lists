On Tue, 13 Apr 1999, Torsten Zirzlaff wrote:

> I just fetched the sources of the 2.2.5 kernel and tried in a first
> attempt to build it from the stock sources. Well after probing the
> networkcard it hangs.

First of all we should know the target platform.
My following statements apply to PC164UX (Ruffian).

>
> My question(s) is(are):
>
> i) Do I need again axp-diffs? Where I could find them?
No.

> ii) Do I need more as a stock RH5.2+errata?
My starting point was RH 5.2 with several upgrades (glibc-2.1,...).

> iii) Which MILO do I need?
I took the latest MILO image from alphalinux.org which was build agains
2.0.36 if I am right. I failed to build MILO against 2.2.5

> iv) What is save to build as a module/kmod?
I made several modules but tulip.o is the only one I am using until now. I
am not entirely happy with the tulip driver (0.90q). I have to force
100Mbit with 'options' and force full duplex with dip switch on the hub.

I am currently in the process to solve a problem with 'hwclock'
(util-linux-2.9o). At the moment there are several workarounds. Easiest is
use 'clock' from an older util-linux package and disable 'hwclock'.
knfsd isn't working until now.
