A few years ago this is tried on a lot of the 233MHz Multia's that
uses the same 21066A processor. In general 266 was no problem (My
Multia is running on 266 for more then 2 years now), 300 was considered
a risk as some people fried their processor. As the AXPpci33 offers
better cooling opportunities than a Multia it may work for you.

Don't expect to gain much with the overclocking, you get more BogoMips
but for real work the 21066A based machines are mostly memory
constraint.
