Well, how upset will you be if the machine dies?

Overclocking *can* cause damage, or it can provide
a low-cost speed-up... I've overclocked many Intel
machnes, and I had an overclocked Multia (266 Mhz)
for a while, but honestly, I don't know what the benefit
would be for you. If you nearly *double* the clock speed
(166 Mhz to 300 Mhz) it will be twice as fast, but I really
doubt the chip can be pushed that far without *heroic*
cooling measures. If you can get the CPU to run at 233
Mhz, you could get a 40% performance boost, or you
could get a 100% performance loss!

I'd suggest looking for an SX motherboard and starting
over again if you need speed.
