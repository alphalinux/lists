Hmm. I just visited www.redhat.com and checked out their catalog (since their
commerce system is back up.) I noticed several absences that trouble me.

First, there is apparently not (as of right now anyway) a "Variety Pack" for 6.0.
I suppose this isn't unreasonable considering that it was probably not a huge
volume seller, but it's a nuisance to me since I actually do have Intel, Alpha,
and SPARC boxen I want it installed on. Since the price has also gone up to
$80, this means that I'm going to have to shell out $240(!) if I want "official"
discs for all three. Compare that to the $40 for RH 5.2 Variety Pack.

I don't know whether to be annoyed by the fact that the Alpha & SPARC versions
are the same price as the Intel version despite the apparent absence of the
"Applications CD." I guess the idea is that this app CD is a "free" bonus,
though. (Does anyone know if the Alpha version comes with this Application CD?)

What's most annoying of all, though, is that there is no "Linux Core" (i.e.
just the disc, no support, no manual) for Alpha or SPARC. It almost appears
as though our Red Hat friends rolled out 6.0 for Intel, with at least the Alpha
as an afterthought.

So, does anyone have any insight into whether Red Hat will be resurrecting the
Variety Pack or adding Core versions of Alpha and SPARC for 6.0? I can't afford
$240, so if the answer is "no" I might have a problem.

(I can always just get the www.cheapbytes.com version, I suppose, or use an FTP
install, but I do like to support Red Hat, just not at these prices.)
