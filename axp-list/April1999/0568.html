Chris Price wrote:
>
> Is it possible that the amount of parity bits required in a mem config
> is not a purely linear relationship with the amount of data bits?
>

It isn't, and has been discussed here before. x72 is sufficient
for ECC. Just realize that with 8 bits, you can do simple parity
or tricky ECC since you have 256 combinations that are representable
with 8 bits, one can come up with a way of using those to do ECC
against 64 bits. The HW guys explained it sufficiently for my taste
last time. Has to do with grouping the 64 bits into smaller overlapped
sets that allow one to figure out what bit is bad, from what I gathered.
I'm sure someone on the list can be more specific.
