  I've seen problems on the UX where the firmware checks bad on
  power-up but checks good when running. The UX seems sensitive

hi kelvin,

yeah it fails no matter what.

anyway. I solved it eventually by installing the 3.0.61 firmware on
the alpha-processer website. Strange that neither 3.0.60 or .61 from
deskstation worked, but that the supposedly same firmware from
another site did work.

I now have another problem. If I put a video card into the 64-bit PCI
slot it doesn't start at all. The fans come on, and that's it, no
beeps, nothing. I've tried with a Trident TGUI9680 and a Matrox
Millenium2. It boots fine with either card in any of the 32-bit
slots - but then milo doesn't recognise cards in the 32bit slots.

I tried putting a Buslogic SCSI controller in the 64-bit slot, with
the Millenium in a 32bit slot - to see if it was a slot problem or a
VGA problem - and again the machine wouldn't boot. So it seems to be
a problem with the 64bit slot.

Also, the machine nearly never powers down when i hit the power-off
button. it's not the switch cause i tried it with the reset switch
plugged into the soft-power jumper on the board. It did work on a few
occasions though - but i can't figure out why.

Basically I'm having a lot of weird problems with this board. I've no
other experience of Samsung boards, so whether weirdness is normal or
not i don't know.

If it was a pc i'd say the motherboard is defective. Any issues with
Samsung UX's i should know about? The case is 300W, and the company i
bought the parts from sells workstations based on the UX and this
case.

thanks for your help,

Paul Jakma.
