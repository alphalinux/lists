Hi Michal,

>Is any specific reason why are you using here a development kernel,
with
>all possible troubles which may come with that, for something which
>sounds like a pretty standard "production" use?

Oops, no! The system in production is an x86/2.2.17 with ipchains.

It's just that I will get a new department hooked up to this network
which needs a more flexible fw with more connection tracking and the
like. Netfilter/iptables from 2.4 would do most of what I need out of
the box which I cannot easily provide with ipchains. To see if this is
a viable path I am now in the process of setting up a testbed with an
alpha to replace the x86 and 2.4 to replace 2.2.17.

>There is something wrong with this picture. Do a bit of a
back-envelope
>arithmetic. Tasks which you are talking about should barely
register,

Top shows itself as the only non-idle task, while "system time" is at
99%. 100 MBit/s in my arithmetic is roughly 10 MByte/s, I only get an
accumalated 3,6 MByte/s when http'ing _through_ that box in both ways
from multiple clients to multiple servers. Am I totally off with some
figures or concept here?

>You have only 100MBit/sec so it will not take long to use it all. We
>are not talking here about an Internet backbone. This is only a
speed
>of a faster standard ethernet connection. Do you really think that
most
>machines around is either only pushing packets, without doing any
useful
>work, or it has vast resources to spare?

I am afraid I can't follow you here (please be kind, it might be a
problem with my English).

To make my picture a little clearer: The machine has no other task
that pushing or filtering packets. On both sides there are Suns
(Netras + a E450 in production, Ultra5s and an Ultra10 in staging). We
produce news content in the order of a few GB of which a small part
changes every couple of minutes. Plus we now have a few hundred, but
soon >1000 VERY active internet users (newspaper/trade mag/online
production).

BTW: This IS a fast Ethernet connection to a city-wide
ring (fiber optic I think) (in Hamburg) that connects us to our
providers' backbone (>=155MBit/s) and thus to our production cluster
at the ISPs headquarter in Frankuft. We do have >=100 MBit/s all the
way there. So I guess this is what you'd call an "internet backbone".
