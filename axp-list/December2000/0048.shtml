The drivers work great, but here's the trick. Current kernels (although
2.4 is an exception) do not support the 1010. You have to install linux
(whatever flavor) from a sub 160 controller, patch and rebuild the
kernel and then shutdown. Once it has shutdown, swap the drive to the
1010 and bootup. Sounds easy and is, only if you have another
controller around to do the initial install.
The patch works well and you may get them from www.lsilogic.com. Follow
their links to the driver and you should be all set.

Hope this helps,

Kevin
