You get more density with UATA drives. Only if you consider
going over a TB are there any advantages to SCSI from
what I see. It's trivial to build a 1TB UATA100 server.
Even then, you can go with those boxes that use IDE but
talk SCSI to your box if you go external. If you only use
internal drives, a 14 bay case is about $200 with a 400w
PS and you can put 12 80GB drives in it for 960GB of space.
Could go 14 80GB drives but I leave space in case I want
a tape drive or something. I'd ask how many people out there
run many servers with over a TB of disk on them?

I see roughly 1% cpu per MB/sec. Doesn't much matter if it's SCSI
or UATA. SCSI is slightly better. From what I've seen, it's
the FS overhead that drives the cpu cost, as long as both
transfers use DMA. Use PIO and you're screwed. There are old
SCSI cards that do PIO also so it's not specific to IDE/UATA.
SCSI HW/drivers just learned quicker that DMA was the way to go.
