Well, I'm the one who should apologize for the confusion.. I had forgotten
that a single dot on a line has a special meaning in SMTP :-(

But the remarkable aspect of this error is indeed that SRM reads the
bootloader fine, but then fails when the bootloader tries to access the
disc through the SRM call-back routines. Also, when I boot from the
harddisc I have no problems mounting and reading the CD.

Sebastian's suggestion to change the jumper for default blocksize made no
difference. I also tried a jumper labeled "mode" (no difference) and one
labeled "reserved" (couldn't read anything at all)

Roger Allen suggested updating SRM to 5.6, but neither the link he gave,
nor the Compaq website, yielded anything newer than the 5.5-1 I already have.
Does anyone know if 5.6 (or better) is really available for PC164, and
where it can be found ? (And will it allow to access the BIOS of my SCSI
card ?)

Roger also provided the info that the RUN/STOP switch used to be a real
physical switch on some very old drives. That didn't help much, because
this drive doesn't have one.

I'm still hoping for other suggestions.

Groeten,
Geerten.
