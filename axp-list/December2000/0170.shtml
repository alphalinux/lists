On Thu, Dec 14, 2000 at 06:57:52PM +0100, Ingo T. Storm wrote:

> Tasks which you are talking about should barely
> register,
>
> Top shows itself as the only non-idle task, while "system time" is at
> 99%.

Process accounting in 2.4 kernels has some changes and weirdness in
it. I did not look very close yet but I think that you have to update
your tools to get a better picture or some numbers will be quite
misleading.

> 100 MBit/s in my arithmetic is roughly 10 MByte/s,

That's correct. There is some protocol overhead in this, of course.

> I only get an
> accumalated 3,6 MByte/s when http'ing _through_ that box in both ways
> from multiple clients to multiple servers.

I would start checking on linux-net mailing list but, IIRC, there are
could be NIC hardware limitations here. 100 MBit/s means that the card
will accept that network beat but not necessarily much more. Did you
try to send something through this box with a more frugal protocol than
HTTP and measure results? 'netcat' or maybe piping bigger files through
rsh?

>
> >You have only 100MBit/sec so it will not take long to use it all. We
> >are not talking here about an Internet backbone. This is only a
> >speed of a faster standard ethernet connection. Do you really think
> >that most machines around is either only pushing packets, without
> >doing any useful work, or it has vast resources to spare?
>
> I am afraid I can't follow you here (please be kind, it might be a
> problem with my English).

I mean that a 10 MBytes/sec transfer rate on a bus is not that much.
Disks are doing that, and more, all the time. You do not suggest that
your machine comes to a grinding halt every time when you are sending
something to, or a fetching from, a disk? True, your filters look at
every packet but these packets are not that small on an average and it
is hard to believe that your Avanti is underpowered for the task. If
you try transfers through that machine _without_ any filtering does this
change numbers in any substantial way?
