On Thu, Dec 14, 2000 at 12:24:48PM +0100, Ingo T. Storm wrote:
>
> as some of you might recall I tried to set up an Avanti (4/233) as a
> netfilter/iptables firewall. It's up and running with 2.4test12 now.

Is any specific reason why are you using here a development kernel, with
all possible troubles which may come with that, for something which
sounds like a pretty standard "production" use?

> Alas I realized yesterday that it will not be able to handle our bandwith.
> Even without any iptables rules and 3 active interfaces it maxes out at 3,6
> MBytes/s and 99% kernel load. We "unfortunately" have a 100MBit/s line to
> our ISP;-)

There is something wrong with this picture. Do a bit of a back-envelope
arithmetic. Tasks which you are talking about should barely register,
if at all, on your machine loads. Does writing to a disk sends loads on
your machine through a roof?

> And the traffic between our staging and our production web/db
> servers goes through this box, so I do expect the bandwidth to be really
> used...

You have only 100MBit/sec so it will not take long to use it all. We
are not talking here about an Internet backbone. This is only a speed
of a faster standard ethernet connection. Do you really think that most
machines around is either only pushing packets, without doing any useful
work, or it has vast resources to spare?

  Michal
