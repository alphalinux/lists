I run several hundred GB of SCSI. The SCSI drives
are slower than the UATA drives. (50GB 7200rpm Seagates
are good for mid-20MB/sec performance)

As has been mentioned, there are reasons to use Cheetahs.
In fact, if you are small I/O bound, the more spindles you
have the better so that multiple requests don't interfere
with each other. All HD's perform worse when you hit them
with multiple I/O requests to different zones on the disk
due to seek times. A Cheetah has the fastest average seek
time so it can do the most small I/O's in that case. It is
interesting to note that if you consider disk size, the seek
speed of the 60GB 7200rpm UATA drive mentioned in the other
post is actually quite good. It averaged around 14.5ms seek
time. The X15 Cheetah is around 4ms. However, since the Cheetah
is only 18GB, and the UATA drive is 60GB, the amount of data
covered per ms of seek is quite close, 4.14GB/ms vs 4.5GB/ms.
Basically, around 10% difference, Cheetahs of course are the
faster drives but not by much.
