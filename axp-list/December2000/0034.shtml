On Fri, Dec 01, 2000 at 01:27:56AM -0500, Greg Lindahl wrote:
> This is exactly what -taso fixes. However, -taso isn't actually called -taso
> in gnu ld, so see the manpage.

Indeed it is.

$ ld -help
...
ld: supported emulations: elf64alpha alpha
ld: emulation specific options:
elf64alpha:
  -taso Load executable in the lower 31-bit addressable
                                virtual address range.

Report bugs to bug-gnu-utils@gnu.org and and hjl@lucon.org
$

> I was under the impression that ld 2.9.1 has
> it, but the archive for axplist should have a note from Richard Henderson
> with the details. He's the one who provided both the clues initially and the
> later implementation. (I'd go look myself except I'm on an airplane...)

Yes, the initial hint came from Richard, but the implementation was mine.
(and so far it's one of my favorite hacks ;-)
First time that patch was included in H.J.Lu's linux-binutils
(don't remember exact version - binutils-2.9.5.0.34 had it for sure).
Later it went into official binutils-2.10 (thus ld from binutils shipped
in RH 7.0 certainly has -taso support).
