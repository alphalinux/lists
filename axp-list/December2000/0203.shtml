On Sat, 16 Dec 2000, Deja User wrote:
> Considering the controversy over the GCC compiler included in RH 7.0,
> is there a possibility that Red Hat will release both a cutting edge
> "developer's edition" and a tried and tested (perhaps even
> hardened?) "production server edition"?

Erm... RH actually did considerable testing on the compiler in RH7. Just
because it wasn't tested or released by the GCC steering committee doesn't
necessarily diminish its quality. Have you tried it yourself?

If you're just looking for something older, feel free to grab the egcs
distribution from previous RH releases.

If you want a "developer's edition" there's always GCC snapshots, which
have been bootstrapping fine for me lately. YMMV.

If you just want a C++ compiler with the new ABI, well we're all waiting
with bated breath... or you could try your luck with a snapshot.

I don't understand the motivation for this question, frankly.
