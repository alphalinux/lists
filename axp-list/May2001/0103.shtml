I have an IDE hard drive installed in a machine
with a UP2000 motherboard. The chipset is the Contaq
cy82c693. I am running linux 2.2.19 compiled with
SMP and with the assorted nfs patches and the latest patch from
ide-linux.org. The system has 1 GB of ram installed, 2 833 Mhz CPUs,
and a Western Digital 200BB drive (20 GB, ATA100).

I cannot get the ide performance of my drive to be any
higher than 3.36 MB/s. I have tried pretty much all
hdparm options. For example:

/sbin/hdparm -d1 -u1 -c1 -m16 -X33 /dev/hda

This give 3.36 MB/s. I cannot figure out how to do any
better. I am not looking to max out the drive which probably
would be 25-30MB/s with the right interface. If I can
get to 10 MB/s I would be happy.

1) Am I missing something?
2) Am I SOL with the 2.2.19 kernel?
3) Is the 200BB drive a problem?
4) Am I trying to do too much with the cy82c693 IDE chip?

If I am SOL, has anyone tried installing a PCI/IDE card into
a UP2000 and had success with it (even if the SRM does not
work)?

Thanks,
Craig
