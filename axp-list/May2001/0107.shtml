My experience with the xp1000::

If u are using the on-board ide chipset, then (probably) no, u are SOL.
I think that compaq mainly wanted the ide controller to use the cheap
ide CD-ROM thats avail nowadays, The chipset supports 2 controllers & 4
drives, but the motherboard has only one ide connector ( where u could
connect 2 drives to it.) Even when i configured the device in ( hardwire
it into the build), it would see it, recognize it, but still no speed
difference. The driver was experimental, though.

Just my 2 US cents
/gat
