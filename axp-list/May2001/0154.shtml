Has anyone here ever used watercooling on an alpha? Maybe some of you
adventurous types who are overclocking?

I periodically read some of the articles over at www.overclockers.com, and
it sounds like watercooling materials have become much more refined just
in the past year or two (water blocks, radiators, pumps, etc.).

While I have no plans to overclock either my as200 or my LX, the thought
of significantly reducing the noise in my computer room is very
attractive. The only problem I can see is that most (all?) Alpha cpus
have threaded posts for screwing down the heatsinks to them--those posts
would make it necessary to fabricate a custom waterblock (a waterblock is
basically a sealed heatsink which water flows through to carry heat
away, much like flowing air through the fins of a conventional heatsink).

Just curious,
Mike
