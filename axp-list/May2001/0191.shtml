> Date: Fri, 11 May 2001 11:07:09 -0700 (PDT)
>
> This is more applicable to cluster computers, but I was wondering if
> anyone has remote hardware monitoring and control capabilities for Alpha
> clusters. Something like on the IBM SP systems, where a master node can
> monitor power, temperature, and fan activity of each chassis, and turn
> it on/off from the HMON GUI. I think this kind of feature becomes a
> necessity once your cluster grows to more than 64 nodes.
>
It depends upon what you mean by Alpha Clusters.

What you are describing is the standard console on the Wildfire systems --
same as on the SP systems. This has the added advantage of working "out of
band" -- meaning that you don't need a working operating system to power
cycle the CPU.

You can monitor all of the things you are interested in via SNMP. On a good
day, you can even control them with it. (Aside from the fact that it only
runs on NT, Compaq's Insight Manager provides "agents" for Tru64 and I
believe now, Alpha Linux.)

The basic problem with Big Brother and similar things is that they are
oriented at monitoring various Processes, not Hardware. And in general they
are not oriented towards controlling anything. (Obviously, since they are
script based, you CAN make them do virtually anything -- but that takes
work.)

However, neither the Wildfire nor the SP systems are "ClusterD computers,"
from the VMS point of view.

Tru64 supports True Cluster -- which is finally (in 5.1), almost a VMS
cluster, which is the only real cluster, despite Sun's claims to having
invented clustering and Microsoft's attempts to prove that clustering can
never work. The various Linux clusters are more akin to the LSF - load
sharing facility than to either the Wildfire type box or a Tru-Cluster.
