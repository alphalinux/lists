Greetings all,

Many thanks to Michal for assistance with finding the
alternate superblock in an EXT2 partition.

What is the best method for setting a Intel Pro 100+
(82559) network card to 100mbit full duplex? I have a
switch and the autodetection is not setting the
interface to the correct settings, all I get is 10mbit
half duplex out of it, despite repeated ifdown/ifup.
The doc says its defualt is auto. SRM 5.8-1 on my LX
doesn't have an embedded driver and can't set any of
its parameters from console. The 21143a based Kingston
card went belly up and had to be replaced. It was a
lot easier to configure that controller even with the
OS halted.

Also, I'm having trouble with my QLA 1080 (U2)
controller. I just "tweaked" the NVRAM settings (from
a PC where the setup program and NVRAM dump could
actually run). While the settings were well within
limits for what the controller/devices were capable of
and was defined using the guide from Qlogic's own
site, I'm getting repeated "command timeout on
controller/target." I'm running a modularized 2.4.3
kernel, and am suspecting the Qlogic driver (1280*.o)
may not be reading the NVRAM parameters correctly. The
error is a timeout for a command on controller 1 (the
1080) to target 0, the 1040C controller (controller 0)
running the boot drive is not affected despite being
similarly tuned at an NVRAM level (however, the driver
is different). This occurs regardless of whether a
device is attached to the single internal 68 pin
connector or not. My first question is how to view the
version of this driver and if possible to either find
the source and recompile it or a more recent
precompiled version. My second question is where might
I find either of these? The link I had for the older
drivers (the 1020) are invalid.

Thanks,
