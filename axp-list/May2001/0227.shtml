On Wed, 30 May 2001, kirk uriksun wrote:

> What is the best method for setting a Intel Pro 100+
> (82559) network card to 100mbit full duplex? I have a
> switch and the autodetection is not setting the
> interface to the correct settings, all I get is 10mbit
> half duplex out of it, despite repeated ifdown/ifup.
> The doc says its defualt is auto. SRM 5.8-1 on my LX
> doesn't have an embedded driver and can't set any of
> its parameters from console. The 21143a based Kingston
> card went belly up and had to be replaced. It was a
> lot easier to configure that controller even with the
> OS halted.

IIRC, the eepro100 driver does not handle any autonegotiation, so that's
why you're having problems with that. I've run into the same situation
quite a few times with the same Intel cards (all either 82559 or 82550).
What I found was that, if the initial link to the hub/switch wasn't
established at a faster rate, it generally defaulted to half-duplex
10b-T. If you know the capabilities of the hub/switch, you could always
force the speed via driver options or boot line options.
