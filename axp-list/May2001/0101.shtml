I've got a question for the list that may or may not pertain to linux in
general. i have an axppci33 board that i want to make into an alpha-based
linux server. i am experiencing difficulties assembling it into a working
system.

i have built many intel-based systems (linux and non-linux) and am familiar
with all the peculiarities involved with them, but this board appears to be
baffling me. this board has two 'at-style' power connectors. truthfully,
i am at a loss for which one should be connected....are is it both?
connecting the at power supply to the upper connector results in the power
supply not even coming on (ie. power supply fan not spinning). connecting
the power supply to the lower connector allows the ps fan to spin, but i
get nothing in the way of a display on-screen or machine beeps and such.

the board has an alpha 166 processor, which came pre-mounted. i have four
ecc simms installed. i am using an s3-based PCI video card (recommended,
according to the howto's I've come across). the only thing is...the
motherboard doesn't appear to have any onboard cache...meaning, the slots
that appear to hold it are all empty. i don't know how relevant that is to
the matter.

while i have seen many linux/alpha howto's, could anybody point me in the
direction of a general alpha assembly howto...or depart me some knowledge
that might send me in the right direction? any help would be greatly
appreciated. thanks.
