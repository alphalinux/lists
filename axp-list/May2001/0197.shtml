>
> I want to recompile XFree 4.0.3 patched with
> gatekeeper.dec.com:/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/X11/diffs-403-01apr2150.gz.

Current "rawhide" stuff from Red Hat includes this and later Alpha
specific patches and more of other things.

> Is anybody knows where I can get the corresponding spec file to do the
> related rpm packages (for RH7.0 Axp) ?

You should find even ready binaries. It is not very hard to recompile
the whole thing but it takes quite a bit of a disk space (of an order
of one gig) and some time.

> If it works, I will post the result on alphalinux as usual.

It works, as far as I can tell. I am running it right now.
Be aware that you need first to install high enough version
of freetype and Mesa libraries.

> (But I promise nothing ;-)

People with glibc-2.1 libraries (RH 6.x releases and similar) can find
recompiled all that for Alpha in

   ftp://ftp.harddata.com/pub/XFree86-4.0.3-Alpha/

Mind you, this is a "work-in-progress" and not recommended if there
is no real need. Pay really close attention to assorted "readme"
files or you may end up with a broken system.

There is also some stuff for Alpha with glibc-2.2 there but a bit
older and you are more on your own. I have no idea, in particular,
if any of the above would work on PWS 500a. Maybe ...

Keep in mind that a connection to this server is relatively slow.
You may be better of looking for "rawhide" on various Red Hat mirrors.
