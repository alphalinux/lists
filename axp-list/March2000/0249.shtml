On Sat, 11 Mar 2000, Lukas Mailinglist Account wrote:

> Hola!
>
> Sorry to bother the list again with this thread, but I looked at
> LinuxAlpha.org and didn't get a clue!
> So, here are my two questions
>
> 1) If I change from ARC to SRM on my LX164, will my Adaptec 2940 still boot from
> SCSI or must I switch the controller?

Yes, the later version of SRM for the LX do have adaptec support. It
will work for the 2940 (I have one of these at work, with SRM V5.6),
however it will not work for the U2W cards.
 
> 2) When plug of my keyboard the system starts something I call "serial output
> mode". All the output of the system is directed to the first serial port!
> Ok, but nothing happens. The machine dosen't boot Linux.
> Is this a normal behaviour? How can I force the system to boot without a
> keyboard?

Is this from AlphaBIOS or SRM, I'm assuming because of your earlier
question that this is AlphaBIOS? If you attach a terminal to the 1st
serial port your should see the output of AlphaBIOS. Though I will warn
you that serial support in AlphaBIOS isn't the most useful thing I've ever
come across. If you're going to attempt this then I'd get the latest AB
available (5.70), and if you really want serial support, use SRM.
