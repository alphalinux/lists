>>> Richard Henderson said:
> On Fri, Mar 10, 2000 at 06:03:27PM -0800, Pete Wyckoff wrote:
> > What I've been seeing on Miata PWS 433a is random data returned to
> > anything that does an inl() across a PCI bus. At least there's no
> > difference with or without your merge patch on 2.3.50. The problem
> > first shows up when the tulip tries to find itself...
>
> I've seen other tulip weirdness with 2.3.50. Ignore it for
> the moment, as I blame the tulip driver. If you've gotten
> that far, you've already done PCI bus accesses to the video
> card if nothing else.

TULIP driver (not de4x5) under 2.3.50 seems reasonably healthy for me (but
only under very limited use as of yet), except for systems with >2GB memory,
which I have patches for (cause: incomplete porting to the PCI DMA mapping
API, ie there were still virt_to_bus() calls sprinkled around).

I think what he may be seeing is the known problem with PCI resource
allocation related to bridges and devices behind them, for which I sent
Richard some patches last Friday; MIATA has a builtin bridge which has the
TULIP behind it, IIRC, and so falls into the suspect category.
