Robert Williams wrote:
>
> Is there a known problem with the tulip ethernet driver
> for axp/PC164? (running RedHat 6.1)
> _____________________________________
>
> I've been testing two different PC164 systems
> for several days now,
> with and without PCI NetGear Tulip ethernet
> cards installed. After about six different cycles
> of removing and then reinstalling ethernet cards,
> these systems have never crashed
> (frozen) when there is no tulip card in the system,
> but they have always crashed within 30 minutes
> when tulip cards are installed. (I run a mozilla make
> job and turn on a busy screensaver.)
>
> I've tried two different tulips, both NetGear,
> one with a genuine Digital tulip chip, and the other
> with a NetGear chip. Both have frozen these systems.
> An inspection of the driver automatically configured
> at boot time using Linuxconf shows the name "old-tulip".
> A 3Com509 has also been installed and the system
> has not frozen when this card is running.
>
> Prior to one of my upgrades from RedHat 5.2,
> the first system had been running fine using NetGear
> tulip cards. After the system started freezing,
> I checked just about everything, going so far
> as to purchace three more pc164 boards+cpus.
> I'm just now getting around to actually pulling
> the ethernet card.
>

New Netgear cards use the latest tulip driver, CONFIG_DEC_ELCP,
not the CONFIG_DEC_ELCP_OLD one. Using the v0.89h one a new
Netgear card will hang your system from what I've seen. If
you want to edit your modules file, the name should be tulip,
not old_tulip. The new tulip is v0.91g.
