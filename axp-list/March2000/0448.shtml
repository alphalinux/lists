On Sat, 18 Mar 2000, J. Pedro Sousa do Amaral wrote:

> Hi,
> I would like to be able to use a terminal emulation program (ZTerm or
> Terminal) on one of my Macintoshes to display boot messages (and others)
> from the serial port on my AS 200. Is it possible? What kind of settings
> would I need at the terminal emulation program? Would 9600 baud, N, 8, 1 do?

Yup, that looks right.

> Do I need to configure protocol (Kermit, Xmodem, etc.) and handshake type

No

> (Xon Xoff, etc.) too?

I think I've always used hardware flow control, but this is on newer
machines. I'd just play around with it and see what happens.

Now, are you running SRM or ARC Bios on this machine? If you want to do
the above, use SRM. Then you can change the console parameter and
everything should go to the 1st serial port instead of the VGA (set
console serial).

Also, remember you'll need serial support in the kernel, you'll have to
tell the kernel to use it (append console=ttyS0 to your boot flags), and
if you want to login over the serial port you'll have to spawn a getty on
the tty. Look at /etc/inittab for that one.
