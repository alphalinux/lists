"Andrei A. Dergatchev" wrote:
>
> I'm curious what's in your opinion is "what most folks who work
> on Alphas care about" ?

There are basically two classes of users I see on this
list. Those that want the best performing CPU and those
that want to play around with Alpha systems to learn
about Linux/64 bit-ness or just to be non-x86. Then
there are the Compaq guys. Not sure how I'd categorize them.
Some of both I guess.

Why else bother with Alphas. x86 would be better cost
wise. (I know there are a few exceptions but for most
non-FP code, it's true)

> Just from the viewpoint of your apparently
> large experience ?
>

I've used Alphas in my business for 3+ years. Also been
active on this list over that same time frame. I started on
RH4.2 to give you some idea. As far as Alpha Linux is concerned,
I have about 16 GFLOPS peak of Alpha processing.

That's not including the half dozen or so x86 Linux
systems I use for various other uses, along with a set
of RS/6000 Power3's, about to become Power3+.

One of the minor tasks I'm using Alphas for at the moment is
to read in and process around a TB of data off of 180 or so
tapes.

Which reminds me, I just installed a 53c825d HVD SCSI card
in a UP2K yesterday and it's working like a champ in case
anyone is thinking of using high end tape devices. I'm
currently using an IBM 3590B11 tape drive for that TB of
data above.
