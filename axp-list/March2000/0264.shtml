The good folks at Red Hat support services
(I have a boxed set) did not appear to be aware
of this problem. I was calling them on a regular
basis until my support expired. They thought I
had a bad motherboard/cpu, and so did I...
I bought a "new" set.

There is no update from RedHat to fix this problem.
This seems like a fairly important problem to solve,
since it makes a PC164 less stable than an NT box.

W Bauske wrote:

> New Netgear cards use the latest tulip driver, CONFIG_DEC_ELCP,
> not the CONFIG_DEC_ELCP_OLD one. Using the v0.89h one a new
> Netgear card will hang your system from what I've seen.

Are you saying that older Netgear cards (the ones with Digital chips)
cannot use the new tulip driver?
My systems were freezing with both old and new Netgear cards.
>From what I have seen, the boxed set RedHat Kernel, 2.2.13,
uses only the old_tulip (v0.89h) driver.

Michal Jaegermann wrote:

> Robert Williams wrote:
> >
> > Is there a known problem with the tulip ethernet driver
> > for axp/PC164? (running RedHat 6.1)
>
> There is now identified a long standing problem with a tulip driver (and
> other, like eepro100, for example) on all platforms. A circular list of
> skbufs may get empty why it should not be and this results in an attempt
> by your kernel to dereference the address 0 which translates to crash.
> For some reasons you are seem more likely to see that condition with
> current 2.2 kernels but you need a pretty heavy net traffic for that.

These systems freeze up with no network traffic, no wire connected.
All you need is the tulip card installed.

> Tulip fixes were included in 2.2.14; eepro100 will show up in 2.2.15.

I see that 2.2.13 includes a new tulip driver (tulip.c:v0.91g-ppc 7/16/99),

the same one included in 2.2.14.
Where are the Tulip fixes included? In the hardware detection?
