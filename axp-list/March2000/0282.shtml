> If it is really true then the only logical conclusion I can see
> is that Q wants their Alpha business to die.

To the best of my knowledge those figures are correct.

Compaq's Alpha business will not die, certainly not in my opinion.
Early last year I heard rumblings from a couple engineers at Compaq
that the big Q intended to migrate their Alpha business
exclusively to high end supercomputer-class systems - relying
on API to cover the hobbyist/small business market.
Keep in mind that Compaq relies on some very large customers
of Alpha hardware to keep the whole unit running; I do not
see those customers going away any time soon.

SGI unfortunately tried the same numbers game (inflating the low
 end to retrieve 5-8X initial cost in profit) and ended up
becoming a glorified x86 shop while easing out their RISC
workstation offerings. Oh, they still have an upper echelon
scalable server class based on R10k/R12k processors;
at the hobbyist level though, the only shipping products
with much of a future are x86.
