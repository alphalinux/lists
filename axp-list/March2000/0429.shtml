No. That's what the HAE register is all about.

EV4 has a 34-bit address bus. APECS and LCA have 4G of sparse
space mapping 128M. It appears as if T2 has its 4G sparse space
broken up into multiple hunks, the largest of which is 2G mapping
only 64M. But that's from reading code not docs.

EV5 has a 40-bit address bus. CIA and MCPCIA have 22G of sparse
space in three hunks. The only one we use is 16G and maps 512M.
