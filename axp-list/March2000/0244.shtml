> ----- Original Message -----
> From: W Bauske <wsb@paralleldata.com>
>
> > Getting 3D acceleration going, while useful, is not,
> > apparently, what most folks who work on Alphas care
> > about. I know I don't have any use for it and I have
> > quite a few Alphas.
>
> Developing 3D programs in a non-accelerated environment is a drag. I'd like
> to make visualation software that works on all platforms, and I presently
> only have my Alpha in a usable state. With Alphas being a great platform
> for number crunching, having an efficient display framework to visualize and
> even manipulate those numbers is essential to that end. For those that only

I agree it would be nice to have 3D acceleration. But,
it seems the people who want it aren't able to get it
working and the people who can probably get it working
have other things to do of higher priority. So, the
people who want it are stuck until such time as priorities
change or new programmers get involved who are interested
in getting it working. We'll see which if either occurs.

Of course, if you're a business, you could consider donating
equipment/money to the right people to influence their
priorities.
