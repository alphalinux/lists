There are some big misconceptions here. First, a DPWS can't be upgraded to
"anywhere near the performance" of an XP1000 no matter what speed processor
you put in it. Second, your cost figures are the figment of someone's very
active imagination. This is a popular game even within computer
manufacturers. You get two groups competing, with one saying "Oh, we could
build it for 1/2 that cost." If you actually do the homework and total up
the actual costs, reality starts to take hold.

So, how about providing us the backup data for your cost figures? (Just to
be even handed, I bet when you do the cost figures, you'll find your
warranty allowance is too high assuming that's what you mean by "support
contract").

Tom

> Here's another thought. Guess how much it costs Compaq to put together

> The cost to consumer is not twice, not thrice, but at least 5 times
