Actually, I e-mailed Daryll about this over 2 years ago and he said
that Alpha was one of the things on his list that he hoped to get to
soon. This was well before glide was open sourced. I'm not trying
to point a finger at people like Daryll, who at the time was doing
this in his free time. From what I understand, glide has been found
to be particularly troublesome to port to Alpha.

On the GLX front, I have found hardly any info as to what is actually
supported under Alpha. Most of my beef is with hardware vendors and
their x86 centric support. I can understand why this is so from the
economics view of these companies. What really bugs me is that much
of the design philosophy behind recent hardware makes getting it to
work on an Alpha particularly difficult. How many of the recent
consumer 3D cards work under Alpha/Linux? Hell, Nvidias newest
cards won't work at all due to the bios translation on Alpha not
supporting them.

I have tried being vocal on several mailing lists and boards to try
and rally support but at most I usually get a couple of followups
which say 'yah, that would be nice'.

And which source are you refering to Dan? Glide is now open sourced
but the response I received from someone at 3dfx (I believe it was on
the linux-glide newsgroup but I don't remember who it was) when I
inquired about whether they intended to port glide to Alpha/Linux was
"It is being worked on by the open souce community but they are having
a lot of problems".

Compaq and API are not helping matters either. It is getting terribly
difficult to find a decent video card which still comes in pci format.
API now offers 1 Alpha motherboard with an agp slot - the UP1000. Have
you seen the prices on these things? Regardless of their cries of
'commodity Alpha', Alpha will continue to be a niche market under this
type of marketing. I have been touting the advantages of Alpha since
I first worked on a DEC300-600 in graduate school, and have owned more
than a few in the last 4-5 years, but unless I am running Monte Carlo
simulations or Data analysis a significant fraction of the time, it is
becoming less advantagous to get an Alpha over a much cheaper x86 box
for my personal use.

-Eric Christy
