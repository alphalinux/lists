> > <non-threatening-discussion-provoking-question>
> > If Alpha Linux is more finicky/troublesome than Linux Intel why are some of
> > you using it?

Depends on what you consider difficult to maintain. Coming into
the Alpha world from experience with traditional Un*x and
using early Linux, AlphaLinux was not bad at all.
That may come across conceited, but I don't want it to..

For someone coming straight from a Win/Mac background,
AlphaLinux must seem like a Grand Challenge problem.
I came out of the Ultrix/SunOS/BSD days, coding
all sorts of convoluted low-level stuff, and tinkering with
things like RSX-11. The Alpha had some quirks which
did not want to go away (not being able to use a PCI ethernet
 card in the 64-bit slots of my DPWS for example), but
I adapted.

If you want a quick and dirty 'why I use AlphaLinux', here it is.
I need to remind you that these are all imho:

1> "Its the economy, stupid". I grew up with a strong sense of
   making the most out of any investment. If my Alpha doesn't
   do personal requirement foo, I do my best to code foo or
   work with those people who are. Giving up on the platform and
   selling all my Alphas does not compute.
2> All operating systems suck, and the shortcomings of
   AlphaLinux are compensated in other ways. I'm sitting
   here listening to mp3's, processing a rather large data file,
   and throwing 2GB tarfiles between my two systems. And
   they are still responsive. I imagine most x86 boxes
   would be sucking floor tile trying to do the same.
3> Its the mystique. DEC made an early impression on me with
   vaxes and pdp-11's, what can I say.

In summary, don't take all of this too seriously ;-)
Ultimately the decision of what platform to buy and use
at home comes down to you.
