Strange thing is the CD drive had the terminator jumped on, and it
was working fine with the hard disks for the first 5 days or so. Then
after a reboot (I wanted to screw around with SRM) it got what appeared to
be a dump of a hard disk sector(s) then later a corrupted /boot/vmlinux,
after 2 or 3 reinstalls I removed the CD drive.

With the "new" CD drive in place it works fine. On the PC I've put the
original drive out of the Alpha I can `cat /dev/scd1 > /dev/null` or
mount it and does as it should do. The only other SCSI device in the PC
is a 2x CD drive, so it would be that something in the electronics on
the ex-Alpha CD drive has gone silly and is interferring with the SCSI
bus.
