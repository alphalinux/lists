"Hoover, Tony" wrote:
>
> If AlphaBIOS/MILO is working for you, use it. Just because RedHat no longer
> "supports" it doesn't mean that it no longer works. However if you are
> wanting to switch to SRM/aboot, you will have to get rid of the Adaptec.

Not entirely true on this. I have a number of alphas including AS200
4/233, alphaPC164's and LX's. All of which I use adaptec controllers
and various other non-srm supported pci cards with srm. True, srm
doesn't see these cards, but linux does. Boot from a floppy using
aboot/srm. Sounds old fashion but it works and for servers that are up
all the time, waiting for the thing to boot doesn't sound all that
painful when you think about it. If your worried about the floppy going
bad keep a couple of raw images around that you can quickly dd to a new
disk.

Just my two cents :)
