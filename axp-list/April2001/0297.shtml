With regards to your message at 01:08 PM 4/11/01, jeff.sturm@appnet.com.
Where you stated:
>On Wed, 11 Apr 2001, Maurice Hilarius wrote:
> > What I can point out, however, is the fact that the UP1100 ( and UP1000)
> > are basically 2.5 to 3 times the cost of the old LX and UX machines. And
> > for that we get a platform that is quite nice for many customers, but
> > others are disappointed in the memory performance or capacity.
>
>Maurice, I'm not following you here. An LX cost around $2500 to build
>at one time. A UP1100 should be only slightly more. Sure any remaining
>LX's are reduced now, but then so will be the UP1100 if any are left in
>1-2 years.
We used to build and sell a basic LX with RAM, HD, CDROM, floppy, video for
$1700
The LX was a 533 with 2MB cache and 1GB RAM capacity, and the UX version
with 1.5GB capacity.
On the LX I had SRM, UNIX options.
And on the LX I had 64bit PCI
This was in 1998.

Today in 2001 the similar config in a UP1100 costs $2600
The UP1100 is a 600MHz with 768MB RAM capacity.
On the UP1100 I have 32 bit PCI
On the UP1100 I have Linux only.

In three years we have gained what? 66MHz and $900 cost increase..
Taking into account 21264 versus 21164A I am sure the new one is faster.
I hope so.. although from a memory bandwidth point of view it is not any
faster.
