> We used to build and sell a basic LX with RAM, HD, CDROM, floppy, video for
> $1700
> The LX was a 533 with 2MB cache and 1GB RAM capacity, and the UX version
> with 1.5GB capacity.
> On the LX I had SRM, UNIX options.
> And on the LX I had 64bit PCI
> This was in 1998.

When I built my LX in 98 it cost me almost $2700 for a 533MHz CPU 2M L3,
128MB, 4GB SCSI disk, 2940UW, permedia2, DE500 nic, and a SB64 ISA.
Anyone
using IDE was in for abysmal disk performance considering the state of
the
linux kernel at that time.

 
> Today in 2001 the similar config in a UP1100 costs $2600
> The UP1100 is a 600MHz with 768MB RAM capacity.
> On the UP1100 I have 32 bit PCI
> On the UP1100 I have Linux only.

and back when the LX's where out in 98 how many 64 bit PCI cards
where 1) within the reach of most consumers 2) practical? Nevermind
the price of ram. Nowadays 512MB ECC can be had for less than $500.
How many people need more than that for workstation applications?
 
> In three years we have gained what? 66MHz and $900 cost increase..
> Taking into account 21264 versus 21164A I am sure the new one is faster.
> I hope so.. although from a memory bandwidth point of view it is not any
> faster.

FP is twice as fast aslong as its in cache. Disk access from PCI
adapters seem faster
(I have almost Identical SCSI setups between my LX and UP1000 at work
and
the 1000 is twice as fast). Now you want to play the MHz game? For what
the 1000 lacked in memory bandwidth the gap could be closed with a
larger
cache. For example one of my 600/4 LX's was outperforming the UP1100 (2M
cache)
by some 40 mins using seti@home!
