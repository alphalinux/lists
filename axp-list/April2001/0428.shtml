I started having problems with what appeared to be disk corruption,
when booting Linux I got random errors ranging from unable to mount
root if the kernel managed to load, aboot complaining that the kernel
image was corrupt and VFS error during booting. I reinstalled Red Hat
6.2 and all went fine, then after booting aboot complained about
reading past the EOF or something of that nature. I'd just purchased
the AS 1000a a week ago for $NZ750 ($1NZ=$US0.45 or about there) and it
had been working fine since. Rather than get pissed off the thought "the
CD drive is poked" came to me in a flash. Replaced the CD drive with a
slower 2x SCSI, reinstalled and hay presto the machine is purring along
very nicely. Rebooted the machine a few times after it had been running
for a couple of hours several times and it is still happy.

So I just put the CD drive I suspected of causing the problem into my
PC (running Linux) and I can't find any errors.

Question: what the hell is going on ?
