On Tue, 10 Apr 2001 KirkE@paccessglobal.com wrote:
> >- EV6 is way too expensive.
>
> You've got that right. Like you, I'm waiting to see the results of the IBM
> fabbing of the Alpha. They are a good source for silicon, Sun Ecache debacle
> aside.

I'm curious what exactly makes EV6 expensive, besides that all
configurations available have 2MB cache or more. If somebody were to
offer a low-cost 1MB version, it would fill a need but also diminish much
of the reason for buying an alpha in the first place: performance. (Don't
flame me because I don't think OpenVMS is a reason!)

> Who would fab the 21164A/21164PCx parts? Mitsubishi fabbed 21164PC for a
> time, they could pick it up again if they could make some profit off of it.

I have a weird hunch that everbody involved with the 21164PC lost money on
it. Some are gone. Why would they try again?

I realize you might buy one. Some might I. I'm going to get an EV6 (if I
ever sell my house). But that doesn't help much unless you can somehow
convince half the Sun owners they need an Alpha.

I'd personally love to see an appliance. We bought a number of the little
Cobalt servers. Every one MIPS-based, every one still in
service. They're great. I was disappointed when they abandoned
MIPS (QED, actually) even though the K6 was a much better performer. I
like diversity in an increasingly all-x86 world. It gave me a platform to
show people, "Look, you do a 'configure; make; make install' and voila! No
need to wait for a MIPS port". You see, I love free software, which
thrives on heterogeneity.

Now they are owned by Sun, and, well... let's say it's an x86 or SPARC
world out there. Maybe PPC too. Possibly IA-64. (Or probably not. It'd
be humiliating though if crap like the Itanic overtakes alpha in volume,
but that's probably unavoidable.)

> Given the lower production numbers, I'd be happy to see one of these in the
> $1,500 to $1,900 range. A .18 micron 21164A with an 8MB L3 should do nicely
> at 1GHz. : )

Can you touch anything with 8MB cache for $2k, let alone Alpha? I don't
know, but I'm skeptical.

It's frustrating that the vendors are so silent on these issues, not that
I expect them to divulge business plans. But it's probably safe to say
that all the players would agree this is fine and dandy as long as
somebody's paying for it.

Digital's efforts at marketing low-cost Alpha were a dismal failure in
spite of their technical merits. Say what you want about their marketing,
but this whole thread has been focused on the technology, hmm... little
bit of deja vu.

Meanwhile, you can still get a UP1100 mainboard w/ CPU for $2k, but how
many people on this thread have bought one? Talk is cheap.

So many have lost so much on Alpha I wouldn't expect any takers.
