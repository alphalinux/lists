Kurt,

>if you go to sunstore and look at prices:

Buying from Sun gets you what you get when buying direct from Compaq (where
possible): heavy duty prices and some assurance that the equipment won't be
too damaged when you receive it.

ultra60 configuration
(w/ 512MB RAM and 2x 450MHz/4MB CPUs) you're talking $16k

Authorized resellers are strongly pushed to have a particular price,
unauthorized resellers are not. If you want to have support on your gear
though, it will have to be authorized and approved by a Sun tech. All
measures of trying the keep the quality high (with associated margin
increases). PC Servers are often not sold this way, many people can't
justify the added expense and feel confident their onsite guys can support
it as well/better than a support rep technician when the chips are down.
Having the ability to buy an off-the-shelf replacement component from any of
several dozen (if not hundred) local companies or large warehouses gives
some people a greater sense of comfort than depending on the original vendor
for similar levels of support.

>if you look at an ultra5, it's around $2800

Can be had for much less elsewhere, and Sun Authorized as well. Average
cost is about $1700.00

>if you look at an ultra10, it's around $4500

Average cost from other authorized vendors is $3000, from "unauthorized"
resellers, its more like $1600.00 (great for testing, if you don't need
direct bronze support on the box).

>sun blade 1000's w/ 2 CPUs are like $20k

Similar to the original cost of the Ultra 60 and quite close to the
DS20/DS20E. Sun Blade 1000, btw, is Ultra III 600MHz or faster, not the old
Ultra II platforms.

>and sun blade 100's (the celeron equivs... due to compilers / chipset /
latency / whatever) are around $3k for a decent
>config.

While it may be possible to build a $3000 Sun Blade 100, average cost for
256MB versions is less than $1800. Average cost from some (including
promotions) is only slightly higher than the Netra X1 (which shares much of
the same architecture) at or below $1,000.
