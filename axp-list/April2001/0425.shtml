Regarding my spinlock lockup... I posted it to the Linux nfs mailing
list and got a quick bugfix.

> spin_lock_irqsave(&xprt_sock_lock, oldflags);
> xprt->snd_task = NULL;
> if (!rpc_wake_up_next(&xprt->sending) && xprt->stream)
> xprt_add_tcp_timer(xprt, RPCXPRT_TIMEOUT);
> spin_unlock_irqrestore(&xprt_sock_lock, oldflags);

Trond immediately spotted a thinko in this code: xprt_add_tcp_timer()
sometimes does something that has to wait for the BH, and it shouldn't
be holding the spinlock while waiting. Fortunately it doesn't need
the spinlock.

He was surprised it hadn't shown up before, as am I; other people are
running 2.2.19 on 2 cpu UP2000s using NFS v3 over tcp, right?
