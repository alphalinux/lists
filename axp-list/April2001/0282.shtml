After all 2,197,317 messages clear everyone's queues, life should return
to normal.

For when an autoresponder goes whacko, I have a simple list of 'dos and
don'ts'

DO:

email the administrative points of contact for the mailing list, the host
for the mailing list, and the host from which the spew is coming.

For example, I sent mail to axp-list-owner and root@redhat.com this
moring, as well as root@usa.net and @offending-host, briefly explaining
the problem, and asking for help.

Eventually (in this case, very quickly) someone will respond, and take
steps to address the problem.

DO NOT:

Flame the offending party on the list, or send email back to the list,
telling them how to unsubscribe|change their address|commit illegal sexual
acts with power tools.

Reply to the list during a 'spewage'.

All these actions do is to increase the amount of messages that the
broken autoresponder thinks it has to reply to -- and each different
message will increase the traffic by roughly a power of 2.

So, calm down, dig up some administrative PoCs, email them offlist, and
wait it out. If its in danger of swamping your mailserver, you can notify
your administrators as well, and hope that they can take appropriate
actions.
