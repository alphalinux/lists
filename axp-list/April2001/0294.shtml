On Wed, 11 Apr 2001, Maurice Hilarius wrote:
> What I can point out, however, is the fact that the UP1100 ( and UP1000)
> are basically 2.5 to 3 times the cost of the old LX and UX machines. And
> for that we get a platform that is quite nice for many customers, but
> others are disappointed in the memory performance or capacity.

Maurice, I'm not following you here. An LX cost around $2500 to build
at one time. A UP1100 should be only slightly more. Sure any remaining
LX's are reduced now, but then so will be the UP1100 if any are left in
1-2 years.

> But realistically the cost of the UP1x00 strikes me as a bit high.
> Follow the curve of Alpha vs x86 and SPARC over the last 3 years and you
> see the former advantages in clockspeed and price disappearing fast. Time
> was the Alpha single CPU platforms we build enjoyed advantages in
> clockspeed, memory bandwidth, memory capacity, and floating point
> performance. At a cost of around $2K to $2500 for a basic workstation.
> Now we have the floating point advantage.

Thanks to AMD and Athlon, x86 had a major price/performance war. Like all
wars, the outcome might be good but you don't want to get too close ;)

Judging from what Mike Foley and others said, it doesn't seem low cost was
a strong consideration in the EV6, or it would probably have had on-die
cache. As with any other engineering decision there are tradeoffs. Good
decision or bad? You tell me.

But getting down to the matter, I am in a postition to recommend Alpha for
our hosted solutions if it makes business sense. I haven't seriously
considered it yet. We buy numerous Sun AXi rack-mount hardware in the
range of $3-4k each, all running Solaris. Price/performance of these is
acceptable, not great. Reliability is essential. To consider replacing
them we'd need solid Java and Oracle support. Perhaps my mistake is only
looking at Linux, not Tru64. The entry point for Tru64 would be a DS10, I
suppose? Could it possibly be price-competitive with the Suns? For Alpha
OEM I have to look to API, which is Linux only.
