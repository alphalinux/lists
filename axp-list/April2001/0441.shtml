On Wed, Apr 18, 2001 at 06:28:51PM -0700, KirkE@paccessglobal.com wrote:
> Greetings all,
>
> As many of us are aware, the "Tulip" based network controllers are becoming
> a rarity, but for boot level support from SRM and across all of the support
> operating systems, they are still the way to go.
>
> Compiled here are a list of controllers with Tulip chipsets. I don't have
> the full list, so if anyone knows others, add those you have discovered. All
> of these should have full support for the generic "Tulip" driver, most will
> also support the DEC 21x4x drivers directly. Looks like Tulip generic
> drivers are the way to go following this lists threads.
>
> Note that these products are all discontinued and my prices may be a little
> high for what some are available for on auction sites.
>
> Accton EN2220 (10mbit) Cheetah EN1207D-TX EN1207D-TC/WOL (10/100 mbit)
> $35.00

The Accton product no. of 21143 chips should be EN1207C-TX, not 1207D series.
The 1207D series uses MXIC chipsets.

>
> D-Link DFE-???
>

D-Link DFE-500(10M/100M), Already discontinued.

Besides, LEMEL's P600-TX (10/100M), about U$30, also uses 21143.
I only see this in Taiwan's stores.
But this card is somewhat strange. It works well with Linux.
Yet it gets error messages under Tru64 Unix 4.0f with tu0 driver

vmunix: tu0: DECchip 21143: Revision: 4.1
vmunix: tu0: auto negotiation capable device
vmunix: tu0: ethernet address checksum error
vmunix: - Failed to configure device in PCI bus 0, slot 8 with driver tu

I suspect this is due to its hardware address starting with 00-00-00.
But unproved.
