>To build that up to a complete system you're looking at $2800-$3000

Sorry, but in making some of the comments quoted in this thread I am not
being a hypocrite.
As a matter of fact I am told we bought and sold more of the UP1000 and
UP1100 than any other reseller in N. America. We are stills selling them.
What I can point out, however, is the fact that the UP1100 ( and UP1000)
are basically 2.5 to 3 times the cost of the old LX and UX machines. And
for that we get a platform that is quite nice for many customers, but
others are disappointed in the memory performance or capacity.
It really depends on what you want to do with it. For some applications it
is quite a nice machine, but for others not the best ticket.

>If you "really" wanted one you would have bought one already! Hell, I
>went
>broke buying my first LX in pieces 3 years ago when I was just starting
>college
>instead of buying a car.
As a matter of fact we own 2 x UP1100 and use them in-house for software
testing and development. When Michal posts to this list about work he is
involved in on Alpha it is a UP1100 he is working on.

> > What I am seeing our customers ask for this:
> > "Give us a machine like the LX was in it's time. Reasonably priced, fast,
> > and flexible."
>
>It's more like "I want everything for nothing and no comprimises
>please".
Naturally!
But realistically the cost of the UP1x00 strikes me as a bit high.
Follow the curve of Alpha vs x86 and SPARC over the last 3 years and you
see the former advantages in clockspeed and price disappearing fast. Time
was the Alpha single CPU platforms we build enjoyed advantages in
clockspeed, memory bandwidth, memory capacity, and floating point
performance. At a cost of around $2K to $2500 for a basic workstation.
Now we have the floating point advantage.

> > We should see that with the UP1500. Pity about the lack of UNIX but then
> > this is becoming more irrelevant every day.
>
>Exactly. Folks who want to run Tru64 are already using Tru64. Most
>users
>couldnt care about Tru64.
Yes, but many people making buying decisions still ant the compatibility as
"insurance" in case what they are doing on Linux does not pan out. If fact
that is rare now, and we do have Compaq to thanks for that in making their
compilers and libraries available under Linux Alpha.
