On Wed, 10 Nov 1999, Ryan D. Schaefer wrote:

> On my UDB, I had a similar problem. It'd power on, but not bring up a
> console or even initialize the TGA adaptor. Of course.. my Multia had
> been working for a couple years previous to this.. so.. I knew the
> machine was at least functional. I found that the power connector on
> the mainboard wasn't making good contact with the power supply
> connector (wiggling the cables around a bit caused the machine to
> spring to life for varying amounts of time). I've yet to fix this (I'm
> currently working on another project) but I imagine that either
> cleaning those contacts real well or replacing them altogether would
> probably solve my problem. I imagine it might solve yours as well.

My UDB ran for a couple months before it started doing the same thing...
I went through two or three batteries before I realized what the problem
really was... At that point I sliced five of the cables, removed the
contact, and spliced in another. I haven't had one problem since :-)

>
> Alternately, you may want to try and plug some different RAM into the
> machine. I know that's an expensive proposition, however, these
> machines, in my experience, have been VERY picky regarding what kind
> of RAM you put in it, and in what quantity you use. I believe that if
> the machine isn't happy with the RAM it would also react in the way
> you described.
>

Do all Alpha's require true parity SIMMS? If not, and if the RAM he had
installed came from a machine that didn't need them, that could be the
problem as well.
