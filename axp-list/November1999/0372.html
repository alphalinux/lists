On 10-Nov-99 B. James Phillippe wrote:

> The RAM came from my AS200; I had 64MB in quantity of 4 8's and 2 16's. I
> bought 2 32's and swapped the 4 8's for the 2 32's in my AS200. My AS200
> is now working happily with 96MB's of true parity x36 memory and I have 4
> 8's left over. I put the 4 8's in the UDB. Again, the memory came from a
> working Alpha machine; I highly doubt the memory is the problem.

You may be right that memory might not be the problem, but just because the
SIMMs in question work in the AS200 doesn't mean that they will necessarily
work in the Multia. CAse in point - My AS200 came with 2 8 meg SIMMs. Though
these SIMMs work perfectly in my AS200, they simply will not work at all in my
Multia. Other SIMMs work fine in the Multia, and the SIMMs in question work
fine in my AS200 and the 486 they're living in now.

Just another thing to check ...
