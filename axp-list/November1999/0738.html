On Sun, 21 Nov 1999, Adam Chrystie wrote:

> I have a connon BJC-1000. It printed fin on my linux redhat 6.0 INTEL...but for
> some reason it will not print on my Red Hat 6.0 AXP except under the K-Desktop
> Browser..and then it prints incorrectly....

I have the BJ 200 - I've had that working on my Alpha (although its now on
my dads PC somewhere along a piece of ether from my Alpha).

> Any ideas? the var printer area says it prints fine according to the status
> file....

OK, what does it and doesn't it do! Try the following things:

  1) Print a simple text file by doing lpr mysimpletextfile
     What happens?

  2) Try printing a known good postscript file - you've probably got a
rather nice picture of a tiger - type:
       locate tiger.ps

     You may find he is gzip'd, if so ungzip him and print him with:
       lpr tiger.ps

Now either of these could do at least the following:

    a) Print correctly.
    b) Print complete gibberish
    c) Print something almost recognisable but in some way corrupted.
    d) Get part way through the job.
    e) Do sod all!

Try them and tell us what happens, together with any messages gathered in
/var/log/messages during it.

  Another test you could try is catting your text file straight to the
printer device behind lpr's back by doing:

   cat mytextfile > /dev/lp0

(All the previous types of screw up are possible!)
