>Some of what you mention is accurate. Two things I would like to point out
regarding higher education and the products mentioned. There is a program
in place to support education, commonly referred to as TEI. It sets
specific discount levels for each product (hw/sw).
>
>With very few exceptions the TEI discount for "QL or QB" (software)
products is set at 38%. Additionally, if your campus takes part in the CSLG
(Campus Software Library Grant) you have the fortran compilers available to
you for free.
>
>In the past the most significant item not found on the CSLG list has been
the base license. This announcement goes a long way in solving what we've
felt was sorely lacking in the program. Most institutions have a CSLG
administrator in place who provides the licence PAKs to the departments
upon request. In addition to the fortran there are hundreds of layered
products. Take a look:
>
> <http://www.spcnet.com/edu.htm>
>You'll find all the TEI and CSLG program information here. You'll also
find a product list for all the layered software available. It's quite a
program!
>
