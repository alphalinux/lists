Tee Emm wrote:
>
> G'day everyone!
>
> I intend to use Red Hat on one of my Alpha 1000 servers.
> Reading most of the stuff lying around, I have come to know
> about MILO and most of its details. So here is what confuses
> me:
>
> - Does MILO work for Alpha 1000? (Strangely, the MILO FAQ at
> http://www.alphalinux.org/faq/milo.html does not mentions
> DEC 1000 anywhere or is that I am ignoring some other
> representation of Alpha 1000?)

The Hardware Compatability List for Red Hat 6.0 (which is really an
ever-evolving HCL for the soon (when?) to be released 6.1) shows the
AS1000 being capable of SRM only. Which means no Milo. This doesn't mean
you can't use Milo if your machine does have an ARC or AlphaBIOS
console- just that Red Hat won't be providing/supporting it in the
future.

> - If MILO isn't for me, where do I begin? SRM or Windows NT
> ARC Firmware?

SRM is the future. Strangely enough, it was the past too.

> - Can I download Windows NT ARC firmware from the net?

Probably, I haven't looked. Try ftp.digital.com
 
> What Red Hat versions can I use for this? Many thanks in
> advance
>
> - Tariq

Right now, 6.0 if you are persistant, and if your machine is not
actually an AS1000-A. The 'A' models currently don't work. B-(
