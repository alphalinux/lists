On Thu, 18 Nov 1999, James Bremer wrote:

>
> Hell, I work at an ISP and we use nothing BUT Linux (x86)
> servers. I also have worked with the other major OS's (Tru64,
> HPUX, Sun/Solaris, AIX, ad naseum), and I have found that
> Linux is emminently more configurable than any of the others.
> It is not as rock solid as Sun is, but I have found the built-in
> functionality (gcc, apache, working sendmail, pop, named)
> to more than offset any advantages Sun might have. In addition
> to the fact that any security problems you have are fixed in a
> matter of hours or days, not weeks or months.
>
> Plus, frankly, it's free.
>
> These are strong advantages I'd be hard-pressed to overlook
> or ignore.
>
> - James

        And there is another issue there about the stability. Lets
face it, 95% of PC hardware is trash. With a proper build you can get
the same hardware stability as a SUN, but it normally requires a fair
bit of effort in parts choice and assembly.

        Personally, I have never had a production Linux box go
down/have issues that were not directly related to me or the
environment (wrong command line, 140F room toasts a drive, etc).

        Apples to apples would bring Solaris x86 into play, and that
is a diff story than Solaris Sparc.
