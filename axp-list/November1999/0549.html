Nick,

That's one of the first series of Alpha systems we, Digital/
Compaq, came out with. We referred to them as the "bird" machines,
because they were code-named :

3000/300 == Sandpiper
3000/400 == Pelican
3000/500(-900) == Flamingo

I've still got a 500 in the lab, running just fine after all
these years. ;-)

They do have on-board SCSI and ethernet, the SCSI is called tcds ==
TURBOchannel dual-SCSI, Tru64 UNIX sees it as:

                                        tcds0 at tc0 slot 6
                                        scsi0 at tcds0 slot 0
                                        scsi1 at tcds0 slot 1

And the ethernet is one of the Lance chips, ln0: DEC LANCE Module
Name: PMAD-BA. That's the main problem I think you will have, i.e.
limited (if any) support for TURBOchannel on Linux. I had two of
the 300s or 400s, they are in parts now. I think I've still got
some of the memory sims if you need them, I can go check. Maybe
someone else can tell us if there ever was TURBOchannel support
in Linux. It's a fairly old (open) bus Digital developed for use
in the old MIPS R2/3000 DECstation systems, and support never took
off like Digital had hoped. It had max throughput of 100MB/sec, not
bad for 7-8 years ago. If you can't get Linux to run on it, maybe
FreeBSD or Tru64 UNIX, it's only $99 for the non-commercial liscense.
