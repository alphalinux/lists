In 1993, I download enough of 0.91 to boot my 486. Then,
I originally used Yggdrasil Linux, switching to Red Hat only
when Yggdrasil failed to publish a distribution with a 2.0.x
kernel. Then I tried Caldera, Slackware, and the version
in Stefan Strobel's "Linux Universe" (Springer Verlag).
The latter, intended for students, was in many ways my
favorite distribution, but alas was never updated. The one
Suse user that I know (also x86) is an extremely experienced
unix user and a fine scientific programmer. He quickly
left Suse for Red Hat at a time when I considered trying
the Suse, and this discouraged me from trying that flavor.

On getting my Alpha, it was preloaded with Red Hat 6.0, and I
doubt I'll change any time soon, excepting to update to 6.1.
The Red Hat's installation (at least on x86) has always been
fairly convenient. The little bit of experience that I have
had so far on the Alpha running RH 6.0 has been satisfactory.

As I learn more about the Alpha, I will doubtless be more
critical of it and its software, but at present, I am
happy with both

Regards,
Ed Lukacs
