I didn't!

I'm running SuSE on my Alphas. I made this choice partly to spite my
RedHat-fanatic collegues, partly because I'd been recommended it. Being new
to Linux (or U*x in general for that matter), I had no preferences to start
with.

I'm mostly lurking on this list; to gather whatever hints I can find, to
contribute some occasional message on my own, when the topic is, e.g.
firmware or hardware related, or like this, generic.

Anyway, I hope you RedHat users had an easier time installing than I had...
The SuSE manual contains just about the exact amount of information to make
it *possible* for you to install from ARC (which I did on my Multia), *if*
you know your Alphas and know what the ARC environment variables are all
about. Installing from SRM (which I did on my AS200) is impossible, if you
just follow the manual with no prior Unix knowledge (just imagine my
confusion when reading the single instruction "create BSD partitions").
However, the excellent Alpha installation guide provided by Compaq gave me
the necessary clues to complete the installation.
