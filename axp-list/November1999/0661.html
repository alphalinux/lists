On Thu, Nov 18, 1999 at 06:34:54PM -0800, Stephen Williams wrote:
>
> cchen@reed.edu said:
> > Has anyone had success with using _ANY_ ultra wide SCSI HA with SRM or
> > ARC on a PC164? I'm looking at the BT-950 and the AHA-2940UW as
> > possibilities since I know they're decent boards with good Linux
> > driver support (for x86).
>
> I've had an AHA-2940UW in my PC164 for a (long) while now, but since
> 2.1 and 2.2 kernels, it's ben a bit flaky. The general consensus is
> that the adaptec board is *not* reliable, and this seems to jive with
> recent experience.
>

Hmmm...I was unable to get the AHA-2940UW to work on my Miata with the
SRM BIOS. It works under Alpha BIOS, though. So far I haven't seen
any "flakiness" with a 2.2.10 kernel. This may be due to the fact
that I haven't really put much of a load on the machine. Mostly I use
it as an xterm and I run Netscape on it.

The ES40s I have use some sort of Symbios (formally NCR) controller.
They came with the machine and work under SRM BIOS.
