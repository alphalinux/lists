Jaap Hogenberg wrote:

> Hi,
>
> >The Linux world is not going to survive and gain widespread acceptance
> >on free software alone.

> Hm, are you sure ? I don't have any commercial software yet on
> my Alpha running Linux. (how many of you guys do ?????)
> IMHO,the tendency is there is less and less people
> willing to pay for software on their desktop.

When I mentioned widespread acceptance, I include the average user, the
corporate user and others. Not just the die hard fans.

Houdini is ported to Linux, you do not think this is going to be free do you?
How about Maya? Oracle isn't free either.

And yes, I am quite sure that commercial application support is crucial to
the long term success of the platform. Surely you do not think that the Alpha
platform has much of a future as nothing more elegant than a vehicle for
freebie software?

> >I see no reason for commercial software to be treated as if it were
> >something evil.

> I don't. I just don't see reason to plug commercial software here either.

Who was plugging commercial software? Satori might port their paint package
if the interest is there. As there is no Satori Paint for Alpha Linux, this
can hardly be considered a plug for commercial software that does not exist.
While you personally may only be interested in the freebies you can download,
some of us use Alpha and Linux in a professional environment. Something the
free software market is amazingly weak in implementing software for.

If not here, how would anyone know that they were even considering it? as an
Alpha user, a professional user at that, ANY tools that are or may become
available to myself or others is of considerable interest, and certainly is
NOT off topic on this list or any other Linux list.

> >As for being on the right list, well, no rules against
> >talking about commercial software.

> You're right. But from what you wrote , it looks as if these people are
> a long way from porting to Linux Alpha , as there is not yet a version
> for Intel Linux available yet.
> There's no rules against talking about vapourware (tm) either.

Not necessarily. I have talked to them numerous times on the subject. As far
as being a long way off, they asked to see if people were interested. If they
get no response, then it is a dead issue. Sad because their software is not
even all that expensive considering what it does. And they are one of the
VERY FEW who even supported Alpha at all.
