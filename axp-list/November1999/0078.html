Without seeing the startup mesages, it's hard to guess what's happening.
Unless you can read REALLY fast, the only way to see the kernel
startup messages is to connect the serial port to another system
or terminal. If you do this you need to append

console=ttyS1,9600 console=tty0

to the "boot_osflags", eg:

>>> boot dqa0 -fi kernels/generic.gz -fl "root=/dev/hd<x> console=ttyS1,9600 console=tty0"

Also you could try /dev/hdd for root=, or get the updated ramdisk
from the RedHat site and try it. With a ramdisk do:

>>> boot dva0 -fi generic.gz -fl "root=/dev/fd0 load_ramdisk=1"

This is the way it's documented (for installation) cause you don't
have to guess what your CD device is called.

good luck

larry
