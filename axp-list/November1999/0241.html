On Fri, 5 Nov 1999, Dr. David Gilbert wrote:

>
> Doesn't this make the $99 figure a bit meaningless then? i.e. if you buy
> the cheap Alpha boxes then you can't run it, but if you buy the more
> expensive ones then you can run it? Wouldn't everyone with these boards
> already have Tru64 licenses ?
Even in spite of questions about firmware, the offer is
still perhaps meaningful for University TEI customers, such as Duke, with
CSLG and ESL contracts. Box can be bought without Tru U license,
OSF-BASE added for $99, OSF-DEV, OSF-SVR, CXX, FORTRAN-O, etc added
from CSLG membership (for which the University pays). Firmware updates
come with the Media (for which we have update service). It is
definitely an incentive for us to continue to acquire Alphas and run Tru U
instead of Sun, SGI, Intel, and even Alpha/Linux for number-crunching
such as math modeling, image processing, which we do a lot. The 64-bit
OS on 64-bit hardware with the best highly-tuned Fortran compiler (and now C++)
is extremely attractive to many of our departments. I know some faculty
that the new offer may push into buying Alpha/Tru U instead of Intel/Linux,
etc. Hardware compatibility must be examined before purchase, but shouldn't
salesoids have do do SOMETHING for their money? ;-)
