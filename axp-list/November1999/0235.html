I own 5 21264's, 2 21164's, and 2 Athlons plus a few other systems.
I think I've got a fairly good understanding of their performance.
Athlons are as snappy as any of the Alphas when it comes to user
interaction with the system. In fact, they outperform the 21164's
for most things. My own codes track SPEC's performance ratios so
I'm inclined to believe the guys who did the tests know what they're
doing. Do keep in mind the people who handle SPEC for a company
are the ones who know what makes their processor run fast. I also
like Linpack as an indicator of double precision FP performance.

> Why? People are claiming Athlons are fantastically fast when what is
> actually the case is that they are fantastically fast for x86 processors.
> If you're going to badmouth Alpha you need to know that there are cases
> which allow an Alpha to far outstrip an x86 processor.

"far" is relative and without you putting your code on an Athlon,
its quite difficult to define. I have put my code on Athlon and
various other platforms and I know what that means to me. 0.3 to
0.5 times a 21264 at the same MHZ for Athlon.

I use processors for what they are good at. x86 architectures
do integer work more economically, $ for $, than an Alpha does.

Alphas do FP work $ for $, about equal to x86. Alpha's main
advantage is it puts lots of FP in a small package which, when
you want to run large parallel problems, is a significant advantage
in terms of space needed to house the systems. 64 bit is nice to
have also.
