The new chipset for the coppermine processors has not been released. It is not
expected out for several more months. The Athlon 500 or 550 outperformed the
PIII 600 in everybenchmark that Tomshardware could test it on. Double Data Rate
SDRAM is the stuff that would make it appear that you are running on a 200MHz
bus, when in reality it would still be the 100MHz bus, but with data being moved
on both the rise and fall of the clock. I don't hate Intel. In fact my dad
works for them, but why pay more for a cpu that doesn't perform as well.

> > Not all the coppermines are at 133MHz bus. I believe that the first of them is
> > the 733MHz CPU and it is still beaten by the Athlon in everything, but the quake
> > test benchmark. Check out Toms Hardware to see the benchmarks. To make it more
> > interesting the results are with the non-existent i820 chipset. Until that is
> > released you won't get the results seen. One of the more interesting things is
> > that the rumor about the Athlon is that it can do 200MHz bus. It can accomplish
> > this by using both the up and down stroke of the clock.
> >
>
> With Coppermine only slightly slower at a given clock rate,
> there is little incentive to buy Athlon. (except those folks who
> despise Intel)
>
> Intel introduced the 840 chipset with the new cpus and that does
> support high performance memory. I just looked at it's specs and
> it is capable of a maximum of 3.2GB/sec, but I suspect a single
> cpu will get no more than 1.6GB/sec since it uses two RDRAM channels.
> That chipset looks pricey though. I also read there were MB
> makers that were going to release 820 based boards but only
> put two memory slots on it to keep from triggering the bug in
> the chipset.
>
> Athlon MB manuals state they support double data rate SDRAM
> (DDR SDRAM??) but I haven't found anyone who makes them to
> try it out. I have Athlons for test systems but am currently
> running PC100 SDRAM. They are definitely faster than older
> PII/PIII at the same clock rate.
