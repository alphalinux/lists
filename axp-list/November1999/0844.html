On Thu, 25 Nov 1999, Steve Rogers wrote:
>
> > You're right. But from what you wrote , it looks as if these people are
> > a long way from porting to Linux Alpha , as there is not yet a version
> > for Intel Linux available yet.
> > There's no rules against talking about vapourware (tm) either.
>
> Not necessarily. I have talked to them numerous times on the subject. As far
> as being a long way off, they asked to see if people were interested. If they
> get no response, then it is a dead issue. Sad because their software is not
> even all that expensive considering what it does. And they are one of the
> VERY FEW who even supported Alpha at all.

I don't know why people need to port from Linux/Intel to other
architectures all the time. They should write real legal code from the
start, particularly for any high level apps. Then it is a matter of
letting the compiler handle the rest. Non open source code seems to have
this problem. Alpha has to be "popular" for people to be bothered to do a
port to it. It seems that porting to Alpha is just another way of saying
"we're cleaning up our code". :)

Anyway, we should always support apps like this being ported to linux. I
probably wont use it, I havent even heard of it before but at least it
gives choice on the matter and would probably motivate someone to do an
open sourced one.
