Perhaps someone with a more complete knowledge of the way em86 works can help
you out here, but my understanding is that em86 is a program that translates
x86 assembly to alpha assembly on the fly. Whether it's "user-space" or not,
I'm not sure. However, em86 is necessary to run x86 binaries on alpha.
also, Andy had mentioned using ldd to determine the library usage of an ELF
executable. You can't use the alpha ldd on a non-alpha executable. However,
em86 comes with ldd86, which is basically ldd straight from an x86 box. That
you *can* use on x86 binaries.
