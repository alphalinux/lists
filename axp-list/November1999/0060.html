On Sun, 31 Oct 1999, Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> Further thoughts on "vaporware" clockspeeds on Alpha:

This is what I thought. Many people that I dealt with weren't able to get
machines with a higher clock speed than 667 and I was told 667 was it
until Q1 2000

> Fastest shipping chips readily available are 667MHz.
> 750MHz is easily done but:
> with current chipsets a chip at 750MHz has a 75MHz memory bus clock.
> To get a reasonable gain in performance and overcome this memory sped

This is/was supposed to be taken care of in the new chip sets that Compaq
is producing?? That was rumor last June but I've heard nothing since, so
I wrote it off as just rumor.

> issue:
> a bigger cache is needed 8 MB ideally.
> There is a problem with 8MB cache running reliably in the
> current CPU packages Cache on SlotB runs at CPU clockrate, I believe.
>
> In simple terms: If API or Compaq shipped a 750 with the same cache and
> other design details as the 667, it would not be much ( if any) faster,
> depending on which benchmark you ran. We are now being told that
> nothing faster than 667 will ship in the near future.

It's scary but having seen the death of various things, I don't like the
writing on the wall. Knowing that there is an incredible demand for the
current production is the big Q just trying to max profit potential by
holding newer faster designs back, or are there going to be fewer and
fewer newer designs?

Anyhoo, If AMD sticks to it's schedule and the 1 gig chip is out, and
nothing faster than our current 667 we will have lost the "fastest chip"
moniker, whether you measure mhz or bogomips. Thats a shame, and you know
it sounds great that the lab boys are running 1 gig at shows , but "If it
ain't shipping it don't exist". Too bad. I had hoped long ago when I was
young and foolish (uhm last year :) that the Alpha would be the first
chip to hit the magic 1 gig and be shipping. Hey I would have put money
on it 2 years ago. Today I realize that I was sadly mistaken.
