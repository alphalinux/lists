On Thu, Nov 18, 1999 at 02:42:57PM -0600, Steven Bonneville wrote:
>
> As any number of people on the list have noted, they totally neglect to
> mention that those Alpha boxes are all SGI/Cray T3E supercomputers. And
> there's a minor factual error; the new list released last week only has
> *four* Alpha-based T3Es; the fifth had been the T3E at the UK Met Office
> but ASCI Blue Pacific (an IBM SP) knocked it to #11. But that difference
> could be because they got old information from Compaq.
>

This is incorrect. The one 512 CPU Compaq machine is a new cluster
architecture that Compaq is selling. It is a 128 node ES40 cluster
with a Quadrics interconnect. We just had one delivered to LLNL last
month (though at the moment most nodes have a single CPU). We may see
more nodes added next year but I am not sure if they have plans to
build them larger or not. I'm working on a smaller scale (8 nodes 32
CPUs) of the same machine porting most of the software to Linux.
We've hit a few hurdles but nothing major. There are quite a few
features that the commercial OSs have that Linux lacks, I'm hoping we
can incorporate some of them into the main kernel.
