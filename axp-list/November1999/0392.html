Are you sure that e.themes.org is not the bottleneck here ? Do a traceroute
to see if there are any sticking points along the way. When I download from
a "close" site (eg MS) I typically get around 180KB/sec with NT or Linux
with a T1. Inefficiencies in the TCP/IP are unlikely an issue -- 180KB/sec
is so low bandwidth compared to what modern systems are capable of. Maybe
your firewall is overloaded ? I've seen horrible performance implications
when running full-duplex networks where one of the devices along the path
aren't really capable of handling FD properly. You might find that HD gives
you better performance in some situations.

I have an interesting comparison..

At work I have three PCs. One is a PII/350 running Red Hat 6, another is a
PII/400 running BSDI, and the other is an Alpha 255 running Red Hat 6. I'm
not trying to smash any particular vendor, but I have evidence that doesn't
make sense. If I download themes from e.themes.org I *always* get around
70kb - 80kb/sec (on a T1) per download under Linux. With BSDI (and before
that, FreeBSD) I get around 40kb/sec. At first I thought it was a fluke,
but it's been happening this way ever since day one of running *BSD. The
x86 boxes have identical AMD PCI 10/100 cards, all systems go to the same
switch, run full duplex, and go through the same PIX firewall.

I was under the impression that the *BSD stack was superior to all
others..is that for specific tasks only? Why am I getting double the
performance under Linux? BTW, I have NT Workstation on a PIII/450 with
256MB and just for kicks I downloaded the same files, but @ apprixmately
30kb - 38kb/sec..
