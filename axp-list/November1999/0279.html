robb@killsoft.com said:
> However, I had so many lockups with my PC164 that I really couldn't
> isolate the sound card as a problem.

I managed to stabilize my PC164 (no sound card) by pulling a certain
drive off the SCSI chain. The SCSI card is an AHA2940UW with two UW
hard disks. The problem device was a SCSI2 (narrow) PD Drive connected
to the 50pin cable connector. It didn't even need to be used, just
powered on and wired in.

I diagnosed this by leaving the monitor in VGA mode when the hangs were
most likely. There were SCSI reset messages that never made it to the
logs when the hang occured.

I'm going to recheck the termination on the PD drive, and possibly
try it on a separate AHA1540 (ISA) card all by itself. Otherwise, even
though I sore up and down it couldn't be a hardware problem, it turned
out to be.
