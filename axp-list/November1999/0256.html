Actually, the problem right now may not even be CPU speed so much, but
getting the fastest&largest caches and improving memory bandwidth further:
I couldn't have believed that a 667 MHz EV-67 21264A with 8 MB 444 MHz
DDR-SSRAM and normal 2.6 GB/per CPU mem bandwidth can do much more than 80
SPECfp95 per CPU, but it can and I was pleasantly surprised - even
21264A-667 trouncing Merced FP (unless Intel comes out with yet another
last-minute SPECfp95 tweak)...

Regarding future CPUs like 21364 and 21464 (EV7 and EV8) the fact that L2
cache, direct memory controller and multiprocessor interconnect are all on
CPU die would help tremendously in system design and verification and
faster system launch. The only additional ASIC the mainboard vendor would
need to find is I/O bridge from the dedicated EV7 or EV8 I/O bus to
standard bunch of AGP, PCI-X etc buses - something like AMD & API LDT bus...
