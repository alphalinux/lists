On Thu, 18 Nov 1999, Chavvon Smith wrote:

> I am currently setting up an ISP and I am debating on using Linux versus Unixware 7 or Tru64. I have read articles from earlier in the year when the benchmarking test had NT out performing Linux as a web platform. Have all the problems with the SMP support and TCP stack been corrected in Linux 6.1 to surpass NT, and can anyone give me their experiences with UW7 and TRU64 in a working environment.

You might like to add a few newlines in there now and then.....

The so called `problems' linux has are really blown out of all proportion.
SMP works well, the TCP/IP stack in 2.2 wasn't multithreaded and after
pouring over the source code for a long time and they figured they could
rig a test which they knew linux couldn't win against NT. Of course NT
would be nearly 4x quicker than linux on the same hardware when you serve
static pages down four ethernet adapters bound to individual processors.
However, as has been repeatedly stated, this isn't a realistic test.
Better to stick a single Gbit ethernet card on. Besides, the sort of
bandwidth they would need to be able to saturate with that system was
beyond ridiculous.

Plenty of ISPs have no problem running their services on Linux.

Try the options in your setting and if linux does the job well enough why
blow a wad on the expensive solutions? You can always migrate up later,
it's not like choosing NT where your only migration option is more NT.
