        Well, I think that the difference between redhat and other
distributions is somehow the difference between a piano and a violin.
What I mean is that if you are an "absolute beginner" (as I was about Unix
when I began to use my Alpha) you can have music from a piano at your
first lesson; with violin you need months (if not years) of deep study
before you can "produce" your first tune. This has nothing to do with the
absolute value of the instrument, but when a computer (to go back to the
question) is the instrument - and not the topic - of your work, you should
prefer an "easy-to-start" tool.
