With regards to your message at 10:51 AM 11/25/99 +1030, Mark Brown. Where
you stated:
>I am buying a new (second) SCSI host adapter for an Alpha (Miata) and
>thought I might
>try Ultra3 if I can get one and if they are not vastly more expensive (I
>am waiting for
>a price at the moment) - I don't currently really need Ultra3 speed but
>hey ...
>
>The only cards I have found are from Qlogic and LSIlogic(symbios) as far
>as I can
>see there are currently no drivers for the Qlogic card but LSIlogic claim to
>support Linux via SDMS which I know nothing about except that it stands for
>'System Device Management Software'.
>
>Just wondered if anyone had tried/looked at Ultra3 on Linux and had any info
>on available drivers or adapters?
I believe that if by "Ultra3" you mean Ultra160 I think the best bet is to
wait for now.
If however you mean Low Voltage Differential then I suggest the Intraserver
6100 or 6101 as excellent choices. Good quality, good drivers, and
excellent support.
http://www.intraserver.com/products/6000series.html
