On Fri, 5 Nov 1999, Luigi Cavallo wrote:

>
> Hi there,
>
> I've seen that I could assemble UP1000 or UP2000 based alphas for a
> reasonable price. However, before attempting it I would like to have some
> suggestions. In particular, why posts in this list almost never concern
> them ?? Any particular reason ? I can't believe they work so perfectly
> and that installation is so easy that none has problems with them...

Mainly because they are both new. The UP2000 is very similar to a
DS20/DP264 machine, so almost everything learned there is applicable. In
the case of the UP1000, well just about nobody has one at this point, and
it's harder to ask questions about a board you don't have :)
 
> Also, suggestions for well supported 100 Mbit/s net card and cheap graphic
> card (I don't need graphics). I know I could check in the HW-compatibility
> list, but in my experience a supported card can still take the sweat out
> of you before working.

I've used the 3Com 3c905B-TX cards quite a lot at work and they seem to
work fine. Also the DEC Tulip cards and even the Intel EtherExpress Pro
cards seem to work fine. Graphics? The old Matrox Mils work well and
should be pretty cheap, failing that just about any PCI Permedia II card
should be OK.

> There's a web address where I can find sort of step-by-step installation
> instructions of Linux on them ? Like the wonderful document that exists
> for DS10 and DS20 boxes ? I have a little experience with axp/Linux,
> since I installed RH on a LX and some UX boxes, but I think that
> things are different with UPs, because the only supported OS seems to be
> Linux, and hence those small NT partitions with linload and milo are not
> needed anymore. But I am not a linux guru, and I cannot figure it out
> without your help.

There is some info on the Alpha Processor Inc. website.
http://www.alpha-processor.com, and there's more on the way. Also a lot of
this stuff wil be made available on alphalinux.org as well.

Good Luck,
