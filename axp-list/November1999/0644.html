Designer Seven wrote:
>
> >From www.top500.org... what totally beats me is the
> Hitachi machines.(SR8000/128) Hitachi has a few
> supercomputers listed on top500... but the amazing
> part is the CPU count (very low).
>
> For example, #5 is a Hitachi machine with 128 CPUs
> while #34 is a 512 CPU AlphaServer SC. Or consider #6,
> which is a Cray/SGI T3E900 with 1324 CPUs.
>
> What are the Japanese making? Does anyone know
> anything about these machines? I mean, if you do a
> bold and probably unrealistic comparison, one of these
> Hitachi CPUs is more powerful than 4 Alphas.
>

They aren't work station cpus.

The Japanese still make large vector mainframes.
Those FP engines are quite probably 4x faster than
an Alpha but they probably cost at least 10x what
Compaq sells their cpu's for. So, in MFLOPS per
$$, the Alpha is a much better deal.

Depends on the kinds of problems you want to solve
and how much money you have.

One thing the top500 doesn't tell you is the price
tag for each system which would provide a different
perspective on the performance numbers.
