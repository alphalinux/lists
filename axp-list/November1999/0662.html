On Thu, 18 Nov 1999, Secret Asian Man wrote:

>Another clueless newbie question...
>
>So I get this beautiful board in the mail today, and I want to put my 9G
>IBM UW drive with it, so I figure I'll need a host adapter. Searching
>through Google and Dejanews doesn't really give me a lot to hope for,
>though...

Search the archive for problems with IBM drives and AlphaLinux. There may
be a relationship between IBM drives and SCSI lockups, or there may not
be.

>Has anyone had success with using _ANY_ ultra wide SCSI HA with SRM or ARC
>on a PC164? I'm looking at the BT-950 and the AHA-2940UW as possibilities
>since I know they're decent boards with good Linux driver support (for
>x86)...

Here's how I had mine set up:
- - Tekram DC390U ultra-wide SCSI adapter (based on the Symbios 53C875 chip)
- - Quantum Atlas II 9.1GB ultra-wide SCA drive, with terminating adapter
- - Mitsumi 6X IDE cdrom drive
I used 2.2.13 and the sym53c8xx kernel driver
No significant problems. I like the Tekram stuff with Linux.

>Also, where can I find a torque driver for attaching the heatsink to the
>CPU? It's not attached and I'm afraid I'll break it if I try putting it on
>with an ordinary driver (either that or I'll not tighten it enough and the
>heat won't conduct and my chip will fry)...

Oh don't be a baby. Just tighten it down as hard as you can. :))

But seriously folks, I tightened mine with a deep socket and 1/4" driver
until it was just firm. I couldn't find a torque spec, so I guessed. No
problems yet.
