On Wed, Nov 10, 1999 at 07:34:04PM -0500, Mark Abene wrote:

> I had a similar problem when I first got my PC164. The fans would spin up,
> lights, camera, but no action. No console and it wouldn't boot.
> The problem was a faulty ATX power supply. I replaced it and everything was
> fine. You should make sure you're using a 300-watt power supply.
> Anything lower, and you run the risk of blowing the power.

Good advice.. unfortunately, that's really not possible for a UDB. Or
at least.. I'm unaware of an aftermarket 300watt power supply that'd
fit. It'd be a real trick to cram in there, and I'm sure the
additional heat would be very unwelcome in the already poorly
ventilated case. Default UDB's come with a 75watt power supply which
generally does fine, although it's prone to some nasty reliability
issues due to heat and, as I've already mentioned, less than efficient
connetors.
