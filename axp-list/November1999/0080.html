> I was hoping to gain your insight regarding SNII.

Well, I'm about half-ignorant about ServerNet II because it's not quite out
yet.

From reading the glossy brochures, it seems that SNII has the capability of
producing big, cheap networks, much like Myrinet. However, just like
Giganet, it seems that the market that SNII is aimed at is the business
world, and the business world thinks 32 nodes is big. So, my flippant
analysis was: "They could do great things, but odds are they won't do great
things for ME." So, when I see prices, I expect the switches to be too big
and too expensive.

Note that Compaq has 3 strategies going at once: Fiber Channel, ServerNet
II, and Quadrics. Quadrics is what the HPC group is using for system
interconnect, but they'd like to move to pure "wildfire" solutions (and btw,
wildfire machines are now being demoed at trade shows... they should have
one at SC99.) FC is used to attach storage, but not as a system
interconnect. And SNII, well, I don't really know what they're going to use
it for beyond the Tandem line. So, I doubt.

I would love to see more competition for interconnect in the 64-1024 node
size. We would all win.

See you there.
