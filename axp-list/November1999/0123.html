Just a quick point of clarification here. It's my understanding that the
533mhz version of Alpha is 21164a rather than 21164, and there is somewhere
between 15-25% performance delta (depending of course on the fp intensive
application).

At 10:17 AM 11/3/99 -0800, you wrote:
>A huge market for where floating point performance is crucial is the gaming
>market. If you want the best platform for OpenGL 3D games such as
>Half-Life, QuakeIII then the Athlon is not only the best performance system
>on the market right now, it is also the best price/performance system (it is
>rare to achieve both). It is *not* a rumor that the Athlon will eventually
>get a 200Mhz bus, it is *fact* (remember that AMD licensed the EV6 bus from
>Digital).
>The memory benchmark on the Athlon systems is impressive. I wrote a Win32
>program (available at http://www.ultrabac.com/bm_mem.zip) that benchmarks
>the memory subsystem, and the Athlon system stands up very well against the
>Alpha systems. A 600Mhz Athlon scores about 50% better in raw memory
>throughput than a 533Mhz 21164, but scores half of a 500Mhz 21264.
>Note that the Athlon is the first AMD system capable of SMP once the
>necessary motherboards are available.
