Adam Last wrote:
>
> OK I've realised I was wrong. The RH boot disks probably aren't broken.
>
> With a large probability the situation is that the version of MILO supplied
> with RH6.0 supports romfs.

The generic.img is in DOS format. The Ramdisk.img is in ROMFS format.
The 6.0 Milo disk for Multia works perfectly with my Multia.
 
> Unforetunately the RH6.0 version of MILO doesn't work on the Multia I have.
> It's only got 16MB so maybe it's running out of memory as Multia's are
> meant to come with 24M.

The 6.0 Milo disk for Multia works perfectly with my Multia. Of course I
do have 64 Megs of RAM. We installed 6.0 on a Multia at work with 16
megs of RAM once... It took 21 hours to install. Won't be trying that
again soon.

> I've been booting using a Debian version of Milo which I assumed would be
> much the same as the RH Milo.

Probably is.

> Anybody got a contact address for the RH people working on Milo? Can I get
> a version of Milo which supports romfs and fits in 16M?

Milo doesn't need ROMFS support. The boot kernel does (if you roll your
own).
