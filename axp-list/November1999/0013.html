        Thanks for the patch. I will try it tonight and let you know the
results.
        As for other people's comments about IBM drives, the one I have is
an old Fast (10MB/s) disk, and not anywhere close to an U2W drive. To make
things even more fun, I have another IBM drive, a 4GB Ultra Narrow drive
on one of the Tekram controllers, and it is rock solid at Ultra speeds
(passes repeated bonnie tests). This disk also worked fine on the on-board
controller at fast speeds, though that was never with a 2.2 kernel and
only with the NCR53c7xx,8xx driver. Here is the specs on the other IBM
drive that works fine:

  Vendor: IBM Model: DDRS-34560 Rev: S71D
  Type: Direct-Access ANSI SCSI revision: 02
Detected scsi disk sde at scsi2, channel 0, id 1, lun 0
..
ncr53c875-2-<1,*>: FAST-20 SCSI 20.0 MB/s (50 ns, offset 15)
SCSI device sde: hdwr sector= 512 bytes. Sectors= 8925000 [4357 MB] [4.4 GB]

        Lastly, as for accessing the SCSI BIOS of any PCI SCSI cards from
Alpha BIOS, I have that option turned on, but I don't get any BIOS
messages from the cards during boot up. I have the 5.66 AlphaBIOS
installed that can see the drives on the Tekram controllers (never tried
to boot from them) just fine. This is not a big deal as those controllers
appear to be working fine with 2.2.x kernels.
        So, I will try out that kernel patch and then go from there.
Thanks for the help.
