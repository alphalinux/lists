The Redhat Milo should work for you as well-it might even be the same.

This is an empirical case. I got the generic image as well and used dd to
write the floppy:

dd if=generic.img of=/dev/fd0 bs=1440k

Then, put this disk in the drive after you get the Milo prompt and type:

boot fd0:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 load_ramdisk=1 prompt_ramdisk=1

Good luck. Pathetic Multias kick ass!

- Alexander Fry
