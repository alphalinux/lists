On Thu, Nov 11, 1999 at 08:34:37AM -0900, Christopher E. Brown wrote:
>
> Don't know much about ISA on an alpha, but I remember somthing
> about the ISA system on x86 and DMA transfers. There is this nice
> little option in the sound section to grab a chunk of mem for DMA and
> hold it (but only when compiling the card in, no modules). Somthing
> about ISA DMA transfers and memory locations above 16MB causing
> strange things to happen.
>
> When loading a module, you pretty much get stuck with what is avail,
> and this could cause strange problems depending on what was happening
> when the system booted.
>

Stands to reason. I'm using the Persistent DMA option in the sound drivers,
and all my sound drivers are built STATICALLY. This was the key.
All my freezing problems have vanished, with constant sound usage too.
With the sound drivers as modules, my system stability was a nightmare.
