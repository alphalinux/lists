Mark wrote:

> What made people on this list choose Red Hat as their Linux? This is
> not a question of challenge, I like Red Hat, but I want to know what
> is that drove YOU to install it over Debian, Turbo, Stampede, or
> SuSE.. Is it the way Red Hat supports Linux? The way it runs? The
> way it installs? I'm really anxious to know everyones' opinion.
> Certainly you must know that some of the die-hard users of other
> distros think their's is "true to the meaning of Linux" and
> not-so-commercial..

Heck, at the time I didn't even have a choise, Red Hat was the
only Linux distro that had a (very good!) Alpha port.

I liked the way Red Hat was organised so I sticked with it,
the only thing I don't like is RPM, I prefer the DIY approach.
Note that I'm not Joe average user, I only install the bare
needed base and then compile all stuff I need myself.

I'm also running Red Hat because they pioneered porting Linux to
non-i386 platforms, like Alpha and SPARC. They also did (and do)
a lot to promote Linux and to get the Big Players involved in it.
Plus, RH gives everything they've developed back to the community,
and they distribute it under the GPL license (like it should:) ).
So in a way, commercial based != bad thing.

I've never needed RH support, so I can't comment about that.
As long you avoid their .0 releases it's okay :)
RH is quick releasing updates and bugfix rpms, but that applies
to all major Linux distro's.

For what it's worth, I've only seen SuSE, Turbo and Stampede
on i386 boxen, IMHO, they all are very RH like/based.

Debian also makes a fine, well tested and stable Linux distro,
but what I personally don't like is the very strict .deb system.

Anyway, you can't really go wrong with any of them, heck, it's all
Linux after all. I think it comes down to personal preferences.

Final note, I personally DO prefer NetBSD and FreeBSD (especially
for servers), unfortunally they don't run on my AlphaBIOS Alpha's :(
