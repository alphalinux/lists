Dan Frasnelli wrote:
>
> > make sense. If I download themes from e.themes.org I *always* get around
> > 70kb - 80kb/sec (on a T1) per download under Linux. With BSDI (and before
> > that, FreeBSD) I get around 40kb/sec. At first I thought it was a fluke,
> > but it's been happening this way ever since day one of running *BSD. The
> > x86 boxes have identical AMD PCI 10/100 cards, all systems go to the same
> > switch, run full duplex, and go through the same PIX firewall.
> >
> > I was under the impression that the *BSD stack was superior to all
> > others..is that for specific tasks only? Why am I getting double the
> > performance under Linux? BTW, I have NT Workstation on a PIII/450 with
> > 256MB and just for kicks I downloaded the same files, but @ apprixmately
> > 30kb - 38kb/sec..
>
> This is circumstantial evidence at best. My FreeBSD systems typically
> transfer large files (>500M) across our fddi at a sustained 350k/sec using
> 10/100 3c905C interfaces. The Linux systems (identical configs)
> choke on large file transfers and sometimes lock up.

As a point of reference, I use various tulip based 10/100 cards
and they typically sustain 7-9MB/sec on large file transfers.
This, whether in Intels or Alphas. It can easily be limited by
your HD speed if you're using older HW. The size of files I
muck with can easily exceed 10GB on my Alphas.

You may have a config problem on your Linux box.
