On 28 Nov 1999 04:26:50 -0500, Chris Kloiber <ckloiber@rochester.rr.com> wrote:
>the ext2 partitions from milo but with the 'sparse superblocks' as you call
>them way you get is a bunch of lines of useless garbage, as though milo is
>looking at a data sector and trying to interpret it as a directory.

Actually, both milo and aboot have/had problems with
a) 4K block sizes b) new (2.2 kernel) fs features such as sparse
superblocks/filetypes. If you make the /boot partition with
mke2fs -O none it should read it OK.

I believe the aboot in Raw Hide is fixed so that it can deal with these.
