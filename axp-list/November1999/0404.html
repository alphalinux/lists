Many thanks to all who contributed. I managed to get Maple, Mathematica
(just the kernel), and Cplex (http://www.cplex.com) happily working on a
DS-10/Redhat-6.0 machine.

A few tips for those who try similiar:
1. Make sure you use the -p flag with your cp, scp, or tar commands
        to insure all permissions are maintained. In particular it's rather
        tricky discovering that /sbin/loader isn't executable since it doesn't show
        up in strace or similiar debugging when running a binary.

2. Make sure you include a.out/ecoff support in the kernel.

3. Don't assume the installation scripts are doing something simple like
        setting LD_LIBRARY_PATH, and PATH and execing a binary. I had many
        maple problems like:
[root@volume bin_DEC_ALPHA_UNIX]# ./xmaplev5
10312:./xmaplev5: : Fatal Error: cannot map libMesaGL.so.2.6
        that were fixed by simply running the vendors exec script.

4. If you can't find out where a license file is being searched for,
        and strace of du binaries isn't working for you assume
        /usr/local/<application>/<subdir>. I installed mathematica
        in /opt and it of course never found it's mathpass file.

Many thanks to Stuart Midgley, Michal Jaegermann, Greg Lindahl and
anyone else I missed for their helpful suggestions.

FYI so far are cplex, fortran, and C applications/simulations are
running 2-6 times as fast as they did under linux-intel on a PIII-500.
