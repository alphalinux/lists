The Register article is really misleading. They write:

> Compaq yesterday launched a supercomputer based on its Alpha technology,
> and in passing, decided to kick rival Silicon Graphics in the chops.
[...]
> According to the Big Q, the AlphaServer SC puts the company in a position
> where it is a contender to sell high end systems costing over a million
> bucks. Compaq claimed that the Alpha chip now powers five out of the 10 most
> powerful machines in the known universe, and has displaced SGI from its
> position as market share leader in this sector.

As any number of people on the list have noted, they totally neglect to
mention that those Alpha boxes are all SGI/Cray T3E supercomputers. And
there's a minor factual error; the new list released last week only has
*four* Alpha-based T3Es; the fifth had been the T3E at the UK Met Office
but ASCI Blue Pacific (an IBM SP) knocked it to #11. But that difference
could be because they got old information from Compaq.

I suspect that by saying "displaced SGI", they're really trying to say
"displaced the MIPS-based SGI Origin 2000". Even so, it's not all that
accurate a statement.

Terry Shannon is quoted later in the article as saying, more correctly:

> "On a CPU architecture basis, Alpha is number one across the board in HPTC
> (thanks in part to the Cray parallel processors)."

In large part, I'd say.
