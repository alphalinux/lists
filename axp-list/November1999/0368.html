On Wed, Nov 10, 1999 at 12:14:58AM -0800, B. James Phillippe wrote:

> Greetings,
>
> I recently acquired an (apparently) new an unused Multia from a co-worker.
> After many weeks, I had finally obtained enough components to get it
> working (memory, kbd, mouse, floppy, etc). However, to my dismay, it
> appears it is fatally inoperable. I'm hoping someone can suggest
> something.
>
> I am already intermediately familiar with several Alpha systems, and I am
> acquainted with some of the peculiarities of the UDB (including the battery
> problem).
>
> My system has 2 pair of working 8MB x36 SIMMS (pulled from my AS200) and a
> new battery. It fails to boot. Removing the battery/re-arranging the
> memory makes no difference. This is a VX40. The symptom is that the
> system will power up (fans, CPU gets hot over time, etc), but the console
> never initializes. The SROM LED on the back flashes hex E, indicating a
> memory configuration error - or mainboard failure. Since no memory
> combination I try (including no memory present) does not change this error
> code, I am dreading the worst. Is there any hope? If not, where can I get
> a replacement? For the cost of the unit, I'd rather replace the entire
> thing (perhaps for a better Multia) than try to get a new mainboard.
>
> Suggestions?
>

On my UDB, I had a similar problem. It'd power on, but not bring up a
console or even initialize the TGA adaptor. Of course.. my Multia had
been working for a couple years previous to this.. so.. I knew the
machine was at least functional. I found that the power connector on
the mainboard wasn't making good contact with the power supply
connector (wiggling the cables around a bit caused the machine to
spring to life for varying amounts of time). I've yet to fix this (I'm
currently working on another project) but I imagine that either
cleaning those contacts real well or replacing them altogether would
probably solve my problem. I imagine it might solve yours as well.

Alternately, you may want to try and plug some different RAM into the
machine. I know that's an expensive proposition, however, these
machines, in my experience, have been VERY picky regarding what kind
of RAM you put in it, and in what quantity you use. I believe that if
the machine isn't happy with the RAM it would also react in the way
you described.

Otherwise.. it seems Starship Computers
(http://www.starshipcorp.com/starshipcomputers/) still sells OEM parts
for the Multia (albeit at insane prices). You may want to try them for
a replacement mainboard.
