My Multia UDB did the same blank screen thing with the wrong memory.
Even some known good parity (old IBM parity memory) didn't work in
it. On the UDB there is an LED on the back that flashes the error
code and it was a memory error. When I finally got some good
(actually genuine DEC) memory it came up just fine. I can't speak for
your video problem since my video is built in but memory can make it
just sit there.

I had 9 chip 8M simms that didn't work, 12 chip 4M simms that did
(but only 16M total) and finally DEC 9 chip 16M simms that worked. I
know I have seen mention of using 12 chip 16M simms but those are an
older design. I still never figured out why it didn't like the 9
chip 8M simms, they were genuine IBM straight out of a PS/2 which
also requires parity memory and they worked fine in it.
