Paul Tomblin wrote (with edits):
>
> But when it does crash, it's cranky and hard to reboot.
> Mostly, it doesn't recognize the external scsi devices at all. I often have
> to power down all the external devices, reseat all the cables, power them up,

Sounds like a SCSI cable/termination problem. Make sure you have one
and only one terminator at the end of your scsi chain and good quality
cables that are not overly long. Some scsi devices can have internal
termination (resister packs installed or jumper setting) You don't
want to have this and an external terminator.

Get one external device and one cable to work first. Next add
one device and cable at a time Likely only one is at fault
