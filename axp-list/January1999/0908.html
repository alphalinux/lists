Hi Peter and everyone,
> An interesting disscussion went on today at the alphant mailing list
> that somehow got on the topic of DU. Turns out that DEC/Compaq is
> planning to release a C/C++ compiler for alphalinux. Can anyone else
> verify this? This would solve our mozzila problems in one shot. It would
> also make porting DU applications like the JDK to alphalinux easy. See
> below.
        That is great news, providing it is true. Compaq needs to be
aware and considerate to those in the academic community, be they
researchers, students, teachers, etc. if/when pursuing the option of C/C++
compilers for AlphaLinux. As a student and a staff member in an academic
institution, I would be more than willing to pay for compilers to speed up
simulations using floating-point intensive algorithms. After all, the
excellent floating point performance of the Alpha processor was the
motivation for buying a DEC system for me. It is worth the 20%-30%
average speedup - but only at a reasonable price or preferably, free for
hobbyist and non-commercial users.
        DEC has traditionally led the charge into the free software arena;
as I recall, it was DEC who among the major workstation vendors supported
the first port of Linux to a non-x86 architecture when such projects were
unheard of. What disappoints me about the SGI and Sun Linux support
is that (as I understand it), they will only fund development to the point
where it is bootable on their systems. And not that the attitude is
technically wrong - after all, any other stance would be a conflict
between the marketing and technical divisions of Sun and SGI.
        Compaq should seriously consider the historical precedent-setting
activities of Digital and set another by fully supporting and enhancing
Linux on one of the most elegant architectures out there today. As I
recall, Compaq has pledged to supporting Linux on their x86 server
systems. To continue open support for the AlphaLinux project appears a
natural outgrowth of that initial pledge.

Best regards and looking forward to an official annoucement,
Daniel
