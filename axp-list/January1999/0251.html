Peter Petrakis wrote:
>
> Thanks for the quick response! I got sh from DU and executed setld and
> got "no space" .

You are not likely to need that. This is just an interpreter for
a shell script called 'setld'. Just change in the first line
'#!/sbin/sh' to '#!/bin/sh' or, if you think that '/sbin/sh' is magical
do on your box 'cd /sbin ; ln -s ../bin/sh . '

BTW - /sbin/sh from DU is a pretty old implemenation of Bourne shell.
