>The way I understand it, you can't tell the modem to run at 115200 directly.
>After you run setserial /dev/cua1 spd_vhi, you set the modem/program to 38400,
>but it actually sets the serial port to 115200. I think you probably are reall
>y
>running at 115200 and just don't realize it, but I could be misunderstanding
>you.

Does anyone know if this 'feature' was fixed in the 2.2 serial driver.
When I first tried running redhat on my alpha, I didn't know that there was
a cludge and you had to use setserial .. spd_vhi to get the higher baud rates.
So I looked at the driver, determined that the baudrate bits were defined in
a slightly different order than x86 linux, and just 'fixed' the serial driver.
At the time I sent a copy of my changes to redhat.

It seemed like a poor solution, in that now the serial driver had to have
ifdef's for alpha in it, rather than being generic. However it was sure
easier than changing the definition of the baud rate bits and having to
recompile every program that knows about serial ioctls.
