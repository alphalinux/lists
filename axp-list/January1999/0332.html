I do not recall seeing other complaints so I wonder if this is only
me or others share a similar experience. dhcpcd-0.70-2.alpha.rpm,
as included in RH 5.2 distribution, seems to be broken. In any case -
we could not get a connection going when using it does not matter what
we tried. After reverting back to dhcpcd-0.65-5 from 5.1 distribution
everything was happy on the first attempt and works ever since when we
are reactivating this connection. Does anybody has an evidence to
the contrary? Possible a resolutions is a simple as recompiling with
a sane set of headers but I did not try yet.

BTW - a bug report filed with Red Hat was rejected because (hold to your
hats, as you are going to learn new and wondrous things) they "would have
to install a cable modem to test a fix". One wonders why Red Hat bothered
to include in their distribution also a dhcp server. Smells to me that
somebody abused some controlled substance - too many penguin mints maybe?
