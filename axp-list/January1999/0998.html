Hi Jim;
        Thought I'd get back to you now that the dust storm from your post has
settled a bit (but be expecting some of the spawned conversations / debates to
rage on for a while.).
        The battle lines between the Intels & the Alphas is nearly as much fun
as any other.. :)
        I saw lots of interesting answers out there, but none that really
answered your question. (which is pretty normal for this list. ). So...

        First off, Alphas are deffinately not for everybody. They do have a
niche, but not as your sole box. The bottom line is: The software just ain't
out there for it yet. If you're after a primary workstation, stick with an
Intel.
        I do have an Alpha, and I use it every day. What for? It's my firewall
/ mail hub / etc... It does all the nasty background tasks that used to burn
clock cycles on my Intel box, thus freeing up the Intel for real work (3D stuff
mostly...). Beyond that, I find my Alpha to be wayy to slow for any practicle
use (I have an AlphaStation 200 4/233 ).
        The only reason I keep it, is that it does what I have it doing very
well, and it was dirt cheap. This frees up the rest of my systems for fun
stuff....
        Anyway... Good luck with it. If I were you, I would strongly consider
off-loading it, and picking up a good Intel.

        Ric
