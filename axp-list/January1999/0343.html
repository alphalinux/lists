On Thu, 7 Jan 1999, Brian E.W. Wood wrote:

> > Is this likely to be a problem with the external hardware, or the multia
> > itself? Should I buy a "new" Multia from cpumicromart?
>
> I had a lot of problems with external scsi devices on a Multia until I got an
> active terminator and started using cables that were all high quality and all
> from the same manufacturer (Amphenol in this case).
>
> The Multia seems to be very sensitive to cabling/terminations.

        I second that. A certain scsi chain (CD and tape) that worked on
an old 486 with an even older adaptec would hang my multia when accessing
the tape. It happened to be a termination problem.

        More recently, my multia started hanging unexpectedly after a few
hours of uptime; sometimes it didn't boot, and hangs got more and more
frequent. Eventually it wouldn't boot again anymore. I seemed to remember
someone mentioning bad power connectors / cables a LONG ago in this list,
or maybe on or maybe on linux-alpha@vger, so out of despair I changed the internal
power connectors and cables and now it works flawlessly.

> If that doesn't work, try sacrificing *two* goats.

        And remember to do that in a room with arcane symbols painted
with penguin blood on the walls. Never fails.
