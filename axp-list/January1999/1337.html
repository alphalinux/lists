At 8:34 AM -0500 1/25/99, rich wrote:
>your boot disk MUST be "rawwrite" and not a fat disk... You do have
>an option --- go to the second console and mount it by hand with
>a -t msdos... However, it is better to simply make any future
>boot disks ex2fs by using rawwrite instead of copy. I know
>I have the same problem, and since I was building 30 of these
>things, well it makes sense to do it right instead of kludging
>it..

When I was installing 5.2 on my AS200, I found that MILO doesn't like the
rawritten disk, and the installer doesn't like the FAT disk. I ended up
making four disks - the MILO floppy, a FAT disk with the avanti kernel
renamed to vmlinux.gz, the ramdisk, and a rawritten disk with the avanti
kernel.

Then, when it asked for the "boot disk," I gave it the rawritten one, NOT
the FAT. It works.
