On Wed, Jan 27, 1999 at 07:43:39PM -0500, Bret Orsburn wrote:
> Is there no way to link a .o file compiled
> for Digital UNIX with .o files compiled for Linux and the Linux standard
> libraries?

No.

> I see that that would require a high degree of binary compatibility between
> the systems, but I had the impression that that had been an objective of the
> Alpha Linux port.

No. The objective was binary compatibility at the kernel interface.
That is something that is useful in trying to bootstrap a system,
and is handy now and then for running Netscape and the like.

Compatibility at the libc level is much harder, as the API that must
be replicated is much more expansive.
