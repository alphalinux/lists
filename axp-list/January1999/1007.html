On Mon, 18 Jan 1999, Jim W. Howard wrote:

>only to find out that I have
>an extremely reliable system for which there are no decent browsers
>available (IHMO) and no Frontpage extensions, and seems half of the apps
>I want to run aren't ported to alpha yet.

Hmm .. Yeah, I imagine this is a problem for a lot of folks. As I said in
a recent message, my favorite browser is Lynx.. so I'm golden on jus
about any sort of UNIX platform in the universe. Pays to have simple
tastes, I guess.

>To make a long story short, I'd like to know what you folks out there
>who are HAPPY with your Alphas do with them.

At this point, code. I bought my UDB in order to have an Alpha to port my
software to .. on the assumption that after the porting was done, I'd
be getting a more muscular machine for actual work. Oddly enough, the
porting easier than I thought it would, and the bottom fell out of my
budget due to the death and subsequent dismemberment of one of our
servers. So, I have to wait to get my grown-up Alpha :(

As for me, .. I'm a realist. I don't like MS any more than the
rest of us, but when I need a box to run canned applications and stuff, I
will probably throw together a Windows machine an call it a day.

Now, luckily for me.. I don't need such machines too often. :) Math
(DSP, crypto and some visualization) and process control are the things
that I do, and Linux is the way to go for that. I run Applixware on my
Linux boxes (Intel and Alpha) for general purpose synthesis.. grab the
numbers out my programs, throw them into a spreadsheet, write up a bit
of explanation and send it up to my boss or my professor.. then I can
go play.

Occasionally I actually fire up Lynx while doing such work..

On my notebook, however, I use Windows .. no math or process control on
this puppy .. just applications. Of course, the fact that Linux (XFree,
really) doesn't support my video chipset in 16 bpp mode, nor my sound
hardware helps push me to Windows easily.

> I'll probably sell mine to
>that sector, and buy myself an Intel.

Well, FWIW, Mozilla will come along and be stable for Alpha .. if you want
to wait. But, if your main interests are web browsing and document markup,
yeah .. Intel is probably your best bet, preferrably with Linux.

I guess its good to be a geek.. you could set me down at a machine with
just 8 leds for output and 32 or so switches for input to the bus line
and that uses the tapping of a radio operator's code key for the clock and
I'd happily code it to do something :)

hey .. wow .. thats actually a really cool idea. I guess its time to pick
up another 8051. Wait till my grandfather finds me using his code key
for THAT. He'll be proud. ;)
