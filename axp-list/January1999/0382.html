>>> Richard Henderson said:
> On Fri, Jan 08, 1999 at 09:37:00AM +0800, Ringo LI wrote:
> > Is any body get any luck to run the Redhat Linux on an AS1000A with the
> > suggest image, Noritake (suggested by Redhat manual).

> Are you using the right one -- noritake vs noritake-primo?

With RH 5.2, unfortunately, it doesn't matter; both the NORITAKE kernels
were compiled *without* the SRM console option... :-(

Check out the kernels/configs/noritake-s* files on the CD.

I thought I reported this months ago, but I still see no errata or new
kernels... :-\

> But, fwiw, I never did get the original RH image to work on an
> AS800 (Noritake); I did get 2.1.121 or somesuch to work though.

Actually, AS800 is known internally as CORELLE and comes only as 5/xxx.
NORTIAKE is AS1000A, and can be 4/xxx or 5/xxx (PRIMO). Also part of the
NORITAKE family is AS600A, known as ALCOR-PRIMO which is also 5/xxx only.
All have EISA bus, IIRC, and differing numbers of PCI slots, builtin
devices, etc.

And something that might be worth trying: the dp264-s.gz and rawhide-s.gz
kernels on the 5.2 CD are based on 2.1.120, IIRC, and are actually
"generic", so they (are supposed to) run on any platform, regardless of SRM
or ARC/AlphaBIOS console.
