Your /usr partition is hosed as you have previously indicated. Attempting
to boot is failing. IMHO you have 2 choices. Use a rescue floppy or try
adding init=/bin/bash to your boot command. I just did this upon rebooting
after upgrading to 5.2 on my LX164.

Too bad I didn't know about the -S as all I really wanted was init=1 which
didn't work. BTW, for any editing you must do with init=/bin/bash I had
to exec sync to get the modified file back to disk and I wound up with
some minor corruption that fsck dropped me into bash to fix. Bummer!
