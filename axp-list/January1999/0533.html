> I installed a aha1505 controller (that came with a scanner) in my
> alpha box but have a hard time getting this thing to work. After
> disabeling PnP using a Intel box and giving the driver the right
> options (irq, io etc) the card is reconized...
> Alas, compiled in the kernel (axp-2.0.35) the scsi bus keeps resetting
> after a cold boot. However using a warm boot (only back to milo, not
> the Apha-BIOS) the kernel panics. The kernel panics also when using
> the aha152x as a module:
>
> Panic: insw: memory not short aligned
> in swapper tast - not syncing
>
> Is the ah152x driver not working on the alpha at all, or is there

Sorry, I don't believe that that driver (aha152x) has been ported to Alpha.

I took a look at it, maybe a year or more ago, and decided against trying;
it's a true ISA card, and needs to DMA only to the low 16MB of memory. This
was among other problems, one of which I think you encountered.

Does your Alpha box already have a SCSI controller? If so, why not use it for
the scanner as well?

Otherwise, I'd suggest trying to find an old NCR 810 PCI SCSI controller,
which is very well supported.

Good luck.
