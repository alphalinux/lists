Jochen Kuepper wrote:
>
> On Mit, 06 Jan 1999 Stephen Williams wrote:
> >I've been running Linux 2.2.0-pre4 for a few days now, *without* *modules*
> >and it is holding up pretty well. This is very good, indeed. It seems that
> >if I have a module loaded, any module, it will arrange to deadlock or
> >otherwise lock tight.
> >
> >So it seems that Linux 2.2.0 isn't stable with loadable modules, at least
> >not on the PC164. Any evidence to the contrary?
>
> I actually just tried to load a module - simply tells me there's an unresolved
> symbol __bzero. Haven't invsetigated. That's on AlphaStation 500 (Alcor).

Which module? Troubles with __bzero? This is really weird. Are you
mixing versioned and non-versioned support.

Yes, I do have an evidence to the contrary althouth I was experimenting
with 2.2.0pre4-ac1 and not just simply 2.2.0pre4. I do have modules
for network cards, various file systems (iso9660, fat, vfat) support
for ecoff and em86, other various things. I tried on two different
Alphas (eb64+ and Ruffian). I was loading and unloading modules,
trying diffrent drivers on networks cards (tulip and de4x5) and
everything seems to be stable and runs just dandy. Even both network
drivers worked (surprise, as I had problems with de4x5 for quite
a while) althouth I had to force a media choice on UX by passing
options to tulip module.

On one machine I have modutils upgraded to 2.1.121 - like they should
be - and there modules autoload and can be made to unload. :-)
The other box requires an explicit use of insmod, but this is what
is to be expected.
