Greetings,
        The best indicator of whether a particular architecture/operating
system/system is a worthwhile purchase is to use the specific application
on that platform and compare it to others. Never trust benchmarks which
claim to test the speed of the "system". One thing I always give SGI
credit for is recognizing this fact, and improving overall system
performance by reducing bottlenecks in non-processor components like
memory, disk, graphics subsystem, etc.
        Even the oft-quoted spec95int/spec95fp ratings for the Alpha
processors are miconstrued by people who rely on marketing buzzwords
rather than technical knowledge. Yes, you may be able to achieve
spec95foobar on this or that processor, but only if you use the vendor's
operating system and optimized compilers.
