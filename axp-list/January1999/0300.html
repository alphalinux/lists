Hello,

        I'm not very happy right now. My Alpha just machine checked again,
and this is the third time in 4 weeks. The first and third time are almost
identical (shown below). The second time was while I was away on vacation
and I called my Apt. manager and asked her to reset my box (all my mail
bounced and I was kicked from lists (including this one) during the
downtime). I have no logs for that machine check*.

Here are the two that I have:

APECS machine check:
  vector=0x670 la_ptr=0xfffffc0000006000
  pc=0x200001ed8b8 size=0x2e8 procoffset=0x110 sysoffset 0x1a0
  expected 0 DCSR 0xffffffff80280005 PEAR 0xffffffff80000000
...bunch of numbers...

In the third check, the only difference is the program counter:

  pc=0x1200ac270

The hardware is an AlphaStation 200 4/233 I bought in May from onsale. I
have never seen this problem until recently. However... the software is
kernel 2.1.131 with my fix to keep PPP from panicking.

The machine checks seem to happen at interrupt time; in the case of the
third one, on a keystroke composing an email. This is a major problem for
me. If anyone can help, I would be ever grateful! If moving to another
kernel will help, I'm game.

* Now, one gripe about this machine check business: I see that all the
output from the machine check is coming from printk() within the kernel.
So we must have some limited ability to continue executing instructions
after my favorite mishap. Why then do we allow the machine to simply halt?
At least, that's what happens to mine. The disks are not synched, the
machine check printk()'s never make it to syslog and the box doesn't
reboot. Is is possible for precautions to be taken to safely recover from
this event?

thanks,
-bp
