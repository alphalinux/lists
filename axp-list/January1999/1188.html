As I said before this system had no hard drive. It came in a digital box
inside a platic bag which had a (digital, I think) sticker that had not
been broken.

A little more info: I found a "Part Listing" in the zip lock bag the
manuals and stuff came in.

The list is a preprinted form with the |d|i|g|i|t|a|l| logo. Printed with
what looks to be a dot martix printer, is the date '05-Sep-1996' and
'Time: 09:26:20'. In the first column it says "AIRF" by itself (whatever
that means).

Then the rest of the form which has:

Model/Part No. Description Qty.
Shipped
BAG. BAG. 1
17-00606-02 PWR CORD,TERM SVT 125V 75IN BN 1
EK-PCDTA-II ALPHASTATION 200 SERIES INSTAL 1
IK-PCDTA-UI ALPHASTATION 200 SERIES USER I 1
EK-PCDTT-CL DIGITAL ALPHASTATION 200 SERIE 1
EK-SFFIS-UG OSF FACTORY INSTALLED SOFTWARE 1

and at the bottom it has "Weight (lbs.) 1.14"

There is also a long number (serial???) on the very bottom left side which
I won't quote so no one else tries to use it.

In the same zip lock bag it has a small 8.5" x 5.5" booklet that is titled
"Factory-Installed Software Quick Reference"

and on the side - sideways:
"Digital UNIX
Formerly DEC OSF/1"

"EK-SFFIS-UG. F01"
