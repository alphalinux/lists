If are searching for leftovers after possible "unwanted guests" and
you happen to have Red Hat based system reasonably maintained do
not forget about 'rpm --verify -a' command. You will collect a lot
of interesting information really fast.

True, not everything you have on your box may be registered with rpm
system and an attacker **might** have replaced your rpm binaries
and indices in /var/lib/rpm/ but this is worth to try; especially
if you are executing from a safe medium like CD. On the other hand,
if you were truly cracked then your 'ls', 'ps' and 'syslogd' are
already new ones. :-) This is a standard 'modus operandi'.
