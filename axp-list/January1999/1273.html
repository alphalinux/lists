On Sat, 23 Jan 1999, Adam Wiggins wrote:

>
> Hi all,
>
> I've been salivating over getting an alpha workstation for some years
> now. My work is frequently heavily math intensive, especially with
> floating point. Now I'm very seriously considering making the switch, and
> have been investigating prices and options from various Linux-friendly
> vendors. Looking at the prices, I'm starting to wonder if it is really
> "worth it". I could build a 400 mhz K6-based system w/ SCSI and lots of
> RAM for probably $1000. The basic Alpha system I'm looking at (533 mhz
> 21164) is probably going to cost me well over double that. However, I've
> become increasingly disillusioned with the shody design of x86 (I cut my
> programming teath on the 68000 and the Sparc, which at least had reasonbly
> clean designs). In particular we've had prototype Katmais (that's
> the Intel Pentium III) in the office for about 6 months now, and they
> are a joke. It's time for x86 to leave my desktop.

I can only comment on the cost side, having researched moderately and
finally finding an affordable motherboard to replace my PII. I ordered a
PC164SX board + 21164PC/533 for a mere US$400 (www.maxvision.com) and found
the same combo at another vendor (www.dcginc.com) for $475. I expect (from
benchmarks and testimonials) to get PII-350'ish integer performance, and I
also get to abandon x86 and go 64-bit (if I don't count my UDB :().
