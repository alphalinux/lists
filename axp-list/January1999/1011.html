                  Hey, what's going on here ???

I knew that the fastest fortran executables could be obtained by compiling
them on a DU box, and take them to the AXP. That's the way I do. My code
is a quantum mechanical too. I knew that g77 on AXP is far slower.
Is that going to change ???

BTW, I've seen that some programs compiled with the portland group fortran
on linux intel boxes fly. The speedup relative to g77 is something
between 20% and 50% (code dependent). With some codes, a 400MHz PII is
comparable to a 600 AXP. True.
My particular code g77 compiled on the Intel 400MHz is 70% of a 533 AXP.
I suppose that using portland would fill the gap.

I asked portland guys if they are going to do something for AXP.
Unfortunately, they are not working on it, and they will not in the near
future.

Provocative opinion: AXP are more expensive than Intel. Less HW is
supported, compiling fortran programs (if the DU way is the best solution)
requires a DU box for compiling and expensive DU licences to run them.
   ??? Still is AXP the best (speed+easy_life/price) ratio ???
