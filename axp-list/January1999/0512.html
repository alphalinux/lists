Hummm . . . IMAP -vs- POP3 services is hotly debated. Do you want to do
folders and other pleasantries or do you want to stick to simple
store-and-forward mail services? It's a matter of choice and a matter of
customer needs. Either will do the job. What's your preference?

How about trying out both and do some stress testing to see which works
well with Linux and your hardware. I can tell you that qpopper can take a
huge number of hits per minute and thrive. It's performance will soley
depend on the hardware. I used a HP 9000 D-350 (dual 101 Mhz) with a 20
GB RAID 5 system and it served 3,000 business users, sending monster
attachments, nicely. That same system also handled 170 majordomo mailing
lists, Xtacacs router authenication services, the primary domain ftp site,
the primary private DNS, 2 apache Intranet web sites, primary router
config storage and retrieval site, and more.
