On purely heuristic grounds, I would be inclined to believe that it IS a
flawed philosophy. I, for one, am not as worried about the script
kiddies. The majority of scripts are well known (hence their use) and
they are best handled with good-basic security practices. The people who
most worry me are hard-core crackers who know as much about Alphas as
they do Intels because they target places who USE them. While the
ability to become "a bit sloppy" in security practices is at first
tempting, it is what crackers are hoping for. That little bit of slack
in respose to scripties is a wide open back door, inviting the real
threat to take you to school.
