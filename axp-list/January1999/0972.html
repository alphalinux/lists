I think there are three uses for a Linux/Alpha system:

1) You need very fast floating point computation and your
   450Mhz Pentium II just is not cutting it. So you spend
   the bucks and buy a fast 533Mhz Alpha.

2) You are the maintainer of some GPL'd software package
   and want to test your package on various different systems.
   In this case a $99.00 Multia would do fine but you'd
   also want to pick up a few other CPU and OS types as
   well.

3) You like weird toys and can afford them or you stumbled
   onto an Alpha for cheap.

I fall into #2 and #3.
