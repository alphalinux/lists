I am another one of those "bought my AlphaStation dirt-cheap at an web
auction joint" kind of person. I bought my Alpha initially to run Linux to
practice for the new Macintosh OS, which is also UNIX flavored. I had my
AlphaStation on hold until I finished a few important exams. However, once I
was through with the exams I bought RAM, keyboard, mouse, hard-disk, and
CD-ROM. After bugging this list briefly I updated my firmware and here I am
with a live Alpha running RH 5.2.
I ran across KDE and installed it and now I have a fairly consistent desktop
environment. With the help of a person from this list I got an MP3 player
and Netscape. And, with the help of a developer I got a visual interface for
my MP3 player. I compiled WebMaker and a few CGIs, and with Apache I have
now all my webpages being served (and edited) from my Alpha.
Dang it! I even compiled a kernel to have my sound board working. I ordered
ApplixWare to see whether I can start doing some writing with my Alpha.

Considering that this list's subscribers and contributors know programming
all orders of magnitude above what I know, my little feats are indeed pretty
pathetic. However, these recent going back to Intel statements prompted me
to write this. It is true that my faster Macintosh is far faster than my
AlphaStation for graphic tasks and overall daily use, but Apache and all the
other network daemons kick arse. Even GIMP, being slower than Photoshop at
comparable tasks, has some advantages over Photoshop and complements it
well.

In summary, I don't regret have bought the Alpha. Actually, I am curious to
see how one of those fast Alphas would perform... ;^)
