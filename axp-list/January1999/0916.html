I guess that is where I disagree with you. I'm a technology lover, so I
got myself a Alpha. But how many of us are there? We're a minority in
the general population. If people like us want money so that we get nice
paychecks and can continue to develop cool technology, we need to appeal
to the mass market. I have no doubt that Alpha is a excellent platform
for Linux, I couldn't agree with you more. But for development to
continue, and to thrive, money is required. And you won't get that money
just by appealing to technology savy people. I agree, we need optimized
compilers, documentations, etc... but for all that to happen, you have
to hire people to do it. And for a business to survive, and justify
hiring those people, they need to justify a profitable business market.
We can all see from the likes of AOL, Microsoft, Intel, that appealing
to the mass market is key to a successful business in technology... even
if the technology isn't that good, appeal to the mass market can still
result in a good business(reference to AOL and Microsoft). That is why I
hope that a truely good technology like Alpha will be made to appeal to
the mass market... and for once, we'll have the best of both worlds,
good technology and popular appeal(hence profitable for businesses to
support).
