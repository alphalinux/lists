I think there are several problems; not just RedHat. It seems to me that
the Linux/AXP userbase has boomed beyond the rate of software to properly
support it. Many of the problems we are seeing here on this list (over and
over and over, such as ipfwadm, syslog, xntpd, aboot, XFree86, smbmount,
qt, etc etc) are not the fault of RedHat. Most are either: poor or
non-64bit clean programming, Linux kernel bugs, or Alpha-specific changes
in development tools (like compiler or binutils). We can't blame RedHat
for the problems, but we can blame them for not acknowledging them and/or
making efforts to see them resolved by the owner. In the case of the
ipfwadm bug, yeah, it's really old. I found the cause and the solution
months ago and posted it here and to RedHat.

I agree with Joachim on taking action. We need to come together a bit more
and start actively maintaining a list of known Linux/AXP bugs, regardless
of distribution. Each problem should have a date reported, symptom, and
fix (if it exists).

I would nominate the www.alphalinux.org website as a home base (it's so
very slick looking, you know!), if the operators do not object. Will
anyone volunteer to maintain such a web page? I do think it could help to
resolve some of our longstanding problems.
