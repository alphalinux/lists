Hi Jaap,

Ok.. so your tip worked great,.. I just had to recompile libffm as a
shared library... however, I noticed that libffm gets loaded ALL the
time, even if a program doesn't need libm/libffm... is this a good
thing? or is the penalty of loading an extra lib all the time
insignificant?

D.

>
>That's a pretty cool tip.. I never knew you could do that. By the way,
>do you know where in the system I can read about (either doc or source
>code) how that environment variable is used?
>
>Thanks.
>
>D.
>
>
>>Fro those of you that want the extra speed of libffm and do not want
>>to recompile large packages (octave for example takes a lot of time to
>>recompile) setting a LD_PRELOAD environent does the trick.
>>
>>I use "setenv LD_PRELOAD /usr/local/lib/libffm.so.1" (csh obviously)
>>
>>if i now do a ldd on a file, libffm gets listed first. I did some
tests
>>and it works fine on my system. To be honest i learned this from the
>>bladeenc author after I asked him is he could link his (binary-only)
>>program with the faster libs. Using this trick bladeenc runs about 25%
>>faster in my box.
