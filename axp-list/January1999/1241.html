Adam Wiggins wrote:

[massive snip]
> My needs include web browsing (Netscape or the equivilent), The GIMP, 3D
> tools (such as Blender and OpenGL acceleration), and running MUD server(s).
> None of this stuff is necessarily bound to any specific platform; hopefully
> with the Xi, Metro-X, and SuSE Permedia-accelerated X servers on the way,
> 3D acceleartion will finally be a reality for the Alpha.

MetroX has Alpha/Linux native X servers for quite some time now, and
IMHO,
they kick ass in the speed department... (I'm running a Fire GL 1000
Pro).
They also sell a native Alpha OpenGL kit, but that one is pretty
expensive.
Since a short while, there is an Alpha native Blender available :)
Gimp, Netscape and MUD all run perfectly fine on Alpha for quite some
time.

> There are also some small
> niceties missing - for example, AGP video, which makes a big difference in
> screen update time when panning around a nearly full-screen image in the Gimp
> in my prefered screen mode, 1280x1024x[16|24].

AGP doesn't make a big difference and you can bet that even my humble
21164a 366MHz Alpha kicks my 21164a 366MHz Alpha kicks my PII@450MHz's butt seriously when it comes
to heavy Gimping and other gfx stuff :)

AGP is only good (opinions vary) for reading cached textures and
stuff directly from main memory (which is /way/ slower than accessing
the videocard's /native/ memory) and this is only used in games...

> So - is it worth it? Will I see enough of a speedup from a (say) PII-266
> to a 21164-533? What about from a PII-450 to a 21164-533? Better yet, what's
> going to make the difference in speed - would it be better to go up in mhz
> (say, to 600 or more) or to get a motherboard with more cache? I'm already
> planning on UW-SCSI and at least 256 megs of RAM, of course.

Depends on what you want to do, for having a good time, lot's of
tweaking and the highest coolness factor on earth > get Alpha.
To be square, dull and "everything just works" > get x86 :)
