> You are comparing apples and oranges. Read my last post regarding
> benchmarking. It really, really (really, folks!) depends on what your
> application is. For floating-point intensive code, the Alpha will win
> hands down when pitted against a P2/266 - especially on large matrix
> manipulations or bitonic searches. But compared to a P2/450, I can't
> really say. Don't have one running Linux to compare it to.

Well, at the risk of being irrelevant, I'll answer that by throwing out a data
point:

I have an SX164 (533MHz) with 128MB of RAM and SCSI, and a buddy of mine has a
P2 450 with 256MB of RAM and Ultra EIDE, both with Red Hat 5.2. After several
comparisons, including a few benchmarks (such as "whetstone", rc5des, and Linux
kernel compilations :) we have reached the opinion that my box is somewhat
slower than his on integer/miscellaneous stuff, depending on what you're doing.
"Somewhat" is between 15% and 30%.

On floating point, my box is about 25% - 30% faster, fairly reliably. (Well,
with libffm anyway. Without libffm, we're about the same.)

This is, of course, highly non-scientific and subjectively arbitrary, but it's
Good Enough For Me(TM). There are times when I wish I'd gone Intel, and times
when I just stand up and cheer over how cool the Alpha is. I don't keep track
of which outnumbers which. :)
