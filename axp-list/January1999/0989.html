Hmm... I can see that certain hacks being architecture dependant, but
what does little-endian have to do with it? I mean, I know byte ordering
is significant in the tcp/ip implementation, but AFAIK, Linux/Alpha is
little-endian anyway... I don't know about Digital Unix though?

However, without proof, and mere speculation, I would think that
software ported over from Intel to Alpha are likely to have a higher
risk of having security holes. Although, the fact that more people have
Intels than Alphas, probably correlates to more security holes being
discovered on Intel than on Alpha. However, if many experienced
hackers/security professionals concentrated on finding holes in Alpha
systems, I would tend to think that they'd find equally as many holes,
if not more.

I guess my point is, security through obscurity is not a philosophy I
would go by... at least not these days... when people can find bargain
deals on systems of most architectures... I got my deals on systems of most architectures... I got my 21164a@500Mhz for
$500... cheaper than my most of my Intel machines.

D.
