> If one were to build a Alpha machine for use as a graphics machine, what
> components would one use that are currently available on the market??
        Most of my work involves number crunching, although the system I
just purchased should be able to carry it's weight graphics-wise.
After all, the video system is the business end of most modern systems.
Chose a Permedia2-based card, specifically the Diamond FireGL 1000 Pro with
8M of vid ram. Depending on your timeframe of purchase, I would wait until
the release of XiG's hardware-accelerated OpenGL server and/or
Metrolink's Extreme3D product to see which chipsets the final versions
support. (www.metrolink.com, www.xig.com)
> I assume CPU would be 21264... ideally
        Push for a large cache. Depending on your budget, I would even
go for 4M or 8M of cache. You'll find that when manipulating large,
complex datasets and images, the large cache will be most appreciated.
A good compromise might be 4M of cache and 256-512M of ram.
> But what graphics card? What software is available that would use
> specialized hardware? And just for fun, what monitor?
        XiG's product and Metrolink's Extreme3D will support some
high-end ($700-$1500) cards, if money is no object. As far as monitors
go, I plan to order a Hitachi 753 on Monday - earlier models of the same
series have won numerous awards. I think your "bro", Peter, will agree with
me on this recommendation (he's thinking of buying the same thing :-)
If money is no object, go for a 21" Viewsonic or Sony (500PS?).
(search out Dejanews for links to various reviews)
> How would the ideal Alpha based graphics machine (exclude clustering)
> compare with a SGI?
        How would you compare apples to watermelons? My 500a, with the
Permedia2 card running at Permedia2 card running at 1024x768@32bpp with XFree86, runs the
OpenGL "ideas" demo at nearly the same framerate as our SGI O2's.
> I am about to get a few more alphas ... either to build a graphics
> machine or to sell off.... so I'd appreciate any recommendations, and
> most of all references... Thanks
        Well, I can only speak from my own experience. Hopefully my
experience with graphics will only get better with the hardware accelerated
OpenGL servers.

Good luck,
