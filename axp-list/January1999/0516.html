And amidst all of this, lays the pain...
        The simple fact is, that what used to be a very simple thing with the
older versions of Win95 (pre-OSR2) now requires special handling, and is no
longer a simple matter.
        You "can" use regestry hacks to get windoze to talk to samba correctly,
but that requires "Regestry Hacks", and I'm pretty much opposed to doing that.
Why? Because if you blow up a box, or have to reload it for any reason (gee,
that's unusual for a Windoze box (heh heh)), you need to remember all the hacks
you put into it.
        Or, you can modify the SAMBA Server end to accept the encrypted
passwords from the Windoze box. Again, requiring hacking files, and making more
modifications. And again, requiring more steps to rebuild a blown server. There
are also complications with adding new users... You need to straighten out
their passwords... Another thing to remember to do.

        All of this adds up to pain. Pain to install & configure, pain to
maintain. On a small scale this is tolerable, but on a larger scale.. Do any of
you really want to do all of that for 500+ boxes? I wouldn't! And to honest, it
would drive my decisions in setting up a large distributed network.

        So anyway... The situation is workable, but not as easily as it should
be. I suggest that the samba developers need to look at this, and supply a more
workable answer.
