> > There was one guy who posted here a few weeks ago about running
> > a loop which simply computed sqrt(). The 450MHz Pentium II
> > outperformed the 533MHz Alpha.
        If you read what I wrote, the statement compared a P2/266 to
the 533Mhz axp for floating point intense code. Unless you actually meant
to imply that the 266Mhz P2 beats out the Alpha at math ops...
 
> Big deal! Pentium has sqrt in silicon while Alpha, as RISC, in an
> external library. Is your main application calculating square root
> tables? Then you better watch if results are not only fast but also
> correct on the whole covered range. :-)
        Exactly. RISC processors rely on the intelligence of compilers to
generate optmizied code, whereas the x86 architecture can take crappy code
and still make it perform decently. One more reason Digital's compilers
under AlphaLinux make for a good value and a worthwhile purchase. Hello,
DEC, are you listening? :-)
