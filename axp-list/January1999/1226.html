I also would not in any way get rid of my Alphas. Ever since I found the
platform I have stuck with it. I like Alphas so much I choose to
make it my business. And as one of the smaller Alpha OEM's I find the
platform to be the best out of all I have tired (x86, PPC, m86k, SPARC,
and others). My biggest problem is I forget too easily and have to ask
about the little stuff. But when it comes to hand assembling an Alpha
pice by pice from the ground up, I love it! I have had fewer problems with
any Alpha I have ever used then with any x86 system. Applix for Alpha
Linux is a great product and I use it daily since the beta version.

What do I do with them? Anything I want to!

What do I see as a way to make the platform even better? A compiler flag
to make 32-bit binaries from 32-bit code.

JMHO,
