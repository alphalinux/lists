>I've only just saved all my hard earned pennies and got myself a UX4

>RedHat as my primary operating system.

No, you are not. There is "unsupported" as in "we are happy and not
changing it" and there is "unsupported" as in "bugger off".

In this case they will not be making any more UX boards ( there are
thousands in the warehouses) and no further updates are needed.

For that matter the LX falls in the same category. The only reason they are
making a few thousand more is that they are popular for clusters, and for
running UNIX. As there is SRM firmware available for the UX ( UX is not a
DEC/Copaq part but a Deskstation/Samsung/API part) the only choice is the
LX. SRM is needed to run UNIX, or to support remote booting over a network
via bootp. Still, they are not making _that_ big a quantity of LX, so it is
not much different..
In either case the chips used to make these boards, both UX and LX are
pretty well out of production, and there will be no further large runs of
them.

All the new development time energy and $$ are going into the DP264 and the
SP264. Some effort in the RX, as it is supposed to be a "low cost" Alpha.
We shall see...
