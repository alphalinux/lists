All you should _HAVE_ to do is the :
boot fd0:/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 load_ramdisk=1
It will prompt you by itself..

To recap ( I am in the middle of doing this as I am testing a modified
install set):
The first disk is from the /milo/images directory on the CDROM. Pick the
one to suit your machine type.
The second is from the /images directory on the CDROM. Again, pick the
right name to match your computer type.
The third is the ramdisk.img file from the /images directory.
All disks are normally made with rawrite on a DOS box, or dd on a Linux box.

The first one you created is a DOS filesystem, and contains linload.exe and
MILO.
You boot with it, and when at the MILO prompt, you put in the second floppy
(which contains the kernel), and execute your boot command ( above).
 You are then prompted for the third disk containing the ramdisk image.
Once that is loaded, you get to the graphic setup/install screens..
