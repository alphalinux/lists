Good Morning,

I'm new to alpha's, but a seasoned amateur of Linux (four and half months
or so).

I couldn't get any of the experimental kernel's to compile on this SX164
(533 MHZ), so I just got 2.0.36, compiled it without trouble. It has the
EIDE controller in it, with a Maxtor 7 gig HD. The computer was is sort
of a proprietary machine, not direct from DEC (it's a long story, don't
ask ;)

 After the initial re-boot with this new kernel, I was swamped with
messages of Unexpected interrupts for interrupts 3, 4, 14, and 15 (ttys01,
ttys02, ide0, and ide1 respectively). These messages popped up as the
kernel attempted to initialize the floppy, the mouse, and the HD, and the
cdrom. This didn't seem to affect anything much, but I couldn't mount my
floppy drive (it would just sit there with the light turned on). Anyway,
I got rid of the Unexpected Interrupt messages by adding the lines
"ide0=0x1f0,0x3f6,14 ide1=0x170,0x376,15" to the milo boot options
under the WinNT Alpha Bios. So Kernel boots clean (still
get ONE unexpected message right after mouse on interrupt 14??). If it
helps, I'm still using the original milo which came on the redhat cd's
(built under 2.0.35, would that have anything to do with it?)

BUT, "mount /dev/fd0 /mnt/floppy" still begins to access the floppy and
never finishes (I let it go 2 hours before removing the disk). Funny
thing, when using the Redhat alpha kernel (2.0.35, came with redhat 5.2),
it works fine. Other notes, the drive is a 2.88M Mitsumi model d359m3.
At least that's what the kernel says ("fd0 is 2.88M; Floppy drive is Post
1991).

For kernel compiling options, I did select the normal floppy thing.

I would like to continue using the self-compiled kernel (I get sound, and
best of all it's CUSTOMIZED;)

Suggestions, comments, help?

Sincerely,

Jano
