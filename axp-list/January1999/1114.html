>
> I've noticed this in fvwm and windowmaker (haven't tried any other ones).
>
> Crank up X. Open an X term. Put your mouse over it, and do a
> ctrl-right_mouse_button. A resize window should open up, but all I see
> is a white box. Note that the functions that appear there DO still work,
> you just can't see them.

Both background and font colours in these menus are configurable.
Is it possible that you, or Red Hat, configured them as white-on-white
or something similar? If you do not have that in your own .Xdefaults
then foreground and background may be in two different places
(like in /usr/X11R6/lib/X11/app-defaults/XTerm and in your window
manager configuration files).

I do not use window manager or account defaults from Red Hat and I do
not recall ever seeing something like that.

There is also a long standing bug in .Xdefaults processing. A comment
I have there probably explains it the best:

! WARNING!! - if your Xclients file ($HOME/.Xclients or a system one
! in /etc/X11/xinit/Xclients) does not explicitly calls 'xrdb -load'
! or 'xrdb -merge', then this file is not run through
! '/lib/cpp -traditional' and results may be not what you expect.
