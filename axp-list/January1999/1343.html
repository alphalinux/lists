        The benefits of Origin scalability may be overshadowed by the raw
horsepower per node of an Alpha cluster. This has always be a paradox
when determining target equipment for floating-point intense algorithmic
analyses or other computations: Single Fast or Several Slow. The cluster
of 12 Alphas strung together by 100Tx ethernet (or SCSI, if you're a Real
Programmer) will always win the cost-performance analysis compared to a
12-processor SGI or other MP machine. Plus, expanding your virtual
parallel computer is as simple as plugging another compute node into the
hub with an AlphaCluster (hey, a new term) solution - upgrading the Origin
is costly to say the least.
        Anyhow, I hope that clears up that. No idea how you extrapolated
that I have such high regards for the Origin; again, it is simply what we
have available to work with. I purchased an Alpha so I can have the
capability to do fp-intense things at home and not rely completely on the
work-provided solution.
