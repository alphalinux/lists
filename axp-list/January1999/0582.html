This seems to be one of favourites recently on linux-kernel list. :-)

Edit your /etc/nsswitch file to reflect reality so you wil not try to
resolve unknown user names over NIS or NIS+. This will be **slow**
unless you have NIS. Why this was not biting before I have no idea.
Red Hat supplied default 'nsswitch' assumes that you talk to a NIS
server.

Another option is to make your machine "Linus friendly" by adding
his account with his uid and gid. :-) This will, obviously, not help
with tar archives created by somebody else.
