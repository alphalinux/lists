I must add my 2 cents: we have a collection of 30 - AS300/266's with 2
NIC's per
and 128 ram and a 4 gig drive. They are scattered throughout the company
and act
as the following:

1. firewalls (internet and departmental)
2. LDAP servers
3. DNS servers (primary and cacheing)
4. Proxies (with apache)
5. IDS using "Shadow"
6. Security and Central Log hosts
7. File servers
8. print servers
9. Email/Spam firewalls for frontending exchange hubs.
10. Intranet www servers.
11. One is my personal workstation.

In general they perform well, stay up, require little maint or attention,
allow us to
redirect and manage logs centrally. They have greatly reduced the WAN
requirements
by positioning the proxies across slower 56K links. The internal firewall
is great, so we
keep better tabs on the employees. Bottom line, they are great workhorses
and
we got a good deal on them when we bought them all at once.

We also use them for development and testing, but enough people have mentioned
that already.

cheers
