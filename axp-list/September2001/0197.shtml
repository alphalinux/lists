Part of the reason may be that they have never been accepted as a 'main
stream' computer. Alpha's have always been the machine to use for a fairly
specialized task, not just as a normal desktop box. You typically can't
justify the cost and the power usage for an overblown word processor.

Plus, most linux distros can't move enough copies (that are actually
bought) for Alpha to justify the engineering costs to their investors.
Intel is a different animal. Everyone has one of those and you can sell
lots of copies of a distro for that because everyone and their brother has
one.

CPQ hasn't killed support for Tru64 and VMS on Alpha yet because it's the
only platform that will currently run those OS's. If x86 would actually
run them at this point, I'm sure that they'd be winding down support now
too on them because there isn't as much of a cost justification except in
the HPTC market.

Kurt
