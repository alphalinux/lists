On Mon, Oct 15, 2001 at 12:07:22PM -0400, Uncle George wrote:
> Jay Estabrook wrote:
>
> > Alpha Linux does its own setting up of PCI bus resources; in the 2.2
> > kernels, if booted from SRM, we saved away the original PCI bus
> > settings, and restored them during shutdown. This doesn't happen under
> > the 2.4 kernels, and some SRM consoles are more sensitive to the
> > changed settings than others.
>
> Gee, isnt this dangerous. I would presume that u save the settings
> because you want to change the settings. It would seem that if u
> pulled the plug on the kernel ( turn off power, hit the reset switch
> ) your SRM is ( about to be ) confused ? Or is the SRM confused when
> transfer of control happens from the kernel to the SRM, and the SRM
> does not recognize the environment that it once knew.

Well, SRM doesn't save it's settings, or get them from NVRAM; ie it's
not like ISA/EISA configuration info. It rebuilds PCI settings every
time it powers on. So "pulling the plug" won't confuse it.

However, once it *is* running, it doesn't expect the settings to
change out from underneath it (T64 and VMS don't do it :-). Linux
does, and when exiting from kernel to SRM during shutdown, then it
really *does* get confused. I believe the mchecks or PCI errors and
such are caused when SRM tries to use either its own registers or its
data from memory that now no longer accurately reflect the PCI
configuration.
