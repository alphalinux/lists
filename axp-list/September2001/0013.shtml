On Thu, 6 Sep 2001, William H. Magill wrote:
> To date, I have still not seen or heard ANYTHING from Intel implying that
> they intend to change the design of the IA64 to make use of Alpha
> technology

compaq has never said that the architecture of IA64 will be changing
either. some people not speaking for compaq have suggested that intel
will incorporate alpha architectural features into IA64. however that is
wishful thinking. i've heard no indication from anyone that intel plans
to directly use any of the alpha technology they have licensed. really,
they just wanted (read: badly needed) the alpha engineers in order to
produce a fast implementation of the IA64 architecture. they bothered to
license the technology so that the ex-alpha engineers would be free to
reuse any good aspects of it in their new design. the alpha-inspired IA64
is expected to come out at about the same time as EV8 would have (not for
a number of years yet). in the mean time the industry will suffer through
several lackluster implementations of the IPF.

> -- only from the Compaq folks... who want us to belive that
> everything they told us about Alpha before was wrong and that we should
> belive them now.

a lot of people are saying a lot of things, especially with HP on top of
it all. anybody who claims to know what is really going to happen is flat
out wrong, imho.

one thing is almost certain however. whatever does transpire will be bad
for alpha linux :(

 -ross, who is going to wait and see (and is speaking only for himself :)
