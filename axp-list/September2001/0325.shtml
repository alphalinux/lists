More interesting stuff from the people at theinquirer.net. The article at
http://www.theinquirer.net/01110105.htm explains how Intel has been useing
VAX systems to run software that makes sure their own processors don't suck.
The funniest quote is "That process is now finished, but unfortunately by
the terms of the agreement, Compaq is unable to shout out to the world that
the Alpha chip is so good that it's even used to make Pentium 4s and
Merceds."

Nothing earth shattering just a slightly funny read for those of us that
enjoy irony.

As far as any legal action on recent Compaq announcements that I suggested
recently, I did talk to a lawyer from a large law firm. It is her opinion
that the only legal action against Compaq would be breach of contract
between Compaq and it's suppliers or customers. (i.e. If you don't have a
contract with Q then there doesn't appear to be a case for you) This of
course is contingent on the contract and what is in it what provisions etc..
As far as announcements about Alpha living on and other recent press
releases that in retrospect seem to be bald face lies? I am told that as
long as these annoucements are made in "Good faith" and proving bad faith is
extremely difficult, there is little legal action one can take there are
exceptions but none seem to be relevant (as far as I could see). FYI
