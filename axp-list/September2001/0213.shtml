As it was already noted in this thread Red Hat "rawhide" is still full
of _current_ rpms for Alpha so, if you will choose go this way, nothing
really prevents you from putting together a machine with "the latest
stuff" and this even without firing up your compiler. If an "official
distribution" will go missing you will not get packaging, printed
manuals (although they are on-line in a machine readable form), a box on
a store shelf and "full support" - whatever that may be; in practical
terms likely you would have fish for updates in "rawhide" stuff or do
'rpm --rebuild ...' on source rpms by yourself but this for things you
put on your own as I do not recall hearing that there will no Alpha
updates for existing and maintained distributions. Yes, indeed, this
will require an extra work on your part but commercial entities, like
Red Hat, also have to make and are free to do their choices.

You also have other options; like joininig a Debian effort, for example.

Last but not least with an exception of some commercial offerings
original sources are still available and Linux kernel has Alpha support
for as long as there are people willing to put in a necessary work. So
while your hardware is still maintainable and suitable for your uses I
fail to see a ban on running it. The real problem, of course, is
bungling by Compaq but you did not appear to talk about that.
