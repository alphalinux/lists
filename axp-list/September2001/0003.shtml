as long as the licenses still hold, there shouldn't be much of an issue
there. The SRMs for the different platforms are developed for that platform.
API/ Samsung came up with its own SRMs for the UP1xxx series. It was based
on another SRM from CPQ, but they had to change quite a bit of it due to the
chipset differences between the platforms. You can almost always use older
firmware to boot linux, as it is not very dependent on the SRM that you are
running. VMS, on the other hand, is tied fairly closely into SRM and that
may be more of an issue. but again, when an SRM comes out it is designed to
run all the current software/ hardware that's available and supported for
the platform.

HP not writing firmware will mainly handicap just the future releases of
'true' CPQ os's.

On the other note, AMD could license the technology from CPQ but who knows
what the price is that they will get and if it is an advantage to them.
Plus, AMD already licenses the ev6 bus to use as its interconnect between
the CPU and its chipsets.
