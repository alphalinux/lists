Greg Lindahl wrote:

>On the initial low end systems that used RDRAM, they were shipped with
>the 3rd RDRAM connector covered for exactly this reason.
>
>However, you said you removed half the memory and it didn't go
>away. So that isn't your problem.

I'm using 850 GB boards, which have 4 slots. They were integrated for
me before I got them, so I don't know if they were covered.

After removing half the memory, 3 of the 4 systems ran without hanging,
but one still hung up. So I can believe that one may have some HW
problems. However, on the other 3, I've swapped memory in and out, and
the symptoms are the same; if they have 2GB (all 4 slots filled), they
will hang, while any combination of simms in just the first 2 slots (1GB)
would let the systems run the benchmark. Bus loading problem or bad
memory?
