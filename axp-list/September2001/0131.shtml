On Mon, Oct 15, 2001 at 01:18:25PM +0200, Heid Oliver wrote:
>
> I recently switched from ARC/Milo to SRM on my PC164, and the system runs
> nicely under 2.4.12. But when I shutdown, I get a "machine check error 670"
> at SRM level. That in itself wouldn't be too painful, but the SRM settings
> (boot_file etc.) get lost during the necessary power cycling.
> Is there anything I can do (besides going back to ARC/Milo)? From previous
> posting I found that a "machine check 670" means flaky memory, but why only
> in SRM during a halt/reboot?

This is one of the things we had in 2.2.x kernels, that didn't get ported
to 2.4.

Alpha Linux does its own setting up of PCI bus resources; in the 2.2
kernels, if booted from SRM, we saved away the original PCI bus
settings, and restored them during shutdown. This doesn't happen under
the 2.4 kernels, and some SRM consoles are more sensitive to the
changed settings than others.

It's not just PC164 that is so sensitive; I've had similar problems on
AS250 and AS500. Luckily, all the EV6 boxes seem more resilient, but that
doesn't make it any easier for folks with older ones... :-\

So, until someone has some time to look into doing this port, you'll
either have to put up with it, or go back to MILO.

Sorry...
