On Thu, Sep 06, 2001 at 03:44:32PM -0700, Eugene Chu wrote:

> I'm using 850 GB boards, which have 4 slots. They were integrated for
> me before I got them, so I don't know if they were covered.

The example I was giving wasn't the P4, it was the i820 or something
like that, which only has 1 channel. The i850, if I recall correctly,
has 2 channels, so the bus loading is quite a different issue.

> After removing half the memory, 3 of the 4 systems ran without hanging,
> but one still hung up. So I can believe that one may have some HW
> problems. However, on the other 3, I've swapped memory in and out, and
> the symptoms are the same; if they have 2GB (all 4 slots filled), they
> will hang, while any combination of simms in just the first 2 slots (1GB)
> would let the systems run the benchmark. Bus loading problem or bad
> memory?

I don't know, you haven't said if you attempted to swap intelligently
to isolate particular bad chips or not. If you simply remove 1/2 of
the chips and fewer systems hang, that could be bad chips. If all the
systems got better, it could be bus loading, but they didn't all get
better.

BTW, this is one reason you might want to buy a complete system
instead of assembling out of parts. A vendor which really adds value
will test the system, and figure out that kind of thing before you
receive it.
