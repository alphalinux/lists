>> OK, there ARE no winners in this mess..
>
> well maybe Intel. It got alpha technology from CPQ, and a bunch of
> development effort on Itanium from HP. Now since both are merging....
> whoohoo! only one company to deal with and one stop shopping for high
> performance chip design and a major part of the itanium development team.

Especially if you look at it from the point of view:
A) Intel has eliminated competition from the Alpha
B) Intel has knocked AMD's competive position for a loop.

CPQ was a big AMD buyer just recently. I doubt that neither HP nor the
"We're all Intel all the way" announcements from CPQ will tolerate that in
the future.

So it's looking like performace choices are now limited to IBM's PowerPC
(formerly known as Power) and Intel's IA64.

To date, I have still not seen or heard ANYTHING from Intel implying that
they intend to change the design of the IA64 to make use of Alpha
technology -- only from the Compaq folks... who want us to belive that
everything they told us about Alpha before was wrong and that we should
belive them now.

Next week at CETS2001 in Anaheim should be interesting.
