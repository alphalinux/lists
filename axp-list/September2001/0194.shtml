The announcement by Compaq on the end of production of Alphas
by 2003 has created a lot of misconception. People have taken it
to mean the end of Alphas systems all together right now.

Kodak RSI has announced the end of support on Alphas on their
graphical display product IDL. Likewise, one of our regular system suppliers
has also removed the storage and peripherals items
for Alphas from their on-line catalogue.

When I first started using Redhat (Linux), I understood
one main attraction of this operating system, apart
being free, was to revitalise obsolete PCs (pre i286 stuffs).

So why a different view is taken on Alphas, especially when
the Alpha systems are much resilient and hence longer life
than most PCs?

Peter
