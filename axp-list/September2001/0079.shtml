On Fri, Sep 14, 2001 at 01:51:53PM -0600, James Fowler wrote:
> In my experience you have to have both FAT and EXT2 (with BSD labels)
> support too. I had to have a
> little FAT partiton at the beginning of the disk with an /etc /boot
> (something elses too). In order to get aboot to work and boot my kernel.

This is the case when you are booting from ARC, and derivatives, where
firmware does not know anything about disklabels and ext2. But he is
using SRM console and 'aboot' - which is a different situation.

Not that FAT, or better yet VFAT, support is not useful then in your
kernel (it may be in a form of a module). You may need, from time time,
to look at those pesky FAT floppies. :-)
