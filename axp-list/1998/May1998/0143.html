Hello:

<sigh> Why doesn't Digital know their own components?

On Thu, 28 May 1998, Bill Roman wrote:

> XLs and XLTs use 36 bit ("true parity") 70 ns. fast page mode (not EDO)
> memory. XLs have six SIMM sockets, and SIMMs must be installed in
> matching pairs; XLTs have eight sockets, and require matching sets of four.

This is a direct contradiction to what 1-800-DIGITAL told me -- the guy I
spoke to had absolutely no idea whether the memory should be parity or
non-parity. He also said it takes 33-bit memory. (He did agree with you
that it was non-EDO)

The funny thing is, the user's guide says, "The system requires either 33
or 36-bit SIMMs that have an access time of 70ns." <sigh>

The Digital guy said they retired the product, but I can go through a
Digital PC centre...if I buy it from Digital, it will work. (Yeah, and
the price will be 3 times as much!) :-)

> These machines are rumored to be a bit finicky about memory; SIMMs that
> appear to meet the above specifications may not actually work.

Okay -- perhaps I'll dredge up some more Intel boxes and try out some
other RAM.

> By the way, your numbers don't add up. You could *add* two 16 MB SIMMs to
> bring your system up to 64 MB -- not *replace* your existing 32 MB.

Oops!! What I meant was, 4 x 16Mb or 2 x 32Mb SIMMs; whatever is
cheapest. :-) By the way, right now I have 4 x 8Mb for 32Mb total -- do
you think I can mix memory by adding in my 2 16's or 2 32's? It's a shame
not to be able to use the memory I already have...

(I just remember doing that on an Intel box with horrific results
happening over several weeks to the file systems!)
