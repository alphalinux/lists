Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> With regards to your message at 10:07 PM 05-07-98 -0500, Jiann-Ming Su.
> Where you stated:
> >Will the Matrox Millenium (not II) and Adaptec 2940 (not W or UW) work
> >with the LX and SX boards? I know this is a bit off topic, but the
> >digital's home page showed that not all possible combinations will work --
> >especially for NT. I'm wondering if anybody has had any experience pro
> >or con with this equipment. A friend of mine and I are considering switching
> >to Alpha and would like to keep some of the old hardware. Thanks.
> >
> They will, but I seriously suggest considering planning for a better SCSI
> controller in the long run. Narrow, non-ultra disks are 8 times slower than
> state-of-the-art Ultra-2 products.
> If what you plan to do is VERY non-disk-intensive, then fine.

That's a bit harsh. Unless you're into RAID and use 10K RPM disks,
the 2940 will work reasonably well. Notice they are trying to minimize
costs of switching to Alpha. That probably means the disks aren't
high end. For most people, myself included, a narrow SCSI is sufficient
for most desktop work. Not all, of course. I use mostly wides for
SCSI but I run one Alpha with two narrow cards in it and it works
well, no complaints and a PPro with a single narrow SCSI and it
too does a reasonable job. Depending on your disks, you get from
4-7MB/second. If you need a server, then go with a better SCSI
card, preferably UW. How many folks out there have U2W cards
and have them working?? I've been thinking about getting one
since it allows longer cabling.
