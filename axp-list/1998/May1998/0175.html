With regards to your message at 11:15 AM 05-27-98 -0400, Steve Frampton.
Where you stated:
>Just thought I'd pass along the following message.
>
<<<snip>>>
>I asked Corel if then will support Linux/Alpha in the future. This
>was the response:
>
>Olaf,
>
>I'm sorry we did not specify the platform. We will be sure to include the
>platform in all future postings.
>
>As soon as we have enough requests for and can prove the number of users on
>Linux for Alpha, PPC and others then we will port to those platforms.
>
>If you have any questions then please let me know. Thank you for your
>interest in Corel WordPerfect for Linux.
>
>Best regards,
>
>Brad Caldwell
>Corel WordPerfect for UNIX/Linux
<<<snip>>>
>So guys, its up to YOU to mail him to show them that Alpha is alive.
Fair enough, so I did what you suggested, and as a dealer / VAR /
integrator of Alpha based Linux systems we contacted Mr. Caldwell.

Here is his response, my response, and his last repsonse:

Brad:
Maurice,

Thanks for your comments. We like the Alpha platform and we have
several boxes here.
We have WordPerfect ported to 64 bit as we offer CWP 7 for Digital UNIX
today.
<<<snip>>>
There are many platforms and our code is portable so we must chose which
platforms we will support. There is not a fixed number for market size
which automatically constitutes acceptance for a WP port. We continue
to evaluate all platforms.

Maurice:
> Hopefully this might not be too difficult for your firm to accomplish.
> We are mainly a Linux shop, and as there is already a WP8 distribution
> announced for Linux, we are hoping that an Alpha Linux release might be
> forthcoming..

Brad:
It isn't the difficulty but the resource bandwidth. Corel is now
wanting a StrongArm port and we don't yet have all the Intel Linux products
ready. So to add an additional port is a juggling act. We will see how
things go with Intel Linux and grow from there.

So, that's where they are at, I guess. It's up to us to motivate them to
think otherwise.
