Quoting Jim Bourne (jbourne@island.net):
>
> We were running into this same problem with syslogd, but only when it remote
> logs. Our news spooler would become completely unusable after only a few
> hours, same error message etc. Once set to log only locally, it stopped.

I'm not running any remote syslogds. One thing that is possibly of interest
though, is that it generally seems to happen around 45 minutes after the hour.
At 30 minutes past the hour, I have a crontab that runs a program called
"newsx" to suck Usenet articles off of my ISP and a friend's news server. I
have no idea how long that takes, but I think it's around 15 minutes. At the
end of the newsx, I start nntpsend to send all the news articles to the
appropriate other servers. I wonder if it's something that nntpsend does
that's driving syslogd insane?

The smoking gun:
grep 'Bad file descriptor' /var/log/message*
