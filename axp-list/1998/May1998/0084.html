Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> Interesting. I have run Symbios53C875 based controllers (Intraserver 3140U)
> on LX533/600 and on PC164-500 machines and got a sustained 14MB/sec into a
> RAID array. On narrow/FAST SCSI disks I got about 2.4MB/sec, on wide 3.5,
> and on Ultra/Wide of 6.5.
> This is with large transfers, big enough to overflow any memory buffers or
> disk caches.
> On the other hand I got about 30 to 50% less on Adaptec 2940UW, and 50 to
> 65% less on 2940.
>

I think 2.4 is a little low:

hdparm -t /dev/sda
/dev/sda:
Timing buffered disk reads: 16 MB in 4.40 seconds = 4.64
MB/sec
Mind you, there was quite a bit of FTP traffic (~30 users) using it at
the time I ran the hdparm on the disk, so I am guessing the number is
higher on a pure test scenario. The disk is a 5400 rpm quantum fireball
ST Ultra-SCSI2 drive on a NCR 83c825 based controller.

> Yes, you are right.
> Personally I think the SX is a waste of time.

Must be nice to have your bankroll.

> Especially one with IDE disks.

Then what is the point of Linux on ALpha??? In my mind, part of the
beauty of Linux is that it will run on anything, and run cheaply. If we
all had the bucks to buy high-end hardware, we would all (ok probably
not all :) )
go out and buy DEC Unix.

> As for office software, it will happen IF there are enough users.
> To accomplish that there needs to be a form of user registration to prove
> the numbers, which would require support and co-operation of the Alpha
> vendors.

Well, I personally like my dual boot machine with NT and FX!32 to run
office97 (ver 1.3 runs almost perfect). There's my office software.

I look at linux alpha like this: I am a Systems Analyst; I am like a
garage mechanic in that when I have a task to accomplish, I goto my
virtual 'toolbox' and select the proper tool for the task at hand;
Alpha/NT is better suited to some tasks (Office 97 and no more IMO) than
Linux is, and vice versa.

> Secondly there would need to be a solid (stock) distribution of Linux for
> it.
> As this does not now exist, I can only hope for big improvements in Red Hat
> 5.1 and other supposedly imminent releases of Alpha Linux.

I think we all need to participate in getting some better organisation
to how linux/alpha patches, driver development, and information (like
the FAQ) are created and disseminated.

I would like to once again volunteer to work on the Alpha FAQ (for the
umpteenth time) as I believe that it has quite a bit of room for
improvement.
