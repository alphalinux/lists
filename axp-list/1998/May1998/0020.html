Maurice Hilarius wrote:
>
>
> Interesting. I have run Symbios53C875 based controllers (Intraserver 3140U)
> on LX533/600 and on PC164-500 machines and got a sustained 14MB/sec into a
> RAID array. On narrow/FAST SCSI disks I got about 2.4MB/sec, on wide 3.5,
> and on Ultra/Wide of 6.5.
> This is with large transfers, big enough to overflow any memory buffers or
> disk caches.
> On the other hand I got about 30 to 50% less on Adaptec 2940UW, and 50 to
> 65% less on 2940.
>

Must have something in your kernel config wrong. I always turn on
experimental stuff and use it if it relates to my HW config. I haven't
seen a SCSI disk slower than 4MB/sec for two years, and have had
Adaptecs
running under Linux for 3-4 years. Only in the past year have I
seriously
used Linux. My fastest UW drive is a 1" high WD 9GB. Peaks at 12.5
MB/sec
and averages 10MB/sec ++. Damn fast. I expect the HH 18GB drives are
a similar speed w/7200RPM. And for those with money to blow, the 10K
RPMs
must really fly. I've seen manufacturer info stating they guarantee
15MB/sec on them. Too expensive for me to justify though and I own
my company!!

> As for office software, it will happen IF there are enough users.
> To accomplish that there needs to be a form of user registration to prove
> the numbers, which would require support and co-operation of the Alpha
> vendors.

YUP. A chicken/egg thing. Who spends time on a product no one
wants to buy. Wish those folks would listen to this mailing list.
Not porting to Alpha Linux now will hurt them later when Merced
happens. Working out 64 bit bugs sooner than later.... I think I'll
have a good laugh when NT goes 64bit and the Wintel folks have to
clean up their act.... (also the Intel Linux folks but they will
already have a substantial portion of their work done for them
by the Alpha Linux crowd. Wonder if they'll say thank you??)

> Secondly there would need to be a solid (stock) distribution of Linux for
> it.
> As this does not now exist, I can only hope for big improvements in Red Hat
> 5.1 and other supposedly imminent releases of Alpha Linux.
>

RH5.0 is tolerably stable. Most problems are corrected with errata. The
big problem is past history. Since previous releases used the old C
library, all those binary RPM's are problems waiting to happen.

Debian is close. I've been listening to their alpha mailing list. Not
nearly as many people interested in Debian as RH though. Probably due
to the fact they haven't been able to cut a CD yet. So, they don't
have the history problem with their version and should have less
issues when they do release. My bet is soon, maybe 30 days. They
froze their tree just a bit ago and the Alpha guys are trying to
keep up with the Intel folks but most of the Debian Alpha maintainers
seem to have slow machines. Anyone got the $$ to get them a better
development system so they can keep up??
