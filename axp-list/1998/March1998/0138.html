On 5 Mar, Keith Baker wrote:
> Is it possiable to do standard (SVGA lib type) stuff on the TGA adapter in

I don't believe so.

> the UDBs? What other ways are there to do graphics... X is a little
> beyond my programming level right now.

Well, you could use a text-based graphics library such as ncurses,
slang, or newt. You could also use one of the zillions of widget sets
for X which make things a lot simpler than straight Xt programming. Qt,
Gtk+, XForms, and others are all available on Linux/Alpha (note that
you'll probably want to be running 4.2 if you want to use Qt, due to
the C++ trouble).
