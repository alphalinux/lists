Most cases have a location and mounting holes at the front of the case,
near the bottom.
I recommend a ball-bearing fan, as they last much longer.
The Sunon KD1208PTB3-6 is an example of a fan that works well for this kind
of thing.
In general, to get a quieter fan, you buy a larger one that runs at lower rpm.
However, you are constrained in most cases by what the enclosure is
prepared for.
Higher rpm= more noise, but also more airflow, so some compromise is
necessary.
The mdel I listed above is quiter than the fans in most power supplies, and
moves sufficient air to augment a small or medium sized case. For a full
tower (over 20" tall) you will need more, either faster or more than one fan.
