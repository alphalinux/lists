On 5 Mar, Joakim Roubert wrote:
> Like SCSI-3 and stuff (if I don't find one cheaper than a narrow
> SCSI-2...?)

Right. I wouldn't recommend getting any sort of wide drive, as they're
more expensive and you won't see any benefit on a narrow SCSI bus. Of
course if you manage to find a real bargain, then you could get it with
the intention of upgrading to a wide SCSI adapter later on.

>> The easiest thing to do would be to just find a narrow SCSI-2 HD, as
>> this is what the controller actually is.
>
> So, then I get most performance, right...?

It's not particularly a performance issue, but more one of cost. SCSI-2
devices are cheaper as they're slower than UltraSCSI.

> Ok.
> But It wouldn't be slower than a narrow SCSI-2...?
> (the nerrow SCSI-2 is cheaper, I reckon.....?)

No, UltraSCSI won't be slower (unless the drive is just inferior, but
it won't have anything to do with the SCSI revision). Each SCSI
revision increases the MHz of the SCSI bus (It's gone 5, 10, 20 as I
recall).
