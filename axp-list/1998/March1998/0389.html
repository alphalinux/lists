> With regards to your message at 02:14 AM 03-15-98 -0600, Wes Bauske. Where
> you stated:
> >Maurice W. Hilarius wrote:
> >
> >> With regards to your message at 12:04 AM 03-15-98 -0600, Wes Bauske. Where
> >> you stated:
> >> >As I said, the problem is very low level. The machine appears to
> notconfigure
> >> >at all. No boot screen ever appears. This is some sort
> >> >of low level PCI compliance problem. Nothing to do with the
> >> >milo/Linux drivers. When/if I get past this, I'll worry about
> >> >whether Linux recognizes it.
> >> I suspect that you have a card with no NVRAM, but a fixed PROM instead. The
> >> Alpha boards work properly with the Symbios 875 class chips, but seem to
> >> expect the NVRAM on the board. Without it they can't seem to set it up. I
> >> tried a card like this and it simply wouldn't address it at all.
> >> The other/better 875 implementations work well, and are noticable faster
> >> than a 2940 Adaptec. You should expect to pay about $160 for it.
> >>
> >
> >Maurice,
> >
> >Is there a specific chip to look for on the card that tells me if this
> >is true?
> Offhand I don't know, but the key question to ask is: "Does the card have
> NVRAM".
> Basically this means that at hardware boot time you can get at the cards
> settings and change the setup, turn on and off various options, etc. On
> AlphaBIOS based machines like the LX and SX when th machine first sees the
> BIOS of the SCSI card it gives you a message to "Press Ctrl-C for setup" or
> similar.

OK. I looked at the box again and it states the card has "serial NVRAM" for
user config and SCAM information so that seems to imply it should work.
This leads me back to the device probably trying to allocate two interrupts
for a single slot. Either that or the card is just broken. One interesting
thing on the card. There is a notation on the card for two pins that
have been removed that says "shr IRQ".

On your Intraserver combo card, does it allocate separate IRQ's for each
logical device or do they all share the same IRQ??

One other thing. The box specifies a PCI 2.1 compliant slot. Are the PC164LX
PCI 2.1 compliant??
