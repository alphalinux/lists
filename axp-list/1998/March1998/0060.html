With regards to your message at 12:17 PM 03-02-98 -0500, Greg Lindahl.
Where you stated:
>> In the meantime, I made the following unscientific test: I
>> had all these sig11 errors when building egcs-1.0.1. Well,
>> today I started from scratch; however, I left the box of the machine
>> open. (The airflow in this miditower box is a desaster...)
>
>That's a good test, although you may still want to re-seat your
>memory.
I DISAGREE!!
That is not a good test, and can cook equipment.
Think of your computer enclosure as a tube.
Now, the tube has a fan on one end, sucking aair out of the tube. So, the
tube has air moving though it at a certain velocity, carrying away heat.
If you leave the covers open, then no air is being pulled away from places
like the board, and only the power supply has any air flowing through it.

So, if in the case above this actually improved things, then the coolling
in his case is VERY bad. He needs much more airflow. A secondary fan at
minimum, at the fron of the case, pushing air in to the case.
Another reason for putting in a second fan is this:
If the only airflow is caused by a fan exhausting out htrough the power
supply, then the inside of the case is running air at lower pressure. So,
the floppy and CDROM drives are also acting as air intakes. Dust then is
drawn through them, and fuzzy stuff accumulates. NOT good for drives like
this. If you have a fan blowing in to the case with more airflow than the
exhaust fan blows it out, then the air going through these drive will blow
outwards, reducing contamination.
