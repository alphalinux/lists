At 03:07 PM 02-03-98 -0500, Jeff DeMaagd wrote:

>I am a somewhat experienced Alpha user, and I have a question about a
>system that I am considering purchasing:
>
>Digital Alpha XL300
>Can anyone comment on the quality and usability of this box? It will be a
>user station, although some sever use will happen too.

Well I have an XL300 as home as my only system, dual booting linux and NT.
The only difference is that I have 64Mb of memory, which I think is the
minimum. I've been so impressed with it (it's never crashed and is much
faster than the UDB it replaced) that I'm getting an XL366 for work as my
day to day system. The price is good and the performance more than adequate...

>I am considering selling all my x86 based stuff to buy this, so I have to
>be able to use this as something more than a toy.

I moved from Intel to Alpha nearly 3 years ago and I've never wanted an
Intel box. I must admit though that I don't do heavy development on my home
system (mainly web surfing, telnet access and understanding linux internals).
