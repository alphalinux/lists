> Christian Lefevre wrote:
> >
> > Hello,
> >
> > I ve got some problems getting my tekram dc390f (ultrawide dual channel) board
> > to work with my 164LX card. The card seems to be recognized in alphabios (it
> > appears as a 53C875 which is good), but it failed to detect the hardware
> > (Seagate AWK fast wide SCSI disks) connected to it. I checked the
> > terminations, tried with only one disk, it doesn t recognize anything. I
> > suspect the bios doesn t load at boot time, since I get no message for the
> > scsi controller (unlike for the graphic card).
>
> With the Adaptec SCSI card, I see messages about SCSI cards in a variety
> of places:
> 1) At boot when it initialises the BIOS
> 2) In AlphaBIOS when it tells me its initialising SCSI controllers
> 3) From MILO as it reinitialises the BIOSs
> 4) From MILO as it loads Linux
> 5) From Linux as it starts its SCSI stuff off.
> 6) In the output of cat /proc/pci
>
> I think your saying you don't see (1), but you do see (2) although not
> with a list of drives?
>
> I'd try and boot Linux off a floppy and see what it sees, in terms of boot
> time and possible cat /proc/pci and /proc/scsi etc.
> Linux should be fairly immune to BIOS screw ups so this should help you
> tie down whether its BIOS or SCSI related.
