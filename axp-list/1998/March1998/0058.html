>From the keyboard of Chris Saunderson
>
> Hi,
>
> After installing 5.0, I need to recompile my kernel to enable loop-back
> device support and generic SCSI device support. However, I'm running
> into a continual problem with gcc generating a sig 6 here:
>
> gcc -D__KERNEL__ -I/usr/src/linux/include -Wall -Wstrict-prototypes -g -O2 -pipe -mno-fp-regs -c FlashPoint.c -o FlashPoint.O
> cpp: output pipe has been closed
> gcc: Internal compiler error: program cc1 got fatal signal 6
> make[3]: *** [BusLogic.o] Error 1
> make[3]: Leaving directory `/usr/src/linux-2.0.30/drivers/scsi'
>
> Can someone shed some light on this?
>
> Thanks
>
I got this same error when compiling MESA an 3D OpenGL implimentation. I
got around it by removing "-O2" from the argument list. I tried it with just
"-O" and that didn't work either. It seems to be an optimization related
bug.

Try building FlashPoint.c by hand and then just continue with the rest of
the build with make. Or you could remove "-O2" from the CFLAGS but then the
rest of your kernel wouldn't be optimized.
