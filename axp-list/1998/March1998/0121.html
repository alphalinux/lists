On 5 Mar, Joakim Roubert wrote:
> I have a more or less standard AlphaStation 4/166 (avanti). I consider
> plugging in one more HD. I beleave there is fast scsi-2 in it (the
> standard controller), but I don't know. Anyone?

Yes, this is correct. I currently have the following drives in my
Mustang (the name for this particular Avanti):

Host: scsi0 Channel: 00 Id: 00 Lun: 00
Vendor: SEAGATE Model: ST52160N Rev: 0344
Type: Direct-Access ANSI SCSI revision: 02
Host: scsi0 Channel: 00 Id: 01 Lun: 00
Vendor: DEC Model: RZ28M (C) DEC Rev: 0616
Type: Direct-Access ANSI SCSI revision: 02

They're both ~2G, and perform well, altho the Seagate is the faster of
the two (The DEC is in reality an older-model Seagate with DEC
firmware).

> And, then, what HD:s can I connect to this; there are a lot of different
> scsi protocols (or what it's called) out there.....

You should be able to connect any SCSI drive with the proper adapters.
The easiest thing to do would be to just find a narrow SCSI-2 HD, as
this is what the controller actually is. Note that an UltraSCSI device
will simply operate at the SCSI-2 MHz'age.

I've also used a Quantum Fireball on a UDB with pretty good success. I
would recommend both brands as being fairly affordable and reliable.
