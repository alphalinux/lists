Does anybody knows what's a deal with isapnptools and glibc 2.0 (RH
5.0)?

I tried pnpdump in a attempt to get some Plug&Pray ethernet card
going. It trips on iopl() call. strace comes back with
mprotect(0x15555832000, 2097152, PROT_READ|PROT_WRITE) = -1 EFAULT (Bad address)
which indeed somehow does not look too healthy (2097152 is 0x200000 -
quite a sizeable chunk in the context).

There was some discussion about iopl() a while ago, which unfortunately
I do not remember too clearly. For an experiment I commented out
iopl() call. Now I am getting SIGSEGV from apecs_outb(), called
by _outb() which is used by initiate() function in pnpdump.
_outb() tries to write "alarm" character on a port 1.

Any sage advice, fixes, docs?

BTW - strace reminds me something. What are legal values which can
be used on the "other end" of /etc/alpha_systype link? Is this
documented somewhere? Is "system type" value from /proc/cpuinfo
a good bet in general? I understand that not for Alcor. Other cases?
