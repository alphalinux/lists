Ok, here is the deal. 'isapnptools' from RH 5.0 on Alpha simply do not
work. On the other hand, current isapnptools-1.13, as well a
distribution stuff fixed in manner similar to 1.13 NOT to use iopl
and only ioperm, do work just fine. You can find even source rpm
for that on ftp.redhat.com and mirrors in contrib.

One minor glitch - #include <sys/types.h> is missing which produces
a zillion of errors and a failed compilation. You may also want
to review Makefile options. This applies both to "distribution"
rpm and the current stuff. How a binary rpm in 5.0 was produced
is one of those mysteries of Universe. :-)

After all was said and done it turns out the the card in question
(ISA ne2000 lookalike) does not want to show up as Plug&Pray at all.
Does not matter what are settings - both on Intel and on Alpha.
I have some other PnP stuff on Intel and it does respond how
prescribed.

Luckily this card can be set up to a fixed address and IRQ with a help
of an MS-DOS program. In such state it will work on 164LX **provided**
Linux will be told on what IRQ and which port to find it. Scanning
always fails. An interesting thing is that the same card was
autodetected when used in the past with a Noname board. Different
ISA bridge, I guess.

With this happy ending,
Michal
