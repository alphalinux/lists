>>> "Rick Windham" said:
>
> The other thing the web site says is that "AlphaServers" were used and that
> is not correct either. They purchased the OEM systems from Carrera.

In the LJ article by Daryll Strauss, he says:

During the work on Titanic the facility had approximately
350 SGI CPUs, 200 DEC Alpha CPUs and 5 terabytes of disk all
connected by a 100Mbps or faster network.

and later says:

At this point the decision was made to purchase 160 433MHz DEC Alpha
systems from Carrera Computers of Newport Beach, California. Of those
160 machines, 105 of the machines are running Linux, the other 55 are
running NT. The machines are connected with 100Mbps Ethernet to each
other and to the rest of our facility.

Carrera's page says:

Digital Domain has 230 Carrera Alpha workstations installed
at the studio, 160 of which were dedicated for rendering.

And finally, the DEC pages say:

Digital Domain relied on the power of more than 200
Alpha processors running 24 hours a day for two straight months,
averaging an astounding 800 million computer instructions per second.

and this:

To complete this Golden Globe Award winning film, Digital Domain used
over 200 Carrera Computers [workstations based on Alpha technology],
working 24 hours a day from the beginning of July to the end of August.

and, again, this:

Furthermore, having purchased four quad-processor AlphaServer 4100
systems, they knew that Alpha speed was their only hope of making an
aggressive release date.

I don't believe there's anyplace where the claim is made that *only* our
servers were used. Unfortunately, neither is there anyplace where what our
servers *did* do is explicit... :-/

But I know I'm glad I didn't have to pay Digital Domain's electricity bill
that summer... ;-}
