On Thu, 12 Mar 1998, Philippe GIROLAMI wrote:

> I've been trying to tailor the kernel to suit my needs but I've run into
> serveral problems. I am using the SRPMS directory and rpm to get
> kernel-alpha-2.0.30-2. I applied the alpha-patches distributed by
> linking the "old" and "new" directory to the "linux-2.0.30" directory.

This you don't want to do. Go get kernel 2.0.33 and get the alpha patches
for 2.0.32 (unless there are some for 2.0.33) off of gatekeeper.dec.com.
Don't worry about symlinking or anything of that nature, just untar the
kernel source from /usr/src, then cd to /usr/src/linux and do "zcat
../alpha-patches* | patch -p1" (or wherever you happened to put them).

> i)make mrproper and make config always works fine.

Hmm - I've never done a 'mrproper'. make config is all you need. It
seems you can even do a 'make xconfig' _if_it's_the_first_time. After
that, xconfig doesn't work.
