On 12 Mar, Robb Romans wrote:
> I'm about to bite the bullet and buy a DEC Alpha machine (XL300 or so),
> but I am concerned about the amount of software available for it under
> Linux.

You might want to note that the ARC firmware is (C) Microsoft ;)

> How much of a constraint is this? I'm mainly interested in doing
> software development (undergrad CS student) and web page creation, but
> I'd like to take care of all the other daily tasks on this machine, too.
> By daily tasks I mean word processing (and I don't mean vi), email,
> photoshop and pagemaker-type stuff, and occasional spreadsheet use.
> Games are not a priority, but might be fun.

Software development: Simple, if you can program for UNIX. C, C++,
Objective C, FORTRAN, and lots of other languages are available.

Web pages: Go get nedit or a similar text editor and you're all set. If
you can't write HTML w/o some GUI app, then it's time to learn :)

Word processing: I'm not aware of any good programs for Linux/Alpha,
although you could try running the Linux/Intel version of StarOffice,
WordPerfect, or Applixware under em86 (no promises that any of them
will work)

E-mail: Lots to choose from. Emacs' various e-mail clients, pine, XF
Mail, TkRat, exmh, etc.

Images: The GIMP and XV are all you'll ever need, unless you want to do
raytracing. Then you'll also want povray.

Spreadsheet: There might be one or two spreadsheet apps out there. You
could also try the stuff that I mentioned for word processing.

Games: Quake! Quake II should be along at some point, as well.

IMHO, Linux is a much better platform to code for than Windows (and
the like); free compilers, and good ones at that, are just part of the
bargain.

> I understand that if I have the source code, I can just recompile it
> for Alpha (right?). But, what percentage of good programs out there give
> you the source code?

Well, more or less. Some are trivial, some are very nontrivial. A good
majority of programs available for Linux are distributed with their
source, although as a general rule no commercial software is.
