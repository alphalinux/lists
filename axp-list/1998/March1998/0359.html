The following is pasted with minor mods to make it more generic
from another message I wrote...
>
> Uh, how to explain. A Golomb ruler is a device or measuring method for
> generating measuring devices!?!?. The idea is to make the fewest
> possible 'marks' on the ruler and have the shortest possible ruler,
> yet still be able to measure the greatest possible number of
> unique lengths.
>
> A conventional ruler that is numbered 0 1 2 3 4 5 6... is redundant.
> If you wanted to measure out 3 units you could use the 0 3 space
> to measure, or the 1 4 space or the 2 5, the 3 6... As it stands, the
> ruler above can measure 1 a unit length, and 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 unit lengths.
>
> An equivalent golomb ruler would be marked 0 1 4 6, or in 'golomb diff'
> notation, 0 1 3 2. With this ruler, you can still measure:
>
> 0 1 for 1 unit,
> 4 6 for 2
> 1 4 for 3
> 0 4 for 4
> 1 6 for 5
> 0 6 for 6
>
> Another would be 0 2 5 6, just the mirror image. So, instead of
> a ruler that is 6 units long with 6 marks, you have only 4 marks.
>
> For any given length, there might or might not be an optimal
> solution. For instance, the best 7 unit long ruler is marked
> 0 1 3 7, but it cannot measure a distance of 5, yet it still
> can measure 6 different unique lengths. In this case, the 0 1
> 4 6 ruler is the optimal 4 mark ruler, since it has the same
> number of marks as the 0 1 3 7 ruler, and can measure the same
> number of unique distances as the ruler that is 7 units long,
> yet it is shorter(less expensive).
>
> This has influence in real world resource allocation. The theory
> behind it is used for determining the shortest possible path
> between a set of places, or how to arrange things that cover a
> certain area with a limited number of units.
>
> A personally important application is the design and construction
> of antenna arrays. If you have a certain size piece of land that
> you want to cover with antennae in the most optimal manner, you
> spread them out so that the antennae are spaced in a 'golomb'
> fashion. Or, if you can only afford to build X antennae, then
> you spread them out with spaces between based on these golomb
> rulers. This provides the optimal antenna arrangement for maximum
> information gathering from the phase of the radio signal with the
> fewest number of antennae, and requires the least amount of land
> for a given quality of reception. The units for separation would
> in this case be the wavelength of the radio waves.
>
> You have probably seen TV antennae with bars that are spaced at
> irregular intervals along a central rod, the theory behind it is
> similar. How long is the antenna going to be, how many bars, then
> space them out like this...and you'll get the best possible
> antenna within those design limits.
>
> I think that there's a link on that page that goes over the theory
