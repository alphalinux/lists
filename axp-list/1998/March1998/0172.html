On Sat, 7 Mar 1998, Ladislav Zejda wrote:

> I have the same problem, but it's absolutely regular:
>
> Kernels 2.0.32 and 2.0.33 always have it any time I boot them for all the time
> they are up, not just randomly 1-2 days, but continuously for more than 2 weeks
> (that's the time between two consecutive reboots).
>
> On other hand 2.0.30 is always fine (also about 2 weeks run time).

For me, 2.0.30 was also broken. Note: to get out from the 'fast' clock,
"soft" reboot (as in /sbin/reboot) doesn't help, because it actually just
drops you into Milo and reloads kernel, I had to do "hard" reboot, as in,
power down and up the machine...

I looked at relevant timing code, and couldn't find anything suspicious.

I made a small hack which slowed time 2x, but of course it didn't help
when machine's clock returned to "normal" speed.

I am pretty sure this is a hardware problem, because /sbin/clock is _also_
running double speed, and as far as I know, there's no humanly possible
way to "speed up" RTC chip in software.

I think its time DEC announced this as known defect.
