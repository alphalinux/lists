> > image is for?
>
> IIRC, it's for non-TGA based AlphaStation 200's. That is, if you've

Actually, it's cleverly encoded by the gnomes at Red Hat, but I know their
secret: the "-s" stands for SRM-bootable. That means you shouldn't boot it
from ARC/MILO.

Both avanti.img and avanti-s.img contain kernels that *should* support both
TGA and VGA consoles; Red Hat is (normally) very careful about that, and
should be made aware of the error if this is true...
