My experience is that fdisk is more predictable... Druid decided to put the
largest partitions in the first places.It is essential (to me) that the small
2 megs partition is put in first position (where the milo boot stuff goes)
and it be declared DOS format, and that the / partition is not too large (at
least when installing on a rather large disk). I installed on a 4 giga disk,
with 200 megs for /, 200 Megs for /var, 1 giga for /usr, 1 giga for /home,
and the rest for other uses...

I also have a 500 megs swap area (I have 512 Megs memory...)
Partitions 1 to 3 are primary, 4 is extended, and from there on, all otehr
partitions are inside the extended partition.

I initially installed stuff on an IDE disk, but this is terribly slow to
format, etc, compared to a SCSI. In a recent release of AXP boards, from
Digital, they state that the EIDE is for CDROM only... So I removed the ide
disk and the machine lives happily.
