> But sometimes, usually after weeks of uptime, the kernel seems to
> 'loose' processes, i.e. these things eat lots and lots of CPU-Time
> (at least they are reported to, the system is not slowed down much...)
> and I can't kill them. They (or the kernel rather?) don't give attention
> to any signal I would send them.

This is a frequent bug; processes are sleeping at negative priority in
the kernel waiting for something to finish that never will. These
processes can't be killed. If they are waiting for I/O, they count in
the load average. Sendmail's use of the load average is bogus; instead
it really wants to make sure that it isn't running 30 times.

Solution? Fix sendmail to use a smarter algorithm, and attempt to
figure out what is causing these processes to sleep waiting for
something to finish that never will.

Hack? Change the load average to just include processes waiting for
the CPU, not processes waiting for I/O. Solaris did the 2nd, although
I think Sun was threatening to change the algorithm back in 2.6 or
something, I haven't paid attention.
