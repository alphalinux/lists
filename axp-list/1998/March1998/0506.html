> 2nd problem is the Cirrus 5446 video card. I selected what seemed
> appropriate during install but when I try to startx, I get:
> (--)SVGA: PCI: CirrusLogic GD 5446 rev 69, Memory @ 0x02000000
>
> *** None of the configured devices were detected ***
>
> Anyone know how I can get this card to work ?

Most likely by replacing it with a card that is compatible with one of the
Alpha X servers...

Many of the X servers have to be modified somewhat in order to work happily in
the 64 bit Alpha environment. I gather that the amount of work needed with
the Cirrus Logic line would be fairly considerable, as it would involve quite
a lot of work with "twiddling bytes," which is not something that can be done
terribly efficiently with an Alpha. In other words, they could get something
written, but performance would "suck," which pretty much makes it pointless to
have spent money getting a high performance Alpha system in the first place.

You may want to select one of the more compatible cards, that generally
include:
- S3 and S3V
- Mach64
- Some portion of the Matrox line

You could FTP over to the XFree86.org ftp server, download the alpha binaries,
and then run them to see precisely what chip sets they purport to support.
That will be about the most accurate way of determining what could conceivably
be compatible...
