On Mon, 16 Mar 1998, Philippe GIROLAMI wrote:

> Hello,
>
> Has anyone had problems with "less" ?
> I'm running redhat 5.0 with kernel 2.0.33 (and patches). I've fully
> updated the system. Every now and then I notice some "less" processes
> left over from what I suppose are "man" commands. They eat up all the
> available CPU power so it's a bit annoying... don't tell me it's because I
> "ctrl-c" out of man pages ..?
> And yes I've looked for newer rpms of "less" but have not found none.

I have had the exact same problem on a P200 I run for my school.
The best I can track it down to is that people often do not know how to
exit man correctly, so instead they press CTRL-Z, and forget about it. I
can tell you that you are correct in relating it to 'man', and that it is
not a platform depedant issue it appears. As long as the user correctly
exits man with a 'q', then everything is fine. It is when they bail out,
that less goes haywire. It is pretty sad when four of these crazy 'less'
processes get going, as the P200 turns into a 386!!
Sorry I couldn't be of more help. If you ever find a solution for
this, I would be very interested in it! Thanks.
