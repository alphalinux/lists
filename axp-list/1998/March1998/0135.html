Zach Brown wrote:
>
> On Thu, 5 Mar 1998, Paul M. Hirsch wrote:
>
> > I beleive someone suggested a NetGear FA310TX card earlier. It is a
> > lovely card for Intel boxes, but only receives packets into long-aligned
> > buffers, which doesn't mix so well with the word-oriented Alpha.
>
> this is a good point, thanks for reminding me :). It basically means that
> the alpha can't have the tulip busmaster the packet right into the skb..
> it has to do a memory copy.. which is why I try to use eepros or 905s in
> my alpha. It won't really be an issue unless you're pushing lots and lots
> of data..

I move GB's with my PC164/PC164LX Alphas using Tulip cards in everything
and sustain 8.5-9.5MB/sec memory/memory without problems. I tweak the
Tulip.c
code to do the copy but it doesn't slow things down much. Your disks are
more likely to be a problem than the buffer copying. So far, I have only
one drive that can outrun the Tulip card (12MB/sec). You'll pay more for
just about any other type of 100B-TX card. Haven't seen anything close
to
the Netgear FA310TX card for price/performance($30). Has anyone tried to
turn on large packets with Tulip cards? Using 1500 bytes seems a bit
confining. 4KB would be more like what FDDI uses. I've seen other
adapters that can do 64KB. (IBM SP switch, HiPPI)
