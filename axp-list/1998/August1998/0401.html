Dave Gilbert wrote:
>
> Hi,
> 2.1.115 includes support for the CMD-646 chip used on both the
> Alpha LX164 boards and on the new UltraSparc/PCI systems however
> by default it can not be enabled.
>
> Go in and edit:
>
> drivers/block/Config.in and just under the line about NS87415 support
> add:
>
> bool ' CMD646 chipset support (EXPERIMENTAL)' CONFIG_BLK_DEV_CMD646
>
> reconfigure your kernel, compile, and sit back!
>
> OK - I've only tried a simple 'dd' based timing test
> but my IDE drive which used to do 1.9MB/s is now shifting in excess
> of 7MB/s and at only 9% CPU.
>
> Looking at the code it looks like if you have particular revisions
> of the CMD646 chip, (rev 5 I think) it may do UDMA - unfortunatly
> I only have rev 1.
>
> As I say I've only tried a simple test, so be careful - but
> at last it might mean that the LX is usable with IDE.
>
> Please join me in awarding the golden-penguin award to Dave Miller
> and Eddie Dost for this achievment.
>
> Wow!
>
> Dave
>
> P.S. Thinking about buying a nice new 16GB IDE drive.

This is good news!!

FWIW, I have two IBM 16.8 GB UDMA drives in Intel boxes
with DMA enabled and get 9-11MB/sec with sequential
accesses (my favorite kind in my line of work).
Test file sizes were 200MB+.
They're fast...
