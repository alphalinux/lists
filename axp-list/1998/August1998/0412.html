At 09:10 PM 8/8/98 -0600, you wrote:
>I am wondering if there is some life left in a old UDB. It of course
>has the built in 10T port and two PCMCIA slots. Is there any support
>for PCMCIA ethernet cards in Linux? if so, what makes are well
>supported?

Yes and several....:)
You can look at http://hyper.stanford.edu/~dhinds/pcmcia and get a good start.
I've been using a PCMCIA ethernet card from Eiger. It sucks in win95
because of bad/incompatabile drivers. But, it works on the UDB OK and it's
plugged into my cable modem...:) (When downloading from sunsite I can get
500-600kb/sec thru the thing so I can't complain.)

In the past, some of the stock pcmcia stuff wouldn't compile out of the
box. When last I did it, I got the stock stuff from above (3.0 I think) and
it compiled with no changes on RH5.0/axp. Haven't installed 5.1 so YMMV....

The only drawback I've seen so far after 3 years of using a UDB at home is
the power connector corrodes. I'm to the point of having to clean it every
few months with Tarn-X. Wish I could find a replacement part with gold
contacts or something. Other than that, the UDB operates as my web server,
web proxy, firewall, mail handler, and just about everything else. If
RealNetworks had a Linux/axp version of their server, I'd be using that too...
