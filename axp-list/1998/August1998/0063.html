> Well a lot depends on where you are located. Earthlink is a nation-wide ISP but
> a lot of local and regional ones offer ISDN. Bell Atlantic offers ISDN service
> as do most of the RBOCs. With the virtual line systems
> required to support the "56K" modems just about any modern ISP has the
> capability of supporting ISDN with little or no additional trouble.
>
> Also, be careful about spending money on an ISDN solution if something better
> is coming to your area soon. I live in New Jersey and we are supposed to be
> getting ADSL service next month, up to 7.1Mb. download speed and 400K
> upstream for *much* less than I am paying for ISDN now. Cable modems are
> another possibility although I have heard both good and bad things about them
> (would *you* let a cable installer take the cover off your computer?). Actually
> the best cable modems have ethernet interfaces so this shouldn't be necessary.
>
> Frame relay is also a possibility if you have an ISP in your LATA who supports
> it. For a full-time connection it can be cheaper than ISDN depending on where
> you live. There are also devices which allow you to use several modem lines
> in combination to achieve higher speeds. At first this seems a bad idea but if
> your ISDN service is time-tarriffed and voice lines are not it might be worth
> looking into.
>
> In short, be sure to explore all your options and try and find out not only
> what's available now but what will be in the near future. Remember that ISDN is
> 20-year-old technology and is relatively expensive.
>
