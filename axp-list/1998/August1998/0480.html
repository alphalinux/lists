Hi,

Let me start by saying that I am relatively new to Linux/Alpha.

I have recently installed Linux on one of four new Alpha systems. These are
PC164LX motherboards with a 533 MHz 21164a. The machines came with the
AlphaBIOS installed. After installing redhat-5.1, I decided to flash MILO
into the nvram, so I loaded fmu.gz. The layout started out like this:

block 0 - marked 'U' but contained the failsafe boot loader.
blocks 1-7 - AlphaBIOS
blocks 8-14 - marked 'U'
block 15 - AlphaBIOS settings

I first tried loading MILO starting at block 8. Unfortunately, I couldn't get
it to boot MILO with any setting of bootopt. I tried 130, 131, 136, 2, 3, and
8 (at least). I found that I could get it to boot MILO if I deleted the
AlphaBIOS and installed MILO at block 1. After that, I wanted to make sure
that I could reinstall the AlphaBIOS. I set the jumper on the motherboard for
the failsafe loader, put the AlphaBIOS disk in, and did "AlphaBIOS
Upgrade...". This all went fine, and I got back to where I started (see
above).

Here is where I screwed up. I went through the above procedure again, but
installed MILO at block zero. Unfortunately I didn't realize that that was
where the failsafe loader lived. I did get MILO installed, though.

So, now I can't reinstall the AlphaBIOS. I don't actually need it, since MILO
works fine for loading Linux. I would like to be able to reinstall it,
though. I would also like to have the failsafe loader available just in case
the MILO image gets corrupted somehow.

Does anyone have any ideas? I have three other identical machines. Is there
is a way to put the contents of the nvram on a disk and load it on the
affected machine?

Thanks,

Greg
