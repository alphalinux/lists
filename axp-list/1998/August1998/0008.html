On Fri, Jul 31, 1998 at 02:49:18PM -0500, Eric Hilton Jones wrote:
> I'm having some wierd problems with io on a lx164 533 machine, and I was
> thinking about trying some of the newer 2.1.xxx kernels to see how they
> perform. Whenever I compile any of the >.102 kernels I get the following
> error:
>
> gcc -D__KERNEL__ -I/usr/src/linux/include -Wall -Wstrict-prototypes -O2
> -fomit-frame-pointer -pipe -mno-fp-regs -ffixed-8 -Wno-uninitialized
> -Wa,-m21164a -DBWX_USABLE -DBWIO_ENABLED -c -o page_alloc.o page_alloc.c
> page_alloc.c: In function `free_pages':
> page_alloc.c:208: internal error--unrecognizable insn:
> (jump_insn 337 329 338 (return) -1 (nil)
> (nil))
> gcc: Internal compiler error: program cc1 got fatal signal 6

This is a bug in the HAIFA scheduler of egcs-1.0.x.
Try to build egcs-1.0.3a without haifa scheduler, use egcs-199807xx or
gcc-2.7.2.3. The gcc-2723 rpm can be found in the RH-5.0 tree ...
