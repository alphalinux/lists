Quoting Jonathan Vafai (jjv200@acf2.nyu.edu):
> When I launch mutt at the console, my screen goes blank.
>
> When I hit 'q' and quit, I get back my shell prompt, and I see the status
> bars scrolled up as they would normally look when you quit.
>
> Is this a problem related to my tga card and the kernel, or a curses
> problem? ..or a mutt problem?
>

Let me guess, you recently installed the ncurses "update" that appeared in the
Errata a week or so ago, right? This security fix version broke something
that a previous version fixed. The work-around is to put the following in
your .profile:

eval `resize`

That will make sure the environment variable "LINES" is set, so that it won't
scroll off the top of the screen. If you use a shell other than bash, your
mileage may vary.
