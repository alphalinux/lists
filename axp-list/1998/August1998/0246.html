Calvin Coghlan wrote:
> Two: Use of menuconfig and
> xconfig are very suspect on the alpha platform. In general are
> considered broken. It is recommended that people only use make config.
> There are some people running ....
> .... running make oldconfig, but YMMV.

The fastest safe method to modify your kernel configuration is to
**delete** all options you want to change from your .config file and
to run 'make oldconfig'. This is equivalent to running 'make config'
with that twist that all known options are left "as-is" and scrolled
through without asking any questions and for unknown you will be asked.
This includes all that stuff you just deleted and maybe some new things
if you changed your kernel version.

For one reason or another "use SRM?" option is never known. :-)

'make menuconfig' is suspect on every platform but sometimes it works.
'make xconfig' usually fares somewhat better but you should be careful.
There are advanced plans to redesign the whole mess but this will
not happen before 2.3.... series.
