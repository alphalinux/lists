On Thu, 6 Aug 1998, Graham Knopp wrote:
> Hello. I have just purchased an alpha that will be used as our
> departmental server. We have some ancient WYSE terminals that I can't
> seem to wean people off of. My question is, what sort of hardware will be
> required to support these dumb terminals? Someone mentioned a serial
> card, but could anyone be more specific than this? What sort of serial
> cards are supported with RH5.1/Alpha, if any?

How ancient? If they are Wyse 30's sneak in one day and smash the keyboard
:-). If they are Wyse 50's or up they have a nice VT100 mode which you
can use to at least be able to run Pine, SLRN, and Lynx (all of which run
fine on a VT100). Native Wyse mode is an abomination acceptable only
in that it was originally somebody else's abomination (specifically,
originally Wyse was a superset of Televideo's TVI 912 command set).

Somebody mentioned that Cyclades serial cards are supported under
Linux/Alpha. Please verify this with the Cyclades people. The Cyclades
serial server will certainly work, since it attaches to the network rather
than in a slot in the computer, as will most terminal servers and such
(the Computone Intelliserver has a very nice way of treating dumb
terminals, but otherwise is kind of klunky).
