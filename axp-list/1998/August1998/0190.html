** "Compaq Pledges Volume Sales for Alpha," **
June 12, "EE Times"

In the June 12 issue of "Electronic Times," Eckhard Pfeiffer made a
surprisingly strong statement of support for the Alpha microprocessor
and Digital's Unix operating system as part of a new corporate focus
on 64-bit computing.

"Compaq will support leadership in 64-bit computing by moving Alpha
and Digital Unix into volume platforms," Pfeiffer said in New York
today [June 12, 1998].

The full text of the article can be found:

http://www.eet.com/news/98/1012news/compaq.html

** "Pfeiffer: Alpha, Digital Unix to Give Compaq Strategic
Advantage," ** "PC Week," June 12

In the June 12 issue of "PC Week," Eckhard Pfeiffer discusses how
Compaq will take full advvantage of the technological superiority
of DIGITAL UNIX on Alpha.

"There is no good in acquiring something and then shutting it down,"
Pfeiffer said, adding that he is spending a lot of his time with some
of Digital's largest customers, allaying fears that the company will
terminate development of Digital technologies."

** "Compaq: Stabilized Alpha Is Best For High-End Apps," **
"InternetWeek," June 22

In the June 22 issue of "InternetWeek," Eckhard Pfeiffer discusses
positioning of UNIX and NT.

The article begins:

"In its first major strategy discussion since acquiring Digital
Equipment, Compaq last week outlined a one-two punch for integrating
Digital products."

"In a nutshell: Intel/Windows NT systems will be on the low end,
Alpha/Unix systems will be on the high end and a mix of both
technologies will serve the midrange."

The full text can be found on:

http://www.techweb.com/se/directlink.cgi?INW19980622S0011

Infoworld Forum, Unix roundup, Fri, 12 Jun

Digital Unix rocks (I still love Linux)
Posted by: tlewis
Date posted: Mon Jun 15 8:10:33 PDT 1998

Digital Unix, or the operating system formerly known as OSF/1,
completely rocks.

For servers, I would not use anything else. Altavista, amazon.com,
and my employer, MindSpring, all use it exclusively.

On the BIND-developers mailing list, there was a funny episode a
few weeks ago where all of a sudden, everyone running one of the
root name servers confessed that they were running them on Digital
Unix. "You are? Hey, me too; what a coincidence!"

DU has the following nice properties:
- responds very well under load; it has a very good scheduler.
This is a critical property in a server OS.
- Excellent programming environment. They are religiously
standards-compliant, and their docs are very good. Also, DEC
supplies an excellent JDK for the Alpha.
- fast as greased lightening. Running on the Alpha helps here, but
still, it's really fast.
- 64-bit OS. We're coming close to having machines in the 32-bit
space of memory requirements, and having a 64-bit platform is nice.
(Of course, Linux is fully 64-bit-capable, too.)

Linux has little to fear from DU; it's not geared as a desktop OS,
and the $500 price-tag is guaranteed to keep people coming to
redhat and debian for their desktop needs for a while to come.
However, for heavy-duty servers, DU is the way to go. Hell, the
Alpha machines are damned cheap for servers regardless of their
excellent performance; all in all, it just makes a great package.
