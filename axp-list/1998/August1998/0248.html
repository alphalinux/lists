> > to do parallel processing. It's faster than a pipe
> > because a pipe copies to kernel land and back and
> > SHM is directly in your memory space so there's
> > only one copy to it and the next process can get it.
>
> Yes, yes I agree. But SHM can be painful too (for
> the programmer who maintains locks).
> It's sometimes really easier to rewrite a piped

> where pipes looks more natural (correct).
>

If you replace pipe with socket, I agree completely.
Most stuff I work on can run on an SMP or a shared
nothing cluster so I use sockets instead of pipes.

Locking/synch is always interesting to get right...
AND fast...
