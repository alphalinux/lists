> Okay, I was wrong, I'm not getting the DMA performance enhancements. There must
> be some kind of cache that persists through a reboot, because right after reboot
> I had a 3x speedup; immediately after that an 8x speedup; but after doing a
> couple of large builds and such and testing again, there was no speedup at all

> Does Linux store the boot messages somewhere so I don't have to reboot and try to

1. There's no cache that persists through a reboot.
2. The above numbers could be explained by a cache getting built up
quickly but eventually getting flushed by the large builds.
3. You can get boot messages from dmesg. You should be able to get
them from /var/log. Messages printed by the startup scripts (stuff
in /etc/rc.d/...) are lost. You could try cut & paste...
