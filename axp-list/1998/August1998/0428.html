Dave Gilbert wrote:

> OK - I've only tried a simple 'dd' based timing test
> but my IDE drive which used to do 1.9MB/s is now shifting in excess
> of 7MB/s and at only 9% CPU.

Amazing, it really works! I got about a factor of 3 speedup between 2.1.111
and 2.1.115; ran the same dd again and it was 8 times faster than under
2.1.111! (Probably because of caching somewhere?)

However, on startup, I get a lot of troubling messages- "partition failed to
mount" and such. There were so many, I was almost expecting it to just
mount everything without DMA; but it did in fact work.

Should all of those warnings be a cause for concern? Or should I just leave
well enough alone and be happy? :-)

> Please join me in awarding the golden-penguin award to Dave Miller
> and Eddie Dost for this achievment.
>
> Wow!
