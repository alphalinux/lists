In speaking with Mike Kinney at Deskstation, just yesterday, he tells me:
"I am in the middle of a project right now, so it will be about a month
before I can get back to ldmilo, linload, and so on.."

In case you don't know, Deskstation are the designers of the UX, which
Samsung mass-produces. The current LDMILO is a work of Mike Kinney and his
associates, to re-create the linload and milo software parts. At the time
they first did this they did not have access to linload, as DEC would not
publish it, so they did some guessing of their own to create the LDMILO. It
has problems with a few things, such as limits in kernel size, rebooting on
shutdown -r, and so on. You MAY have just pointed to another bug. I don't
know, but I will ask him. As you may have seen Jim Paradis from Compaq has
gone ahead and obtained permission to publish the linload.exe sources, and
this is now available, so Mike and his associates now have this code to do
ldmilo better.

As for the reboot problem he posted me some patches yesterday for the
2.0.34 kernels, so I will be trying them momentarily and I can let you know
how this works out..

On another note, if you have a UX board, you should take a close look at
it, spoecifically at the crystal immediately by the ethernet RJ45
connector. It is a small metal unit sticking up about 1/2" from the
motherboard. If this crystal says "MP250 25.000 CTS TAIWAN" on it ( use a
magnifying glass) then you have a problem with your ethernet. If you run it
on a 100T connection it will work very badly.
If so, it should be removed and replaced with a better crystal. Sunny is
one brand that has been tested and works properly. This is, of course, a
25MHz crystal.
