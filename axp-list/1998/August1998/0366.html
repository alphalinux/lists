And I get to disagree with Kit here about Adaptec :-). They're the
Microsoft of SCSI controllers -- they have driven all of their competitors
to marginal status by force of sheer marketing rather than through
technical excellence. Go browse through a copy of Computer Shopper and try
to find a vendor who sells Mylex, or Symbios, or Advansys. It's hard.

This isn't to say that Adaptec sells inferior product. Their products
appear to be at least competent, and at times excellent. I *will* say that
usually Adaptec's products sell for more than their competitors, due to
the fact that Adaptec has pretty much locked the competitors out of the
retail chain -- i.e., no competition == higher prices.

So yes, it's great that Adaptec has seen the light and is now moving full
speed ahead to support Linux. That doesn't mean I'll hurry to run out
and buy one.
