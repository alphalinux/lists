The consideration on a PC is not the boot device, it is the BIOS - most
PC BIOS test for keyboard and video display card in their Power On Self
Test (POST), some newer BIOS allow you to not test for a keyboard, but I
do not think you can get away from the video card requirement...

The whole issue of not being able to install Linux headles on an Alpha
seems to me to only be a big problem if you didn't plan for a keyboard
and mouse in your initial plans (as in "I put the systems in the
equipment racks without video cards/keyboards, and i will be a real
hassle if I have to go back and reconfigure the machines now...).

If you want to know the real, final answer to headless Alpha installs,
I would look into what was done for the various render-farms built for
projects like the movie Titanic - in the pictures I have seen there were
a minimal amount of monitors for the large number of alpha systems in
the room... They may have used Keyboard/Video/Mouse (KVM) switches, but
I tend to doubt it... The machines may have been pre-configured before
being deployed.
