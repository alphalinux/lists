dca2 wrote:
>
> At first I will be trying to install both NT and Red Hat Linux. If I
> can't (or shouldn't) mirror,

Do you want to mirror NT using Linux? Access control does not have
one-to-one translation between the two and you may loose some of this
information. Also Linux supports FAT/VFAT but NTFS support is more
"iffy" due to a lack of information (what is known it was
reverse-engineered and some guesses may be wrong).

Mirroring Linux itself is not a problem. A while ago for that purpose
I wrote a perl script which basically creates a list of files according
to some criteria and feeds it to 'cpio -p' (removal of an obsolete stuff
is a bit of an issue). Nowadays I would likely use 'rsync' for
something like that (I did not try, but likely it can be coaxed to
do this) or a software like 'mirrordir' (lava.obsidian.co.za
/pub/linux/mirrordir).

> would it be easier or wiser to install the
> 2 OS on separate HD should I decide to buy both?

Keeping NT confined to its own play-pen may be a wise policy.

As for controllers - I would suggest something NCR based. Check
Diamond stuff for inexpensive options.
