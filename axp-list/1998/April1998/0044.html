>>> David Hamm said:
>
> Just got RH5 installed on LXPc164 and would like to compile and install 2.
> 0.33 kernel.
>
> 1. ftp://gatekeeper.dec.com does not have a 2.0.33 milo. Where could I get
> one or is there a way to use one of the other milo sources? I'm under the
> impression that you have to have matching milo source to upgrade kernel.

Uh, do I need to say this yet again? :-(

You do NOT need a MILO with version matching the kernel to be booted!!!
That is only important when you BUILD a MILO.

> 2 The kernel RedHat ships is not modular. I'd like to build a very modular
> kernel but am suspicious as to why the Stock RedHat Kernel is not modular.

Perhaps for a couple of reasons:

(a) modules have been broken on Alphas at various times, and RH wisely (IMHO)
has felt it more important to get releases out than wait for that situation
to be fixed.

(b) the range of peripherals installed on Alphas has been much narrower than
on INTELs, so there's been no real need to have to build 10 SCSI drivers,
or 20 Ethernet drivers, for example.

> 3. Must I rebuild milo every time I rebuild my kernel.

NO!!! See my previous rantings on this subject... :-\

> 4. Do I need to modify Milo after adding ram to the system?

NO. It should automatically detect how much you have.
