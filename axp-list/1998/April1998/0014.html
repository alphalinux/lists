Hello everybody,

To my great surprise the people from netscape appointed me as a
Mozilla-mirror, but several hours later my AlphaStation went down.
/var/log/messages turned up this:

syslogd: select: bad file descriptor
last message repeated 234234 times
last message repeated 950485 times

The numbers aren't correct, btw, but you get the idea. Apache ran just
fine, but telnet quit after saying "Escape character is ^]". I think that
everything needing to log something to syslog was blocked, including su.

Is this a known problem? Usually I would delve into the source and fix
this myself, but sadly I don't have time for this now.

It happened both with 2.1.24 and the RH5 stock 2.0.30 kernel. It almost
happened again today, but I was able to kill syslogd in time to avoid
meltdown.

I use glibc-2.0.5c-9, sysklogd-1.3-19, with the stock /etc/syslog.conf.

Any clues?
