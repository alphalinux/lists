No, think of it more as your opportunity to give something back to the
Linux/Alpha effort, by debugging and fixing the problems in the code... :-)

Since there are *far* fewer folk running Linux on Alphas, there are quite
a few drivers that work on INTELs that have never been "corrected" to run
on Alphas as well.

The instances of Alpha hardware being the cause for non-working peripherals
is much fewer than the instances of driver code written with assumptions
that are not true when running on an Alpha.

Please consult the Alpha FAQ and previous postings by many folk to this
list for tips on what to look for when porting code.
