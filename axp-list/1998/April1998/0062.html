Dave Gilbert <gilbertd@treblig.org> writes:

> On Sun, 12 Apr 1998, Harvey J. Stein wrote about Samsungs 21264:
>
> > Some highlights:
> >
> > The 21264 is also slot compatible with the K7 microprocessor from AMD,
> > giving OEMs a single motherboard solution to cover a range of price
> > points.
>
> Byte mentioned this a few months back; as far as I remember the story
> was that the K7 and 21264 were going to create a 'slot A' to run in
> competition to the Intel proprietary Slot 1. I think what it said
> was that the K7 bus was electrically compatible with the 21264,
> saving AMD the design effort of designing a new bus, and giving the
> Alpha access to what should be cheap motherboards.

For what it's worth, I just found this on AMD's site
(http://www.amd.com/news/corppr/9771.html):

Sanders noted that in 1999, AMD plans to begin shipping its
seventh-generation processor, the AMD-K7(TM) processor. He said
that AMD plans for the AMD-K7 processor to be assembled in a module
mechanically interchangeable with Intel's single-edge connector --
"Slot 1" -- module and will feature the bus protocols of the Alpha
EV-6 processor. The AMD-K7 processor will be capable of clock
speeds higher than 500 megahertz.
