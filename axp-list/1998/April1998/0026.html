On Thu, 2 Apr 1998, Bradford Smith wrote:

> Hi. I'm an alpha newbie building a kernel. I read the FAQ at <http://www.eleves.ens.fr:8080/home/quatrava/AXP-port-FAQ/index.html.en> and it was helpful.
>
> I'm using the standard packages from redhat 5.0, except that I have applied the latest glibc (glibc-2.0.7-6.alpha.rpm). I do not have the libc package installed at all.
>
> I ran menuconfig, and set things up as I wanted, issued a 'make boot' and (45 minutes later; this is a UDB) I had a vmlinux.gz.
>
> MILO loads and unzips the file, but as soon as I get the "jumping to the kernel" message, and the dashed line across the screen, nothing happens. My (admittedly novice) opinion is that something is wrong with the executable format of the vmlinux.gz file.
>
> Is there something simple I'm missing?

1. Use shorter lines or answering is nearly impossible.

2. The only reliable way to configure the kernel is 'make config'
Both 'make menuconfig' and 'make xconfig' are known roads to failure.
