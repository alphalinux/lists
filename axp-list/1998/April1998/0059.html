All,

It's beginning to seem as if NIS is simply never tested before new glibc
rpms are released. I finally got around to upgrading from
glibc-2.0.6-9.alpha.rpm, and discovered that NIS is non-operative with
either glibc-2.0.7-4 or glibc-2.0.7-6. I downloaded and installed the
latest portmap and ypbind packages from 'upgrades' - no joy.

There is a cascade of yp_call failures (from glibc itself) during boot,
and all attempts at using ypcat to show maps result in a "..Can't talk to
portmap. Reason: Connection refused" error.

I forced a downgrade back to glibc-2.0.6-9 (without changing anything
else) and everything began working properly again.

Can someone familiar with the glibc churn please take a look at this?

Steve
