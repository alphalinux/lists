On Sun, 12 Apr 1998, Harvey J. Stein wrote about Samsungs 21264:

> Some highlights:
>
> The 21264 is also slot compatible with the K7 microprocessor from AMD,
> giving OEMs a single motherboard solution to cover a range of price
> points.

Byte mentioned this a few months back; as far as I remember the story
was that the K7 and 21264 were going to create a 'slot A' to run in
competition to the Intel proprietary Slot 1. I think what it said
was that the K7 bus was electrically compatible with the 21264,
saving AMD the design effort of designing a new bus, and giving the
Alpha access to what should be cheap motherboards.

> - KP21264-3.5X, Est. 43 SPECint95/64 SPECfp95

I want one......

> - KP21264-3.5X = $6,500

Ah - $6,500 - hmm, I think I might wait for the price to drop a little.

> clock speeds are they running at? Do they really work with any
> motherboard which takes an AMD K7? Wouldn't it at least need a
> different BIOS?

Presumably - but I suppose you could put both in all motherboards?

> Is anyone working on getting such a beastie to run
> Linux?

I think it was Jay a few months back posted a /proc/cpuinfo contents
for a 21264.

<heavy speculating here>
It strikes me that with the financial mess in Korea that perhaps
it might be easier and cheaper for people to get locally produced
processors rather than ship in $s worth of Intel parts and similarly
that if they can export some of these Alphas they'll get lots
of Won - this could be good for Alpha.
(But heck I'm a computer bod not a finance person.....)
</heavy speculating here>
