Peter Boutzev <boutzev@usa.net> writes:
> I used minlabel utility to partidion /dev/sda....
> Here is my partition table:
>
> /dev/sda1 -> 8MB (boot)
> /dev/sda2 -> 450MB(root)
> /dev/sda3 -> 32MB(swap)
[..]
> trough the rest of the installation as usually without repartitioning my
> /dev/sda with fdisk (I made it with minlabel)... All works fine, the
> Install finds the ftp server retrieves "HDLIST" and so... But when it
> tries to make the ext2 filesystem on /dev/sda2 it halts....

That looks bad, it shouldn't do this. Do the partitions overlap
somehow, what ID's do they got?

> After all this, I jumped back to minlabel, and deleted all partitions
> except the boot (/dev/sda1)....
> At RH Install I used Disk Druid and added 2 new partitions - the root
> and the swap...
> I have seen that Disk Druid doesn't see the boot partition that I made
> with minlabel - anyone knows why???.... I tried with fdisk too but fdisk
> doesn't seem to be working at all with a disk that is minlabeled...

Disk Druid just works for fdisk style labels. You cannot use it on a
Jensen, if you wan't to boot from that disk.

> So, using Disk Druid I successfully added the root and swap
> partitions... After this I made all as usually... The mkfs.ext2 doesn't
> halt, and the installation begins... After 17MB's are already installed,
> the installation becomes more and more slow, after what it kills all
> processes and halts....

> I am not really sure where is the problem... Anyone knows???

In case the partitons didn't overlap, I'd suspect it is a hardware problem.
>
> I tried to re minlabel the disk, and minilabel didn't see the partitions
> I made with Disk Druid at RH Install... I think that Disk Druid isn't
> the right prog to partition HDs on Jensens, but I am not sure...

It's definatly not the right way ... You could partition you disk
with Disk Druid and/or fdisk, but you wan't be able to boot from that
disk. It's fine for a second disk, and needed if you want to share it
with NT for example, but you cannot reasonable do it on the drive you
want to boot from.

> But the minlabel partitions seem not to be working (mkfs.ext2 halts on
> them)....

How often did you try this? Enough to get a proper correlation?

I'd rather suspect a hardware problem. I would try a different hard
drive, or maybe removing the ethernet card and trying to install from
CD.

Good luck, Martin
