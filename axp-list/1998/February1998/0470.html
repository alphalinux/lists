>
> My ISP assigns dynamic IP when I log in with my Win95 client. I assume
> I can configure the Linux box do expect a dynamic IP when it calls
> my ISP with PPP, right?

You do not even have to do anything special about that. Just do not
give your ppp daemon any fixed number. It will negotiate one with
the other end (and will add a default route through this new number).

Your other interfaces, on a machine directly connected to a phone line
and on other machines, should be from these "reserved, internal" IP
numbers set aside for such purposes. IP number, contrary to a popular
usage, is really associated with a network interface and not with
a machine itself. Your Linux box should then "masquerade" for your
other clients. There is HOWTO on the topic as well.

Oh, you may need some modules loaded to support things like ftp.
Contrary to what you may read they DO work.
