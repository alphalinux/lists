chris@nirvana.net said:
> I've seen a lot of people talking about problems with 5.0 that they
> never had with 4.2. I just want to get my udb up and running linux
> without too many problems, should I get 4.2 or 5.0?

If you just want to get some work done, you should stick with 4.2 for
a spell until 5.x gets along for a while. There are a handful of
problems with 5.0 on alpha. You will have to hack at it, apply some updates,
and move to a later kernel (i.e. 2.0.32 for the gtk tools to work) but
it does work.

If you are interested in bleeding edge, and providing some constructive
criticism that will lead to a better 5.1 (or 5.2 or whatever) then 5.0
works well enough. If you feel really brave, many of us are running with
2.1 kernels on the alpha. You will need a recent milo for this.
