>>> Oleg Gusev said:
>
> I think we need a FAQ for compiling MILO and FMU but before at least
> the following questions must be answered :
>
> 1. What is 'quickstrip' and is it necessary to
> 'quickstrip makerom' ?

It was a hacked version of a "strip", done by Jim Paradis, IIRC. Exists
under RH 4.2 and 5.0 full installs, along with a manpage. Still worked,
AFAIK, under 4.2.

> 2. Why 'ld' uses '-non_shared' OSF/1 flag and is it ok simply
> to replace it with '-Bstatic' ?

Prolly because it was first developed under OSF and never changed, and either
still works under GCC or is benign because there no need for any shared libs
when it *does* do the link. I dunno; try it the other way and see what happens.

> 3. What 'gas' is required to (safely) compile palcode and where
> one can get it?

The GAS under stock RH 4.2 was sufficient to compile all the MILOs used for
RH 5.0.

> 4. On what platforms 'fmu' was tested and is known to work ?

For that you'll have to see the MILO HOWTO or ask Dave Rusling
<rusling@linux.reo.dec.com>, as he was the last one at DEC to do so.

> 5. Is it possible to compile MILO and FMU without x86-digital
> library and what is the legal situation about compiling GPLed
> pci.a, cia.a, etc. kernel parts with no-source-available library ?

Yes, its possible to compile and run such a MILO, but I have no idea about
running FMU, although I tried to make the compiles clean when I released the
last set of MILO sources.

> 6. What is hwrpb->sys_type for PC164 ?

Look at include/asm-alpha/hwrpb.h, and the code in MILO's hwrpb.c; the former
from alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.4, the latter in milo-sources-2.0.30-0.4, IIRC.

> I tried to compile MILO and FMU on AlphaPC164 500Mhz,128MB DRAM,
> S3 968 video, Adaptec 2940UW and compiled it after some tweaking,
> get rid of TGA console code, MILO works, but reports double (996.0)
> bogomips and the clock is two times slower than normal.
> FMU crashes with tons of debuggind information, so i'll try to
> connect serial console to find out what goes wrong.
> This is 2.0.33 kernel with 2.0.32-0.1 patches and my minor patches
> and 2.0.30-0.4 milo sources.

I'm not sure how well those MILO sources match up with the 2.0.32 and patches;
my last successful builds were against 2.0.30 plus alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.4,
and under a stock RH 4.2 install (prolly with the stock updates only).

Please, *do* build the PALcode if possible, it's likely that the "canned"
PALcode (osfpal.nh.uu) doesn't initialize the clock correctly (or even at all).

And I know that there are problems building under RH 5.0; as I said above,
I can vouch for the builds under RH 4.2 only.

> PS. The replies like "this is not supported" and "don't use FMU -
> boot from ARC" are not welcomed for obvious reason - I want
> to remove all traces of micro$oft from my alpha :)

Then I won't say anything but "you're on your own"... :-\
