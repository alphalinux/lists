Chris Conner wrote:
>
> As far as I can see, the cable will not fit any normal 50 pin scsi hard
> drive (or is that scsi-2)? Can someone tell me what I need to look for? A
> normal ultra scsi drive?

The connector is a small 50-pin connector sometimes called a "Honda"
connector (this is what the DEC manual calls it) or a "SCSI-2"
connector. You can get cables with this connector on one end and a
standard Centronics (SCSI-1) connector on the other. Newer drives (such
as the Jaz) use this Honda connector.

"Ultra" SCSI can come in the narrow (50-pin) or wide (68-pin) flavors. A
modern Ultra-narrow drive would probably use the Honda connector.

Just about anybody who would sell you a drive should be able to sell you
the proper cable to connect it to your UDB, although maybe I'm spoiled
by being in NYC - we have a vendor whose motto is "we have it or we will
make it - 1-hour service) !
