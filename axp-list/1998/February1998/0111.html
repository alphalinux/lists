Hi folks,

I'm having fun bringing three DEC Alpha Multia 166MHZ VX40B-F2's (a.k.a.,
noname and udb for Universal Desktop Box) up under RedHat Linux 5.0 and
have been reading the mail hear since I started. So, first, hello to all...

Re the DEC Unix license issue you might be pleasantly suprised if you check
out:

http://www.starshipcorp.com/

These folks are located in New Hampshire and are a great source of DEC
Alpha and Mac stuff. Their blurb on DEC's Unix follows:

<<DEC UNIX 4.0 (OSF UNIX)

DEC UNIX requires both a license (DEC #QL-MT4 AE-6Q - $780) and media (DEC
#QA-MT4 AA-H8 - $340) to
run. It also needs at least 64MB of RAM, a one gigabyte hard drive and
either a fast CDROM (preferably 8X) or a
DEC approved CDROM to load properly. Contact Starship if you need loading
instructions. It's acceptably quick
on the Multia but again requires a high degree of computer knowledge to
load and operate.

Minimum Recommended: 64MB RAM & 1GB HD

Sources: Starship, DEC, DEC Resellers (SEE LINKS & NEWSGROUPS )>>

So if you can get a license for free, the media (2 CD-ROMS, I believe) is
only $340.00, which is less than SCO Unix, right? (They also sell a 2
CD-ROM of RedHat Linux 5.0 for DEC Alpha boxes for $29 and a SCSI 1/3
height 2X CD-ROM player for the same price. They are the only non-DEC
source for pureunobtainables like the SCSI cable that attaches (via
miniature 50-pin connector) to the Multia boxes. I wish I had found them
sooner: I bought three (new in opened box) Multias for $299 ea via
www.onsale.com. ONSALE takes a big chunck out and gives the balance to
Starship who is the source so you can probably buy a 166 MHz Multia (0K
RAM/no HD) with floppy only for a little less. Suprisingly their memory
prices for 16MB 72-pin ECC (i.e., 36-bit) 60 ns SIMMS is a whopping $45.00
each (you have to buy them in pairs). I got six (3 pairs) at auction for
$29.00 each. Still Starship is a terrific source for Alpha stuff and their
hardware prices (if you do a reasonable amount of business with them) are
generally lower than what's quoted on their web page. Importantly, also,
they are a small company of really nice folks, which makes it a pleasure to
deal with them.

Have fun...

Dennis
