Hi Mike !

> Is anyone out there burning CD's off a RedHat 5.0/Alpha 164lx system?
> If so what software are you using (will the cdwtools package work?) and
> what hardware?
> We have a HP SureStore 6020 (with a SCSI interface) and on the alpha
> end we have a NCR 53C875 Ultra SCSI controller. I know we will need an
> adaptor to go from the older style SCSI port on the burner to the ultra
> wide on the alpha.

I'm using cdrecord on RH 5.0 intel.
There is no reason why it should not work on alpha and
your HP-writer is supported too (according to README).

If your writer is external I recommend to buy a cheap NCR53c810(a)
instead an adaptor.
Reasons:
a) No silly termination problems: If your HP is the only external
device you must terminate mixed internal/external (on wide).
Expect problems. (I think you have an external one. The internal
is called 6020i)
b) You can maybe get a NCR cheaper than an adaptor.
c) You can plug/unplug your writer without shutdown.
(If you have only one channel you can switch your writer off
but you can't unplug it while running !)
d) No signal reflection problems or too long cable.
Remember: SCSI uses only parity checking and nothing else...
