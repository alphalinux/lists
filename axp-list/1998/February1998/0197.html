The current 00README.errata on ftp.redhat.com says, among other things,
this:
Package: glibc
......
+ (20-Jan-1997) NIS stuff and other misc problems fixed. The
vast majority of users only need the first two, but you need
to upgrade to amd-920824upl102-11 and portmap-4.0-8 at the
same time, or those services will stop working.
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
+ (28-Jan-1997) Updated version.
Solution:
+ Intel: Upgrade to:
[112]glibc-2.0.6-9.i386.rpm
.............
[117]portmap-4.0-8.i386.rpm
^^^^^
+ Alpha: Upgrade to
[118]glibc-2.0.6-9.alpha.rpm
..............
[123]portmap-4.0-9.alpha.rpm
^^^^^

The problem is that the highest portmap rpm in updates for alpha
is portmap-4.0-7.alpha.rpm and is SRPMS portmap-4.0-8.src.rpm

Does anybody know if this is just a typo in 00README.errata,
an oversight or the real trouble?

BTW, glibc-2.0.6-9.alpha.rpm does not seem to include a fix to strtod
posted on this list by Richard Henderson. Seems like a quite important
one if you are doing any number crunching work. It was likely just
too late for it to make it in the last update.
