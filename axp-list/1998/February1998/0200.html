I think we need a FAQ for compiling MILO and FMU but before at least
the following questions must be answered :

1. What is 'quickstrip' and is it necessary to
'quickstrip makerom' ?
2. Why 'ld' uses '-non_shared' OSF/1 flag and is it ok simply
to replace it with '-Bstatic' ?
3. What 'gas' is required to (safely) compile palcode and where
one can get it?
4. On what platforms 'fmu' was tested and is known to work ?
5. Is it possible to compile MILO and FMU without x86-digital
library and what is the legal situation about compiling GPLed
pci.a, cia.a, etc. kernel parts with no-source-available library ?
6. What is hwrpb->sys_type for PC164 ?

I tried to compile MILO and FMU on AlphaPC164 500Mhz,128MB DRAM,
S3 968 video, Adaptec 2940UW and compiled it after some tweaking,
get rid of TGA console code, MILO works, but reports double (996.0)
bogomips and the clock is two times slower than normal.
FMU crashes with tons of debuggind information, so i'll try to
connect serial console to find out what goes wrong.
This is 2.0.33 kernel with 2.0.32-0.1 patches and my minor patches
and 2.0.30-0.4 milo sources.

Oleg.

boot from ARC" are not welcomed for obvious reason - I want
to remove all traces of micro$oft from my alpha :)
