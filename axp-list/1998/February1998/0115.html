Jeffrey,

Not sure about the RH5 problems (we are still on 4.2 on the Multia
until the 5.0 CD arrives, could be fun..) but I can say that ours
has been on for about 1.5 years now excluding longer power cuts,
adding of memory and reboots. Yes it does get hot, but as long as
you allow for this and give it a little room, all should be fine.
BTW. This is a 233Mhz one as well, which I suspect would get
slightly hotter than the 166.

Also, I am quite impressed by this little box, despite being a first
generation Alpha, it is pushing out rc5DES keys
(see http://www.distributed.net) about twice as fast as my 166Mhz 6x86
despite the 6x86 being idle most of the day, and the Multia running an
almost unused Web server and E-mail for 10 people. Of course my XL300
at home is slightly over 1.7x as quick as the Multia despite still
being bogged down by NT4 ;-)

Alan
