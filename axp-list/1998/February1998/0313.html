> Friends,
>
> I have a UDB running Linux 5.0. My problem is that the time/date will not
> stay accurate. After my initial boot the date is off by about 19 years.
>
> Has anybody had this problem before?

Indeed. That is due to "ARC" time format at the CMOS clock.
On file /etc/sysconfig/clock you need to set: ARC=yes

Effectively that makes then:
/sbin/clock -A -s
if your CMOS is running local time, and
/sbin/clock -A -u -s
if your CMOS is running UTC time. (mine runs UTC..)
