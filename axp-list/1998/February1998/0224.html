Hi,

The problem is not with MILO, as it sets the SCSI_ID correctly.
But as MILO boots the kernel, and loads the device drivers the first time.
It will reset the SCSI_HOSTID to "7", this has probably something to do with
the NCR53x8xx driver, as it can't find any NVRAM settings and thus resets the SCSI_HOSTID back to "7"

Now my real problem is that i'am unable te re-compile the milo sources, and thus can't hack my way into the kernel sources.
