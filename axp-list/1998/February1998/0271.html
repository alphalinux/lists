Chris Conner wrote:
>
> I have a 166Mhz UDB that I would like to setup redhat linux on, but it only
> has a 340 meg 2.5" scsi hard drive. This is enough for redhat, but I would
> like some more space. I don't know what kind of drive the udb needs, I've
> seen people talking about 2.5 and 3.5 inch drives, but I am clueless. If
> someone could point me in the right direction, I would like to replace the
> existing drive with a new ~1 gig drive.

UDBs came in various configurations. Some used 2.5" scsi drives and
others used 3.5" ones. The 3.5" configuration required you to give up
the PCI slot because the drive occupied this space.

The power supply is somewhat marginal, enough for a 2.5" drive or a 3.5"
one but NOT both at the same time. You would also have to be careful of
3.5" drives as some use more power than others.

Your best bet is to go with an external drive, assuming yur UDB variant
has the external scsi connector.

Just my opinion.
