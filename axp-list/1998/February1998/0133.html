Just my two cents on the multia heat discussion: We have been operating a
multia round the clock for close to a year now, with a internal 3.5 1G
drive, and yah, it gets warm, but no problems. I have noticed a lot of
ahrd disks in our multias have failed (the 2.5 variety) but I'm not sure
if that is due to heat or some other outside factor. So far our only
multia motherboard failure was a multia that forgot how to display
correctly synced video.. (we are running ~40 multias here, most with 2.5
hdds)
