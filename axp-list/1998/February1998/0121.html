partition (the option was always greyed out in the menu). The help
files say that I have to "select free space" on the drive and create a
partition from that, but it doesn't say how I go about selecting free
space using the Disk Administrator program.

What they mean by 'free space' is space not already allocated to
existing partitions. Who would do such a thing you might ask? and I
couldn't answer. Anyway, the only way to carve out new partitions from
existing ones is via a repartitioning program; Linux has the FIPS
program that works well for FAT16 partitions; if you need to split
FAT32 or NT partitions, you will have to use a commercial program
called Partition Magic, I think. I have used it a little bit (for
FAT32) and it worked fine.
