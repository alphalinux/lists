On 25 Feb, Edward G. Prentice wrote:
> I've gone way past my 30-day installation period and can't get support
> from RedHat. I wasn't far enough along to ask these questions before.

All of this stuff is beyond the scope of RH's installation support,
anyhow.

> 2. I just ordered a 56K PCMCIA modem for my multia (that has two PCMCIA
> slots). Can you tell me step-by-step instructions for setting it up
> so I can dial my ISP and do TCP/IP? How do I make /dev/modem be alive?
> How do I tell it to dial out on /dev/modem to establish PPP with
> the ISP that I have been dialing from my Win95 system (via PPP)

I'm not familiar w/ PCMCIA devices (might be a HOWTO somewhere for
this..), but I am familiar with using regular modems to establish PPP
connections. You can take the easy way out and use netcfg to configure
a PPP link, or you do the "right" thing and read the PPP HOWTO (one of
the best) and configure the scripts manually.

netcfg will likely work faster, doing it manually will be a good
experience for a newbie.

> 3. The console clock on my Multia won't let me save the correct date.
> It forces it to the year 2018 - and then when my system boots, the
> system time on machine is always off by a couple of decades.

2 decades exactely, right? You need to tell Linux that you're using
ARC, by using timeconfig.

> 4. My X-server runs, but the screen size seems to be wrong - it thinks
> the screen is bigger than it really is, resulting in windows that hang
> off the edge. Any idea what I did wrong or how to fix it?

This is a "feature" known as a virtual screen. The easiest way to ditch
it is to open /etc/X11/XF86Config in your fav. text editor (pico is the
simplest to use for basic newbie editing), and find a line that looks
like:

Virtual 1024 768

and change it to:

Virtual 0 0

There's more than one of these lines, and the numbers may be different.
You specifically want the Virtual line under the

Section "Screen"
Driver "accel"

section.

> 5. I'm hoping to setup my Alpha as a gateway for my LAN to connect to
> the real world (via dialin to my ISP) - Any suggestions on how to
> get it right? Can I setup my alpha as a DNS server for the other machines
> on my LAN?

Yes, you can setup your box as a DNS. You'll want to read the DNS
HOWTO, which is living in /usr/doc/ldp somewhere, as well as at
http://sunsite.unc.edu/LDP/

If you only have one IP number from your ISP, you'll need to use IP
Masquerading to allow all of your boxes to access the Internet. There's
a mini-HOWTO on this topic, as well.

> 6. I'm a bit nervous about upgrading to RedHat 5.0 - should I be? or
> might it make things easier rather than harder? My concern is not
> for the reliability of the newer software - it is probably better than
> 4.2. My concern is that my system works now, and "if it ain't broke,
> don't fix it".

Which is the correct mentality. Now, when 5.1 or 5.2 comes out, you
might want to consider upgrading, as most of the bugs will have likely
been ironed out. However, I wouldn't recommend jumping up to 5.0 just
for the hell of it.

> I'm really looking forward to getting my alpha on the net so I can get

You already have Apache installed, and it's not difficult to configure
:)
