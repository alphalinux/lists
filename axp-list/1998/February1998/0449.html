On Wed, 25 Feb 1998, Tad Grosvenor wrote:

> I know this isn't the perfect place to ask this, but I am running an alpha
> with linux and you all seem to know quite a bit about hardware. I have
> two fixed frequency monitors, both of which work at
> 1152x900 (and hang off of the screen a little bit) The largest of them is
> supposed to work at 1280x1024 but I can't find a modeline that will work.
> The one I have is close, but the screen scrolls vertically.
>
> Modeline "1280x1024" 110 1280 1328 1512 1712 1024 1025 1028 1054
>
> Modeline "1152x900" 94 1152 1224 1324 1540 900 904 920 940
> This one works, but the image hangs off of the right hand side of the
> screen.
>
> Anyone know how to fix the top one, or know of a modeline that will work
> with a Radius (sony) gdm-1950 monitor?

Hmm... I have used a gdm-1950, and the exact 1280x1024 you discribed
worked for me. Except I only used negative syncs (add -vsync and -hsync to
your modeline). This may be the default setting and then nothing will
change if you add them.

What I remember mostly from that particular monitor is that if you change
the screenmode from what it is set for, you need to adjust a matrix of 5x7
(or so) potentiometers just to get a sharp picture (for the three colors
to shoot at the same spot all over the screen). This is due to the
flatness of the screen. Anyway, it was a non-trivial adjustment, so you
might want to adjust your screenmode to the preset matrix setting instead.

That's where xvidtune comes in! With this program, you can visually
adjust all the modeline paramters except visual width and height + pixel
clock. I guess you know what screen size you want, so then only pixel
clock needs to be changed if you can't get a good screenmode with
xvidtune. Generally higher pixel clock for higher sync rates and vice
versa. BTW: Pixel clock is the value 110 in your modeline for 1280x1024.

Hope this helps.
