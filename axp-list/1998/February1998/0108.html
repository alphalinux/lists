On Fri, 13 Feb 1998, Tommy Cheung wrote:
> Try Bonnie. Also, those numbers doesn't look that bad actually. Depends
> on what type of SCSI card and harddisk you are having. As always, theory
> seperates from reality :-). For example, if you read properly of the USB
> spec, it isn't that great. Firewire is the better way to go.

Uh.. for what? Comparing firewire to USB is like comparing a Ferarri to a
Toyota. The Ferarri is nice and has better, but the Toyota is cheaper.
Are you trying to win a race or go to work? From what I know, USB is
meant for low data rate devices - like keyboards, mice, joysticks,
tablets, fax/modems, telephony, audio, cheap scanners. Firewire is for
video, external drives, video cameras, etc. The USB spec. papers even
state this sort of thing.

But hey, neither are out in any sort of massive scale, so any argument for
now is moot.
