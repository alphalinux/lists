On Tue, 24 Feb 1998, Stefan Traby wrote:

> Hi Mike !
>
> > Is this a debug message? and how do I get rid of it? Every time I do
> > anything with modules (eg: insmod, lsmod etc) on my noname it puts this
> > message on the screen::
> >
> > <sc 347(0,0,0)>
> > and that repeats each time I do anything with modules.
> > Whats it mean? and again how do I get rid of it?
>
> The message is ok, it's a kernel message.
> There are problems with PAL-code when making syscalls from kernel itself.
> See "linux-2.x.xx/arch/alpha/kernel/boot/traps.c" for a
> detailed description. (Yes, here it comes from.)
>
> You could patch the kernel to get rid of it; but this is IMHO a bad idea.
> If you really want to do that, exclude exact this (347) number only.
>
> BTW, the first number (347) identifies the call "query_module".
> The "number to function (syscall)" mapping can be found in
> "linux-2.x.xx/include/asm/unistd.h".
> The three numbers in brackets are the first three args to the specific
> system call.
>
> I think it's good to know where (and why) the message comes from.
> The default action should be "Be happy and live with it." ;-}
