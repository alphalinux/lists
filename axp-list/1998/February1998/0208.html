> OK, I think we're going to get it now. I have some new knowledge and key
> information.
>
> 1. During boot-up I get the following message:
> Starting system loggers: eth0: No link beat found, switching to aui media.

We had the same problem with a 166 Multia. It seemed fine under RH 4.2
but it didn't work under RH 5.0. I tried recompiling the kernel, and got
the same message on bootup. My solution was to plug into the AUI port.
Bang it worked fine, since I had an AUI adapter it wasn't a problem.

If you find a solution let me know, if you don't get an AUI adapter and
plug it in. They are handy little things in the network world.

> 2. I tried to setting ewa0_mode to twister using the following command:
> "set ewa0_mode twisted". After that tunet (an srm based network tester)
> stopped autocycling through the different interfaces. I thought we had it.
> I thought that would tell the driver not to switch to "aui media" and it
> would work. However, it seems that ewa0_mode gets reset to auto during the
> reboot.

tried that also, didn't work.

> 3. Other information: This is the second node on this network. The other
> machine has a 100mbit card and it's a 100mbit hub. Could it be that this
> machine will not recognize the 100mbit link beat? I know it will only
> connect at 10mbit but maybe it's failing because of the 100mbit hardware on
> the network? I'm grasping at straws here.
>
> Does anybody know how to permanently set the ewa0_mode to twisted?
