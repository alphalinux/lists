Jonathan DuBois writes:
>
> and printf("%.8f %.8f\n",x,y); should be
> printf("%.8lf %.8lf\n",x,y);

Just for the record. There is no 'l' modifier on printf 'f' format
specifier (there is one for (f)scanf and maybe this what mixed you up).
'f' in the context means "double". The above "should" is a BUG and gcc
will warn you about it.

The Standard says "If an h, l or L appears with any other conversion
specifier , the behaviour is udefined". In case of 'l' "any" means
everything but d, i, o, u, x and X.

Most compilers/libraries are acommodating enough and will simply
interpret "%.8lf" as "%.8f", silently or complaining, but they are free
to do **anything** and still remain Standard conforming.

OTOH "Lf" conversion does exist but this is another story.
