Does anybody has a handle on the following?

In RH 5.0 distribution emacs on a console when stopped with ^Z
sometimes (repeat - not always) seem to be loosing track of a keyboard
processing. <Return> gives a shell prompt but emacs, when brought back
with 'fg', apparently interprets all keys, from that moment on, literally.
Makes wonders for a buffer you edit. :-)

Yes, I did recompile emacs with a set of headers with FD_ISSET macro
fixed with a proper cast. This made emacs to behave much better but
this nasty buglet remains (it seems to show less frequently, though,
but maybe I am seeing things). I do not recall seeing it in previous
distributions but they all had emacs 19 and this one is 20.

An obvious workaround is "do not do that" but old habits die hard
and this is annoying. Any ideas if this is a bug in emacs, somewhere
in a tty layer or something even more sinister which may bite hard in
an inopportune moment?

Michal
