On Thu, 26 Feb 1998, Edward G. Prentice wrote:

> Where do I find... No, *HOW* do I find the HOWTO for the PCMCIA and/or
> PPP on my machine? I should learn to fish (figure out how to locate
> answers on my own).

Most of the documentation can be found in /usr/doc and its subdirectories,
/usr/doc/HOWTO and /usr/doc/FAQ

> My ISP assigns dynamic IP when I log in with my Win95 client. I assume
> I can configure the Linux box do expect a dynamic IP when it calls
> my ISP with PPP, right?

Yes, not a problem. Should be ready out-of-box. I haven't used PPP yet
(our ISP is upstairs and they let us plug into their T1 for $20/IP) but we
also have a UDB acting as a gateway for a VPN, and may be adding dialin
support thanks to a 28.8k PCMCIA modem we just bought for $30. I need to
learn how to set up a PPP server now. :)

Let me know if you run into any difficulties with the PCMCIA modem. I
just recompiled the kernel last night to include removable IDE support and
haven't rebooted the machine yet. Figured I'd also take that time to let
the new UPS charge up...

> Again, Thanks for your prompt answers. I'm going from being discouraged
> to being encouraged about the possiblity of actually getting this all to
> work.

They work, and work well. Trust me. :)

One caveat: I do get a lot of paging faults (unaligned kernel traps).
I'm told they're harmless, but basically just munch your CPU. Not a big
deal for a gateway machine, but still ... Also, I'm waiting for RedHat 5.1
before even thinking about upgrading. I'd suggest the same unless you
want to play with beta releases (anything *.0 should be considered
'beta'). Be sure to download all the errata as well. From what has come
across the list, RedHat 5 does fix a lot of problems, but at the same
time, glibc is relatively unstable (the whole g++ thing, etc, etc).
