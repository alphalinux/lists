Matthew S. Woodworth wrote:

>
> 3. Other information: This is the second node on this network. The other
> machine has a 100mbit card and it's a 100mbit hub. Could it be that this
> machine will not recognize the 100mbit link beat? I know it will only
> connect at 10mbit but maybe it's failing because of the 100mbit hardware on
> the network? I'm grasping at straws here.
>

If this is a two-machine network and the other machine is 100-base-T and
the hub is 100 speed then I would expect that you would have a problem.
See if there is a way to force the other machine to 10Mhz. speed, or
else perhaps force your UDB to twisted pair mode and fire it up first so
the 100 speed machine sees the 10Mhz. signal.

You can't have one mcahine at 100 speed and the other at 10 unless you
have a VERY intelligent hub :-)

You might also try connecting the two machines directly using a
"crossover" cable.

OTOH you could spend $40 and get a small 10-base-T hub.
