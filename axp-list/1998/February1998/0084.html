Loic Prylli wrote:
> I need only a small patch to em86.cc, the check for the "31 bit"
> kernel patch is IMHO wrong, so I disabled it (the check is made on
> stack variables, but we do not care as a special stack is created for
> the x86 code, (and the kernel 2.0.33 with latest alpha-patches does
> not try to apply the 31 bit limit to the initial stack).

No, the check is correct; it's the kernel that's wrong 8-) 8-(

When my "31-bit" patches got into the kernel, one of them dropped
through
the cracks; the one to enforce a 31-bit *stack* on 32-bit processes.
While programs may work *most* of the time, there may be times (such as
signal handling or native thunking) when an item on the Alpha stack
needs
to get passed in to the x86 program.

I'll post some kernel patches as soon as I get my kernel versions
straight
(be patient with me; I'm moving offices and working out of three
different
locations these days...)
