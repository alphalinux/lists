Wes Bauske wrote in response to my post:

> Since when did the X server, compilers, and browser become partof the OS??
> They are important applications but certainly not
> part of the OS. Maybe you're thinking like someone else I won't
> mention here.

The compiler was part of Unix since about 1978 when Unix(TM) shipped
with a compiler; X has been part of the OS since the start of this
decade whenevery other Unix system shipped with it.

I don't care about distinguishing what is part of the OS etc.;
I regard the OS as the thing which makes the machine 'usable'.
These days X is part of usability, and for a Unix system a compiler
is also needed.

> I use a Matrox and an ATI RAGE II. Both work except for the 20
> windows problem.

Yeh - a 20 window limit - its a laugh isn't it.....

> Though I don't use C++ in my line of work, I
> thought egcs 1.0.2 was working??

EGCS works OK - but its development and is not 100% stable - just
see what a mess it makes of building the X server. Anyway its stable
for most C++ work - but again its no use for the basic user - he doesn't
want ot have to mess about; he wants to put his Linux distribution disc in,
tell it a little about how he wants it set up, go away for a coffee and
come back with a working machine.

> IDE does *work*, just not in
> DMA mode. Still haven't been able to confirm DMA mode in the
> 2.1.xx kernels works on my systems yet though.

Yeh IDE works; but at 2.5MB/s - great; perhaps I should just go back to a
386.

> Course, you could
> always put up the $50 for a NCR810 SCSI card.

And then pay through the nose for discs; not these days when I can
buy an 8GB IDE disc for trivial amounts.

> > Once Linux/Alpha distributions have a stable environment in which people
> > can work, then people can basically use the machine as a production
> > machine and fix porting problems in applications. But at the moment,
> > having used Linux/Alpha very happily for 5 months, I would not recommend
> > it to be used by anyone on a day-to-day basis.
> >
>
> You've used it "happily" but don't recommend it. Seems a contradictionto me.

Yeh - I was unhappy with it to start with; and it still REALLY annoys me from
time
to time. But I'm capable of debugging things and working through problems; many
Linux/Intel users can just get on and use their machine - you can't do that on
Alpha.

> Shouldn't live on the bleeding edge. If this bothers you, go back to RH4.2or wait for RH5.1. Are you
> using a Matrox Millenium II or the old
> card? Mine is the old card and it works fine. It's interesting to me that
> people just expect things to work for their HW setup. I usually go
> find out what works and only buy that to start with. Doesn't matter
> what OS/HW combination it is. That's pretty much the smartest
> way to go to minimize headaches later.

You can't buy the old Mill's any more. Yes I do expect one of the most popular
cards to work, especially when the source is out there.

> Well, it's there enough for folks to buy 50-100 systems for compute
> farms so I guess I disagree with your conclusion.

Yeh - but not for general use; Linux/Alpha works OK where you can afford to
have a bit of a fight to get your single application running and then leave
it burning CPU cycles all day.
