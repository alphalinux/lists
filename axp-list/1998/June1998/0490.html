>>> Greg Lindahl said:
>
> RedHat doesn't distribute the .config files used to build their
> "default" kernels, even though a bunch of people have asked them.

The "default kernels" for 5.1 were built by me, and the .config files
I used are available where I put the kernels for RH to pick up:

gatekeeper.dec.com:/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/Test-pre34-11b/*.config

Pick "arc.config" for ARC/AlphaBIOS booting, and "srm.config" otherwise.

> If 5.1 actually shipped with 2.0.34, then the question is: which is
> better for alphas, RedHat's 2.0.34 or Jay Estbrook's 2.0.34? I'd bet
> that Jay's is a bit better, but maybe someone knows for sure.

The kernels I built were based on Alan Cox's pre-2.0.34-11b sources, along
with a port of my patches for 2.0.32. As such, some of the drivers in the RH
5.1 kernels are not as recent as those in released 2.0.34 (which is what the
recent RH update RPMS for the kernel contain) or the released
alpha-sources-2.0.34-0.1. This *may *not be A Bad Thing (tm), as I've
experienced more instability with the released 2.0.34 drivers than with
those in pre-11b, and I believe others have reported this as well.

As I stated in a previous post, the alpha-patches-2.0.34 are intended to be
a "work-in-progress"; as such, I expect changes to those drivers to be a
necessary thing (either down-grading to a previous known-to-work version, or
upgrade to an as-yet unincorporated version). So PLEASE, report successes,
problems, and workarounds to the axp-list, we'll work on this together...
