On Mon, 29 Jun 1998, Michal Jaegermann wrote:

> Christopher Curtis writes:
> > However, if you do recompile, you're better to support the new
> > hardware non-modularly.
>
> Actually, if only feasible, the opposite is really true. If you have
> problems you have a much bigger flexibility with a module (and a better
> chance that you will get a meaningful error message on an attempt to
> loade instead of a total crash) and is faster to try some variation,
> option or a new module instead of recompiling the whole kernel from
> scratch over and over.

True ... but I was talking of a "process". First, you compile module
support. Then when you get new hardware, you only need to compile the
module that the hardware needs. After that, ... "if you do recompile,
you're better to support the new hardware non-modularly."

> There are also some things (VFAT support, or iso9660 - maybe, or
> "scsi-generic" for writing CDs) which are used only from time to time
> and are much better of as modules. Also initializing Plug&Pray sound

Yes, I agree. I just didn't want to try to enumerate all the things that
would be better as modules; besides, they do tend to be minor. Some
filesystems, maybe a couple drivers (pcmcia), but by and large the
hardware you have and want to support rarely benefits much from modules,
and there are often some wierdness that ends up making option modules
non-optional anyway (I compiled a serial driver as a module once, but
after so much idle time in X the driver would get unloaded, or gpm stopped
working, or something like that, so the mouse wouldn't work). I still
think that most of the emphasis people put on modules are rarely worth the
consideration when you're talking about a determined and stable system,
but I don't mean to put down modules where they are most needed and
useful, which, imo, is primarily unknown systems and those with extreme
memory needs. The next useful thing, of course, is hardware upgrades, but
I presume you're not advocating compiling every single option as a module
at once in case you ever want to use any of them (versus compiling one
module at a time as new hardware is acquired).

Dunno about PnP sound cards, but I imagine that once the settings are
determined, there's no need to retain them as modules either.
