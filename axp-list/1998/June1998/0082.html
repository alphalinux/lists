X has a large footprint in terms of networking, which is a legitimate complaint to
this day. It could have been a lot more lightweight. That's especially true on
single-workstation environments.

But what's really wrong with X? There are too many window managers and too many
widget kits. If everyone likes MFC and a Windows 95-like window manager, that's
what has to be done. And some pre-canned apps. NeXT and Be went in the right
direction, but went proprietary.

Gnome and KDE are the kind of solutions you'd wish there were several years ago.
But KDE uses Qt, yet another widget library for X, and Gnome enforces no window
manager choice on you.

And then there's X11R6.4's licensing, a move that's a deathknell (what was TOG
thinking? Making profit on something contributed YEARS AGO?)

Anyway, my rants. And the local bitmap crap IS hideous!
