> allright people, I 'm ready to believe that 2.1.x R stable ones.
> if U explain me why RedHAT, Slackware linuxes go with 2.0.x kernel.

2.1.x are experimental kernels. Use at your own risk.
2.0.n are snap shots of 2.1.x kernels which have been blessed.

n and x do not coincide. That is to say, 2.0.30 is not 2.1.30
released.

Lots of folks run 2.1.x kernels. Many of those people swear by the
2.1.x kernels. If you don't know the risks, use 2.0.n kernels.
