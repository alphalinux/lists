Hi Steve !

> > My guesses here are that 08:02 is not your root filesystem, that you
> > compiled something as a module that you shouldn't have (like your HD
> > driver, or ext2fs, or something else needed to operate the disk), or that
> > your setup is basically wrong.
>
> Correct -- my SCSI card was compiled in as a module.

;-}

> I'm still having problems (missing 'net-pf-4' and 'net-pf-5', and a
> complaint about unresolved symbols in 'ncr53c8xx.o', and an unaligned trap
> right after the "Partition check".)

Missing net-pf-4 and -5 is ok, these are the net protocol familiy (net-pf)
ipx and appletalk.

simply add

alias net-pf-4 off
alias net-pf-5 off

to /etc/conf.modules to get rid of these messages.

Unreserved symbols in ncr53c8xx.o ?
You compiled your SCSI-controller as module and later you included
it to your kernel to mount your root-filesystem. I think that
ncr53c8xx is now in your kernel and ncr53c8xx.o is still in
/lib/modules/$(uname -r)/scsi because of your first compile.
So don't care about it or remove ncr53c8xx.o

The unaligned trap after partition check is a known problem.
Don't care about it, it doesn't hurt.
