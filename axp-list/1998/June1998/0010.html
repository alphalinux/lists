Here's what I said last October:

First off, we really don't recommend FLASHing MILO if there's any way to
avoid it. It reduces flexibility, and increases the chances that something
might get messed up in FLASH.

Now, I say it reduces flexibility for the following reasons:

1. You'll only be able to boot LINUX from it, not NT or Digital UNIX.
That may not be an issue *now*, but may be later on. And yes, one
can re-FLASH ARC or SRM console, using the DebugMonitor, but this
is not for the faint of heart... :-\

2. MILO has been developed primarily to do two things:

a) provide the appropriate PALcode for the LINUX kernel
b) provide a way to boot the LINUX kernel

It is *NOT* really intended to completely replace ARC or SRM as a
manager of the hardware. There are myriad details of hardware init
on the various platforms that MILO depends on the other consoles to
perform, before it even runs. If you FLASH MILO, you will be leaving
those details undone, with possibly bad results.

--Jay++
