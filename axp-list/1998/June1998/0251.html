All,

rather than just sign off this list an disappear into obscurity, I thought that I'd
let you all know what's happening. This Friday is my last Friday at Digital after
13 years. I work for the part of Digital Semiconductor that was bought by Intel
last Autumn (Jim and Jay work for the part bought by Compaq). This means that
as the business transitions to Intel, I transition also. As for Alpha, I've not been
doing an awful lot of engineering, just keeping a watching brief. Jay (surely one
of the coolest guys on the planet) has picked up the mantle of MILO and done a
good job. Meanwhile, I've been playing with StrongARM - I run a group which
generates tools and OS porting software for StrongARM.

I am not, as you might imagine, going to Intel. Intel does no engineering in
Europe (they are very US-centric). No instead, I (and my group) are going to
ARM in Maidenhead. Basically we'll carry on doing what we were doing (and
then some) but become less StrongARM-centric. ARM's a lovely architecture
('cept the memory management unit, but I'll not mention that) and the company
has a good feel - I think that I'll enjoy it there. I wish the StrongARM group
well as they transition to Intel - I believe that Intel will do great things with
StrongARM.

I also believe that the FTC announcement ensures the future of Alpha and
that Maddog (another of the planet's coolest individuals) will continue to do that
vision thing that he does so well. One man (Maddog) with a long level (Linux)
moved a big company (Digital, 65,000) - after that Compaq should be easy.

Meanwhile, Linux and Alpha Linux will continue to be part of my working and
personal life. All (and I mean all) of the StrongARM software that I have
developed in the last two years was developed on my trusty PC164. My home
Cyrix box has also helped. That will continue at ARM and ARM has a goodly
number of Linux enthusiasts within it. I also want to expand my involvement with
the ARM Linux work being organised by Russell King.

So, so long and thanks for all of the fish. I'll drop out of this list and then
rejoin when I've got my compute environment set up at ARM. My e-mail
address is extremely likely to be david.rusling@arm.com

Ka'plaagh!

Dave
