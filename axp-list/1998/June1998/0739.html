With regards to your message at 10:32 AM 05-19-98 -0700, Gary A. Allen.
Where you stated:
>
>My machine is an AlphaPC 164LX running at 533 Mhz. The original installation
>is Red Hat 5.0. I'm running Linux 2.0.32 . My SCSI card is an ITIpci SCSI
and
>Ethernet host adapter. I have two SCSI hard disks and a SCSI CD-ROM.
>
>The problem:
>
>When I do a hardware restart, my SCSI bus gets restarted three times, viz.
>once for the AlphaBIOS, once by MILO and once again by the kernel. Each
>restart of the SCSI bus involves searching for the various SCSI devices.
>These multiple restarts of the SCSI bus is the most time consuming part of
>rebooting the system. It seems to me that the SCSI bus should only go
>through one restart during bootup.
>
>Question:
>
>Does anyone have a clue about how to deactivate these unnecessary SCSI bus
>restarts?
>
Well, for starters, in the ITI setup (press CTRL-C at the prompt on
startup) you may turn off scanning the bus for devices on a by-device
basis. Just turn them all off.
That's 1 down, 2 to go! ;^}
