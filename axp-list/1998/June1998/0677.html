I think I figured out the bogomips number. For a given computer, take the
frequency (truncate below the millions), multiply it by the number of
integer functional units and you should arrive close to the right number.
UDB - 166 Mhz * 1 IFU ~= 166 bogomips (real ~ 164bm). Pentium I MMX - 200
MHz * 2 IFU ~= 400 bogomips (real ~ 404bm). This works for what I've seen
before, and this algorithm makes sense. It doesn't characterize the
system in general like cache, ram, HD bus, it only gives a general idea of
how fast the processor is.
