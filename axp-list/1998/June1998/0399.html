OK, where should I start.....

GCC 2.8.1 is a newer version of GCC; from the same guys, and thats all you
get - the compiler. Then you'll need to find a libstdc++ to go with it.

EGCS takes the development snapshots of GCC and works on them, releasing
snapshots every week or so and releases every couple of months. EGCS is a
lot more experimental than GCC; they have things like wacky optimisers and
stuff. EGCS optimisations eventually get merged into GCC.
EGCS is normally distributed with its own version of libstdc++ which
matches the compiler.

Which to use? Well if you want stability then use GCC; if you want the
leading edge then use EGCS. The leading edge can mean more performance,
bug fixes before they get into GCC - and potentially new and more unusual
bugs and problems. So its a trade off; sometimes you may find that EGCS
works where GCC 2.8.1 won;t because a bug fix has got in; sometimes hte
opposite because a new EGCS feature hasn't been tested fully yet. Having
said that, the EGCS guys are nice guys and tend to fix bugs fairly fast
when you give them a good bug report.
