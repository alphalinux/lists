> I'm a bit confused as to what Redhat considers the "default" kernel
> provided with their CDROMs. This is for at least two reasons:
>
> 1) I've been unable to compile an identical kernel (RH 5.0).
> Every kernel I compile is MUCH larger than the kernel provided.
> I don't understand why!
>
> 2) The provided RH5.0 kernel (2.0.30) came with module support,
> while the RH 5.1 (2.0.34) apparently does not.

RedHat doesn't distribute the .config files used to build their
"default" kernels, even though a bunch of people have asked them.
However, folks have had luck using "make config" and then "make boot".
If you just type "make" you get an uncompressed kernel, which is
much larger than the kernel provided.

If 5.1 actually shipped with 2.0.34, then the question is: which is
better for alphas, RedHat's 2.0.34 or Jay Estbrook's 2.0.34? I'd bet
that Jay's is a bit better, but maybe someone knows for sure.
