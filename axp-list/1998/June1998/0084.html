At 09:07 AM 19-06-98 -0400, Christopher Curtis wrote:
>On Fri, 19 Jun 1998, Huw Davies wrote:
>
>> I've ftp'd down the complete 2.0.34 kernel and tried to build it on my
>> XL366 and my UDB but I've had several problems with it.
>>
>> On both systems the kernel compiles correctly but on the XL366 the system
>> dies with a kernel oops after attempting to turn swapping on. The UDB dies
>> during the boot as it fails to mount the root file system.
>
>I had the same problem. Did you compile in sound support? Did you accept
>the default values? Do you read something about a bad IRQ when it fails
>to boot? The MSS default IRQ is 11. This is the IRQ of your SCSI
>controller. Recompile with a MSS IRQ of 9.

Indeed this was the cause of SCSI not working on the UDB. I'd just assumed
that the values stored in /etc/soundconf would do the trick. Adding them in
manually and the system now boots.

Unfortunately, the new kernel boots and shows that networking no longer
exists. A quick look at the .config file created shows that no ethernet
drivers had been built. In fact, a fair number of the "usual" questions
weren't asked during a "make config". I copied the .config that I had saved
from the 2.0.32 kernel but this doesn't seem to do the right thing either.

Am I doomed to stay with 2.0.32 or am I am doing something really stupid -
I blame work if I am as I can only look at Linux for a few minutes or so
each day....
