> there is a limit to his kindness. If I simply use a trio of IP addresses
> on
> his subnet do you think it will work? He gave me three IP addresses on the
> RAS subnet (the same subnet I dial into). Are you saying that I'll need to
> have my own subnet and that I can't just use addresses on his?

Generally remote networks need to operate on their own subnet. Stealing
a few addresses doesn't quite work, although NT RAS does for simple
remote dialups with a trick known as proxy ARP. I'm not sure NT will
proxy/forward an entire remote network however.

To do it properly, you will need a static IP from the RAS server, a
dedicated subnet of at least 8 addresses, and your network admin must add
a route table entry for your subnet. It *might* be possible with dynamic
routing such as RIP, but all this is more trouble than it's worth IMO.

Since what you are doing is common and not simple or economical with
standard routing techniques, alternatives like NAT and IP masquerading
in particular are highly popular. If you really don't want to use your
UDB as a gateway, you can do NAT on NT too with the help of commercial
software. (But you might find a modem for your UDB is much more affordable.)
