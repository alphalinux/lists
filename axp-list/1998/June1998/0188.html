"installing" the modem is not a problem. I'm currently using a modem and a
local network. I might not use my UDB as the gateway machine for the Cable
Modem after all, because I'm already using the built in networking. But the
alternative is an ISA ethernet card in my 486, and I'm not sure if that would
have the bandwidth for cable modem.

One of my co-workers had the cable guy in installing his cable modem in a
Windows box. The cable guy looked at his basement network and said "As soon
as I leave, you've going to plug it into your hub, aren't you?" He said no,
but the cable guy didn't beleive him. So as soon as the cable guy left, he
plugged it into a second ethernet card on his Linux/Intel box.
