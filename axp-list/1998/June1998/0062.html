On Mon, 22 Jun 1998 Ian Grant wrote:
> On Sat, 20 Jun 1998 edhall@ayched.com wrote:
> > I've recently upgraded my 164LX to Red Hat 5.1. As a result, one of my
> > numerical programs (which happens to do lots of square roots) has slowed
> > down by a factor of about seven! Recompiling with profiling confirms that
> > it is now spending an overwhelming amount of its time in "__ieee754_sqrt".

> I haven't seen this, but I'm not too surprised. IIRC there was a bug in
> the glibc square root function on Alphas, so your previous code may have
> been faster, but the results could well have been wrong.

Maybe, but I doubt that a fix would cause it to slow down by over an order
of magnitude.

> You could install the SRPMs for the old glibc and the new one, locate the
> sqrt code and do a diff to see what's happened.

Good idea; I tried a somewhat different tack, and used an adaptation of Cray's
fast math library's sqrt(). Now my code runs faster than ever. And at least
for the domain I'm using it over, the Cray routine seem accurate.
