>
>[Sorry about the delay in the announcement, it's been a bit crazy here... ]
>
>I have made available in the usual location, gatekeeper.dec.com, both a set
>of Alpha-related patches against pristine linux-2.0.34, and a full source
>tree with those patches already applied. Look for:
>
> /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-patches-2.0.34-0.1.gz # patches
> /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-sources-2.0.34-0.1.tar.gz # sources
>
>The Alpha-related patches are essentially a port of my previous patches,
>2.0.32-0.1, to the new kernel sources, with certain additions and deletions.
>Added to the patches is support for new hardware (Samsung UX/BX baords), as
>well as fixes which have accumulated since 2.0.32-0.1 were released, namely
>the EM86 STACK_TOP fix, the old/new MIATA fix, among others. Deleted from
>the patches were many changes related to the read/write routines' arguments,
>and changes that had been made to accompany the rather patchwork LIBC we all
>used until GLIBC-2 came along.
>
>These patches/sources are intended to be more of a work-in-progress, with
>more frequent updating, than previous versions. This is primarily due to the
>more rapid development/release of drivers, along with the more frequent
>appearance of new hardware, in platforms, controllers and peripherals. Also,
>I'm not totally convinced that some of the driver work that went into 2.0.34
>is as stable (on Alphas, anyway) as some of the older versions.
>
>Another addition, the latest stable version of the ncr53c8xx driver, 2.6n,
>is now included, as there are just now appearing some SCSI controller cards
>that require its enhancements.
>
>Also, because of some problems encountered with Donald Becker's tulip.c
>driver, version 0.88, which appears in pristine 2.0.34, I replaced it with
>an older version, 0.83, which, unfortunately, I didn't test to make sure it
>would build with the 2.0.34 sources; sorry - my fault.
>
>Some people on axp-list have found that a later version of tulip.c (0.89H?)
>solves their problems; sufficient positive feedback along these lines, with
>this and other drivers, would be good enough reason for the next set of
>patches/sources to include those "validated" drivers.
>
>I am depending on all of you to help determine the best set of drivers to
>use on Alpha, as we here at DEC simply no longer have the time, or access to
>hardware, to test all the possible combinations that are present in your
>machines.
>
>Please, continue posting any problems encountered with the patches/sources
>to axp-list, as I follow it very closely.
>
>Good luck.
