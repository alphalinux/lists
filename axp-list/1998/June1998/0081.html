On Mon, 22 Jun 1998, Paul Tomblin wrote:
> > Does X-Free even support the printing extensions? I know they don't
> > support multiheadedness (yet?)...
>
> It's hard to do printing in an operating system independant way.
> As for multi-headedness, I used to have a two headed VaxStation GPX running
> two heads on X10! I'd be real suprised if X11 doesn't do it.

Why in God's name would you want printing extensions to a windowing
system? I don't get it. Talk about bloatware.

As for multiheadedness: X11 does multi-head just fine. That is not to
say XFree86 does it, but other servers do. MetroX and AcceleratedX do
multi-head very well. I've had tri-headed Linux boxes working well
with MetroX.

> > There's just a few integration issues that are missing ... XFree allows
> > you to change resolutions without restarting, but what about color depth?
>
> XFree, probably not.

Right.. XFree != X

> Being able to run different window managers and different
> upper level apis (Motif, athena, raw Xt) is both a blessing and a curse of X.
> Mostly a blessing, I think.

Absolutely! One of the things I like most about X is the different apis
that are available and that manifest themselves on my desktop during
the course of getting work done.

If my desktop consistently looked like Win95 or KDE I'd go insane..
it's bad enough that I have to go out of my way taking steps to avoid
business beige.

This is not to take anything away from the KDE crew, of course.. I have
KDE installed and use many of the "KDE" applications, but the whole
scheme rubs me the wrong way. Filebrowsers and trashcan icons aren't
for me. Just give me an rxvt with a neat pixmap background and I'll
be happy.. when I'm feeling nostalgic I'll turn on the ampex vt220
terminal and burn the eyes out of my skull with the amber screen.
