> Hello,
> I just purchased a Matrosx Millenium I 4meg card for my UDB. However,
when I tried to install the card, the machine didn't boot up. I figured that a
video conflict might be the source, so I tried removing the card and switched
back to the onboard TGA. Unfortunately, the box still will not boot. I don't
get any flashes from the LED, nor does my monitor ever switch on (it stays in
power-save mode). I have already tried reseating the memory, reinstalling the
card, checking all power connections, but to no use. Can anyone please offer
any help?
>

I tried a Matrox Mystique and mine wouldn't boot either, the machine seemed to
die at the SRM console on the TGA output and nothing on the Matrox. Took the
Matrox out and the machine got into ARC but all hell broke loose with the NVRAM
apparently having been wiped. Anyway, I then tried an S3Trio64V2 card with 2MB
and it got into ARC and I corrected the various errors (date/time and boot
parameters) and got back into Linux but once that started booting the kernel
woudln't recognise the VGA card so the thing booted with the TGA live. I
compiled the kernel with TGA disabled and tried again and this time it fired up
OK and I installed the 5.0 XFree_S3 rpm and got X up on my crappy SVGA monitor.

Then I plugged my Sun monitor in with the appropriate modelines from my TGA set
up and the thing would not work, seems the S3 is rather more strict about the
scan frequencies than the TGA and I couldn't get anything to work. Back to the
SVGA at 1024x768i to try it out and found that yes, the TGA card is pretty slow
by comparison with this rather feeble PCI card, and it was nice to have 16 bit
colour. But the bloody thing locks up hard, can't even ping the machine. So,
the card is out and I'm back to the TGA.

Any suggestions on a better card, one that works maybe? I read somewhere that
the Diamond Stealth 3D is flexible enough to drive my monitor, anyone got one
running in a UDB?
