With regards to your message at 08:12 PM 05-11-98 -0300, Carlos Carvalho.
Where you stated:
>OOOOps!!!... I am/was just getting to put milo on flash! This is quite
>a disappointment :-(
>
>Here's how I understand the situation. Please correct me if I'm wrong.
>
>I have two machines with arc/alphabios. The only way to make the M$
>firmware boot linux is to put linload and milo in a dos partition :-(
>This is a very ugly hack which I'd really like to get rid of. I'll
>probably leave it booting from floppy instead :-(
Why is this "an ugly hack"?
It works, is stable, and further allows the making of a boot floppy a
simple pice of work. You can even be cretive and just dd to/from the FAT16
partition and a preformatted floppy to make emergency disks.

>The other possibility is to put milo on flash. I have a LX164 and an
>EB164. Is it really dangerous to put milo on flash with these boards
>or just obsolete? I intend to keep the kernel current, but I'll only
>touch milo when it no longer boots the new kernel.
No, it's not dangerous, of course if you screw up the flash you can deal
with the joy of learning how to do a fail-safe recovery disk.

>In fact I think milo depending on the kernel isn't good. I'd rather
>keep the kernel as much self contained as possible to avoid
>incoherences. It seems this is done using the raw boot loader. How can
>I setup the machine to do it? Do I have to buy SRM? Can't I use the
>fail safe booter?

I have to ask "Why"??
What bothers you so much about linload and milo???
They work, they're stable, they are legal, and they are VERY easy to use.
As a mater of fact, the creation of the small FAT partition from AlphaBIOS
utilities makes it much simpler to prepare your SCSI hard disk later from
FDISK. With the small FAT partition on there fdisk happily recognises the
disk architecture.
