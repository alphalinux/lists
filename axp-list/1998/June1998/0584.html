On Thu, 2 Jul 1998, Stephen Gennard wrote:

> Hi Chaps,
>
> I am looking for a little advice. I am looking at buying a Alpha PC
> based machine that has a EIDE drive to run Redhat 5.1 linux on it.
>
> I saw some comments on the net saying the performance of linux on
> a alpha based EIDE machine was not too hot.... is this true?

I have replaced 4 EIDE drives in 5 machines and tha 5th is beginning to
show some problems(bad sectors). This combined with the lower performance
of scsi. Has lead me to the inescapable conclusion that EIDE is not the
way to go for a system that requires some reliablilty. For the extra few
dollars SCSI drives seem to provide better bang for the buck in terms of
reliability.

I have some SCSI drives here that are over 5 years old and still rolling
and in the past 7 or so years I have only had 2 SCSI drives fail in live
systems.

I know this makes no sense since the drives are supposed to have the same
mechanics and media but from what I can see there is some difference.

my $0.02 worth.
