>From InfoWorld:

"IBM in talks with Compaq about Alpha licensing

By Stefan van Mierlo
InfoWorld Electric

Posted at 7:14 AM PT, Jun 18, 1998
IBM is negotiating the licensing of the Alpha architecture from Digital
Equipment, now officially part of Compaq, a spokeswoman for IBM in the
Netherlands confirmed this week.

IBM confirmed that prior to the Compaq acquisition, Digital approached IBM
and that talks are now being held between Digital's new owner, Compaq, and
IBM. Big Blue did not want to elaborate on ongoing negotiations between the
two companies.

The U.S. Federal Trade Commission forced Digital to enter negotiations with
IBM and other processor manufacturers in April of this year, when the body
gave its seal of approval to Intel purchasing Digital's Alpha manufacturing
plant.

The settlement followed a period of litigation between Intel and Digital.
Digital sued Intel on May 12, 1997, claiming patent infringement. Intel's
response came on May 27 of the same year, when Intel countersued Digital. In
August 1997, Intel followed up with another lawsuit, accusing Digital of
using Intel patents in the Alpha processors. Eventually, in October of last
year, both parties agreed to settle.

The settlement resulted in Intel and Digital agreeing to exchange their
respective patents for a 10-year period and to Digital's sale of its Alpha
manufacturing facilities to Intel. Intel, in turn, is obliged to produce the
chips for Digital -- now part of Compaq -- for the next seven years.
Development of future versions of the Alpha processor will still be
conducted by Digital, which kept the intellectual property rights to the
Alpha. This means that Digital can have third-party involvement in the
development of the processor.

The FTC had to give its approval for the settlement, considering the
dominant position of Intel in processor chips. Documents released by the FTC
reveal that the agency would only agree to the terms of the settlement if
Digital would also make its Alpha technology available to chip manufacturers
other than Intel. The FTC went so far as to mention these companies: Digital
must enter talks with Korean vendor Samsung Electronics, which already
produces Alpha chips, as well as with Advanced Micro Devices and IBM.

On Thursday a Compaq official in Hong Kong said his company was holding
talks with AMD over licensing Alpha technology. "
