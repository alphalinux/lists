With regards to your message at 09:00 PM 05-21-98 +0300, Ioan MURESAN.
Where you stated:
>
> I installed milo in a small partition (FAT 1440 k) and I want to boot
> linux (RH 5.0) from first partition (root partition) of the first SCSI
> with:
>
> boot sda1:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda1
>
> or,
>
> boot -t ext2 sda1:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda1
>
> linux can't boot
>
> With:
>
> boot fd0:avanti.gz root=/dev/sda1, linux is booting from a FAT floppy
> disk.
>
> I think that my milo version can't recognize SCSI.
Noooo... I don't think you want root to be on a FAT partition!
Look at your line:
boot sda1:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda1
Now isn't your root partition ( where the kernel, bin, etc, and so on live,
on sda2?
That is to say the SECOND partition, which is of course an e2fs partition?

Should this not be:
boot sda1:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda2
