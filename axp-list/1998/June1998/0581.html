Hello fellow alphaheads,

My Noname with Red Hat 5.1/Alpha is having some trouble with EGCS
(1.0.2 shipped with Manhattan) compiled kernels. After booting it's
stable for some time, but after doing some heavy stuff that makes use of
the swap partition, it starts to give "Unable to handle kernel paging
request" errors (see the log below) and processes start to die,
typically kswapd goes first, and then other processes, one by one...

At first I thought 2.0.34pre6 was broken and got back using my
previous 2.0.30 kernel which has been pretty stable for long time,
and it worked fine with the Manhattan setup too.

Until yesterday, when I had to recompile the 2.0.30 to add SCSI tape
support. I used the exact same source that was working fine with
Hurricane (compiled with GCC), but now I'm getting same kind of kernel
Oopses with 2.0.30 too!

BTW, the are the shipped kernels compiled with EGCS or GCC?

It seems to me that dropping GCC from the Alpha distribution was a
mistake -- or I'm the only one having problems like this? Oh, and
please make sure your Alpha has been using a swap partition before
telling me your kernel is okay!

Pablo
