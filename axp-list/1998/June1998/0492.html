On Tue, 12 May 1998, Ben T. Eisemann wrote:
> First off I thought I would let you know that I gave up trying to configure
> my seagate on my udb (sold it to a friend). Now with that hard drive out
> of the way and some money in my pocket I am in search of a hard drive.
> First off, I was considering purchasing a 1GB jaz drive (dont have enough
> money for the 2GB) and connecting to the UDB and using the included 1GB
> catridge as my hard disk.

> A little background note, I am going to be using this system in a research
> course next year where I will experimenting with parrallel processing over
> a heterogeneous network. Since the box will stay at the school most of the
> time I would like to from time to time remove the hard drive and all
> precious data for safe keeping.

I don't know how much insertion / ejection that the cartridges are
designed to take, but I believe that every cycle introduces the ever so
slight chance that you'll damage the disk or drive.

> Ok back to my question.
> Will this setup work? How reliable will it be? Any other concerns I should
> know about?

Removables are inherently less reliable than fixed disk drives. I have
heard that a bad Jaz disk can ruin an otherwise good Jaz drive, on two
separate occassions, each occasion ruining three or four drives. Both I
heard about because close colleages had the experience.

> Secondly, I would like to hear what everybody is using in their UDB? For
> compatibility reasons if i decide not to go with the Jaz.

If you don't need to exchange disks, consider a Syjet. The disks are
cheaper and cost less, you get two disks with the drive (at least in US),
the capacity is 40-50% larger, they both run about the same speed
(performance), and the drives usually are cheaper than Jaz. Mine has
worked OK with my UDB until a friend jarred the drive harshly. It was
under warranty, so SyQuest gave me a new drive in retail box. I still
haven't figured out what to do with four 1.5GB disks.
