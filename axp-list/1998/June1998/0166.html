Quoting Steve Frampton (frampton@mail.flarc.edu.on.ca):
>
> If I do a 'tar ztvf /dev/sr0' I see all the files that got tarred before I
> aborted. I removed the tape from the drive and tried again -- yep, there
> they are again!
>
> I was just wondering what this device is -- a ramdisk, perhaps? -- and if
> I might have done something bad. :-(

Do an "ls -l" of /dev/sr0 and see if it's even a device driver. You might
have just created a normal file in /dev, which isn't very useful.

crw-rw---- 1 root disk 9, 0 Jul 1 1996 /dev/st0
^
that's the flag that will tell you it's a character special device, rather
than a plain file. You might also see a 'b' there for block special. Either
way, device files will have those two numbers (in this case 9, 0) which tell
you the major and minor device number.

You can look up major device numbers in /usr/include/linux/major.h
