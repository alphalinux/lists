> I'm new to Alpha Linux and I'm having trouble installing my AlphaStation
> 200 4/233. I've looked for information, but so far nothing has worked.

Sounds REALLY familiar -- am going through the same process right now.

> When I try to boot the MILO disk, I got "File system not recognized." I
> copied the avanti image (renamed "milo") and linload.exe to a DOS
> formatted floppy, but I try to boot this MILO disk, a wierd flashing
> green pattern covers the screen after the "Swapping to PALCode at 0x..."
> message. The system appears to hang at this point.

That's the video card... Seems like the RH5.1 isn't quite perfect. Take
out the TGA card and throw in a standard VGA card (I used a PCI one, but
ISA will probably work as well) and try it again. MILO doesn't appear to
be configured to handle the TGA card.

> 4. Are there any other pitfalls I should be aware of?

More than likely. The "avanti" images on the RH5.1 disk didn't work for
me -- it started dumping out zillions of "unexpected interrupt 0" messages
shortly after the kernel started initializing. I was able to use a "lx"
image, but I don't think it was the one on the CD...

The lack of documentation was a real pain -- took over a week of trial and
error to finally figure out what was going on. I took lots of notes -- if
I think about it when I'm at work tomorrow, I'll forward you a copy of
them.

Good luck -- Mike
