Bart-Jan Vrielink wrote:

> > Can someone explain to me exactly what the problem is?
> > Why is the Alpha slower at IDE than the Pentium systems
> > under Linux; even a Pentium configured for the lowest
> > spec IDE controller is faster.
>
> The problem is that the IDE drivers are not very optimized for those
> machines. I've heard there has been some improvement on those drivers, but
> I don't know who is working on it.
> The controller itself is fast enough although not 'Enhanced'IDE.

I was looking for a more technical description of the problem; I thought
that the generic IDE drivers under x86 are giving better performance than
under Linux. I can understand specialised drivers which can use the
new features of the newer controllers to get speed; but why is the
generic one slower.

> Why did you spend your money on such a fast CPU and not on some good and
> reliable disks. SCSI is soo much better than IDE.

Because in some parts of the world, SCSI discs are 2 or 3 times more
expensive than IDE and the best IDE drives are a lot faster than
all but the fastest SCSI discs.

For example, our local dealer sells a 16.8GB IBM Ultra-IDE drive for
#382 (UK pounds, inclusive of tax) and sells a 9GB IBM SCSI-Wide (7200RPM)
for #417 and he also sells an 8.4GB IBM Ultra-IDE for #186
Now a friend has just bought one of those 8.4GB IDE drives and was getting
9.4MB/second out of it on a new Pentium2.
My 7200 RPM Barracuda on my Alpha's SCSI is giving me about
6MB/second.

So on that basis I'd happily swap my Barracuda for an IDE drive
because its cheaper, quieter, cooler and it should easily equal
the speed.

Dave
