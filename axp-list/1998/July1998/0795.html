I have been seeing some odd behavior (SCSI errors and system
crashes) on my UDB in last couple of days. Over the weekend I copied the
UDB's system from its internal 340MB SCSI disk to an external 4.5GB IBM
SCSI disk. I then removed the internal disk, and connected an Exabyte tape
drive to the external scsi chain. I then tried compiling a kernel with
Fast SCSI enabled for the scsi controller. On boot up everything looks
fine with the Exabyte drive accepting 5MHz sync SCSI and the HD accepting
10MHz sync SCSI. Everything was working fine for a day, including an
incremental backup of the system to the tape drive.
Today though the system was crashed from an attempted backup the
night before. I rebooted and tried the backup again, only to shortly have
the system crash again. I rebooted then to an older kernel without fast
scsi support. Everything seems fine now, but there is no tape support in
this kernel (building a new one now with tape and no fast scsi), and scsi
HD performance is in the gutter (2MB/sec :( )
Is it foolish to try and mix different scsi speeds on the same
bus? Should I just stick with the lowest common denominator for all
devices on the bus? Also, is there something I should have done to the
internal scsi chain when I removed the internal drive? I just removed the
drive and the cable. I saw no evidence of jumpers or such to turn on
termination at the card. Thanks!

PS. The 4.5GB IBM disk is an Ultrastar 9ES 7200RPM Ultra SCSI disk
that has been connected to the system for two weeks and has been used as a
scratch disk with no problems.

PPS. Thanks to everyone for all of the information on the XL366. I
am currently in the progress of purchasing one. Until then I have to limp
along with my UDB. :(
