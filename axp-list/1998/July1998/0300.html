I've got a list of about 8-10 users who are all experiencing a lot of
problems with memory violation errors. I've compiled information from
all of them, and am simply lost as to what the cause may be. Nobody
seems able to help, and we are all about ready to go back to Intel boxes
out of desperation.

We all get errors of the type:
ipop3d: memory violation at 155558d7130 rp=15558ca81c (bad
address=15555d834a0)

This occurs with a variety of programs, but seems particularly prevalent
in mysqld and other daemons with a relatively high load. Here's the
confusing details:

1) One of us only has this problem with Mysqld. I only have this
problem with RPM's that I have compiled in house from SRPM's. Another
of us has these problems with an out-of-the-box installation.

2) We are all using RH 5.0

3) Most of us have PC 164LX, 533Mhz motherboards, but one of us has a
UDB 166. Most of us have tried two motherboards and two sets of RAM.

4) Glibc seems to be the location pointed to by ldd output most of the
time, but others have found the location to be in other libraries.

5) I have tried using both a base install, and a fully updated setup
(all of RH's errata RPMS). Same results both ways.

6) We have tried kernels ranging from 2.0.30 to 2.0.33 with a variety of
options.

7) I did not notice this problem on my first install of the OS. Later,
I needed to re-partition my drives, and that's when the problem
appeared. I have since re-installed three times with no luck getting
the problem to go away.

Does anyone have any suggestions for us? These errors eventually kill
most of our daemons, so the Alpha boxes are essentially useless to us.
Surely, there must be something we are all doing wrong since the
majority of the list seems to have no idea what we are talking about.
Any suggestions as to where we should start looking?

Thanks,

Dana
