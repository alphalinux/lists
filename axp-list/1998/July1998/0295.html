> Current error sd08:03: sense key Medium Error
> scsidisk I/O error: dev 08:03, sector 423000, absolute sector 601176

The drive itself reports a "Medium Error".
For this message to appear, a number of things happened to your machine:

*) the SCSI-driver sends an SCSI command to the drive, e.g. read/write
*) processing the command, the drive detects the damaged sector
*) the drive enters the message out phase to transmit a status message
(command complete) with status code "check condition".
*) the driver issues the "request sense" command to obtain the status
page for the last "check condition".
*) therein it reads 03 hex in the lower nibble of byte 3, which says
"the last check condition was due to a medium error"

Now the driver has to trust the drive about this - the drive even reports
the exact sector number (write it down and check that this number is
always in the same range, more or less).

Anyway, I would never rely on this drive to be functional again.
Not after reformatting and running it for a week without error.
Never.

Your SCSI hardware seems to be functional, otherwise there is no way
to come that far, and the chance that this is a software bug is fairly
small I think. Do not rely even on a new disc to be OK, I know of
several Quantum-Atlas (new discs) failing after a month or even after
2 weeks... (though this was under heavy news-server usage).

> nasty and go-and-hang-myself and stuff?

If you put sensible data on this drive again, I guess yes.
I would say, you put in a new drive and restore your backup.
Or at least check your data. Other (yet unused) files may be gone...
