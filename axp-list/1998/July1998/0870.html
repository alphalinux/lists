I just downloaded the 2.0.35 kernel that was in the updates area on my
friendly neighbourhood RedHat mirror. I compiled it and installed it, and it
seemed to boot ok. But "uname -a" reports that it's 2.0.34. And a "gzcat
/vmlinux.gz | strings" shows that there are some strings in the kernel that
say 2.0.34 and others that say 2.0.35. Does this mean that the kernel source
file is screwed up some how?
