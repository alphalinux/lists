> That would require a cable to convert the 2.5" 44 pin cable to normal

> soon you are reaching into your pocket for money add a few hundred

I think I mentioned this.. I put all my SCSI drives in an external

>
> > 4. 18"?!?!?!? who cares? I personally have no need to mount my IDE

>
> Take a tape measure, run it from the middle of the case and back again,
> that is aprox. how long a cable you would use to put the drive in the
> external housing you proposed in #3. I would estimate that you would
> need at least a 12" cable to simply get the drive out of the way of the
> connectors on the back of the Multia.

Yeah.. if 18" really is the approx. cut off for IDE data, then pulling
this little feat off quickly heads toward being completely impractical.

As I said in my other message, I've never actually run IDE cabling
very far. I've run a floppy cable well over 24". I also gutted an old
Lee Data machine (some sort of communications equipt. It had 24 BNC
ethernet connectors on it, a full height floppy drive, and a few other
gadgets). The floppy data cable was pretty long.. almost as long as
my arm span. It was very heavily shielded, thats what makes it stand
out in my mind.

I can't think of any reason that floppy data can reach that far but
IDE can't. It's all crappy ribbon cable, and the electrical properties
of all the interfaces should be roughly the same.

Hell.. I might try to run this cable just to see if it works now.. now
I'm curious.
