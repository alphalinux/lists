Not to start a flame war, but I wanted to defend my subjective
objections...

Eric Johanson wrote:
>
> 1. snake it to a external IDE connection, mounted on the expansion slot.

He has SCSI connector there already.

> 2. IDE is slow. but it Still holds data.

But it's still slow.

> 3. If you feel that you will be short on power, use a external PS, inside
> a nifty Hard drive case.

That would require a cable to convert the 2.5" 44 pin cable to normal
3.5" 40 pin connector, and then you would need an external PS (which
I gather he does not have in his bin, he didn't mention it) - pretty
soon you are reaching into your pocket for money add a few hundred
megabytes of SLOW data storage (relative, compared to built-in SCSI).

> 4. 18"?!?!?!? who cares? I personally have no need to mount my IDE
> drive to the ceiling.

Take a tape measure, run it from the middle of the case and back again,
that is aprox. how long a cable you would use to put the drive in the
external housing you proposed in #3. I would estimate that you would
need at least a 12" cable to simply get the drive out of the way of the
connectors on the back of the Multia.

> 5. Short on space? Have you ever really LOOKED at your multa?
