> It's worth pointing out 5 is a very recently Sun-invention.

Not exactly. Sun and Linux both got the runlevel stuff from SVR4, which
defines a power off runlevel:

0 power off
1,s single user
2 multiuser
3 multiuser w/NFS
4 available for custom definition
5 halt (go to firmware)
6 reboot

Somehow Sun got levels 0 and 5 reversed in Solaris, but aside from those
two numbers being swapped the definitions are the same as SVR4.0 .
