On Sun, 5 Jul 1998, Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> On another note, those who run their computers with the covers off are
> asking for trouble. A chassis cooling fan needs an enclosure for the air to
> flow through. I fthe covers are off it does nothing to cool your computer.

As I've said before I think that depends if your computer was designed or
thrown together. If it was thrown together from a standard case with
a standard motherboard and a collection of other bits I don't think
there is any design of airflow at all.

You are of course right that any type of case extraction fan can't extract
if its not closed properly - but thats a luxury of a design that I suspect
many of us have to do with out.

(On a positive note I've just bought a beasty called a 'heat terminator'
which is a set of fans which sit on the front of a 5.25" bay with the
hard drive behind it and cool them off. I've put it in a bay above
my Seagate Barracuda, that bay has a 3.5" IDE drive in and the air is
going over that
and also between it and the Barracuda and makes the front of the case
almost acceptably cool. You can't feel much airflow from the device
but it sure has made a difference to the front of case temperature.)
