On Thu, 16 Jul 1998 23:08:00 -0400 (EDT), Christopher Curtis wrote:

>There really isn't an issue KDE or GNOME. And the issue isn't free versus
>less free. It's between reality and vapor. KDE is reality, and in
>reality it is good. GNOME is coming but is not viable. Other dists ship
>with KDE and that makes people happy. RedHat does not, and that makes
>different people feel differently. Either way, it is a RH business
>decision and not really worthy of debate in light of the horrid shape of
>recent RH distributions. Personally, I think they should stop all
>sponsored GNOME development and put together a dist actually worth buying.
>Once that is done, they can continue their crusade.

Opinions vary.

>In the meantime, let people use KDE if that's what they want. Linux is
>supposed to be about choice, be it choice of dists, choice of window
>managers, or choice of "environments". I find RH's decision implorable
>personally to ship nothing instead of something very good, just like I
>hated every second of their Win95 clone WM and their stock kernels with
>who-knows-what applied patches. Why aren't these patches part of the
>dist anyway?

We do not stop anyone from using KDE! I don't understand this assault
we have been receiving. We don't ship a lot of things for one reason
or another. We try very hard to make it so we are not impeding the
ability of people to exercise their own choice.

As far as the patches to the kernel, most of them make it into the next
release of a kernel. They are just patches that provide a greater
level of stability or functionality to the Red Hat user.

>Let's not bring the *.advocacy flamewar to this list, please...

Exactly, which is how I started this whole thread. Let me repeat,
please don't start a thread about Red Hat do this or don't do this
because XYZ does it. We have chosen a path which we feel is the right
way to go. We try very hard to not impede anyone along the way, but we
have made our choices. If you have specific issues with the quality of
our release, then I'd love to hear those reports.

Thanks,

Mike
