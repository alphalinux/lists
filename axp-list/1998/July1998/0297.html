I sure tried. I built gcc-2.8.1 and binutils-2.8.1 first, then attempted
a bootstrap build of glibc-2.0.6 under the new gcc and binutils. The
dynamic linker eventually failed when it reached rpcgen.

I may have done something wrong... but once I plugged in ld-2.0.5.so
from Redhat the build completed successfully, and I was able to rebuild
gcc and binutils against the new libc. Everything but ld.so worked as-is.

> Months and months ago I posted, several times, complete instructions

Sorry. I didn't know about your post, and didn't find it during a quick
scan of the archives. I sort of know what I'm doing, but may have missed
a step.

Moving to RH 5.0 is one way to go, but for now I need to support both
RH 4.2 and new glibc binaries. If I am not mistaken the library names
collide, so I must create a new root for the glibc 2.0.x libraries and
set LD_LIBRARY_PATH accordingly.

Besides, I wanted BWX enabled in the tools. I was astonished at how much
BWX reduced the size of the executables.
