Hello,
I bought one of these a few auctions ago (just got it yesterday),
and wanted to let you know that the machine is setup as follows:

Was a DU machine w/16 Megs installed for (Hughes Aeronautics I think),
so it has SRM installed (I assume).

The PCMCIA adapter is an ISA card that takes up one slot (there is
a total of 3 slots, one ISA, one ISA/PCI, and one PCI).

The 8 Meg video card comes with WinNT 3.51 drivers that MAY BE
transferable to WinNT 4.0 (based on what I read in the manuals
that accompanied it). The card comes in a NEW DEC option pack
with manuals/drivers/etc.

There are two 8 Meg SIMMs installed in the system unit as shipped,
there are 2 4 Meg SIMMs in an accessory bag in the box.

There is a total of 3 memory manks available (two filled with the
above memory).

There is no keyboard, no mouse and no HD - it appears that the
system was removed from Hughes unopened, the HD with DU on
it was removed, along with the keyboard/mouse.

It has 512K cache installed.

It comes with the "rails" to install a CD-ROM in the space provided
(there is a faceplate to cover the hole).

There is space for 2 3 1/2" SCSI hard drives, one exposed for
removeable
media (JAZ, ZIP, FLOPPY TAPE?!?!?), and one CD-ROM drive.

It was set for 230V (does DEC just do that since it is the safest
setting)

It is a nice, clean unit (never been used!) and it reportedly has a
one year warranty. It's a nice, low profile case.

I paid $309 + $20 S/H, I think it was a fair deal

I have learned after I bought mine that at least one person was able
to get the same unit for the vendor offering them through Onsale for
$219
with local pickup. That is/was a very good deal IMHO. (See
http://www.azdigital.com - they have complete systems for $995 listed)

The manual mentions floppy tape drive (there is an additional plug
on the floppy cable for another floppy drive (why?!) or Floppy tape
drive -
is that true, can this machine have a floppy tape drive under WinNT?
Linux?

Just wanted to let you know my reactions/thought on this machine...

Ken
