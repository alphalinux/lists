>OTOH, the C runtime is. And, I thought UNIX appeared in
>1969, not 1978. Had to have a PDP-8 or something like that
>to run it back then. First UNIX I used was on a PDP-11, long
>ago....far away...

PDP-7, not -8. Dunno if anyone ever made a UNIX that ran on an
-8, but it'd be an interesting toy. The -11 was more akin to the
-7 than the -8; the -8 was the end of the line of 12-bit machines,
in essence, following on the -5, though I vaguely recall that
either the -12 or -15 were 12-bit and maybe somewhat similar to
the -8 in terms of the ISA (Instruction Set Architecture).

(The -8 was my first "machine" in terms of learning assembly code;
the -10 was my second. Never did any actual assembly coding on
the -11, aside from just reading up on its ISA, as I did for the
-1 and so on.)

I have the "famous" Computer Engineering book from Digital, so if
needed, I suppose I can look things up in it to get the facts
straight. The above is mostly from core. :)
