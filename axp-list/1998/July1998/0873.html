> > DUMP: short read error from /dev/sda6: [block -557397338]: count=1024,got=0
> > DUMP: bread: lseek2 fails!
>
> Is it only when you use the tape? fwiw, I just backed up about 500MB to a
> local tape without any problems using tar and st0 on a UDB.

It's a potential disk problem, nothing to do with tape. I used to get
this intermittantly on my sun cluster backing up its TB of
disk. Actually what I really suspect is that dump has a bug that
causes it to occasionally get garbage data from an inode, so it
attempts to read bogus block numbers (note the negative number). But,
short of actually fixing dump (unreliable piece of shit that it is), I
would just double-check the hardware. Occasionally I'd find a problem.

If you restore a bunch of dumped partitions, you'll note that a few
percent of them are invalid, possibly for the same reason. Generally
it's just one poison file.
