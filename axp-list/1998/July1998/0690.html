David Alan Gilbert wrote:
>
> Various people have commented on my SCSI/IDE comparison and correctly
> said that SCSI can be better for some things and to be careful about
> the benchmarks.
>
> Perhaps I should have worded my reasons for wanting IDE differently;
> even if I was to use SCSI for my main drive, it would be really nice
> to be able to buy a cheap, large capacity IDE drive for all those
> other things which you accumulate - you know, the 3 or 4 versions of EGCS,
> 5 or 6 kernel source trees, that Mozilla compile tree (Hmm, perhaps
> a couple of terrabyte for that...), 2 or 3 root file system images.
>
> So I don't actually care whether the IDE drive is as fast as the SCSI;
> but I think everyone is agreeing that given the right drivers, on a PC,
> the IDE drives actually do a pretty good job; perhaps they aren't
> as good as a good SCSI drive for real apps, but they are actually
> not too bad. But I'd like to be able to get reasonable performance out of
> the drive, and on a PC I can do that and at the moment on an Alpha
> I can't.
>
> And for the money, perhaps I'd be better spending #180 on an 8GB IDE disc
> and using the #200 I saved on another 128MB of RAM - I'd like
> that choice.
>
> Now, back to the original question, a technical description of the problem
> which is slowing IDE down on the Alpha please. (Someone said
> look at the list archives, unfortunatly I've been unable to find the
> appropriate article and the search engines don't seem to be helping -
> pointers would be welcome).
>

I vaguely remember something about the ide.c DD has to do a
bunch of 16bit data moves, something an Alpha is not good at.
Take a look at the source for ide.c and it's related routines
and you should see the problem. Look for data copy loops.
