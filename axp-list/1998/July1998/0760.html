> And for those of us who would LIKE to have the commercial software on the
> Alpha platform you would recommend what, please?

Well, one part is already there, BRU2000 PE is included on Alpha.
For the Real Audio components I'd venture a guess that the projected sales
for Alpha didn't make a case even close to attractive to make a port
even close to cost-effective, not to mention the fact that there wasn't/isn't
a browser available on Alpha that will support Real Audio, diminishing the
appeal of the product for the customer. The reality of the situation is that
Linux/Alpha is not yet three years old and probably amounts to less
than 1/10 (just a guess) of Red Hat's total distribution sales. Linux/Intel
is just about twice as old (yes, Alpha did benefit from the prior development
on Intel, so that's not a pure comparison.) The BRU folks already had an
Alpha port, they started working on it right after Linux Expo '97.
They have a market for BRU on Linux/Alpha in the server and scientific market.
By comparison the Alpha desktop area (where Real Audio would be most
useful, all other things being equal) is a smattering of the Intel desktop.

I *like* Alpha *a lot*. The best way to get the commercial software
support on Alpha Linux is to make the proper inquiries to the ISV, not
hammer Red Hat over the differences in their product line.
