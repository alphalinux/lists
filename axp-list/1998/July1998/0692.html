On Fri, 10 Jul 1998, Jiann-Ming Su wrote:

> I'm not sure why RH went with egcs in 5.1 when Linus has stated publically
> that there are problems with egcs. If gcc-2.8.1 was stable, why not stick
> with it? Oh well...

I believe that impossibility to compile glibc 2.0.x with gcc 2.8 and
2.8.1 was one of reasons. I also found some other programs that did not
compile with either gcc 2.8.x or 2.7.x. What's worse, some programs
actually compiled but gave wrong results.

I currently use "gcc version egcs-2.91.26 19980502 (gcc2 ss-980401
experimental)" and I have yet to find a program that would not compile or
perform differently from what I would expect. Linux 2.1.106 and glibc
2.0.94 compiled flawlessly with this release.
