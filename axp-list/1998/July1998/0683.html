Aakin Patel wrote:
>
> Hello.
>
> MY alpha has this overwhelming tendency to have floating point exceptions
> in Povray, as well as with a few other heavy math operations. Has anyone
> else experienced something similar to this, and if they have, do they have
> any ideas as to what may be causing the problem?
>

If they occur at random, with many, different, programs, then you may
have an improperly compiled driver module.

Are there any modular drivers that you compiled by hand? If so, you
may have left off the -mno_fp_regs (or whatever the flag is) option
that tells gcc to not use the fp registers, since they are not swapped
out during an interrupt and must be left intact. Otherwise, gcc will
use the fp registers (for non fp operations) in an interrupt handler
and when your task swaps back in in the middle of an fp operation, the
registers are now all fowled up.
