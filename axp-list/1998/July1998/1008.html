On Fri, 31 Jul 1998, Chris Price wrote:

> > > It was advertised as comign with the DEC PowerStorm 3d10 graphics, what I
> > > received was a 9FX 330. Same card, or a different card? And, if its not,
> > > should I call and complain, or count my blessings as I got a better card?
> >
> > AFAIK, both will 'not run with'/'are not supported under' Linux,
> > so they are about the same value to me, (which is none) :(
>
> Yes, the 3d10 in its day was a pretty good 3d card, but if you
> want 'bulletproof' Linux support for a Video card I would personally
> recommend the Matrox Millenium. You can probably buy one used for ~70-80
> bucks.

1.) The PowerStorm 3D10 card is the same as the #9 card. Digital just
bought the card from #9 and sold it under the name PowerStorm 3D10.

2.) This card has nothing to do with 3D. It has *no* 3D acceleration
whatsoever.

3.) Instead it is a plain S3 based video card with 2MB RAM suitable for
resolutions up to 1280x1024 @ 72HZ, 8bpp and should run under
XFree 3.3.2 just fine.
