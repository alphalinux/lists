On Fri, 24 Jul 1998 Jay.Estabrook@digital.com wrote:

> > Oh, come on. You may change the partitioning scheme from Intel to BSD
> > on-line using a combination of `fdisk' and `dd'. Been there, done that.
> > Just make sure you enter exactly the same numbers for partition offsets
> > and sizes.
>
> The problem is not usually the partitions and their sizes, but the lack of
> space for the bootstrap to be installed in front of the first partition.

Well, "SRM Firmware Howto", which hopefully everyone reads before
installing Linux on a SRM equipped Alpha machine, explicitly states that
aboot needs some free space to be left at the beginning of the disk.
Unfortunately, the document does not say that an Intel-style partition
table is incompatible to SRM.

> How many times have you used "fdisk" DOS-style and started the first
> partition NOT in cylinder 1?

I did so exactly once. ;-) When I was installing Linux on an Alpha for
the first time I was unaware of the Intel-style partition table
incompatibility.

> > The whole process takes usually about ten minutes, including
> > double-checking of all the numbers, which is highly recommended. ;-)
>
> Yes, and one can often re-install from a fast SCSI CDROM in less than that.
> Fastest I've ever seen is a DP264 (EV6 CPU) with 12X Plextor, less than 3 min
> for a full RH 5.0 install... :-) :-) :-)

Yeah, if you happened to have a CD. When using a network install it may
even take a couple of hours (installing across a WAN took about two hours
in my case).
