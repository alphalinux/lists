On Mon, 3 Aug 1998 mgraffam@mhv.NET wrote:

>So, I think we have the advantage.. but we still need to promote UNIX.
>The main argument I always hear is that since UNIX is so old, it must
>not be up to the task for newer technology type stuff. People are used
>to MS software rolling over on itself every few years, they don't know
>what to think. We need to keep telling them that if the software is
>put together well the first time, you can expect it to stay around.

Heh. Linus makes new releases of Linux about weekly. 2.1.113 came out 2
days ago. 2.1.112 came out 4 days before that. MS has made one upgrade
to Win95 in three years. 2.1.111, 2 days. 2.1.110, 5 days...

Unix isn't old, it's brand-spankin'-new.

Nice article at http://www.forbes.com/forbes/98/0810/6209094a.htm

"With thousands of programmers working on Linux, the rate of incoming
improvements and new features for the program has accelerated. For all
their resources, an IBM or a Microsoft couldn't have moved faster. Whereas
new versions of typical commercial software products are issued once a
year or once every three years in the case of Microsoft's Windows
operating system, new open-source programs like Linux and Apache are
posted monthly, if not more frequently."
