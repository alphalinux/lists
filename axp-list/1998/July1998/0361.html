On Sun, 5 Jul 1998, Buda Sandor Attila wrote:

> During the install when i wrote the disklabel (fdisk <b> ... <w>)
> on the 3. tty it complains about :
>
> fdisk(11) : unalignet trap at 0000000120008ef8:000000012010c334 29 2
> fdisk(11) : unalignet trap at 0000000120008ef8:000000012010c33c 29 2
> fdisk(11) : unalignet trap at 0000000120008ef8:000000012010c344 29 2
> fdisk(11) : unalignet trap at 0000000120008ef8:000000012010c34c 29 2
>
> What thas it mean? Maybe that is the reason the install halt.

No, it's harmless. It means that some application (fdisk, in this case)
accessed a section of memory in a fashion the Alpha doesn't support, and
that the kernel had to compensate by emulating the access. The Alpha
requires that words of data be naturally aligned (so a 4-byte int on a
4-byte boundary and an 8-byte long on an 8-byte boundary, for instance).
Aside from a performance hit, it's harmless.
