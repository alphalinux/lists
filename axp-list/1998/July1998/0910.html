>>> Thompson Freeman said:
>
> I was swapping a small drive for a big drive on my UDB, and performing a
> full install. The old drive is 1gig with a single partition. The new drive
> is a 4 gig Seagate, partitioned (roughly) into one 3 gig partition and one
> 1 gig partition. Installation went fine, but now milo complains with
>
> ext2_mount:too many groups(376) max is 256

It means you're using an old MILO... ;-}

Seriously, at one time, MILO had a hard limit of being able to handle only
256 groups, which limited the size of a filesystem it could handle.

Recent MILOs have removed the limit completely, I believe, so grab the
latest NONAME/UDB MILO from RH 5.1 or off gatekeeper.dec.com:

/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/Test-Milos-2.0.30+/udb-noname
