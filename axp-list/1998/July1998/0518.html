>
> Hi. I'm trying to put a 2nd ethernet board in an alphastation 200,
> with redhat 5.1. "ifconfig eth1" always gives me "unknown interface".

If you have both compiled in a kernel then you have to tell it to scan
for the second interface. BootPrompt-HOWTO has details. The most
relevant qoute from this document will be

The most common use of this parameter is to force probing for a second
ethercard, as the default is to only probe for one. This can be
accomplished with a simple:

______________________________________________________________________
ether=0,0,eth1
______________________________________________________________________

Another method is to load both, or only the second driver, as a module.
A line similar to this:

alias eth1 ne2k-pci

in /etc/modules.conf is all what is needed (some cards may need also
"options" line)
