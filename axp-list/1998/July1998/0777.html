On Fri, 17 Jul 1998, Ryan Kirkpatrick wrote:

>
> I am looking at buying an XL300 system in the near future to
> replace/augment an aging UDB.

being the owner of one of each of these, it sounds like a good move to me
:)

> What type of SCSI controller is integrated into this board? I know
> it is SCSI II Narrow, but I have seen mentions that it is both normal
> controller and fast controller. Which is it? Also, is the chipset of this
> controller supported by Linux? (I am sure it is, but I just want to double
> check).

the scsi controller is the NCR810 (the same as in the udb, i think?).
quite compatible with linux in any case. not one but TWO drivers for your
kernel recompiling pleasures ...

> Next question is, I know this board has 8 SIMM slots, and I know
> it takes 72pin True Parity SIMMs. What I would like to know is what are
> the rules concerning the populating of these slots? I imagine they have to
> be filled in pairs, but can one pair be say 16MB SIMMs and another 64MB
> SIMMs?

actually i believe it requires simms in groups of 4 (much like the PC164
and family). this is so that it can have a 128 bit datapath to memory ...

> Lastly, I know there are people out there on this list with XL300s
> running Linux. Are there any "gotchas" that I need to watch out for? If
> so, what are they?

handy tip: the machine is sometimes referred to as XLT (such as when
making the kernel), as distinguished from the XL, an EV45 machine.

> Also, overall, how well does Linux run on this box?

i am running linux on a udb, two XL300s and a PC164, and the XLs are by
far the most trouble-free. the machine is beautifully designed, and i
think it would survive ground zero ... very solid.

i hope you enjoy it!

-ross
