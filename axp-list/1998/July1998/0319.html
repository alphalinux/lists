Hello:

Kudos to Jay Estabrook for getting X going on my XL so quickly. :-)

But my Alpha is still giving me grief. Trying to order parts for it is
really difficult because nobody, including Digital itself it seems, knows
anything about it!

I've got 4 x 8Mb 72-pin SIMM's for a total of 32Mb. I would like to
replace said SIMM's with a couple of 16Mb SIMM's to bring the system
up to 64Mb. Trouble is, I have *no* idea what memory I need to purchase.

There's parity, non-parity, EDO, non-EDO, 60 ns, 70 ns, etc. Ideally the
60 ns RAM would be great 'cause it's the cheapest. Not wanting to
purchase something and not have it work and lose out on some money, I
decided to swap RAM between it and an Alpha system.

First of all, I tried some EDO 60 ns 16Mb SIMM's from a brand new Pentium
MMX 166Mhz box ("Cemtex" clone) in the Alpha, system wouldn't boot
(monitor power-save flashes). I then took the Alpha RAM, and put it into
an older Pentium MMX 133Mhz box (another clone), and tried it out -- it
worked without problem.

So, I thought that the RAM from the Intel box would work equally well in
the Alpha box. However, it does not. ;-( ;-( ;-( ;-( The RAM from
the Intel box was not EDO, but as far as it being parity or non, 60 ns or
70 ns, I have no idea.

Here are the machine specs, I'm hoping somebody (Jay?) can tell me what I
need to purchase [ no offense Digital, but I can get it a lot easier and
cheaper elsewhere ;-) ]

It's an Alpha XL 233. Model #A20WW. S/N #KA543ERFPN

(one of the *original* XL's)

The memory from the Alpha has "5021245-01-B1P1" on it, the memory
chips have "M5M44400BJ and 4518AOL-7" on them. There are two sides,
one side has 8 chips, the other side has 10. [ If memory serves...
I'm not opening that case *again*! :-) ]

Please help! After seeing Red Hat running smoothly on this thing, I
know longer have a 'hate-on' for Alpha nor for Red Hat. In fact, after
playing around with 'rpm' I'm seriously thinking of moving my home
Intel-box from Slackware to Red Hat 5.0! (And I'm a diehard Slackie!)
