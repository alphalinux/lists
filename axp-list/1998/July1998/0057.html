On Tue, 2 Jun 1998, Ken Hansen wrote:

> 1) Extremely in-elegant to have cables oozing out of your
> system case.

This is true, it won't look too nice from behind.. but none of my
my gear is much to look at anyhow.

> 2) IDE on Multia is DARN SLOW.

IDE is always slow. The drive won't be used to host /usr. The stuff
I have planned for it is a ~1g archive (no, it isn't porn :) A lot of
it is crypto related) of stuff that I'd like to free off of my pentium.

> 3) It will take power away from your Multia PS, generating
> more heat.

The power usage should be minimal.. the drive power shouldn't even need
to be hook up, and the data lines won't pull that much..

Also, the main SCSI drive that was in the Multia is now in an external
case (brainless PC case) getting powered from that p/s. Any power loss
on the Multia side from the data lines gets more than offset by removal
of that SCSI drive.

> 4) You will probably have problems as your IDE cable starts
> to grow much beyond 18".

Hmm.. why is IDE signal life shorter than a floppy drive? (or MFM HD..
remember those?) I've ran data cabling of this sort longer than
18" with no problems.

> 5) Are you really that short on space...

Not yet.. but I see the day when I will be. I'm planning on getting
Mathematica and VariCAD for Linux/Intel for use on my Pentium. The
HD requirements for those packages will push my Pentium over the
edge. I can't get another drive in my Pentium (without investing in
more equipment) and no spare drive that I have is sufficiently roomy
to hold my current data and these two software packages. My solution
is to let another machine host the bulk of my data, freeing the
Pentium's drive up. The only machines that could pull that off for
me are my notebook and the Multia. I have to opt for the Multia since
I'm not the only one who uses the data, and my notebook is with me
a lot.

Other than the intrinsic hack value, I don't like the idea much, but
I'd rather hack it all together with gear that I have than buy new
junk just for archival. There are so many toys that are so much more
fun, I'd rather buy them :)

> Did I say I didn't think this was a good idea? Just checking...

I think you mentioned it :)
