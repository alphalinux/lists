Many thanks for your efforts in getting Mozilla compiled to run on the DEC
Alpha. I have been using the DU version since I have a license but I have
resisted giving the libs to my friends because that would not be legal. I am
thrilled to now have a legal option for a browser to give them.

I'm running your statically-linked version on an SX system running RedHat 5.0
and the browser works great, seeming to be faster than the DU version of
Navigator 3.04 that I had been using.

Of course, if I give your binary to my friends the first thing they will say is:

How do I get java (and other plugins) to work? I do not know the answer to this
so I thought I would ask you for your thoughts on the matter. Obviously Mozilla
barfs on my present version of java_301, saying it has an invalid ELF header.
This makes sense since the DU version is ECOFF and not ELF.

Not that I want to ask you for even more work but what is it going to take to
get plugins in general and java in particular to work?

Again, many many thanks for your efforts and an invaluable contribution to the
Linux/Alpha community.
