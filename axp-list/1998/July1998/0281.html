Well, Linux supports partitions much larger than 2GB in general.
I run single partitions on 9GB drives. Up until 2.1.92 though,
it didn't support single files larger than 2GB. I've tested 2.1.9[78]
with 4.5GB files and it works, except NFS and rcp are unhappy
campers. I understand the newer kernels are supposed to support
NFSv3 but have not been able to confirm that. That also requires
the latest nfs client/server packages. Not sure who to talk to
about rcp crashing my machine if used on a large file though.

I haven't got one of the big drives yet(18/23GB) so I can't
help with how to format them. I've been thinking about getting
some Fujitsu 18GB drives. Probably next time I want to increase
space.(probably in next 3 months) I saw them at Megahaus
for $1300 each. I'm hesitant to buy non-local disks due to
the failure rate I've seen in the past year. Locally all I can
find are IBM 18GB for around $1600. 20% is a high premium
to pay for local service though.
