Quoting Chris Price (cprice@missing.its.to):
> We, the tech people, know that NT has it's place in IT systems.

And that place is up front where the pointy haired bosses can see it, but not
connected to the network where it can harm anything.

> most of us realise that the proliferation of NT into mainstream IT is
> actually harming our profession of Systems Administration by introduing
> what I like to refer to as the 'point, click and ship systems admin'. Just
> as in the Fedex ads, running an NT server is as simple as 'point, click
> and ship'.

The main harm NT is doing to the profession of Systems Administration is that
it's making a generation of computer users believe that it's all right for
servers to be as flakey as desk top machines, and need rebooting twice a day.

Most Unix sysadmins I know refer to NT sysadmins as "NT Rebooters", because
that's their solution for every lockup and crash.

At our office we had a 300MHz Pentium II NT proxy server and firewall that was
crashing twice a day under the load. We replaced it with a 486/33 running
Linux, and it's been up without crashing for 7 weeks.
