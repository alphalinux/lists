Here's the problem:

We, the tech people, know that NT has it's place in IT systems.
We also know that there are other tools to use to solve problems. We
realise that NT is not a 'fit-all' tool for all IT occasions. In fact,
most of us realise that the proliferation of NT into mainstream IT is
actually harming our profession of Systems Administration by introduing
what I like to refer to as the 'point, click and ship systems admin'. Just
as in the Fedex ads, running an NT server is as simple as 'point, click
and ship'.

*WE* realise all of these things....

The people making quarterly junkets to 'Gartner Group' seminars on
'trends in IT computing', the same people that have been out of mainstream
IT support/development/maintenance, are now our bosses -> The illustrious
IT managers/CIO's.

If Linux is to suceed in the enterprise at any level, it is the
managers/CIO's that must be convinced of its attributes. Unfortunately, it
is these individuals who are more concrened with their golf game than
actually learning new info about current technology. They would sooner
listen to Gartner Group, or Byte Magazine than listen to the people that
really know what is going on -> *US*...
