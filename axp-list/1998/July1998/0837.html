WaiSun Chia wrote:
> I agree...the TGA is actually an _EXCELLENT_ card.
> It has 3D acceleration, 32bpp and 8MB VRAM _WAY_ back in '94..

Careful with that "3D acceleration" remark -- the TGA has quite
primitive 3D support -- span based, with no texture mapping.

> I can volunteer some time, but my expertise in low-level
> X-server coding is limited, although I'm quite adept in C.

Not a problem -- that's were all the other XFree86 developers
began. Don't let the size of the source tree scare you; the
xfree86 portion (xc/programs/Xserver/hw/xfree86) is rather small
and isolated.

Besides the time and motivation, one other thing which will make
life a lot more pleasant when working on a server is a terminal
or another machine networked to the development machine. Without
this it's just about impossible to do any debugging.

> I think with some group effort and with a guru's guidance, we
> as a community can pull of an awesome TGA-based X-server!

While I would like a faster server, I'm not sure a huge effort
should be made to fully accelerate a EOLed graphics chip. It's
my understanding that Digital's current line of graphics cards
are based on descendents of the TGA2 line, which is probably a
more modern design. Right now I'm working on the hardware
cursor; the only other things I'm considering now are fills and
blits.

If people want to help, I'd be happy to direct them to the
necessary information and provide what assistance I can with the
source.

-tor
