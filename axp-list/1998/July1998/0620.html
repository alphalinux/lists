Be carefull about what benchmarks you look at. For single stream workloads,
IDE can be faster given the same disk structures. The converse is that if you
are actually doing multible things then SCSI is much faster. Basically SCSI
has a little more overhead on the setup but can actually have multible things
going at once where IDE can only do one thing at a time. One example, I've
yet to see an IDE based CDR system that actually works. Also, its a not
unknown secret that most of the IDE drives have been optimized for some of the
benchmarks. Try running multible copies of the benchmarks at the same time
and see what the results are. This last time I did this the SCSI systems came
out way on the top. Also, if your looking for for a good deal on pretty fast
drives then the UltraStar ES drives from IBM are pretty good. Another thing
to look at is random reads and write to small files. Most benchmarks use
large sequencial transfers that do not really mirror real life work loads.

And yet another example. I still use a 4.4x Pioneer CD-ROM even though I can
get 32x cdroms for <$30. The 4.4x has a very low access time and in most
workloads is faster than those 32x cdroms. Throughput is not everything.
