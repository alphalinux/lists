Alice is a wonderful lady, one of the gems at Red Hat, has been there

[...]
> > either Intel, Alpha, or PPC code. I understand that in the case of
> > Linux, the size of the distribution makes that impractical, couldn't
> > you, upon verification of a home Multi-Platform installation, include
> > the CD for the second Platform? I would think that would be a minor
> > thing. The installation manuals, and the SRPM disks should be the
> > same, so it would only require 1 extra CD. You could make it an option
> > when placing the order, maybe an additional charge of $3 to 5 for the
> > extra disk would be well received, and help to defray your costs, and
> > our expenses when upgrading.

Herein lies the problem from a logistics standpoint.

First, the manual. Now that *real* tech-writers have control of it
(as has been the case since right after my name quit appearing in the credits)
the growing size has resulted in the segregation of an Alpha addendum.
Fortunately
the quality of the manual has increased at a pace greater than or equal to the
increase
in size. So, if you have Alpha and Intel there's not just a CD, there's an
additional
book(let) as well. Not that it matters a whit, adding two parts isn't much
worse than
adding one.

*BUT* adding that one part is nearly impossible given the logistics of the
packaging
and shipping methodology involved. Nowadays a fulfillment house handles all
the
busy work for Red Hat. It's simply more efficient. Fulfillment houses
by-and-large
do an excellent job when you give them a recipe for a product and the
ingredients and
let them at it. When you start offering it on a "Have it your way" basis
you're asking
for trouble. A couple of years ago when things were packed by hand, in-house,
this
would have worked nicely. But now, knowing what I know, I just don't see a
way for
it to happen.

The Power Tools is your best bet.
