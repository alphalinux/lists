>
> I'm curious ... is there a standard for inittab layout? Some of you may
> recognize my suggestions as being very Solaris-y. For those unaware,
> SunOS/Solaris do:
>
> 0 halt (go to firmware)
> 1,s single user
> 2 multiuser
> 3 multiuser w/NFS
> 4 X (+multiuser w/NFS)
> 5 power off
> 6 reboot
>

It's worth pointing out 5 is a very recently Sun-invention.

> What is the "Linux standard", or is there one?
>
> 0 halt (power off?)
> 1,s single user
> 2 multiuser
> 3 multiuser w/NFS
> 4 *unused* ?
> 5 X ?
> 6 ?
>

There isn't one; most use a System V-like setup:

0 halt
1,s single user
2 multiuser
3 multiuser w/network services
4 multiuser variant (unused in RedHat)
5 multiuser variant (X11 in RedHat)
6 reboot

Early versions of Slackware used 6 for X11.

Since Linux now supports poweroff, following the Solaris convention
probably would make sense.
