It is a newbie question! ;-)
But first a word of caution. MILO is not supported on the Tincup,
therefore you have no choice but to use SRM to boot Linux. But please
acquire a copy of SRM _LEGALLY_ ! Since it was a NT machine previously,
this means that your machine is not licensed to use SRM...
After purchasing your SRM license, you can download the latest version
of the SRM software at gatekeeper.dec.com.

After that, you need to do a bit of homework and your reading assignment
is:
http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/srm.html

A friendly reminder, RH5.1 has problems with BSDlabels which SRM/aboot
needs, therefore you have a couple of choices:
1. Install RH5.0, then upgrade to 5.1.
2. Bug the hell out of Redhat to fix the BSDlabel problem during
installation!
3. Fix it yourself!
