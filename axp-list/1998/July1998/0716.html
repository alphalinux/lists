Below is a conversation that I am involved in with RedHat regarding
their supporting those of us that purchase supported "boxed-sets" for
our machines, and happen to have multi-platform systems at home. Please
read through the thread, and lend a voice to this. What I am asking for
from RedHat is the option to get multi-platform support with our boxed
sets, rather than requiring that we purchase full separate boxed sets
for each of our platforms (ahem.. even NT includes multi-platform
versions on their distribution).
If we can get more voices involved, maybe we can get some
support on this issue.

> I have been a purchasing customer since I started using RedHat

> included)).
> I do however have a simple complaint that I hope you take to
> heart.
> While at work, boeing will purchase whatever is necessary for
> the work machines. That is not a problem.
> However, at home, I have a problem. I am now "Multi-Platformed"
> at home, using a DEC Alpha (with RedHat) as my firewall machine, and
> Intel boxes for my working desktops. this has placed a huge strain on
> me if I want registered - supported - current versions on both of my
> platforms, as it now requires that I purchase 2 sets, 1 for the Alpha,
> and 1 for the Intel. so your "free" OS now costs me nearly $110 to
> upgrade.
> While I do not begrudge you your upgrade costs for the CD's, It
> is a bit of a burden when running Multi-Platform. I recently ordered
> (and have received) 5.1 for my Alpha. I just can't justify buying it
> "again" for the Intel. Is there not something you can do to help out
> us home users with more than one platform to support?
> While I hate to draw the comparison: Even Microsoft puts
> multiple versions of NT on the distribution disk, so it will load
> either Intel, Alpha, or PPC code. I understand that in the case of
> Linux, the size of the distribution makes that impractical, couldn't
> you, upon verification of a home Multi-Platform installation, include
> the CD for the second Platform? I would think that would be a minor
> thing. The installation manuals, and the SRPM disks should be the
> same, so it would only require 1 extra CD. You could make it an option
> when placing the order, maybe an additional charge of $3 to 5 for the
> extra disk would be well received, and help to defray your costs, and
> our expenses when upgrading.
>
> Please give this some consideration. I'd like to purchase an
> upgrade, and be able to upgrade my whole site, not just selected
> platforms.
