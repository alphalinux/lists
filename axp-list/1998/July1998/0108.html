As far as setting the ARC flag in /etc/sysconfig/clock (or more
accurately, using 'clock -A' instead of just 'clock)... <shrug>. You can
have it either on or off, depending on how you set your hardware clock.
You can change from one to the other by playing with 'clock' commands.

As far as the system clock running at double speed... that's been
fixed as of kernel 2.1.106. I don't know _what_ changed, but something
did, and my system clock now maintains correct time. 2.1.107 and 2.1.108
also have the fix (as I presume all later kernels will), but they're too
unstable for me (2.1.107 goes nutty even with the ALSA sound driver;
2.1.108 works, but the console code's changed removing paging, and
including the framebuffer device causes it not to boot).

If you need it on a 2.0.x kernel, time to hit the source. ;-)
