>Craig, Wes, I mean no offense, BUT:
>While I agree with elements of what both of you said, is it productive to
>have this conversation in a public forum?

It is, in a normal, professional forum; it is *often* done to
ask people to clarify and properly re-file bug reports.

It is rare for such a request to be treated with such contempt, and
for some others who witness that contempt to claim that both people
are equally at fault.

>Feelings are getting hurt, tempers are beginniong to flare up, and mostly
>because of the peer-exposed face-saving aspect of where you guys are going.

I'm not trying to save face *at all*, and would appreciate it you
would either be specific in your criticisms of my attempts to improve
the quality of bug reports against products like g77, or simply
avoid making them in the first place.

>I think you both are right/wrong/mean well/strive for knowledge, and mostly
>fighting through communications and "thinking-while-writing" issues.

I disagree.

>Consider this a virtual beer bought for you guys. Obviously you are both
>very intelligent and hard-working individuals.

That's a nice sentiment, but you left out "professional". I believe
I've conducted myself fairly professionally and ethically. If you
have *specific* criticisms of how I handled this, you're welcome
to email me personally, but I believe my criticisms of the original
bug report(s) were professional, and appropriate for this list, as a
means to educate the list regarding how to properly formulate such
bug reports.

What exactly did I do wrong? Take the statement "not what I want to
have happen" as an indication that `-pg' might be incorrectly handled
by g77? Check it out on my Alpha platform, both in gcc and in g77,
with `-v'? Respond asking just *why* it was believed to be wrong?
Also ask for more details on the behavior, and for them to be reported
to the proper forum? Or is it that you just don't like it when
somebody stands up for themselves when told they can't read, instead
of folding? I mean, if you believe in the life philosophy "there are
no right answers", then that's all the explanation I need for your
stance; I don't believe in that.

>Now (PLEASE!) shake hands, return to your corners, step out at the bell,
>and go chase some bugs in code, not each other..

No thanks. I'm not in this business to get into pissing matches about
how to parse English, and don't appreciate having someone call into
question my ability to do so; nor to have my, IMO, quite-valuable
advice regarding how to submit bug reports and ask questions
about software thrown back in my face like that.

Had it been even *remotely* possible that I had misread the original
bug report as such, I would have immediately spotted it and
apologized, because that's what *I* do when I make goofs, either
in public or private. You can look up my recent posts in comp.lang.fortran
if you don't believe me -- but then, you might *also* notice that people
disagree by making specific, technical points, almost always in a
profession manner there, and when a few don't, the rest of that audience
has tended to object accordingly. I feel no need to save face; I got over
that kind of ego-boo some 15-20 years ago, and have definite recollections
of how much of a time-waster, among other things, it was, and how
widespread that tendency is (or was, anyway) among programmers.

What I *was* on this list for was to provide up-front info, where
needed, on the status of g77 for the Alpha, and to get the occasional
heads-up on Alpha issues so as to properly maintain my machine. I have
some good support for the latter; since the former appears to no longer
be welcome by present inhabitants of this list, at least coming from
me, I'll unsubscribe now. I've got too much email to read as it is
anyway. I believe there's at least one other g77 person reading
this list, at least there used to be, so this should not be considered
any great loss.
