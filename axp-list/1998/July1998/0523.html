Huw Davies writes:
> Well I'm trying to install a Sony DAT drive in my XL-366 but I've got this
> minor annoying problem - I've got more SCSI devices than plugs on the
> cable. I've managed to do this by replacing my floppy with a SCSI Zip
> drive, then I've got a CD-R, two SCSI disks and a DAT drive. Has anyone
> built (and successfully used) a SCSI cable extender or would I be better
> off building a totally new cable with the right number of plugs?

I did build a new cable but that was for the ncr5x875 controller I
added. That worked fine. Because I found that the case of my XL300 was
getting a bit full (the airflow in the top part was being severely
disturbed by all these cables) I put the DAT in an external case. Now
the DAT and the CDROM are connected to the build in interface and my
two hard-disks to the new card, works great.

One thing I noticed was an increase in hard disk speed. As the build
in controller is able to handle 10Mb/sec I found this amazing as my
fastes HD can only supply 7Mb/s, but then again SCSI is a misterious
world sometimes...
