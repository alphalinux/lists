On Tue, 7 Jul 1998, Brian Craft wrote:

> I modified drivers/net/Space.c to put pcnet32_probe at the top,
> followed by de4x5_probe. This results in the AMD board being

The general idea behind adaptor probing is that rarely do people have more
than one ethernet adaptor (which is quite incorrect, as my machines each
have two or three adaptors installed...) and as unnecessary probing could
cause lockups, better safe than sorry. So what you have to do is add
something like the following to your OSLOADOPTIONS (I think):

ether=0,0,eth0 ether=0,0,eth1

> What am I missing?

/usr/doc explains in detail MultipleAdaptors, though I think it's geared
towards x86. You just need to stick it in ARC/MILO instead of LILO.

> Also, the AMD code is generating LOTS of "unaligned trap" messages.

Dunno what to tell you about that. Maybe try compiling with a later
version of GCC. Or possibly even EGCS. I haven't had any problems
compiling kernels using EGCS 1.0.3a that I bootstrapped on my multia, but
I guess I'm the exception around here...
