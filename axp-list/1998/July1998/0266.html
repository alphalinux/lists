I've seen a lot of questions on this topic lately, and thought I'd toss
a solution or two out there. For those of you looking for a graphical
web-browser for your Alpha boxes, try one of these ideas:

1) Install KDE. It has a file manager that does duty as a web-browser
(ala Internet Explorer). It doesn't support all the whistles & bells
that the current generation of Netscape does, but it does ok. KDE is
available at:
http://www.kde.org (ftp site: ftp://ftp.kde.org )
Get the source tar ball. You'll need to compile your own. The only
binaries you'll find are for intel. But it seems to compile clean on the
Alphas (I have a build in progress right now, and it's going clean).
To use this, you'll also need to go get QT (a graphics library). It's
available at:

http://www.troll.no

Again, get the source, and compile it (I got the ***.src.rpm, and did
rpm --rebuild qt...src.rpm to compile it, and then rpm -ivh qt...rpm to
install. Again, this went without error on my Alpha.
This will not only get you web browsing, but an fairly decent desk-top
as well.

2) There's a project going on over at Troll (http://www.troll.no) to
port mozilla to QT! You need to get their stuff, plus go get the
mozilla source code, and roll up your sleves. It's reported to work
pretty well. I've dl'd the stuff, but I won't get at it installing it
until tomorrow, or Sunday. It looks promising though. It's a good
potential alternative to the Motif installations.

Good luck all!
