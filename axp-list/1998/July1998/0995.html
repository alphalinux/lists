Chris,

That is your problem. I had the same problem, what happens is your
system runs out of FDS, and no new files can be opend or written
(/var/log/messages), that would explane why you see nothing in the
messages log. Stuff starts to segfault, and die. Yet processes that
don't need to access the hard disk still run fine. You also might
notice the machine will ping, and when you telnet to it, most ports
will open.

If you telnet to port 110 (pending on the version of pop3d you run if
any at all) it usually reports an error something like: "no free file
handles", or something to that nature.

To see your current fds usage you can do 2 things:
cat /proc/sys/kernel/file-nr
or compile lsof, and that will show you exactly what is using the fds.

To up your FDS, just issue a command like:
echo "4096">/proc/sys/kernel/file-max
echo "4096">/proc/sys/kernel/inode-max
