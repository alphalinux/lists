OK; the reason I said 1978 was to my knowledge that was the time
that Unix first made it into the outside world (although since
I was 6 at the time I don't have much recollection of it).
I think thjat was version 7; perhaps version 6 got out before that?

1969 was the start of development of Unix; and I think it was the
summer of 1974 in which they ported it, in C, to an Interdata
machine (As I recall from the Bell Systems Tech journal that I read,
the machines were on different floors, they didn't have remote
reboot and the machines took different tape formats which they
had to convert on a different floor....)
