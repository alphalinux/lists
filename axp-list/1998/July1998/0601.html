David Alan Gilbert writes:
[snip]
> For example, our local dealer sells a 16.8GB IBM Ultra-IDE drive for
> #382 (UK pounds, inclusive of tax) and sells a 9GB IBM SCSI-Wide (7200RPM)
> for #417 and he also sells an 8.4GB IBM Ultra-IDE for #186
> Now a friend has just bought one of those 8.4GB IDE drives and was getting
> 9.4MB/second out of it on a new Pentium2.
> My 7200 RPM Barracuda on my Alpha's SCSI is giving me about
> 6MB/second.

I have noticed samething simular, but... I did my timings with hdparm
and true the IDE disk came out faster (9MB/sec in an Intel box, 5400
rpm) my SCSI disk (same rpm) did only 7Mb.sec (in my alpha
box). However real world performance was better for the SCSI
disk. When I used bonnie for timing I noted less difference in max
throughput. Seek times however, were much better with the SCSI disk,
hence the better real world performance.

For me the choice is still simple (ecepecially since the XL300 does
not even has a IDE interface), mainly because SCSI allows me to move
stuff outside my box and generally is more simple to set up (for me at
least). BTW Using the buildin SCSI controller my disk would only do
4.9 Mb per sec, when I added an ncr53875 controller it went to
7MB/sec, maybe you can win some speed as well.

Cheers -- Jaap
