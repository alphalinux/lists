I'm new to Alpha-Linux, so please give don't get upset if I'm asking

I need a little help from you. I tried to install RedHat-5.1 on a
AlphaStation 500 (Bret, Alcor). In short, I failed.

The hardware: AS500, 21264/266, 288MB (!), DE4X5, TGA, SCSI HD and CD,
Qlogic ISP SCSI HA.

In detail, here is what happened:
I added an entry to the WNT AlphaBios (4.48) to boot linload.exe and milo
(xlt-arcor) from a FAT floppy. Everything worked, the MILO recognized (show)
the SCSI adapter and booted the kernel (arcor.gz) from the RH5.1 CD.
I got a bunch of ,,unexpected intr 31 and 32`` from the kernel however, but
the installation program started successfully. Unfortunately, I was not
supposed to install Linux right now, but just to try whether it's possible
to do so. (The box still has a running WinNT3.51 installation.)
I had a look at the /proc/*, so maybe you can ask me to provide you with
info on this.

I read a little bit more in the Alpha Install. Addenum and learned, that I'm
supposed to upgrade the firmware ARC/AlphaBIOS. I thought, that this would
possibly fix the unexpected intr problems.
I got 4.56 and 4.57 from Digital's server and installed 4.57. And then the
trouble started!
When booting milo, it just got hung upon loading the SCSI adapter driver.
Here are the messages.

... and then the system locks with the HD LED indicating busy!

One oddity: The Show hardware config of the AlphaBIOS tells me that IRQ 1d
is assigned to the SCSI adapter. So maybe, the PCI Bios isn't working as
