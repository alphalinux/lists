I believe Mr. Tibbetts is correct in one basic aspect. The "official" boxed
sets for the Alpha (and Sparc as well) do NOT, in fact, contain the
majority of the special add-ins that RedHat bundles with the Intel version.
In fact, AFAIK, the alpha set IS the GPL set with a manual. Since a
significant portion of the price associated with the "official" set is
support, it seems entirely reasonable to pay for that once, one manual, and
receive the non-GPL software once. I suspect that many of us would be far
more likely to pay for the alpha version if it came with the software
RedHat bundles with the Intel version. At this point, I'm far more inclined
to download it or buy a CD from CheapBytes or LSL. Buying the PowerTools
set does absolutely nothing to solve the dilemma, since, as I understand
the contents, it's nothing more than the 3 GPL sets and archives (i.e. also
does not contain the commercial SW for alpha). Again, that being the case
it's far cheaper to download/buy CB/LSL for the same material.

BTW, SOME of US that purchased UDB's from onsale (actually the knotheads at
Starship) are quite proficient at installing and maintaining Linux based
systems (and I might add it's more difficult to do so and take advantage of
all of the UDB's capabilities than on many other platforms). I'd frankly
prefer if RedHat used their, um, dominant? position within the Linux
community to press commercial vendors (particularly those whose software
RedHat bundles) to support the Alpha platform. I'd even be willing to pay
for my part of the priviledge, if it were offered!
