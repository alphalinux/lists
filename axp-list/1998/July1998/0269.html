It took some work for me to get my system up with DU, Linux, and NT. By
far the most restrictive environment was DU.
My system is a pc164. I have a Tulip network card from DEC, a TGA vidio
card (the xserver in DU only seems to work with the high priced Powerstorm
and TGA cards, so I'm stuck at 256 colors.) svga cards will work in 640x480
256 color mode only. I've tried this with a matrox card. Some day I may
try to build XF86 under DU. The scsi condroler again came from DEC and is
based on the NEC xx810 chip. The real problem I had was with the cd-rom.
Most scsi cd-rom seem to work once DU is installed, but when it comes to
installing from a CD, I went through 5 or 6 before I found one that worked.
I now use a Toshiba model XM-5701B. All the other cd-rom seemed to work
untill about 40 min into the install, when I would start to get read errors.

Basiclly, my machine is built for Digital Unix, and happens to run linux
and NT. But it all works great now.
