Chris Price wrote:

>
> Oct 5 07:30:08 master kernel: tty00 at 0x03f8 (irq = 4) is a 16550A
> Oct 5 07:30:08 master kernel: tty01 at 0x02f8 (irq = 3) is a 16550A
>
> Strangely there is no entry for /dev/tty00 or tty01, is this an
> error in kernel, my system or otherwise (ie: is it an error?)?
>
This is the same on Intel as Alpha. The kernel message had to call
the port by some name. It just used "tty". Whoever made those
special files in /dev was also free to use any name he wanted as long
as he assigned the correct major/minor device numbers. The two authors
chose different conventions that's all. The Kernel did not actually
look in the /dev directory. This is likely clear as mud but if you
look at the serial device driver you will see that "tty" is just a
constant in a printk function call. It should be changed to
"Serial Port Number" to avoid confusion
