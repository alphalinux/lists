At 11:30 AM 11/18/98 -0500, you wrote:
>At 12:25 PM 12/18/98 -0500, you wrote:
>>Yeah... The only places that I found PPro's they were $>>Yeah... The only places that I found PPro's they were $119@180mhz...
>>Celerons go for $>>Celerons go for $99@300mhz
>
>PPros can be bumped upto 200 Mhz with no sweat (proper heat mitigation
>required)

Not only that, you can get the pII XEON upgrade chips for PPRo boards in
the future (333 mhz w/512k of L2 cache running at processor speed).

>1) I *think* the celeron is SMP capable, but I don't know...
>

Stock Celeron is NOT SMP capable. However, if you are willing to *DRILL*
the chips and rewire it a little bit, it can be made SMP capable.

>2) No cache is not a problem, if your RAM is fast enough, but the PPro
> has a 256K cache vs. 0 in Celeron...

Cache DOES matter, espescially in a SMP environment, since both processors
are sharing the same RAM.

Also, there are two other options. If you really don't mind getting your
hands dirty, buy a celeron 300a for around $150, they come with 128k of
integrated L2 cache running at processor speed (think mini-XEON). Not only
that, but you can overclock them to 450 mhz without a problem.

Or, get a PPRO 166 cpu w/512k of cache and overclock it to 200 mhz.. This
what I did. I bought a motherboard from centrix-intl.com for $115 with
integrated ultrascsi (or if you already have it, they have a dual-processor
board available for $50 without SCSI). I bought the CPU's for $100 each.

>The CPUs would be cheaper, the perfomance would be a bit more, and the
>motherboard would be a lot more expensive (twice the price?) If you really
>think you will upgrade the machine during it's likely service life, the
>investment in a PII SMP machine is a good idea, if you want a darn fast
>smp box without spending a lot of money, the PPro is a very good choice,
>IMHO...
>
>Ken
>khansen@njcc.com

They may be better off buying a dual-capable motherboard and a single
PII-333 for $250's a processor. Then you have an upgrade path, and it
didn't cost you an arm and a leg.
