> > I am wondering if #1 the celeron is SMP capable

        In orginal mode, they are not. However, a cuople of Japanese
students created a great hardware hack that allows full SMP operation.

>>#2 works well in SMP...
> > I know its got no cache... how much does this effect it? especialy in
> > SMP...
>
        The celeron 300A and 333A models have 128k L2 cache *right on the
chip* itself. This is a different setup than PPRO technology that used a
seperate cache silicon die and mated it with the processor in one package.

        If you goto slashdot.org and do a search for past articles using
'celeron', you should see the page for the SMP hack. I dunno about
performance, but my 300A running at 450mhz (thats right 450, and compiles
kernels/software with *NO* hiccups) is a very fast processor (considerably
faster than my amd K6-2 350).

>
> > Seems 2 celleron system would be cheaper than a PPro... and upgradeable
> > to PIIx450 (at least)
>
        If you bide your time, PPRO technology can be had for cheap. I
built a complete 2 x PPRO200 system with 128 ram and 12 gig scsi disk for
under $1000 (some stuff from onsale.com and some from ebay, and some
locally). Seems to work great!
