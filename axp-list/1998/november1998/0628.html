With regards to your message at 01:14 PM 11-26-98 +0100, Armin Ollig. Where
you stated:
>hi,
>
>i managed to get a DU license from my university.
>However SRM still does not work.
>I put a NCR810 based SCSI card in (64 and 32bit slots) but i do not get a
>device for it (its a ASUS SC220 (no bios)).
>"show device" only lists the floppy and the ide controller.
>"show config" reports a "NRC810 SCSI controller" in PCI slot X.
>So why is there no device ?
>I read the SRM manual over and over. I still have no idea how to manually
>create a device in SRM.
>
>I get the same phenomenon with dlink ethernet cards. I plug them in, "show
>device" does not show a new device, but "show config" reports a "DEC 21140
>network controller" in PCI slot X. Same with a 21041 based card.
>
>My hardware is a AlphaPC 164. Not LX, or SX, just _164_.
>I bought it with ARC. Do i have to exchange a ROM or is there any other
>way to get the 164 to work with SRM ?
>
Then you probably have incompatible cards, gentlemen.
With regards to the ethernet, you NEED a genuine Digital DE500 card.
For the SCSI I recommend the Intraserver 3140U-U Note the U at the end,
without it yoiu can only run SCXSI-2 Fast, not UltraSCSI. Their drivers
only install on UNIX on the -U versions of the cards, which have a
"special" ROM onboard.
I have had success with SOME 53C810's notably the ASUS version, main thing
to look for is does the card have NVRAM onboard..
For SRM you buy the license, and flash the ROM from the ARC console with
the new firmware. Easiest and cheapest way to get SRM is the SRM-SDK,
P/N:QR-21B02-03
