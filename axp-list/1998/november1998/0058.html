> Hello everyone,

Welcome to the family :-)

> With the help of a friend, we were able to install RH 5.1.
> Then we were able to compile a new "simple" kernel (2.0.35)
>
> My box is an AlphaPC164LX (with NT ARC console)

Similar to mine - mine is an LX, but it has the AlphaBIOS rather than ARC.

> But I still have problems.
> MILO and the system have difficulties to probe the IRQ : I mean it needs two
> or three attemps to detect my IDE Drives on each IRQ (14-15)

Yeh thats not too uncommon; with pretty new 2.1 (I';m still running
2.1.115) you can enable support for the IDE chip on the LX (a CMD646) -
and it make sit start up cleaner and also gives you a decent transfer
speed - your present speed will be pretty bad.

> And it detects my ethernet card (based on the 21041 chipset) in the IRQ 18!
> Under NT, this card is on the IRQ 3 (I know this is one of the IRQ for COM
> but I don't care)

The Alpha has a different Interrupt numbering scheme from PCs (I dont know
what NT is doing...). Typically you'll find that ISA style things
(keyboard, PS/2 mouse etc) have similar IRQs that you expect on an Intel
box, but PCI devices start from about 16 upwards.

> Then the network stops working after 15-20 minutes (I can't say exactly).
> The network part of the kernel wasn't compile as a module.

Dont know that one - try and look in the logs to see what was going on.

> The last final problem is with XFree. The S3 sever refuses to start with
> 16bpp
> my video card is diamond Stealth 64 DRAM (PCI) with 1MB.

1MB of RAM seems a bit mean to start 16bpp in? What resoloution were you
trying to do that at? Can you also give us the error messages you were
getting from XFree.
