On Mon, Nov 23, 1998 at 07:12:12AM -0500, Dave Beck wrote:

> I'm trying to put together a mini-HOWTO for netbooting (via SRM of course)
> the alphas. We have come into a mess of Multias recently so I will be
> concentrating on them, but as others send me their "info" I will include it.
>
> The primary factor in creating this FAQ was the plain and simple LACK of
> accurate documentation on how to build a netbootable kernel, etc. So I
> would appreciate any advice from others who have done so successfully. As
> of yet, however, I still don't have any of these UDB boxes netbooting. ;)
>
> Any help / thoughts / ideas are appreciated.
>

When building the kernel.. `make bootpfile` works for me. It drops a
file in <insert source directory>/arch/alpha/boot/ named
'bootpfile'. Just move that file onto your bootp/tftp server in the
appropriate directory, play a bit with the SRM commands.. and you're
on the way to netbooting.. It works for me on the two Avanti systems
I run as well as a Multia. Of course... this is compiling the 2.1.x
kernels.. things could very well be different for 2.0.x
