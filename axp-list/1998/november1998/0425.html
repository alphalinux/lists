On Fri, 20 Nov 1998, Stefan Schroepfer wrote:
> I'm not aware of a paper, but the following rules may serve as a
> starting point:
> - Don't store pointers in ints (it should be safe to use unsigned
> longs for this purpose, but why not use pointer variables).

Rather goofy that a compiler ever lets that happen. So much for the goal
of 'easy portability'. Goofier yet is that people that think themselves
as competent programmers do this.

> - Don't make assumptions on the size of data types, always use
> the sizeof () operator.

Oh, that's why it's in the language! :)

> - Avoid assembly language.

I don't mind assembly language, just make sure there are IFNDEF's for
general purpose code too. Makes maintainance a bit harder, but sometimes
raw performance matters. :)
