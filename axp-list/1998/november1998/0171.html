I've never done any gtk programming, but one of the weirdnesses you get used
to in Xt and Motif programming is the XtCallback structure. Callbacks are
called with three arguments, the last two are of type XtPointer, which is
usually typedefed as void*. But they are often used, both in the Xt libraries
itself and in user code, to pass integers instead of pointers.
