>
> try the following
> main() {
> double x = 1.0;
> double y = 1e-155;
>
> x *=(1e-155*y);
> printf("%e %e %e\n",(1e-155*y),x,1e-310);
> }

compiling under digital unix (cc no options) generates:
cc: Warning: fp2.c, line 6: In this statement, underflow occurs in evaluating the expression "1.e-310".
       printf("%e %e %e\n",(1e-155*y),x,1e-310);
----------------------------------------^

the output is:
0.000000e+00 0.000000e+00 0.000000e+00

compiled with cc -fptm u generates the output:
Floating point exception (core dumped)

here is what man cc, has to say about -fptm:
  -fptm n
      Generates instructions that do not trigger floating-point underflow or
      inexact trapping modes. Any floating point overflow, divide-by-zero,
      or invalid operation will unconditionally generate a trap. -fptm n is
      the default.

  -fptm u
      Generates traps on floating-point underflow as well as overflow,
      divide-by-zero, and invalid operation.

so you don't need -ieee to fix this under digital unix.
