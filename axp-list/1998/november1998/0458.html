Remember this:

> I finished up a lapack-rpm that makes use of K. Goto's (currently
> available in libgemm-980927) assembler dgemm/sgemm, as well as M.
> Dayde's and Iain Duff's RISC-optimized Level 3 BLAS. The package is
> based on the lapack-2.0-10.src.rpm.
>

The directories on our server were locked for a few days. There's an
update available now. If interested, have a look at

ftp://ftp.neuro.uni-oldenburg.de/pub/alpha/lapack.html

Even tho work is still in progress (J. Wesner and K. Goto at the fast
math stuff, K. Goto at the BLAS,...), this one works fine - passes all
testing and timing routines. Very easy to install!

A comment by J. Wesner in comp.os.linux.alpha concerning atan2 in
libffm makes me a little nervous. It may be that this fct. does
actually not work perfectly. He also states that libffm.0.30 is in the
works, so don't worry too much :-)

Right now, I'm starting to look into an FFT-rpm (which is again based
on other peoples work). Any other suggestions (not too many at once
:-)?

Oh yeah, there's something like an advanced gnuplot called xmgr (which
I use at times to do regression and that type of thing when I'm in a
hurry). I found a source-rpm for i386-exclusive a while ago, but this
is bull because it works pretty straight forward on Alpha, too. Anyone
interested, then I'd put up the spec file...
