On Sun, Nov 15, 1998 at 10:50:20PM -0500, Michael J. Donahue wrote:
> >> _wd * wd = (_wd *)m_menuFactoryItems[k].callback_action;
> Ulrich's question is the first thing to check. Anyone with experience
> porting 32-bit apps to the Alpha knows that pointer casts are red flags.

Without even trying the compiler test, I can assure you it's our
casting problem. :) Unfortunately, it's deeper than our code, it's
GTK+. The callback_action member is a "guint", which should be defined
to be sizeof(unsigned int) on your platform. This works on Intels, breaks on
Alphas (and any other platform where sizeof(unsigned int) !=
sizeof(unsigned int *)).

So, now it's time to find out why the GTK+ guys are using guints as
pointer substitutes. I really feel bad for not digging into the code
as soon as Brian put out this message, but I'll soon get to deal with
the Alpha issues every day, thanks to him. :)
