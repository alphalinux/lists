Joakim Roubert wrote:
>
> On Thu, 19 Nov 1998, Eric Cifreo wrote:
>
> > I had this same problem. Switch the speed in your connect script to
> > 38400, even if your modem is a 56K. You can do a 'setserial
> > /dev/modem spd_hi' after you get it working, to boost your bandwidth.
>
> Will this raise the speed, even though the script says 38k4?

I too had this connect problem. By lowering my serial port speed in the
networking configuration tool (RedHat 5.1) to 19200, I was able to dial
out. I made the assumption that if I could choose 115200, then the
serial port would accept it. Notta. I was able to use up to 38400
without resetting the serial port. I am using a Zoom external 33.6.

The resolve is to use 'setserial /dev/modem/ spd_hi (or spd_vhi)'
