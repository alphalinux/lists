Well, I too am impatient. And I would really love to see what my Alpha
can do. What language you _should_ use depends on what you are trying to
do. Math Ops in assembly will blow the doors off of C, but I wouldn't
want to even think about an application in assembly. There was a post
yesterday (after I wasted far too much time on trying to do so myself)
about postscript, pdf, and html documents on Digital's web site. Among
the gems are several regarding C/C++, assembly, and Fortan I believe.
They are DU specific, but should nonetheless be helpful to we
Penguin-Heads. Here is the text of yesterday's message:

--------
>Howdy all. There was some talk a few weeks ago about finding a recent
>copy of Digital's Guide to Assembly Programming in postscript format.
>Alas, the most recent was from 1994 for OSF. So, I took the liberty of
>ripping the current (3/96, DU 4.0+) page and converting it to
>postscript. It is available on my box, axp.ce.gatech.edu, via anonymous
>ftp as /pub/asm.ps.gz It isn't perfect, but it should do for most
>purposes. If there is sufficient interest, I may do the same for the rest
>of the available documentation. Any comments as to the output quality (if
>they are constructive) would be appreciated. Else, > /dev/null

This may fit in your /dev/null, but I did find existing postcript docs for
4.0a:

http://www.unix.digital.com/faqs/publications/pub_page/v40a_docs.htm

Or if you want to pick your doc type and/or version:

http://www.unix.digital.com/faqs/publications/pub_page/doc_list.html

Note that I found this minutes after I finished printing the assembly doc
set for OSF/1 3.x (100+ pages). Ouch.

I appreciate your effort, though.

JRDM
