Scott Lewis wrote:
>
> Good point. How do _you_ call an assembly routine from C/C++?

It depends on the compiler but normally C pushes the arguments
onto the stack. The called function pops them off and places the
return value on the stack. What is placed on the stack is either
the value itself or it's address. There is a gcc option I
believe that will allow you to see the assembly generated by
the compiler. Experts can correct me but it is not hard to
mix assembly and C. Also, I have not done this with gcc, but
I think it supports in-line assembly rite there in your *.c file.

I'd like to here from someone who has done this. My experience
in this area is not with Linux/gcc. It goes back to IBM/360,
CDC6600, Perkin Elmer and CP/M Z-80.

The DEC UNIX assembly manual has examples of both C calls
assembly and assembly calls C
