On 10/22/98 2:11 PM, Scott Lewis <scott@bach.ce.gatech.edu> is rumored to
have said:

>If you do purchase these, would you kindly post to let the group know how
>they work?

I did purchase a pair of those SIMMs at the time I posted the initial
question. After I took my PhD general exams, which delayed the process, I
finally found some time to assemble the different parts of my AS200. I
started to assemble the machine on Wednesday at 19:00 and Thursday at
16:00 I had my AS200 running well. The SIMMs work well, thus showing that
those modules can be installed in the AS200. I kept the two modules that
came with the machine. I bought the machine from Onsale.
I assembled my machine with a NEC CDROM player (6x), a Seagate Medalist
Pro 4.5 MB, the mentioned memory (64x2) and the on board memory (8x2),
and a Matrox Millennium 8MB.
I followed mostly the intructions and hints from
<http://adam.cheshire.net/~rdp/alpha/as200.html>, which is the least
confusing source of documentation. I found the RH manual and addendum to
be terrible. Certainly, there is room for improvement in those manuals!
ZP
