> I've used gold plated ram in tin sockets with no troubles. It would be the tin that
> would have the corrosion but I suspect it would be very slow. Might be worse in
> humid surroundings but most computers are in ac these days. I have seen it on the
> power connectors more often, tho. As a matter of fact, one MircoVax Box (BA23) had a
> power connector that would corrode ever so slightly but the corrosion would cause RF
> interference that would show up as memory errors or bus errors and would affect the
> tape drive. Replaceing that power connector would solve the problem. The old ones
> had a tendency to over heat also, due to the corrosion. That was under a heavy load
> tho.
>

Thats consistent since we are talking about galvanic corrosion. The
higher the current the faster the corrosion.

> I think you've hit on the solution. Let's just attach a hunk of zink to the case and
> put it in salt water.
>

Actually there is a product sold under the name of Tweak, I think it
was originally patented under the name Stabilant, that decreases
contact resistance while increasing corrosion resistance. It is a
weird polymer that only conducts under pressure, so only the points
of contact in a multicontact socket conduct. The original demo of it
had a tv set immersed totally and running at the time. It is rather
expensive but you only need a drop or two per connection/socket. So a
drop of this on the simm socket should work wonders for those who are
worried. Look for it at high end audio stores.
