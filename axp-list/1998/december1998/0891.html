On Sat, 19 Dec 1998, Pooh wrote:

> Does the ASUSCom ISDN card have a linux driver?....how well does it
> perform?.

It does... Together with a lot of other cards And it performs just great
(on i386 box, but I don't see why it wouldn't an alpha).

Another benefit of ISDN card - that I forgot to mention befor - is that I
can use two ISDN channels separatly (one for PPP to ISP and another for
something else - like dialin or voice mail or...).

> why yes?
>
> Because it WORKS. You hang it off the serial port (or the 16750 card)
> and it'll run (at 230kbps with compression on the 16750). No conflicts,
> nothting to configure...just looks like a BIG honkin modem. Has nifty
> little status/data transfer lights so you can SEE that things are/are not
> happening. No extra drivers needed...(well..maybe for the 16750..but that
> really shouldn't need a special driver...just should use a regular serial
> driver.) This is 99.99999999999999% guaranteed to work.

Well, I'd say it looks JUST like a modem. ISDN comes with many goodies and
a device emulating JUST a modem ignores most of them OR you need special
software (suplied by the manufactorer - and probably just for Wintendo OS)
to make use them.

> If you get an ISDN card, it takes up a slot, you need a special driver
> (unless it has a 16750 UART built on/emulated), it may/may not take up an
> irq, port, dma, etc....all pretty precious resources.

Well, it's all true, but it goes just the same for additional 16750 UART
(I never had one, but I guess it needs an IRQ, an I/O port just like
16550A, right?).

> now....it all comes down to a matter of price and personal preference.....I
> prefer external modems over internal....I've had way too many problems with
> internal modems over the many years of my computer experience to WANT to use
> one myself. However, internals are cheaper...so people buy them.

Personal preference has a great deal here, together with needs (I can
spare a ISA slot for ISDN card, somebody else can't). A price is also
important, ~30$ for an ISDN card is much nicer than ~80$ for 16750 alone,
not even mentioning an external ISDN TA (probably ~250$ or more).

>
> do whatever you want...I was just making a suggestion....
>

Well... Thanks. :)))
