On Mon, Dec 07, 1998 at 10:01:47AM -0500, Greg Lindahl wrote:
> > Is this the origin of the
> > rule of the thumb that swap space ought to be at least twice the size
> > of RAM?
>
> No. On other Unix versions, all pages must have a disk page for every
> memory page. This isn't true for Linux. The behavior you describe is a
> bug. It's not a bug that your swap gets used -- the kernel is just
> trying to keep the free list big by speculatively writing out dirty
> data pages to disk. But then that there is resource exhaustion is a
> bug. However, the symptom you see may still not help identify the
> problem.

Also, if it's a bug (and I think it is), it's not only the Alpha kernel:
I saw the same behaviour on a pentium running 2.0.34 the other day....
