With regards to your message at 04:12 PM 12-03-98 +0100, Dauter, Oliver.
Where you stated:
>Hi everybody,
>
>I´m interrested in a parallel zip-drive for data exchange of ´my´ machines
>on different sites. I read some Howtos and list-archives, but never found
>something about it _running on alpha_ (on i86 it sounds quiet easy :)). I
>use an AS 400 4/233 with RH 5.2 and Kernel 2.0.35.
>
>Also I want to buy my AS400 some extra memory. Everything I found about it
>says I need 36/33 bit standart SIMMS with 70ns. Can I use EDOs or FPMs? Does
>they need parity?
>
>Can someone out there give me a tip, please?
>
>Oliver Dauter
They need to be true parity, not "logic" parity. In other word REAL 36 bit
SIMMs. Also, look at the sockets in your machine. If the are silver colored
leads use "tin lead" SIMMs, if gold, then gold, of course. EDO will not
work, they must be Fast Page Mode.
Quality is quite important. Don't go for cheapies. In general SIMMs with
Samsung or Toshiba chips are often very good. Stay away from Hyundai and
NEC if you intend to run Digital UNIX.
