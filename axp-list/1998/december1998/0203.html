This is a bt of an exorbitant price to pay for a cable like this, -*maybe*-
The "68 pin connector" you mention could either be an HD68 connector or a
.8mm pitch Centronics connector. If the former, a cable should cost about
$17.50 If it is the more "exotic" mini Centronics about $45.
I guess the best way to tell is to ask: "What brand and model of SCSI card
_do_ you have?" The NCR53C875 is the chip on the card, not the card itself.

Next, we need to address one little detail:
A 68 pin SCSI connector means a wide (16 bit) SCSI bus. Now, think of wide
SCSI as simply 2 narrow(8-bit) buses in the same connection. The problem we
have to deal with is to ensure that if you connect only narrow devices on
this external connector you need to disable any termination on the low
8-bit portion, and make sure it stays enabled on the high 8-bit half.

>So, I started looking at Cheaper Than Dirt (TM) Brand ISA SCSI

>This is US$34, which is more in line, and I'd like to pick it up, assuming
>it'll work in my SX164. Unfortunately I see no "ZIP Zoom" driver in the
>kernel source. So, I have a few questions:
> 1. Does anyone have any experience with this particular adapter,
> and will it work in my SX164?
> 2. What IS this adapter? (i.e. does it have drive support under

>Now, I asked a similar question once before, and I believe Jay said that

>Dan Morrill

In any case, this is all quite "do-able" without sticking yet another card
and set of drivers into your system. The $68 price tag for the cable is a
bit outrageous.
Attaching narrow devices to wide bus connections is not a problem, as long
as you make sure the termination is deal with.
