Paul B. Brown wrote:
>
>
> It isn't an *official* kernel?? Then why is it being offered for
> download?

Because, as Jay patiently explained for an umpteenth time and even
listed assorted reasons, an "official" 2.0.x kernel - without patching -
does not work on Alpha. This situation is different for 2.1.yyy
development kernels and so therefore you will not need any extra
patches when 2.2. series finally will show up.

If you think that you are satisfied with an *official* 2.0.x kernel
then there is nobody who could try to prevent you from trying to run it
on Alpha. Go ahead and be happy.

BTW - I mailed to Jay a set of alpha patches for 2.0.36. They "carry
over" to 2.0.36 a set of his patches for 2.0.35 - the latest patch set
which I had on hand. As a matter of fact it is pretty trivial to perform
such update "feat". You will get a few rejects which has to be resolved
"by hand" (updates to tulip.c and de4x5.c are already included in 2.0.36
so you do not have to worry about these). I do not want to post these
patches on my own as Jay, in the meantime, may had some other changes
and it is enough of "alpha-patches" and associated confusion floating
around already. Maybe he will find some time to review them and to post
them "alpha-officially". :-)
