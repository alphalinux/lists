Read the manual page for dump. You can tell it how big the tape is, either with
the -B option, or this the -d density and -s feet options. It defaults to a
2500' tape at 1600BPI (which I think is an old reel-to-reel standard?). The
other option is to dump to stdout, in which case it assumes the tape is
infineite in length :-)

Oh btw, the calculations aren't as easy as one might think; 61000BPI on a 90M
tape doesn't give you the right answer either; the calculation doesn't work for
helical-scan tapes (how far does the head really travel?). Even with cartridge
tapes you have to be careful; eg a QIC150 cartridge is 600' long at 10000BPI,
but it has 18 tracks. Best just to use the -B option, or fake it untill it
works.
