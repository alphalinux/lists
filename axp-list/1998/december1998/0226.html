When I got my first RH linux, after years of other versions, I had an
annoying runin with what I thought was a problem. I chased it down found
the alias.

After thinking about it (2 seconds) I recognized the value of the -i for
routine actions. Root has TOO much power to freely be rm-ing casually and
if root shoots herself in the foot, well, too bad.

As root, to get around the alias, In either use the
'rm -rf offending-directory' or, give the path to rm
/bin/rm -<options> <files/directories>. Either works and is worth the
effort.

After my confrontation with the RH alias, I created a "saferm" alias for
ordinary users and recommend its use.
