>>So, I started looking at Cheaper Than Dirt (TM) Brand ISA SCSI
>>controllers, and of course I found the Iomega "ZIP Zoom" SCSI 2 adapter.
>>This is US$34, which is more in line, and I'd like to pick it up,
assuming
>>it'll work in my SX164. Unfortunately I see no "ZIP Zoom" driver in the
>>kernel source. So, I have a few questions:
>> 1. Does anyone have any experience with this particular adapter,
>> and will it work in my SX164?
>> 2. What IS this adapter? (i.e. does it have drive support under

I don't remember. The 'Jaz Jet' is an usually an Advansys chip (PCI). The
LDP has/had a Zip-HOWTO that covered this deep in the bowels of the doc.
Check it out. Don't expect support from Iomega getting this thing to work,
period. Even in a system that they say should support it, as they charge
20$ a pop for phone help, even if you did just buy 500$ worth of their
product.

Remember, the kernel source cannot know/include every manufacturer that has
products based on certain chipsets, wich this happens with the ESS chip
lines, DEC's net chips.

>In any case, this is all quite "do-able" without sticking yet another card
>and set of drivers into your system. The $68 price tag for the cable is a
>bit outrageous.
>Attaching narrow devices to wide bus connections is not a problem, as long
>as you make sure the termination is deal with.

I paid 28$ (in the USA) for my external-wide to external-narrow 3-foot (1
meter) cable. It works like a dandy. Yes, setting port termination
low-off, high-on is recommended. For a while I had all high (whoops) it
worked for a while, but the SCSI bus eventually locked.

As for pricing, I'm with you on that. CompUSA tries to sell high-density
50 pin SCSI cables for 60$. I thought that was horrible until I saw same
at a start-up store for 120$. Es ist schrechlich.
