No Bernard. These baords are from the AT days, not the more recent ATX.
Nothing "standard" about the power supply. It needs to be an AT power
supply, with 2 additional 6 pin connectors, providing 3 pins carrying 3.3V
and 3 pins carrying ground connection on each of these 2 additional
connectors. The power provided is additional to the "normal" 5, 12, -5 and
-12V on the "normal" AT power supply. The connectors are often referred to
as P9, P10, P11, P12.

What happened: In the AT standard, you needed 5,12, -5, -12, and gorund.
Then systems came out with CPUs, PCI buses and other devices requiring 3.3
V power.
To provide this the manufacturers added these 2 new connectors. This was a
kind of "transition" design.
Then came ATX as a standard, which provides for the 3.3V ( along with a few
new features, such as "soft power" control).

As for the case: AT design is what you need, with 1 small change: the mouse
and keyboard connectors are placed together, in the location where an AT
keyboard connector "normally" is on a case. So the hole has to be enlarged
a bit. If you have some handtools like a drill, file, and pliers you can do
this yourself. It is not complicated.

The other thing I think should be mentioned is that these boards do not
have a built-in SCSI interface, and as the ARC BIOS on them is a bit older,
not many modern SCSI cards will be seen by the BIOS for the purpose of
booting. We have had best luck with the Intraserver 3140U-N and 4140UE-N
for this purpose. Diamond Fireport40, Buslogic BT958, and Adaptec 2940 did
NOT work.
