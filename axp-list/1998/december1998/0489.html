Oscar Levi wrote:
>
> On Thu, Dec 10, 1998 at 11:47:57PM -0600, Robb Romans wrote:
> > Sheesh, 60 bucks. I don't even need one and I ordered two :)
>
> Beware. I have two of the first round machines that have everything
> in them except for socketed CPU and cache. These machines are not for
> the faint hearted. Without a floppy or hard drive you will be
> challenged to make them run. While there is an IDE interface, the
> connector is non-standard. The floppy is a scsi device and I don't
> think anyone makes SCSI hard drives that fit into these cases.

The so-called "slim line" 3.5 inch drives fit perfectly. These are
.75 inches tall. I picked up a 1GB drive for $45.00 It was a
DEC RZ26. You can buy new drives that will fit also.

> you can run an external, but the key utility to these machines is that
> they're self-contained.

I agree. I am told that Multia will boot off the network and run
"diskless". I have a disk so have not (yet) tried.

> Since the world is going 100BaseT, the
> internal net card may be useless. For example, I have 100BaseT only
> hubs.

Cheap trick: Drop a cheap 10Base2 card in one of your other computers
that has a spare slot. That computer then becomes a router and connects
your 10Base2 (coax, aka, BNC) LAN with your 100BaseT LAN
