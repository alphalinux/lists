Dave Beck wrote:
>
> Er... Well I can't say that I just changed a tire while my car was
> doing 140 kph on I95 and all. You are correct about "shutting down"
> the device about to be "unscrewed," but that doesn't change the fact
> that your critical chunk of code/data (the kernel) is getting tweaked.
> I should point out that I prefer the inconveinence of a kernel compile
> to the down time (and overhead) incurred as a result of kernel modules.
>
> Of course a kernel compile for me takes under 2 minutes on my dual box.
>

I guess I've just gotten used to several systems that support
dynamic device reconfiguration without re-booting. Linux, Solaris,
and AIX all allow that. You just have to be careful what you do
to keep things in order. I remove/add tape drives all the time
as modules and muck with that SCSI chain and it usually works
well. Now, of course I run tape drives on a separate adapter
just for that reason. Wouldn't do that on a chain with disks,
unless I could close all file systems on the drive before doing
anything to it. I've done that sort of thing on AIX though, and been
burned only once in the past 8 years.(lost my OS partition due
to SCSI errors trashing the file system)

For what I do, it's more just configuring external devices than
anything else. I don't see much need to do that with devices
plugged into the PCI bus because typically you can't hot swap
those adapters anyway so you have to power down to do the
change.

One improvement I'd like in Linux is a command similar to AIX's
"cfgmgr" which does an automatic rescan for new devices and loads
the needed modules without having to do it manually though.
Basically, similar to how Linux already does things at boot
but user invokable at any time. Anyone know if such a thing
is available or could be made to work?
