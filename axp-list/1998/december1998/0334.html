I got my alpha lpd working again--maybe the following will help a gru figure out what's
going on:

The first thing I did was search my system for all instances of `lpd'
and found the lock file:

  /var/lock/susbsys/lpd

I noticed it had the reboot time. I removed the file.

Then I noticed that there were two copies of lpd running--maybe that's
why the connect problem: one was already connected to the /dev/printer.
(Actually I believe there will normally be two processes, but only when there is an
active job. I believe the second lpd was somehow left as an orphan
and was holding the lock, thus preventing normal operations.)

With the lock removed, I again executed

  /etc/rc.d/init.d/lpd stop

which deleted both lpd processes (I may have had to do it twice--
I'm not sure and I've not been able to duplicate the dual lpd's again.
I suspect that if I had done this twice before, or manually killed all
lpd's, I could have gotten a normal restart.)

Then I executed:

  /etc/rc.d/init.d/lpd start

and

  lpc restart lp

and everthing was up and running fine again.

I wish I could give a better description of what was wrong, how
to duplicate it, and how to fix it, but at least this fumble worked
for me.

Good luck.
