> So, I started looking at Cheaper Than Dirt (TM) Brand ISA SCSI
> controllers, and of course I found the Iomega "ZIP Zoom" SCSI 2 adapter.
> This is US$34, which is more in line, and I'd like to pick it up, assuming
> it'll work in my SX164. Unfortunately I see no "ZIP Zoom" driver in the
> kernel source. So, I have a few questions:

You obviously have to look at teh chip to be sure, but my ZIP Zoom (with
cheapo 25pin external SCSI connector (uses the Iomega SCSI cable that
came with your ZIP drive)) is an AIC-6360 and uses the aha152x driver.
I think most, if not all of them, are similar. Mine is actually etched
"Adaptec 1502e" on the silkscreen, but I've heard of people with 1502[a-z],
1505, and 1520. They (usually) all work with the aha152x driver. I've
only used mine in an Intel box, as my UDB doesn't have a ISA slot (thank
God!).

$34 seems kinda high--I'd only pay 5 or 10 bucks for one of these cards
today, but then I already have one, so that's kind of unfair. Mine was
free from a brother who had three of them. :)
