I have two Alphas, one is PC164 based with a 500Mhz CPU,
the other is 164LX based with a 533MHz CPU. Both are
running under a very similar software configuration: RH51
with a few updates, binutils-2.9.1.0.4-3 (from orig distrib),
glibc-2.0.7-19 (updated).

egcs-1.1.1 configures itself as `alphaev56-unknown-linux-gnu'
as expected on the 164LX based machine, but (as all other
egcs versions I tried) as `alphaev5-unknown-linux-gnu'.

If I set the target manually on the PC164 machine to
`alphaev56-unknown-linux-gnu', the build process of egcs stops
in the 2nd stage (at least in a stage where stage1/xgcc is
used for compilation) when executing gengenrtl (illegal
instruction).

What's the reason for all that? I thought that all 21164 CPUs
at 366MHz and above are `ev56'.
