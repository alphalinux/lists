At 02:25 PM 12/21/98 -0500, you wrote:
>It doesn't hurt to be patient.

I have ordered two, one at the higher price (with floppy), I think it
was $99 + S/H, the second one (without floppy, but *with* PCI riser card)
for $59 + S/H. The first one came within a week (haven't had a chance to
boot it yet), the second one took about two weeks, but it came with an
extra battery (I suspect) - it was in the box, not in the Multia case,
so I guess by mistake/christmas spirit they included another battery...

>CPUMicromart is selling about 200-500 of these multias a week. The
>salesperson I spoke to was very helpful and nice. They don't have a lot of
>info on these machines, because they just picked up about 9000 of them in a
>DEC FRU closeout. So, if you want a Multia cheap, be patient, deal with
>the problems and get one.

I'd agree - the Multia makes sense if you have access to cheap RAM (like
in your parts bin) and have an old SCSI drive or can run them diskless...
IMHO the cost of RAM is what drives these machines out of consideration -
$160 for 64 Meg of RAM (4x$40) in a $59 box is a bit silly, but I guess
stranger things happen...

>I got two. They didn't double bill me, the order took 4 days longer than I
>was told, but who cares?! It's the holiday season and I got Multias for $59!

All in all, I'm pretty happy with CPUMicroMart myself, but, realize what
you are getting!
