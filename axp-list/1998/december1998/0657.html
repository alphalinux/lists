On Tue, 15 Dec 1998, Jonathan Vafai wrote:

> What is the difference between the kernel that is at ftp.kernel.org
> and the one available via cvs at vger?

The kernel at ftp.kernel.org is a mainstream one, maintained by Alan Cox
(2.0.x) and Linus (2.1.x). Some people say it would be targeted mainly at
the i386 platform.

vger carries more advanced code, includes more patches/fixes, is more
portable, but also has some experimental code. It was a habit that patches
coming from vger were included into the mainstream one, but since an
argument some time ago, this is not the case anymore (it was some argument
about a 800kb patch, iirc).
