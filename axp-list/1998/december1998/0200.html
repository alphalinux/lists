I currently have a parallel port ZIP drive, but I promised it to my
father, since I also have a SCSI (2) ZIP drive kicking around. I have an
NCR53C875 UW SCSI controller, but its external connector is the 68-pin
micropin form factor, and the ZIP SCSI cable is 25-pin on both ends.

I found a cable that looks like it *might* work, but it is US$68, which
just isn't something I'm going to pay to get a drive working for which I
paid US$60 in the first place. Besides, I'm not sure it'll even work.

So, I started looking at Cheaper Than Dirt (TM) Brand ISA SCSI
controllers, and of course I found the Iomega "ZIP Zoom" SCSI 2 adapter.
This is US$34, which is more in line, and I'd like to pick it up, assuming
it'll work in my SX164. Unfortunately I see no "ZIP Zoom" driver in the
kernel source. So, I have a few questions:
        1. Does anyone have any experience with this particular adapter,
            and will it work in my SX164?
        2. What IS this adapter? (i.e. does it have drive support under
            a different name?)

Now, I asked a similar question once before, and I believe Jay said that
in general ISA SCSI cards on Alphae are problematic, so I'm not hopeful,
but I figured I'd ask anyway, before I break down and buy another parallel
port drive (which I can get cheaper than the cable) and try and unload
this SCSI deal.
