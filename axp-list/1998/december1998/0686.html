On Wed, Dec 16, 1998 at 06:08:50PM +0100, Thorsten Koch wrote:
> Hello,
>
> does anybody know if the 21164 can get more than one result
> per cycle ?
> Is there a sequence of instructions like
>
> addt $f0,$f1,$f2
> addq $1,$2,$3
> mult $f3,$f4,$f5
> addq $4,$5,$6
>
> that executes in less than 4 cycles ?

I think the question is whether or not the processor issues multiple
instructions per cycle. I believe that the 21264 does this, but the
21164 is only pipelined. IIRC, multiple issue makes sense only when
coupled with out-of-order execution. This, too, I believe is a 21264
feature. Also note that to execute more than four instructions in
four cycles requires a fetch unit that can fetch more than one
instruction per cycle. Again, IIRC, the memory bus must be at least
64 bits wide on the Alpha for this to happen since instruction are at
least 32 bits wide.

I'm not sure you would see any given fragment execute in less than 4
cycles. Instead, you might see the aggregate performance of a
sequence of instructions be less than the sequential fetch time.
