I work at an ISP with a v.90 modem service. Although I do not
follow specifically that area, I see all the users calls to technical
support and there are quite a few similar to yours.

I would tend to say that with a probability >99% your Alpha has nothing
to do with this issue. This should be a modem protocol negotiation issue,
and the parameters to consider first are: (a) what modem you have
(brand, or at least chipset); (b) what modem (or access server
with modems inside) your ISP has; (c) what modem initialization
strings are used on both sides.

I would suggest to open a call with your ISP, giving them all
the modem information on your side, and listen to what they
suggest. At present there are still a lot of interoperability problems
in the v.90 world. You have a good chance (say, 75%) that your
problem could be solved by crafting the modem initialization
strings. For the other 25%, you may be running across a genuine
modem interoperability problem. V.90 modem firmware is changing
nearly weekly these days. If your ISP uses an access server
with downloadable modem firmware and the currently installed
release is more than one-two months old, updating the modem
firmware could solve the problem with no debugging work.

In all cases I do not think that discussion on this topic belongs
to this list; feel free to email me privately if you wish.
