The first question that comes t my mind is "What sort of cache memory are
you using ?"

The noname boards used a set of 1 discrete chips for cache and it won't run
without them.

You may want to strip your configuration down to motherboard, cache, memory
and power supply. Then connect a serial device of some sort to the com1
port and power it up. If you don't see anything at all, try the other com
port. You wouldn't be the first to get them mixed up.

If you don't see anything on the serial port, the reasons are fairly
limited...

        Your cache may be of the wrong type or not properly configured.
        Your main memory may be of the wrong type or not properly
configured.
        Your console flash may be blown or the jumpers not properly
configured.
        You may have a bad board...
