>Next year, we'll have the requirement of 4-8 alphas to be used in sort of
>an embedded application and we've currently based all our cost estimates
>on 164LX 600MHz boards with SRM and DU (yes I know, Linux is cheaper but
>there's a big deal of third party software we need to run).
>
>In any case: I gathered from this list that the 164LX and 164SX
>boards maybe are not available anymore in the near future so I've
>looked into the 164RX which seems to meet our requirements quite
>good. The only problem I have when looking through:
>
>http://www.digital.com/alphaoem/dsc-pc164rx.html
>
>is that it mentions neither the SRM console firmware nor Digital Unix.
>
>Any ideas anyone if SRM and DU will be available for the 164RX ?

After speaking with the people within Compaq, TOEM will not offer any NEW
products to resellers with Digital UNIX as an option.

However, Samsung is looking to build at least 3,000 LX motherboards next
February with 2mb and 4mb cache. Therefore, we'll have a UNIX solution for
a limited time.

The bad news is, some idiot within Compaq believes OEMs like myself are
causing to much competition for the System Business Unit. Until Compaq
realizes we actually help UNIX sales, nothing will be done. One quick look
at the UNIX market you can see Compaq has the smallest market share of any
UNIX operating system. In fact before Compaq purchased Digital, Digital
sales have been flat for "10 YEARS"!!!!!!!

In addition, Compaq and Samsung both see Linux as the # 1 market for Alpha
sales. UNIX accounts for about 25% of our sales and could be much better but
the constant battle with us and SBU just gets worse.

My only hope is Compaq will see that the same idiots within SBU just merely
go about their business blaming eveyone but themselves for poor Alpha and
UNIX sales and do nothing to open the market.

If you noticed we are now selling Sun clones. Guess what, Suns allows us to
bundle Solaris 2.7(64-bit)with every system sale and we compete with them.
However, Sun realizes a sale is a sale. Bottom line, Sun just picked up
another Solaris seat and Digital just lost one.
