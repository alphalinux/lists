Buy an internal 68-50 pin adaptor. They exist. Don't pay more than 30$
for it and that is still steep. Sorry, the rules of physics is against
you on your expectations.

The reason so many people have trouble with SCSI is that few people know
electromagnetics (or just follow the directions in the first place). The
second internal connector is used to save cost on adaptors. You CANNOT
use all three connectors and expect them to work. My roomate wondered why
his SCSI stuff was screwed up. He ran for years without a terminator at
one end. No terminators cause reflections and
harmonics, as does having a three-way bus, as this significantly affects
the bandwidth performance of a system, As this is called 'Stub tuning'
meaning that is only done to optimize the analog bandwidth of a
transmission line, but done wrong, and you can play havoc with your
expectations.

> Current situation is now:
>
> HD drive 0 (id 0) is connected to 68 pin internal connecter
> HD drive 1 (id 1) is connected to 68 pin external connecter
> internal 50 pin connector remains free.

2 of three. ONLY two of three. Ignore the manuals and rules at your own
peril.
