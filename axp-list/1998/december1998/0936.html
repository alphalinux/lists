At 11:17 AM 12/21/98 -0500, you wrote:
>
>Help, I have an axp pci-33 board that I got a while back. It had been
>sitting in storage for a little while until I could get the parts together
>to assemble it. I've put together the following pieces
>
>1 Quantum 6.3 Fireball scsi II
>1 PC AT case
>1 S3 chippped video card with 4 mb of memory
>1 3.5 mitsumi drive
>4 32mb x36 (True parity) ram (Total 128mb)

What is the wattage of the Power SUpply - I think you really want a 250
Watt PS, but slightly less may be OK...

Is the RAM known good, by that I mean it works in another machine?
What is the timing/speed of the RAM? Mfg.?

Do you know the video card is good?

>I put it all together, and flipped the power switch..........
>
>NOTHING, nada, zero, the The power supply comes on, the case fan spins, I
>can hear the HD spin up, but thats it. Nothing to the video output, and
>nothing to com1 (though I don't have a terminal program that does 0 stop
>bits!) ANY help would be rgeatly appreaciated.

I suspect that the RAM is the problem - DEC Alphas are notorious for
being very picky about RAM. I would try and buy/borrow some good RAM
(Kingston, for example, although there are others) and try again...

>Any advice??

Just the above... also, make sure the jumpers on the Motherboard are
set to the proper settings, and if you added any cache, make sure it
is all plugged in correctly, etc... Just double-check everything!
