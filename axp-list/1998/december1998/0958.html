>>> Bob Taylor said:
>
> Would someone please explain to me why alpha-patches-2.0.35-0.2 is not
> incorporated into the "official" kernel sources?

There were two major areas of concern voiced by Alan Cox, when I originally
submitted the 2.0.34 alpha-patches to him, for inclusion in 2.0.34:

1. Simultaneous "support" of VGA and TGA in the console code caused many
   files to be modified. This was deemed unacceptable, as they were not
   "bugfix" changes, but functionality changes, and of use only to Alpha.

2. Several important common header files, in order to work correctly on
   Alpha, needed certain fields' data types changed. This was also deemed
   unacceptable; the header files were include/linux/{ipc,msgs,shm}.h, very
   important to correct operation of some applications, most importantly X.

[I think there may have been a few other small things, but can't remember
 offhand what they were. ]

This problem disappears with 2.2; the addition of framebuffer support allows
both VGA and non-VGA support to co-exist in a kernel, and the header files
in question now match the Alpha versions... ;-}
