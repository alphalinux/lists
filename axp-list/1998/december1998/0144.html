It can be done. The SyQuest SyJet 1.5GB Parallel was actually a SCSI drive
with a parallel->SCSI adaptor :) the Best Buy sales person didn't know what
he was talking about when I asked about that anomaly. It is nice, but
slow, as par port is slow, but it is usable for backup. Linux has some
support for this. You can chain multiple drives to that too, whereas the
documentation says no (they lie or misconstrue, but they probably don't
want to support that kind of stuff). It is really an special cable made by
a company called Shuttle. I have two that I am not using. I suppose I
wouldn't mind giving one up for about shipping cost, but it would be as-is
b/c I haven't used them since I sold my rePentium 133, and I have real SCSI
on both my systems. I have no driver disks or docs either.
