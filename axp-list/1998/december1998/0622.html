>>> Bob McElrath said:
> On Tue, 15 Dec 1998 Jay.Estabrook@digital.com wrote:
> >
> > The pristine 2.0.36 sources do *not* include all the patches you get
> > with the alpha-sources and alpha-patches 2.0.35-0.2 on ftp.digital.com.
>
> Why not? Perhaps these should be submitted to Linus for inclusion?

First off, Linus hasn't been the one in charge of the last couple of 2.0.xx
releases; that's been Alan Cox.

Second, the alpha-patches touch a bunch of stuff that Alan was not happy
about, and thus rejected from 2.0.34 (and I never submitted them to 35 or
36 for the same reason). Among these sensitive spots are:

1. simultaneous VGA and TGA mucks about in the console code a lot. this has
   been made acceptable in the 2.1.xx kernels, but I have *no* intention of
   backporting all that framebuffer stuff, even if Alan would allow it.

2. some structure fields in certain headers (ipc, msgs, shm) are different
   size under Alpha (partly historic) than under Intel; the fact that the
   Alpha ones match the 2.1.xx version doesn't matter.

3. some other minor things that I'm forgetting; if really curious, look at
   the diffs between pristine 2.0.34 and alpha-sources-2.0.34-0.1.

But please relax; 2.2 has them all... :-)
