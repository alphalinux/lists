   these two things aren't really that linked apart from in my
head so don't go jumping to conclusions!
   Motherboards: we've got a couple of 533MHz 21164a's
loitering around as the result of trying to destroy my lx164
(or getting VMS to run on it, whichever you prefer). We
thought we'd make another machine or two with them, until
we contacted dec (we're a digital oem) and were told the
price for a motherboard was £1033 ($1700?), which is a bit
steep. Anyone know what the going rate for a Samsung board
is, or for that matter what digipaq/comtal sells them for in
the US?
   Memory: completely unrelated. We recently acquired a
large amount of memory for alphas when one of our customers
upgraded a load of alphastations. Thus we've ended up with
about 70 pieces of 16Mb SIMMS that came from alphastation
250s. They're genuine DEC (even got Digital Equipment
Corporation written on them!) and work in alphastations and
nonames: not tried them in anything else but I'm sure someone
round here will know what else they'll go in (don't *think* they
work in most multias but I haven't tried it). We haven't really got
a definite plan on how to dispose of them yet but I was wondering
if any of the good people on this list might have an interest in
them..
