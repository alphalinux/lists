>
> yeah but with the adapter isn't there a noticable decrease om bandwidth?
> I would think there is some loss from the modem - cable - PC. With the
> prices of ISDN every kilobyte costs you money.

if you put it on a serial port that uses a 16650 UART, yes... you'll be
limited to 115,200 bps.... losing just a slight bit of extra bandwidth....is
it significant?....I doubt it.

If you're worried about speed... get a 16750 UART card (~$80), it'll run up
to 230kbps. So, if you run compression on your ppp session on the ISDN T/A,
you can get upwards of that full 230k.
