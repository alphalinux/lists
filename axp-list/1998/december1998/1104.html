Thanks for all of the ideas. I wrote a script and when I did tail -f
/etc/HOSTNAME I got:

X(719): unaligned trap at ooooooo12022d8a4: 00000155564f7244 2d 8 (and 3
more)
then the change came.

ps -aux showed pid 719 to be /usr/X11R6/bin/Xwrapper owned by root. (I
am dialing in from a user not root)

It happens both with usernet from redhat and my own script. If this is
not a big problem I certainly can live with it. Not much of a security
problem as I don't log in often or for long periods. Rarely more than 15
to 20 mins at a time.
