On Tue, Dec 08, 1998 at 03:24:38PM -0500, Daniel Stringfield wrote:
> On Tue, 8 Dec 1998, Oscar Levi wrote:
>
> > I, too, use SRM. I like it better than MILO because it doesn't
> > require the MEMORY_SIZE environment variable and it boots *much*
> > faster than MILO. The only catch is that you must partition with BSD
> > labels. fdisk has an option for this. Make the label before
> > installing the OS or you may (I did) have trouble with the CHS
> > layout.
>
> One thing I had problems with was I had to make the partition normally,
> then do a bsd disklabel as well. I don't know if this is the "correct"
> way, but the installation script kept saying I didn't have enough disk
> space, despite the fact it said it had 75% free, until after I did this.
> I don't know if it is implied that you also make the regular linux
> partitions with fdisk + BSD disklabels, but this may be something to look
> at.

I had the other experience. I partitioned with fdisk some time ago
and had installed RedHat. When I wanted to install Debian and use
ABOOT, I found that attempting to apply a bsd label to the already
partitioned disk changed the CHS layout and confused both me and the
operating system loader. I solved the problem by clobbering the disk
and repartitioning with BSD labels first. Then, all was OK.
