> I'm considering buying an Alpha workstation, based on a UX board as
> they appear to be faster and cheaper than the rapidly vanishing LX
> boards. I intend to get 256 MB of SDRAM to start with and soup it up
> to at least 512 MB as soon as my wallet recovers. However I'm
> concerned because in the past several people mentioned problems with
> large memory configurations.
>
> Is anyone out there running on UX boards with more than 256 MB? If so
> have you had any trouble? Did you find a work-around or have you
> gotten stuck with unsolvable problems?

Our experience: I have spent perhaps one or two hundred hours on
three UX's during the period july-october, doing all kind of tests.
Eventually we gave up and swapped them with LX's. All the previous
problems disappeared and we could put the machines in production almost
instantly. My only regret is that we did not do this before.

Of course, UX problems could have been solved by now, and there
are people who always have been happy with them.
