Hi John,

See my response to a response I got to my post. My problem was including
the frame buffer options... once I took them out, the kernel booted just
fine.. in fact, it makes my disk access a LOT faster.... you can
literarly feel the speed without wishful thinking.

I don't know if that might be your problem.. but it worked for me. My
only other problem now, is that either my init scripts or something is
too old, and my routing table entries are all duplicated. It doesn't
hinder anything... but just want to fix it.

I've had 2.1.131 running as a gateway and workstation 11 hours now.. and
counting... not a single problem yet... but we'll wait and see... I did
find some patchs that Alan Cox released for the 2.1.131 kernel... I
didn't use them, as I didn't know about them when I compiled my kernel..
until I notice a problem, I probably will put off worrying about those
patches. I'm just happy that 131 is working and my disk access is so
much faster....

But if the frame buffer exclusion doesn't work for you, perhaps you will
want to try the patches..??

Good Luck.
