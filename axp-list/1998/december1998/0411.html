On Wed, Dec 09, 1998 at 01:03:02PM -0700, Maurice Hilarius wrote:
> If performance is an issue, I suggest the Mylex DAC960 or the American
> Megatrends cards.
> I also suggest you look at SCSI/SCSI RAID, as they require no device
> drivers, and cna be battery backed, so that in the event of a power failure
> or crash you do not lose the data in the cache on the controller. A large
> amount of RAID performance can be gained with a big cache, but, of course,
> this means more of your precious data is living in a cache buffer at any
> given time..

I'm using a DAC960 on x86 and the performance is good. However, there
are no Linux utilities to configure the card yet, and needless to say
the BIOS configuration program won't work on an Alpha.

The AMI MegaRAID has significant driver problems for the time being,
at least on 2.1.x. I think Linux tools are available for it, however.

Check out the ICP Vortex cards. That may be an option... Otherwise,
SCSI-SCSI will work, but it will not be nearly as fast as controller-based
RAID.
