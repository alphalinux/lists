> I'm having a DEC 2000axp station on which I've setup Redhat 5.2 linux
> with kernel 2.0.36. I'm also having two EISA DE422 ethernet adpater in
> it .
> I could only get one to work by compiling the driver into the kernel.
> I've tried to add boot parameters like "ether=10,0,eth1" but it din't
> help the recognition of the second card. I've also tried with modules
> but that didn't work at all.
> The EISA parameters off my ethernet adapters are:
> ETH0:
> irq=5
> mem=0xd0000
> ETH1:
> irq=10
> mem=0xe0000

When I tried to get an 3C595 card to work, I noticed that it wasn't
enabled even though it was correctly configured with the ECU. It was
my guess that usually Digital Unix uses the information in the NVRAM
written by the ECU to actually enable the card during the boot, and
that Linux actually ignores the ECU settings.

So you might need to patch the driver to enable the card by hand. This
is just an idea, it might not work.

HTH, Martin

PS.: no, I don't recall which register you need to write to. Either
check EISA specs or look at the driver if it has this feature for the
first card ...
