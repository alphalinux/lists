I ordered one at the "old" price of $99, but when I ordered they didn't
have the internal PCI/SCSI riser card units, just the internal SCSI drive
versions... Well, I knew I would want to add a CD ROM drive to my unit
(to, um, install the OS), but SCSI interface cards were about $25... For
$60 I get another unit, *with* the PCI/SCSI Riser and some spare parts!

See below for further responses...

At 10:39 PM 12/10/98 -0800, you wrote:
>On Thu, Dec 10, 1998 at 11:47:57PM -0600, Robb Romans wrote:
>> Sheesh, 60 bucks. I don't even need one and I ordered two :)
>
>Beware. I have two of the first round machines that have everything
>in them except for socketed CPU and cache. These machines are not for

What would a socketed CPU offer you? CPUs are *very* expensive (esp.
compared to $59!) and the units they offer now have 256K cache - it
could use more, but it is something!

>the faint hearted. Without a floppy or hard drive you will be
>challenged to make them run. While there is an IDE interface, the
>connector is non-standard.

If you have a Multia you can move the existing HD into the new "bargain"
machine, or net-boot them and run diskless - be sure you have lots of RAM
though!

The IDE interface is a standard laptop 2 1/2" interface - Maurice has
offered these in the past for sale, may still have some... BUT, the IDE
performance on these units (IIRC) is not good...

>The floppy is a scsi device and I don't
>think anyone makes SCSI hard drives that fit into these cases. Sure,

I don't know about the floppy, I suspect it is a standard laptop drive...

As for SCSI drives, you can find them up to about a Gig as Mac Powerbook
upgrades (2 1/2" SCSI drives were used in *some* models). I would
*strongly* advise anyone thinking about these units to get the external
SCSI/PCI riser card version...

>you can run an external, but the key utility to these machines is that
>they're self-contained.

That is a fair point, but as I said, net-boot is an interesting option!

>Since the world is going 100BaseT, the
>internal net card may be useless. For example, I have 100BaseT only
>hubs.

This is one of the reasons I suggest getting the PCI/SCSI riser card -
you can add a $40 100BaseT card!

>I also paid much more than 60 corn for mine.

I paid a lot more for my first Multia, sold it for about half what I
paid, then bought two more recently (out of shear amazement - I happen
to have a bit of Parity RAM & HDs I can use to make these run in my
parts bin...)
