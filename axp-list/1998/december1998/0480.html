> > Sheesh, 60 bucks. I don't even need one and I ordered two :)

Good!

> Beware. I have two of the first round machines that have everything
> in them except for socketed CPU and cache. These machines are not for
> the faint hearted. Without a floppy or hard drive you will be
> challenged to make them run. While there is an IDE interface, the
> connector is non-standard.

Standard connector, just less commonly used size. A decent hardware
hacker can make one of anylength by ordering 2mm 44 pin connectors and 1mm
ribbon cable from Digikey - this would be used for the laptop drives. As
for size adaptors, I've seen adaptors for 8$ at a local computer show.

> The floppy is a scsi device

Since WHEN? That sure isn't a standard SCSI connector, and even so, it
doesn't show up on SCSI chain diagnostics. It is a normal floppy. I've
seen other floppies with that kind of a connector.

>and I don't think anyone makes SCSI hard drives that fit into these
cases.

That just depends on the riser card & bracket availability, but they
advertise one that can fit a 3.5" drive.

> they're self-contained. Since the world is going 100BaseT, the
> internal net card may be useless. For example, I have 100BaseT only
> hubs.

Hmmm. I picked up a 16$ DE435 PCI NIC new-old-stock just so that my MIATA
can communicate to the Multia using coax.

> I also paid much more than 60 corn for mine.

Oops. These machines are nifty, but I don't consider it to be usable as a
primary machine unless you only now have a 486. Usable as an Xterm
oh yeah.
