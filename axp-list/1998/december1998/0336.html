With regards to your message at 12:21 PM 12-08-98 -0500,
jhohertz@golden.net. Where you stated:
>
>If it's not a Yamaha, it's just not worth your time.
>
>cdrecord works fine for me, but beware that late 2.1.xxx kernels are not
>very good for cutting, and you're liable to make a lot of coasters if you
>try. Stick to 2.0.xxx kernels for cutting for now.

I tend to disagree with this.
We have used some of the Yamaha drives successfully, but in general they
tend to be a bit "touchy". In other words they tend to make a higher
percentage of beer glass coasters than the Sony, Plextor, and Matsushita
drives. The differences are pretty trivial, but I can say I do not think
the Yamaha units are the best.
