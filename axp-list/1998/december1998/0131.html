On Thu, Dec 03, 1998 at 11:59:08AM -0500, Greg Lindahl wrote:
> If the compiler would make a symbol available based on the -mieee flag,
> i.e. #define __mieee 0 or 1, then the include files for the math
> library could pick routines which do or don't generate denorms.

Actually this is a good idea. And the compiler already defines
-D_IEEE_FP. And the define can even be replaced by

   extern double sqrt (double) __asm__("_sqrt_no_denorm");

which works out better on the whole.

The question, then, is what to do with the other problematic
return values, Inf and NaN? Should Inf be replaced by the
largest finite value?
