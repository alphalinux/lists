True, but they are still not shipping theri OpenGL hardware accelerated
drivers for Alpha Linux yet. They have said "December 1998".
My last note from them, a week ago, said:
"..it would be best to purchase one of the Elsa
GLoria cards which we have specifically tested:

ELSA GLoria Synergy PERMEDIA 2
ELSA GLoria-L GLINT 500TX
ELSA GLoria-L/MX GLINT 500TX
ELSA GLoria-XL GLINT MX

Of course the Synergy card with the PERMEDIA-2 chip will be the least
expensive, but it will also be the last to be supported by our hardware
accelerated OpenGL implementation. Current plans call for the GLINT MX to
be supported first, then GLINT TX, then PERMEDIA-2 (of the cards listed
above).

The current software-accelerated OpenGL implementation should work with
any of the cards listed above without a problem. "

Other cards with the Permedia2 include the Diamond FireGL Pro 1000 ( now
discontinued) and the AccelstarII, which, unfortunately, has a Permedia 2V
chip, and is NOT supported by the Metrolink software. I know, as I tried..

So, it is in the works, but be prepared to wait a little while longer for
hardware accelerated OpenGL on Linux Alpha...
