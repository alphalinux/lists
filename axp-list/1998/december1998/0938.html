I suspect that Maurice was just overwhelmed by the spirit of the holidays!

I think the board is a decent board, with the 233/266 Mhz CPU and if you
throw some cache on it (available on *many* old 486 motherboards) you
are on your way to a decent little box... The PS is standard AT, the
board is a bit big, but will fit in most "server" cases, and with the
SCSI built-in, decent perfomance is possible for a low-cost system.
The price for RAM may be a suprise to some, but 36 bit SIMMs are not
that unusual...

Had I seen the offer earlier, I would have tried to get one, but to be
honest, it would probably have just sat next to my "spare" Multia (you
know, the one I got for $59 + S/H) waiting for a purpose...

So, although I'm sorry I missed the offer, I suspect my wife is happy
that I missed it!
