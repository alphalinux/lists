Michal Jaegermann wrote:

> In general -
> checking docs is **really** something that should be done before
> you venture on web.

Michal, you're right, and this is sound advice.

As we all know, however--and I repeat to encourage newbies who have made a
reasonable effort to help themselves--this advice [RFTM; RTFDocs] is also often
unusable by newbies because of
     1. The number and variety of docs both online, on CD's and otherwise;
     2. Many docs are poorly organized or poorly written;
     3. Other docs are out of date or contain important errors;
     4. The sheer volume of what is out there;
     and,
     5. Overlapping and often inconsistent treatment of same or similar
subject matters in different docs.

As the supporting resources improve--and they seem to be improving--we'll need
less and less help from user groups and net resources. Until then, newbies, do
all the homework you can, but please do bring your questions! If the AXP list
overloads, we'll find other ways--more lists, a newbie list, etc.--to meet the
demand.
