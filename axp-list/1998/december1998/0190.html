With regards to your message at 07:35 PM 12-04-98 +0100, Luigi Cavallo.
Where you stated:
>Hi,
>
>I was trying to install an old IBM monitor on my LX-533 RH 5.1
>
>Thanks to the suggestions of some peoples, I found out the proper
>data to put in the XF86Config file. However, it doesn't work...
>
>I begun to suspect that the graphics card could be the problem,
>so I run /usr/X11R6/bin/SuperProbe v2.15, and this is the output.
>
>First video: Super-VGA
> Chipset: Oak OTI-087 (Port Probed)
> Memory: 256 Kbytes
> RAMDAC: Generic 8-bit pseudo-color DAC
> (with 6-bit wide lookup tables (or in 6-bit mode))
>
>The problem is that I have a Millenium II .....
>
This is not unbelievable. The Milleniums use an Oak "core" for VGA.
Even Windoze 95/98 "automatically" detect and install Oak VGA drivers for
Millenium I and II. Only after you nstall the drivers does it know about
the rest of the story..

>Clearly, my graphic card section in the XF86Config is setup for
>a Millenium, could it be this the problem with my IBM 6091 ???
No. Monitors don't "change" video cards. They might not display the output,
but that is all.

>Finally, with a Philips 107B or a Sampo 17" monitors everything
>works fine. X-windows included...
Yup, because they are "multisync" monitors, which adjust to different video
modes on the fly. Fixed frequency monitors don't do this.
