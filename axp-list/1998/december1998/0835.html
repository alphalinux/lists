> I was just looking at my riser card (internal SCSI HD model) and
> noticed that it has all the traces for the PCI connector as well as
> the external SCSI connector. Has anyone ever just picked up the
> appropriate parts and connected the external SCSI connector so that
> the internal HD and an external CD could both be connected? I have
> finally found some memory and I have a HD but I am stuck on a way to
> get the software installed. I though I would do a dual boot NT/Linux
> and start with NT since it looks like a simpler install. I have done
> many linux installs, intel and 68000 but never an Alpha.

I also like the FTP installs, but try to install 5.2, as that is the most
recent that I know that installs over FTP on the Multia model. You can
use a local Linux machine with a CD-ROM

As for the connectors, it's up in the air if soldering on a new connector
will add external functionality. There could be other missing components,
so check the riser to see if there are unused pads for other components.
I wish I could answer, but without having both of them to compare, I don't
know.

If you have confidence in your ability to solder 50 pins neatly and
without irreparable error, by all means try it, but will a new header
connector fit?
