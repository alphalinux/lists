On Wed, 23 Dec 1998, Peter Petrakis wrote:

> I've found out a little more about the problem. i watched milo and the
> kernel boot closely and found that it's finding the zip on scsi id 6 and
> activating the drive but when it comes to alocating partitions it looks
> like this at the end of milo and the kernel:
>
> sda: sda1 sda2 <sda5 sda6>
> sdb:
>
>
> There's no entry for sdb. My root partition has always been sda6 but after
> the new install it got 5. Might that be the cause of it? I really don't
> know what to do. Thanks for your help.

It is possible to ext2 format /dev/sdb. In other words, there *is*no*
partition table, and you should be able to directly mount /dev/sdb (note no
trailing partition number). If this is the case, modify /etc/fstab
accordingly. You can actually squeeze a little more space out of 'em this
way since there's no partition table.
