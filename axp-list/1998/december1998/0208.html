Allrighty then, I am _almost_ sure it is an Intraserver, they used lots of
those.
If it is ( Oh, go have a look, on it's upper surface, close to the back of
the machine, it will say 3140U-4 or similar on a white field. Probaly on
the outside face of the plate at the back of the case is a decal that says
SCSI SE with a diamond shaped symbol.
Anyway, to use this get a HD68 to DB25 ( or whatever is on your ZIP) cable.
Should be under $20. If not I will sell you one, delivered, for $25.
Now, on the SCSI card, there are 2 jumpers, and if you look on the board by
them it will say:
HDR3 TERMLOW by one, and : HDR15 TERMHIGH by the other. To disable the low
termination byte you remove the jumper from the HDR3 TERMLOW pair of pins.
If you want to be able to easily enable/diable it you can get a switch
that plugs into these 2 pins so that this can be toggled on/off for when
you don't atttach and use the ZIP drive. Alternatively a second internal
cable assembly can be used to plug into the second internal SCSI connector
on the card, and out to a "dummy plate" on the back with an HD50 narrow
SCSI connector. Then, you disable that same jumper, and use a narrwo SCSI
terminator plugged into that port when you have no device attached to it.
Anyway, go cable huntng again, and if you are stuck, let me know, we stock
all these various types. Enough spam, before I annoy somebody.. ;^}

That is because the driver version is newer, and works better! The ftp site
