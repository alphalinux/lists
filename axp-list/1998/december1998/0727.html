Note he said 2.0.36, NOT 2.1.xxx. Some folks don't like the
bleeding edge...

A simple solution would be for some kind soul to donate
an Alpha box to Alan Cox so he can test it himself.
Without his interest in solving the problem, it won't
get fixed any time soon.

> QC is a corporate buzzword. You're in the wrong universe.
>

Don't know what you do but those of us who write SW
for a living and get paid for it, whether working
for a corporation or not, do care about QC and maintain
test suites for our code. Check out how extensive the
test suites are for EGCS if you think that's a a bad
idea.

Try dealing with irate customers when your code doesn't
do what you told them it will do. If the only one who
uses your code is you, maybe QC isn't much of an
issue. You just irritate yourself.

Non-Intel QC of Linux and lots of other GPL'd code is
not as good as it could be. See solution above.

Course most people don't care about 64bit coding now so
they'll live to regret their decision to ignore Alphas
in the next year or so. It's a pain in the *** leading the
way sometimes.
