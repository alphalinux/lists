With regards to your message at 04:26 PM 11-29-98 -0800, Michael Schmitz.
Where you stated:
>
>I decided to switch my LX164 to the SRM console (in order to try
>NetBSD, but that's another story). So I took the latest SRM
>version V5.3-3 and flashed in over the AlphaBIOS. Since then
>using the aboot-0.5-7 sources from the RedHat source CD and the
>axplinux-2.0.35-2 kernel, I've gotten as far as starting to load
>the packages on to the hard disk. After a few packages, the system
>invariably stops with a "machine check while executing in PALcode."
>
>I've replaced the hard disk, I've tried netinstall from the RedHat
>CDROM mounted from another machine, but I still get a machine check
>in random places during disk I/O, maybe after 20 or 30 packages are
>installed.
>
>I think that SRM console software (and its UX Palcode) may be broken, or
>else the system board is flaky. I ran some of the SRM console's
>diagnostic commands.
>
>"memexer" with 1 thread runs overnight with no problems
>"memexer" with more that 1 thread immediately machine checks
>"test" runs OK
>"sys_exer" immediately machine checks
I think you just answered your own question here. Under SRM the boards are
MUCH more sensitive to bad memory than with AlphaBIOS. My opinion is that
you need better RAM.

>Question 1: Is this normal behavior for an LX164? Maybe the
>SRM isn't fully implemented or tested on LX164 and these problems
>can be ignored. Or maybe I need to replace hardwareg. Anyone know?
>
>Question 2: Is there a known working SRM LX164 boot disk image out
>there anywhere? There doesn't appear to be one on gatekeeper.
>
