This is probably completely unrelated, but the Adaptec 2940UW version for
the Macintosh has severe problems with the new G3 processors. This has
been covered extensively in the Mac press recently.

The cause is rather interesting; the G3 is very aggressive in doing
speculative execution (tracing down both paths of a possible branch before
it's even COMMITTED to reaching the branch). In the course of doing this
it may read I/O locations, and in strobing such memory addresses, confuse
the peripheral card. Apparently Adaptec cards HAVE this problem.

Now, the official Mac G3's have code in their ROM (except it isn't ROM
anymore ;-) to set a "guard bit" to prevent this. It seems to me that also
tosses away some of the functionality of the chipset, but hey, better than
having your hard-disk trashed. The machines which have been UPGRADED to
G3's, but still have the 604 ROMs, are the problem. (I have one.)

So perhaps part of the problem the Adaptec cards have with other
processors--I've read complaints about both Intel and Alpha platforms--has
to do with speculative execution setting off one of the card's firmware
bugs.

We haven't had trouble with our Alpha. But that's because we use another
UW controller ;-) Now if I could just figure out how to get the DAMNED
FLOATING POINT to compile and not dump core...
