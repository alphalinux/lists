3 AA Alkaline cells in a rewired 4-cell holder work fine for me.
My 3 Multias arrived with measurably bad (2V) batteries.
4.5V sounds as though CPUmicromart finally did start putting in new ones.
They said they were going to do that. A 4-cell holder will fit in the
case, if you patiently tuck it in.

To eliminate the battery as a problem, just disconnect it. The system
will work fine that way, just not holding the startup settings defined in
the ARC console.

My Multia's didn't work right off, even after disconnecting the battery.
Maybe they need to be discharged overnight. I don't remember if I tried
shorting the battery terminals.

It is pretty simple, BTW, to build a kernel with sound support. The
builtin sound system works fine. You need to set the mixer levels at
startup for some reason. There's noise that comes from one of (probably
unterminated) line inputs.
