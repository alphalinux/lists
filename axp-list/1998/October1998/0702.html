But using -miee may lead to slower code, at least that's what I
understood from several discussions about it on this list. So you
probably do not want to do it this way. As I said, 1.0e-310 is a
denormal, it is not zero, and further references to it lead to FPEs.
It's these things that give me headaches - C. Whaley found one
uninitialized array in the superscalar gemm-based BLAS that happened
to hold several of these denormals => FPEs. Using -mieee (instead of
proper init) solves the problem, too, but the cost is too high.

Why do you want to use this number anyway?
