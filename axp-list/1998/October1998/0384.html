With regards to your message at 04:46 PM 11-19-98 -0500, Greg Lindahl.
Where you stated:
>> Yes, but partitions dedicated for swap usually run faster. With an extended
>> partition and many logicals you pay no real penalty for this, other than a
>> rather large and confusing fdisk table..
>
>Where's the proof that dedicated partitions for swap are _significantly_
>faster? Oft stated mantras aren't necessarily true.
>
>> Besides just how much swap can you really use?? If you are using a MB of
>> swap, something is "fishy"..
>
>I _use_ a gigabit of swap, and nothing is fishy. What's the point of
>having machines that can take gigabytes of ram if we can't easily have
>gigabytes of swap?
>
I have tried it.
On Red Hat 5.0 machiine, with 64MB of RAM and 5 32MB swap partitions. Ran a
bunch of apps and ate up memory.
Turned on the swap partitions, one at a time, and tried again.
Then tried it with swap files. The files were giving noticably more
sluggish response.
Never did it as a formal benchmark, but you could see the difference..

I think you are right though, _somebody_ should devise and run some formal
benchmarks, just to quantify the difference. Any volunteers?
