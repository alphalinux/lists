Well, I see Microway has put up an ad for a dual 500-MHz processor
21264-based system. Asking price: $20K. This only includes 512MB of
memory, and only 9GB of hard-disk space (at 7200 RPM, no less). I would
have expected more memory and disk space. See

   <http://www.microway.com/21264dp.html>

I love Linux, but for that kind of money and performance, I think I'd want
to go with DU to take advantage of it.

It has a 618-watt power supply, but my impression was the motherboard ALONE
was going to be drawing that much--I was planning on ironing my pants with
it ;-) Would putting a couple of 18GB Cheetahs in, and 2GB RAM, be
feasible?

They claim a 10x performance over quad-Pentium systems, but of course I
take that with a grain of salt. Also, the cache is only 2MB per processor.
Since Microway had been going up to 8MB on the 21164, I'm wondering what
the limitation is.

I'd like to buy one of these, not as a personal workstation to read my
e-mail, TeX stuff, etc., but for number-crunching only.

Any other vendors with plans to offer something in this line? What, how
much, when?
