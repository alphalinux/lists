> glibc's locale stuff uses MMAP_INHERIT flag, and
> the kernels *warn* about it. That happens *only*
> when you have LC_CTYPE set to something.

MMAP_INHERIT is one of the three "offending" flags. The other
two are MAP_HASSEMAPHORE and MAP_UNALIGNED. The db problem is
caused by MAP_HASSEMAPHORE, and I believe it happens on any
system with vanilla settings.

> Comment the printk() away, and be happy.
> (It is already commented away in the lattest 2.1.* kernels.)

I can do that, and in fact it is essentially what I did, even
if acting on the application rather than on the kernel.
But in principle one can envision situations where an application
may fail because it relies on services (whatever they are, and
I don't know what they are!) declared in the headers but actually
unavailable.

> > If all this is true, include/asm-alpha/mman.h should be

> > three definitions.
>
