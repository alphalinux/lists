On 19 Nov 1998 phumpherys@utah-inter.net wrote:

> I got my Multia running with redhat 5.2, and starting compling
> various utilities, like ssh. It took *forever*. I have an old
> P100 that's faster, by quite a lot.
> Any thoughts? How does a i586/100 beat a axp/166?

        To add one point that has yet to be mentioned.... Compiling on
Alpha takes longer than Intel because the instruction set is larger (due
to RISC / CISC) and therefore it takes longer to generate and assembly all
the needed machine code. Something like the 2:1 ratio is about right. I
would not compare archs (i.e. alpha vs. intel) based upon complier speed,
that will just get you into trouble. Compare based on execution of
programs, like databases.
        As an information benchmark, my UDB (166MHz/64MB) was able to
perform a complex database query (mSQL) faster than a P100 (32MB) and even
a P133 (16MB) was able to perform the same query on the same data. UDBs
are defintely not speed demons (my XL-366 Alpha makes the UDB look pokey),
but they are good solid machine that provide P90-P100 performance at a
nice price. They should be considered starter alpha machines, not a good
representation of current Alphas. Remember, UDB came out when 486s were
the rage, so for thier time, they are speed demons!
        Also, I echo that remarks that these machine require at least 64MB
of RAM for decent performance. About everyone who has ever reported
slow/poor UDB performance (how many times has this thread been through
this list over the past two years? 10? 100 times? :), they have had 16 or
32MB of RAM. So, if you don't have 64MB and want your UDB to perform at
its peak, you now know what to send your money on. :)
        Hope this helps!
