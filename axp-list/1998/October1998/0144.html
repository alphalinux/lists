I just got one of those $99 DEC Multias. What a nice looking
and well built little box. I have quite a bit of experience
with Sun and with Linux/i386 but none at all with the Alpha.
Now to get it working....

I ran into a few problems.

1) Memory.
  
  I bought four 8MB 72pin 36-bit SIMMs. These have 24 identical
  chips on each SIMM (12 on a side) and have a "-6" on the part
  number which I assume means 60ns. I load this in, attach a
  CRT and keyboard, press the power button and, nothing happens.
  Zero. The CRT does not even see a live video signal and goes into
  power save mode. I tried using half the memory at a time in
  various combinations. No luck.

  Next I round up some 30 pin SIMMS and a pair of 30 -> 72 pin
  converters. I put this in and now I see something on the
  monitor. It is all green with a white block cursor on the lower
  left. But that's it. The machine is totally unresponsive.
  Waiting doe not seem to help after 30 minutes it is the same.

  I would have expected at least some beeps or some kind of error
  message to be printed to the screen.

  Is something other then generic 36-bit RAM required?

2) The machine came with a 4.5V clock battery. Does anyone know
  how to check it. It measures 4.5V under no load condition but
  I know this is a poor way o test. Or should I assume the little
  cube shaped thing is dead and replace it. I assume a could use
  three AA batteries in series. Any problem with this idea?
