Did anybody read what Chris wrote?

> I bought four 8MB 72pin 36-bit SIMMs.
                            ^^^^^^
This should be just fine--as I read it, 36-bit SIMMs equals parity SIMMs.
I'm surprised it didn't work. Let's see, Chris, you also mention 24
identical chips--12 on each side... Are you sure? My guess is that of
the 24 chips, you have

   - 8 1x4Mbit (or 4x1Mbit) chips on side A (4MB, no parity)
   - 8 1x4Mbit (or 4x1Mbit) chips on side B (4MB, no parity)
   - 4 1x1Mbit chips on side A (one-half of the 8Mbit (1MByte) for parity)
   - 4 1x1Mbit chips on side B (the other half of the 8Mbit (1MByte) for parity)

So, if you look closely, you should see that four of the chips on each side
actually have a different part number. This should still be just fine,
as it works out to 8MB with parity.

Moreover, some people said you needed ECC memory instead of parity. That's
bogus. As far as I know, there is no such thing as ECC memory in 72pin
format--use parity (36-bit memory as it's advertized), and let the Alpha
use it as a 72 bit data path, generating 8 bits of ECC for each 64 bits
of data.

> Next I round up some 30 pin SIMMS and a pair of 30 -> 72 pin
> converters. I put this in and now I see something on the
> monitor. It is all green with a white block cursor on the lower
> left. But that's it. The machine is totally unresponsive.
> Waiting doe not seem to help after 30 minutes it is the same.

The fact that you get somewhere with the 30 pin SIMMS suggests that maybe
the 72pin memory that you said you had isn't working. (??) I don't know
what to make of this unless somehow, the 8MB SIMMs you have aren't really
parity (despite my "chip counting" above), or are bad or something.

The white block cursor in the lower left is an indication that your
battery is dead. At least that's what it did for me. When I unplugged
the battery cube and tested it, it read 1.05V. I replaced it and the
booting commenced.
 
> Is something other then generic 36-bit RAM required?

That's what I am using. I have tried generic 36-bit 8MB, 16MB, and 32MB
SIMMs--all have worked perfectly. If you want, copy down the exact
numbers and letters on all the chips on each side of each SIMM, (email
me privately), and I'll look up exactly what you've got.

> 2) The machine came with a 4.5V clock battery. Does anyone know
> how to check it. It measures 4.5V under no load condition but
> I know this is a poor way o test. Or should I assume the little
> cube shaped thing is dead and replace it. I assume a could use
> three AA batteries in series. Any problem with this idea?

As someone said, if it reads 4.5V under no load, it should be okay. My
dead one only registered 1.05V. But if you get the solid block cursor
in the lower left, that sounds like a dead one too. I believe you can
try to boot without the battery installed--you'll just have to reset
the clock every time you power off. You could try this just to see
if you get further. If you do, replace the battery anyway.
