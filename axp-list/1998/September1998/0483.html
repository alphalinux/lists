Hi all,

I summarize my current state of affairs with the Samsung
PC164UX a.k.a. Ruffian.

Problem
-------
As previously reported, I have been plagued by instabilities caused
by paging-related errors, clearly observable under memory-intensive load
but they also occur with a light load (as Linux uses all the memory
anyway).
The errors, as observed under 2.0.35, fall into these categories:
(a) kswapd going 'Oops 0'
(b) Erratic 'Unable to handle kernel paging request' from an application
(c) 'Aiee, killing interrupt handler' from swapper
observed on three distinct machines, one with Fujutsu MAB 4.5GB and
other two with Seagate Cheetah 9GB, each tested with 256 or 512MB RAM.
Which one is actually observed depends on the configuration details
and on the compiler used (I used mostly gcc 2.7.2.3 and egcs 1.1).

Similar or related symptoms have been also observed by other users of
this machine on this list (Adrian Gartland, Tilo Wettig, Ulrich
Stille on 2.0.x but not 2.1.x, Jay Estabrook?), but other users did not
observe them (Michal Jaegermann).

A test on one of my machines using 2.1.122 produced either error (a),
or a new kind of error (coming from page_io.c which changed in 2.1.x):
(d) swap_cache: replacing page-cached entry on page fffffc0007692000
Kernel panic: Tried to swap a non-swapper page

Empirical fixes
---------------
I have been able to eliminate (at least under my test conditions)
the problem on 2.0.35 by doing ANY one of the following actions:
(1) reduce the amount of memory used by Linux (from MILO)
down to 224MB (even for the 512MB it was necessary to go
down to that number);
(2) redefine the partitions so that both swap and Linux (not just
the kernel!) sit within the first GB of the disk;
(3) increase the parameter SHMMAX in include/asm/shmparam.h from
0x3fa000 to 0x1000000.
Everything was done on an empirical basis, following suggestions
sent me by various people and a lot of fiddling, and for none of
them I have a real understanding of what is actually happening.

(2) was done following a suggestion of Deskstation support;
They have merely given me the setup of their Linux system and have no
understanding of the problem. They say that the hardware has been
extensively tested under NT, so that this must be a s/w problem.
They explicitly stated that they have difficulties in giving Linux
support because "Linux changes so fast".

(3) is a suggestion from Tilo Wettig, since that fixed his cdrom burner.
I do not know the implications of this change.

However, none of these actions seem to be effective in removing the
problem on 2.1.122. This seems to suggest that they are
masquerading the problem, making it less likely to occur, rather than
removing it. Also, it suggests debugging the problem may be faster
on 2.1.x, since it is more visible. However, these findings are in
sharp contrast with Ulrich Stille's findings, that 2.0.x crashes
but 2.1.x does not. This may be due to the compiler, as he
uses gcc 2.8.1 that I do not have installed at present.

We will probably put the machines into production using 2.0.35
with fix number (3) and crossing fingers, but the problem is really
still open.

Thanks to everybody who corresponded with me on this issue.
