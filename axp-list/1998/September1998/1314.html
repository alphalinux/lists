> I have read the overview of alpha cpus, systems, etc several times
> (perhaps not closely enough). However, no mention is made of the DEC 3000
> Model 300/300L AXP. Where do these fit into the mix?

They don't.

> Also, what generic linux kernel, if any, would be appropriate
> for such a system?

None.

The DEC 3000 series was the first Alpha-based workstation ever
produced. Its system bus was TurboChannel; a bus that DEC developed
and had hoped to make an industry standard, but it got lost in the
noise of the EISA/VLB/PCI battles. It doesn't run Linux because
Linux has no support for TurobChannel. I believe NetBSD runs on
it, though...
