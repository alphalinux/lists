Paul Tomblin wrote:
>
> Can anybody explain why cua0 would work when ttyS0 doesn't?

Probably you managed to change 'clocal' settings on your tty line.
Check with 'stty -a < /dev/ttyS0'.

> I thought they
> were basically the exact same kernel driver, but the "cua0" had some extra
> locking logic for the benefit of uucp?

There are some small differences how they can be opened. It sounds like
you just found one. :-) Indeed, beyond that 'cua?' devices include some
form a very simple-minded locking which does not really work when you go
beyond a very basic usage. For that reason it is considered now a mistake
that they were provided in the first place and in current development
kernels and future 2.2.xx you will get a nastygram in log files every time
when you will try to use one. In the future they will vanish completely.
