On Fri, 25 Sep 1998, Wes Bauske wrote:
> While we're adding cents....
>
> I've been subscribed to the Debian Alpha mailing list for the past
> 6 months. From observing what they complain about I'd say the following:

Hi Wes. I've been following debian-alpha too...

> 1. They hate RH. Several members of the list, to put it mildly,
> are jerks. I'd say they exhibit the mentality of a 10 year old.
> They can't reply rationally when someone complains about a
> problem in the distribution.

Most lists have a few folks like that. There's also some guys on
debian-alpha who are very capable and trying hard to put together a
quality distribution.

> 2. They have as many, if not more problems with their distribution.
> I see nothing to indicate it is done better than RH. They hide
> behind the word "beta" to rationalize the amount of updating
> done to their distribution and fault RH for having to update
> things "too much".

Sure, they have some known problems. RH has too, as they have admitted
on this list. At least RH has committed to improved quality. Debian
meanwhile has simply refused to release their Alpha port until they think
it is ready. We'll have to wait to see the outcome of both...

I don't see anything wrong with Debian remaining at beta status. It's
the choice of the maintainer to decide when it is ready for release.
Both Debian and RH are affected to some degree by the stability of egcs
and glibc, neither of which are maintained by RH or Debian.

It's been a bumpy road for Alpha users, but as glibc and egcs mature, and
once Linux 2.2 is released, I think both distributions will stabilize.
And once we have stable distributions, commercial software will follow
(like Metro-X and Applixware for instance).

> 3. I don't consider RH that great of a distribution either so
> my view on both is not all roses.

I don't blame RH, although they tend towards quick releases.

> 4. I prefer the way discussions go on the RH mailing list
> (most of the time) compared to the Debian list. I'd suggest
> you subscribe to both lists and see which one you prefer.
> The people on the list are the ones you'll be dealing list
> for info/help.

axp-list has a lot more general discussion. The debian-alpha list is
still mostly Debian-specific issues.

Personally I defected to Debian two weeks ago (although I still have RH
4.2 on disk and occaisionally need it when I can't recompile a program).
I like their distribution because the folks maintaining it use it every
day (some have been using it over 12 months now) and are self-motivated to
fix bugs and refine the distribution.

Redhat on the other hand sells a distribution based on Linux/Intel. They
have expanded into the Sparc and Alpha markets, but unlike the
maintainers at debian-alpha it doesn't seem to be their primary focus
(someone please correct me if I am wrong).

I didn't find Debian to be riddled with bugs. My experience may have
been different than yours. Beyond the usual installation glitches (those
are unfortunately the enduring problems, since the core maintainers
aren't reinstalling every day) my system has been running flawlessly.
Debian is the first I've seen that didn't install everything on my 164SX
in the year 2018, because the hwclock program correctly detected my
real-time clock. So far the networking and X11 utilities are working,
and I haven't yet experienced the syslogd problem (but maybe I am just
lucky). I get the feeling that all of the main packages have been tested
by someone. I didn't get that with RH 5.1.
