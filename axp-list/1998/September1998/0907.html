> I do not know about "normal operations". When I first tried that I
> collected a few thousands entries (something between three and four
> thousands - not counting "repeated xxx times") in my log files before
> I even finished to boot. This was IRQ 14, if I remember correctly, and
> my system was working - in a fashion, as I managed an orderly shutdown.

What device(s) want or use IRQ 14?

EB64+ had stock 16 PIC IRQs for ISA interrupts, and 16 (used only 11)
additional IRQs for the PCI devices and cascade.

As with all the Alphas with additional PCI interrupts, their IRQs are numbered
from 16 up; and ISA 14 is usually IDE 0 and 1.

Did you have any IDE disks/CDROMs in that EB64+? If so, did they work?

> Jay, if you remember, I even wrote you about this at that time.

Hmmm; yes, I seem to remember something, but I think you didn't complain
loud or long enough... ;-}

> Checking older sources I found that this particular
> printk was before turned off so I did the same and now everything seems
> to be happy through 2.0.34, 2.0.35 and quite a few different prereleases
> of 2.0.36.

I'd be interested to know if it is still happening, though, and what kind of
count you get of "ignored" IRQ 14 interrupts. Something is not right, as the
very first one of those should cause the interrupt to be masked off and, unless
masking is broken on the EB64+, that should prevent *any* more from occurring,
until/if a driver is assigned and the interrupt is unmasked by that driver.

> The system which displays this "interesting" behaviour is EB64+ using ARC
> console 4.49-4.

Probably the latest (last) for that board, right?
