Hi everybody,

We've been having some problems with the stock Red Hat 2.0.35 kernel, so
we went for 2.1.125. The compiled vmlinux.gz is over 800K, and aboot
refuses to boot it, complaining about block sizes and unknown compression
methods.

I've searched the archives, dejanews and altavista to no avail.

However, 'file' shows that it's compressed just like 2.0.*. I think it has
something to do with the large file size.

Some years ago I tried booting with milo, but it wasn't much of a success,
so I'm hessitant to try that.

My questions: Has anybody managed to boot 2.1.x with aboot on an
AlphaStation? Is aboot known to have problems with large kernels? Has
anybody managed to boot using milo on an AlphaStation 4/233?

Thanks in advance for your help.
