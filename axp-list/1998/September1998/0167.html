strdream@iserv.net said:
> It does bug me that UNIX used to be free and "open", now it's a
> vanguard of don't-sit-there-until-you-pay-us-dearly companies.

Hmmmm, when was Unix "free and open"?

Was it in the early days, when AT&T sold you a source code license for
$160,000. on a single CPU? And remember, that was when $160K was worth
something (1977 or so).

Was it free and open when AT&T started the "contamination" issues? When they
talked about "trade secrets", but then would not tell you what the trade
secrets were, so you could avoid them? When AT&T passed on the royalty
stream to USL, Novell and (finally) SCO?

Only in the academic community and then only at research institutions, where
they could get the blanket $350 license might someone consider Unix "free",
and it was never "open", it is just that people ignored the licensing
considerations.

But for Hartford State Technical College in Hartford, Ct. the charge would
have been $160K per machine before companies like Digital and Sun brought out
binary-only licenses.

Nope, NetBSD(R), FreeBSD(R), OpenBSD(R) and Linux(R) are the only free and
open operating systems, and none of them are "Unix", which requires a very
expensive branding process controlled by The Open Group.

md (who has a long memory)
