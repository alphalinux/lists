> > Other Notes.......................: This is a development machine; I
> build new kernels regularly, and mozilla, and much other stuff. It get
> rebooted to new kernels often. I have an AWE 64 Gold, ISA. Real-Time
> Clock support is NOT enabled in the kernel (I found that performance fell
> to _shit_ if I turned it on... why, I dunno).
> Most noticeable performance improvement: turning OFF support for the
> Real-Time Clock. With RTC off, I've had the load ave. over 2 and still
> had good responsiveness.
This is by now a known problem.
I found that hardware interrupts get corrupted when RTC is enabled. A fix,
other from disabling RTC, is not yet known.
As a test: Try to play xquake with RTC enabled. It will show strange hangs
at certain points of the game. During those hangs the machine is completely
dead. Sometimes xquake hangs completely! So be careful and sync before ;)
Jay Estabrook and Richard Henderson know about this problem but have no
idea what's going on.

Besides: I am happy that other users also see this and that it is not a
fault of my motherboard ;)

Linux Alpha archive at
http://www.unix-ag.org/Linux-Alpha/

Any additions are welcome!
