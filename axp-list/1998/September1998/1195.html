We had the same problem about 6 months ago on a UDB. The problem
is in the use of the Tulip driver. The onboard ethernet
controller in the UDB responds to the Tulip driver, but the tulip
driver doesn't recognize the connection on the BNC connector -
even if you force it to look there. Oddly enough, we were able to
use the Tulip driver with the Twisted Pair connector just
fine....

We ended up using the Twisted pair connector until I could
download a new kernel package (I don't like the pre-packaged
RedHat ones) and compiling a new kernel. After many hours of
guesswork, and even some attempted hacking of the tulip driver, I
ended up trying the "DE425, DE434, DE435, DE450, DE500 support
(CONFIG_DE4X5)" network driver, and leaving Tulip support out of
the kernel. This worked like a champ with both connectors, and
still seems to work fine today.

I'm not exactly sure which is the 'proper' driver to use, but
like I said, the "DE425, DE434, DE435, DE450, DE500
(CONFIG_DE4X5)" seemed to work just fine with both media types.
Maybe someone on this list can clarify why this is so, and why
the Tulip driver didn't work correctly....
