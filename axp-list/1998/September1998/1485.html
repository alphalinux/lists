>>> "Steve G" said:
>
> I have a DEC Alpha workstation 233 and an avanti.img boot disk. After
> booting off of that disk, and then telling aboot to boot vmlinux.gz, it will
> start booting, and then hang at a SCSI line. Here's a screenshot.
>
> SCSI: aborting command due to timeout: pid 0, scsi0, channel0, id0, lun0,

If you're booting from SRM console, you should have used the avanti-s.img
file to create the floppy with the kernel.

The "avanti.img" is for booting from ARC/MILO. The kernels are different in
the way they deliver interrupts, which is what that error message indicates.

> I have
> an adaptec AHA2940 in the PCI riser card and also a 3Com NIC in the other
> PCI slot. It appears to me that it finds both scsi hosts.
>
> Do I not have
> support for something in this vmlinux.gz file?

AFAIK, all the canned kernels from RH are configured for NCR, QLogic, Adaptec,
and BusLogic SCSI controllers, so this isn't the problem.

However, if you truly are booting from SRM console, be aware that *it* will
not know what to do with the Adaptec, and you won't be able to boot a kernel
from a disk attached to it, only from the builtin NCR.

Good luck.
