Sorry to dredge up this tired old topic again, but it's really
frustrating not being able to get my alpha online.

I tried to configure ppp through the guis in Afterstep and KDE to
no avail. (Condescending groans from the elders...)
I know, I know, be a man, do it with your scripts and the good old
cli.

So i've tried. Read the ppp docs, the ppp howto, the faq, the serial
howto, the net-2 howto, visited several modem-config sites, studied
my modem manual. Even the lively cua* vs ttyS* discussion from
earlier today was enthralling.

The kernel: 2.0.34 (with serial ports and ppp support included)
The modem: US Robotics 56K Fax external.
The port: ttyS0 UART:16550A
The daemon: v2.3.3
The problem: The exact same configuration works on my Intel/linux
machine. (But it's a slow P75 with a small hard drive, and now the
wife wants it for a Winbox.) Syslog reports the connect script up
to connection and then "serial line looped back."
I've tried the recs for this precise problem as described in the howto.
1.Carefully reviewed my scripts for typos/mistakes. None found.
2.Tried adding lcp-max-configure 30 (and even 75) to options.
3.Added another expect-send pair to chatscript \~ ""

Minicom can dial and connect, I give login and password, see the
daemon start on the ISP's server, execute pppd locally by hand, no go.

My command-line attempts produce the same: successful exchange of
login and password, connection then
:serial line looped back
:connection terminated
:exit

My loopback device is working.
I know these machines are meant to be networked and connected all
the time, but there has to be a possibility of using it as a stand-
alone workstation with dial-up ppp on demand. Right?

I'm about to recompile the kernel for lack of any other ideas.
Can anyone point me in some direction I haven't thought of?

Thanks in advance.
