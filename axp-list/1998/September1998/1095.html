With regards to your message at 12:24 PM 10-01-98 -0300, Kit Cosper. Where
you stated:
>>>
>>> Actually Linus has stated repeatedly that he wants the name
>>>to be used freely by anyone who wants to use it properly. All he
>>>has ever asked is that you give the attribute;
>>>
>>> Linux is a trademark of Linus Torvalds.
>>True, Kit, but down the road, if anyone EVER asks "Did you get permission,
>>then it can be shown that the proper route WAS followed.
>>Besides, as we had our noses rubbed in recently, it is VITAL that we show
>>some due diligence in enforcing the usage of the trademark.
>>You CAN NOT just tell the world, "Go ahead and use it and mention my name"
>>or you will LOSE the trademark if challenged in a court.
>
>
> If the attribution is there then there is no need for further due
>dilligence. We don't get permission from Intel or Digital/Compaq when
>we use their trademarks in advertising, but we *do* give the proper
>attribution. Just as Red Hat mentions Motif and other things in their
>manual and gives the attribution on the publication page. In the year or
>so that I worked on the manual (2 revisions, 3.0.3 and 4.0) we never
>got permission from anyone to use any trademarks, but we *did* give
>the proper notification that they were trademarks. The potential
>problem arises when there are cases that the attribution hasn't been
>made, the situation is known to the trademark holder, and corrective action
>is not taken. After a few of those you may be at risk of loosing the
>trademark. You can only address those circumstances you are aware of.
>
>--Kit
>
So then, by this logic we should be able to register a site called
www.intel.com, and by merely mentioning on the webpage that Intel is a
registered trademark of Intel Corporation we are OK?
I think Intel has lawyers that might debate that point with you.
IMHO simply making a request for use of the names, and keeping those
replies on file would be a prudent gesture.
