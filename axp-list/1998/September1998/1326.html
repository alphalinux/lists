> I have read the overview of alpha cpus, systems, etc several times
> (perhaps not closely enough). However, no mention is made of the DEC 3000
> Model 300/300L AXP. Where do these fit into the mix? Also, what
> generic linux kernel, if any, would be appropriate for such a system?

As Jim Paradis already pointed out, the TurboChannel Alphas are different :).
However, the DECstation Linux porting team is working on TurboChannel
support for Linux and I have a nice 3000/300X Alpha that I'm trying to
get aboot to work on (Thomas, if you're reading this, I can't reach the
people from the Physics dept. here who have the 3000/300L Alphas - don't
know what happened to them, perhaps they were bitten by VMS ;-)).

Low-level programming information for the complete 3000/xxx series is
available from DEC, you can find a copy on
http://decstation.unix-ag.org/docs/dec_docs/DS5000_200_spec.ps.gz
or on any NetBSD mirror, it's called
"DEC 3000 300/400/500/600/700/800/900 AXP Models System Programmer's Manual".
