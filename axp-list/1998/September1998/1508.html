>>> Umut Ali Yener said:
>
> One of the alpha servers of my company has just been replaced, and I had the
> chance for a trial to replace its NT with Linux. However as an AlphaServer
> 3300 it is not listed in the hardware compatibility list. And what's more
> there is a comment stating that 3000 series workstations are not compatible,
> so I as a last chance I decided to ask to this list, if I can install linux
> on this computer, which milo and boot images to use and if the answer is
> negative, do I have any chance to install any other operating system other
> than that NT.

The AlphaServer 3300 platform is the NT equivalent of the AlphaServer 800
for Digital UNIX and VMS, I believe. Unfortunately, there is no MILO for it;
you must boot LINUX from SRM console.

The 3000 series mentioned above are the old, TurboChannel-based machines,
which first appeared 5 to 6 years ago. LINUX does not currently run on them,
though at least one of the *BSD flavors does.
