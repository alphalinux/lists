What a load. On 2 occasions I have spoken to Mike Kinney at Deskstation.
The first was an enquiry that asked for him to finish/fix ldmilo.
He then complained he did all he could, but that as linload.exe was not
published as source, he could only guess at and approximate its
functionality. Afte some phone calls and email Jim Paradis decided to solve
this problem by getting permission and clarifying it was OK to publish
these sources, which he has done a few months ago (late May?).
I waited a while to see if anything would happen, and then in August I
called him and asked if he now had plans to look at this. HEtold me that it
was a low priority,a nd maybe "in a month". The month has now passed, so I
think I will ask him again this week.

The second was to ask if they could modify ARCSBIOS to support booting over
a network, in BOOTP fashion. He left me a message explaining that this
wouls be a lot of work as he "would have to support a lot of diffeent
network interfaces." I called him and asked why he thought this. As the
UX/AKA Ruffian has a built-in Tulip 21140 chip I saw that this would be
sufficint for almost everyone. I suggested that they might see some
interest on Beowulf style setups if they followed this simple path. I even
asked if they could quote a cost for this work, if necessary. He called me
back a few days late and said that they had "no available resources to do
this".
