First off, I'd like to wish a hearty Thank-You to all the members of this
list, and especially the ones listed in the addresses of this message. I'm
sorry if I forgot anyone in specific.

This has been the most valuable resource I could ever find. Most lists are
just a lot of people posting questions and not a whole lot of answers. I
have to say this list is definitely an exception to the rule.

**For anyone out there with an Avanti Alpha and kernel questions, feel free
to direct them to me.** I feel like a regular scholarly source on the
subject after all that I've done. Here's a breakdown if anyone's
interested.

-Installed RH 5.1 from scratch on an Avanti Alpha workstation 233.
-Need to update kernel to allow for Firewalling
-Tried 10 different kernels. 2.0.xx and 2.1.xxx, would not compile
-Commented out SRM=1 in the top-level Makefile
-Still would not compile kernels. Weird compile errors.
-Decided to upgrade egcs (gcc in my case)
-Got egcs-1_0_3a.tar.gz and would not compile
-Found out that I had to make config, and then make dep to get egcs to
compile
-Did that, then made egcs. BTW, egcs -version still shows old version,
gcc -v shows egcs-1.0.3 release. (whew)
-Rebooted so the Alpha gods would not be upset
-Started compile of linux-2.1.124.tar.gz from scratch
-Went to the machine next day and it was finished compiling with no errors
-Copied vmlinux.gz to boot floppy and booted from MILO
-IT WORKED!!!

I couldn't have done this without everyone's help. I'm still going to have
to get the added NIC to work and configure a firewall, but at least I have
the kernel support to do it now. (so what if it took me 1 week to get it
there...)

Thank you all once again. I'm definitely going to hang around on this list,
so maybe I can offer a little insight to someone in a similar situation that
I was in.

Maybe, just maybe this alpha isn't as bad as I thought it was...

Thanks everybody,

Steve
