Pradeep Sagam wrote:
>
> I'm trying to build a custom kernel ( RH 5.1 - 2.0.34 ).
> Used menuconfig, with no modules.

Using menuconfig is even less recommended than 'make xconfig' and both
are suitable only when you **really** know what to expect and you are
willing to repair your configuration files by hand if required. You may
be for nasty surprises. Fixing this stuff is harder than it looks like
at the first glance and it was postponed to future 2.3.x series.
This is not Alpha specific problem and 'make menuconfig' may even
work - if you are lucky. Your chances with 'make xconfig' are quite
a bit better.

If you already have some .config file and you want to modify it then
use 'make oldconfig'. After **removing** from .config options you
want to change run 'make oldconfig' and you will be asked only proper
questions. :-) Quite easy, actually, and it works.

> I am able to build vmlinux ( I guess
> there is no zImage - the Makefile has none )

Makefiles on all platforms, AFAIK, have an entry for 'make boot'.
'zImage' is Intel specific. Pay attention to messages on your
screen after you 'make boot'.
