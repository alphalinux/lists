I'm pretty new to the process of tracking & upgrading linux versions. To save me
from the perils of my own speculation, could some kind soul fill me in on the
Big Picture relationship between:

The 2.0.35 kernel sources from the Linux Kernel Archive
The 2.0.35-1 kernel sources in the RedHat 5.1 update archive
The 2.0.35-0.1 kernel sources on Gatekeeper
The 2.0.34-0.1 patches on Gatekeeper
The 2.0.35-0.1 patches on Gatekeeper

Less ambiguously:
//www.kernel.org/pub/linux/kernel/v2.0/linux-2.0.35.tar.gz
//ftp.redhat.com/.../updates/5.1/alpha/kernel-source-2.0.35-1.alpha.rpm
//gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-sources-2.0.35-0.1.gz
//gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-patches-2.0.34-0.1.gz
//gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-patches-2.0.35-0.1.gz

It appears that the 2.0.35-1 sources from Redhat have the 2.0.34-0.1 patches
applied, but the 2.0.35-0.1 patches do not apply cleanly.

Are the Redhat sources and the Gatekeeper sources parallel lines of development?

If I use the 2.0.35-0.1 sources from Gatekeeper, do I lose Redhat's changes to
the "official" 2.0.35?
