On Sun, 18 Oct 1998, geoff. shukin wrote:

> Hi!
>
> I was wanting to fool around with some RAID in an effort to get better
> performance from some older SCSI drives. In the RAID Howto, it talks
> about recompiling the kernel for RAID support. I can not see this is a
> Linux 2.0.34 kernel.

It's there--look for "Multiple devices" when you do a make config.
You'll probably want to use 2.0.35 instead of 34 because the raid
drivers are newer and include support for raid-4 and raid-5.

> Is RAID supported under Alpha Linux?

Yes, on my as200 /usr and /home are both striped across two disks.

> Do I have to upgrade to a 2.1.xxx kernel to get this support?

No, although I bet 2.1.x uses more recent md drivers.
