On Wed, 16 Sep 1998, Scott Lewis wrote:

> Anybody know if the soundcard that comes in the AS200's from onsale is
> supported? I can't seem to find out any information on it, much less able
> to compile a kernel for it.

Hi Scott,

Yes, it is supported. There is a complete section on the PCXBJ
adapter in your AS200 manual. The card is "Windows Sound System"
compatible. The default hardware settings are:

IO: 530
IRQ: 9
DMA: 0,1

The card works pretty well under 2.0.x, though I believe the feature set
under 2.1.x is better. I got mine working under 2.0 with these settings:

#define CONFIG_ADLIB
#define CONFIG_MSS
#define CONFIG_AUDIO
#define CONFIG_YM3812

#define CONFIG_AD1848
#define CONFIG_SEQUENCER

#define MSS_BASE 0x530
#define MSS_IRQ 9
#define MSS_DMA 0
#define DSP_BUFFSIZE 65536
#define SELECTED_SOUND_OPTIONS 0x00880204

Note that I do not have MIDI enabled; it should work fine if you enable it,
I just havn't had the time to test it myself. One caveat with RedHat-5.1
is that the mixer settings are really cranked by default, and the audio
will sound terrible. I used these settings with better results:

(from /etc/.aumixrc)

pcm:16:16:P
speaker:0:0:P
mic:16:16:R
mix:12:12:P
igain:0:0:P
line1:0:0:P
line2:0:0:P
line3:0:0:P

Finally, I've had no luck getting 2.1.x running on my AS200 so far (using
SRM). But I have heard reports that the WSS driver locks the system during
bootupt on (at least some) Alpha's. YMMV.

Hope this helps,
-bp
