>>> Bret Orsburn said:
> I'm pretty new to the process of tracking & upgrading linux versions.
> To save me from the perils of my own speculation, could some kind soul
> fill me in on the Big Picture relationship between:
>
> //www.kernel.org/pub/linux/kernel/v2.0/linux-2.0.35.tar.gz
> //ftp.redhat.com/.../updates/5.1/alpha/kernel-source-2.0.35-1.alpha.rpm
> //gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-sources-2.0.35-0.1.gz
> //gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-patches-2.0.34-0.1.gz
> //gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/alpha-patches-2.0.35-0.1.gz
>
> It appears that the 2.0.35-1 sources from Redhat have the 2.0.34-0.1 patches
> applied, but the 2.0.35-0.1 patches do not apply cleanly.

Yes, I think that the RH 2.0.35-1 sources are "official" 2.0.35, perhaps
with some alpha patches based on (most likely) the gatekeeper 2.0.34-0.1
set, and perhaps including some of their own. But I don't know for sure,
I've never looked that closely.

> Are the Redhat sources and the Gatekeeper sources parallel lines of
> development?

Not really, though fairly frequently the RH sources contain a lot of the
patches that show up on gatekeeper.

> If I use the 2.0.35-0.1 sources from Gatekeeper, do I lose Redhat's changes
> to the "official" 2.0.35?

Yes, quite likely, and perhaps not something that you want to do; let me
explain a bit...

The Gatekeeper sources are maintained primarily to work for building kernels
for Alphas; nothing more, nothing less.

The Red Hat sources, on the other hand, while useful for building kernels,
often have additions/deletions/modifications of the Gatekeeper patches,
usually in the header file area, and are intended to build working
applications.

This is the most important reason I recommend *always* installing/building
alpha-patches and their kernels somewhere other than /usr/src/linux, so that
the RH headers will be left as they intended for the building of user
applications.

In general, I believe the RH headers and the alpha-patches headers are
reasonably in sync; please report any problems you might find related to the
latest Gatekeeper patches and the RH development (rawhide) kernel sources.
