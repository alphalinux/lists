I had this problem on a 5.1 initially, then it disappeared, possibly
following an upgrade. Anyway, I remembered that 'ntpdate' from
ftp://ftp.redhat.com/pub/redhat/redhat-5.1/alpha/RedHat/RPMS/xntp3-5.93-2.alpha.rpm
triggered it consistently, and when I replaced this package with
ftp://ftp.redhat.com/pub/contrib/manhattan/alpha/xntp3-5.91_export-3.alpha.rpm
the problem went away. This may be perhaps useful to know for people
who are trying to trigger the bug (either developers or masochists,
or perhaps these two words have the same meaning ;-)

This was discussed on the list on july 21. Unfortunately RH's archival
tool was not working those days, so here's a quick summary:
Uncle George analyzed the problem and traced it to a combination of
- stupidity of ntpdate in repeatdly calling a failing poll system call
- lack of a \n in traps.c:do_entSys() in the kernel
- klogd trashing the stack, due to lack of \n and consequent overflowing
and proposed some patches. These patches however did not work for
Paul Tomblin.

In all cases, I think that RedHat should issue an update of xntp3-5.93
which solves this problem.
