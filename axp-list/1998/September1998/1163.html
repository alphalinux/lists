Bret Orsburn wrote:

> I've only found one version of gtop (0.0?!) and it references a couple undefined
> functions -- g_string_hash and g_string_equal. I've tried a couple different
> versions of gtk+ to try to resolve these (1.0.1-3 and 1.0.4), with no luck.

gtop is part of GNOME, and as such very early in development- pre-alpha or alpha
(version, not processor). The gtk+ stable branch is up to 1.0.6, but gtop requires
the development 1.1 branch of gtk+ (which is split into glib 1.1.3, gtk+ 1.1.2),
also the latest ORBit and gnome-libs, possibly libgtop as well (depending on how
new your gtop is). I like it, but I rebuild GNOME and a lot of its apps from CVS
about once a week.

Unfortunately, due to the nature of pre-alpha software, GNOME sometimes compiles,
sometimes doesn't, sometimes runs, sometimes crashes, and if you're going to be on
the bleeding edge, you deal. This is really not a valid basis on which to judge
Linux.

Yeah- regular top comes with procps, which you may need to update.
