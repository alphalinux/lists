Hi there Bruce,

I have sucessfully installed and am running RH5.1 Linux/Alpha on my
Deskstation 533Mhz with 21164PC processor.
I booted directly off the CD-ROM 1 from RH5.1. I did not use any boot
diskette or Ram diskette.
I just use the linload and MILO from the CD-ROM1.

Here is semi-detailed configuraion of my workstation and a step-by-step
method to boot off the
CD-ROM.

Details:
DeskStation Rebel 533 with AlphaBIOS 5.62-1
System Alpha PC164SX
21164PC 533MHz, 64MB RAM,
Generic DualChannel PCI IDE controller with fixed hard disk ST34342A 4GB
and ATAPI Sony CD-ROM CDU711

Here is what I did,
1) Set up AlphaBIOS to load linload from your floppy drive A:.
2) Copy linload.exe and your SX164 image from milo/images from the CD-ROM1
to a DOS formatted floppy.
3) Rename SX164 file to Milo.
4) Insert the created floppy into A: and turn on your machine again.
5) At the AlphaBIOS prompt, choose the Linux OS , this makes AlphaBIOS load
linload, which in turns loads MILO.
6) After some messages, you will get a MILO> prompt.
7) At prompt, type boot floppy. This will give some scrolling text and will
make floppy drive
LED light up indefinitely. Just ignore it, and take note how Linux
recognized your CD-ROM
drive. (eg, hdc for IDE ATAPI CD-ROM, scd0 for scuzzy for your case maybe).
8) Now turn on the machine again with the created floppy in A: and CD-ROM1
of RH5.1 in CD-ROM drive.
9) When you get to the MILO prompt issue a ls -t iso9660 hdc:kernels to
list kernel images
available on the CD-ROM. In your case, the hdc would be scd0 or
something close.
10)Now boot kernel directly from CD-ROM issuing this command ( for my case )
boot -t iso9660 hdc:kernels/sx164.gz root=/dev/hdc
for you case try this
boot -t iso9660 scd0: kernels/sx164.gz root=/dev/scd0 ( could be scd1 if
CD-ROM connected
to 2nd scuzzy control).
11)A RH5.1 installation screen should appear. The rest is quite easy.
