Hello Mark,
I personally do not have much exp. with Alphas and C programming,
but I was under the impression that the C Compiler was the weakest link
in RH Linux... It has been widely discussed that the Digital Unix
compiler
produces MUCH faster executables than the compiler included with Red Hat
Linux.

I think your client will need to look into alternative compilers
for use on the Alpha if he wants Linux to beat WinNT...

I don't think this is a swap problem, but 8 tiny swap partitions
seems abit extreme - can he reorganize it to be two partitions, each
as big as actual RAM (512 Meg)? I don't think it will make much of a
differnece, but I wonder how good performance will be if he goes to swap
out a 130 Meg image - one partition will not handle it...

I have always regarded SWAP as voodoo science - you find what
works and don't ask questions (there are many such things in computers,
like trying to predict how the database optimizer will work, etc. You
can make good guesses, but you never REALLY know what is happening...).

Good luck,

Ken
