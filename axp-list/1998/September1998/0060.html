Shane Steven Sturrock wrote:
>
> I did setserial /dev/modem spd_vhi and tried higher
> than 38400 but the modem would stop working again.

This is not at all surprising as a serial port on UDB is hardware
limited to 38400. Maybe on a good day you could get away with
'spd_hi', i.e. 57600, but you effectively told it to operate
at 115200. A bit too much, I guess.

BTW - "serial line loops back" is, in my experience, a really "generic"
ppp error. You can translate it "for whatever reasons you did not
get a lasting connection". There is a lot of things which can go
wrong - starting with a misconfigured serial port, through misbehaving
modems on both ends, sending stuff the other end does not expect, etc.
pppd and chat have debugging flags and reading your logs from failed
attempts, with debugging on, usually quickly helps. If the other
end is NT you may need 'MS RAS' support - which often means recompiling
pppd. PPP-HOWTO explains details.
