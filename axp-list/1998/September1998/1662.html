Paul Gavazzi <gavazzi@access.digex.net> writes:
> I am assuming that redhat's install overwrites the aboot stuff but, even
> after reinstalling aboot I can't get the machine to boot.
>
> I must be missing something fundamental. Has anyone done this?

It sounds like you use fdisk or disk-druid to partition you
disk. Don't do this, as an fdisk style partition will indeed overwrite
the boot sector and aboot. At least use the 'bsd' compatiblity option
of fdisk, but I believe it's better to partition you disk with
'minlabel.exe'.

I heard that the RH-5.1 instalation disk doesn't recognice bsd-labels,
so try the RH-5.0 image instead.

HTH, Martin
