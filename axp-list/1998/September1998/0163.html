i have the same weird problem with my SX164... when playing sounds my
Soundblaster 16 (vibra chipset) will just stop midstream and my system
will be completely locked.

but i have found that if i take the card out, plug it into my pentium
system, it works fine. then i put the card back into my SX and it works!
and then maybe three or four weeks later, while playing a sound it will
lock up again!! i can't seem to see any pattern to what i'm doing when the
lock-ups occur... it's very strange.

i don't know much about how ISA devices actually work, but it almost seems
as though the SX system isn't clearing some kind of buffer or something on
my sound card... and so that when i put it in my pentium, it is
cleared...???

matt

> xquake is my favourite game but I wanted to test the performance. Anyways in
> some rooms of the dungeon the game hangs and the framerate drops to about
> 1fps or even slower. I also saw at those moments using xosview that no
> hardware interrupts were coming through to the kernel; I also tried to ping
> the machine when hanging without success. Just touching the mouse sometimes
> resolves the hang or the machine freezes completely.
> Today I compiled various packages and found out that the machine bacame very
> slow and inconvenient to work with. At a certain point even the X console
> beep which I set to 1/10 sec. lasted for more than 5 seconds instead!
> And now for the strange part: When I play mp3 files using mpg123 at the same
> time all performance is back again, no hangs anymore.
>
> My conclusion: Something is wrong with the SX164's hardware interrupts.
> While playing sound many interrupts are caused by the soundcard (in my case
> a SoundBlaster AWE32).
>
> I am just sorry that I have too less knowledge of the Alpha's hardware and

> going wrong here.
