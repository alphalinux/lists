In the course of an attempted hardware rearrangement involving several
machines, I swapped two PCI boards (a Matrox Millenium II, 4MB and an
Adaptec 2940U) in an LX-533 MHz machine. What I really wanted to do
is to replace the Matrox by an S3Virge card as I don't need grafic on
this machine. However, since suddenly the SCSI BIOS didn't initialize
anymore, I replaced the Adaptec 2940U with another card of this type
and put the Matrox back in. (Don't know either why this would
suddenly not work anymore, but anyways...). After this the
AlphaBios/SCSI BIOS is satisfied, but now the machine hangs in the
Milo stage: When initializing my normal boot sequence, the machine
loads the PAL stuff, then quasi-reboots as normal, a few more messages
look normal as well (something adjusting latency etc.), but then there
is endless scrolling of numbers etc:

What I can read says:

pyxis_machine_check: NOT expected

followed by lots of numbers, then the message repeats forever.

I am positive the thing hangs in the MILO stage, it never gets to boot
the kernel. I am currently a little bit desperate, since this is more
or less a production machine and I wanted to use a local holiday here.
Ideas are appreciated: For what it's worth, this is the summary of
hard and software:

LX 533MHz, 256MB memory, Adaptec 2940U
Matrox Millenium II, 4MB
A Dec 21140 based Network card

RH 5.1 with all patches; the milo is the one that came with RH 5.1

With the exception of the SCSI Adapter (which replaced another 2940U),
this is the configuration that used to work now for the last 6 months.
The original Matrox is back in, and all cards are in the slots in which
they used to be. I tried a few permutations, but to no avail.

Somehow that reminds me of a problem Pascal Bouchareine had a while
ago, but I can find only the problem description, not the solution in
the list archives. Further, it seems that for him a grafic card would
not work. That's not my problem: both the Matrox as the S3Virge
display fine until the machine gets stuck.

Can/could a hardware problem only show up in the Milo stage: If there
is a PCI problem, I would have hoped that the AlphaBIOS notices something.
