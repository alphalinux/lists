> Well, I have an AlphaStation
> 600 - that's some kind of alcor, other than that, I really don't know,

Last I knew, one could buy the Technical Reference Manual for the AS600
Series from DEC, part number: EK-AS800-RM.A01. This is dated July 1995, so
there very well may be later versions. It'll tell you all you want to know,
and prolly a whole lot more... ;-}

> Let's
> see, it's got 256 Megs of ram, a tulip and an NCR, tga -E2, runs redhat.
> Currently I can only run one milo image, which displays garbage on my
> screen, but actually boots a kernel. Other milos don't display garbage,
> but don't get very far. I think the tradeoff I made was acceptable.

I don't recommend running an ALCOR from ARC console, booting MILO, as I am
not sure of the stability of such a MILO. It would be much better to run it
from SRM console, booting a kernel directly (via "aboot" of course, or BOOTP).
This is how Linus runs his, IIRC... ;-}

The garbage screen is because that (old!) MILO doesn't have up-to-date TGA
support for the -E2 and -E3 TGAs. All the kernels *do*...

> The kernels it boots MUST be 2.0.33 through 2.0.35, or bust. Dunno why,
> just gives me weird halt codes, or doesn't boot at all, depending on if
> I'm using SRM or ARC (has anyone been able to make sense of this
> particular machine's SRM console? It just doesn't do anything!) Now
> that I've vented.

In what way does SRM not "do anything"?

You *do* have to understand a bit more, at times, to get it to do what you
want, but under some circumstances, it's much preferable to ARC or
AlphaBIOS. I like to use it during heavy kernel development, because it
offers much quicker turnaround of test loads by using BOOTP from a build
machine.

And you do have to have different disk setup to boot from harddisk...
