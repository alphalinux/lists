> >my UW HDD, because I'm told the SCSI bus would run at whatever

>
> This is a Myth. Actually SCSI is an intelligent interface, and transactions
> with different devices occur at the bandwidth and bus speed of each device
> transaction. Additionally with cache buffers allocated by the O.S., and
> cache in the devices, this will not be a problem.

You are right that the SCSI bus will not operate at the speed of the
slowest device.

However, a slow SCSI device will definitely affect other devices.
Just the fact that they take longer on the bus will affect system
throughput.

Tape drives are real bad for slowing SCSI systems, and a lot of
people put them and CD-ROMs on separate controllers for that reason.

I have heard that putting drives on wide cable and the slow stuff on
narrow helps. It helped on an Ultrasparc at work. I'm not sure why.

My 4mm tape drive definitely slows my system down far beyond the
fact that two devices are sharing the bus.

Some devices tend to hog the bus more and its good to keep them
separated if you can.

I have heard mixed reports about Zip drives, and I think their
performance depends on which version you have (they changed them
over time and there is a new Plus model now).
