Some observations...been-a-hacking.

/sbin/loader gives the message:
513:/sbin/loader: Fatal Error: loader cannot be invoked directly

where 513 is the PID of the process. ps reveals:
(0)<mcelrath@draal:/home/mcelrath> ps aux | grep loader
mcelrath 512 4.3 6.1 22120 15728 ? S 11:48 0:01 /sbin/loader
mcelrath 513 0.0 0.0 0 0 ? Z 11:48 0:00 (loader <zombie>)

Thus, the loader error is generated by a *SECOND* invocation of
/sbin/loader. The first succeeds, and loads netscape (or other program),
but for some reason the kernel tries to load it *again*. The zombie process
can't be killed...

any ideas? Anyone know what a zombie process is?
