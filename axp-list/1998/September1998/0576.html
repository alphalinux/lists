On Mon, Sep 21, 1998 at 12:00:43PM -0700, Murray Stokely wrote:
> completed without errors. HOWEVER gcc -v still lists the old version
> because it appears that its still using the stuff in /usr/lib/gcc-lib
> from egcs 1.02 instead of the new stuff in /usr/local/lib/gcc-lib. I
> tried making the former just a symlink to the latter but that didn't
> work. What manual steps do I need to take to tell the compiler where
> all its new files are now located? RPMs anyone?

I have my standard compiler (at the moment egcs-1.1b) under
/usr/bin/gcc,g++,f77 and I rename my newly compiled compiler binaries to
/usr/local/bin/egcc,eg++,eg77, so I can select which one I want to use. Note
that you can also achieve this by issueing gcc -V egcs-2.91.57 (or whatever
version you want to pick).
