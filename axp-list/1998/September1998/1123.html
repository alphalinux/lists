On Thu, 1 Oct 1998, Joe Reiter wrote:

> What's the difference between making "vmlinux" and making "zImage" when
> compiling your kernel. On my x86 machines, I always use zImage, but on my
> Alpha I always have to use vmlinux. What are the differences?

PC's have some limitations on both the size and location of BIOS-bootable
images. This mandates compressed images (zImage/bzImage) for images over a
certain size. The Alpha doesn't have these restrictions. Anyway, I think
the preferred target for kernels on Alpha is "make boot", producing a
compressed kernel in arch/alpha/boot (the compression in this case is
merely for disk-space conservation). At least, that's what everyone's told
me. :)
