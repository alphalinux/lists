Actually, SRM does not recognize *any* filesystems or partition tables.
All it knows about is the offset and length of the primary bootstrap
loader; this information is carried in sector 0 of the disk.

The primary bootstrap loader is what understands partition and filesystem
formats. In the case of Digital UNIX, it's called "osfboot". In the
case of Linux/Alpha, you can use aboot.

I'm working with Erik Troan (hi, Erik!) on making the SRM install procedure
much smoother in Red Hat 5.2...
