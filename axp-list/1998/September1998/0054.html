Quoting Jeff Sturm (jsturm@gatecom.com):
> The ttyS0 port by default observes modem control signals, which prevent
> open() from completing until CD/DTR (IIRC) is raised. The workaround is
> to use "stty -clocal" which configures the port to ignore modem control.
> The catch-22 is that "stty -clocal </dev/ttyS0" will hang forever on a
> blocking open.

Hmmmmmmmmmmm. I have a vague recollection of seeing my wife in the Setup menu
on the vt220 for something (usually it's because the damn thing switches
spontaneoulsy between the mode where the arrow and esc keys works and where
they don't). When I get home tonight, I'll have to check to see if she didn't
accidentally change the modem control settings on the terminal.

Thanks. This is the most useful information anybody has posted in this whole
