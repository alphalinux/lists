Does the error message say "Connection refused"?

If so, no one is listening on the FTP socket.

Use the command "netstat -a" and look for a line like:

tcp 0 0 *:ftp *:* LISTEN

This indicates that FTP is being listened for on your
system. If it's missing, then there is a problem with
your /etc/services, /etc/inetd.conf, or you need to
restart inetd. When ever you make changes to those
files, you must get inetd to reread them. Just changing
them does nothing until inetd looks them over. Send
inetd a SIGHUP to get it to reread the files, or, if
you prefer, fix the files and reboot if it's your personal
system. SIGHUP is the way to go on servers. Then check again
with "netstat -a" to see if FTP is available.

If it doesn't show up under that command, the service won't
be available to use, at least on everything I've ever worked
with...
