> >
> > Yes. The 4100 is a member of the RAWHIDE family, which also includes the
> > 4000 and the 1200 (the latter aka TINCUP).
>
> shoot. Does it mean that the RedHat-5.1 distribution that I've recently
> got is going to do me no good ? I've looked into
> ftp://ftp.redhat.com/pub/rawhide/alpha/images , but haven't found anything
> new, so I went ahead and downloaded the original 5.1 from
> ftp://ftp.redhat.com/pub/redhat/redhat-5.1/alpha .

Sorry, as Michal pointed out as well, 4100 is RAWHIDE family of Alphas,
whereas "rawhide" is also the name of RH's "development" release.

Support for 4100, in the form of a kernel floppy image, is part of RH 5.1;
find it as rawhide-s.img in the kernel images directory...

> I hope that I'll be able to provide you with some feedback before
> Monday, unless I have to re-download from a slow FTP site.

Again, go with RH 5.1 as-is...

> I've promissed that I won't touch the machine's configuration, which I
> guess translates into not touching the harddrives. So I must run this
> thing over NFS.

Where are you going to install the bits to be used for NFS? Even root-on-NFS
requires an appropriately set-up disk, as, AFAIK, the CD is *not* correct for
this operation. Anyone know different?

> Hmm, strange, both redhat:/pub/rawhide/alpha/images and
> redhat/redhat-5.1/alpha/images have the same rawhide-s.img dated May 12
> 02:59 + timezone.

Not strange; if you look at the dates, a *lot* of the "rawhide" files are
copies of the 5.1 (perhaps updated) files. Only the things which really
needed replacing/updating are different, AFAICT...
