With regards to your message at 09:11 AM 10-18-97 -0600, ferret@frii.com.
Where you stated:
>I installed RH 5.1 on my Alphastation 200 4/233 some time ago and I've just
>been using the floppy to boot. I've grown tired of this method and I want
>to copy MILO to the DOS partition on my hard drive and set up ARC to boot
>it automatically. I can't figure out how to access the floppy to perform an
>ls of it. Also, what is the syntax to mcopy from it to the the DOS
>partition? Once I've copied this to my DOS partition, how can I tell MILO
>to automatically boot Linux from the correct paritition?
>
>Beyond that, I just tried to read a man page about mcopy and I can't even
>start the man utility. When I attempt to bring up man I receive errors
>indicating that there is an unaligned trap <memory address> and then a
>segmentation fault. What is the problem with man?

You need to do a proper DOS format on the partiition first.
Arcinst.exe will do this for you:
http://www.windowsnt.digital.com/support/drivers/drivers.asp

Next, while mcopy will work, it might be just simpler to mount the DOS
filesystem on the hard disk FAT partition.
mkdir /mnt/boot
mount -t msdos /dev/sda1 /mnt/boot for example would do..
Then, you want to copy linload.exe and milo to this FAT partition.

In ARC, under "Set up the system" is an area for creating boot options.
Essentially you need a boot option pointing at linload.exe to execute on
the proper partition on the hard disk.
It also allows you to pass parameters to it, such as:
boot sda2:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda2 (for example)
