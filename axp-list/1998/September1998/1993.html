Agreed. I packed away my multia for an AS200. This machine is about
three times as fast or more during compiles (no more hour+ wait for
kernels) and has more expansion slots. I've got my soundblaster 16 in it,
a 4 meg s3 video card, and 2 cdroms since I was finally able to put the
scsi hard drive into the machine, unlike the multia.
I'm a physicist by trade, and even with egcs being relatively shaky in
the fortran area I still find it fast as hell when doing the stuff I need
to do with it. But the fact remains. I paid under 250 bucks for this
machine from onsale. I'm not expecting 2500 worth of performance, but it
feels like I'm getting my 25,000 worth when doing the things that I need
done.
