Well, I upgraded my glibc to 2.0.7-19 and downgraded net-tools to
1.33-4 before I finally figured out that I didn't have ip forwarding
enabled in the kernel. Doing

echo 1 > /proc/sys/net/ipv4/ip_forward

solved all of my problems. Using the UDB instead of a 486 to do the
masquerade also fixed Netscape's DNS problem.

BTW, upgrading glibc got me into real trouble. Somehow a couple of
critial libraries were not installed, and that leaves the system
almost dead. (I'm running my UDB headless.) Fortunately, I have a DU
copy of XEmacs, which doesn't use these libraries. I could still do
copy-file, rename-file, make-symbolic-link, etc, (but no dired). I use
an nfs partition to set uid root on XEmacs and fixed the libraries.
