On Fri, 11 Sep 1998, Shane Steven Sturrock wrote:

> I thought I would post this for the benefit of the archives.
>
> I had the same problem as many others, a modem (in my case a PCMCIA
> 14.4 job from my P75 laptop) refused to work with the same script
> that worked previously when plugged into my UDB (2.0.34 with PCMCIA,
> PPP etc compiled in) complaining about serial line looped back. Nothing
> suggested in the PPP-HOWTO would fix the problem but minicom would work
> although it always made the connection at 38400 so I figured what the
> hey! Changeing the speed in my ppp script to 38400 I tried again and
> the thing worked! I did setserial /dev/modem spd_vhi and tried higher
> than 38400 but the modem would stop working again.
>
> Anyone got any idea why this might be the case? Either way, at least
> it is working and I never got more than about 4KB a second down the
> thing
> anyway but if I do get a faster modem it would be nice not to be limited
> to 38400......

spd_vhi sets the port to 115200, most 14.4 modems did not support
these speeds. spd_hi might work (57600), but with a 14.4 modem anything
past 38400 is of questionable use.
