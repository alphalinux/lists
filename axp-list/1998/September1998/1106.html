On Thu, 1 Oct 1998, Jochen Kuepper wrote:

> As discussed here several times, older glibc math libs had bugs that were fixed
> somewhere inbetween your two versions. Due to these bugfixes some routines
> (like sqrt) got slower. You should use the newer glibc if you depend on your
> results.
> There are two well optimized libs that provide parts of the libm, namely libffm
> and libfm, maintained by K. Goto and J. Wesner. Check the web for these if you
> want some real speed improvements.

Speaking of which, on my UDB (running Debian and the latest glibc 2.0.7
rev), I ran a comparison between using libffm and just libm. Here's what
I got:

libffm + libm:
1000000 sqrts in 1.68946 sec, 591907 per sec

libm only:
1000000 sqrts in 9.90054 sec, 101005 per sec

As you can see, this is a HUGE improvement in sqrt times. Also, keep in
mind that libffm was really written for the 21164's (UDBs have 21066's),
so if libffm can possibly get better for the 21066's, I could probably get
better numbers.
