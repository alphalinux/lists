Dan Morrill wrote:
>
> OpenLDAP has an #ifdef LINUX around the points where it uses
> these signals; however, it uses SIGSTKFLT and SIGUNUSED,
> which are present on Linux/Intel but not on Linux/Alpha.

Hm, running 'kill -l' on my Linux/Intel prints exactly this:

1) SIGHUP 2) SIGINT 3) SIGQUIT 4) SIGILL
5) SIGTRAP 6) SIGIOT 7) SIGBUS 8) SIGFPE
9) SIGKILL 10) SIGUSR1 11) SIGSEGV 12) SIGUSR2
13) SIGPIPE 14) SIGALRM 15) SIGTERM 17) SIGCHLD
18) SIGCONT 19) SIGSTOP 20) SIGTSTP 21) SIGTTIN
22) SIGTTOU 23) SIGURG 24) SIGXCPU 25) SIGXFSZ
26) SIGVTALRM 27) SIGPROF 28) SIGWINCH 29) SIGIO
30) SIGPWR

> In other words, the authors of OpenLDAP have assumed that
> all Linuxes have the same signum.h, whatever platform they're
> running on.

I am afraid assumption of a type "all platforms are exactly like
mine at this moment" lead straight to a buggy code.

It seems that you can easily solve your immediate problem on hand
by picking up two otherwise unused signals and two defines.

Michal
