On Thu, 24 Sep 1998, Jochen Kuepper wrote:
> IIRC, only X11 needs /lib/cpp.
> Since it is not the right place to have a executable anyhow, the X11 people
> should document it. ( Maybe it is for XFree86 ? I do not know. )

OK, that's fine for systems with gcc, what about the others?

There have been many packages which depend on /lib/cpp, not many are
still around (PostgreSQL still does, IIRC). Back in the early days of
Unix you could almost always count on having a real /lib/cpp. It's still
best for compatibility to have a /lib/cpp that does something. Richard's
suggestion is perfect for gcc-based systems.
