On Mon, Oct 05, 1998 at 01:06:37PM -0300, Jay.Estabrook@digital.com wrote:
>
> Were your filesystems really big? I had a spot of trouble the other day trying
> to use an EXT2 FS that was 4Gb or larger. I repartitioned to 2Gb or less, and
> it all went fine. Of course, YMMV, and I may be blowing smoke... ;-}

Most likely you are. :-) I myself was dealing on Linux/Alpha with
partitions quite a bit bigger than 4 Gb and 4 Gb is nearly everyday
occurence. :-) Not so long time ago I mentioned a report from a guy
from Boenig about his experiences, positive, with a Linux partitions of
around 850 Gb (yes, this is not a typo).

OTOH milo indeed gets hiccups if a kernel it boots is located at "too
far" inode and the best way to ensure that this will not happen is to
keep a partition on which your kernel image is located reasonably small;
under 2 Gigs fits into this category. But this may as well be your
minimal FAT partition if you have one for an automatic boot. Milo is
happy with this filesystem as well. Another possibility, good for
other reasons as well, is a relatively small root partition with
all you need for a basic boot there and not much more.
