> Then the DE500 Card I tested would not even allow my machine to boot when
> using the DE500 driver, and the tulip.c driver would not even allow the
> kernel to compile.

Lots of people can get the kernel to compile with tulip.c, and there
is no "de500" driver -- you must mean the de4x5 driver.

I suspect your tulip problem has to do with trying to use a really old
tulip.c with a new kernel, or vice versa. tulip v0.83 is what 2.0.33
used to use, while v0.89H or whatever is happy with 2.0.34 or
up. tulip v0.83 won't work with 2.0.35, because an interface changed.

The de4x5 driver works better on the de500 for most people; are you
sure you are correctly building the kernel?
