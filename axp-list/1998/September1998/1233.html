With regards to your message at 09:13 AM 10-05-98 -0400, Dan Morrill. Where
you stated:
>Sorry if this is a FAQ, but I couldn't find any information on it.
>
>I have a SCSI ZIP drive that I would like to use on my SX164.
>However, I don't want to put it on the same SCSI controller as
>my UW HDD, because I'm told the SCSI bus would run at whatever
>SCSI mode the ZIP drive runs at (which is definately NOT UW. :)

This is a Myth. Actually SCSI is an intelligent interface, and transactions
with different devices occur at the bandwidth and bus speed of each device
transaction. Additionally with cache buffers allocated by the O.S., and
cache in the devices, this will not be a problem.

>So, I'm going to go out and buy Ye Olde Cheape ISA SCSI controller
>exclusively for this ZIP drive. Does anyone have any experience
>with this?

Go ahead, but you are wasting money.

>Specifically, what cheapo ISA SCSI cards work with an SX164, and
>am I going to have any problems having two SCSI adapters in one
>Linux box?

The Diamond Fireport40 is inexpensive and works well on SX and LX boards in
Linux.
Adaptecs have driver and revision problems.
