Hi!

Stephen J. Gaudet wrote:
>
> Hello Andreas
>
> >This is just FYI: I upgraded AlphaBios on a LX from version 5.62-1a to
> >5.66 and now the system is auto-booting, even without a PS/2 keyboard
> >attached! This is great, as it means I can now install AlphaBios based
> >Linux-Servers "headless", and I was asking for this feature for about
> >12 months now... :-))
> >
> >Thanks to everyone who convinced the AlphaBios team in adding this
> >very basic feature!!
>
> It is a great feature but...it has no serial control via com1. Which means
> you can set it to count down and boot headless. However, can't stop it or
> make any changes via console. Good but, still needs work.
>

Are you sure?

I just tried it with a serial terminal connected to COM1 (9600, 8N1),
and it worked fine! I even could enter setup, change some parameters,
display CMOS configuration, and so on.
_And_ it auto-boots _without_ manual interaction after the timeout-
value specified in the CMOS setup, just as I'd expected!

I tried this on both LX and SX machines, and it works fine on both
systems! I can't say anything against it...
