I got em86 to run on my UDB (very slowly I might add!) by compiling my kernel
with em86 support as a module. Once the rpms are installed I had to manually
insmod /lib/modules/2.0.35/fs/binfmt_em86.o at which point I could run
netscape. I'm sure it runs quite well on the faster Alphas but on a UDB its
unusable. I wish Compaq/DEC would release the rest of FX!32, this would get
the alpha a huge jump on the Merced (not that it doesn't already have one)
because it would make even my humble UDB able to run Intel/Linux binaries at a
reasonabe speed. The fact that so many companies are coming out supporting
Linux but only on Intel is likely to work in favour of Merced and restict the
market for Alpha systems severely.
