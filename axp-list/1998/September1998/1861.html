> disk all ready, and I got the updated 5.0 ramdrive.img (Thank You! to Jay

Welcome. So far, it sounds like you're okay here.

> Boot on floppy, something strange...most accursed damned box does not know
> WHAT the hell /dev/fd0 is. It DOES know what dva0 is. Odd. /dev/dva0 is a
> canonical name for the floppy, right? so hell, you can predict the rest...

Actually, no. Apparently no one at Digital (not Compaq; Digital ;)
remembers what the naming scheme behind dva/dka/etc is, but, rest assured,
they are SRM-specific names and have no bearing on conventional Linux
device file names.

> boot dva0 -file vmlinux.gz -flags "prompt_ramdisk=1 load_ramdisk=1"

Oops. The RedHat bug bites again. This time in the manual. The
manual/docs are missing the vital "root=/dev/fd0", which should also be in
the -flags section. So your boot line should read:

b dva0 -fi vmlinux.gz -fl "prompt_ramdisk=1 load_ramdisk=1 root=/dev/fd0"

> so (creative thinking) I do mass variations trying to stuff root=/dev/fd0,
> no dice, the box thinks I'm insane for suggesting it has a floppy device.

Something in your command line wasn't right....or perhaps I err..? Maybe
there is a problem elsewhere. The root=/dev/fd0 should make things work,
but perhaps not in your case..

Some of the members on this list are working to put together a
comprehensive Linux/AXP website with pointers to all sorts of
documentation. IIRC, the site will be www.alphalinux.org (BTW folks,
how's that coming?) when it is ready. In the meantime, there is a
smattering of other sites with information that could help. Unfortunately,
I can never recall the URL's off the top of my head.
