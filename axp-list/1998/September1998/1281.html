Well, I do... cacao is a research project, not an end-user or developer
tool. The cacao developers have invested their time into JIT and GC
technology. If you are interested in such things, you can download
excellent papers on stack machines, exception handling and register
allocation (among many others) from this site (some are in German).

The cacao research is important to me partly because they have proven,
at least in theory, that JIT compilers on Alpha can outperform their
Intel counterparts. You wouldn't know that based on benchmarks of
available products. Compaq's JDK1.1.6 for Alpha/NT is a disappointment,
probably because they haven't done as much engineering as Symantec or
Microsoft did on their JIT compilers.

Cacao may even become a useable product after they release source code
later this year. My hope is that it can be integrated with Kaffe or even
the JDK so it can leverage the mature class libraries available.
