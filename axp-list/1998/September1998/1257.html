On 6 Oct 1998, in message <98100520341006.00916@.localhost>
Peter Petrakis <peterpan@wn.net> wrote:
| I couldn't send messages to the list for some reason that I just figured out.
| The list server is case sensitive. I had PeterPan@wn.net as my email addrss and
| it didn't work. When I changed it to peterpan@wn.net the message was sent to
| the list and i recived it in like 5 seconds. Is it supposed to do that?

Certainly. It can't do anything else. Why?

Because according to the specs, the hostname part of an address is
case-insensitive, but the localpart (left of the @) needn't be (that's
a per site decision). So without special knowledge about your
particular host, it's not allowed to decide that aaa and aAa are the
same person.
