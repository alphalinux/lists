On Thu, Oct 01, 1998 at 03:25:17PM +0200, Nils Faerber wrote:
> Hello!

> I recommend using 2.1.121 since 2.1.122-123 are NOT stable.
> With my SX I have the problem that modules are not cleaned up correctly and
> that some bug causes programs to crash randomly. 2.1.121 does not show this
> effect.
> You might also have a look at the SX164 page at
>
> http://www.unix-ag.org/Linux-Alpha/Architectures/SX164.html
>
> and include the IRQ patch. You should also _disable_ the RTC in your kernel
> in order to avoid IRQ problems...

Thanks for the feedback.
I have noticed the problem on 2.0.xx kernels as well as most of the
2.1.xx kernels.

I have just looked at the SX164 page. Thank you for the pointer to this
information. I have applied the irq patch (or something very similar) to
all of my more recent kernels (otherwise they are not bootable).
Unfortunately I don't think these problems are behind the xdvi problem.
I'm beginning to suspect my X server, as it was definitely unstable with
the Millenium II as released initially for RH 5.0, but I have not seen
any problems recently except for the xdvi related glitch.

PS.
I have just tried running xdvi from my Alpha (at home) using my 486 box
(at work) as the display terminal (over ppp via a modem). xdvi behaves
perfectly when displayed using the Xserver on my 486. I think this
confirms that the Xserver on the Alpha is the source of the problem. Does
this sound correct?
