Hi all!
Thought I'd throw my two cents into this.
I didn't have any big problem with aboot (ok, it has some
troubles with the newer kernels...), what I found to be the biggest
problem was that the install process didn't understand BSD Disklabels!
This meant that you had to do the install, then go in and duplicate your
filesystems on the BSD labels...
Reports have it that earlier versions (like as recent as 5.0)
did not have this problem, that it was introduced at 5.1.
Can we get that one fixed? It would ease a lot of the
installation woes that this mailing list it filled with.

> Actually, SRM does not recognize *any* filesystems or partition
> tables.
> All it knows about is the offset and length of the primary bootstrap
> loader; this information is carried in sector 0 of the disk.
>
> The primary bootstrap loader is what understands partition and
> filesystem
> formats. In the case of Digital UNIX, it's called "osfboot". In the
> case of Linux/Alpha, you can use aboot.
