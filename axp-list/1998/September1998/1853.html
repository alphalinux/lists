Steve G wrote:
>
> Dammit, I think I'm screwed here somewhere. Yes, I have on frequent
> occasion wiped the /usr/src/linux dir because I've been trying multiple
> different kernels. It looks like your /usr/include/asm symlink points to a
> directory under /usr/src/linux... Mine points to
>
> [root@hymen include]# ls -la asm
> lrwxrwxrwx 1 root root 24 Oct 14 04:13 asm ->
> ../src/linux/include/asm
> [root@hymen include]#
>
> Since I have no real compiled kernel in that directory, what do I do now???
> Everything under /usr/src/linux changes each time I un-tar a new kernel. I
> don't suppose this would be a problem if the kernel would compile all the
> way, right?
>
> Is this going to require a re-install of Linux? If so, how is the correct
> way to clear the contents of the /usr/src/linux dir when you want to un-tar
> a new source? I thought you just rm -Rf * in the dir that the symlink
> points to. I guess this was wrong. Do you just keep a backup, or change
> the symlink to point to a new directory symbolic to the kernel you are
> unpacking? This sounds like the way I should have been doing it.
>

Best thing to do is re-install the rpm that has the
kernel headers in it. Might have to force it though.
>From that point on, do not put your kernels under
/usr/src/linux. Put them anywhere else you like but
leave the /usr/src/linux part alone. This is important
because RH have modified the headers to fix a few
compile problems in some applications and you want to
keep those changes around.
