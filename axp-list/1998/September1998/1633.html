On Wed, 14 Oct 1998, Ronald Bruck wrote:

> >If my internal IDE ZIP drive starts clicking, I have to
> >reboot the machine. Seems like it doesn't find the tracks
> >on the media under some circumstances.
>
> This is a VERY bad sign. ZIP drives are subject to a phenomenon known as
> the "click of death"--you might try searching the web, because there's a
> guy who keeps a website about this.
>
> As I recall, the click of death is caused by electromagnetic interference
> caused by surrounding motors, monitors, etc. You could try moving your
> internal from one bay to another; if it's external you obviously have more
> leeway.

Hmm..

I have no experience with Zip drives (I've yet to get one .. though I've
been thinking about it), but it seems to me that there should be an
easy way to shield the Zip.

Try putting aluminum foil in a plastic bag, and wrapping the Zip in this.
You may need to ground the foil.

A lot of window screening is made from nylon these days, but if you can
find a piece of metal screening, putting that on the outside of the
plastic bag may help too.

Shielding the power supply area too, may help further reduce EMI.

In such a packed area like a computer, I am not sure if this will help
much, but I suppose it is worth a shot.. it is good enough to shield
old TVs from ghost images from monitors.
