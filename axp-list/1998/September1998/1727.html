On Fri, 16 Oct 1998, Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> It has been a while since I last configured a PAS16, but I DO recall it
> used 3 IRQs and about 4 or 5 I/O ports in total.

The PAS can use up to three IRQs - one for the PAS, one for the SB, and
one for the onboard SCSI. I/O port usage is probably similarly high.

> It has both it's own "native" sound interface and a built-in SB16 emulatin,
> bboth of which require separate I/O ports and IRQs.

This is not true. It has the MV chip, AND a SB chip on it - no emulation
involved. But it's only an 8 bit SB chip - not 16. The 16 was
considerably more expensive when this board was released.

> In addition there are I/O ports and so on needed for MIDI chip and for game
> port.

If you bought the MIDI adaptor (not standard) and chose to use the game
port. The game port is going to use I/O ports anyway, whether it's on
this board or not.

> I strongly recommend you consider another audio card. After seeing just how

The PAS is an awesome card and I still use it today. Not only can you
slap two of these things together, but it has (rudimentary) SCSI (good for
an old CD-ROM or maybe tape), a 48k sample rate, and excellent sheilding
(granted, it's no Turtle Beach, but still better than many cards being
produced today). Do watch out that you don't fry your amp though, as it
has a 4 watt per channel preamp output.

> many system resources the PAS consumed I gave up on them. So did many
> others apparently, as the PAS and other products from MediaVision are now
> "historic".

MV went chapter 11 some years ago and never reentered the consumer market.
They had the first 3D sound card and a nice video capture board that's
basic but decent that you can find for about $50. The PAS is still a fine
card, and you shouldn't be afraid to use it. However, there's no need to
use the onboard SB chip. Just don't bother.
