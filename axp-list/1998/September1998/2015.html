> Thanks for your comments. What I am mainly interested in is general
> performance.

The numbers he quoted were for SPEC95fp and SPEC95int, and you can
actually predict those results based on the sizes of the working sets
for those benchmarks -- specfp has a bigger working set, so it gets a
lot more boost for the bigger L3. My codes tend to have either bigger
or smaller working sets...

But in your case, the only way to know is to test. I'd bet that the
bigger caches wouldn't help you that much. And yes, adding L3 does
slow down main memory; you can see this effect in the streams results
for the machine. Given the high price of the cache, I doubt it makes
economic sense.
