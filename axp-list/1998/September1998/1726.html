With regards to your message at 02:56 AM 10-16-98 -0500, Bob McElrath.
Where you stated:
>I'm having some problems with an ISA Pro Audio Spectrum 16 card on a DEC
>AlphaPC164LX motherboard. I have configured the kernel (2.1.125) to use
>both the PAS and its Sound Blaster emulator. (2 audio devices). However,
>when I attempt to use the PAS I get the following error:
>
>Oct 16 02:49:45 draal kernel: Sound: DMA (output) timed out - IRQ/DRQ
config error?
>
>When I attempt to use the SB emulation of the card I get:
>
>Oct 16 02:52:10 draal kernel: Sound: DMA (output) timed out - IRQ/DRQ
config error?
>Oct 16 02:52:10 draal kernel: Sound: Error. Audio interrupt (0) after
freeing buffers.
>Oct 16 02:52:11 draal last message repeated 79 times
>
>The obvious answer is that I've got the IRQ and/or DMA configured
>wrong...but I don't (I've checked several times, both the SB and PAS are
>configured properly wrt IRQ/DMA). After playing several files,
>/proc/interupts reveals:
>
>(0)<root@draal:/root> cat /proc/interrupts
> 1: 3496 keyboard
> 4: 2397 + serial
> 5: 25 soundblaster
> 8: 670187 timer
>10: 0 PAS16
>16: 6755 BusLogic BT-958
>17: 9279 eth0
>
>Could there be a general problem with ISA interrupts on this board?
>
I think you are missing some things.
It has been a while since I last configured a PAS16, but I DO recall it
used 3 IRQs and about 4 or 5 I/O ports in total.

It has both it's own "native" sound interface and a built-in SB16 emulatin,
bboth of which require separate I/O ports and IRQs.
In addition there are I/O ports and so on needed for MIDI chip and for game
port.

I strongly recommend you consider another audio card. After seeing just how
many system resources the PAS consumed I gave up on them. So did many
others apparently, as the PAS and other products from MediaVision are now
"historic".
