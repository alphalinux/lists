On Tue, 8 Sep 1998, Jon 'maddog' Hall, USG Senior Leader wrote:

> Was it in the early days, when AT&T sold you a source code license for
> $160,000. on a single CPU? And remember, that was when $160K was worth
> something (1977 or so).

That number seems quite high. I remember looking at SCO Unix for ~8k
(roughly the same price point as Novell NetWare) back in the mid-80s.
Also available at the time was Dell UNIX and several other SysV Unix
systems. Perhaps the source license was $160k.

The reason I recall this is that, at the time, a small canadian
company had a DOs/Unixy OS that was "unlimited" user and sold for less
than half of a commercial UNIX. At around $3k, QNX was still quite
expensive.

> Nope, NetBSD(R), FreeBSD(R), OpenBSD(R) and Linux(R) are the only free and
> open operating systems, and none of them are "Unix", which requires a very
> expensive branding process controlled by The Open Group.

Except you can't get Win32 branding for you OS, even if you did pass
all the APIs properly. Instead you'd probably burdened with a bunch of
lawsuits. In that regard, Unix is pretty "Open".
