>So then, by this logic we should be able to register a site called
>www.intel.com, and by merely mentioning on the webpage that Intel is a
>registered trademark of Intel Corporation we are OK?
>I think Intel has lawyers that might debate that point with you.
>IMHO simply making a request for use of the names, and keeping those
>replies on file would be a prudent gesture.

First, intel.com is already registered. ;-) www.intel.com
isn't a valid domain name. If you were to register intelinfo.com,
for example, and made the attribution that "Intel" was a registered trademark
of Intel Corporation and IntelInfo was in no way associated with Intel
Corporation you'd probably have the bases covered well enough that they'd
leave you alone.

But we're not talking about a corporation with a cadre of lawyers, we're
talking about Linus. He has made it clear that he has no intent to restrict
the proper usage of the name, just that he want's the TM clause included.
The only time any stink has ever been raised is when someone has used the
name in a commercialized setting without the attribution. Prudent, perhaps.
Required, no.

I have nothing else to say on this matter, since we are quickly moving
beyond the scope of the original discussion.
