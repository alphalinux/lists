Brian E.W. Wood wrote:

> Note that 1.1b is the latest version of egcs, not 1.0.3a.

1.1b has given me internal compiler errors on a couple of programs that compile
flawlessly with 1.0.3a, so I went back. Likewise with snapshots since sometime
in July. The most recent snapshot gave me an ICE while building kernel 2.1.125.
That's why I advised Steve to go with 1.0.3a.

(Yes, 1.1b compiles them fine with -O instead of -O2, but I prefer to be able to
compile hassle-free out-of-the-box without different CFLAGS with different
files.)

For example, try http://www.ctcms.nist.gov/~powell/eval_sec.c , with cpp output
in eval_sec_cpp.c ; coplile with:
gcc -DLINUX -O2 -DOOGL -c eval_sec.c
(Yes, I submitted a bug report to egcs-bugs, three times for two snapshots and
release 1.1b.)
