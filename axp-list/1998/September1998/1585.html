On Tue, 13 Oct 1998, Bill Roman wrote:
> > Keep in mind that
> > their 'special' unit doesn't include a video card, monitor or hard drive,
> > and its SCSI is old standard.
>
> I bought a new XL 300 from them last spring, ready to run, just add
> monitor (although more RAM didn't hurt). (Check with Great Lakes on
> exactly what the XL 366 includes.) And unless you are running a server
> or are planning to make use of above average disk bandwidth, the narrow
> SCSI-2 interface is generally adequate.
>
> > Try The Lizon Corporation, they have an SX system for
> > under 1500$US, and it is fairly decked out.
>
> Depending on configuration, the SX system has the potential to be
> substantially faster. So the tradeoff is an older, slower, but absolutely
> rock-solid reliable, DEC-built, DEC-warrantied box; or a faster machine
> at a slightly higher price, with a combination of hardware components
> put together by a third-party system integrator, and thus requiring more
> evaluation on your part.

Sorry for the confusion. The 1500$ SX sytem I stated includes hard drive,
video and monitor, which GLComp's XL366 does not for 875$ or so. The main
thing the XL366 may possibly have better than the 533SX is more cache, but
I've seen computation speed results that shows the SX as slightly faster
than LX (possibly due to larger L1 i-cache) at some tasks.

The SX system board can be replaced much easier and at lower cost than the
XL366's, as the SX board is AT or ATX compatible (dang, I forget), so a
total board swapout should be a simple thing.

Before you buy, make sure you know the warranty. The one I bought from
them (a Miata 500a) I'm sure only had the 30 day warranty that they give
standard. If I ever have any problems down the road I'll check for sure.
