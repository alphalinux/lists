Dan Morrill wrote:

> I have a SCSI ZIP drive that I would like to use on my SX164.
> However, I don't want to put it on the same SCSI controller as
> my UW HDD, because I'm told the SCSI bus would run at whatever
> SCSI mode the ZIP drive runs at (which is definately NOT UW. :)

Are you sure? You could always try it first.

Notes: - I am not a SCSI expert.
- The examples I'm about to give is for i386 (not alpha) and
Adaptec 2940U (not wide)

I have a Seagate Barracuda (ultra SCSI), an old Seagate Hawk (fast SCSI)
and an external SCSI ZIP on a 2940U controller. Here's part of what
/proc/scsi/aic7xxx/0 says:

(scsi0:0:0:0)
Device using Narrow/Sync transfers at
20.0 MByte/sec, offset 15
Total transfers 4151084 (2444145 read;1706939 written)
blks(512) rd=18732995; blks(512) wr=11588659

(scsi0:0:1:0)
Device using Narrow/Sync transfers at
10.0 MByte/sec, offset 15
Total transfers 162861 (67846 read;95015 written)
blks(512) rd=3309965; blks(512) wr=13273156

(scsi0:0:6:0)
Device using Narrow/Async transfers.
Total transfers 55 (55 read;0 written)
blks(512) rd=102; blks(512) wr=0

The first is at 20.0 MByte/sec Narrow/Sync (ultra), the second is at
10.0 MByte/sec Narrow/Sync (fast) and the third at Narrow/Async (ZIP).

Everything works fine, and I still get 9 MB/sec transfer rate from the
Barracuda.
