>> Hi all,
>>
>> does anyone here know how to setup and mount an internal ATAPI-ZIP Drive
>> under Linux ? I tried it using vfat FS but the drive made some quite
>> strange "click" noises while trying to mount until I decided to reboot the
>> machine ? Any ideas ? Did anyone get this thing running under Linux ?
>
>If my internal IDE ZIP drive starts clicking, I have to
>reboot the machine. Seems like it doesn't find the tracks
>on the media under some circumstances.

This is a VERY bad sign. ZIP drives are subject to a phenomenon known as
the "click of death"--you might try searching the web, because there's a
guy who keeps a website about this.

As I recall, the click of death is caused by electromagnetic interference
caused by surrounding motors, monitors, etc. You could try moving your
internal from one bay to another; if it's external you obviously have more
leeway.

I have two Zip drives, both in Macintoshes, both internal; one clicks and
one doesn't. So far I've had no disk failures, but I keep the disk in the
one that clicks ONLY when I'm writing to it.

Obviously not all clicking is the "click of death". But you need to
investigate this.

I used to hold a very low opinion of Syquest, because I kept having trouble
with their cartridges. So I switched to Jaz and Zip. Now I have a very
low opinion of Iomega :-)
