About the big-series/lower-prices issue...

Ahhh, so this explains why Compaq, DEC, IBM, Intel and all the other big
companies are so cheap (not!) compared to smaller ones. Thanks for that
clarification...

...meaning that it might also be that bigger companies equals slower
companies and huge bureaucratic overhead and tens of thousands engineers
sitting in small cubicles having no idea what they are supposed to do
while hoards of office-workers carry around million tons of paper
stating this weeks policies on how to be a happy cubicle, and the
greyhaired yuppies in the boardroom make their big plans to get bigger
and better, and a decade or two later has to lay-off 50000 people
just to survive next year, because the big company made big and expensive
and outdated and slow products and people buying the stuff don't care if
it's a big company with high volume lines, as they can get better stuff
cheaper from that new, small and nimble company where the guys in the
boardroom actually care about the company and it's products and are
less worried about how big a fortune they'll have when they are sixty...

...meaning that we don't know what will happen, really, and in the
end both the big and the small company and all the yuppies inbetween
will be dust, and in another part of the galaxy a small protein will crawl
out of the mud thinking that if it joins up with that sexy-looking
protein in the next mudhole it will be even bigger and better...

...meaning that I probably should sleep more and I really must read
Asimovs Foundation again...

/ronny
