With regards to your message at 11:18 PM 01-15-98 -0500,
rmcilhar@smart.net. Where you stated:
>> I'm presuming that you're looking at the 164SX from Aspen. I'm running
>> the 164SX from Enorex (which is presumably fairly similar) with the
>> Millenium II without problems. The only downside is that the 164SX
>
> I don't think it's an SX. I just read the Digital Semiconductor motherboard
>page and it looks like it might be an LX or something else.
>The systems I am considering are the Durango II or BlackHawk with a
>533Mhz 21164a processor.
>
> Is it worth getting 4MB of L3 cache? Where or how will I see a performance
>improvement with 4MB as compared to 2MB? Is it worth buying a board that
>I can later upgrade to 4MB?
>
The Durango II is a stock Digital Semi LX board. The Blackhawk (when it
ships) is a Pyxis chipset on a Aspen board design. PErformance differences
should be nil. Main difference in Blackhawk is 4 cache sockets and 6 RAM
slots. Biggest disadvantage on it is price.
Overall a nice design.
Definitely NOT SX. ANY board can be upgraded to 4MB, some ore easily than
others. Very few places out there with the equipment to do the surface
mount removal and installation of cache chips, tho'.
4MB DOES buy some performance.
