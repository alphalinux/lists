Joakim Roubert wrote:
>
> On Thu, 15 Jan 1998, Huff, J. Taylor wrote:
>
.....
> > OSLOADER=multi(0)disk(0)fdisk(0)\linload.exe
> > OSLOADPARTITION=multi(0)disk(0)fdisk(0)
> > OSLOADFILENAME=\milo
> > OSLOADOPTIONS=
>
> I don't have OSLOADFILENAME=\milo.

Well, then you do. :-) If you omit it then this is a 'linload' default.
It seems that you can skip also OSLOADPARTITION as linload will look
for milo in its own location.

The above is a very good thing as some "genius" wrote AlphaBIOS in
such manner that you cannot set these two unless OSLOADPARTITION
is big enough to hold the whole boot part (quite sizeable) of NT system.
You cannot possibly dream of booting something else, can you?
Defaults are running nicely around such stupidity.

> ... and then not to set any OSLOADOPTIONS

OSLOADOPTIONS is a good place to keep the whole default boot command,
which will be passed to milo, thus saving you all this typing and
making possible to boot your machine automatically on a power on.
