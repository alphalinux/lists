>The most basic question I have in all this, Why are we doing this
>to ourselves?

I don't know about the libs, but the basic answer to your
question about the compilers is that we are trying to
determine, by actually trying it out, whether a more
cooperative, open approach to developing a compiler will
be better than the approach that the gcc2 project has been
using for the past couple of years.

It is a confusing time, I admit, but I think that by mid-year,
the conclusion and direction will be fairly obvious to most
observers, and by the end of the year, if we can get all our
ducks in a row, there'll be really only one version of gcc
under development -- something that hasn't been true for many
years.

Read the various egcs announcements and mail archives, plus
USENET discussion I suppose, for other viewpoints, opinions,
and so on. <http://www.cygnus.com/egcs>, I suspect.
