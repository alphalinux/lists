>>> Christopher Curtis said:
>
> DEC is going to introduce a line of Alphas that will run ONLY Windows NT.

Hey, this isn't the first time; how many times have you heard me talk
about "true XLs", and "no SRM console available for XLs"?

> They, according to the article, are going to remove the firmware that
> would allow it to boot other OSes (including DEC UNIX and OpenVMS ... and
> presumably Linux).

Again, nothing new; so they won't develop an SRM console for it. And for a
*lot* of the DEC machines, there's never been a "proper" MILO anyway, as
MILO is based on PALcode developed for the Digital Semiconductor
motherboards. These boards may differ in very significant ways from their
conterparts, enough to make it (nearly) impossible to properly adapt to the
different hardware details. Does it make sense to run firmware on a box with
EISA when all that firmware knows is ISA? Or on an multiprocessor box when
it's uni all the way? Or on a box with multiple primary PCI busses when it
knows of only one?

> According to the report, it is doing this "at a time
> when it is struggling to grow Alpha product revenue."

Uh, when have we *not* been struggling? ;-}

> I think perhaps the Alpha may be dead afterall ...

Don't you believe it!

> So much for the price cuts ...

Do you think they'll be so cheap that everybody will want to buy them and
stop buying any of our other boxes? Do you believe they'll be in the $2-4K
range? Do you believe they will offer hardware/performance/whatever that
none of our other boxes do or will?

Not likely, IMHO.
