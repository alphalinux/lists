Holy bizarreness, Batman!

The major concern I'd have is whether or not you'll get decent context
switching times. Indications are that MVS systems (and their friends) like to
have big processes that just kind of keep running. The UNIX approach of
forking off processes for everything under the sun is liable to have some
unfortunate effects on performance.

On UNIX, things are "tuned" for having reasonable times to create new
processes. MVS, VMS, and NT are all noted for having somewhat "less
reasonable" costs for starting new processes. Which is a lot of the reason
why NT folk are really into threads; they *need* threading to get decent
performance...
