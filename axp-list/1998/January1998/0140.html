I have a UDB (running 4.2) that has two PCMCIA modems. They were designated
as ttyS2 and ttyS3 -- until today. The machine was brought up today and now
they come up as ttyS3 and ttyS16. I have no idea what changed. How do these
get assigned?

/var/run/stab is:
Socket 0: Serial or Modem Card
0 serial serial_cs 0 ttyS3 4 67
Socket 1: Serial or Modem Card
1 serial serial_cs 0 ttyS16 4 80

I have looked through config files, scripts, and the cardmgr source. I can't
figure out what happened to ttyS2 and where ttyS16 came from. If I delete
/dev/ttyS16, it just gets re-created.

It wouldn't matter except that mgetty and ppp are looking for ttyS2. If I
change everything to ttyS16, is this problem going to happen again?
