I don't recommend putting MILO into FLASH, as a general rule, for two major
reasons: 1) you can really mess your system up if something goes wrong, and
2) MILO was never intended to be the primary console software. Let me explain
a bit more about this last point...

The ARC/AlphaBIOS and SRM consoles for a given platform know a great deal
about the hardware details of that platform, and how to initialize it from
cold start. In general, the MILO code knows about certain major pieces of the
hardware and how to initialize them, but blissfully ignores many smaller
details, and assumes that they will have been correctly initialized by a
previous console.

For example, we use the same identical MILO for both UDB and NONAME platforms.
However, one of the low level HW details that differs between the two is that
the serial port interrupts on NONAME are edge-triggered (like most normal ISA
interrupts), but those on the UDB are level-triggered (like PCI interrupts).
MILO depends on the correct setup of those interrupts by the initial console
so that it doesn't have to know exactly which platform it is running on.

And, as a final argument, we (DEC) are no longer actively supporting the
ability to FLASH MILO. The original author of MILO and FMU, Dave Rusling,
is now concentrating on StrongARM related work, and no longer has the time
to devote to this. Due to an already heavy work-load, Jim and I will be
able to concentrate on maintenance and enhancement of MILO only, not FMU.

That's not to say that someone else couldn't pick it up; we'd be happy to
help support such an effort.
