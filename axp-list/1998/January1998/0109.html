> Is a multia always equal to a "UDB"?

Yes

>I would welcome any personal experience regarding the performance
>of the 266mhz UDB versus some of the other platforms running Linux.

The UDB has a crappy memory bus. At $800, (approximate street value)
you get what you pay for.

I _strongly_ urge you to look at spending $2000 instead, and getting
a 533Mhz 21164PC based system (PC164SX mainboard). You won't be
disappointed. Actually, let me phrase that differently:

When you use one, you'll be disappointed that you didn't spend the
extra $$$ to buy it.

Yeah, I'm biased; as a counter to that bias, it is worth noting that
I purchased a PC164LX system from DCG with my own money (no employee
discount) and am very happy with it. Running em86 on a UDB is painful.
Running em86 at 500+ Mhz is not a problem.
