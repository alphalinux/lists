Adrian Feiguin wrote:
>
> When compiling many applications on my Alpha with RH4.2 and egcs-1.0.1, I
> use to get the message:
>
> warning: cast from pointer to integer of different size
>
> Can anybody tell me why this happens, and if there is any flag to avoid
> the consequent compilation errors?

On PC's and most other boxes integers are 4 bytes and pointers are 4 bytes.
Now your not supposed to store a pointer value in an integer but it
won't do any harm on a PC because they are the same size. It is however
grossly horrid in most cases.

On the Alpha, pointers are 8 bytes long so if you try and squeeze them
into a 4 byte integer you will loose something somewhere - and probably
crash natsily if you get the pointer back and try and use it somewhere

In general the error means that the code is broken and should be fixed.
