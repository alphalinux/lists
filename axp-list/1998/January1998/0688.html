> Okay, this is far beyond my understanding. I never really understood MILO
> anyhow.

There's some reasonable treatises on MILO to be found at:

gatekeeper.dec.com:/pub/DEC/LInux-Alpha/Miniloader/docs

> Are you saying that it (in PeeCee speek) is essentially a BIOS?

Yes, well, sort of; best idea would be to read the PALcode section(s) of the
Alpha Architecture reference Manual, available at:

http://ftp.digital.com/pub/Digital/info/semiconductor/literature/dsc-library.html

> I always (and still) find it rather annoying that the hardware needs to be
> probed three times just to boot ...

Don't PCs get probed twice? The third time is just a side-effect of using
MILO to replace the ARC/Alphabios console. If you boot directly from SRM,
or happen to FLASH MILO to replace the console, it's only twice...
