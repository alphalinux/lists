Folks, problems don't exist when your cross-compiler host
long/void* size is same or longer than your target long/void*
size. The original question was running cross hosting on
32-bit SPARC with 64-bit Alpha as a target. Now THAT has
certain problems, especially in the optimizers which are
mighty important in the codec core routines.

I do know, I have done cross compilations over the years
between weird pairs of systems, though always with that
sizeof(target-long) <= sizeof(host-long) constraint.
