Ah! The "clue" part? When is a large organization ever totally clueful? ;-}

> I still don't see, though, how alienating non-NT users increases revenue.

Why does this alienate you? Did they say *anything* about LINUX? They only
mentioed UNIX and VMS, right? They don't *care* about LINUX, they care about
selling boxes into the NT market. Period. The desired perception is that DEC
is interested in competing head-to-head price-wise in the NT market, and that
the price is not "burdened" with UNIX/VMS "overhead".

> Well ... neither has there been such a PC, but most PCs are not *designed*
> to boot only DOS/Windows.

I'd argue that they were originally designed to boot only *DOS*; that they
boot *anything* else is a tribute to subsequent hardware tweaks and the
software writers... ;-}

> Is it that difficult to have a generic boot?

One could argue that the Alpha *core* is plenty generic; it's the only
RISC chip that is supported running UNIX *and* NT, isn't it? :-)

Tailoring the PALcode is (IMHO) ultimately the cheapest way to "enhance" the
core functionality of the chip, without burdening the actual hardware
overmuch with supporting the orthogonal views/needs of the operating systems.

Again, there's the official PALcode write-up in the Alpha Architecture RM,
to be found at (sorry it's so long):

http://ftp.digital.com/pub/Digital/info/semiconductor/literature/dsc-library.html
