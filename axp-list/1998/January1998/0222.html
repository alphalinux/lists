Stefan,

I think you missed the point, as well as sleeping in class when
syllogistic reasoning was discussed. The original writer was under the
impression that the cross-compiler had to have the concept of a 64bit
register on the host as well as the target. This is not the case. The
difference between the (32bit, say) host compiler and the (32->64)
cross-compiler is that the latter has a specially crafted code generator,
which reads the same RTL as the former but produces output that can be
assembled on the target. I believe the original writer was confusing an
emulator with a cross-compiler. (BTW, I have been writing compilers for
over 20 years, the last one I did was PL/I for DU on alpha, which was
bootstrapped by cross-compiling from Ultrix).

Books to read:
"Compilers, Priciples, Techniques and Tools" Aho, Sethi & Ullman
Addison Wesley 1986

"Engineering a Compiler" Anklam, Cutler, Heinen & MacLaren
Digital Press 1982 (Yes, same Cutler as NT)
