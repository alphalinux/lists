On Thu, 15 Jan 1998, Murray Stokely wrote:

> You should probably go with Intel for your budget. You can build a
> Pentium 200mhz+ MMX system with a nice video card, drives, etc for
> that kind of money (assuming you can use monitor, keyboard, mouse, etc
> from your 486).. All you could get on the alpha side is a UDB or
> cheap XL-266 or something.

For $500-$900 the best option would be to upgrade your Intel. You can
find all these prices online, but an AMD K6 with MMX (pooh-pooh intel)
costs around $180 ($210 with shipping, fan, etc) for a 233MHz or like
$120-$150 for the 200MHz; the Shuttle HOT-603 (with the AMD 640 chipset)
goes for like $130. (Cost so far: ~$300). A respectable 2MB video card
can be had for $50 (PCI: Intel/Alpha) and you can use your IDE HD. I
imagine you can use IDE in an Alpha, though our Alpha is SCSI. If you
have to get SCSI, it'll cost more, but I see 5GB EIDE drives going for
just over $200 now. (~$550 with the video card and drive). A 32MB DIMM
goes for, what, about $60 now? So with two 32MB DIMMs, and all the above,
you've about hit $700. I think memory costs more in the Alpha as well
because (at least ours) requires matched pairs of PARITY memory.

To try to return to an Alpha-based topic, what can one find a Alpha
CPU/MoBo for, and which MoBos support standard non-parity DIMMs (SDRAM?)
Their presence is not as prevalent as Intel on the 'Net.

Also, on another tangent, anyone seen the new Suns? You can get a
'Darwin' line Ultra-5 for $3000. Interesting thing is that it comes with
a 4GB *EIDE* drive, no SCSI at all, and a PCI bus.
