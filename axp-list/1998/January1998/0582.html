Harvey J. Stein wrote:
>
> I saw it mentioned on the EGCS mailing list that the haifa scheduler
> isn't helpful on the x86 platforms because egcs doesn't yet include
> any instruction or function unit info.
>
> Does anyone know whether this is also the case on the alpha platforms?
> In other words, with egcs on linux/alpha, which produces faster code -
> haifa enabled or haifa disabled?

I'm not sure - but on EGCS_971225 they moved to having Haifa enabled
by default for the Alpha platofrm; and 971225 was a fast release - it
gave me about 20% more than gcc 2.7.2.3 (although a lot of that may
be due to optimising for the 21164a).

Note however that 980115 is VERY slow for me - about 20% slower
than gcc 2.7.2.3!!
