OK Digital, chime in here. According to the press release and our
salespersons, and technical liasons at Digital, INTEL DOES NOT OWN ALPHA!
They purchased the chip foundry (for a tidy sum) and licensed the right to
use the technology. I don't think Intel can legally control the release of
Alpha components. Digital still owns Alpha and controls its destiny. As far
as the 800MHz goes, we are supposed to take (or have taken) delivery of an
8400 system in Texas with 8 of these beasties in it. Now if the part is
being produced by Mitsubishi (the 'PC' chip), then they may have decided to
not go into production with the chip. It is sadly true, that Digital is out
of the semiconductor manufacturing business, but not out of the
semiconductor development business. With access to multiple foundries,
Intel, Mitsubishi, Samsung etc., they will just have to farm out the
production of their designs. It could work out much to their favor (and
subsequently to ours). Rumor has it here in Richmond, that the turn of the
millenium will usher in the 1GHz Alpha (21264 or its heir) and a 4 way SMP
on a chip.
Besides when Wintoys NT 5 is released, there will be only one platform that
will run it in 64 bit mode out of the box. Guess which one that is! Digital
UNIX has long been recognized as one of the most technically advanced
'true' Unices available, so much so that it supposedly will be ported to
the new Intel 64bit mutt, (asuming it is ever produced) and sooner or
later, Linux ALPHA will be stable, be it from Redhat or someone else. I
think all of these will ensure the long life of Alpha technology and
Digital, (if they don't screw up and put a bozo in the chair's seat.)
