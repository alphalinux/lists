I hate to be the lone naysayer in all of this, but I am having a ton of
problems with the SX. I suspect that part of this is general RH5.0 stuff
related to moving to glibc and the fact that the board is very new and not
well supported. The only thing I have successfully compiled is the
kernel, and I've had problems with em86. KDE dies with a bunch of
protection faults, and I can't compile it from source. I also can't
compile XEmacs. I have been surprised with the low level of documentation
and development for this architecture. I also run Red Hat on a SPARC and
have had very few problems. If I am doing something wrong, PLEASE correct
me, I'd love to be wrong about this. Point me to something besides the
azstarnet FAQ for help or let me know where I can find info about helping
with development! All this aside, it is very fast with the software that
will run. BTW, I bought the SX building block from DCG and have installed
64M SDRAM, Western Digital 5.1 and 1.2 meg IDE drives, 24X IDE cdrom,
Matrox Millenium video, 3Com 3c509B ethernet, and Soundblaster 16
audio.
