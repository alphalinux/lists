On Fri, 16 Jan 1998, Todd Henderson wrote:

> Where do the Multia machines, like the one at the auction, someone
> pointed out, stand for power? Pentium class? 486 class?

Mine is roughly equivalent to about a P100 I have found, and it is
a UDB 166 (straight Multia), though it does have 64mb of RAM, which help
it quite a bit. For user interactive work, Xwindows, web browsing, word
processing (Lyx) it is a tad bit slower than my P100 with 32mb of RAM.
Though for databases (msql) and web servering, it beats out the P100 every
time, and can even beat my P133 laptop (16mb RAM) on database queries. So,
it would be safe to say it is P90 to P100 speed overall, with a very good
I/O subsystem.

> I take it they'll run Linux, will they run NT also, and(I know this
> is the wrong list), but how would they compare to the InHell
> machines with NT and FX!32?

Yea, UDBs will run NT if you really want them to. That is what
they were originally designed for, as small, relatively fast NT
workstations. The person I bought my UDB from, ran NT on it for sometime
with no problems, and I have a friend who runs NT on his UDB on a regular
basis, no problems. Don't know about speed under NT, but I think he once
said that running Intel apps on the UDB with FX!32 was not too bad at all.
That is after the multi-hour wait for FX!32 to do the initial conversion.
Anyway, I really like my UDB, and I don't plan to buy any more
intels anytime soon, just alphas. :) That is my two cents, hope it helps.
