On Sat, 17 Jan 1998, Alex Schoots en Judith van de Wint wrote:

> I've spend some time recently checking out what cards should work in the
> Multia, haven't bought & tried one yet, but came to the conclusion that a
> Diamond "Stealth64 Video 3240XL 2MB VRAM" (or the 3400XL with 4MB VRAM) is
> the one I would go for. Several sources of information pointed in this
> direction, although Ryan's experience obviously doesn't. Can someone else
> pop in and help us out :-)

The specific card I was unable to get working was a Diamond
Stealth 64 Video VRAM, about 2.5 years old now. It was based on the S3 968
chip. I still have the card, as it is the video card for my P100, and
there are only a few quirks with it under X-windows. But I remeber I had a
heck of a time getting it working under Linux when I first got it. It was
one of those cards that just did not like working with any drivers, except
specific ones, straight from Diamond. So you milage with other Stealth 64
video cards may vary, and I hope it does. Also, this card is basically too
large (physically) for the UDB anyway. Sorry for the confusion.
