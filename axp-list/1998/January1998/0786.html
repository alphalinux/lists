*That* is frankly more interesting than all the other issues.

"Blah, blah, blah, economies of scale..."

"Deal with AMD..."

Hmmm...

It's not clear from AMD's web site just what the relationship between the K7
and the Alpha EV-6; it would *appear* that Digital sold some technology to
AMD relating to "bus protocols." Which is an absolutely *fascinating*
idea, as it allows AMD to "eat into" Intel.

It's not 100% clear; this might very well be Digital using the "fruits" of
the lawsuit settlement to indirectly injure Intel by reselling what they got
from Intel to AMD. That's speculative on my part, and could very well be
entirely incorrect; it is just as likely that Digital is using their
ownership of the technologies that they themselves developed, and is, in
effect, selling weapons to *two* sets of "enemies." In any case, hopefully
this makes Digital some money and causes consternation at Intel...

It would be most gratifying for there to become some *real* cheap Alpha
motherboards come out of the dealings. And even more wonderful if some
joint ventures between AMD and Digital turned into a more "open" SMP
standard thus making both AMD K7 SMP systems available and cheap, as well as
the same for Digital Alpha SMP systems. And that completes the circle;
*Compaq* might also find these sorts of combinations attractive...
