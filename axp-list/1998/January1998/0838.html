Sounds to me to much like Novell's aquisition of USL when they decided
they needed Unix. They never quite got their corperate heads around the
non-pc platforms and were distracted from the real threat to their market
for rather a long time.

Compaq are an 'upstart' PC cloner. Yes, being the first has given them
a strong legitamacy, but still they are basicly a PC clone manufacturer.
I can see they want Digital's high end NT alpha servers - with NT moving
up in the word, they need to sell serious power for the servers.
What I cant see Compaq coping so well with, in much the same way as
Novell is all Digitals non PC, non wintel product, the semiconductor design,
Digital Unix and expecially the VMS group. A lot of stuff other than the
alpha NT division are a long way from Compaq's core area of expertise.
We will have to wait and see, but I certainly cant see Compaq being
able to hold together divisions so far removed from their business wintel
PC and server markets.
