>Interesting point. Obviously the shoes were not yours and therefore
>stolen. However, there is a legal principal pertaining to intellectual
>property, and that is, that if you do not act to protect your rights you
>essentially abdicate them, meaning that it becomes public domain. Thus
>you get a free license.

Uh, care to *identify* that legal principal? Or is it one you
just made up? Then again, please don't, not here, because
this thread is *completely* irrelevant to Alpha/Linux issues.

I suggest this thread end here, and anyone who actually wants to
know the *facts* can go read the Copyright FAQ, or go to one of
them old-fangled buildings called a "library", find a few books
on copyright law, and read them. Make sure you read sufficiently
recent information -- if it doesn't mention changes to the US
copyright laws since 1970 or so ("Berne Convention" is one phrase
that comes to mind), it's probably too old.

IANAL, but as far as I know, the only "intellectual-property"
law that approximates the idea that "if you don't act to protect
your rights [...your property] becomes public domain" is the law
that pertain to *trademarks*. Not copyright. Nor patents, for
that matter. And even with trademarks, "public domain" is a
very short-lived status. (Ask the Linux people about the trademark
status and history of the name "Linux" for just one example.)

But *please* don't argue this out here. I'm posting *only* to
provide a counter to gratuitous misinformation. If you want to
argue about it, first *read* something definitive on the topic,
as I've done. Then, consult a qualified intellectual-property
*lawyer* to find out whether your theories about how the law
works are even close to the truth. After all that work, I hope
you'll know enough to refrain from posting misinformation, and
have sufficient appreciation for the availability of the online
materials (Copyright FAQ) and proper forum (*not* axp-list) such
that you'll point people to those and take any arguments you have
up with *them*, not *us*.
