On Wed, 14 Jan 1998, Michael Brennen wrote:

>
> Several months ago I had two UDBs fail w/in a couple of weeks of each
> other exactly this way. After weeks of working with this I traced it
> down to bad p/s to m/b connectors. The greed leads carry 3.3 volts to
> the chip, and the plastic on the connector had discolored from the
> resistive connection. Rather than pay DEC ~$130 each for replacement
> p/s, I nipped off the bad connectors and butt spliced others on. I
> had to settle for cutting up p/s extension cords as I could not find
> the connectors and pins as piece parts. They haven't failed once
> since then.
>
> -- Michael

I can second this solution as correct. My UDB started flaking out
on me shortly after the start of the semester. It would be working just
fine one second, and the next deader than a doornail, for no apparent
reason. I suspected heat, and taped two 24V fans to the air vents, and ran
them while the machine was online. The UDB then stayed up for 1.5 days,
and then out it went again. I found reference to the above procedure in
the email archives, and in closely examining the MB, I found that the
plastic around the two green leads was discolored. I took the connector
apart, spread the connector springs, stratched up the surface of the
connectors on the end of the wires, and put the entire thing back
together. The system came up fine and has been up for 3+ days, no problem.
I still have the fans on it though.
I suspect that the contact between the green wires and the board
generates a lot of heat, and the metal breaks down due to the heat and
create corrosion, which does not conduct electricty very well at all. So
either replace them (what I will do if it fails again) or just get the
black corrosion off and you should be set to go.
The UDB is an excellent little machine with a decent amount of
kick, especially for the size and price. Just requires a bit of love
and care. :) Good Luck!
