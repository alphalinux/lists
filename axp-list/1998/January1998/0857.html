W. Kyle Gilbertson wrote:
>
> I've received many replies to this situation, and essentialy the following
> is true:
>
> Software RAID is supported on Alpha Linux w/ kernel 2.0.30, ... etc. RH5.0
> The Switch to turn on Software RAID is under "Floppy, IDE, and other Block
> Devices" section of the kernel config file. One must turn on "Multiple
> devices driver support", and select one's desired RAID level, JBOD or 0.
> Unfortunately, I was asking for mirroring (RAID1). This does not appear
> to come stock with RH 5.0.
>
> It is my understanding that RAID level 4 and 5 are running to some degree
> on Intel Linux, but this is the AXP list which at one time was a synonym
> for "Alpha List"... as in the "Alpha AXP Architecture Manual".

I seem to remember that the higher levels of RAID were only working in the
latest kernels; so I think the answer is to get yourself a nice new shiny
kernel.

I'd be reasonably surprised if there was a problem with it on Alpha if it
works on Intel.
