It is and has always been my opinion that McAffee sucks, sorry. I would
never use that software again. But do remember that their paranoia is
primarily just that: Parinoia. The most effective method of averting a
virus infestation is education. Do not load software downlaoded from a
BBS. Only FTP software from trusted sites (eg, a vendor). Disable the
"Boot from Drive A:" feature. And read the page about virus hoaxes (eg,
"Good Times", etc). Don't execute code that performs an n-th level binary
loop and burns up your cpu (yea, whatever...)

Today, there are really only two significant virus threats. One is false
virus threats that create panic (don't read this email or it'll erase your
hard drive!); the other is Micro$oft Word macro viruses. These are the
most common and most insidious viruses today. Actually, the first threat
can be real. Many MS programs use MS Word as an email reader (overkill?)
and these people will be vulnerable. I think right now the best option is
to use Netscape to read mail, and get a virus scanner that is also a
Netscape plugin. That will protect you not only from malevolant ActiveX
controls, but also email bourne virii.

Names that I liked in the past (I've also been OS/2 and Linux for several
years now, so these may be dated) are F-Prot, ThunderByte, and Dr.
Solomon. At least one of these has a Netscape plugin version.
