Hello!

On Thu, 19 Aug 1999 22:10:32 -0400, Ken Shiring wrote:
> It is very surprising to me to see messages posted on the topic of turning
> the DEC 3000's into useful machines again ;).

Just good, clean fun :)

> Can anyone tell me what happened to the old(er) effort led by Michael
> Engel? When I actually managed to reach him earlier this year, he said
> that their group's efforts included a port to the DEC 3000 series as well.
> His port page can be found at http://decstation.unix-ag.org .

I had contacted Michael Engel back in April, though I have not been in
touch with him since. At the time, he told me he had tried aboot and
bootp on a couple of DEC 3000s and had run into trouble. Certainly
the DECstation work he's been a part of is highly relavent to the DEC
3000 (turbochannel, same hardware).

And yes, indeed - their web site is *quite* useful. Got copies of many
docs there. And the DECstation port is now entering the stable kernel
- a bunch of it appeared for the first time in the kernel proper with
2.2.11 (at least was in -pre). Myself, I picked up a couple of
DECstations just so I could give it a try - and it might be useful for
a DEC 3000 port to "see" the turbochannel code "in action", too.

The plan for me was to use as much of the DECstation code as possible;
no need to repeat their hard work. From what I've seen so far, that
is clearly possible.

<snip>
> If this latest effort is indeed different from Michael's team, good luck to
> you. I have been wholly unsuccessful in contacting anyone from Michael's
> team for more than one email. Perhaps he is too busy with his port for the
> likes of me :> ...?

Thanks for the well-wishes :) With the aboot problem licked (patch)
and bootp working (a few magic entries in /etc/bootptab), it would be
a good idea to send Michael a mail. Will do.

> Let me also say that I have acquired a DECstation 3000 model 300LX, and
> will happily serve as a guinea pig for anything you have to try. Due to an
> unfortunate agreement I had to sign with my employer, I cannot participate
> in actual DEVELOPMENT of the kernel for the DEC 3000, which I would dearly
> like to help with since I have quite a bit of hardware programming
> experience. Therefore I must resign myself to the lowly position of
> alpha-tester :(.

That is too bad about your agreement, 'cuz I don't know diddly :)
Well, maybe it isn't *that* bad, but I am still climbing the
kernel/alpha learning curve(s). I'll get there... (this list is
proving to be a great resource).

With any luck, you'll have something to try soon. I hope. At least
it'll boot (it doesn't yet).

> If anyone knows more about port efforts for the DEC 3000, by all means post
> to this list and explain.

Here's one, and it'd be great if there were others! For this effort,
I've hooked up with a like-minded fellow, George Armhold. And for
anyone who's interested, we both have web pages, updated
semi-regularly (hopefully more often in the future):

http://www.phys.ufl.edu/~prescott/linux/alpha/dec3000-port.html
http://www.cs.rutgers.edu/~armhold/linux/alpha-3000.html

Unfortunately, we're not too far yet... but I was honestly surprised
at how far one can get with just some Makefile mods and a few hacks -
the alpha maintainers deserve muchos kudos. We'll get there - wish I
could say when.

Cheers,
Craig

P.S. In the meantime, if you wanted to put something besides DEC Unix on
your 3000/300LX, you could try NetBSD.
