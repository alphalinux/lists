Use the kernel image from generic.img and try the updated redhat ramdisk
at
ftp://ftp.cc.gatech.edu/pub/Linux/distributions/redhat/updates/6.0/images/alpha

Or you can just try using Redhat5.2 ramdisk.
BTW, the two numbers can not be 08:02. That represents the second
partition of the first hard disk. But you should have specified floppy as
your root.
