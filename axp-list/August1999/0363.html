Peter Petrakis wrote:
>
> I've seen this exact same behavior on my LX with a ncr53c895. It was the
> result of a bad U2W hardrive.
>

Humm. I've got 3 U2W's and an UW on the card. They
work fine on 2.2.3 so I kind of doubt it's the HD's.

Also, note the <0,*> part of the message. That's the
controller, not a drive.

ncr53c895-0: command processing resumed
ncr53c895-0-<0,*>: WIDE SCSI (16 bit) enabled.
ncr53c895-0-<0,*>: FAST-20 WIDE SCSI 40.0 MB/s (50 ns, offset 15)
            -----

I have seen it complain about a specific drive
but the notation above changes and shows the scsi id
of the drive. That happens occasionally when a drive
doesn't spin up on it's own. I just power the disk
off and back on to get it spinning.

> |
> | > /* wsb */
> | > ms=ms*2;
> |
> | You know that this is safe or it just happens to work?
> |

I came to this solution after booting with "ncr53c8xx=verb:2"
on my 2.2.3 kernel and seeing how it computed the number of
msec's it used that ALWAYS worked with that kernel. If it
helps, I tried this on a couple different kernels and it
seems to behave OK. (2.2.5 and 2.2.11)

Basically, the routine kept getting 80mhz and it would not
work. Of course, this may be due to my mixing U2W/UW drives
but the 53c895 with transceiver is supposed to handle that
correctly. Also, I'm using a SE active terminator on both
sides of the bus so it really can't run U2W LVD. So, it's
possible the 895 and U2W disks are attempting to negotiate
a 80MB/sec handshake and since the terminator doesn't support
it, it's hanging. For, now, I'll leave it in. When I get a
U2W LVD terminator, I'll try that but the same problem occurs
on a 53c875 so I'm not hopeful that's the problem.

As usual, YMMV.

System is still running at the moment with this change but
I won't be stressing it until latter today.
