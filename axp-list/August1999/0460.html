Note also for all working on this latest port effort that they have many

Just for fun, let me throw in what I know (and think I know) about the
history behind the DECstations. Please know that I am not certain about
all of this, but am reasonably certain about most of it :).

<history>

It seems the 5000 series came first. It is based on the MIPS CPU
architecture, but surprisingly has such a similar design to the 3000 series
that they can almost be considered the same, even from a driver's-eye-view.
 The only appreciable difference it seems is that your compile target is
MIPS and not Alpha. Every other device in the box seems to be wired the
exact same way as the 3000's.

MIPS came first because DEC (now Compaq) had not yet released the first
Alphas. At the time of DEC 5000, DEC was a member of the MIPS consortium
(along with IBM and SGI). However, shortly after the DEC 5000, DEC pulled
out of MIPS and released its own CPU, the Alpha 21064. DEC promptly
changed the CPU on the DECstations to Alpha, and the new boxes were called
the 3000 series.

What's even more surprising is that there is PALcode in the 3000 series
which will enable them to execute native MIPS instructions. DEC actually
left the system Flash exactly the same (using MIPS instructions), and just
tweaked the PALcode Flash to perfectly emulate a MIPS. So when you boot a
3000 (assuming you haven't re-flashed the board), the CPU initially
executes MIPS code, which theoretically is the exact same boot code on the
5000 series.

</history>
