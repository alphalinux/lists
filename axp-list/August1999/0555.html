I got one of those babies, too so I'll try :-)

>
>Hi everybody. I'm new to alphas, but not linux. I just got one of the
>multias from linuxstore.com, and I'm having a terrible time getting things
>setup. I can't get the thing to boot at all. I'm hoping this lists members
>might be of some help. I put a 2G SCSI disk in it, got what I was told is
>parity memory SIMMS, and a dec floppy drive for it. I see no power
>connector for the floppy drive. I connected it via the tape; is that all?
Yes, that is all and that works for me.

>Where does the floppy drive get its power? Also, how can I tell, by
>looking at the sticks, whether or not I have "true parity" memory? Would
If there are 9 ICs on it it is parity (8bit + parity bit).

>it be easier to setup an IDE drive? I don't see how the 2.5" laptop IDE
>drives would get power unless it is from the cable on the motherboard.
And that is what it does. That is the exact reason it uses a 44pin IDE cable
instead of the regular 40pin cable.

>
>When I try to boot this thing, I can hear the SCSI drive crank once, then
>nothing happens. My monitor appears to be receiving no signal from the
>multia video port - the light on the monitor goes orange from green. The
>floppy drive doesn't appear to be accessed at all. Where do I start my
>troubleshooting?
>
What distribution do you use? RedHat 5.2, 6.0? How do you attempt to boot it?
There are instructions on www.thelinuxstore.com regarding how to deal with
booting and setting up the Multia with RH5.2. That info helped me.

It is a three floppy process, ie. a MILO floppy, a boot floppy and a root
floppy, but before you even attempt that you of course needs a signal on your
monitor :-)

The first monitor I attempted to use wasn't able to sync on the signal that
came out (I think it was 1280x1152, but I could be wrong), but then I got a
faster one and then I got a signal. Typing "arc <enter>" at the >>> prompt
got me started with what looks like a textscreen and from there you must set
up the NVRAM to contain info about how to boot from the floppy, and that is
described in one of the documents on www.thelinuxstore.com.

I hope this helped, and you may return if/when you need more info :-)
