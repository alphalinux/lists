With regards to your message at 01:01 PM 9/5/99 -0700, Jeff DeMaagd. Where
you stated:

>I have a 4 GB hard drive that is failing, and this is motivating me to
>blow some good cash to get my system set up the way it should be.
>
>I presently have an Adaptec 2940UW and either a 2940U or just a plain
>2940 - I forget which for the moment. I am considering replacing my UW
>for a U2W and buying two 9 gig U2W drives.
>
>Are there any 64 bit PCI SCSI cards that work in a Miata's 64 bit
>slots? I know the normal 32 bit Adaptecs in either of those slots set
>the firmware off, saying it isn't a supported configuration.
Not many. The Intraserver BAB is compatible. It has dual LVD, dual Intel
ethernet, and a Permedia2v video with 8MB all on one LARGE card (BAB is the
nickname for "Big Ass Board").
Really, though, there is not much real need for 64 bit for this.

As for hardware PCI RAID cards the Mylex DAC960 works well. See Leonard
Zubkoffs drivers for Mylex/Buslogic.

And, finally, RAID on 2 drives is an expensive way to do things. I would
recommend just buying a good backup device.

The only efficient use of space in terms of drive usage is a RAID5 setup
and to use that effectively you need at least 3 drives, and 4 for redundancy.
