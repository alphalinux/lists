I originally installed rh 6.0 on an alphastation 200/233 (avanti) but I
recently upgraded to a pc164 366. I decided to rebuild egcs-1.1.2 to take
advantage of my architecture (ev56). I ftp'd the source code, made it, and
it seems to work fine. With one exception.
  It will not compile a working kernel. They all lock up around the scsi
detection stage. The stock redhat egcs works fine. I patched egcs to
gcc-2.95, and it does the same thing. I read the FAQ and included the
-fno-strict-aliasing addition to the CFLAGS variable in the makefile -
same thing.
 Has anyone seen this before? I'm using kernel 2.2.10 and I have a
ncr53c8xx card.
