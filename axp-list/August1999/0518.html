The electrics in the UDB floppy interface are not pin-compatible with a
34-pin 3.5 floppy drive but if you have a schematic, you could remap the
pins on a cable into a floppy as you said.

I imagine most people make a boot disk with disk partitioning utilities and
do an FTP install. It's available on one of the first few installation
screens. You just need another machine running *ftpd with the CD mounted
as a directory or copied over.
