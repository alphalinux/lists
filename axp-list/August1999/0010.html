Christopher T. Lansdown wrote:
>
> Has anyone tried out gcc 2.95?

You mean release, not the previous snapshots? Yes, I did but I am not
the only one.

> Any stories of how well/poorly it works on
> the Alpha?

Well, my Alpha is running on 2.95 compiled kernel (2.2.10-ac12 -
basically) and nothing crashed yet. :-)

Somebody reported on this list that various problems with programs
like ghostscript, and not only, which were doing strange things in
RH 6.0 distribution, are gone after a recompilation with 2.95.

In first impression it seems to deal much better with denormalized
floating point numbers and it has a separate set of libraries for
cases when you are forced to compile with -mieee flag. C++ compiler
is stricter in following ANSI standard and some things which would
be swallowed previously now are causing compilation failures (not
without a reason).
