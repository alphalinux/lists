And also vs. assorted tests, branches and maybe calls. Take that

>
> I agree, core's are useful.

If what I wrote suggested something opposite that I did not express
myself clearly. I meant that cores are not something you cannot live
without. Nice to have sometimes but hardly crucial; still this should
be fixed.

The problem with a mythical "customer" sending a core is that often
they will have either 'ulimit -c' set to something and the "interesting"
part invariably will be in this unrecorded part. :-) Or they will
have core sizes unlimited and with many troublesome programs and
nowadays memories this will create such huge file that it will be hardly
feasible to look at it - not mentioning sending it around.

When I was consulting for some outfit with commercial support contracts
for their "professional" software nobody from suppliers ever wanted
to peek at core files they were collecting at an alarming rate. :-)
