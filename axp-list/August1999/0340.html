A follow up on my latest parts.

I've tested 2 Tekram DC390F's, a Tekram DC390U2W, and a
Digitalscape Datastorm32. Results:

DC390F: SRM recognizes it and it's disks. Does not like
        the fact my cables exceed UW length (3m, I'm around 3.5m)
        and occasionally does SCSI bus resets. A 53c875
        based card, cost was $75.
DC390U2W: SRM doesn't recognize it's devices. Handles my
        cables being overlength because it uses a special
        transceiver chip to allow separating UW from U2W.
        A 53c895 based card, cost was $168.
DataStorm32: SRM doesn't recognize it's devices. Does not
        like my overlength cabling. A 53c895 based card
        but doesn't use the transceiver chip, cost was $138.

Also:
Fireport40: SRM doesn't recognize it's devices. Does not
        like overlength cables. A 53c875J based card. I
        forgot what I paid for it.

All cards are recognized by Linux. The issue is whether SRM
sees them.

I still have the Qlogic 1040B differential card and the
IWILL 2935UW card to try. After looking over the IWILL
though, I'm betting it won't work, but I'll still try it.
It appears to be based on an Initio INIC-950P chip. Their
web site does say it works with Linux so it's probably OK
with Intel systems. Anyway, It's looking like the Tekram
DC390F is the way to go if you use SRM.

A side note:

One thing I read about the Qlogic driver that may explain
the problem I had while fixating with my Yamaha CDR-RW is
that it doesn't support disconnect. Also, under SRM, the
Yamaha CDR-RW shows devices for ALL LUN's at it's SCSI id.
I suspect that may give some SCSI drivers trouble.
