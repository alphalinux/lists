http://www.seagate.com/support/disc/specs/st91685ag.shtml

check out this link for info about the drive connector -- I've never heard
of such a thing, but the pictures here should make it fairly easy to build a
converter. the only thing you have to be careful about is to use the 5 volt
part of the drive connector to hook into those other 4 pins, and NOT the 12
volt -- twelve volt would fry the drive from what the specs say. pins 41 and
42 should have 5vdc on them and pin 43 is your ground. pin 44 is not used
(it says 'reserved' in the spec sheet).
