On Thu, 26 Aug 1999, David Webb wrote:

>
>
> Hi,
>
> I'm using an lx164 with the 2.2.11 kernel.
> I'm going to be installing a Quantum fireball plus
> Ultra66/33 hard drive in the system. I know that I'll
> need a Ultra66 controller card, cable, and kernel
> patch if I want the drive to transfer data at it's
> highest speed. What I don't know is weather the
> on-board eide controller will work as an Ultra33
> controller. If it will, then I may not bother with the
> Ultra66 card and cable. The motherboard manual calls
> the on-board controller an eide controller. I don't
> know if this means that it is not an Ultra33
> controller. Is there a HOWTO or web site that someone
> can point me in the direction of that talks about
> this?

As I posted to the group the other day I have an Ultra66 drive and Ultra66
controller in my LX164.

The CMD646 will not do Ultra (or at least there is a revision of the chip
which will but the one in my LX164 is older than that - and there is some
doubt about even the newer versions being able to do it)

The Promise Ultra66 card does work on Alpha/Linux - I have the 2.3.14
kernel built for it; but don't expect AlphaBIOS to see the drive on it or
boot off it.

However - and it is a big however! - for me at least the CMD646 on the
motherboard seems to be faster than the Promise Ultra/66 card. On my IBM
34GB 7200RPM Ultra66 drive I get approximatly 12.5MB/s off the CMD646 and
9.5MB/s off the promise Ultra/66 (measured using both hdparm and a 640MB
dd to /dev/null)

Obviously something odd is going on here - I think I'd take bets at it
being a problem in the Promise Driver in Linux.
