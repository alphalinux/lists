On Wed, 1 Sep 1999, Ken Crandall wrote:

> OSS works on the Alpha, with the caveat that the hardware must be
> "alpha-ready". A great deal of sound cards make some 32-bit assumptions
> and don't play well in 64-bit land.

Hmm. Why should this be a problem? Since you "contact" a soundcard by
using the kernel's I/O functions, the hardware should not even notice that
it is applied to a 64bit machine.
I heard of problems with big/little endian mixed hardware, though, since
if you transfer data, you don't always know the exact boundaries and so
can't determine how to flip bytes.

Correct me, if I am wrong...
