> Now, I'm a bit curious about what the kernel (2.2.11) is
> trying to tell me about the EIDE ports:
>
> PCI_IDE: unknown IDE controller on PCI bus 00 device 68, VID=10b9, DID=5229
> PCI_IDE: not 100% native mode: will probe irqs later
> PCI_IDE: simplex device: DMA disabled
> ide0: PCI_IDE Bus-Master DMA disabled (BIOS)
> PCI_IDE: simplex device: DMA disabled
> ide1: PCI_IDE Bus-Master DMA disabled (BIOS)
> hdc: COMPAQ CDR-8435, ATAPI CDROM drive

cat /proc/pci to see what kind of IDE controller it thinks it
has. Normally it's part of the ISA bridge chipset... here's what it is
on the XP1000:

  Bus 0, device 7, function 2:
    IDE interface: Contaq 82C693 (rev 0).
      Medium devsel. Fast back-to-back capable. IRQ 239. Master
Capable. No bursts.
      I/O at 0x170 [0x171].
      I/O at 0x374 [0x375].
      Non-prefetchable 32 bit memory at 0x1080000 [0x1080000].

If there is no driver, you can ask the IDE Guy about it...
