amd is the "automounter" deamon. If you're on a home system, I doubt you
need it. In this case, if you needed it, you'd know what it does. :)
#############################################
Want an easy way?

rpm -qa | grep amd

When it lists the amd pkg...

rpm -e <amd pkg>
        It'll rempove itself from all the run-levels.

then: ps -ef | grep amd (to get the PID)
kill <pid> (to kill the daemon.

there's a longer harder way to do this.. but the above will work (the
purists won't like it though..

good luck!

        Ric
