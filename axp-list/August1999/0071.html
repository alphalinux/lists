I finally resolved it.. it was a stupid thing... it turn out that on the DC
switch (power button) there is another switch that will enable/disable the
power button. And that switch is on if the case is closed. There is this
little metal that sticks out and suppose to enable this switch, and all I
have to do is tighten the screw really tight until i hear the click on the
switch, then the power button will be enabled.
