On Fri, Jul 30, 1999 at 02:39:21PM -0400, Christopher T. Lansdown wrote:
> Is there a speed difference between dealing with ints and dealing
> with longs on the Alpha?

Yes. It ought to be small, but present. Comparisons, for instance,
have to be done as longs; most of the time the compiler can figure
out that the ldl or addl has produced a proplerly sign-extended value,
but sometimes not.

> Oh, on another note, how much of a speed hit is a cast from
> int (long) to float? Thanks.

ev6 is relatively cheap -- one gr->fr move, and then one convert
insn. Probably 5 cycles latency max. Pre-ev6, no gr->fr move so
have to store+load from memory. Call it 9 cycles?
