Thanks all,
        I received many suggestions (I owe everyone involved many thanks)
But in the end it seems that one of 2 things is true:

1. The motherboard, pci bus or similiar was damaged.

2. The SYM53c896 isn't 64 bit clean, or doesn't like being behind a
    PCI bridge or similiar.

I base my conclusions on trying endless combos of:

1. Linux-2.2.3 (generic-up-223)+ redhat-5.2 ramdisk.img (since 2.2.3
    doesn't handle romfs).

2. same with the rh6.0 generic.img + rh6.0 errate update for ramdisk.

3. Linux-2.2.11 + redhat-6.0 ramdisk.img on cd (newer kernel has romfs).

4. Linux-2.2.11 + redhat-6.0 errate update for ramdisk.img.

5. Linux-2.3.11+rth1+rth2 + redhat ramdisk errata version

6. Most kernels + compatible ramdisk + 4 GB eide disk... everything
        seemed to work great. Did full install and left the machine
        running for a day or so.

All the above either failed miserably because of not having a rom fs,
command line mistakes, or other compatibilities. But by the time I was
done I had many "bootable" combos that would fail after installing
10-20 packages (after selecting timezone, packages to install, mke2fs,
and similiar). I also noticed that the NCR53c896 driver actually
has reasonable parity error reports, while the SYM driver
seems to have less accurate error reports.

I then was guessing that my cable setup was wrong, or that the
cable or terminators were bad. My first step was to transfer the
controller, cable, disk, and terminator to an x86, which worked
great, did a full redhat-6.0 install quickly with no errors/locks or
similiar. The full redhat-6.0 is >= 500 packages I believe the alphas
were dying before package 50...

So that leaves me with the driver not liking alphas, pci bridges,
or similiar or broken hardware. I'll call the vendor tomorrow. I'm
open to suggestions for anything else to try, I have an older 21164 as
well as many x86's to help.

Oh I did get a statement from the vendor:
>This machine [DS-10] has been downgraded to RedHat 5.2
>
>Kernels built on RH6.0 (egcs-1.1.2) will not boot with the Symbios 53c896
>
>Kernels built on RH 5.2 (egcs-1.0.3a) do not demonstrate this problem.
>
>The appropiate people at Compaq, RedHat and intraserver have been made
>aware of this problem and a fix will be made available.

Anyone know if this is true, people emailed they had similiar setups,
although I don't think anyone explicitly said the SYM53C896 (dual
channel lvd) works great with Redhat-6.0 on alpha, many said they
have similiar setups working.

Any clarification greatly appreciated, as is any addiitonal troubleshooting
ideas before I call the vendor.

Many thanks
