        Quick question for those people knowledgable about CD-Rs and
Linux/Alpha. I am looking at getting a Sony CDU-948S, what looks like a
good quality, yet relatively inexpensive, CD-R. Now, I know that this
drive is supported by Linux in general (as from the CD Writing HOWTO).
But, as many of you probably know, just because a piece of hardware is
supported by Linux, does not mean that is will work with an Alpha (i.e.
BusLogic Flashpoint SCSI cards...). So, my question is... Does this drive,
the Sony CDU-948S work (and how well) under Linux/Alpha? If not, what is
another good drive to look into that meets these requirements?
        Just to give you a few quick specs on my system: XLT 366, Debian
2.1, and the drive would be connected (along with other HDs, tape drive,
and cdrom drives) to the onboard NCR SCSI controller.
        Thanks for any help you can give me.

        PS. I am not interested in RW capablities.
