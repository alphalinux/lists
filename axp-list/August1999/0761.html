This is from memory: Q7 D1 A220, for 16bit DMA D5 I think, or try
piggy back on D1. Use the kernel's Soundblaster 16 driver. The chip
is mostly SB16 compatible, and that driver was set up to handle the
little quirks of several different 'compatible' chips.

I believe this worked on both the 2.0.x and 2.2.x series kernels, I
haven't tried 2.1.x or 2.3.x.

For those looking for specific docs on particular machines, look in:

http://www.alphalinux.org/docs/

For the PWS owners:
http://members.brabant.chello.nl/~s.vandereijk/miata.html

The instructions and information are even more detailed than last time
I checked. Wow, even my Miata overclock information is there.
