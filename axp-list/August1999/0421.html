On Tue, Aug 17, 1999 at 01:18:25PM -0600, Michal Jaegermann wrote:
> I got these with gcc-2.95 and 2.2.11-ac3 but these were warnings
> and a kernel did compile and booted.

The warnings are natural. They result from macro expansion of

        if (3 < 64)
            ... something << 3
        else
            ... something else << (3 - 64)

where the second never-to-be-executed statement has a negative
shift count. The warning, unfortunately, doesn't consider such
"obvious" non execute cases.

The original report had a cc1 core dump though.
