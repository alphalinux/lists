Hi geoff

Disconnect the drives and then turn it on. There may be a problem with
that. If you don't have parity memory, the system will not function or
test the memory. Try looking on the SIMMs themselves -- you should see a
multiple of 3 chips (i.e. usually 9, 12 or 15). Often the tag chip (the
parity) will be a faster speed. If you see a silkscreen number on the SIMM
(Not on the chips) that has a "36" or "x36" you are in business.

If you power up the system with RAM and no drives and nothing happens,
leave it on and let it sit for a while. Type "arc" and press enter. There
may be something wrong with the video that going into arc will reveal.

The Multia's video is hard set -- make sure your monitor is compatible.
The UDB will not synch with a monitor, has to be the other way round.
There are jumpers on the main board.

If you still get nothing, you should try to change the memory or get it
tested to ensure parity. Also, if you have the PCI slot, you can add a
video card and see if you get anything. Most cards that don't use funky
addressing can be supported using VGA BIOS emulation in the Multia.
Doesn't guarantee that your OS will support them, but at least you'll get
some video if something is up with your onboard chip.

good luck

izaak
