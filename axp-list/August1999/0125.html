On Fri, Aug 06, 1999 at 11:07:30AM -0700, Mark Henry wrote:
> Dear All,
>
> In preparing to add support for the x86 emulator module, I've downloaded the
> latest kernel source and am going through the 100 odd pages of kernel
> options - agggggh! - so I don't include too much unnecessary stuff (i've
> never recompiled the kernel before...)

        Any 2.2.* series kernel is ok, if you just include EM86
        kernel compilation option, all will be fine.

> I've taken the machine apart and have a good idea of whats under the hood in
> terms of hardware but there are a lot of kernel options that I'm not sure
> about - such as DMA and bus mastering IDE devices etc.

        "Generic PCI IDE" is the way to go - DMA might not work, though..

> I've tried to search Digital/Compaq's site for the exact specs on the
> machine but their site is extremely difficult to find anything on - at least
> I can't locate any PWS 433a specs.
>
> Does anyone have such info. or know exactly where I can go for this?

        Copy my .config file from:
ftp://mea.tmt.tele.fi/linux/linux-2.3.13-alpha-DECPWS433a.dot-config

        Just drop it into your /usr/src/linux/ directory, and
        then execute 'make oldconfig'. (Rename it to ".config",
        of course.)

        Then just normally: make dep clean; make boot modules
        then do the kernel installation.

        I have Adaptec SCSI card, I don't know what you have.

        That .config *can* be used for 2.2 kernels as well.
        Some questions may present themselves, or some answers
        are ignored, but that is not a big thing.

> Many thanks,
> Mark
