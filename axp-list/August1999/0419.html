I've used two different 895 based cards. I've had timeout
problems depending on which kernel level I use. The issue
seems to be that the sym/ncr drivers detect incorrect
frequency counts for 21264 based systems, at least sometimes.

I sent a workaround to the problem over the weekend. Check
the archives. If ncr/sym say your card is more than 40000 KHZ,
it won't work, at least none do on my XP1000. As of today,
I've had my XP1000 up under 2.2.11 for 2 days with the fix
and have moved a minimum of 50GB on the disks attached to
two ncr53c8xx cards without problems. Also, it can sustain
20+MB/sec so I know it's running UW on both cards.

Since your card is a dual channel card, it probably has a
PCI to PCI bridge built in, at least my Adaptec 3940 does
and it's dual channel. If you can, try a single channel card
and if necessary the workaround and see if it behaves. I
expect to have 4 DS10's shortly so I can do similar tests.
If the tests go well, that will expand quickly to more DS10's.

You can find cheap single channel cards via the shopping/
comparison sites quite easily. I like the Tekram DC390F
for price/performance (have two at the moment). I do need
to make sure it boots but it's devices show up under SRM
so I assume it will be OK.
