On to those of your questions that I can answer:

Jonas Gustavsson wrote:
>
>
> What windowmanager is generaly considered to be the most stable/supported one out there(MetroLink, Xi or Xfree86)? Preferably hardware accelerated stuff.

MetroX, X1, XFree86, are not window managers. They're X graphics systems.
(they're the "back end" to the window managers).
        Which to use"
        They're all good. However:
        XFree86 is free. It's a solid performer, and so widely used that it probably
has the best support structure that you could as for. But be aware: It's a free
product. It's not without it's problems. Configuring it is a little harder than
some of the other X systems. I use it on my Alpha, and it's never let me down.

        MetroX (By Metrolink). Again, a solid performer, but limited in it's support of
graphics cards & monitors. XFree86 supports a wider base of cards. You have to
pay for MetroX, but the price is reasonable (_about_ $40 USD or so). It's easier
to configure, if that's an issue. And the support from Metrolink is fair. I'm
running it on an Intel box. Again, a solid performer. But be aware: You still
need to install "parts" of XFree86. There are subsystems built into Linux that
require it. Not a big deal.

        Xi. The Rolls Royce of them all. It's the most expensive out there (That I'm
aware of). I've never run it, but know folks that do. performance wise: It's
probably the fastest of the bunch, but can be problematic.

Personally: I'd start with XFree86. It's included with Redhat. If it doesn't
work for you, then look into one of the alternatives.
