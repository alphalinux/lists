Ric Tibbetts wrote:
>
> Xi. The Rolls Royce of them all.

Well, Rolls Royces are not supposed to crash. :-)

> It's the most expensive out there (That I'm
> aware of). I've never run it, but know folks that do. performance wise: It's
> probably the fastest of the bunch,

It is. It is also the least flexible. If for some reasons you need a
bit different configuration than the canned one - tough. You will not
get it. XFree86 is the most flexible but a price you pay is an
occasional wizzardry required on a setup. When I run some comparison
tests earlier this year XFree86 was the only one of the three which did
not crash for all configurations I tried. Depending on a card it may
get a better performance than Metro. Commercial servers support
multihead operations which are not yet in XFree86. It is all related to
your hardware, needs and expectations - YMMV.

> >
> > The same goes for Audio(OSS or something else)?

drivers actually can be preferable; especially for assorted Plug&Pray
stuff. www.alsa.org.
