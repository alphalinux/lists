Jim Mellander wrote:
>
> I've been looking for the 2.2 version of em86 - where do I get the
> rpm's?

Sigh! Write a hundred times on the nearest blackboard:

  "em86 has NOTHING to do with a kernel version and works just fine
     with all 2.2.x kernels".

Maybe this will help (at least with killing this recurring posting).

The trouble you see is a question of __libraries__ which is totally
kernel independent. Binary em86 rpms work with libraries for which
they were compiled and that is about it. Somebody was looking
at what it takes to recompile with glibc2.1. I do not know what
happened to the project. In the meantime use LD_LIBRARY_PATH
to switch to the previous library version for programs requiring
em86 (the fact that dynamic linker apparently changed behaviour
without changing its name may make that "interesting"). Obviously
you need these older libraries available as well.
