It doesn't *yet*! Warning: shameless plug to follow....

Compaq Analyze is the successor to DECevent and I'm the DRI for getting this
ported to Linux/Alpha. It is targeted for EV6 platforms and beyond but in
general will work with binary error logging that supports the "new" Common
Event Header. This is already working cross-platform on Tru64, O-VMS,
WNT/Alpha (and Intel but it can't process live error logging for fairly
obvious reasons) with about 90% common code. That's because most of it's
Java with some breakouts to C/C++ for the stuff that's truly native. The
challenge on Linux is the conspicuous lack of binary error logging,
something we are addressing.

This group is symbiotic with our efforts. Those of you missing a DIA or
UERF (let's be fair, these are radically different--the latter just dumps
stuff in barely human readable form) on Linux may be benefited by our tool
when it comes out. But you folks can be useful to us as well: Linux is a
tough beastie compared to our other OSes because of how little control we
have on its guts. Instead of relying on people we pay to get things done we
ultimately have to rely on one smart guy from Finland. It's a different
world!

So, work with us on this. Your help is appreciated!
