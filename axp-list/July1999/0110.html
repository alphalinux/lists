On Wed, 7 Jul 1999, Brian Beuning wrote:

> 2. Get a SCSI box with more bays, and add more SCSI disks. Seven
> bay SCSI boxes seem to be fairly expensive.

Or chain to a second box/and or hack a smallish PC case for the job ..
this latter option is often cheap.

> 3. Add an internal 3.5" SCSI or IDE drive. People warn about heat problems
> in the multia that I don't want to learn about first hand.

FWIW, I got a 3.5" SCSI drive, and a 2.5" IDE drive in my Multia, running
'round the clock with no troubles.

> 5. Get an internal 2.5" IDE disk. A couple of places sell 6.4 gig for $190.
> The disk is slow (4200 RPM), but it is lots of space at a
>competitive price.

So get a slightly better drive for $220 -- I have a 2.2g IDE drive in my
Multia that I get 3.4 - 4 meg a second off of... not bad for the price,
but it eats CPU time to talk to an IDE drive.

> 6. Get a 2.5" IDE and an IDE-to-SCSI converter. Why? It would cost $190 plus
> the card and the disk is still slow. The converter isn't going to speed it
> up any.

It won't speed up raw disk access that much (if at all) but it won't
eat CPU time -- so system performance will seem zippier.

> My best choice seems to be #1 or #5.

I like #6 -- one day I might pick up an IDE-to-SCSI converter.
