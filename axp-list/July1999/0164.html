On Fri, 9 Jul 1999, James Peret wrote:

> I am trying to setup a firewall on my PC164. i have two ethernet cards,
> a DEC tulip and a 3com 3c905b-tx. I am running redhat 5.2 with a 2.2.7
> kernel.
>
> What is the best way to get linux to recognize two ethernet cards. I
> tried turning on a second adapter in linuxconf, compiled the second
> ethernet driver into the compiler, and followed the firewall howtwo on
> redhat's site.
> Problems are:
>
> RedHat's howto is for intel, they say nothing about milo. (can i pass
> the same parameters to milo?)
>
> compiling the second ethernet card into the kernel hangs on boot.
> without the driver i can connect to the second card only. (the first
> card seems to be disabled; it is in an upper pci slot).
>
> linuxconf won't recognize the second ethernet adapter.
>
> Has anyone configured an alpha as a firewall? if so, how?

I have several systems with 3 network cards.

I have used modules. If you go the module way you can configure the
devices in the /etc/conf.modules file.

But you can pass the same parameters that the intel version expects to the
kernel and it should work.
