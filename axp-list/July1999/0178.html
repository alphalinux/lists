On Fri, 9 Jul 1999, Richard Henderson wrote:

> The oops seems to happens just after cdrecord opens /dev/sg/c0b0t3u0.
> This sets off a modprobe (cause of devfs) for what module i don't
> know - maybe none, which causes the segfault in dik_show_code.
  
  Build a new kernel without modules and see if by chance the
  problem is solved. At which point, add modules back in until
  things fail again.
  
ok, i recompiled with scsi generics and scsi cdrom built-in instead
of as modules, and the problem has gone away.

There was just a post on l-k about Oops when inserting modules,
specifically sg and sr_mod. So it seems to be a known problem. (see
[Patch] Oops with serial module (was:RE: problem with automounter in
2.2.7). So hopefully there'll be a fix in the next kernel release.
  
cdrecord still segfaulted with -ENOMEM. But that seems to be due to
cdrecord not supporting the new sg interface in 2.2.9+. After
applying a patch to cdrecord from the homepage of the sg maintainer
it worked fine.
