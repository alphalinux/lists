An upgrade to V5.4 helped some... We've been off of maintenance for
a while, but I found all the helpful things on gatekeeper...thanks for the
info...

First of all, bootflags has to be set in boot_osflags- neither '' nor ""
are apparently sufficient to cause all of the arguments to be put into the
flags field.

Then booting generic did:
(boot dka500.5.0.1.1 -file kernels/generic.gz -flags root=/dev/scd0 load_ramdisk=1)

(hangs)

Appending a 'console=serial' to the options did not help. Booting from the
floppy images did not help (no surprise there). This is a machine has been
known to run linux (2.2.4- and unfortunately somebody wiped the disk it
was on- otherwise I would just do a variety of other steps rather than
bother with the list here). It currently runs NetBSD just fine.

I think this is where I left this with rth- when/if I get a clear enough
shot at the machine (it's a shared resource at the NAS unit at NASA/Ames
Research Center), I'll find out why it's not working for Linux.

I still stand by my previous statement that "All alpha installs for me and
nearly all sparc installs over the last 3 years have not gone well". I'm
not a stupid person but this RH-Linux "It works for me" stuff is making me
feel stupid and somewhat angry (responses like, "Take over the world? not
bloody likely, me buckos..." start flooding the mind)....

But anyway, thanks for the attempt to help. I suggest that RedHat might
want to do a slightly better job of saying what is actually known to work.
I've spent the money to buy the CD's for the last 3 years or so because
that's the right thing to do, but I don't intend to continue as it's not a
good 'out of box' experience. Nor is it possible to sell NAS' management
on allowing some Linux development for these machines when the CDs that
claim to support Alpha 4100s clearly don't support the ones we have- they
point to a perfectly functioning NetBSD system (which I did the port for)
and say, "No need- see?". Tsk.
