>
> I have a few questions about the new layout of RH6.0 and kernel builds...
>
> First off, what is this /etc/milo.conf file all about

It is not new at all. But I do not think that it ever documented.

> how do I use it?

Mount your boot floppy and have a look at the example. This is just
a text file so you can write yourself something similar, experiment
a bit, and you will know. :-)

> What packages update or affect it?

None.

> Is it
> edited by hand when a new kernel is installed or built?

You may create it with an editor when you feel like it. It is not
required for anything. It may just save you some typing and you may
bypass it anyway. You may write a bunch of different stanzas with the
different labels and different options but the same kernel. Just a
convenience.

>
> I heard that recompiling the kernel is no longer necessary.

????

> How can that be?

It was always like that. You booted so it runs and you do not have
to recompile. Usually you want to - to customize to your hardware -
but this is your choice.

> Is the stock kernel configured with *all* drivers? That couldn't be.

"Stock" 6.0 alpha installation comes with some 5 Meg plus of modules.
Take your pick. A distribution kernel is pretty fat on its own.

> If
> that is so, what is the procedure for building new kernels on Alpha? I only
> recall the "old" Red Hat method of downloading sources and going through
> the make mrproper...yada yada yada.

Nothing changed here. :-) Although 'make mrproper' was required only
in rare circumstances.

> Is there an easier way to build kernels now?

Hm, 'make oldconfig; make clean boot modules' does not strike me as an
extremely hard thing to type.
