I have other UNIX boxes where I get similar failures to your's.
Passing a float/double as an arg doesn't always produce the result
you would expect with high optimizations. So, I avoid it in
general in my own code. To me, it's just an idiosyncracy of coding
and optimizing C. I've seen it off and on over the past 2 years
or so. Possibly the optimizations in the newer compilers are
trying to be smarter than previously.

One of the previous replies at least now explains why it's a
problem, namely, the variable comes into the routine in a
FP reg and if you want to muck with it's address, there isn't
one without eliminating the optimization of using FP regs for
calling args.
