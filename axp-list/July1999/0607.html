Greg Lindahl wrote:
>
> > The RH 6.0 install uses a RAMdisk floppy in a format different from previous
> > releases, and so, previous kernels will not work with it.
>
> Ack, yack. Just to make the lives interesting for those of us who play
> with the boot sequence.

RH 6.0 is using romfs on (compressed) ramdisk images and 'genromfs'
is a part of the whole distribution. As long as you have a kernel which
supports that file system then you are ok.

You can also use ext2fs formatted ramdisks. The problem is to sqeeze
all the time growing amount of stuff on a floppy. romfs is much
simpler, less capable and has much smaller overhead than ext2fs or
minixfs. OTOH it is easier to manipulate a contents of ext2fs ramdisk
as with romfs you have to recreate it every time.

You can substantially reduce what ext2 file system takes from your ram
disk not only by formatting with '-m 0' but also by specifying the least
possible number of inodes you can get away with. :-) Red Hat was never
doing that and most of the time savings will be substantial. Do not
forget also to remove lost+found from your ramdisk; it is not very
likely that you will need it. :-)
