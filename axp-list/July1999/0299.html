On Thu, 15 Jul 1999, Rebuck, Gregory wrote:

> At first I thought "Ya got me!" But after looking at the site I feel a bit
> vindicated. They state clearly that Free-VMS is an OpenVMS *look-alike*.
> So is it really VMS? You have to decide. Fairly similar to the Linux
> question. Linux isn't really UNIX but in some ways it out-UNIXes UNIX!

If Free-VMS gets as close to 'real' VMS as Linux does to UNIX -- then
in practice I'll refer to it as VMS in some scenarios.. same way a lot of
people do with Linux/UNIX. When talking to a kernel hacker, or driver
programmer -- I'll be painfully accurate, but if I'm talking to a newbie
who is just learning bash programming Linux == UNIX.

> There's alway some bozo out there willing to port some OS or application to
> his own platform. I personally am not too excited about VMS but even less
> so with regards to Intel!

Same could be said for Linux at the start. Unix on x86? Why? I remember
thinking that way.

> There's a difference between doing something for your own satisfaction
> and producing something that people really want!

Linus didn't start out to save the world -- he started out because he
wanted a better 'UNIX' than Minix on his 386. It snowballed (avalanching
might be a good term these days, if you are optimistic) and the rest is
history.

The lesson: great things start from humble beginnings.

> Linux "lacks" (don't slaughter me on this word--Linux is just not yet
> gaining much interest in the desktop and IMHO *won't* until it can
> coexist as both a hacker's environment and a commercially viable
> product; AFAIK a Netscape browser still is not available for
> Linux/Alpha unless you count Mozilla which if I recall was an early
> version of Netscape) the basic kinds of apps that are central to WinCE.

Myself, I'm not too intersted in Linux as a desktop OS per se.. but
I don't understand what you mean about apps. Linux has plenty of small
lightweight apps -- UNIX philosophy and all -- and if anything, this is
the desktop user's main COMPLAINT.

Which is why I don't really WANT Linux to succeed on the desktop -- that
would imply large, integrated (-> bloated) apps that hog resources.

But, all this misses the point in a free VMS and pocket-Linux for me..
<puts on R. Stallman mask> ..

It's about free software. I don't care about the open source development
model. I don't care about the best way to develop powerful, stable
applications -- and I'm not overly concerned with price.

It's about having the freedom to hack on the source and add features that
I want, and to quash bugs that affect me. I can't do this with CE or
PalmOS .. I can with Linux.

To rob a phrase from the current issue of Linux Journal:

It really is about free beer.

<puts R. Stallman mask back in the closet>
