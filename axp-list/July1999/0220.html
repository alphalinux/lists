Nope, you are right. But the direction you are looking at the implication
is different from mine.
When the language definition says you cannot take the address of a
register, it means that it is a semantic error on your part to try to take
the address of an automatic variable declared as a register. It won't
compile. However, it is a compiler error to not correctly provide the
address of an auto variable, even if you've turned every optimisation under
the sun on. (i.e. Compiler options about use of variables are very different
from use of the keyword 'register')
