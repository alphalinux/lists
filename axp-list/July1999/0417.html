Peter Rival wrote:
>
> On the flip side, I do totally agree that the "it works for me" syndrome isn't very
> helpful.

I think that you expect to much. Most of us, maybe with small
exceptions of singular points like Jay++ :-), even if touched more
than one Alpha have seen only a small fraction of all possible
combinations of various boards, firmware variants, hardware
configurations not even mentioning possible differences in installed
software. If someone with more "exotic" setup has troubles one can
only try to guess what possibly went wrong; on the top of it quite
often, not always - mind you - but **really** often, what we see is
some kind of an "operator error" even if not immediately clear from
a description which one.

Personally I am finding "it works for me" responses actually helpful.
If I have troubles with something then an information that things
are basically sane and either my understanding of a situation is still
faulty or I have pay closer attention to various components of my
system is really valuable. It tells me that I am not wasting my time
in some blind alleys even if I do not see a clear path at the moment.
Sure, it would be nice to get a step-by-step instructions. The trouble
is that whomever responded quite likely also does not know that for
your particular situation (never really knew, or it was a long time
ago and details were forgotten, or your description omits multiple
crucial details, or ... ).
