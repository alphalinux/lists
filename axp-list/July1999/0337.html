Brian E.W. Wood wrote:
[...]
> Be sure you aren't comparing apples and oranges. The SETI site explains that the
> newer versions of their software do more science and will thus take more time
> to complete a work unit. Unless the versions being compared are exactly the
> same you may get very misleading results. I haven't checked but perhaps the
> most recent version available for NT/Alpha is older than that for Linux/Alpha.
>
> It is for this reason that the stats on their site are misleading.

Also bear in mind that user-time calculations with some recent SMP
kernels are way too high. I'm verifying some new boards using SETI@home
as background load. A dual-EV6 system turns in about 8 results a day -
but the website reports my average turnaround time is way over 30
hours. Perhaps there are enough people out there using recent SMP
kernels (like me) to skew the statistics. Does anyone know of a fix for
this that would get SETI@home back on the right track?
