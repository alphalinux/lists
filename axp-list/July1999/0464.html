Hi, You should leave at least 3MB space in the beginning of the disk for
aboot. aboot is less than 100 KB, but maybe you need the space for other
use later. aboot is in aboot-0.5-10.alpha.rpm. Redhat 6.0 will install it
for you, so you don't need to look for it.
There is no special requirements for partition except that you should use
BSD labeling. You can use sda1 as swap and sda2 as /.
For detailed information, you can come to visit my website.
http://www.iol.unh.edu/linux/alpha/started.html
