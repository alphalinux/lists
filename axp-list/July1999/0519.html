> root]# swapon /dev/hda7
> but met "swapon: warning: /dev/hda7 has insecure permissions
> 0660, 0600 suggested"

I guess when it says that /dev/hda7 has insecure permissions, what it
really means is that file /dev/hda7 has insecure access permissions.

How about
root]# chmod 600 /dev/hda7

Grab your nearest Unix textbook, or tutorial, and look up "file
permissions". The man page for chmod may tell you something useful too
(so you can decypher '660' and '600').
Remember ``chmod'', it's as fundamental a command as ls, cp and mv.
