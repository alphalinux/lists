On Thu, Jul 08, 1999 at 02:08:31PM -0500, Todd Henderson wrote:
>You wouldn't happen to have access to a DEC Unix(tru64) machine with
>tru64 fortran would you?

No, but since the fortran on tru64 is the same as linux AFAIK I'd expect
minimal differences in runtime.

> Also, did you specify -arch ev6 ?

Nope, but I was told this was default on this particular machine
(not sure if the compilers smart enough, or it's a configuration
of the compiler).

On Thu, Jul 08, 1999 at 03:36:27PM -0400, Greg Lindahl wrote:
> this is the "standard" "safe" optimization level you want:
>
> fort -O4 -arch ev6 -tune ev6

I just tried it, helped a bit:
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache fort -O4 -arch ev6 -tune ev6 8:31

Another variation (surprisingly slower):
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache fort -O6 -mcpu=ev6 -ffast-math 8:54

For completeness sake here's the complete table now:
PII-450-512k cache g77 -O3 75:02
Celeron 450A-128K cache g77 -O3 74:44
Alpha 21164-600 4 MB cache g77 -O3 29:27
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache g77 -O3 17:16
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache fort -O6 -mcpu=ev6 -ffast-math 8:54
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache fort -O3 8:42
Alpha 21264-500 4 MB cache fort -O4 -arch ev6 -tune ev6 8:31
