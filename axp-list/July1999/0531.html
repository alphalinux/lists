hmm..
  I hope someone can shed some light on this 'cos it's got
me completely confused! Until last Thursday I had a shiny
working LX164 (600MHz/512Mb RAM, 6.4Gb IBM udma ide
disk, qlogic isp scsi w/quantum viking, symbios 53c810 w/
cdrom, de435) which i'd happily taken up the development
track from 2.1.100 (ish) to 2.2.10. Hadn't yet started on 2.3.
It was based on RH5.1 but had been heavily upgraded. 5
days ago I started getting lots of errors from the qlogic
card with the driver timing out and it failing to find data. I
shut the box down and cleaned it, evicting several spiders
in the process (guess they were sheltering from all the
building work going on round here, which also explains
several inches of dust inside the cab).

  Upon restarting the system, things got weird. I use
SRM & milo boot floppy. Booted up. fine. tried to boot
2.2.10. Gets to unzipping, says "done" and then the
box reboots. Keep trying. Fortunately I've got about 40
kernel images in / which i nearly cleared out but never
did. Finally I find that 2.1.131 will actually boot. Further
testing reveals the following -

Can't boot any kernel from 2.1.31 ac 11 onwards: these
either autoreboot after unzipping, report "length error-halting"
or occasionally pop up an unaligned trap error and freeze.
Can't get anything from the magic sysrq key. kernels
2.0.35 (as came with the box), and 2.1.X (x > 119, didn't
go back any further) all work happily. As I mentioned,
all of these kernels worked before (apart from 2.2.8, which
I compiled but never booted 'cos 2.2.9 came out a couple
of days later) and I'd avoided all the problems other
people seemed to have had - no oopses, uptimes of 40
days+ (mainly terminated by upgrading to the next kernel).

So, what on earth could be causing this behaviour? The
only clue i can really gather is that 2.1.131 works and
131ac11 doesn't. I seem to remember that Richard
Henderson put in a lot of changes relating to MILO and
linux's detection of hardware/system types from it at
this point. As my milo is quite old (22/9/97 - it's in
the "latest" directory at gatekeeper, but it's a lot older
than the ones at redhat) this might be a problem. But
then again, they worked before, with the same MILO.

Anyone anywhere have a clue what could cause this
or is it finally time for me to rebuild from scratch?
(mind you, i must admit, i really liked 2.1.131, but
it does constrain future development a bit!!)

cheers
john
