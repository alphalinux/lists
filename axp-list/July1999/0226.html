Ok, then perhaps 2,2 will have reached some stability *on the alpha*.
I am not complaining! I think that a lot of people here are doing
fabulous things in carrying on the AlphaLinux development,
and I am very grateful to them.
I just observe that one cannot afford to stay on the software
forefront when running a service for thousands of users that should
never crash.

> > Now I have one server on 2.0.37 and other three on 2.0.36 and
> > they are solid as rocks. Can't run X on the one with 2.0.37,
> > but this improves security :-)
>
> I have 2.2.10 running rock-solid; glibc-2.1.1 and XFree86-3.3.3.1 run
> very well.

Ok, then perhaps we should try to be scientific and compare
a few indicators about the machine load, so perhaps we understand
something more. The machine that gave me problems with 2.2.x typically:
- gives about 300-400 as output of 'netstat -n | wc -l'
- 'Used' memory stays around 300MB (with 512 physical)
In one month of uptime:
- update (bdflush) collects about 120 minutes CPU
- about 12M pages in and 50M out (from 'grep page /proc/stat' )
- about 250M context switches (from 'grep ctxt /proc/stat' )
- about 350k processes created (from 'grep processes /proc/stat' )

The box looks far from being overloaded and I believe that these figures
could be much higher. But as a matter of fact I was not able to reach
2.2.x stability in these conditions.
I should also say that I have a lot of other things that demand
my attention and I cannot spend the whole day to experiment with
the boxes..
