Its the xfs thing biting you as it did me with RH6.0 first.

The solution is to make the following change to your XF86Config file in
/etc/X11

    #FontPath "unix/:-1"

FontPath "/usr/X11R6/lib/fonts/misc/"
FontPath "/usr/X11R6/lib/fonts/speedo/"
FontPath "/usr/X11R6/lib/fonts/Type1/"
FontPath "/usr/X11R6/lib/fonts/75dip/"
FontPath "/usr/X11R6/lib/fonts/100dpi/"

I'm surprised the problem still exists even with the updates to the X rpms
applied.
