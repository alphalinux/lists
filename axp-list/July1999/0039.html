I suspect that my AlphaStation has suffered heat death and want to replace
the CPU. Can anyone sell me one of these? This is one of the old Onsale
AlphaStation 200 4/166's with a 166MHz 21064A in it.

Please respond via email--i will give preference to vendors who do not bug
the list with their ads. If you also want a 21064A or a copy of all the
ads i receive, email me. i'll post a summary to the list if there is a
lot of interest.

The long story, if you want to disagree with my diagnosis, is:

  o i discovered the CPU fan inlet was basically completely clogged with
    dust. the problem began when we started having hot weather.
  o i returned from a week-long trip to find the machine halted at
    ``machine check in PAL code'' or something. upon reboot,
  o the LED's indicated ``SCSI controller failure'' and it wouldn't boot
    or echo anything to the serial console. once i got it to boot,
    and it stayed up for a day, but this was a freak accident it seems.
  o the last time it machine-checked, it wouldn't reboot. i jabbed the
    reset button about ten times in rapid succession, and that seemed
    to fry it totally. i now get all eight LED's on, no change in
    the LED pattern from powerup on. This is the same behaviour i
    observe if i remove the CPU.

Thanks for your help, all.

To those of you who have working AS200's, I suggest you take off the front
faceplate (easy. no screws or wires, just four plastic tabs) and clean
dust off the CPU fan inlet about once a year.
