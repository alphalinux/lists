(First, sorry if the formatting is toast - my mailreader refuses to send
mail out today, so I have to fall back to text mode. What a lost art that
is...I was thinking about sending this by telneting to the SMTP port of
one of our servers, but decided not to...;)

The latest status on this problem goes something like this - I hacked the
RH distributed ramdisk.img to add a /dev/console with correct major/minor
numbers (5,1) and a correct tty0. Now everything proceeds nicely, right
up to where it says something akin to "Install starting" (or "Starting
install" or something like that...). At that point, nothing more comes up
on the screen, but the system _does_ respond to keyboard input which is
more than it used to (e.g., now I can hit enter and have a new line come
up on the screen). My guess is that the installer just doesn't like the
serial console - maybe it can't handle the control codes that are being
sent by the install program. But then, I'm not an ASCII graphics person,
so what do I know?

BTW, do we know what changes were made to the SPARC image?
