Rewind, and erase.

The 'YEP' paragraph has no connection to the ISO C language at all.

There is no way an C coder can specify that a register be used for
either a variable or a parameter. One may hint, or suggest, but not specify.
It it the compiler's responsibility to ensure that anything one takes the
address of has an address, and thus is not solely stored in a register.
It was a bug, which is why it appears to have been fixed. Compilers,
however, are permitted to keyhole optimise and convert known length calls
to functions like memcpy into more efficient code. MSVC++ does this without
even breaking into a sweat.
