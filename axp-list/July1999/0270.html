You must be joking but I'll play along. VMS is available on VAX and Alpha;
seeing VMS on Intel would be ludicrous to both. For one thing the former is
somewhere between maintaining-its-ground' and slowly-fading-away. It's a
solid OS but it's not generating any new excitement. I know firsthand that
new technologies like Java were never considered when VMS was in its prime.
The two don't play ball together. VMS/Alpha runs Java but has to kludge
everything to do it. VMS/VAX was dropped Java a while back because IEEE
floating point could not be emulated satisfactorily.

Anyway, would you *really* want VMS on Intel? I find three things curious:
you picked 1) Alpha based discussion talking about 2) Linux and the topic is
3) "RH 6.0 can't see Multia ethernet." Did you take a wrong turn at
Albequerque? C-:<
