This is also the reason why it costs roughly $500 MORE for the SRM version

Compaq, like most other large companies is organized to actually make a
profit -- unlike .coms. In a large company it is very difficult to "weed
out" the parts that are causing the overall corporate profits to decline or
fail to grow without some kind of "cost center" structure. Hence individual
departments are frequently "cost centers" and they are required to "sell"
their services to other corporate departments in-order to receive money for
salaries and the like. Move up the food chain from department to division
and, yes, the division head can choose to make his division "less
profitable" by subsidizing those parts which are necessary but unpopular.

In Academia, we call this "subvention" and it allows things like the
Folklore or Latin departments to continue to exist even when they have a 2
or 3 faculty members to each student! But it means that the Engineering
School and the Business School can't hire the faculty needed to lower their
student faculty ratios from 50-100 to 1 to 20-30 to 1.

It is only in Government where the Civil-Service system guarantees
employment for life (yes, more tenured faculty members are fired each year
than Civil Service employees) that the notion of accountability for
generating enough income to pay your own salary is a novel concept.

Research Professors are expected to obtain sufficient Grant funds to cover
salaries and related costs -- including the "tax" which a University of Lab
puts on that grant to fund the "central facilities" -- like the Library,
and the OC3 Internet 2 connection.

===
The real problem with Linux and the Alpha is that the two come from
different worlds. Linux comes from the PC world and Alpha comes from the
Enterprise.

The Linux program within Compaq is funded by the Tru64 Unix side of the
house where Maddog campaigned heavily and long for it. (Read June's Linux
Journal if you want the details.) It is driven and funded out of the
Enterprise computing division (ie the Alpha side). Why? because the
"Industry Standard Server" folks (ie the PC side) don't have to try to sell
PCs... they just take orders, and they have tied their fortunes to
Microsoft.

The future of Tru64 and of VMS is tied to the success of the Alpha.
That's why you can run Alpha Linux on the GS160, and why there is binary
compatibility between Tru64 Unix and Alpha Linux.

Without Kurt's instigation, Maddog's vision and Digital/Compaq's direct
support of Linux on the Alpha -- it simply would not exist. All you would
have available to you today is Linux on Intel.

[I followed Kurt and became the last chair of the UNIX-SIG in DECUS.]

The sad commentary about the Linux community is that "linux" to 99% of them
means "Linux on Intel." Linux on Alpha is denigrated just like the
Macintosh is denigrated by Windows users.
