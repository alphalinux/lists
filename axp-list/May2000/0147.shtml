Maurice Hilarius wrote:

> Frankly, one must face the fact that it is a marketing tool as well, and
> they DO sell computers!

All that I am saying is that I believe that they will sell more computers
and make more money if they donate resources to making free software tools
work better (optimally?) on Alpha hardware, or make their tools 100%
compatible with the rest of the GNU/Linux system and sell them cheaply (in
order to sell more expensive hardware--which is sort of what compaq seems to
be doing). My guess is that compiler sales generate very little revenue for
most hardware companies (anyone have data?) and so the mind share benefits
of supporting free software far outweigh any potential profits from selling
closed source tools. (Of course, I'm in academics which basically means
that I have no clue how to make money, so take my financial advise with a
grain of salt. ;-)
