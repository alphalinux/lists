spink@pa.dec.com wrote:

> Well gcc does have some problems that are hard to overcome. I believe at
> least some people from compaq have been helping with gcc, but I think a better
> solution is to have the gcc front end hooked up to the GEM backend(GEM is
> actually the optimization core of all the compaq compilers. Basically all the
> compilers for WinNT, VMS, and Tru64 represent an intermediate format to GEM
> which does the optimization and finalization).

This would be perfect, particularly if something similar could be done for g77.
As long as the pricing is not prohibitive, GNU/Linux compatibility is more
important from my perspective than either open source or free beer.

> Also I would be interested in what high quality optimization intel is doing for
> GCC. I believe that if you compare irc to gcc you will see a large
> difference as well.

I believe that intel funded cygnus to improve the pentium optimizations in gcc
and these have or will be folded into the main gcc source. (At least that is the
impression I got from some of the cygnus cold fusion press releases.) It also
sounds like they are putting a lot of resources into GNU/Linux support for IA-64.

Still, I'm a big fan of Alpha hardware and plan on spending a fair chunk of
startup money on a small Alpha cluster next year. On the other hand, if someone
ever does manages to get close to Alpha in floating point performance and
throughput at comparable hardware cost, AND supports high performance GNU/Linux
tools, then I will reconsider.
