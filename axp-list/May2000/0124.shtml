Thanks, everyone, for the valuable input on building an Alpha web
server... The particular opportunity to get a 3300R passed, and that
seemed to be the concensus on which was best for the job.

In addition, one big consideration was "field-replacability" of major
components. The idea that I could, in a pinch, walk into one of hundreds
of local computer stores and replace the MoBo, CPU, or RAM, while I might
be searching the internet for sources of parts for retired Alpha systems,
weighed heavily on my mind.

So right now, I'm leaning toward the Intel direction, with an Athlon
chip, and maybe even hardware RAID, if I can verify what drivers are
available for that.

Anyone have any good arguments to change my mind back to Alpha? I'm sure
another 3300 will crop up on ebay soon, and eventually, I'd like to be an
"alpha-male" (sorry -- probably an old, worn newbies joke). I'm eagerly
watching my friend Mike's efforts to get a 3300 together for alphalinux.
He is my guinea pig on this... :)
