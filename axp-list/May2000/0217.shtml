>
> Hi there,
>
> I have an new UP2000 (Dual 21264 750 MHz) there and I am wondering
> that it is getting really hot (at the side near the CPU location). I
> estimate 40 - 50 0C (degree centigrade) but haven't measured it yet.
>

Are you sure it's not the power supply? I have several UP2K's
and it is the power supply generating most of the heat, at least
on PowerRac cases. I would assume power supplies in general can
dissipate quite a bit of heat but in particular, these are large
ones so they are correspondingly hotter compared to, say, a PC.

If you have a current meter, you can measure how many amps are
being drawn by your system and figure approximately how many
BTU's it will be giving off. I believe the conversion factor
from watts to BTU's is "watts * 3.41 = BTU's". Also, I believe
"watts=voltage*amps". You can buy a voltage/current meter at
your local hardware store if you want to know precisely what
is happening.

Just went thru this with a HVAC guy and upgraded my power center
and air conditioning in my computer room for more equipment.
