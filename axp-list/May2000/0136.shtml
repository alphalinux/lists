> If compaq really wants to win over linux users, they should add high
> quality optimization for alpha directly into gcc.

That's easier said than done. Compaq's compilers use a completely different
(and apparently more powerful) intermediate representation, so the things
they do in ccc aren't directly applicable to gcc.

> I'd hate to see compaq/alpha squander the
> potential gains it could make in linux mind share by not fully
> supporting open source tools.

Compaq supports AlphaLinux in a major way. Compaq has open-sourced some
other tools for AlphaLinux, such as some performance tools. I'm working with
Compaq guys on page coloring for Linux. And everyone was pleasantly
surprised when they made their compiler free-as-in-beer for enthusiasts. You
should give them credit where credit is due, and not focus on one area too
much.

I would like to see Compaq make additional investment in open source, but
the top 2 areas I see for AlphaLinux are (1) making sure that ethernet
drivers for Linux work on their motherboard ethernet chips before the
motherboards come out, and (2) helping Cygnus with some of the outstanding
binutils problems for AlphaLinux, namely the restrictions on .got sections
that make big programs hard to link.

> (I do appreciate their releasing
> free-as-in-beer compilers! It just seems that there is a lot of code
> duplication in maintaining separate compilers rather than beefing up the
> alpha code generation in gcc.)

It's just not that simple. Compaq wrote that compiler for VMS, Tru64, and
x86/NT. That's what paid for overall development, and the improvements that
are still going on. The port to Linux was fairly inexpensive. An identical
amount of money spent on gcc would probably not improve it much.

Additionally, my business involves selling Alphas for high performance
computing, and for that I need a good Fortran 95 compiler. Compaq supports
F95 in their compiler; no amount of love or money will make GNU F95 be
finished tomorrow. It's nicely underway, but...

Changing topics: I wonder if anyone is thinking about writing an Alpha
back-end for SGI's open source compiler? It has a free IA-64 backend; SGI
has not released the mips back-end. It should be possible to do an alpha
back-end, but it would be a fair bit of work.
