spink@pa.dec.com wrote:
> Well gcc does have some problems that are hard to overcome.
> I believe at least some people from compaq have been helping
> with gcc, but I think a better solution is to have the gcc
> front end hooked up to the GEM backend(GEM is actually the
> optimization core of all the compaq compilers. Basically all
> the compilers for WinNT, VMS, and Tru64 represent an intermediate
> format to GEM which does the optimization and finalization).

That's essentially what SGI did. They used c/c++ frontends from egcs
with their own backend. This interface is at the tree level, not RTL,
since SGI's backend is not based on RTL.

I don't know how Compaq could do this though unless the GEM backend were
GPL. Perhaps GCC could emit an intermediate format that GEM can
understand?

GCC will also get better as more optimizations are performed on trees,
not RTL. Some of this will be apparent in 3.0... for instance, C++
inlining is now performed completely in trees.
