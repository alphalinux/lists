As long as both fans are running and not obstructed by cables, etc, you
should be fine. These systems are spec'd to run at 35C ambient. That means
that we test them in a thermal chamber at a significantly higher temperature
(42? 45?) as well as testing them in the corners of the
temperature/voltage/frequency graph.

On the other hand, if the reason things are hot is that you've run your SCSI
cable across the mouth of the fan, then you should move it. :-)

As far as the discussion concerning heat and electrical consumption goes
(but remember students, heat != temperature), the power supplies used in the
CH320 are 70-80% efficient. That means that a system that wants 400W will
have already dissipated 133W (400*(.75/1-1)) in the power supply before
anything gets to the motherboard.

It's 3.42 BTU per watt and 12,000 BTU per ton of A/C. Depending on your
configuration and how hard you are running them, you'll need an additional
ton of cooling for each 4 to 12 or more systems depending on whether you are
running your dual 1 GHz maxed configurations flat out all the time or have
uniprocessor 667 MHz systems just loafing along.

Tom

P.S. Like many systems these cool front to back. So line them up in racks
with the fronts of adjacent rows facing each other and the backs facing the
backs of the row behind. That way you aren't blowing hot outlet air into
the intakes of the next row.
