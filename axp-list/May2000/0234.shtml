Hi to all Alpha-Linux-users !

My company develops (and supports) a commercial SQL-DBMS
named "Adabas D" (see http://www.adabas.com/sqlindex.html )
which is available for several Unix platforms, including
Linux/i386 and Compaq-Tru64 (and Win ... ;-) ).

We received some few customer requests for a version
running on Linux/AXP, and consider doing this.
However, such a decision would be much easier (and more
whole-hearted) if there were more demand for it.
So I ask you as potentially interested people:

   Would you / your company consider acquiring a commercial
   SQL-DBMS running on Alpha systems using Linux ?

If yes, I would appreciate an e-mail giving a rough estimate
on number of DB users as well as expected DB size,
just in order to get some overall feeling I could present to
my management.

Please be assured that
1) I will _not_ use your answers for any follow-ups,
   marketing actions etc. and will not give them to anybody.
2) Any answer will be taken as your personal current best guess
   which nobody assumes to bind either you or your company.

As usual in IT and management decisions, things are urgent,
so I welcome fast answers (but will of course not reject later
ones).

Thank you for your assistance !

Regards, Joerg Bruehe
