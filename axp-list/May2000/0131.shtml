With regards to your message at 01:17 PM 5/16/00 -0700, Timothy H. Keitt.
Where you stated:
>If compaq really wants to win over linux users, they should add high
>quality optimization for alpha directly into gcc. Intel is already
>doing this for their cpu's. I'd hate to see compaq/alpha squander the
>potential gains it could make in linux mind share by not fully
>supporting open source tools. (I do appreciate their releasing
>free-as-in-beer compilers! It just seems that there is a lot of code
>duplication in maintaining separate compilers rather than beefing up the
>alpha code generation in gcc.)
I believe that Compaq is doing a very good job at releasing software and
compilers for Alpha Linux.
They offer fully optimized C, C++, and Fortran now.
For education these are free, and for others very reasonably priced.
I can't see them giving this away to everyone yet, as they likely have a
substantial investment in costs to accomplish this.
Frankly, one must face the fact that it is a marketing tool as well, and
they DO sell computers!
