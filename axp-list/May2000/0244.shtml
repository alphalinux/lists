David Webb wrote:
>
> I'm running RH6.1 (2.2.15) with Milo 2.2.15
> and AlphaBIOS 5.7 on a pc164lx.

There is a bunch of milos on ftp://gatekeeper.dec.com
You may want to try one or another from these.

> When I
> booted linux from the AlphaBIOS, Milo hung
> when it got to the 4.3 GB drive.

In case if you wonder if milo can handle drives of these
sizes then it definitely can. Here is a relevant piece of
'dmesg' output from a milo booting Alpha I have here at this
moment:

sym53c875-0-<0,*>: FAST-20 WIDE SCSI 40.0 MB/s (50 ns, offset 15)
SCSI device sda: hdwr sector= 512 bytes. Sectors= 8467200 [4134 MB] [4.1 GB]
sym53c875-0-<2,*>: FAST-20 WIDE SCSI 40.0 MB/s (50 ns, offset 15)
SCSI device sdb: hdwr sector= 512 bytes. Sectors= 17850000 [8715 MB] [8.7 GB]
sym53c875-0-<10,*>: FAST-20 WIDE SCSI 40.0 MB/s (50 ns, offset 15)
SCSI device sdc: hdwr sector= 512 bytes. Sectors= 8925000 [4357 MB] [4.4 GB]

All three happen to be IBM drives but this likely is not relevant.
I was definitely using drives of similar sizes, and likely bigger,
with LX. Cannot tell you what were the biggest disks on a machine
booting with milo but probably something between 30 and 40 GB.

Milo may have problems loading a boot image located with bigger than 2 GB
offset into a partition (NOT in a drive); it may also not read ext2 file
system formatted with 4 KB blocks and "sparse_superblocks" feature,
but these are not relevant to the problem on hand.

> It makes no difference if the 4.3 GB HD has
> been formatted as FAT or NTFS.

Sure. How this drive is formatted, or if formatted at all, makes
absolutely no difference at this stage to the game.

Maybe this drive has to be spin-out and a driver in milo does not
provide a required impulse? Things like that can be often set
with jumpers on a drive. You have to check on a manufacturer
web site for drive specs. Unfortunately I do not know of any way
of passing driver options (ncr53c8xx driver has a long list of
these) to a driver in milo.
