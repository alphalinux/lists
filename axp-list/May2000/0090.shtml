Mohan <MKANNAPA@ford.com> writes:

> Here is the screen shot.
> ------------------------
> P00>>>boot
> (boot dkb0.0.0.2001.1 -file 2/boot/vmlinux-2.2.14smp-001 -flags
> root=/dev/sda2 console=ttyS0)

Why are you booting with root=/dev/sda2? If dka0 is Tru64, then that
is obviously wrong.

Linux is trying to mount an AdvFS partition as its /. This does not
work for obvious reasons.

Your real root partition appears to be /dev/sdc2. Set your
BOOT_OSFLAGS appropriately.
