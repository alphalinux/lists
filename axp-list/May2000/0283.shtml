Peter,

Thanks for posting this as it reminded me I needed to post the solution
to a problem I was having.

Until recently, I had a similar situation with my 164LX and a Soundblaster
Awe64 card. I'm using MILO, not SRM, as I still need to boot to NT.
Anyhow, I've been compiling Xmame with the Compaq C compiler and having
odd sound problems. I was getting terrible stucking noises or it just
wouldn't work at all. The KDE sounds were not much better. Updating the
kernel to 2.2.15 didn't help either. About a week ago I took a long shot
and I built a updated MILO against a 2.2.15 kernel (Thanks, Stefan!) and
then it started working...! :-)

Alan

Peter Petrakis wrote:
>
> You may recall a while back when I was trying to get my isa soundblaster to
> work under SRM.
> http://www.alphalinux.org/archives/axp-list/January2000/0348.shtml. It would
> cause the system to halt when you played sounds. Well I've appeared to have
> resolved the problem. I'm currently running kernel-2.4.0-test1/aboot-0.7 .
> What I had to do was use the 2.4's new isa plug and play capibility and
> compile the sound drivers into the kernel. If I compiled the sound drivers as
> modules it would crash the system when I played sound. So now I have sound
> working with SRM and I'm much happier now that I can play my mp3's again.
