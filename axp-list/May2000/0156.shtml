I believe that the 3300 series boxes are the ones which were "bastardized"
for NT. That is to say, it was a Marketing decision (not Engineering) to
PREVENT any operating system other than NT (read VMS and Tru64) from
running on the box. Compaq sold them at about half the price of the
comparable box capable of running VMS and Tru64. But other than the "NT
only" designation there was no difference from the VMS and Tru64 capable
boxes.

As I recall, the primary issue is that the 3300 series has no SRM console;
it has the ARC console only.
I don't believe that there was ever any "check the processor type and
abort" code created -- since without the SRM console neither VMS nor Tru64
can be booted.

The full details are in the tru64-unix-managers archives, it's been
discussed several times since Compaq dropped NT on the Alpha and these
boxes became available -- cheap -- because they have no "supported" OS.
