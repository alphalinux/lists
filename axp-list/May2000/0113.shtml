Mohan wrote:
>
>
> PROBLEM:
> -------
> This dual boot configurations *WORKS* but only with manual
> intervention. What I mean is, when I boot linux with the
> Tru64 UNIX disks in the shelf (i.e DKA0,DKA100,DKB100) the
> linux kernel panics??
> If I physically remove the Tru64 Unix disks (i.e DKA0,DKA100,DKB100)
> from the shelf the linux kernel boot up *FINE* without any problems.
>

Quite likely your disk letters are being renumbered when
you add the Tru64 disks so what you think is your root
partition isn't.

Your boot parms below say root=/dev/sda2 and here you say
Tru64 is DKA0. /dev/sda == dka0, at least on my systems.
You want root=/dev/sdb2 it looks like when the Tru64 disks
are left in place.
