On Wed, May 03, 2000 at 03:06:54PM +1200, MACKAY, Lincoln wrote:
> Now that I have a kernel booting (on an 2100), I am having trouble with
> display drivers.
>
> Redhat 6.2 does not have a display option for the Compaq Qvision display
> card that is installed in this machine.

It's not just Red Hat, it's XFree86. The QVision support languished for
years, never being updated, and IIRC, got dropped not too long ago.

In addition, it was never ported to Alpha.

> If I select Generic VGA, I get an error from the Anaconda install script
> telling me that the XFree86-VGA16 is not available.

Generic VGA support, aka VGA16, also has never been ported to Alpha.

> Any suggestions?

Find another card that *is* supported and ported, like Matrox Millennia.
S3-based cards of the same generation as ther QVision also should work,
like 928 (ISA), or 864/964/968.

You may have a problem with more recent cards, in that the BIOS emulation
on the 2100 may be old enough to not work on the VGA BIOS code. In that
case, try updating to the latest SRM console, and retry.

Good luck.
