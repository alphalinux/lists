I got past this one fairly easily. The problem seems to be that the floppy
drive is set at 2.88, not 1.44. To fix this, go into the "Edit Environment
Variables" portion of the Supplemental Menu and set FLOPPY=1. That should
fix the I/O error. Unfortunately for me, I still can't get either RedHat or
Mandrake to install. RedHat always hangs at the "Enter Root Password"
screen and Mandrake stops at TCP when it is first booting off of the CD.
This was all described in my posts back at the end of April. I've just
about given up on getting Linux running on this beast and am at the point of
putting NT back on to just use the system as a disk farm. I've looked at
every resource I could find on the net to get past this with no luck at all.
Any new hints would be appreciated.
