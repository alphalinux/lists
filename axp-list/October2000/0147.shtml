W Bauske wrote:
> > It's a bit disingenuous of Sun to claim Solaris is a 64-bit OS.
>
> Huh? A default of 32 bit is just for compatiblity. People I deal
> with use 64 bit mode. It is 64 bit. Other OS's do the same thing
> because it gives people less trouble when they do the initial
> build on the new OS level. Lots of people don't care about 64bit-ness.
> It's just a waste of bits to them. Those that care will set their
> compile flags accordingly.

It's better described as a hybrid 32/64-bit OS.

People need their 32-bit apps to run on Solaris. That doesn't mean all
the utilities that ship with Solaris need to 32-bit, but they are.

One might mention that none of the core OS utilities need to 64 bits.
That is true, I don't think /bin/ksh really benefits 64-bit pointers.
But in that case why doesn't Alpha have a 32-bit mode? Evidently there
are advantages to having both.
