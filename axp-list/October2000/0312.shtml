W Bauske wrote:

> Iso-H wrote:
> >
> > > greatest, too fast to be able to appeal to the majority. Or are we content to
> > > just appeal to the high end?
> >
> > eventually: "high end" == "dead end"
> >
>
> What makes you think that? Even Intel/AMD want a piece of the
> high end action with IA64/sledgehammer. High end systems will
> always exist. Heck, someone has to be the fastest at any given
> point in time.

Right, but high volumes in low-end have a tendency to drive development faster than
high-end. So it's natural that Intel/AMD are moving into "high-end" because that's
tomorrow's low-end. They've already overtaken Alpha in clock speed (where Alpha used
to be way ahead), and arguably in performance/price (can you find a single benchmark
which proves this wrong- which doesn't need large memory?); it's a matter of time
before they beat it in raw performance- and probably not much time at that.
