Charles L. Chandler (clc31@cornell.edu) wrote:

> A few small segments of the marketplace have been into 64-bit computing for a
> while now--mostly for large-scale, highly-specialized applications. Over the
> next couple of years, however, we are likely to see the mainstream begin to
> move to 64-bits as well (with the introduction of ia64)--that is, an ISA fully
> compatible with the majority of current applications but transitioning to a
> new paradigm of computing. My view is that competing 64-bit technologies
> (alpha, sun, sgi, etc.) already have the edge on any upcoming technology, but
> they are not positioned to take advantage of the mainstream. That is, these
> next several years will provide a unique opportunity for already established
> 64-bit technologies to branch out and take advantage of the rookie ia64 bids.
> How is Alpha prepared to address the mainstream in the next several years? If
> people are going to upgrade their computers, how can we make it more
> advantageous to get an Alpha than an itanium?
> Even the low-cost variants, like UPxx00, are still very expensive for the
> majority of computer owners. In fact, the majority of people don't even buy
> the fastest computer available; there's an entire market segment devoted to
> the "entry-level" machine. In order to address current 64-bit computing
> technologies' lack of volume, don't we have to appeal to the majority? Maybe
> we are retiring good technologies (LX,SX,etc.), in favor of the latest and
> greatest, too fast to be able to appeal to the majority. Or are we content to
> just appeal to the high end?
> It seems to me that we need to provide solutions for more than just the high
> end. The business sector is seeking to meet a specific set of requirements
> for general office computing; the home computing market is seeking to meet yet
> another set of requirements. No current 64-bit technology seems to address
> all of these, and none are in the same price range as x86 variants.
> In addition to competitive pricing, there should be other incentives to
> switching platforms. As an example, Sun has the SunPCi add-on which
> "seamlessly" (according to them) integrates Windows programs into the Solaris
> operating environment. We don't have anything like that for Alpha, do we?
> I brought up these points in an attempt to foster good discussion on salient
> issues. These are the questions that have been running around my head for a
> while now. Comments are welcome.
> -Chaz

If you think the "UPxx00" are expensive, wait until you see InHell's
price tag on their IA64!!! I guarantee it will be much, much more!

JMHO,
Ron
