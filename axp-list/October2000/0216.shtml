On Thu, 19 Oct 2000, David Rysdam wrote:
> I just came across something a cow-orker labelled as
> "RAM for Alphas". I thought Alphas and Intels could
> use almost all the same hardware, including RAM. Is
> this right?

most alphas take true parity memory, which has one extra bit per
byte. these extra bits are used for doing ECC checks on data to correct
single-bit errors and detect double-bit errors, thus providing improved
reliability. true parity RAM will work fine in intel boxen, but most
intels dont take advantage of the extra bits. regular intel memory won't
work in most alphas, however.

hope this helps
