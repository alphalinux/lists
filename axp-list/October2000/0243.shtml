> 'make boot' is a target which runs a default target from
> arch/${arch}/boot/Makefile and what precisely it does depends on what
> was put in all these makefiles; but a convention is that this will
> produce a compressed kernel for the current architecture. If this
> happens to be "i386" then 'make boot' is exactly equivalent to
> 'make zImage' (see for yourself in kernel sources). OTOH nowadays many
> configurations for x86 actually require 'make bzImage' for a bootable
> kernel so an advice printed on your screen by scripts/configure is not
> really correct there either. :-)
>
> In the past I submitted twice or three times patches which would make
> this compilation advice on your screen more "generic", or which would
> at least make this "run 'make zImage'" to show up only on x86 and
> "run 'make boot'" otherwise. They are pretty trivial. Somehow I was
