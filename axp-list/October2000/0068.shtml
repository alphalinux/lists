On Friday, 6 Oct, Ivan Kokshaysky wrote:
> Are you sure 'wtint' was working in previous version? Months ago I looked
> in the palcode from SRM 5.7 for lx164. There was some code at address
> 0x2f80 (wtint entry point). I don't remember details now, but that code
> had _absolutely_ nothing to do with "wait for interrupt".

Well, in my case it behaved according to the documentation, but I'm very
doubful it ever clocked the CPU down, or even in fact waited for an
interrupt (neither of these is required by the architecture specification).

> I strongly suspect that 'wtint' was dropped (or significantly changed)
> some (long?) time ago, but documentation was not updated...

What I know for sure is that it worked very well in AlphaStation 255. The
heatsink was cold during idle time. Apparently, somebody decided that
people who buy Alphas can't afford wasting their precious CPU time...

Thanks for your observations.
