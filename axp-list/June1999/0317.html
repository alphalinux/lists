On Mon, 14 Jun 1999, Todd Fleisher wrote:

> I recently put a 2nd NIC in my Multia and put it into production to
> route over to my DSL bridge. Shortly after this, I began to notice
> tons of errors consistently coming across both interfaces (much more
> on the local firewall interface, most likely because it sees more
> traffic than the Internet interface). The system is running linux
> kernel 2.2.9 compiled with advanced router support (as well as most
> other options common to a linux router). Here is selected output from
> ifconfig (the box has been up for 14 days):
>
> eth0 (Local Firewall Interface)
> UP BROADCAST RUNNING MULTICAST MTU:1500 Metric:1
> RX packets:1455182 errors:0 dropped:0 overruns:0
> TX packets:0 errors:802427781 dropped:1626671 overruns:0
>
> eth1 (DSL Interface)
> UP BROADCAST RUNNING MULTICAST MTU:1500 Metric:1
> RX packets:855484 errors:4050 dropped:0 overruns:0
> TX packets:0 errors:245896270 dropped:694372 overruns:0

Did you just recently move from a 2.0.x kernel to a 2.2.x kernel?
Basically you just need to update your net-tools.

This one is documented in Alan Cox's "Clue Point Two" at
http://roadrunner.swansea.linux.org.uk/clue.html:

  "My network tools say I have billions of errors
      See the Changes file; you need a newer net tools. The reporting
      being wrong is a non fatal problem so worry about it later."
