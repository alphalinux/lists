On Thu, 24 Jun 1999, Christopher W. Curtis wrote:

>Nonetheless, there is an expected minimum requirement of english
>(lowercase 'e') writing skills when something is to be
>published to a primarily English reading audience. "Not my native
>tongue" is an acceptable excuse for less than stellar comprehension of
>the language, but substituting 'u' for 'you' does not fall within this
>category.

Everyone ought to calm down and grow up a little.

Every now and then we all use abbreviations like "IIRC" (if I remember
correctly) "YMMV" (your mileage may vary) .. etc.

Using 'u' instead of 'you' is really no different.. it is a phonetic
abbreviation.

Getting down on people for using such abbreviations is simple stupid at
best. Loads of people are lazy in their use of spoken English -- think
of common slurred words and phrases like "gotta" or "hafta" .. we
let that slide with no problem -- using 'u' instead of 'you' is just a
lazy way of typing.

So.. lets all just calm down, have a few beers.

And to those that hold language as a holy cow, I offer some advise
when in NY:

a) Don't give us a hard time about the accent and bad english

b) Remember to get a hot dawg with kraut and mustard from one of the
   street vendors before the mayor gets rid of them.
