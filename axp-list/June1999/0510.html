Ok, I've checked with Jay Estebrook and with the marketing folks:

The 512meg refered to on that page is an artifact left over from
testing, where configurations with 512meg were the largest that were
tested at that time.

It was never intended that the number 512 be translated into implying
a hard limit of 512megabytes; Linux itself (currently) has a 2 gigabyte
limit, and as my machine shows, runs just fine with 2 gigabytes of memory
on a DS20.

So the the Web page is wrong, and will get fixed ASAP.

Thanks for bringing it to our attention.
