Jeff Bolle wrote:
> I was wondering what scsi controllers people have had the best luck with
> under alpha linux, and which were ones to stay away from. I am looking for a
> relativly inexpensive uw scsi controller, and have been unable to find a
> place that gives a good review/benchmark of scsi controllers. Thanks.

I've got an Intraserver 3140 in a 164SX at home. Seems to perform
quite well. At work, we have a bunch of 164LX machines, some using the
Qlogic ISP 1040B and others using the Intraserver, also with no
problems.

I've seen plenty of reports of problems with the Adaptec cards on this
list, though they do seem to work fine for some folks.

IIRC, I think I paid about $140 for the Intraserver card, but this was
a while ago.

You can get a better idea of prices at www.streetprices.com or
www.pricewatch.com, for example.

Probably a better recommendation, though, is to call up your favorite
Alpha/Linux-friendly vendor; they should know best what works well and
what doesn't, and would be happy to sell you a good board, I'm sure.
Check the list of vendors at
http://www.alphalinux.org/hardware/vendors.shtml. You might also want
to check out the vendor's web sites, as they might say what cards they
put in the machines they sell.

Hope that helps,
Craig
