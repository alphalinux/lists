On Sun, 13 Jun 1999, Wes Bauske wrote:

> "Tommy C. King" wrote:
> > Try strip mode, that will work.
> >
> > Two IDE running on RAID-0.
> >
> > That should give you at 16 MB/Sec granted.
>
> Yeah but it was supposed to be 16MB/sec on a single drive.
> Striping works with either type of device.

I'd agree with that - although I think you have to be careful striping
with IDE. However if I go ahead I'm looking at getting a pair of 10GB
drives rather than 1 big 20GB - the 10GBs are the same speed as the 20GB's
except they just have fewer platters - so striping might be interesting.

> > My old ALTAS-2 SCSI harddisk give a average of 8MB/Sec. New IDE drives beat
> > the hell out of it, and hell cheaper :-).
>
> Whenever you compare different generations of HW, you'll
> see big differences.

Tsk - while no one has really answered my question, we now seem to have
the old flame war round again - tsk!

The problem, as I have said many times before, is that here in the UK I
can buy current generation Ultra-EIDE drives for significantly less than
the price of SCSI drives - perhaps even last generation SCSI drives.

e.g. http://www.scan.co.uk/drives.htm which is a pretty reasonable
supplier in the UK lists:

22Gb IBM-GXP 'Titan' UDMA-66 (8.5ms 512K 7200rpm #289.00 (ex tax)

18.2Gb LVD SCSI IBM 18ES 7ms 2Mb Cache 7200rpm #490.00 (ex tax)

OK, so the SCSI one is a bit better spec - but look at the price
difference - and that 22GB UDMA-66 is a good drive by anyones measure.

In terms of bang/buck the IDE one (in the UK) is a lot better.
