>>> Todd Henderson said:
> I posted this sometime back and Peter was kind enough to respond, not to
> question his wisdom or anything, but does anyone from Compaq on the list
> care to comment. Specifically about booting the DS10 from IDE? We were
> told when we sent in our PO, that we needed SCSI disk to boot Linux from, it
> was not approved or tested or something for IDE drives and Linux.
>
> We will be getting one in to test, but it'd be nice to have some feedback
> from the Compaq types, or anyone that has first hand experience, while we
> are trying to get the DS10 in.

The latest consoles for the DS10/DS20/ES40/XP1000 all now have a separate
device for IDE, rather than SCSI emulation as previous boxes. So you can now
boot from IDE CD-ROM as hda, for example, via:

>>> boot dqa0 -file kernels/generic.gz

I've not tried with a disk, but assume it'll work the same. I'll try to test
it later today, and will report if I find any problems.
