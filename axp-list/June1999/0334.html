On Wed, 16 Jun 1999, Todd Fleisher wrote:

> Yes, as a matter of fact I did. I figured this kind of thing would
> eventually come around and bite me in the ass. Which brings me around
> to my next question: is there a safe way that I can upgrade my glibc?
> I am currently running 2.0.7 and found 2.1.1 under the redhat-6.0
> tree.

That could be really touchy. If you don't know exactly what you're doing,
you might just run the Red Hat 6.0 upgrade instead.

> here's some pertinent package information:
> [root@wakko ~] rpm -U net-tools-1.51-3.alpha.rpm
> failed dependencies:
> libc.so.6.1(GLIBC_2.1) is needed by net-tools-1.51-3
> libc.so.6.1(GLIBC_2.0) is needed by net-tools-1.51-3
> [root@wakko ~] rpm --query glibc
> glibc-2.0.7-29

You might try the source rpm, or if that gives you problems go straight to
the source:

http://www.tazenda.demon.co.uk/phil/net-tools/
