On Tue, 22 Jun 1999, Christian Groessler wrote:

> I think it's a compiler bug.

It is, see below.

> I would expect the "val=READ_WORD()" line to generate an unaligned access
> exception,
> but this doesn't happen. Instead, only one _byte_ at address xadr is read,
> resulting
> in val=0xcc. Val is 0xcccc on intel machines.

I encountered a similar problem some weeks ago; it's the way egcs
generates load-instructions for bytes and shorts: your code results in

        ldq_u $1,0($2)
        extwl $1,$2,$1

This can't work if the adress is not aligned (ldq_u loads the quadword
statring at $2 with the low three bits cleared...).

That's definitively a compiler bug. I solved the problem by upgrading
to egcs 1.1.2
