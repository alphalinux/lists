On Thu, 10 Jun 1999, Christopher W. Curtis wrote:

  Someone else would have a more authoritative answer on this, but I belive
  that the Linux kernel can only handle 1GB of physical memory, but the rest
  (up to 2GB) can be used for cache. This may be a limitation of the Intel
  architecture, though.

linux on intel by default is limited to 960MB or so. But you can
change it to 2GB.

AFAIK, Linux on alpha shouldn't be limited in theory, but in practice
there are problems with PCI address space mapping into the address
space at around 2GB, and linux not being able to work around it
(yet). Ie linux on pci alpha is capped at 2GB.
