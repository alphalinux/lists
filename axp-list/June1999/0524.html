I tried to print on an inkjet printer, through the standard
ghostscript filters. Well, is it only me, or is the 'gs' (ghostscript
5.10) supplied with RH 6.0 really broken on bitmap images? It seems to
have a severe color matching problem, on printer as well as on screen,
displaying almost everything in black, with a few false-color dots.
For example, the testpage prints a red hat, but no white face or any
other details of the logo. The behaviour is independent of the
postscript level of the input file, and all files display well on a
different make of GhostScript 5.10 (ARM/RISC OS, to be specific).
Any solutions?
