uhmm... it's not samba, samba doesn't run su, it calls setuid().

like i said: *look at your cron jobs*.

eg /etc/cron.daily/updatedb:

su nobody -c "/usr/bin/updatedb --output=$TMPFILE --localpaths='/' --
^^^^^^^^^^^^
prunepaths='/tmp /var/tmp /usr/tmp /afs /net' --netpaths='' 2>/dev/null"

anyway, it's perfectly normal to see those messages in your logs on a redhat
system.
