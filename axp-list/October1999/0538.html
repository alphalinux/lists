> random with sound, but not NEARLY as much as any other kernels I've tried.
> Is anyone else on the list booting from SRM and successfully using an ISA
> sound card?
My box is a DPW 500au which has an onboard ES1688 talking across the
ISA bus; boots from SRM fine. Somebody working with Alan (Cox) a couple
months ago messed with the sb16 detection code, causing automatic
detection of some chipsets to report the wrong chipset. You might want to
verify that /dev/sndstat is reporting the right revision level, etc.

> Would the kernel care whether or not an ISA card was configured
> with isacfg in SRM? I would think not, since my soundcard does work, when
> the driver doesn't freeze the whole system. :)
Actually, the kernel does care because it relies on SRM to feed it info
about interrupt and i/o lines. When I reconfiged the irq on my modem and
audio, the kernel was not happy until I updated the info in the firmware
with isacfg.
