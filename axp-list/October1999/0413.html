Seeing as this is an object of contention, my point simply is this: if
you have a problem, and you have something different in your
configuration from the broader installed base (almost an oxymoron in the
Alpha world, but still...) that is removeable, remove it before you send
all of us off on a wild goose chase. In this case, I have seen several
wierd results in the 2.2 series _regardless of architecture_ with 2.95,
as well as in userspace. If changing it is not good enough for you,
don't shoot the messenger for suggesting your altered configuration
could be a problem (be it fact or not).

OBreality: Replies like the one to which I am responding are in fact
useful. Replies like some others which take great pains to sever one's
head, are less than useful AT BEST. I know everyone has bad days (like
me today) but don't snipe someone's head off for making a guess and
trying to be helpful. IMO, it's better to try and be wrong than to sit
on your hands with your config that "works" and not help at all. I am
currently trying to debug more major problems than I can even count
(like QLogic cards being totally busted on Alpha in 2.3 > 12); probably
why I misread what Alan was talking about.

All that said, I believe this was a problem with a PC164 - way back at
the beginning, no? I currently have mine running 2.2.12ext3 (about to
be ext3a) without any hiccups for days, and before that at 2.2.12
without hiccups for weeks. Can the originator of this thread compare
current notes, and we'll see what we're up against here?
