Michael Huntingdon (hunting@ix.netcom.com) writes:
>Assuming the system in question is the Linux ready flavor
>(dj-pb10a-)..yes.
>According to Compaq the system mentioned would also have been
>ordered and
>shipped with the scsi controller (kzpba-ca or kzpcm-da), one
>of their scsi
>disks, and a 3x-bn50a-01. If this criteria has been met, then
>the system is supported.

This is the crux of the problem methinks: the ds10 I received
was supposed to be "Linux ready" but contained a KZPBA-CX
instead of a CA. This necessitated building a replacement
kernel for the bootdisk (quite how i would do this without
another alpha to hand is another matter).
 From past experiences I doubt if Compaq themselves are
particularly to blame. I think the distribution channels are
in the habit of sticking in anything that vaguely matches
the product description.

Given the various levels of feedback this thread has got, I
think we ought to elevate this particular installation issue
to a FAQ.
