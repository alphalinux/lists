You can have a 0 in your network number. You CAN NOT have a 0 node address
with-in your network. the 0 "node address" is your network address. your
network number with all the zero bits from your subnet mask set is the
broadcast address for your network.

for instance...
192.168.0.0 is the network address
a subnet mask of 255.255.255.128
would give a broadcast address of 192.168.0.127
and valid ip addresses for machines in the network will range from
192.168.0.1 to 192.168.0.126

however if you wanted to have 254 ip's in your network, just change the
subnet mask to 255.255.255.0, and your broadcast address will be
192.168.0.255. ( simple binary math )
