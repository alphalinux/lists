Yes, its name was ARCSBIOS, codeveloped with Deskstation. Deskstation is
out of business with only its motherboard sales still operating until the
EOL of the Ruffian RPX-2/RPX-4. Samsung sold this same board as the 164UX
and 164BX (BX minus the Ethernet and SCSI).

ARCSBIOS does have one or two nice features, but it is a dead end. There
is no interest in placing ARCSBIOS (which is designed for NT and only NT)
on any other system. SRM is the way, I just hope Compaq management really
understand the imprortance of Alpha firmware to the Alpha markets.
