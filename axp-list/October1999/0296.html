Currently I'm running 'rawhide-release-19990923-1', which
has a few RPMS that are newer even than the 6.1-intel equivs.

Just checking in on a mirror, they appear to be up to
'rawhide-release-19991012-1.noarch.rpm' for alphas, with
a few files with recent (>19991010) for an mtime.
This offers at least a small amount of encouragement...

On another note, how profitable is Alpha for RedHat?
Personally, I downloaded RH-6.0 and burned a CD for
my 5 machines (1 old, 1 very old, 3 ancient)
Later on, I purchased a copy of RH-6.0-Alpha, just to "show support".

I suspect the following about Alpha users in _general_ (like myself):
1. They have access to a high-speed internet connection.
2. They have more machines on average than an Intel user.
3. They are fewer in number.

So, for (say) 10 intel machines
- there are 8 owners.
- 1/4 (2) of them download/copy the software.
- 6 of them buy the software.

But, for (say) 10 Alpha machines
- there are 3 owners.
- 2/3 (2) of them download it.
- only 1 purchase.

Add to that the fact that there are more intels out there.
It wouldn't surprise me to hear [100-to-1] [intel-to-alpha] sales numbers.
