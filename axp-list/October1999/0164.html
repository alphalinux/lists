LSI bought Symbios Logic from Hyundai (or was it Mitsubishi?). The reason
I recommend IntraServer is because they directly support just about every
platform, plus the Tru 64 OS requires an IntraServer to enable U2 and other
advanced features, even if your stock LSI has the same ASIC and feature set
(driver hacks to make you get certified hardware).

I also really like Qlogic boards, they have the superb ability to dump
their NVRAM to a text file, let you tune it by hand and shoot it back into
the NVRAM to use your new settings (you can reach all those settings the
config utility won't let you touch). By the way, do you know if the newer
Alpha workstation and servers use the later QLA1040B controllers or are
they still shipping with QLA1020x (SCSI II Fast, Fast/Wide)? I was sure Tru
64 and Open VMS were supporting the later Ultra family of Qlogic at least
(don't think they support one U2 boot controller yet).

You might be interested to check out the source for the driver from their
beta driver download page (not for NT, for Linux). I'd like to hear from
you what you think of their code.

Other than LSI based controllers and QLogic (Atto is one of their OEM
partners), I'm not impressed by most of the other controllers out there.
Mylex's re-named Buslogic controllers are close, but lack the tools and
hackability of the above favorites (at least in my experience).
