At the risk of straying far off topic ...

There's a reason why my habit is ls and not dir. The reason has to do with
1.) the way ls -l and dir display, and 2.) the fact that I'm blind.

Because the actual filename in a dir output is in a column near the right
of the screen -- preceded by all manner of possibly, but not necessarily,
useful info, I have to work harder to get at the filenames with my screen
access technology. I think what I do with keyboard commands is what most
sighted users do with their eyes without even thinking about it--focus on
the filename and then glance at remaining data as needed.

With ls I can count on a simple filename dump at the left and read it far
more quickly. It works, usually. In other words, it works so frequently
that I haven't bothered changing my habits.

Moral of the story, imho, is that I'm not the one off standard here --
just in case it matters.
