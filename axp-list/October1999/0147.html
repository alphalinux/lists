Dave Wreski wrote:
>
> ... and am now receiving different
> compile errors:
>
> make[2]: Entering directory
> `/usr/src/linux-2.2.13pre15/arch/alpha/math-emu'
> gcc -D__KERNEL__ -I/usr/src/linux-2.2.13pre15/include -Wall
> -Wstrict-prototypes -O2 -fomit-frame-pointer -fno-strict-aliasing -pipe
> -mno-fp-regs -ffixed-8 -c -o fp-emul.o fp-emul.c
> fp-emul.c: In function `udiv128':
> fp-emul.c:118: warning: left shift count is negative
.......

It says here "warning:" and not "error:"!

As explained on this list a long time ago by Richard Henderson
these warnings are coming from an unused part of an expanded macro
with compiler not noticing that it has nothing to worry about.
As such they are troublesome to avoid.

The bigger gotcha is that related math-emu changes cause em86 not
to work anymore and, so far, nobody knows true reasons for that;
math-emu support as used in 2.2.11 cooperates with em86 and provides
a "drop-in" replacement if you need/want em86. At this moment I am
not aware of a better fix.

> Also, it did stop completely with a segfault at
> one point,

This was something else even if it printed somewhere close on your
screen. Which compiler you were using?
