Kevin Brown wrote:
>
> Part of the SMP problem for X86s is the fact that all the CPUs share the same
> bus to the memory whereas on Alpha they each have their own bus to use. The
> newest CPU from AMD, the Athlon, uses not only the Slot A connection, but also
> the bus architecture from the Alpha, so I'll be pretty happy to see SMP Athlon
> boards come out since the Athlon has been wiping Intel's but on the benchmarks.
>

I hear the latest Intel PIII's (coppermine) are even with
Athlons. Reason being they moved to 133MHZ memory. At
least AMD are keeping Intel on their toes.

Both systems are choked by their memory subsystems, even
moving to 133MHZ SDRAM. I believe both Athlon and Coppermine
are capable of 4 flops per clock but there is no way the
memory can deliver sufficient data to see it happen.
Same is true of the Apple G4 Altivec. Wish they'd get
the memory subsystems sped up.

Should be interesting to see who delivers 1GHZ systems
first, probably AMD in 1Q2000.
