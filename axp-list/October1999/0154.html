Your point is a good one Kirk. Since I am no longer with Q I can say that
one of our largest OEM customers did the work to port OpenBoot to their
Alpha platforms. They even offered us the use of their OpenBoot code
(but of course they wouldn't have supported it). We also looked several
times at using it on some internal development. The decision was finally
to stick with SRM because of the PAL implications (and because it
was done and could be easily modified for our R&D).
    If there is interest, the company founded by the creators of OpenBoot
(which name escapes me at the moment), probably has rights to the
port done by this Silicon Valley file server company, (one of whose
principle developers moved to the file serve company), and could
provide OpenBoot very easily, and supply the support (that is their
business). I won't put the names on line, but would put an individual
on the right track . This would appear to be an OpenBoot opportunity
to expand their (yes, there is a profit incentive) market. In retrospect,
It would
probably have been to Digital's advantage to use OpenBoot from
the beginning, though I doubt Microsoft could have been convinced
to use it.

Mike Breen

> I have been noticing that indeed, we are in need of a console firmware to
> be standard across all new Alpha systems which can support our remaining
> OSs (most of them UNIX, one VMS). The alternative is quite different and
> probably much cheaper if implemented: OpenBoot, a firmware used on SPARC
> machines and a few PowerPC development systems (not yet released).
> OpenBoot is quite nice, easy to navigate and fully UNIX oriented. Its
also
> free of charge to use and portable across any and every platform. Its
> memory footprint is similar to SRM (which I've seen as System Resource
> Monitor and System Refernce Manual, depending on the age of the system
> documentation), OpenBoot has nothing to do with Compaq and does not
require
> permission or liceinsing as such. A full OpenBoot firmware is better than
> a stripped down SRM any day, though a full SRM is pretty tough to beat
> feature wise. OpenBoot supports remote consoles, diagnostics, scripting,
> OS config, debugging, remote firmware upgrade, remote boot (obviously) of
> OS and remote diagnostics. It aint' bad. If you've had a chance to play
> with a recent Ultra SPARC machine, its one of the nicer features that give
> me that "warm fuzzy." I love a good firmware.
>
> Anyone listening? API and Slot B clone vendors should investigate this
> IEEE 1275 specification and give buyers the option: More costly SRM
> equipped version (it's Compaq licensed code) or OpenBoot version (no added
> cost since source is free). The OpenBoot version may be easier to build
> around since their are many Sun devleopers who could be leveraged for the
> task (boot code source would be readily portable once you convert from Big
> Endian to Little Endian that is). It should also make writing console
> applicaitons (for say, configuring your RAID contoller) much easier to do
> without having to buy a development kit from Compaq. There are also quite
> a few SPARC console apps for OpenBoot which could be ported to ease the
> transition (there are few for SRM, so this could be a plus). The OpenBoot
> UltraSPARC console app that dumps all NVRAM boot and advanced system
config
> options to a text file (which you can edit and tweak the hell out of)
would
> be great. Once you create a backup copy of your config to file, you could
> tweak away and load the new parameters to test, facilitating some more
> performance out the system in the process while providing a safety net.
>
> Anyone interested should query the all important parties (as I intend to)
> to get OpenBoot if SRM proves too costly or Compaq drags its feet on
> licensing the Slot B clone board vendors (disasterous effect on Alpha
sales).
>
> Keep up your spirits, there IS light at the end of this tunnel,
>
> --Kirk
