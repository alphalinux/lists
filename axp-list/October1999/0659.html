Look at it this way:

21264 EV-67 Alpha in 0.25 um aluminium process reaches up to 833 MHz for
shipping systems in 1999. To reach 1 GHz and above you need 0.18 um EV-68
part available first in 2Q 2000, whose copper-SOI version will go up to 1.5
GHz by end-2000. Both IBM and Samsung should manufacture EV-68, EV-7 and
EV-8 parts (the accent may be on IBM for highest-speed Cu-SOI EV-7 and EV-8
parts - the fastest EV-8 parts could reach 2 GHz in 2002, with 300+
SPECint95 and 500+ SPECfp95 performance estimates using 3+ MB on-die L2 and
eight parallel on-chip Direct RDRAM channels for 12.8 GByte/s main mem
bandwidth per CPU - and that is without using SMT feature).

AMD will definitely have 0.18 um part shipping before the EV-68. So yes, if
that part goes way beyond 1 GHz, and AMD implements their new promised
compiler optimisations, then you could expect that part to match 833 MHz
21264 at least for a while. The guideline is: 833 MHz EV-67 21264 in
Tsunami dual-bus system (DS20, DP264) with 8 MB DDR-SSRAM cache at 555 MHz
(2/3 CPU speed) reaches 48 SPECint95 and 103 SPECfp95 per CPU using Tru64
compilers.
