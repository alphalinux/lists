John,

I had the same problem on an XP1000, where fdisk didn't see cylinders behind
522....
I got around it by partitioning the disk with another Unix system, although
I understand that is not an easy option for everybody.
A lot of people have reported RH fdisk to be broken, but I haven't seen a
fix yet. If you have a RH5.2 hanging around you can partition your disk by
starting the RH5.2 install.
(I haven't tried to replace 6.0's fdisk with 5.2's...)

/Lucas
