> I picked up a Cheap Byte's
> CD at a computer show for $5.
        That's nice - but I thought we were talking about Red Hat's 6.1
retail package. You can get dists from LinuxMall for $1.98 - but you get
what you pay for... the CD. Like I said, the $80 for the retail package of
RH gets you a whole lot more than a CD and a book.
        RH is well aware of the "caliber" of the user in the Alpha
community, and there are those that will likely never do more than download
the ISO and burn a CD, but there are plenty of sys admins out the who _will_
buy the retail package (or have it bought & thrust upon them) - particularly
given the insurgence of the low profile rack servers and the idea of
"Internet appliances" - the Compaq DS10L will be one such machine, and you
can be sure that lots of them will ship with RH installed. The DS20 is
another prime target given that NT will die on it in a few months time.....
Red Hat is predicting (banking on, really) that 25 million servers will ship
with Linux installed in the next 2 years. That's servers, in the
enterprise, not solely IA boxes for netsurfers and MP3 downloaders... We
happen to believe them to be correct - we get more calls every day looking
for Linux.

> It might be wise for an absolute
> beginner to Linux to purchase the box set the first time. But most of
> us can do without the full-blown version.
        Interesting comment - I've been using Linux on IA since late 95 -
but this DS20 was my first Alpha load. I felt like a total newbie on the
install..... Had I spent $80 on the current 6.0 package from RH (I got it
free) I would likely be a bit miffed given it's lack of content (and that
includes functional software). But I still get a month of free phone
support and all that jazz. Even if I never crack the manual I've likely
gotten my money's worth - I've been shipped documents from and made
contacts inside Red Hat that I will likely use over again - is that worth it
to everyone? I guess maybe not, but it certainly is in my line of work....
        It will be interesting to see what the 6.1 release will hold,
particularly since RH (finally) just got a stable of Alpha gear from Compaq
a week or two ago...... I've told both Compaq and Red Hat that they have
some pretty big strides to make before the "Alpha pieces" meet the "IA
pieces".... Being Compaq's single largest Alpha reseller I'd like to think
what we say holds some merit, but then who can tell.....
