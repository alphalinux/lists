Depends on what you call "everyone". Almost everyone I know is not running
KDE or Gnome - this beeing our institute (many boxes) my friends (again:
many boxes) and our Linux User Group (many boxes). I believe new users are
lured into using KDE/Gnome since many distributions default to it and new
users don't see the choice. It's like the command line or the vi: If
you've been told that its horrible your are less likly to try.

I do not say that there arn't areas for KDE/Gnome. If you intend
to get a "better" Windows than Windows KDE is sure a good way to
go. Personally I rather look at the applications and have a lean but
flexible window manager myself (used fvwm, now WindowMaker).
