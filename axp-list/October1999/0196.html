>
> Its worth adding that you could add disklabels to your partitions and
> mount by disklabel, or mount by UUID. That is a pretty robust
> solution, which would mean your mounting would still work perfectly if
> you swapped all the disks around.

With this small catch that you have mount your root partition to read
mount points and their labels so, unless you use initrd, you have a
small chicken-and-egg problem. :-) It is easily fixable and one can
argue that it is better to edit one position in /etc/fstab than many.
This is definitely attractive when you have lots and lots of disks.

> You can also use scsidev to make some device entries by controller,
> lun etc, and use those in your fstab.

The whole trouble here was that depending on a scan order and slot
positions you will get swapped controller 0 and controller 1 so I am not
sure how scscdev, or other stuff like 'devfs', would help in this case.

A solution with modules for "higher" controllers, like adopted by
the original poster, works if you have controllers with different
drivers so you know what is a loading order.
