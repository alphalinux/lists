Well, I'd also like it if we could just talk about techie things, however
at the end of the day Alpha is a matter of money like any other product.
And I should know that, since we did bet our business for quite a while on
API products. We only took off (and took off exponentially) in success once
we moved from AlphaNT to Tru64 UNIX, some 6 months before Compaq made that
clumsy but perfectly undestandable decision.

My point is: Alpha was in this situation precisely because of lack of
active marketing (including public roadmap and commitment info) necessary
for the users to gain long-term confidence in it. Now Compaq is opening its
cards at least, and yes DECUS Singapore had free attendance for all
sponsored by Compaq (over 600 highly relevant attendees; interesting
program; good food) and good Tru64 and Linux sessions. Now let's see how
API sales conf in Boston goes on in a week or so - I hope for some positive
miracle...
