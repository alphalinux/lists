> What we as a community could do would be to have some webpages which
> do a better job of tracking what's wrong with various distributions,
> e.g. our own errata pages. Then people could not only fix their
> systems, they could see what other distributions might have fewer
> bugs. We all agree that the distributions themselves don't always do a
> good job of reporting their own bugs.

        ALO has a couple lists of known problems in RedHat/AXP
releases:
http://www.alphalinux.org/docs/redhat6.0-probs.shtml (6.0)
http://www.alphalinux.org/docs/redhat-probs.shtml (5.2)
        It could be better, but time to update these seems to be
a rare resource as of late.
 
> Volunteers? What I imagine is some webpages split up by distribution
> and release name in which people could create items and comment on
> existing items. For example: RedHat 5.2: "man" is broken and
> segfaults; later someone could add "fix now on redhat site", etc.
        I'll have to run this by the ALO colo provider, but I think
they'll agree to it... If someone out there wants to update the RedHat bug
lists and/or add bug pages for other distros (Debian, SuSE), ALO would be
willing to host the lists. We don't have to, of course - wherever is most
convenient.
