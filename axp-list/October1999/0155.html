Alex,

Always buy SCSI drives that are low profile (i.e. less than half height) to
get the maximum data capacity per drive platter possible. Drives using
massively magnetic drive heads and PRML are preferrable becuase they
optimize performance and allow higher capacities. U2 drives, for all their
marketting noise have no real practical performance advantage over
UltraSCSI drives (the 80 MHz signalling rate also imposes additional
sensitivity to cabling and termination issues).

Seagate 9.5 GB LP drives are at the best price/performance curve.

--Kirk
