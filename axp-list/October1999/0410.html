Peter Rival wrote:
>
> I definitely wouldn't trust 2.2 series kernel built with 2.95. Someone high
> up the hierarchy (methinks it was Alan Cox, but it could have been Linus...)
> specifically stated that 2.2 was not coded with 2.95 in mind, and if you
> insist on using it, it's only a coincidence that it works.

Ugh! I love quotes out of context. One may learn wondrous things.

Concerns you mentioned are based on the fact that gcc-2.95 works
somewhat differently with i386 assembler (some would argue that these
"features" on which older kernels rely on are outright gcc bugs). This
affects to a greater degree kernels from 2.0 series and, as a matter of
fact, many people run i386 compiled with gcc-2.95 (or even with a more
aggresive compilers - like pgcc) without any ill effects; Mandrake
distribution is doing something of that kind. Alan Cox does not like to
wonder if some bug reports are caused by changes in a compiler and is is
hard to blame him. Most things which surface are not exactly obvious.

> That's not a
> direct quote, but I believe it is the spirit in which it was intended.

Now could you explain, please, how this affects Alpha? BTW - I happen
to be running on Alpha kernels compiled with 2.95.1 Recently I run
intesive compilation jobs resulting in few hundreds of megabytes of
ready binaries without a single hiccup. My X server, OTOH, goes from
time to time catatonic and that one definitely was not compiled with
2.95. :-)

If you have real bug reports against 2.95 on Alpha (I filed one
recently about g++ from 2.95) then, I am sure, everybody would be
interested.
