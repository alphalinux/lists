THe beep codes are hardware error conditions. You reset the firmware every
time you power cycle the machine (cold or warm reboot). You must correct
the hardware problem which is causing the problem, the firmware is not your
problem per se.

How many beeps and in what order? This tells you alot about what is
causing the problem. If you are running in SRM and have selected VGA video
but do not have a supported video controller or a malfunctioning controller
(or a controller in the wrong PCI slot, i.e slot 0), memory which is coming
out of its 200 pin DIMM sockets, failed memory, failed CPU, improperly
inserted CPU daughtercard, or board level (chipset) failure can cause these
issues. Memory and video are by far the most common. Take things out and
re-seat them. If your memory DIMMS have less than sparkling pins, give
them a quick rub with a pencil eraser (keep yourself grounded) and try
again.

Make sure your video controller is in PCI 0 (closest the keyboard
connector) and that it is seated properly.

Good luck,
