I've been running 5.1 + mods on my Ruffian very happily for nearly a year
now, and broke down and decided to try getting 6.0 to run.

hahahahahahaha.

If i follow the instructions for making a boot disc, and boot from my
already-installed milo from the floppy, it decompresses two lines of #
then hangs for a bit.

After a while, it starts printing out

PYXIS machine check NOT expected
pyxis_machine_check: vector=0x670 la_ptr=0xfffffc000020a5a0
oo pc=0xfffffc00007a6180 size=0x1b8 procoffset=0xd8 sysoffset 0x160
                    ^^^^

The last four digits of pc change, and the screen scrolls by for a long
time until it stops at 68c0. The previous number was 6680.

And then it's well and truly hung.

I've tried to get it to boot my old kernel from my sda2 partition and
mount the cdrom as root, but it hangs when it tries to mount the cdrom:
ncr53c875-0-<5,*>: SYNC transfers not supported

FAT bread failed

Erm. Odd questions. This is my bios info:
ARCS BIOS 1.3.061/SN3010000.51088A APC 164UX/BX 67MHz/600MHz
21164A Rev 5

The RedHat book cheerfully tells me to upgrade my bios, but this is a
non-Digital board, so i've not been able to find a new bios yet. I've
not tried upgrading my milo yet. [I'm a little leery about replacing
the milo currently installed which *works* with a milo which may not
work.]

Any hints on what i'm doing wrong or how i should modify the install
routine? I know that at least *some* other people are running 6.0 on
a Ruffian, and i'm wondering what they did that i've not clued to yet...
