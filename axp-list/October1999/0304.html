Tom,

I assume you are talking RH 6.0....

boot sda1:/boot/vmlinuz root=/dev/sda1

should do the trick.
The problem is the location of the linux kernel on your filesystem.

For future reference, you can view the contents of linux and msdos file
systems at the Milo prompt. Simply use 'ls' and 'cd' like you normally
would in a unix environment (except that there are no args like 'ls -al').
'ls sda1:' will show you the contents of that filesystem. 'sda1:' by itself
will change that parition to be the current one, and then simply 'ls' will
list its contents.

Also, the Milo 'help' command will list more advanced features.

Mike
