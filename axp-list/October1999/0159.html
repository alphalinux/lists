Yep, UltraSCSI is 20 MHz, Ultra2 is 40 MHz and Ultra3 is 80 MHz. I also
neglected to mention that a new SCSI controller would be necessary to
achieve the rate. Cabling and termination must be within about 2% of the
90 Ohm impedence and must be LVD (Low Voltage Differential) or the
controller will revert the bus to "safer" UltraSCSI speeds.

My point on UltraSCSI versus Ultra2 (which was admittedly lost completely)
is that the internal transfer rate of the drives between UltraSCSI and
Ultra2 (even with newer electronics to optimize such things as prefetch and
dynamically reconfigurable segmented caches) are little more than a few
percent faster. On a system in which there will be 3 or fewer drives, the
Ultra2 spec doesn't offer a significant performance improvement since U2's
primary performance advantage is in disk arrays (I'm becoming more lucid,
can you tell?)

My current favorites for cross-OS support on ALpha is the IntraServer
series of controllers, some of which feature one or more U or U2
controllers and a Fast Ethernet controller, all on one board.

>NO!
>The 40Mhz signalling rate (80Mhz is Ultra3 which isn't really out in any
>force yet) is only performed when ALL devices on the bus are electrically
>happy to drive the bus with low-voltage differential (LVD) protocol. This
>*improves* the electrical transfer properties, and *increases* the maximum
>cabling distances (to 25M IIRC) and decreases the chance of parity errors or
>transmission retries. If the controller (Initiator) detects the presence of
>*any* device or terminator incapable of using Ultra2 SCSI (LVD) then the
>whole bus reverts to std Ultra SCSI with all the cabling limitations and
>speeds. This is why it is important to make sure that all devices on an LVD
>bus are in fact true LVD devices. Some SCSI vendors such as Sony with the
>SDT-9000 DDS4 DAT drive claim that their device is Ultra2/LVD compatible --
>but what they mean is that it can be used on an LVD bus in SE (Single-ended)
>mode. This clobbers the whole bus to SE, and again you are limited to 1.5M
>length (3M if 4 devices or less) and max 40MB/sec (ie 20Mhz signalling with
>UW) transfer rates. One thing easy to overlook is the terminators. You
>*must* use LVD terminators if you don't want your bus to run as a
>single-ended std Ultra bus.
