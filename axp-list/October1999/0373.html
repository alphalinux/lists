Greetings ...

Yes, I can confirm that there are PC164 systems that just will not work
with SCSI at all.
I own such a system. I tried Adaptec, Symbios Logic, IntraServer and a few
more adapters
that I can't remember. Non of them worked. I bought new memory, but that
did no good
either. All would boot up just fine, but would eventually hang. Often with
absolutely no
error message. And I mean "hang", it would take a hard reset (power off /
power on) to
regain control. I spent months screwing around with SCSI cables,
termination and everything
else I could think of. I eventually got tired of the "bull shit" and used
IDE drives. They work
OK, but "they ain't SCSI". Everything new I do now is on Intel based
hardware. Intel
may not be as elegant as Alpha, but Intel just seems to work and is one
hell of a lot
cheaper. And *MUCH* less aggravating. I don't buy hardware to spend all
my free time
trying to make it work!

No "flames" about Alpha vs. Intel are necessary from Alpha "bigots". This
is America, you
can buy and use any hardware you want. I really don't care what you think
is "better",
whatever that means.

_don_
