On Wed, 27 Oct 1999, Alex Angerhofer wrote:

> The problem with this as well as with other drives in the range at
> or above 9GB is that they are all UltraSCSI, and many come with a
> wider connector. There are SCA -> SCSI interface adaptors that
> reduce the connector width back to 50 pins, but I am not sure
> whether they would work on the alpha's.

They work fine. I have a pair of Quantum Viking 2GB drives and a
Quantum Atlas II 9GB drive, and will soon have an Atlas III 9GB drive,
all of which are SCA drives and are connected to my as200 using
the adapters you mention.

> Furthermore, the UltraSCSI drives are spec'ed at 20 or 40 MB/s which
> is more than my current NCR53C-810 controller will yield.

In that case, the controller will be the bottleneck, and you will see no
more than 10MB/sec (actually more like 8-9MB/sec). If that will be an
issue, you should look into getting additional controllers.

> My questions at this points therefore are:
> 1. Would it be possible to get an UltraSCSI drive to work with a SCSI-2
> controller on the AlphaStations, possibly with the interface adaptor
> that reduces the connector to 50 pin? Has anyone done this in the
> past successfully? I realize that there would be an obvious
> degrading in speed from the specs. I am also wondering whether the
> old SCSI-2 adaptor will actually be able to handle harddrives larger
> than 8GB.

Size is independent of bus speed. Also, I think you may be confusing
some of the terminology (as complicated as SCSI terminology is, that's
very easy to do :-) ). Others here I'm sure can illustrate better than
I, and hopefully they'll correct me. UltraSCSI is not the same as SCA.
SCA stands for Single Connector Attachment and includes both power and
data, having been designed for hot-swap.

Ultra SCSI is basically SCSI-2 renegotiated at twice the speed (20MB/sec
instead of 10). There's actually a lot more to it than that, but to you
and I that's pretty much all that matters.

> 2. Would it be possible to add the UltraSCSI drive as a second drive to
> the machine by getting a PCI bus SCSI controller?

Yes. In fact, if you install an LVD (80MB/sec) or UltraWide (40MB/sec)
controller, you will be able to put several drives on one controller.

Actually, I would relegate the on board controller to only handling slow
devices such as cdrom and tape drives, and use the new controller for
hard disks.

> This way I would
> keep the old controller on the motherboard hooked up to the original
> 1GB Quantum HD and plug the IBM HD in to a new SCSI controller on the
> PCI bus. This might be the better solution since it seems to
> preserve the speed. Would it be possible to boot off the new disk at
> all? Following Kirk Erichsen's and Paul Bunn's suggestions I am
> looking at the Symbios 22915 PCI->Ultra3 adapter or its little
> brother, the SYM8951U. Is there anything that looks questionable
> with this plan of action?

Sounds like a good plan to me.
