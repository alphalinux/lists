Wes Bauske wrote:

> With Coppermine only slightly slower at a given clock rate,
> there is little incentive to buy Athlon. (except those folks who
> despise Intel)

I think being substantially cheaper makes a good incentive. Of course the current
Athlon's are not copper yet. This has Intel comparing a newer technology to an older
one and still failing. the Dresden fab will be cranking out Copper Athlon processors
very soon, which will widen the gap that AMD already leads in even more.

There is little to no incentive to waste your money on the Intel processors,
especially considering Intel's track record. Athlon has a vastly superior motherboard.
You will not be upgrading your system with a new motherboard every 4-6 months like you
will with Intel just to stay current.

I went through this with Alphas.

I bought a 533mhz. Alpha system back whenb they were 4995.00 (several years ago) beat
the hell out of any of the Intel offerings for speed. (I use Lightwave 3D for
animation) A friend of mine bought a new PC system that was top off the line back
then) over the last few years, to stay current he has bought 5 motherboards just to
stay current, going from an AT motherboard to an early ATX, then the socket 7 was
limited to 200mhz, so he had to get a different board to accomodate socket 7 with MMX,
then to PII, he bought a 233, 266, 300 etc. just to keep up. completely changed ram
twice as I recall going from his original to EDO and then to SDRAM. during all these
changes and the thousands of extra dollars he spent, his Intel box ended up costing
WAY more than I paid for my Alpha box, and he still could not lay any claim to being
faster or for that matter even close. MY original Alpha just keeps working right
along, no upgrades, no modifications, no swapping parts and it still fares well
against even the newest of Intel systems.

The Athlon, long term is going to be the same way. All the hardware sites say Intel
has hit the wall with their outdated technology, and all have said the Athlon has
barely scratched the surface.

Where then is your incentive to buy Intel unless you are only interested in the Intel
Inside sticker?
