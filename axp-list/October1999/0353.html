With regards to your message at 09:03 AM 10/15/99 +0200, Jaap Schellekens.
Where you stated:
>On Fri, 15 Oct 1999, Martin Kurz wrote:
>
> > for use with a scanner I want to install an Adaptec 1505 ISA pnp card
> > into my PWS433a
> > (with alpha bios) as a secondary SCSI controller.
> > So far I had no success. while pnpdump returns reasonable data isapnp
> > complains about a resource conflict, when fed with the config. file.
> > Neither of the two io base addresses the board can do work. Has anybody
> > got a clue what I'm doing wrong? Kernel is 2.2.9, isapnp
> > is the latest version.
>
>I tried the same but gave up. I think the aha152x driver needs some work
>before it runs on the alpha (so i was told, correct me if i am wrong)
It is not just a problem on Alpha. It is a problem on all machines. Adaptec
is so insistent on keeping their silence on driver related matters that we
are all forced to reverse engineer their products. As a result Adaptec is
just not a particularly good choice in general. Certainly the 2940 family
has had more time and attention spent in getting a satisfactory result, but
even so we find that these cards are less reliable in Linux than a Symbios
Logic or Buslogic product. The difference being that those companies
actively support Linux, and are willing to share information with device
driver developers.
