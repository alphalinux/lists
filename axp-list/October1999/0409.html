On Mon, 18 Oct 1999, EXT Robert Harley wrote:
> ge2 = (ge1 & ~eq | eq & ge1<<1) & 0xAA;

For the C coders out there, an expression of the form

D = (A & M) | (B & ~M);

can be turned into

D = A ^ ((A ^ B) & M);

i.e. change (^) the bits which are different (A^B) and within (&) the mask

Now it appears that the alpha can do (B&~M) as one instruction, and
there's no need to create a temporary ~M, so this isn't a win for us.
However, as it is a win on other architectures, and isn't a loss for
us I'd thought I'd share it.
