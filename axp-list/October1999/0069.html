>> My guess, it will be the "pretty gosh darn hard" part, porting PALcode to
>> something other than SRM.

>> HOWEVER, i have not given up hope that you fellows will release SRM to any
>> and all takers to grow the Slot B clone markets and standardize your new
>> products (never really liked AlphaBIOS anyway). If you have any leverage,
>> please advise management against licensing SRM at some ridiculous price

>> B will only work for Compaq positively by making SURE every Slot B board
>> can be flashed to accept SRM over the PC BIOS used by the K7 Anthon.
>
>As for SRM licensing, that one has been around the block several times.
>The other thing you might want to keep in mind is that Slot-B is not a
>Compaq design, and as far as I know Compaq is not using it in any of its
>boxes.

My point exactly, why!? Slot B is an open standard (Compaq co-developed
it, costs are entirely based on design costs, not licensing). If Compaq
doesn't adopt Slot B in ANY of its product offerings, what kind of signal
does that send? It would be like Mercedes Benze and Bosch co-developing
Disc brakes only to continue to use less efficient drum brakes (which
didn't happen). Why develop something for someone else to use and gain
nothing from it directly? As for SRM licensing going around several times,
someone needs to stop and consider the ramifications of NOT licensing SRM:

1) AlphaBIOS died with NT, no more AlphaBIOS
2) Continuing to ship AlphaBIOS on systems means only Linux can be used
(with MILO) even though it is inferior to SRM (designed to address UNIX and
VMS)
3) ARCSBIOS was owned by Deskstation, Deskstation is dead and it was NT
firmware anyway.
4) SRM is a superior firmware (with the only aspect being the lack of
filesystem support at the firmware level ala ARC/AlphaBIOS) and would make
a nice standard.
5) Not licensing SRM to Slot B vendors KILLS Alpha because the clone market
will never be able to grow without Compaq support and SRM code.
6) Changing over to OpenBoot firmware will be lenghty, expensive and
troublesome, the only reason to do this would be to work AROUND Compaq
mangement, but this is nearly impossible. No Alpha firmware for the Slot B
market means, once again NO Alpha. Where are these commitments we're
hearing about, are we the only ones to see the glaring holes?
