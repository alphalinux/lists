To be honest, there is nothing wrong with using a graphical environment on a
workstation. That's what it's for.. There are many useful tools in Gnome
that make me more productive. Enlightenment is more overhead than Window
Maker, but that's why Enlightenment is configured for a workstation install
and FVWM for Server...and I wouldn't run any GUI on a server. As a
workstation where I use different apps for productivity, web browsing, ftp,
testing, and playing games, Linux + Enlightenment + Gnome beats the sh*t out
of Windows. There is nothing better than being able to play Doom or Quake
while monitoring Glance Plus at the same time on the same machine. Maybe
even capture some packets with Ethereal to so who the hell is ARPing the
hell out of my network! Windowmaker is a *very* cool manager and the beauty
of it as that I can alternate whenever I want!

Mark
