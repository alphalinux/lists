On Tue, 26 Oct 1999, Wysong Richard C SrA 85 GP/CP wrote:

> If it lacks all the boggy overhead, why is it everyone is using KDE/Gnome?
> (not sarcasm, i'm wondering since I haven't used it)

We have a mixture of machines here ranging from old SUN4C machines through
low P75s up to the Alphas. I have WindowMaker on them all because it is
pretty enough to keep people happy, is easily conficurable and runs like a
champ even on the older hardware. For example, on my P75 laptop (40MB
RAM) I have to wait around 1 min before I have a usable desktop under KDE,
a little longer under GNOME, but with WindowMaker I am off and running in
less than 10 seconds. Same is true for the old SPARCs.

I have used a few of the GNOME/KDE apps on occasion but generally come
back to text based tools. I also love having WindowMaker dock apps, it is
incredible what is available.

I guess because I never had to use Windows for anything I don't feel the
attachment to the Windows way of the doing things. I dropped out of
computing from the mid to late 80's and was dropped onto UNIX terminals
(actually VT52 emulation on a BBC micro). When I eventually got a
workstation it was a SunOS 4 box running SunView, quickly dumped for X11.

I hear people use Windows for real work but I'll believe it when I see it.

Shane
