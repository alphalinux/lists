Dear flummoxed:
    What u have caught, as it has caught many of the unwary, is the methods of
maddness employed in passing parameters via register on the 64bit alpha.

    What u see in your int compare is only what u believe is happening. In
reality there is a long compare happening ( there is no int compare as part of
the hardware instruction set ). At the time of compare, the subroutine presumes
that the Hi-Order Word ( 32bit ) is properly sign extended ( +=0fill, -=
0xffffffff filled ) BEFOR-HAND . So if u look at the register ( with gdb ) u
will see ur 9600 in the right half, but something else in the left half - junk.
One might presume that since the variable is 32 bits, that only the right half
will be used - BUT THIS AINT JUST SO. this is especially so in compares.

This sorta makes the case for getting ur parameter casts properly done.

And I'm sure there is gonna be a question of the JNI for java/alpha. The JNI for
JDK1.2 got a whole lot better at figuring out what to do with parameters &
return values. But then that is what SUN designed into their assembler/JNI
interface, and I had little choice but to follow. The JNI interface for jdk118
was a lot simpler, more stupid, and not at all intelligent about what gets put
in the unused bits of the register.. :-\
