Tom Browder wrote:
>
> This undocumented trick coninues to confuse us folks who use other
> architectures as well as the alpha.

I would have a small quarrel with an "undocumented trick" description
(cf. 'man make') but indeed in the past I submitted small patches both
to documentation and a screen message. So far they were ignored, and is
a small mystery to me why, but complaints on axp-list will not get you
very far. Submit similar patches yourself again and again and maybe one
day they will get through. For the time beeing try that on your kernel
sources:

--- linux.orig/scripts/Configure~ Mon Feb 1 13:03:20 1999
+++ linux/scripts/Configure Mon Jul 26 18:04:34 1999
@@ -525,7 +525,11 @@
 if [ ! -f .hdepend -o "$CONFIG_MODVERSIONS" = "y" ] ; then
     echo "*** Next, you must run 'make dep'."
 else
+ if expr "`arch`" : "i" > /dev/null 2>&1 ; then
     echo "*** Next, you may run 'make zImage', 'make zdisk', or 'make
zlilo'."
+ else
+ echo "*** Next, you may run 'make boot modules'."
+ fi
 fi
 echo

> Or, better, why can't the make commands be the
> same?

In some sense they ARE the same as 'make boot' works across all
platforms and on i386 happens to be exactly equivalent to 'make zImage'.
Have a peek at Makefiles and see that for yourself. OTOH they cannot
be made totally the same as you have a lot of platform specific
targets. For example 'make zlilo' or 'make bzImage' do not have
neither sense nor a reasonable representation on Alpha. Look
into arch/alpha/boot/Makefile for further examples.
