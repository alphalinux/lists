This level of detail typically only comes, directly or indirectly, from
non-disclosure presentations,
so called because those receiving the information sign a legal agreement
promising not to
disclose it. The indirect sources are the rags like The Register who troll
for tidbits leaked to
them and then invent enough stuff of their own to fill in what's missing.

The problem is that there's never any way to tell what the quality of the
information is. The
person you're listening to might have heard it directly from the source last
week, or they
might have heard it from someone who heard it from someone who heard it from
someone...
It's easy to find instances of information that's been distorted beyond
recognition by this
game of "telephone" or which, while accurate at one point, has since been
out dated by
changing plans.

Of course, those who are really "in the know" don't talk in public because
they
know that when the next round of proprietary information is distributed,
those who didn't
respect their agreements for the last round won't be included.

Tom

rules for the results he quotes.
