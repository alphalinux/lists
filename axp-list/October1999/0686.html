> I will likely get beaten for the simplicity of this - but how do I apply a
> kernel patch? Point me to the appropriate how-to (kernel??) & I'm off and

http://metalab.unc.edu/pub/Linux/docs/HOWTO/Kernel-HOWTO

> I understand the (likely) syntax as "patch -pnum < 'patchfile'" -
> but where & when do I patch from? Do I patch the source & then compile or is
> a patch for a compiled kernel?

I'd be very wary of a binary patch for any executable, even moreso the
kerne.

I keep kernel patches in their own directory under /usr/src/, with
/usr/src/linux/ a symbolic link to whichever kernel I'm working on.
The usual sequence of events is:
        cd /usr/src/linux/
        patch -p1 < ../patches/patch-foo

All of this should be covered in the HOWTO mentioned above.
Let me know if you need help with building a kernel.
