The premiere issue of Maximum Linux has been in stores and is getting harder
to find, but I picked up a copy and installed Linux Mandrake on my x86 box.
Apparently it's "Red Hat Linux modified" with better tweaks, compiled with
pgcc, has has some of the annoyances (?) from RH 6 taken out. If you like
Red Hat (and I assume most of us do :-) has anyone considered pushing
Mandrake for Alpha? Shouldn't be too hard since RH already supports
Alpha..right? One of the nice things is the mandrake.org site and the
community that goes along with it. It is catered towards the new to
intermediate, but very user friendly. Although their target is to make
"Linux for the desktop" or "Making Linux easy" it's not really that much
different than what Red Hat has done (they use the same installer and setup
Gnome, KDE, and Anotherlevel for you).
