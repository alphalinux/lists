On Fri, 14 Mar 1997, schaeff wrote:

> I'm close to buying an Alpha, but before I do I need to know
> that POVRay will run on it. There are apparently some
> issues about 32-bit vs 64-bit word size, etc, things that I
> don't really understand.
I can confirm that POVRay does indeed compile and run on an Alpha, and so
does Rayshade.
I must admit I have only tried it on Redhat 3.0.3, but not yet on Redhat
4.1 which I recently installed, but I doubt there will be any problem.
Compiling POVRay didn`t give me *any* compiler warnings or errors.

As for speed:
I haven`t done any timings. But I did trace a bit of Lego (see my
home-page) and the UDB didn't take much longer than my P90. But running
POV and reading and writing over NFS may have had a bit of effect on
speed, it was only a trace of about 45 mins.
The newer versions of POVRay (I used 2.2 before) and gcc (any
comments, anyone ?) may speed things up a bit.
