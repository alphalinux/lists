I also seem to be having problems with utmp handling on my RedHat 4.1
installation. For example, I have just logged in and ran three
xterms. 'who', however, reports a strange login time on the third
xterm:

econommx ttyp0 Mar 9 00:56 (:0.0)
econommx ttyp1 Mar 9 00:56 (:0.0)
econommx ttyp2 Dec 31 23:36 (:0.0)

Even stranger, if I run screen, and try to hide one of my login
sessions (with the "C-a L" command), I get the error messages:

Utmp slot not found -> not removed
What? Cannot remove Utmp slot?

Later, after I exit (not detatch!) screen, 'who' outputs the
following:

econommx ttyp0 Mar 9 00:56 (:0.0)
econommx ttyp2 Dec 31 23:36 (:0.0)
econommx ttya0 Mar 9 02:17 (:0:S.0)
econommx ttya1 Mar 9 02:17 (:0:S.1)
econommx ttyp1 Mar 9 00:56 (:0.0)

But I exited all the shells that screen spawned! Those middle two
lines (the ttya* ones) shouldn't be there.

Do the xterm and screen programs do their own utmp handling (that is,
do they bypass the library routines and modify the files directly)? I
remember hearing something on linux-gcc about programs that do their
own utmp handling breaking under the new libc. I assume that's what's
happening.

And so people can yell at me and tell me to upgrade to the latest
stuff before mailing the lists, here are the rpm versions of (IMHO)
relevant stuff that I'm currently using:

XFree86-3.2-5
screen-3.7.1-4
glibc-0.961212-4
