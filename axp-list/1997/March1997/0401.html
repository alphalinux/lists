Marco Corte writes:
> I had Linux installed (RH-4.1) and properly working on a AlphaStation 255
> and I used MILO (on floppy) to boot it. The disk was partitioned with
> fdisk.
> I followed the steps proposed on SRM-howto to be able to boot directly
> from the hard disk with aboot-0.5. I left some sectors free at the
> beginning of the drive (approx. 2 Megs). Result: the partition table got
> corrupted, so I haven't Linux any more :(

Hi,

I had a similar problem; Michael Schwingen helped me out of it.

To be able to boot with aboot your HD should have been formated with
a disklabel.

What I did was reformat using 'minlabel' (available in rpm format) and
reinstall RH (fdisk's bsdlabel was too buggy). You can find the whole thread
in the archives at www.redhat.com.

Good luck,
