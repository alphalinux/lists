OK, I'm trying to compile the new release of
g77 on my redhat-4.1 box.

I got the src.rpm for gcc-2.7.2.1,
and the 2.7.2.2 distribution, unpacked the second,
and applied the patches to it.

But, when I then try to apply the g77-0.5.20-included
patch to the resulting distribution, the patching fails
while patching "configure", which then does not work.

If... I apply the g77 patch to the clean distribution
and THEN apply the RedHat patches, it gets about halfway
through the first "make" of the compiler, with lots of
warnings about "alloca", and then dies because it can't
find "crt0.o"...

If... I just try to make gcc without g77, the RH patches
and the gcc-2.7.2.2 distribution work OK.

If... I just apply the g77 patch to the clean distribution,
(e.g., following the g77 instructions) It dies on the first compile:

[root@eddie gcc]# make "LANGUAGES=c"
cc -DIN_GCC -g -I. -I. -I./config \

-DGCC_INCLUDE_DIR=\"/usr/lib/gcc-lib/alpha-unknown-linux/2.7.2.2.f.2/include\"\
-DGPLUSPLUS_INCLUDE_DIR=\"/usr/lib/g++-include\" \
-DLOCAL_INCLUDE_DIR=\"/usr/local/include\" \

-DCROSS_INCLUDE_DIR=\"/usr/lib/gcc-lib/alpha-unknown-linux/2.7.2.2.f.2/sys-include\"\
-DTOOL_INCLUDE_DIR=\"/usr/alpha-unknown-linux/include\" \
-c `echo ./cccp.c | sed 's,^\./,,'`
cccp.c:322: conflicting types for `sys_errlist'
/usr/include/stdio.h:216: previous declaration of `sys_errlist'
make: *** [cccp.o] Error 1

Any ideas, anyone?
