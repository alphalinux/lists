Hi -

I posted a question about Alpha hardware vendor
recommendations a while back, and I really appreciate
the feedback! I intend to checkout DCG and Hard Data
within the next few months.

I have a couple more questions related to software:

1) is Linux on the Alpha a 32-bit or 64-bit OS? I believe
it's a 32-bit OS if it's compiled from the same source
as the Intel version. What are prospects of a 64-bit
Linux on the Alpha? From what I hear 64-bit isn't
too important yet and 32-bit will do just fine for a while,
but I'm curious.

2) I read that the Alpha Linux version doesn't support
modular kernals. Does the Alpha need to worry about
reaching critical kernel sizes using a monolithic kernel?
Modularity would seem to be a good thing to me, so would
the Alpha version support it in the future? If not, would
someone kindly point out the error of my ways?

I'm obviously a newbie to the Alpha architecture, and the
only Linux experience I have is from my playing with MkLinux
on my PowerMac. From what I see and hear I think Unix
will be around still despite what some camps may say, and I'm eager
to learn Linux. Linux is also a nice departure from DU 4.0 that
I tinker with at work. ;-) Hopefully I can contribute to the
effort soon!

Many thanks,
