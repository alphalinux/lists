Geerten,

I appreciate your running so many different tests. Thanks!
There is a problem, though: when you render a graphic,
rendering speed is not consistent throughout the job. When
rendering a simple part of the graphic, POV will move very
quickly; but when it runs into more complex objects it
crawls. Likewise, when rendering solids it's much quicker
than when rendering glass. This makes it impossible to
predict from the first 100 lines how long it'll take to
render the next 500.

Nevertheless, your results at least show that your computers
are way, way faster than mine. Oh, fortunate Geerten. :)
I'm especially interested in your conclusion that RAM size
is such a huge factor in POV speed. I suspect that other
people's results will bear this out. You're most likely
correct when you say that my cheapest way to better
performance is to get more RAM.

But I want an Alpha anyway. Linux is the OS that interests
me most, so what I'm after is the machine that'll run Linux
the best. As far as I can see, that's an Alpha.

Thanks very much for running these tests. I'm saving your
results for inclusion on the web page. If you do run a
complete render at 800 x 600, I'll be very glad to
know the results. Possibly others will find the information
useful as well.
