A common question on this list (judging by the archives) but I didn't
see any reference to this problem, and now I'm wondering if I'm
just plain missing something obvious.

UDB 166, RH 4.0
I grabbed the pcmcia-2.9.1 sources from my Intel laptop, tried to
compile it, it failed, finally got it to compile.
Compiled and installed module 2.0.0 (or was it modutils, one of them)
except insmod and kerneld.
Compiled and installed insmod-bfd 0.2
Grabbed kernel 2.0.29 and applied axp-diff 2.0.21
compiled kernel with module support and modversion info

depmod -a returns something about "can't allocate 5638202000 memory"
or something.
cardmgr just exits, sometimes it complains about no pcmcia entry
in /proc/devices
/proc/devices doesn't have a pcmcia entry (it does on my Intel laptop,
but that is 1.2.13 under RH 2.0, so I don't know if it should still have
such an entry)

It looks like the kernel doesn't notice that I have pcmcia (the slots
are there, but is this an indication that they are broken?) I haven't
tried the strip -x on the modules, is that my problem?
