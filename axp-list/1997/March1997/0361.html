Just to update people on my progress which is essentially none,
I've tried building gcc-2.7.2.2/g77-0.5.20 every which way including
with glibc-0.96.1212-4 and glibc-2.01 (binaries from prep.ai.mit.edu).
I've tried with and without patches in just about every permutation.

With a few minor modifications, I can get the code to compile (won't
detail them here since I'm not sure if they are right yet). But the
compiler refuses to compile itself or the version that it compiles
generates faulty code.

I don't have absolute confirmation but I believe at least one of the
problems is with how the gcc-2.7.2.2 is handling string constants or
maybe strings in general. All the crashes I find for gcc, xgcc, and
cpp under the debugger seem to involve char* or string literals in
the code; e.g., xgcc will die on trying to make a copy of
version_string but if I recompile and link version.c it will get past
that part of the code; cpp appears to die in calls to strlen passed
string literals -- such as in calls like printf().

I don't know much at all about the internals of gcc so I'm pretty
much stuck here with respect to solving this problem. Perhaps this
info will be useful to someone who is more familiar with gcc/g77?

Thanks for listening and sorry I can't provide a solution.

--erik
