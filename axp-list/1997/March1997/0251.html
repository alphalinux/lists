On Mon, 10 Mar 1997, Michael Champigny wrote:

> Can anyone tell me which 4MB video cards fit in the UDB?

I have gotten both an 4MB ATI Graphics Pro Turbo card, and a 4MB Matrox
Millinium PowerDoc card to fit in the UDB. The Matrox card is a bit longer
(by a few mm) than the ATI so a bit more manipulation has to be done to
get it to fit, and the screws for your floppy drive can't be screwed in
all the way, just the first few threads. As someone else said, the ribbon
cable for the floppy drive is kind of in the way, but everything works
fine without bending it so much you damage it.

And maybe a even more important question: Do they work? The Matrox card
did not work. I tried so many different things to get it to work, but it
just wouldn't... first of all it took about 2 minutes for the ARC console
to come up, but it did come up just fine. Right when I told it to Boot
Linux it loaded milo, but after that the screen froze. The system
continued to boot just fine though, but the display didn't work. I tried
all kinds of versions of milo and nothing worked. Once the system booted I
telneted into it from a remote machine and set my display variable to :0.0
and ran startx (Of cource using the SVGA server). I ran up stairs and the
screen was now blank. I went back to the remote machine and nothing worked
on the alpha. I couldn't even ping it.. so, simply, the Matrox card does
not work in the UDB.

The ATI card, on the other hand, works great. The system boots up just
fine, just as fast as if there were no card in there. Of course you have
to compile VGA support into your kernel, and NOT compile TGA into it, also
grab the latest milo (2.0.28), it's the only one that worked for me. The
only problem is I can't run X in 1280x1024 in 24 bit mode. It simply wont
work. In fact, 1024x768 doesn't even work in 24 bit mode... just 800x600.
So anyway, I'm running it at 1280x1024 in 16 bit mode at 76Hz just fine.
When you do startx though, it defaults to 8 bit mode, which sucks. So I
have to do startx -- -bpp 16 when I start it.. but big deal.

It kind of sucks that the Matrox card doesn't work though because I would
rather use it. It can take advantage of the higher refresh rates that my
monitor is capable of. But certainly the ATI is much nicer than any 8 bit
color..
