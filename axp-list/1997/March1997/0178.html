The Alpha chips (as are most RISC chips) are very sensitive to the ordering of the instructions. The 166 Multia (which uses the ev4 chip) uses a dual issue strategy where it can execute 2 instructions simultaneously. The 233 Multia uses the ev5 chip which can execute 4 instructions in parallel. Before this can happen, the instructions must be in an appropriate order. All Digital compilers reorder the instructions based upon the chip specifications, instruction latencies, etc.
