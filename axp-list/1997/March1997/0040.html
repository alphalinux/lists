I tried this trick on my PC164 machine (the one that crashes reliably
with rpm --verify -a -v):

> Next I tried to repeat the problems by lying to linux about
> how much memory was installed (via "setenv MEMORY_SIZE 64" in
> MILO) and thereby running with 64M of memory. The problems
> went away. The problems seem to remain when running with 128M
> of memory.

This indeed fixes the problem. I also have 128Mb of memory, and by
using this trick the machine is stable and completes the rpm --verify
without problems.

Cheers,
Miguel.
