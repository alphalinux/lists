On Fri, 7 Mar 1997, Jerry Feldman wrote:

> The Alpha chips (as are most RISC chips) are very sensitive to the ordering
>of the instructions. The 166 Multia (which uses the ev4 chip) uses a dual
>issue strategy where it can execute 2 instructions simultaneously. The
>233 Multia uses the ev5 chip which can execute 4 instructions in parallel.
>Before this can happen, the instructions must be in an appropriate order.
>All Digital compilers reorder the instructions based upon the chip
>specifications, instruction latencies, etc.

the 233 is a 21066A, which makes it an ev45 , Im not sure if the 166 is a
21066 or a 21066A, but neither of them are ev5 (21164). If they were, they
woudl kick butt :)
