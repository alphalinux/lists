I upgraded the DRAM in my PC164 433 MHz from 32 to 64 MB by adding 4
2 x 36 SIMMs in slots J9 through J12 and removing jumper J1. On powerup
the ARC firmware reports that there is 64 MB. However on trying to boot
I get the message "CIA Machine Check: reason for machine check unknown
(0x5001a0)" continuously with no further progress. This is before MILO
would normally boot the kernel. My config. is NCR53C810 SCSI-2 controller,
1 GB Quantum XP31070W hard disk, and #9FX Vision 330 graphics accelerator.
It was working ok before the upgrade. Now even if I revert back to the
original 32 MB SIMMs, I get the same message. Has anyone encountered a
similar situation and/or is there a known solution to this problem?

MILO is 2.1.13, Red Hat kernel 2.1.13.
