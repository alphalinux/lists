>>> "Jerry Feldman" said:
>
> The Alpha chips (as are most RISC chips) are very sensitive to the ordering
> of the instructions. The 166 Multia (which uses the ev4 chip) uses a dual
> issue strategy where it can execute 2 instructions simultaneously. The 233
> Multia uses the ev5 chip which can execute 4 instructions in parallel. Befo
> re this can happen, the instructions must be in an appropriate order. All D
> igital compilers reorder the instructions based upon the chip specification
> s, instruction latencies, etc.

Sorry, no, the 233 UDB uses the 21066A (aka LCA45) while the 166 uses the
21066 (LCA4). Both had (the same) design bottlenecks that show up as
memory/cache subsystem performance degradation.

Please see Neal Crook's excellent discourse on these topics in the FAQ at
http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/alpha-sys.html.
