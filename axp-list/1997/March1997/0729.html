At 04:49 hrs. 03-25-97 -0600, you wrote:
>so, the PCI33 is the "noname" board in a Multia/UDB? And those
>pins next to the disk controller _ARE_ an IDE inteface?
No, the "noname" is the AXPpci33 board, which uses the same CPUs and
chipset as the UDB/Multia. Condsider a UDB to be a miniturized noname, with
onboard video and ethernet.
>
>Why, then, are there extra pins, and why won't my IDE
>cable fit onto it?

This is the connection for a notebook computer style IDE drive.
Most any IDE drive designed for a notebook will plug in. If it is a drive
for a desktop, it is slightly different.
