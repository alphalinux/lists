At 08:50 AM 03-28-97 -0500, you wrote:
>Hello... I was thinking about getting a new alpha. (My lil 166 UDB just
>isn't cutting it - anyone want a UDB? ;)
>
>Anyway... I was looking through Digital's site, and came across their
>Alpha PCI 64-275. Does anyone know about this motherboard? It looks
>nice, but does Linux have any problems with it? Now, it's also a 21064A,
>not a 21066A... the 21064A is the "better" chip, isn't it? Thanks a lot.
The 21064A is quite a bit faster than the 21064/66 chips, especially with
the fact that this board has 2MB of external cache. Performance benchmarks
fall about half way between the UDB and the PC164 products.
Digital has, however, discontinued this board. Aspen also manufactured a
similar board with the same CPU, cache and chipset, and called it the
Alpine. They too have discontinued it, but may have some refurbs left...
Check www.aspsys.com about this at :
http://www.aspsys.com/online.html the part is the Alpine Rev. B motherboard.
Finally, Digital makes a board from their TOEM division called variously
the AXP233XL or Avanti, which is a passive back plane / CPU& memory card
design. It comes with 233 or 266 Alpha CPU cards, and even an optional
Intel Pentium card.
Pricing for this is still very high, but rumour is that they will soon be
slashing prices on this board. Stay tuned...
