>
> I would have though that sending numerical addresses to the resolver was
> a bit pointless.

Quite to the contrary! You may have a number and you want a name.
There are numerous situations when this is required. Nameserver works
both ways. Try to feed IP number to 'nslookup' or 'host'.

> Putting loopback, my eth0 address and my network address in /etc/hosts
> seems to have fixed it.

Your loopback and addresses of your interfaces ***should*** be recorded in
/etc/hosts. If they are not there by default, then this is either bug in
a setup process or an operator error. Can't tell without knowing exactly
what and how your were starting. Network address, if really required
there, is a kind of fishy. /etc/networks may have this information but
normally it shold not be necessary.
