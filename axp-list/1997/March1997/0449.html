pete,

I ran it a number of times:

On Pentium 90MHz, 48Mb, at 800x600.
On Pentium 90MHz, 48Mb, at 320x240.
On Pentium 90MHz, 48Mb, at 80x60.

I didn't have time to complete any of these, but they all seemed to
indicate a total time of about 7 to 10 hours (sorry I can't be more
accurate) for the full size picture.
Almost all of the memory was in use during these tests, CPU was running at
99%.

On Alpha 21164 366MHz, 64Mb at 800x600.

This one died as a result of a floating point exception when it was about
two thirds through, but it would have completed in 4 to 5 hours.
Memory usage was slightly more than 48Mb, CPU at 99%.

On Alpha 21164 366MHz, 64 Mb at 160x120.

That ones running now, hoping for some more accurate result.
Again, more than 48Mb of RAM is being used, and the CPU is 99% busy.

In my opinion, memorysize is the key. If your 486 mainboard allows
upgrading to 64Mb or more, then that will be the cheapest way to get better
speed. It should be possible to render this picture in about half a day,
rather than three days.
A UDB (with sufficient memory) would be better (similar to my Pentium) but
would be more expensive.

( 160x120 is done now: it took 26 minutes, 31 seconds. This would indicate
11 hours for the full size. Surely, this can't be right ? )

Well, I can certainly recommend getting an Alpha based machine, just for
the fun of it. But not necessarily as the best means to improve your POVray
performance.
