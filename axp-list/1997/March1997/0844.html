We have a number of programs with a predilection for going into state 'D'
- uninterruptable sleep. It seems that tar, gz, and make when it is

'D' means uninterruptible short term disk wait. I suspect that in your
case it is neither short nor disk-related: I am guessing that tar does
something longish to the tape (like positioning/rewinding) and your
SCSI driver doesn't disconnect the tape device for the duration.
Disconnect is an option in most SCSI drivers, but I understand that
it is considered to be a little dangerous.
