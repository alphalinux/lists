On the subject of internal connectors in/on the UDB and for everyone who
wants to install a 'larger' drive:

I've succesfully installed an IBM DORS31240 SCSI inside the UDB and
took its power from the mentioned 4 pin connector. The IBM drive takes
minimal power about 300 mA at 5V and 200 mA at 12V.

Other than the fan and the serial ports I can't imagine what uses 12 V
in the UDB so the 0.7 Amps (no typo) should just barely do for a 3.5"
drive.

I managed to 'squeeze' in the 3.5" drive just below the floppydrive by
modifying the cage a little (cut off the one leg going underneath the
cage) and did some minor enlargments on the holes at the sides so the
mounting screws for the drive would fit in.
With the IBM drive I have about 1 mm of clearance between the drive and
the nearest chips, but since these are all plastic I don't expect
problems from that. (The circuit board of the IBM drive is very small
too) A couple of spacers enlarged the 'gap' to 2 mm without giving
trouble for the floppy drive.

Even the PCI slot is still available this way but the card has to
small...

Performance is good at rates of over 4 Mb/sec much faster than the 1
Mb/sec I had from the Toshiba, the drive makes some noise but not as
annoying as the click-pause-clunk of the Toshiba drive..

Only problem so far is the floppy drive, the original cable, a 'foil'
type of cable is too short as it has to go from under the HD behind the
power connector and up to the floppy drive. I also tried to bend it in
45 degree angles to get the cable out from the side but it also ended up
being too short. I haven't found a solution yet for this, most audio and
video equipment use the same type of cabling internally these days but
the spacing is wrong. I've tried to solder to it, no avail, the plastic
layer melts away and you end up with a 'blob' of shortcircuited cable.
If anyone knows a supplier for this type of cabling I'd be interested.

I can imagine that most people need the floppy drive to initially
install/boot from.... So I don't recommend this upgrade if you havent
installed the system on the original HD yet..

About the UDB supply: according to the power supply specs on the label
it can deliver more than enough power at 5V (7A), the CPU runs from the
3.3 V supply. So I guess two 2.5" drives should be no problem as these
run from 5V only. The 5V drives stay quite cool too so the internal
temperature rise should be minimal.

While copying the stuff from the Toshiba to the IBM I had both drives
connected and powered to/from the UDB and had no problems.

BTW if anyone knows how to change the ID of the Toshiba drive I'd also
like to know, I had the idea of putting it inside my external CD-ROM
just to have some 'swapping' space. The drive has no visible jumpers
AFAIK.
