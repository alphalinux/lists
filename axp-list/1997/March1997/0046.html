Robert Williams wrote:
>
> Upon resetting my PC164 system time and rebooting
> this morning, the system fails to boot
> at the point where the network is brought up,
> and the 3C509 card broadcasts continuously,
> consuming nearly 100% of the ethernet bandwidth.
> I'm not sure these two events are connected.
> The system was working over the weekend, with
> an incorrect time setting.

I've answered one of my own questions.
The clock options are UTC and ARC. The correct
one for the PC164 appears to be ARC, I think.
There is a file, /etc/sysconfig/clock,
that flags the appropriate setting.
Mine had somehow had both UTC and ARC set.

However, I still can't boot into multiuser mode,
level 3. Could this be related to my previous
problem with "amd"?

The failure to boot occurs where the system
announces that it is booting to level 3, followed by:

Eth0: setting Rx mode to 1 addresses

then nothing
