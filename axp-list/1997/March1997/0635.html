What specs do you have?

I have one that explicitly mentions it, even gives all the pinouts. Now, my
spec is titled "Quicksilver Hardware Specification" and is labelled "Digital
Confidential"; it was, I believe, never published (or made available) for
customer use. Quicksilver was the DEC internal name for Multia.

I believe the only spec available to customers is the so-called Service
Information; if so, then this spec reflects only what was *sold*, so that it
could be serviced. DEC never *sold* the Multia/UDB with IDE disks, so, it
doesn't show up there. This doesn't mean that the hardware didn't support
it.

I believe there has been discussion of use of IDE on the UDB before this,
in the axp-list@redhat.com, with successes noted.

As reference material, I'll point you at the set of pins on the motherboard
below the rear ends of the floppy/2.5" SCSI disk.

Let me know if you want the pinouts, but you probably wouldn't believe me
anyway... :-(
