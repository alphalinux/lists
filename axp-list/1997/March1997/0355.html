>>> "Paul Christenson \[N3EOP\]" said:
> I have a problem... I sit in a lab with an Alphastation 200 and a couple
> of 500s. The problem is that they all run Digital UNIX, and I want to
> stick Linux on one to hack around on.

The AlphaStation 200 is part of the AVANTI family, which includes the 400 as
well as the 250 and 255.

The AS500s are part of the ALCOR family, along with the AS600s.

> I have the Yggdrasil Winter 1997 "Linux Internet Archives" set; disk 8 has
> RH 4.0 on it.

I think you'd be better off checking the 4.1 archive, as there is an AVANTI
kernel there that is supposed to be booted from SRM; I think it's called
avanti-s.gz, IIRC.

> First of all, I don't know what kernel to use on the Alphastations; none
> of the docs mention it. Second, what few docs exist are for using MILO,
> not aboot.

There's information in the FAQ about booting from SRM:

http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/srm.html

System-related info is at:

http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/FAQ-4.html

> Once I get Linux running on the Alphastation, then maybe I'll attempt it
> on the VMEbus Alphas that we're working with... <G>

*That* ought to be fun; one of the DMCCs, is it? It's possible you might
find bus support under one of the other platforms, like m68k, but you may
have to roll your own...
