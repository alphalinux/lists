Once upon a time, Alvin Starr said:
[snip]
> Sombody out of florida(I think) wrote a 386 instruction emulator and had
> some level of PC emulation running. But I do not remember who it was or
> if it compiled and ran on the alpha.

Then, of course, there is the x86 emulator distributed as a part of Andrew
Tanenbaum's Minix. It's called Bochs. There is a web page at
http://world.std.com/~bochs/ . Hmm, it's a lot more complete than I
expected. It claims to run on any UNIX/X11 machine. Supports Windows 3.1
in standard mode.
