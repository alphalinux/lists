Agh, I am new here, but thought to throw my two bits in....

I bought a UDB from Onsale (www.onsale.com). The price was very reasonable. I think for about a grand you can get one with 64Mb of RAM and a decent disk (>1Gb). The vendor is Starship Computer guys in New Hampshire. 1-603-659-2912 voice, absc@ameribusiness.mv.com. Talk to Anita. I HAVE NO AFFILIATION WITH THEM OTHER THAN BEING A SATISFIED CUSTOMER. If you do call tell them I sent you. The UDB came with a 30 day warranty, and at 40 days the SCSI controller started to choke and they replaced it. They have been good with tech type support as well.

So far I am amazed with this little box. Its size is AMAZING! It comes with a stand that maket it a 'tower' configuration. The overall box size is 12.5" sq. x 3" tall. The MB is very well organized, the ethernet, SCSI2, video and sound is all built onto the board. There are 2 pcmcia slots. ! prll, combo serial and external scsi. Overall I think a whole lot cooler than an IPC/X. Serial port is kinda neat, no room for 2 25 pin connectors, so they ganged 2 ports into 1 connector and a 20 split out cable makes it work, ala RS-432 --> 2-RS-232's.

Getting everything to go was tricky, but like any os, once I got evrything up and going all the frustration was worth it. I am able to dual-boot NT4.0 & Linux.

If I could only get X to run .....
