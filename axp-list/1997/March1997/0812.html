What keyboard does and what it does not depends on a loaded keytable
when on a text console ('man loadkeys') and loaded mapping when in
X ('man xmodmap'). There are defaults, of course.

For example, personally I always load "emacs2.map" in a startup file,
plus private modifications, as I cannot suffer Caps_Lock in a position
into which some nitwit sticked it on a PC keyboard. An absence of cramps
is to me much more important than correct key markings.

For X windows you can find now, on 'powercd', a program called
'xkeymaps'. It will alow you to edit your keyboard to your liking
and it will write a file you can load with xmodmap. It is a great
help as I never found docs which would describe what is really wanted
here and what is its meaning (not that I was looking very hard :-).

Saying that I did not notice 4.1 doing anything funny with a default
keyboard. Maybe just your hardware went south? What 'showkey' has
to say? <tab> and <shift-tab> were generating the same code, AFAIK.
