On Feb 28, 9:40pm, David R. McGown wrote:
> Subject: RE: web browser
> Before we rush out in mass and request that Netscape provide a port of
> Navigator for Linux/Alpha, please consider that some basic tools they need
may
> be missing.
>
> Currently, the only version of Motif available is the static ECOFF 2.0 from
> Metrolink. Of course, Netscape *could* build an ECOFF version, but it would
be
> fairly large. However, since ELF/glibc is still a changing target (perhaps
it
> has settled down now), ECOFF may be the best way to produce a binary that
would
> be compatable with all Linux/Alpha distrbutions. No doubt most of us
> are using ELF/glibc now, and we would probably prefer to see a shared library
> version, which means a couple of things:
>
> 1. glibc must stabilize so that a shared binary has a reasonable chance of
> working with the next version of the libraries.
>
They are having stability problems with version 5 libc variants for Intel
Linux.
The glibc 2.0 moves VERY fast in comparison.

> 2. With (1), Metrolink would need to release a more modern version of Motif
> 2.0 (I assume Netscape is using Motif 2.0) for Alpha/Linux that would
> allow one to build ELF linked binaries, perhaps statically linked to Motif
for
> distribution, but compatable with glibc.
>
In the 3.x series, they use Motif 1.2.4. No reason to move as 2.0 does not
have market penetration in the other *nix platforms.

> My gut feeling is that it is premature to start to pester Netscape about a
> Navigator binary, unless we are willing to accept an ECOFF version. Let
things
> settle and stabilize a bit. I think getting Metrolink to produce an ELF
Motif
> is key to porting Netscape and many other commericial binaries.
>
> Just my $0.02

And let's not forget, they do these ports for purely commercial reasons. So
there has to be an economic argument for them to follow. These browsers bring
in so much money that Microsoft gives theirs' away. Much like shaver blades and
free handles, you might think... Offer to convince your company to buy site
licenses, and see what you get...
