>>> David Paris said:
>
> I have gotten both an 4MB ATI Graphics Pro Turbo card, and a 4MB Matrox
> Millinium PowerDoc card to fit in the UDB.
>
> And maybe a even more important question: Do they work? The Matrox card
> did not work. I tried so many different things to get it to work, but it
> just wouldn't...

Sigh. The ARC and SRM consoles for the UDB are quite out-of date; I think
the BIOS emulator version is 1.15 or even earlier. I was only successful in
getting the Millennium going using 2.0x BIOS emulation code (on different
platforms), which is found in the latest ARC/SRM/AlphaBIOS and MILO.

Unfortunately for David, it turned out that just having it in MILO was not
good enough... :-(

> The ATI card, on the other hand, works great.

Yes, they fixed the BIOS emulator until it *did* work. I was working on the
Digital UNIX kernel drivers for the ATI over 3 years ago, and when I got the
first Mach64 card, I had to physically remove the BIOS ROM from the card in
order to boot up; it hung the emulator... :-(

> When you do startx though, it defaults to 8 bit mode, which sucks. So I
> have to do startx -- -bpp 16 when I start it.. but big deal.

You can put a "DefaultColorDepth 16" option in the XF86Config file's
"Screen" Section (before any "Display" Subsections) that will set the
desired default. Which, of course, can be overridden by that same command
line argument.
