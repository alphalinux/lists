Okay - I was trying to compile PVM 3.3.11 on a vanilla RH4.0 166UDB. It
didn't work by a long shot. So, I hear that with a 2.0.28 kernel it has
been done so I get the kernel source for 2.0.28 and try to compile that.
Also no avail. I try to go back and compile the old 2.0.18 sources and
THEY won't work.

So.... I get the new glibc RPMS, specifically:

glibc-0.961212-4.alpha.rpm
glibc-devel-0.961212-3.alpha.rpm

I get those installed (along with the new RPM 2.3-1). 2.0.28 compiles
like a champ at this point. No problems. So, I try to boot the new
kernel, and it works; all is well. Then I get the infamous hang at

eth0: media is TP

So, I reboot back into single user mode and try to alleviate the problem.
As I have not been keeping up with the list as of late, it takes me a
while, but I discover that the /etc/nsswitch.conf appears to be the
culprit. I delete all the nisplus and nis switches from the file and try
to 'telinit 3'. At this point it churns merrily along and completes its
course and dumps me at the login screen.

At this point, no matter what the user name, when I enter it at the
login: prompt and hit enter, it automatically tells me I have an invalid
login. I don't have shadow passwds installed (atleast I SHOULDN'T, I
don't have an /etc/shadow anywhere).

I can activate all the services I need in single user mode, so access to a
network, etcetera is possible, it just won't let multiusers login.

Anyone experience these problems and have a remedy short of
reinstalling/upgrading to RH4.1?
