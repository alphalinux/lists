> Heat: The UDB's case is so campact that many folks have discovered that
> they have problems with their machines due to it overheating. The problems
> range from memory errors to the machine refusing to boot. For this reason,
> it is actually strongly suggested that you go with an external only hard
> drive to reduce the amount of internal heat. For what its worth, I'm personally went from an external hard disk to an internal hard disk and i'm not having
> any problems. I'm just a thrillseeker I guess :-)

Actually I picked up two of the 233Mhz UDB's about two months ago.
They both have 500-meg internal HD's, and while one of them is still in
its box, the one I *am* using has an uptime of 33 days now (RH 4.2). The
left side of the case is fairly warm, but not alarmingly so. The only
thing that bothers me about it is that 2.5-inch HD's are a bit loud.
Other than that, a neat little box. If I never see a toasted power lead,
I'll consider these a great buy for Linux machines.

> Video: The built in video adapater on the UDB is commonly known as the TGA1
> which has 2MB of RAM on it. Due to its design, it can only do 8bpp with
> will give you 256 color. It can do a resolution up to 1280x1024 though
> which is really nice. Your model has a PCI slot in it so you can put a
> different graphics card in it that will do > 8 bpp color.

The TGA seems to make up for the lack of color in speed. It's very zippy.
I guess that's another compliment for the UDB, in case anybody else is
considering buying one. They have a lot of nice stuff built in.
