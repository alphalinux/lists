On Thu, 4 Sep 1997, Eric Hilton Jones wrote:

> Has anyone been able to figure out a method to play mp3 files on the udb?

I am using the mpg123 player with great success. It takes about 20% cpu on
my 266MHz axppci33 (same chipset and CPU as in the UDB). If your cpu is
only a 166MHz one, it will naturally be slower, but I think it should work
in realtime anyway. I am using the -b 256 option to increase buffering.
There are some annoying skips in the audio without it.

If you are using glibc2 I have a precompiled mpg123 in my anon ftp:

ftp://wopr.campus.luth.se/pub/axp/mpg123

On the other hand I think it was rather easy to compile. I'm sure there's
a newer version out there than the binary (0.59g).

/Andreas
