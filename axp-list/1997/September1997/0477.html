> This is not true with the Power PC, BTW The alpha design team didn't

Why?? Many CPUs don't allow misaligned accesses and the ones that do will
usually suffer a pretty big performance penalty.
It's not the CPU's job to fix broken programs.

> In any event to answer your question,
> its the job of the compiler to properly align things. If you get this
> message then its really a compiler bug, i.e. the compiler that was used
> to create the object module, which when excuted causes the trap.

align objects this is almost impossible to overlook. It's also not the
kind of bug that is likely to appear only under rare circumstances.

Usually it's just broken software. Plain and simple.
