While I remain unable to get the ARC console to boot MILO off my
SCSI disk, I can now get MILO to boot the kernel from the SCSI
drive (rather than off a floppy, as I had been doing).

The odd bit is that after getting a MILO prompt and prior to issuing
a boot command, I have to do a `show'. Or at least, that's the only
way I've been able to get MILO to initialise the BusLogic driver
without getting wedged. Upon executing the `show', the card is
initialised, the SCSI devices identified, and the partitions on
the SCSI disk enumerated. Thereafter I can boot using something like:

boot sda2:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/sda2

...without any problems.

If I do not do a `show' prior to attempting to boot, the machine
will get wedged as previously described.

Yours etc.,
