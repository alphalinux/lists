Hi Rob, hi others,

Rob Zook <rzook@informix.com> writes:
> At 03:07 PM 9/11/97 +0200, you wrote:
> >Rob Zook <rzook@informix.com> writes:

> >The pam-config entries for anon-ftp in RH4.1 seem incorrect. I don't
> >recall if installing a newer anon-ftp package or newer pam-rpms solved
> >the prob for me, but that's definitely the way to go.
>
> Well, I'm not having a problem with anonymous ftps, just ftp with
> a login other than anonymous. Does pam affect that as well?

I checked with my installation and afair installing
wu-ftpd-2.4.2b12-6.alpha.rpm - which includes the right settings for
/etc/pam.d/ftp - is the easiest to apply remedy for ftp-authentication
troubles.

Though, as suggested elsewhere in this thread, it might be a more 'proper'
solution to the problem as a whole to consider getting rid of this well-
meant but troublecausing pam-thing in the long run. But perhaps when we all
just'd get used to it - who knows - it could be a thing not too bad...
or even good?! No pro-pam voices here in this group?

> >If you ever expierience trouble with authentication, your first thought
> >should be to blame it on pam ;-)
>
> Including ftp with a login other than anonymous?

Yes.

Not because pam is necessarily a Bad Thing[tm], but because this way
you get used to working with a new mechanism. And having this mechanism
on your system definitely means you _have_ to get used to it.
