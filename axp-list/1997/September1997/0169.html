I recently posted about recent power problems that I've had with a pair of
UDBs. They would quit working,and then at some arbitrary point later would
start working again. I thought it might be heat related, but high volume
forced air experiments have shown that not to be the case. One has been
worse than the other, but both developed the same problem within a couple
of weeks of each other.

I think I *finally* found the problem.

I found yesterday that while poking around the motherboard power supply
connectors with VOM leads that it would suddenly start working again.
Since then I have been able to verify empirically that pulling and
reseating the connector will restore it every time.

Examining the p/w connectors carefully this afternoon, I noticed that the
white connector housing had discolored around the two green wires, the 3.3
volt feed. I surmise that this is the chip supply, though I don't know
for sure. The two green leads were getting too hot right at the m/b.

Checking the pins carefully on the worst machine, I could see that the two
green wire connectors had lost a bit of spring, and the faces of connector
and m/b pins were slightly dirty. I replaced the connector with a new one
to get new connector pins with good spring in place. I also carefully
rubbed the m/b pins down with a fairly hard pencil eraser to clean them.

I did this to both machines, and they both came right up. I won't know if
this has really fixed it for a few days, but I have a fair amount of
confidence so far, as this would explain the intermittent symptoms that I
have seen.

Thought this might be a useful tip with all the UDBs out there.....
