I get the same results on my UDB. I remember some while ago someone posted
that they have sound working on their UDB perfectly. In /proc/ioports,
instead of a CS4231 his showed a CS4231A. However, a quick look at the
/linux/drivers/sound/ad1848.c shows that the CS4231A driver is downwards
compatible with the CS4231. So in theory, if it's using a compatible
driver, it should still work fine. The differences between the two aren't
large enough that the wave channel shouldn't work properly.

But this is real life, and it is indeed mis-identifying the chip to start
with, which in my relatively kernel-clueless mind suggests that there's a
fault in talking to the control registers on the chip. I don't think I can
hunt this down on my own, but if anyone can give me suggestions I'll do
what I can. Here's my /dev/sndstat and my /proc/ioports for your own
edification:
