Pet peeve: don't use -ansi unless you know what you are doing.

It is rarely useful. What is does is provide a strict ANSI/ISO
compliant namespace, which _nobody_ (well, hardly anybody)
writes to. Everyone writes to some superset of that, be it
POSIX, BSD or Win32.

But I can say, other than that, your listed headers compile just
fine under glibc 2.0.5. So whatever the brokenness was, it has
been fixed.
