Jeremy wrote:
> Recently, I got a AlphaPC 164LX 533 overclocked to 600, with 128 megs of
> memory, a big ole' 4gig drive hanging off an Adaptec 2940, and a Digital net
> card.
>
> So, here's some symptoms and some ways I get the thing to bomb.. Basically,
> the big error I'm *ALWAYS* getting is
> ``Memory Violation at pc=xxx rp=xxx (bad address = xxx)''

Well, step 1: have you tried clocking it back down to 533MHz?
Overclocking
is by its very nature risky business; sometimes it works, sometimes it
doesn't. If it doesn't, the first thing to do is clock it back down
to the manufacturers' spec before complaining that it's flaky...

If you do this and you *still* get memory problems, you might have a
marginal SIMM.
