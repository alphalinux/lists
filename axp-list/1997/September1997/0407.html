> I am offered a couple of Multias P100 based for a low low price, so I
> may well decide to spend a few buck in those boxes, in order to build a PVM
> device.
> My question is : does someone knows if the board is designed to support
> higher frequencies/cpus, as I'd like to get ridd of the P100 and put
> P133/166/200 in place, if feasible.
> Many thanks to you all

To quote from the Multia Manual "All Intel-based models have an
upgradable Pentium processor". The instructions for replacing the CPU
tell you to "set jumpers W2, W4, W5, and W7 based on the Intel processor
installed.

Unfortunately the Intel-based system jumper table does not give specific
information as to what processors may be used. It simply tells you the
functions of the jumpers like "host buss frequency" but does not tell
what position is what frequency etc.

So the answer to your question is yes, it will support higher speed CPUs
but you will need more information than is in the manual in order to do
so.
