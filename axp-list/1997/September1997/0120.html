Well, after spending mucho $$ to outfit this Alpha UDB, I'm not only
having RAM problems (and *was* planning on buying another 64MB RAM
tomorrow, to see if they were with the new SIMMs), but now the floppy
drive is pretty much useless.

Whenever I try to read anything from the floppy under Linux, I get
a bunch of "floppy probe 0: error" or messages somewhat like that.

Even working in ARC, no floppy disk can be read. Under Linux,
formatting (e.g. via `fdformat /dev/fd0H1440') seems to work fine
until it goes to verify, when it suddenly can't find sectors.

It was working fine as I started trying a RedHat Linux install to
try and figure out whether the problems I was running into at the
time were due to Craftwork Linux or some hardware problem...I got
far enough to determine that it was a memory problem, as RedHat
had at least as many of these problems as Craftwork. But at some
point during this process, the Redhat floppies stopped working,
and I figured, at the time, I had somehow overwritten them or
something, and just went back to using Craftwork, which was still
installed on the hard drive, as a means to figure out the memory
problems.

(Note that some of these disks have been verified, even formatted
and written, just fine on my GNU/Linux i486 system...I've been
depending on floppies to transfer stuff from my 486 to the UDB,
to test out g77 on the UDB.)

Is there any reasonable, inexpensive fix to this? Or would I just be
throwing good money after bad (well, the UDB was a gift), so do you
think I should just abandon (toss) the UDB given that it has both
memory and floppy problems? (Maybe I can still make use of it once
I get my LAN up and running, but how many more problems is this box
going to have?!)

At one point I wondered if the ARC environment had been fried, and
tried resetting to factory defaults. This didn't help; the floppy
drive remained useless. (The system seems to think it's a 2.88MB
drive at this point -- would that be a problem even for `fdformat
/dev/fd0H1440' and, if so, how do I fix this?)

I can probably still make good use of the new external box, which I
spent the most $$ on, as there's a possibility of getting a new
higher-end Alpha...but at times like this I feel like selling all
this half-working hardware and spending the rest of my life in a
subway station playing the saxophone or something. :(
