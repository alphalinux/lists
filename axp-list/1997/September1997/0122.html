>The UDBs are notorious for reporting memory errors when there are heat
>problems. Also, sometimes it really is bad memory or memory that is on its
>way to being bad and the UDB is just catching them earlier because its more
>sensitve.

Okay, thanks. I doubt it's a heat problem; I've removed the internal
drive, and practically do handstands to keep my basement office (which
faces NNW) cool. (I'd prefer 74-76 F ambient myself, but for the
equipment, I try to keep it in below 74. Right now it's 73.1, a bit
on the warm side of where I usually keep it.)

>> Whenever I try to read anything from the floppy under Linux, I get
>> a bunch of "floppy probe 0: error" or messages somewhat like that.
>
>This is not a good sign. If you are using a known good floppy and can't
>boot then odds are your floppy is toast.

Sigh. Anyone have specs handy for just what kind of floppy I should
get, ideas where to get it, etc.? It's a slim notebook-style floppy,
apparently, but that's all I know so far.

>> At one point I wondered if the ARC environment had been fried, and
>> tried resetting to factory defaults. This didn't help; the floppy
>> drive remained useless. (The system seems to think it's a 2.88MB
>> drive at this point -- would that be a problem even for `fdformat
>> /dev/fd0H1440' and, if so, how do I fix this?)
>
>The floppy is most likely a 1.44 but the floppy controller is a 2.88
>controller. IF you actually had a 2.88 drive hooked up it would work at the
>higher capacity.

I don't think getting a 2.88MB is worthwhile at this point. Just a
working 1.44MB would be real helpful.

Thanks for the feedback!
