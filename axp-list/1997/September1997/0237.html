As a UDB owner and current maintainer of the UDB/Linux FAQ, I'll take a
stab at answering your questions.

> I have the opportunity to buy a 2nd hand DEC Multia 166mhz, 32mb RAM, 340mb
> HD, no monitor.
>Any comments on what this workstation would be worth? and will Linux run on it?

Various UDB users have reported that the performance of the machines for
non-Floating Point Intensive application is equivalent to a P100. This is
mostly in line with my own impressions. The 166Mzh machine cannot have its CPU
upgraded so you are stuck with this speed. You can't even overclock this model
to squeeze some more speed out of it.

Things to watch out for.

Disk space: Alpha binaries are larger than intel binaries by 15$ to 33%.
This means that 340MB doesn't go very far. You can make a reasonable
network workstation from that. Otherwise, you are going to have to hang an
external hard disk off the back of the unit.

Heat: The UDB's case is so campact that many folks have discovered that
they have problems with their machines due to it overheating. The problems
range from memory errors to the machine refusing to boot. For this reason,
it is actually strongly suggested that you go with an external only hard
drive to reduce the amount of internal heat. For what its worth, I'm personally went from an external hard disk to an internal hard disk and i'm not having
any problems. I'm just a thrillseeker I guess :-)

Video: The built in video adapater on the UDB is commonly known as the TGA1
which has 2MB of RAM on it. Due to its design, it can only do 8bpp with
will give you 256 color. It can do a resolution up to 1280x1024 though
which is really nice. Your model has a PCI slot in it so you can put a
different graphics card in it that will do > 8 bpp color.

You'll use the noname.img file to boot the UDB. RedHat 4.2 for Apha works
like a charm.
