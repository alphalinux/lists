It is true that anytime you cool with other than ambient air, you do need
to consider local condensation of humidity. I didn't mean to lead anyone
astray on Peltier devices, but I have seen them used at a local startup
(Silicon Valley) as an interim step to using liquid nitrogen to cool their
cpu's, which they expect to run at about 800 MHz.

I would think that in a normal environment (home or office, the latter is
usually much dryer) that a fan past the Peltier device should be adequate
to prevent condensation. Of course, you probably don't want to put a
steaming cup of coffee in front of the fan.

Does anyone know if the clock speed on an XL266 can be changed? I don't
see any jumpers near the crystal, but there are a couple near the RT
clock.
