On Thu, 18 Sep 1997, eaml wrote:

> I believe that DEC sees the potential of Linux. Proof of that is that they
> took a team of DEC engineers to help develop the kernel for Linux/Alpha.
> And also EM86.

Close to a year ago I posted to this list that I would not be able to use
an Alpha in a mainstream server, as the list of available commercial s/w
was not complete enough. A Digital employee asked me precisely what apps
were missing, and I replied with a list that I was short then.

ViaCrypt PGP was one of them, as was an Alpha port of Stronghold. The PGP
binary is a.out format, which em86 would not run at the time; I had no
time to hack a.out because I have a perfectly viable platform in front of
me that lets me get on with making a living. I think C2 dropped the Alpha
Stronghold port some time ago, and I haven't heard that they picked it up.
The Linux RealAudio binary did not initially run with em86 (missing
ioctls), and I've not heard an update on its status.

Now I have the Solid SQL server for the Intel platform, and it is in
binary format only. These types of critial performance servers are not
the kind of thing that I want to run under an emulator. I have a pair of
UDBs, which are not powerful enough to give me a valid understanding about
how Solid might run under em86 than on a native P[56] platform.

My contact with Linux/Alpha is entirely through this list. If there is a
better forum to follow I would very much like to hear about it. Digital
seems to have mostly dropped off this list, and the efforts that were
going on a year ago have gone silent (underground?).

Bottom line for me: the 500 MHz machines look very attractive, but until
Digital helps make a market by assisting a wide variety of application
developers to port current apps to Linux/Alpha, I cannot consider it for a
mainline server. The two UDBs are doing find running standard chores like
FTP servers, etc, but such apps are no longer sufficient to run a
frontline server.

-- Michael
