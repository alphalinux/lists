On Mon, 8 Sep 1997, Craig Burley wrote:

> >The UDBs are notorious for reporting memory errors when there are heat
> >problems. Also, sometimes it really is bad memory or memory that is on its
> >way to being bad and the UDB is just catching them earlier because its more
> >sensitve.
>
> Okay, thanks. I doubt it's a heat problem; I've removed the internal
> drive, and practically do handstands to keep my basement office (which
> faces NNW) cool. (I'd prefer 74-76 F ambient myself, but for the
> equipment, I try to keep it in below 74. Right now it's 73.1, a bit
> on the warm side of where I usually keep it.)

Ouch, I kick the fans in if the house goes over 65F, and keep the
computer room even lower. I do however run all machines for a week at
100+ (normally 107F (high as the heater in the back room will take it)) as
a torture test, a mem test for several days, then Linux at full load.
Both my UDB's (VX40F3 boards) with the 3.5 inch 340 meg maxtor SCSI hdd's
will run at normal load till 80F, however when fully loaded (CPU and disk)
I start getting errors after 10 hrs at 75F. It's getting into fall here,
and with the window open this evening the thermistat in the comp room says
57F (low 40's outside), the therm sitting in the airstream of an idle UDB
reads 76... Between the 2 UDB's I can heat half this place, so I wouldn't
totally rule it out as a problem. An even *slightly* flaky simm would
give trouble next to that heating element DEC calls a CPU.

BTW: Fully loaded in this context mean as many copies of e2fsck, dd,
find, badblocks, and a c prog that randomly abuses memory as can be
started before running out of memory and or a several minuet interactive
delay.
