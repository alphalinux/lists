On Fri, 12 Sep 1997 12:01:15 -0400 (EDT), Vernard Martin wrote:

[Delitia]

>Various UDB users have reported that the performance of the machines for
>non-Floating Point Intensive application is equivalent to a P100. This is
>mostly in line with my own impressions. The 166Mzh machine cannot have its
CPU upgraded so you are stuck with this speed. You can't even overclock this
model to squeeze some more speed out of it.

Huh? Last time I looked in my UDB the CPU was socketed into a ZIF type
socket very similar to those that are used on pentium motherboards....and it
fact (I just tried it) the CPU w/heat sink is very easy to remove.

The mainboard appears to contain jumpers that according to the tech manual
allow for CPU speeds up to 300 MHz (although only speeds up to 233 MHz are
supported by DEC)

So is there something Im missing here? From what I can tell it should be
easy to overclock or upgrade to a faster CPU.

Just my two cents.
