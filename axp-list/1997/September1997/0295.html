A> A Peltier decice is a thermoelectric junction between two conductors,
A> which, when a current is passed throught the junction can cause the
A> temperature to drop. There are firms that manufacture these, but I
A> can't point you in the right direction, a EE should know?

Well, I'm an EE, but I don't remember the exact details about these devices as
well. At least the heat energy has to go somewhere, it's just "transferred"
from one side of the device to the other side which gets even hotter because of
the power consumption of the peltier device itsself. To remove that heat, it's
very important to have an additional fan on the peltier device as well as on a
normal cooling device. Besides, I've heard about problems with condensing water
on the cool side of peltier devices.
