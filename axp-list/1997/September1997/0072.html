Behold: on that day, 5 of September in the year 1997,
Huw Davies spake unto me, saying:

> At 11:34 AM 9/4/97 -0400, Vik Bajaj wrote:
> >The 2.0.30 stable kernel seems to be unstable on the alpha platform;
> >are any of the 2.1.X kernels stable yet?
>
> Both my Linux/Alpha systems run 2.0.30 and I have never had a crash with
> either of them. I'll admit that the XL-300 at home is only on for a couple
> of hours a day and that it spends most of it's time idle, but the UDB at
> work does real things and has been up now for 8 days, 18:52 and the reason
> for the last reboot was to fix lpd (see earlier postings).

Well, I compiled and ran 2.0.30 briefly, but found it sufficiently
irritating that I was forced to retreat to an older kernel. Basically
I was getting scads of:

kernel: unaligned trap at fffffc0000383740: fffffc00058a13f9 29 2
kernel: unaligned trap at fffffc0000383778: fffffc00058a13f9 29 1

Always the same two traps and addresses.

Before you say it, yes I know that these aren't real problems, that
the kernel catches the access and fixes it up, and that everything
works ok. But it's bloody annoying to get a pair of these every 15-20
seconds anyway... The machine was fairly stable (up for ~4 days,
until the console wedged -- but I suspect that that might have been a
hardware flakiness), but it was sufficiently annoying getting this
junk on the console that I retreated. If this is a widely known
problem and you happen to know of a quick fix for this, do let me
know. Otherwise I'll probably just wait for the next kernel rev...
