Hi all,

a while ago someone had a problem getting a DAT tape to work,
I forgot who it was.

I dragged a HP C1533A DDS-2 SCSI-2 DAT home last weekend and
tried to get it to work (PC164 w/AHA2940UW). I built the driver
as a module --- worked perfectly (Ah, binutils-2.8.1-1 or 2 from
ftp.redhat.com/pub/alphabits/glibc1-updates/ and modutils from
RH 4.2 CD).

The only thing strange thing that happend was the following:
IF I do not chmod a-x /sbin/kerneld
THEN the kernel failed to boot
Actually the DAT *was* found during the scsi resetting business
and the st module was loaded automagically, but the kernel/MILO
went into an endless loop. MILO boots, reads in the kernel, hands
over control. The kernel fails to get going and MILO's back in
control (similar to a reboot).

Note that *after* disabling kerneld, the st module was *still*
inserted --- no idea how this happens. Anyway, I tarred my whole
disk to the DAT w/o errors.

Greets,

Jo
