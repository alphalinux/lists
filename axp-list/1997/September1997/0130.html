On Tue, 9 Sep 1997, Craig Burley wrote:
> While I can't imagine getting the ambient here down much lower than I do,
> for personal comfort reasons as well as affordability, it *is* good
> to know there are people out there "crazier" than me. I've talked
> to various HVAC types, even gotten an estimate on doing some minor
> improvement to vent heat up from the basement (so we don't freeze on
> the 1st floor ;-), and everyone seems to think it's weird I even
> *care* about what temperature my computers run at. (I think even
> my wife wonders about me.)

I don't know about crazy, Alaskan maybe. Any I do hate it in the
summers. They are nice, but local lows are -45F winter, and highs of
~+85F summer (think we broke 90F a rew days this year). I try to keep
things cooler, both for the computers, and me. At 72 I am not happy.
Winter is nicer, though I can remember several times last year when I had
to stop typing, my left hand was not working right. You see, I had a Vax
11/750 (bout the size of 1.5 VW rabbits) running in the room, so the
window was open. The room was nearly 80, but -40 air was coming in the
window and rolling over the left side of the desk. So I had a partially
numb left hand in a hot room. :(

I can also say for shure that LCD panels and those low power
laptop drives get VERY unhappy beyond -20.

> Maybe now I'll worry less about the ambient going below 70F, though,
> especially when I'm not in the office. Right now I have only one
> computer (an old, hot, 486) running; pretty soon I'll have two (add
> the UDB, hopefully), then three (another Alpha?), maybe even four
> (an UltraSPARC?), though all but one will normally have the monitors
> powered off. I'm concerned about this; the rough estimate for a
> special built-in A/C unit in the office is around $5K or so, which
> I don't happen to have in my wallet right now. Might have to relocate
> some systems w/o monitors; fortunately, I have cat-5 cabling to several
> locations in the condo already built into the walls (foresight on my
> part, I hope it actually works ;-), so I could put one machine in
> the other basement room near the electrical panel, another in the loft,
> maybe one in my wife's office (if it's quiet enough), etc. I'm hoping
> the much more modest plan I have an estimate on suffices.

Don't worry about the temp so much, most systems would not care if it were
40, so long as condensation is not a problem. As for ac, I know of server
people that have build a box around an avail window, and installed a
*small* RV air conditioner in the top. Bit cheaper than central. I just
grab those generic 20" box fans, if things get 2 warm one on low will keep
the comp room workable. If you can just rig a fan or 2 and pull air from
the rest of the place. Just watch out for dust.
