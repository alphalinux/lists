On Sep 10, Michael Brennen wrote:

> I found yesterday that while poking around the motherboard power supply
> connectors with VOM leads that it would suddenly start working again.
> Since then I have been able to verify empirically that pulling and
> reseating the connector will restore it every time.
>
> Examining the p/w connectors carefully this afternoon, I noticed that the
> white connector housing had discolored around the two green wires, the 3.3
> volt feed. I surmise that this is the chip supply, though I don't know
> for sure. The two green leads were getting too hot right at the m/b.

I had the same problem some time ago (last year?) with a UDB where I didn't
really understand what's going on (first ramdom crashes and more and more often
problems with reboot until it was completely dead). finally DEC replaced
(to my surprise;) the case including the power supply unit/cords which fixed
all problems. later I saw the same contact problems: no more completely blank
pins at the power plug on the board and slightly brown areas on the main board
around these pins due to heat...

> Thought this might be a useful tip with all the UDBs out there.....

yep, if I only would have known earlier;) it took my quite a long time
to understand these crashes and reboot problems, sigh;)
