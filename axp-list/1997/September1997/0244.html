On Fri, 12 Sep 1997, Terry Bayne wrote:

> Huh? Last time I looked in my UDB the CPU was socketed into a ZIF type
> socket very similar to those that are used on pentium motherboards....and it
> fact (I just tried it) the CPU w/heat sink is very easy to remove.

A lot of people are buying the UDB model FX40 from www.onsale.com - an
online auction from where you can get an Alpha 166MHz UDB for around $400
(I think they're starting at $300 now) with no memory, video, or storage.
The FX40's CPU is soldered to the MoBo and uses an older design, which is
really not capable of being overclocked. The FX41 and FX42's (dunno about
the Pentium based UDBs, if they exist) are ZIF sockets and likewise have a
newer design, which allows (without support) for overclocking. Also, the
FX40's cache is non-upgradeable, whereas the newer models' is.
