>I start getting errors after 10 hrs at 75F. It's getting into fall here,
>and with the window open this evening the thermistat in the comp room says
>57F (low 40's outside), the therm sitting in the airstream of an idle UDB
>reads 76... Between the 2 UDB's I can heat half this place, so I wouldn't
>totally rule it out as a problem. An even *slightly* flaky simm would
>give trouble next to that heating element DEC calls a CPU.

Yeah, that's similar to the problems I've long had here in my home
office, though in this new condo, it's in the basement, which helps
a *lot*. (In our first condo, my office was in the 2nd floor SW-facing
bedroom; during the spring/fall days, outside temps 55-70F or so,
we'd have to A/C the rest of the house down to around 63F to keep
my office below about 78F, even with only that one vent open!)

Anyway, I doubt we can afford to run our home A/C sufficiently to keep
ambient here in my office down below 70F. (It's 71.7 right now.) I do
have two smallish fans to blow the heat stack off the top of my 486
(tower under the desktop) and its monitor. The thermometer I have has
an external sensor I've placed above the tower just under the desk;
I try to keep the temp of that air (basically coming out of the tower)
below 80F, usually in the 78-80 range, below that I don't worry so
much. The little Brookstone-bought fan that pushes air through that
area knocks about 2F off that temperature, which is nice, and it's
fairly quiet in most directions. (The Holmes fan for the monitor is
much noisier, so I might replace that with a new Brookstone.)

While I can't imagine getting the ambient here down much lower than I do,
for personal comfort reasons as well as affordability, it *is* good
to know there are people out there "crazier" than me. I've talked
to various HVAC types, even gotten an estimate on doing some minor
improvement to vent heat up from the basement (so we don't freeze on
the 1st floor ;-), and everyone seems to think it's weird I even
*care* about what temperature my computers run at. (I think even
my wife wonders about me.)

Maybe now I'll worry less about the ambient going below 70F, though,
especially when I'm not in the office. Right now I have only one
computer (an old, hot, 486) running; pretty soon I'll have two (add
the UDB, hopefully), then three (another Alpha?), maybe even four
(an UltraSPARC?), though all but one will normally have the monitors
powered off. I'm concerned about this; the rough estimate for a
special built-in A/C unit in the office is around $5K or so, which
I don't happen to have in my wallet right now. Might have to relocate
some systems w/o monitors; fortunately, I have cat-5 cabling to several
locations in the condo already built into the walls (foresight on my
part, I hope it actually works ;-), so I could put one machine in
the other basement room near the electrical panel, another in the loft,
maybe one in my wife's office (if it's quiet enough), etc. I'm hoping
the much more modest plan I have an estimate on suffices.

If only this wasn't a condo, and we actually *owned* the back yard
ourselves. Then I could dig out a special underground, cheap-to-cool
room for all these computers. Yeah, that'd be the ticket. (And
I'd have another worry: flooding.)
