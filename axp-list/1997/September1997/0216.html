On Fri, Sep 12, 1997 at 01:52:53PM +0900, Koichi Kawabata wrote:

> I'm runnning RedHAt 4.2 Linux on DEC PWS 433a(Miata) and having a
> problem with its clock. The system clock (displayed by 'date' command)
> goes too faster than the CMOS clock.
>
> I found the same problem are reported by Andreas Ulrich Trottmann
> on this list as follows:
>
> > Second: The clock runs quite fast -- after one "real" hour, the system
> > time has advanced about one hour and ten minutes. The CMOS time reported
> > by clock -Au -r is correct, however.
>
> Does anybody know how to solve this problem?

Umm, actually, I still haven't found /the/ solution... I haven't done
anything I think that could have fixed it, but by now, the clock runs at
correct speed /most/ of the time. However, from time to time, it runs too
fast again. Kind of a Heisenbug (if you look at it, it vanishes) :)

Up to now, nobody on the list has had any ideas concerning the "bug",
although several people (all of them Miata owners) are suffering from it.
