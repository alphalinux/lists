donald@swiftuganda.com wrote:
>
> Hi all.
> My problem is like this.
> I was installing Redhat on my box which is a 486 with 33Mhz and with an S3
> chipset. As I got to the stage of X configuration the monitor just
> stopped working and switched off and even if you switch it on it can't get
> back on. So I think it is a problem as in power was low or my monitor got
> blown up. What do you think people?
> Thank you
> Donald
>
>

Hmmmm....don't know that I am the expert here and I really don't know
what motherboard you have...but here are a couple of ideas. On a Mikasa
motherboard I know there is a jumper on the motherboard for switching
from the on board Cirrus chipset to the new board you have put in. If
you have a motherboard that has an on board Cirrus chipset, you might
try taking the S3 board out, resetting the jumper to the on board Cirrus
and seeing if it works. I had a similiar problem, only it was with
Digital Unix, and the problem turned out to be that the monitor could
not handle the sync rates the chipset was told to send it. When I put a
Digital monitor on the problem cleared up. I had the same thing happen
about turning the power on and off and the monitor not working. BTW,
the monitor was fine when I put it on another machine. If you have a pc
you might want to try that. I have never heard of the SRM or milo
blowing a monitor up.
