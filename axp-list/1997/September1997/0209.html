At 08:23 PM 9/11/97 -0600, Tim Sullivan wrote:
>Hello,
>
>I've been trying to install V4.2 on a UDB with an external 2.16GB SCSI
>drive. With fdisk I specified 540cyl, 128hds, 64sect (0-63). The
>install formatted the swap partition with a surface check (cyl 501-540)
>then made the ext2fs with surface check on partition 1 (cyl 1-500). It
>then displayed a pop-up error message:
>
> Mount Failure: Invalid Argument
>
>Any ideas what the problem might be?

I had exactly the same problem. I made two changes at the same time so I'm
not sure what fixed the problem. What I did was instead of having three
partitions (/, swap, /usr - guess who was brought up under BSD ?) I
repartitioned the disk into two (/ and swap) and then the install
completed. I don't know whether it was rewriting the partition information
or only having two partitions, but it fixed the problem...
