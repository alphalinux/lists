In fact, the problem of removing a large core file I mentioned yesterday
appears to be just a part of a more general one on my UDB (166MHz, 24 MB RAM,
518 MB internal 2.5'' SCSI hard drive, 40 MB swap, RedHat 4.2 running). About
a week ago I installed g77-0.5.21 (for completeness, I could not compile it
on my machine, probably because of too small RAM, so I compiled it on my friend's
UDB, then after 'make bootstrap' and 'make compare' tarred the whole /usr/src/gcc
directory and put it on my UDB, and installed). For simple FORTRAN programs,
everything works fine. When I try to compile my Quantum Monte Carlo program, which
has about 150 kB of source code, the compiler often crashes with signal 11.
It appears to happen in an unpredictable way, although the probability of crash
strongly increases with increasing optimization level. It has never crashed with
no optimization, on the other hand, almost always crashes with -O2 and sometimes
with -O1. The crash produces a huge core file, the largest one was about 24 MB,
and when I delete it, it disappears but continues to occupy disk space. I get the
disk space back only via fsck which finds some wrong inode and fixes it.
I have read the GCC-SIG-11.FAQ and learned that it might be in fact
a hardware problem. However, about a month ago I compiled the kernel and apart from
the Flashpoint option, I did not have any problem in the compilation. Also, the
whole GCC-SIG-11.FAQ seems to mention only Linux/Intel and not Linux/Alpha. Has
somebody had a similar problem, and how can I diagnose whether it's a true hardware
problem ? Or, can it be that g77 simply requires so much memory for compilation that
my RAM and swap just cannot handle it ?

Thanks a lot for any suggestion,
