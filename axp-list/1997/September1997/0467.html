No, this seems pretty universal with RedHat alpha.

My interpretation of gnu, gcc and glibc faqs is that user apps should never
call include/linux tree, and that where a gnu/something.h exists, you should
use that instead of something else, ie use gnu/types.h instead of sys/types.h.

Most of the problems that I have seen seem related to sys/types.h and
the way it arbitrarily (and often needlessly) leads to inclusion of
include/asm files. I've resorted to placing #ifdef CFM into my
/usr/include tree. Yeech.

Just wait till you start playing with __fd_set. ;^)

FWIW I'm abandoning the RPMs and going back to primary sources.
Unfortunately, even the basic tools ( eg gcc ) don't really seem
to exist in state-of-the-art form (2.7.2.3) for alpha.

FWIW 2, my powerpc linux box seems to have much cleaner headers
and I was thinking I'd try to reconstruct based on the dependancies
there.
