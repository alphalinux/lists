Am installing RH 4.1 (would upgrade, but I decided to toss out a 1GB Seagate
that's been giving me grief in exchange for a Quantum fireball).

So, make the three disks and start off (boot, root, and milo). Disk gets
partitioned, new milo sent out to partition 1, a swap partition, and what will
be root partition.

Do a floppy boot and start installing. Process fails at about Xaw3d.rpm. Says
it cannot install any more rpms. On a hunch, I went over to the shell to
discover that the hard disk is mounted RO, and it and cdrom are mounted in
/tmp. Would
that I could find their node entries in /tmp (they are gone at that point).
A look at the error log suggests the ncr53c8xx driver is having all kinds of
trouble with the CD-ROM's Sense Key. Okay, I double-checked my work: there's
no difference in the termination of these devices than from yesterday.

So I go back to 4.0 and try to install it. Better, but it still crashes, at
about installation of the "dev" package. Ah, what the hell, let's install 3.03.
THIS WORKS. Difference is that the boot disks for that puppy uses 53c7,8xx
drivers, and the later distributions use ncr53c8xx. Don't get me wrong; before
I upgraded/installed, I used it too, with no problems. What gives? Better
still,
Eric informs me that the boot floppy has both drivers on it. If that's so,
can anyone give me the command line boot parameters to select the scsi driver
I want?

At present time, I am eliminating unneeded devices from the chain, and moving
the terminator closer. It's been a weird day; does anyone have words of cheer?
Suggestions?

TIA,
