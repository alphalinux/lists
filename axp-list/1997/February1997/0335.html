>On Tue, 11 Feb 1997, Topi Kanerva wrote:
>
>> is there *any* way to point out that there *is* market for applixware on
>> linux/alpha?
>> i know even many *local* linux/axp users who would *love* to buy an axp
>> version of applixware.
>
>If we sold one Applix for every copy of Red Hat Linux/Alpha we've sold
>the port would remain unjustified. It's pretty hard to argue with that
>logic.

In fact, it isn't. With things like the Infomagic Developers Set including
RedHat/AXP, not every RedHat CD out here was sold by RedHat. Also, I
suspect that a large portion of linux/AXP users have decided to go ALPHA
after seeing it at a friend's --- which means that each CD probably results
in more than one installation.

In fact, I'd say the number of copies of RedHat/AXP sold is nothing more than
a lower bound for the number of Linux/AXP systems out there --- certainly
not an upper bound.
