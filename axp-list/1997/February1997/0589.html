> However, I also don't really understand that you'd rather the system
> hid from you errors in your program that can do just as bizzare
> undefined things on other systems, as Jim Nance pointed out for SunOS.

actually my point wudda have been the the primitive os calls
open/close behave in predictable ways.
the first close() I would get a return of 0;
the second close() I would, and expect a return of -1 with errno set.
I do not expect an system crash to happen. If a system crash does
happen, according to this discussion, it is not the fault of the system
designer, but rather the fault of the poor victum, who has stumbled on
to the failing condition. Who is at fault - they both are. Who should be
more responsible - The OS designer - that person knows, or should know
that things like that are bound to happen.
I can only say that the analogy is the same with fclose(). I expect
fclose to give me an error return - something more gracefull than a
segfault. Fclose should know that the ptr is invalid - points to no open
channel - and give an appropiate error code.
