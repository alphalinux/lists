I can mention a few things just plain wrong with the installation
process. If, for some reason, things don't go absolutely right, it is
almost impossible to massage things around the installer. A few
things that make it exceptionally difficult:

Lack of /dev/fd0
No mknod, mount or other very important tools

Unfortunately I don't remember exactly what problems I was having, but
it was very frustrating. That is one thing I like about slackware, it
gives you the control you need to make things right when something
goes wrong, instead of rebooting (which the installer has done to me
many times).

--brett
