I assume that sda1 (C:) is the system partition. If that's the case,
you will never be able to make the partition an NTFS partition. I
partitioned my disk with sda1 a 10 MB FAT system partion (contains NT
boot stuff and MILO stuff), sda2 is swap, sda3 is Linux, and sda4 is
NTFS, and everybody seems happy. I think your "can't load root fs"
problem may be that you're not specifying the root partition when you
boot from the HD. Try using the MILO command "boot sda3:vmlinux.gz
root=/dev/sda3" to boot from the HD.
