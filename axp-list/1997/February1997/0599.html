It's not a bug, it's a feature :) MILO talks w/ the first COM port (the
UDB's actually have two, btw...but you need a Y-cable to actually use the
second one) at bootup. This is nice if you're having problems or for
debugging, but can be a little weird in day-to-day usage. I'd recommend
either trying to find said Y-cable (last I checked this was impossible; you
could always make your own if yer comfortable w/ that, tho) or just not
turning your modem on until after MILO has done its thing.
