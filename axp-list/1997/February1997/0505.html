> I am new to this list, and I am thinking of jumping into the big game!
> I may be acquiring a UDB with 166MHz processor. I am probably going to
> start with 16MB of RAM. Will this cut it for Linux?

That all depends on what you plan to do with the machine. This should be
reasonable to get it up and running initially. However, if you plan to use
X windows then you'll definitely want some swap space or you'll be
extremely cramped. I've found that the alpha binaires are a good bit bigger
than their Intel counterparts so you need corresponding more memory (real
RAM not just swap) to get similar performance.

> I don't know enough about the UDB, yet, but is it possible to add on
> another PCI SCSI card to get external ports going? Will it support SCSI
> interface [it is a step up from SCSI-2, it has one port only, I believe it
> is called SCA].

Most UDBs (if not all) come with an external scsi port on the back and use
an NCR chipset which is supported by linux. You can easily use any external
scsi device including a hard drive with the machine. In fact this is
recommended since its nigh impossible to get a 2.5" scsi hard drive that is
of any decent size nowadays.

> Also, if I got WindowsNT 4.0 WS, could I get it to co-exist with Redhat
> Linux/Alpha Linux [whatever it is called]?

Yeah, I hear that you can but I've never done it myself.

good luck
