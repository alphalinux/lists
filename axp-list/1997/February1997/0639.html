On Tue, 18 February 1997, at 18:30:55, Erik Troan wrote:

> | "RPM is the greatest thing since swap-space" - Bryan C. Andregg

Brett McCormick responded:

only if you happen to be running a redhat system... otherwise, try
unpacking one without converting into a cpio file..

Actually, I wonder what RPM wizards would say: on a couple of
occasions I had to fight RPM into submission, and I wasn't sure what
is the best/recommended way of reinstalling.

Don't get me wrong---I also think that RPM is great; I encountered
problems on two occasions only: when I worked with a Craftworks system
and an ancient RedHat with pre-2 RPM installed. I noticed that when
RPM is out of sync, it can do weird things including SEGV crash (more
details later).

In both cases I cajoled RPM by:

- getting a latest version from RedHat site ( from
redhat/code/rpm/rpm-2.2.x/rpm-2.2.8-1.i386.cpio.gz to, say, /tmp directory

- installing it:
cd /; gunzip < /tmp/rpm-2.2.8-1.i386.cpio.gz | cpio --extract -v

- rebuilding the databases (perhaps preceded by 'rpm --initdb')
rpm --rebuilddb

The rebuilding step proved necessary on couple of occasions where new RPM simply crashed
when presented with an old (perhaps corrupted?) database.

Is the above a good way about it?
