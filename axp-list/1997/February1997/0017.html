At 01:32 hrs. 02-02-97 -0500, Thomas Clouser <tclouser@tiac.net> wrote:

> I am permanently removing NT off my UDB, this will free up two
additional drives (3 total). What is the most recommended division of Linux
across the three drives (i.e. put \usr on a seperate drive from \ ).
>
Putting /usr on another drive is fime, but there is one "gotcha" with the
Red Hat install to watch for. /Usr will never unmount at shutdown, and you
will have to endure a lengthy fsck at boot time following. The cure for this:

cp /usr/lib/libnss_{db,dns,files}-1.93.so /lib
ldconfig -v
rm -f /usr/lib/libnss*
