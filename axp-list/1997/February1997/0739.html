> I have used slackware for years on a PC and i would like to know, why
> do you call it "outdated"? It has plenty of new tools and apps and an
> easy graphical instalation. I have been interested in the port myself and
> would like to know what makes it "outdated".

a) It doesn't provide the degree of logging *and control* of package components
that newer packaging formats (like RedHat/Debians') provide.
b) It does not provide a dependancy-checking tool for determining what
packages require other packages.
c) There are components that are rather old and buggy. Slackware is the
only distribution of TeX materials that I know of that still includes the
fonts that Don Knuth specifically requested *not* be distributed about
2 years ago. It's a couple releases out of date on Ghostscript, using
2.6.1, which is no longer supported at all by the nice folks at Aladdin
Software. The list probably goes on.

There simply isn't much effort going into continued development in
comparison with the efforts going into other distributions.
