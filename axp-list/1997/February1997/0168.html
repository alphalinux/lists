On Wed, 5 Feb 1997, Kevin Osowski wrote:

> I hate rpm. I can say no more. Is there a way to extract from a rpm
> without using rpm.

rpm is a fine solution for people who just want a stock basic machine. It's
then trivial to apply any updates from RH on it, and everyone is happy. My
problem with rpm is if John Doe thinks it's cool to release his software as
rpm only. A few days ago i was tempted to see how the netscape wrapper someone
posted would work compared to earlier ones I use now (and still cause havoc).
He just released it as rpm. I then went on the quest to find rpm for the i386.
(I dont run a standard distr on our i386's). Apart from the various
rpm.x.y.z.rpm, I couldn't find it. Not on sunsite with the other archive
programs. Through Redhat's WWW server, I managed to find a url, that led to
their ftp server, which was overloaded and/or crashing (Granted, the next day,
I heard they had RH 4.1 out, so I guess the mirrors took their toll).

So, if anyone at RedHat would be so kind to upload an rpm.src.tar.gz to sunsite,
so we dont have to rely on RedHat's server alone, that would be great.

Paul
