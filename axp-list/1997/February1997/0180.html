>>> David Applegate said:
> I have a DEC Alpha XLT-366 (an XL-266 that's been upgraded by DEC to a 366).
> I just upgraded to 256 meg (in 2 banks of 4 32MB simms). If I only populate
> one bank (ie, 128 meg), it boots and loads Linux. However, if I populate
> both banks, the system hangs during the load with the message
>
> Swapping to PALcode at 0x8000 ...

Sigh... :-(

It appears, at the moment, that current MILO/linload are experiencing a
problem with memory size 256Mb or above.

Unfortunately, the XLT-366 *must* use the latest linload, as prior versions
didn't work with AlphaBIOS. I believe that older linloads didn't have this
256Mb problem, but I can't be sure as none of my machines have more than 128,
but I think I remember other folks on the net saying that they'd been running
fine with 256 or more.

So, for now, downgrade to 128Mb and keep an eye on this list, the problem
is being worked, and we'll announce a fix ASAP.
