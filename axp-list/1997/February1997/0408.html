>i can get my udb running rhl/axp 4.0 to make a ppp connection where it is
>not connected to a local network. however, what i want to do is have it
>make a ppp connection (to a sun running solaris 2.5, but that shouldn't
>matter) where any machine on my local network can talk to any machine
>on the network at the other end. the situation is that the network at
>the other end is not connected to the outside world.

This is really a couple of questions. If you mean you can't get a ppp
connection when you are also running on a local ethernet you may want to
add "noipdefault" to your pppd command line. Without this ppp tries to
negotiate the same IP from your provider as it uses on the local net, and
in all probability your ISP can't provide that address (or if it can you
have chosen your local net addresses badly:-)

As for connecting a machine on your local net to others outside of you -
remember that when you are using ppp you are node on the same net as the
ppp server, and machines other than the one connected via ppp can't be
seen. One solution is IP Masquerading, check out the HOWTO on the subject.
