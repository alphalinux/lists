I would recommend that you get 32MB of ram. 16MB results in almost constant
swapping when running X.

Both the UDB's I have had came with external SCSI-2 connector. It is my
understanding (perhaps not entirely accurate) that the UDB's that came without
an external SCSI connector have a 3-1/2" SCSI drive (1 GB) internally mounted,
also sacrificing the PCI slot.

I have had WindowsNT 4.0 WS installed as well as Linux on my UDB. It is very
easy to set this up for dual booting (much easier than Intel), due to the ARC
firmware. In fact, NT installs easier on the Alpha than on Intel (IMHO).
However, you may find, as I, that Linux gets more performance out of the
hardware than NT.
