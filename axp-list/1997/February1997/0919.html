There are no native Linux Alpha java-aware web browsers available, AFAIK.
In reading this list over the past 8 months, I have *never* seen any mention of
free or low cost alternatives to the OSF/1 (Dec Unix) libraries. Many of us
(including myself) who do not have access to DEC Unix would love to get these
to do exactly what you want to do. Similarily, no NT emulation exists.

As much as I like Linux/Alpha, I would reconsider using it as your
only workstation. If all you are content to run is freely distributed/
source available software, or just as a development platform (but note no
Motif/ELF at this time), then it probably does not matter much. But if you are
looking a running commercial apps such as an office package (like Applixware,
StarOffice, etc.), Netscape, or other apps, Alpha/Linux is just too small to
economically justify the cost of porting (not insignificant, due to running
down 32/64 bit problems and general instability in the C libraries). Also,
no Motif/ELF restricts the porting/development of X-based software
running on this platform at this time. Of course, this situation may change
once there are sufficient numbers of Alpha/Linux boxes out there, and ELF/glibc
stabilizes.

There are ways of working around the current state of Alpha/Linux. I have a
Linux x86 box networked to my Alpha, and can run Netscape and commercial apps
(Applixware, NExS, WorkPerfect, Visual SlickEdit via X. With exported home
directories to the x86 system via NFS, it works fairly well, however the
graphics are a bit slow. But it is good enough for me to use my Alpha as
principal workstation.
