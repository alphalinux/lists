Having some experience in both platforms and considering your problem
domain; Alpha. No question. Large matrix FP ops is an area in which the
Alpha particularly excels (compared to the Pentium-Pro in SMP.) As to gcc
possibly not taking full advantage of Alpha clock cycles -- dunno on that
one. But, by the time you make your decision I'd bet this will not be of
any significance anyhow....

Look into the systems available from Microway. They are on the web at
www.microway.com or phone 508-746-7341. They specialize in systems for
folks with your needs. The Alpha MB's they use are DEC PC164's but with
2MB of L3 SRAM instead of the usual 1MB to particularly assist in work
with large arrays. These folks also support the Intel Pentium-Pro SMP
platforms. They have several white papers on their web site which you
might find informative.

You might also wish to look into the systems soon to be available (around
May or so,) from DEC and also Aspen Systems which use SDRAM for _much_
improved main memory I/O bandwidth.
