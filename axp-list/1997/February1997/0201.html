Michael Sandfort wrote:
>
> Hi,
>
> I hope this is an appropriate forum in which to raise this
> question. I will, in about 4-5 months, be purchasing a
> research computer from which I will be demanding as much
> speed as possible in the performance of many matrix
> multiplications and inversions using C/CLAPACK. My options
> are essentially a 2x200Mhz Pentium Pro system running Linux
> SMP or a 433Mhz Alpha system running Linux-Alpha (128Mb
> memory on either machine). I have absolutely no experience
> with benchmarking, so I have some difficulty interpreting
> the published test results for these two systems.

There is no comparision that will put a Pentium Pro, even SMP, anywhere
close to any 433 Mhz alpha.
>
> I am looking for some information on the comparability of
> these systems as well as the anticipated state of stability
> of the respective kernels 4-5 months from now. I have heard
> that the alphas are very fast machines, but also that Linux
> on the Alpha is a little chancy at this point (ie gcc problems,
> not taking full advantage of the Alpha's fast clock).
>
> Also, any information on the running of DU binaries under Linux
> and the licensing/expense issues involved would be appreciated.
>
Gcc and the libs should allow for NIS by then. Not that anyone wants to
hear this on this list and I will most likely get blasted for saying
it...While Linux does not take full advantage of an Alpha, on the lower
end the difference is negligable between Linux and Digital Unix (OSF),
however, on the higher end machins there is siginificant performance
differnces between OSF and Linux. At least this has been my experiance
and I own many Alphas (including the 1000 that I am writing this on).
