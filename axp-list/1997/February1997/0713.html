I'm in the process of upgrading from 3.0.3 to 4.0.

I have had the entertaining experience of changing MILO versions,
moving up from whatever the old version was to 2.0.12. (Apparently
the old one can't load 4.0-based kernels; 2.0.12 can't load
3.0.3-based kernels. Ne'er the twain shall meet... Happily I can fit
both MILOs on the 1.44MB "boot partition..."

At any rate, I've at last got MILO and the 2.0.18 kernel behaving
fairly well.

I thought I'd boot by:
MILO> boot -t iso9660 sr0:kernels/noname.gz root=/dev/sr0

The kernel loads fine; it then searches my LAN for a plase to find an
NFS mount rather than looking on the CD-ROM that it just loaded the
kernel off of.

Oh, well.

I'll just have to make a root floppy (on my Cyrix box):
# dd if=/mnt/cdrom/images/ramdisk.img of=/dev/fd0 bs=64k

MILO> boot -t iso9660 sr0:kernels/noname.gz root=/dev/fd0
... and lots of sensible kernel messages ...
gets me up to the point of:
VFS: Insert root floppy and press ENTER

Great! I'll just press ENTER.

[MS-DOS FS Rel. 12, FAT 12,check=n,conv=b,uid=0,gid=0,umask=022,bmap]
kernel: unaligned trap atfffffc0000393294: fffffc000000a411 29 1
[me0x53,cs=480,#f=185,fs=55972,fl=54919,ds57907,de=53191,data=61232,se=27865,ts=1627421107,ls=2972]
Transaction block size = 512
Kernel panic: VFS: Unable to mount root fs on 02:00

The floppy can't be bad; I've switched floppies multiple times on the
off chance of bad sectors.

Any thoughts out there?
