>i've got a seagate barracuda st32550n drive i'm trying to connect
>to a multia. i go into fdisk to set the drive params, and it won't
>accept values great than 63 for number of sectors. the geom for this
>drive is:
>
>cyl: 3510
>heads: 11
>sectors: 108

As it happens, 3510 * 108 == 6318 * 60. You should be able to tell
fdisk your geometry is:
c 6318
h 11
s 60
and work cleanly.

My drives also have more than 63 heads. I'm using the same kind of
mech on a UDB and on a PC, though, and the PC bios already had its
own conversion of the real geometry to something that PC bioses can
cope with, in a way similar to the above. I just copied the PC bios's
interpretation; if that makes you feel better than entering numbers
you just pulled out of a system of equations, you can do that just as
well.
