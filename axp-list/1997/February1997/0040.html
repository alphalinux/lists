> Do you know what the jumpers (J1 J2) and or the switch (0-F) does?

It's strange that no docs came with the board, but here's what mine say
about the jumpers:

On the 2Mb board, (aka 3D30, I believe) the jumper closest to the rotary
switch is the ALIAS jumper, the other the VGAEN. Put the jumper ON the VGAEN
if you want to enable the VGA. Put the jumper ON the ALIAS if you're running
the card on an EV5 CPU-based platform (which I believe you are not).

The rotary switch chooses the resolution/refresh rate you wish to run X in
under Digital UNIX or VMS (*not* NT, however). Settings B/C come closest
to standard VGA, ie 640x480 at 72/75Hz.

I believe that running under ARC or AlphaBIOS, the rotary switch setting is
ignored, and you get the normal VGA-style screen. Under SRM, I would think
that the VGA would be used normally in console mode, but when running DEC's
X server, it would look to the setting and set the resolution/refresh rate
accordingly.
