Well, I guess this is confusing. There's a discussion going on right now on
this list, about hangs at bootup, which can be solved by removing
/etc/nsswitch.conf. That's on RedHat 4.1 though. 4.0 also featured a hang at
bootup, which can be solved by _adding_ an /etc/nsswitch.conf.

(Actually, your machine doesn't hang initializing the ethernet board, but
immediately after that, starting network daemons and such. Without the
nsswitch.conf it tries to resolve addresses through DNS first, and looks at
the /etc/hosts file after DNS times out. If you're patient your machine will
boot in the end, but it's better to create a /etc/nssitch.conf wich directs
adress resolution to the local files directly.)
