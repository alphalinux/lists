I've recently added a Diamond Stealth 64 DRAM to my Multia.
Yesterday, I tried to switch from ARC to SRM (I heard about
"audio_test -mclip 1" and wanted to hear it), but SRM would give some
kind of protection violation(??) and dump some kind of debugging trace
out to the first serial port (it was some kind of debug monitor,
because I could step through what appeared to be Alpha assembler code,
but I didn't know what else I could do).

Once I removed the Diamond video card, SRM came up just fine. (And
after I finished playing, I set os_type back to NT, put the video card
back in, and everything booted just fine.)

Curiouser and curiouser. Here are my questions:

What happened? What did I get on the serial port? How do I use it?
Is there a way to get SRM to output to the serial port instead of the
video? Is there documentation on all the SRM (or ARC, for that
matter) environment settings and commands (the service manual
[EK-MULTS-IN.C01] doesn't list all the commands and none of the
environment settings)?

And the $64,000 question is: How do I change the SRM startup script?
(Is there a startup script?) Better yet, how does SRM work, in all of
its glory (i.e. nitty-gritty details)?
