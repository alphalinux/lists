Historically the FCC has always tried to avoid passing any regulations regarding pricing of Internet connectivity. They are still taking a very slow and cautious road responding to increasingly loud RBOC demands.

I am personally on the fence on this issue. No, the current unlimited scheme is NOT a good business plan. But, the RBOCS/Cable and possibly electric companies are going to be the only options for home connectivity in the near future (not to my pleasure), as they own the infrastructer and can afford to undercut the ISPs. The only question I have, is if my theory is true, what are the RBOCs motivation in shooting for usage based pricing??
