Erik Troan said:
> If we sold one Applix for every copy of Red Hat Linux/Alpha we've sold
> the port would remain unjustified. It's pretty hard to argue with that
> logic.

No it isn't. :-)

Since Red Hat/Alpha costs twice as much as Red Hat/Intel and doesn't
offer any extra goodies, you can't really expect very many people to
buy it when they know they can get the exact same product on the net
or on your (or others') archives CD set. Therefore, assuming I'm
right (which I often like to do), you can't make any kind of sales
projections for Applix/Alpha from sales figures for Red Hat/Alpha.

In addition, it seems to me that the availability of decent
commercial-grade software should increase the demand for Red
Hat/Alpha...

I don't mean to tell you how to do business, but it seems to me that
you guys are shooting yourselves in the foot on the pricing of Red
Hat/Alpha, if not the decision not to port Applix to Alpha. (I'm not
saying I disagree with your decision, just your reasoning behind it.)

Just my $0.02...
