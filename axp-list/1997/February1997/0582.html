I have tried some little C/Fortran programs of mine
on PPro 200 and alpha 21164a 433 Mhz, 1 M Cache, both
running Linux. I have little experience yet, so I just
give my general impressions rather than timings.

i) The alpha would be a factor 2-3 faster on scalar programs
when using the DEC compiler on alpha.
Using gcc on alpha the gain was less then 2. On PPro I always used
gcc/g77.

ii) The PPro was very fast for a highly cache-intensive program
(matmult) where data fits into the 256 K 2nd level of the PPro. I still
need to do more tests on this though.
One should expect that the alpha gains a lot for data that find
into the alpha L3 cache and not into the PPro cache.

So, if you can not wait until gcc improves for the alpha you
should consider getting an alpha with digital unix (If your choice
is alpha). You will not get the Specfp95 numbers cited for
alpha with gcc. Anyone knows if the PPro SpecFp95 6.2 can be
reached with gcc/Linux ?

But, if you really can split up your jobs effectivly,
what kind of Alpha server with digital unix do you get for the price
of 10 PPro ?

And, to some extent, your decision might depend on the data/programs
that you typically use. Scalar/matrix ? If matrix, which size ?
Will the matrices fit into the machines cache ?

No experience with your options 2,3,5.
