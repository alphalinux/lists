> Erik Troan wrote:
> > Is /var on a separate partition btw?
>
> That's it! I normally want to keep /var
> on a fairly large partition since it can grow
> unexpectedly large. Preferabley it shouldn't
> be on the root partition because of this.
>
> I copied /var to the root partition and the update
> is working now. This looks like a problem,
> since the default is /var -> /usr/var.

I had this problem the other day with 3.0.3. I assume 4.1 is the same way.
The problem is that the redhat update program mounts your filesystem
under /mnt or something like that. Since /var is a symlinke to /usr/var
its pointing to the wrong place if you have your partition mounted
somewhere other than /. If you make var a symlink to usr/var (relative path)
then it works much better. Perhaps the update script or RPM needs an option
to check for absolute symlinks and change them into relative ones?
