T'Hawk,

Lesse, I saw amd, sendmail, inn, and samba all try to take the boot down.
Everyone except amd is back up on my box (I went in single-user and removed
them from the inittab). Even with the updated rpm's, you still get a lot of
grief about no ypserver, but the quantity of gripes seems limited to whenever a
modification to utmp occurs (e.g., login or su). Going to 2.1.24 has helped
considerably from the 2.0.28 in this matter. Plan is, as soon as Thorsten
Kukuk's server comes back up, pull down one of each and build. Modifying the
nsswitch.conf (for me) to not look for nisplus servers has not been panacea.
But things are working (need to re-enable amd though)

I wonder how much of this will go away when we stablize to 2.1 glibc, or 2.2
(since Uli releases intermediates every Sunday, we'll be there soon). I've made
a very large tarball partition to figure this out...
