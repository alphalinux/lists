> If folks won't support a $49 product (as of Red Hat 4.1) I seriously doubt
> enough folks will shell out $199 for Applix. If we extrapolate Intel 4.1
> sales to recent Applix sales and do the same for 4.1 Alpha we get some
> numbers which will not come anywhere near convincing Applix to port to
> the platform.

I hadn't heard that you had dropped the price for Red Hat/Alpha...
That's great news. Let's see how that improves your sales figures.
It should definitely help, possibly more than you might think. For
example, I had told the bookstore that supplies my users group with
your products not to bother carrying the Alpha and Sparc versions...
I couldn't see anybody paying an extra $70 (approximately) for a
manual. I just called them up to tell them to add some of both to
their order for 4.1 though...

> > In addition, it seems to me that the availability of decent
> > commercial-grade software should increase the demand for Red
> > Hat/Alpha...
>
> Affordable Alpha's are what increases the demand more then anything else. If
> the current trends continue, that may happen in a big enough way to justify
> commercial Alpha software.

Most likely you are right. But, just like Linux on the Intel, cheap
hardware wasn't enough to drive the market. I got my first alpha a
few months ago because it was incredibly cheap, but I've considered
selling it since there's really very little I can do with it
(relative to my Intel boxes, that is). All it's really done for me is
give me a craving for high-end alpha hardware, which isn't at all
cheap.

All of a sudden I'm getting flashbacks of the Linux world in '94.
Remember when Doom for Linux came out? I think that proved that Linux
was worthwhile more than anything else. Is Quake doing the same thing
for the Linux on the alpha?

> > I don't mean to tell you how to do business, but it seems to me that
> > you guys are shooting yourselves in the foot on the pricing of Red
> > Hat/Alpha, if not the decision not to port Applix to Alpha. (I'm not
> > saying I disagree with your decision, just your reasoning behind it.)
>
> We agree (and lowered the price as a result). I've wanted to lower the price
> for a while, though I'm nowhere near convinced it'll increase the profits
> on Red Hat/Alpha one iota. While we'll sell more our profit margins were
> slashed by lowering the prices (after all, our costs stayed exactly the
> same) and I doubt we'll see enough volume increases to outweigh that in
> and significant way.

Maybe. Time will tell. :-)

I'm going to stop rambling now...
