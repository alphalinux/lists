Hi!

I'm currently in a team of (mainly) students who want to present Linux and
Linux/Alpha on a Networking and Un*x exhibition that takes place next
week.

I've joined the team as an (err...) "expert" for Linux/Alpha. Just to make
sure that I'm not making false promisses concerning Linux/Alpha I'd be
very happy if anyone could comment on the following items:

+) All the firewalling and IP-Masquerading goodies that are in the 2.0.x
kernels are working as well as their Linux/Intel counterparts, right?

+) What about high-end networking? Which networking hardware could be
recommended for Linux/Alpha when it comes to 100MBit Ethernet, FDDI or
(maybe even) ATM?

+) How about "the other guys", i.e. Linux/Alpha and Novell or Linux/Alpha
and WfW/NT? -- Do SMBFS-Samba and NCPFS-MaRS work on Linux/Alpha? (There
will probably be a lot of people around being interested in using Linux in
such networking environments.)

Thanks for your input!

Christian
