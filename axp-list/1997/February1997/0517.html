In article <199702131754.SAA27473@noel.feld.cvut.cz> you wrote:
: Hi,

: can anybody point me to reliable benchmark results comparing Alpha and
: PentiumPro?

: (I'd like to upgrade our department's linux computing server and have
: money for some 10 to 20 single PPro boxes - can Alpha provide beter
: performance/price ratio? Should I even consider Sun?)

: Or can anybody send me results of benchmark on Alpha which I could
: repeat on PPro?

>From the performance point of view, I would say, that our Alpha
(PC164=21164/366MHz/64MB with 128Bits MemoryBus) lies between
a Pentium Pro 166 and 200. However, if you plug SIMMs into all
of your SIMM slots you can have a 256Bits MemoryBus Bandwidth
and you will get a much better result.

However, I do not have exact benchmarks and I don't have the time
to run these :(

There are two things to say:
1) Alphas can be differently configured and PentiumPro too.
Eg. the MemoryBusWidth is important. (However, 256Bits fill
all your RAM-Slots and upgrading will be expensive!)
Alphas come with 1MB and 2MB second level cache.
2) When your computers get older, PCs are only garbage that
nobody wants to administrate. Alphas will remain to be Alphas
and will still be maintained. (Sure, this is a bit philosophical)

However, the point is, how costs the Alpha and how much the
PentiumPro? In Austria, I would say Alphas are _more_ expensive
and they do not have that much more performance.

A good solution could be to buy one/two Alphas as Server/Cases
where CPU-power is needed and some 586(not Pros) as workstation.

The best price/performance have Cyrix, than AMD, than Pentium.
A german computer magazine has benchmarked these chips.

I do not know, what you want to do with the machines, so some
of the things may be irrelevant.
