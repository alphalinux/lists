> First question?
> Why is the time always incorrect in Linux,

Not "always" but only when you set it incorrectly. If you boot
from ARC console then set your time configuration (CLOCKMODE) to "ARC".
You need, or need not, option -u in your /etc/rc.d/rc.system
depending if you are using UTC or local time. Red Hat script always
supplies -u with -A to 'clock'. Adjust as required.

> What is the most recommended division of =
> Linux across the three drives (i.e. put \usr on a seperate drive from
\ =
> ).

If you put /usr (not \usr!) on a separate partition do not forget to
move /usr/lib/libnss* librarier to /lib or you will have problems when
shutting down.

There is no "the most recommended division". It depends how you are
using your space and how big are disks. But I would start from
moving /home and /usr/local onto separate partition(s).
(/usr/local may live, for example, in /home/local with a symbolic
link to /usr/local). /var would be the next candidate.

A tip: if you leave a small root partition make sure that your /tmp
is big enough. It is pretty nasty experience to get a "file system
full" on a big compile. This big size can be achieved by the following trick:
- /tmp is a link to, say, /var/vtmp (small yet)
- you mount a much bigger partition on /var and it also provides /var/vtmp
- your /tmp is all of sudden much bigger, as its link points to a new
location.
