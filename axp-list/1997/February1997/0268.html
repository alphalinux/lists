Thanx Mikael,
I saw that some people were having problems with the machine taking
5 minutes to reach the ARC console on a UDB. I don't mind a small bug here
or there - as long as I can get a fast 1024x768 16 bit. Primarily I don't
want to have to wait any extended period of time for the system to boot, let
alone just
getting to the ARC console, pr in the worst case get the system home and have
it not boot at all.

I am interested in finding out about any problems using the Matrox Millenium
on the AXPpci 33 Motherboard. Anyone who has this configuration, Please send
me your experiences.
Thanx all
Traveler@Neosoft.Com

On Sun, 9 Feb 1997, Traveler wrote:
> I am about to purchase an AXPpci33 motherboard with the 233 MHz chip.
> I want to use it with a Matrox Millenium 4 MB I have sitting here.
> As I was looking on old archives of Linux Mailing lists I noticed that
> some people were having problems using it with their UDB's.
> Can anyone tell me if I am going to have problems with this configuration.
> I want to use it with Linux & NT.

It will work fine. Though, there seems to be a bug in the XFree86 Matrox
server, which 'garbles' the screen with high dotclocks (135Mhz is fine,
188Mhz is not) when used in 32bpp mode. With a 4M card you probably won't
need so high clocks so you should not be affected by the bug.
