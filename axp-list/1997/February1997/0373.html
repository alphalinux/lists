Yes, I can confirm that this is definitely an issue. I will also say that
while the FCC may be taking email and letters on the subject, there's
nothing you can really do to stop them from imposing this rule. As I work
for a large internet provider, we've known about this for several months
now, and as it appears to us, the FCC has the power to put those
regulations in place based on current legislation ( as bad as this may
sound...) I think we also all need to take into account that even though
this may slow the pace of growth of the internet in america, it is a much
needed fee to improve the telephone infrastructure. All current internet
backbones are already oversold, and even MCI's backbone, which is
currently being upgraded to OC-48, is becoming lagged in regions where
cable modems are being installed because of the great amount of bandwidth
the cable companies promise to provide to their customers. I think we all
need to look at this as a much needed source of funding for an out of date
and technologically lacking phone system.
