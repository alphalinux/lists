> Due to negligence at 11:07 PM 2/24/97 -0600, Adam Biskobing let this roam

> |> Well, I have finally gotten my UDB up and running and am alarmed by
> |>the amount of ECC memory errors that occur(even though it says they are

> |>this normal??
>
> It's normal for a UDB with the factory SIMMs in it, as far as I can tell :)
> It's been suggested before that reseating the SIMMs helps the problems,
> but, at least for me, it didn't. It's also been reported that the problem
> vanishes when new SIMMs are installed ...

My UDB has the factory DEC SIMM's in it, and I have never once seen any ECC
errors. I have had it since mid July '96 and the memory's been rock solid.
The SIMM's have been out of it a couple of times, and one set even spent a
week in my new pentium while I was hunting out the lowest price for a set
for it (the pentium). The UDB's been up and running for 54 days now, so any
heat troubles would have appeared by now, but I have seen no ECC errors.

Therefore, I would say it's not normal to be seeing ECC errors, even with
DEC's SIMM's in the machine.
