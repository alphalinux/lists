Hi,

I'm in need of more cpu-power and would like to get some recommendations.
Though, I'm not in an extreme rush, so I can wait if I can get a better
deal. From what I can see there is 2 choices (if I want motherboard only).

1. Buy a pc164 now (with a 21164A cpu)
3. Wait and see of good the 21164pc+mb is, and then decide.

Here are some facts,

* pc164 is more expensive than the 21164pc+mb will be
* The 21164pc will have better memorybandwidth due to SDRAM
* The 21164pc will have some new 'multimedia instructions'

The one that matters most to me is the memorybandwidth. From what I've
understood, the 21164 cpu's using DRAM scores about 160M/s in benchmarks
like stream (is this result with 128bit or 256bit mempath?!?), while
the new SDRAM alphas will score +300M/s from what I've understood. Seems
like a noticable difference. Though, from what I've understood the SDRAM
alphas will _not_ have 2nd or 3rd level cache, which seems like a bad
move.

So, atlast, my questions to you:

* How much is a pc164+cpu+cache (something like 366-433Mhz and 1-2M cache)
(If your running a company selling these, feel free to send me your
price in private mail to me.)
* How much speed diff between 1 and 2M cache for general usage? (like
gcc compiling)
* Any guess/knowledge about how fast a 21164pc system will be?
* Any guess/knowledge about when the 21164pc+mb will be available for
consumption? And if it uses a new chipset, supported when by linux?
(I guess the last questions is about a month after release or so due to
the great support from the Digital guys).

Finally, buy now and be happy, or wait and see?

Thanx for your input!
