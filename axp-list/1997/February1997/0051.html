mount -t iso9660 /dev/cdrom /mnt/cdrom

This assumes that the actual device (usually /dev/scd0 for SCSI cdroms) is
symbolically linked to /dev/cdrom, and that you have a mount directory created
as /mnt/cdrom.

To unmount it, use "umount /mnt/cdrom"

BTW, this is a general Linux question rather than Linux/AXP specific. Although
many of us are perfectly willing to help out on any questions you may have, it
is better to keep the questions topical to Linux/AXP as a favor to those who
have more specific interests. There are many excellent mailing lists and
newsgroups where general Linux assistance can be found, such as
redhat-list@redhat.com and the comp.os.linux hierarchy. If you are new to
Linux, there are some excellent resources found on the WEB, and documentation
covering many of the kind of questions that you face as a new Linux user are
readily available at your closest Linux Documentation Project mirror site.
Check out http://sunsite.unc.edu/mdw/hmirrors.html to find a mirror site
closest to you.
