Thanks to those who took time to reply to my plea for help, either back to
the list or to me personally.

As it turns out, I lead you all astray. A closer look at the console output
showed that the problem was with milo and not the kernel. I've installed
the 2.0.29 milo and also built the kernel using the "experimental"
NCR53C8XX (BSD based) driver. The system now boots (and reboots) without
requiring NT.

All I've got to do now is convince my boss to buy the XL300 to replace my
Multia and I'll be a happy chappy (for at least a week :-)
