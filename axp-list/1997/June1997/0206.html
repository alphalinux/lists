> Does anyone know if there is a site with AXP Linux files for POV Ray, or
> any of the other rendering packages?
Just grab the POV sources from povray.org and compile. There are some
problems (see Terry Rider's reply) but it works anyway. You could also
grab an older version like 3.0 which works without the problems Terry
Rider is trying to solve.

Also available is Rayshade (and the enhanced version Netshade) which
compiles just fine on AXP. It uses (but is not required) the Utah
Raster Toolkit, which also compiles, but with a few minor patches.
I use Netshade which has the advantage of some improved and new
primitives over Rayshade, and has networking capabilities. That is,
letting a bunch of workstations work on one image at the same time. I am
using this on a daily basis, using an UDB, a P90, and sometimes also a
486DX/33.
However I feel I should warn you that POV is noticeably faster than
Rayshade/Netshade. But that could also be because of the bigger range of
output quality options that POV has.

Feel free to ask me more about rayshade/netshade
I could supply you with binaries of POV and rayshade/netshade or I could
give the necessary patches for rayshade/urt.

POV: www.povray.org
Rayshade: http://www-graphics.stanford.edu/~cek/rayshade
Netshade: http://www.gyugyi.com/l3g0
