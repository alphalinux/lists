For a change of pace in this newsgroup, I thought I'd pass along a recent
success story featuring Linux/Alpha.

Just this weekend I successfully installed an AlphaStation 200 4/100
(that's a 100 MHz 21064) at my old fraternity house. It serves as a PPP
campus gateway, with IP masquerading, for several i86 Linux boxes, a few
Win95 clients, a Mac running MkLinux, and even the occasional NT boxen.
It also runs named, ypserv, httpd, and smbd.

For a low-end Alpha box, this server performs very well.

In the near future the box will probably serve a printer and/or CD-Rom
burner.
