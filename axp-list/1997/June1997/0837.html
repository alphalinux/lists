Now that I'm Cabriolet-less, and having to use my old 486/33 (without
X, which I can't install mainly because I can't get the most recent
versions of either S.u.S.E. Linux or Craftwork Linux to install...though
it seems like the former is due to a bug, the latter perhaps due to
my having a fairly offbeat monochrome video card, since it gets only
so far while booting and says "unable to initialize console" or some
such thing), I'm really hoping to get the UDB up to snuff shortly.

I've done some research, and am pretty sure about the following:

1. Upgrade the UDB on-board RAM to 64M or maybe 128M. (Dunno
what's on there now -- someone else indicated they'd added
64M to yield 88M, so maybe that means my UDB has 24M right now.)

2. Get a usable keyboard for the UDB. (Ideally, a Northgate
OmniKey/101, like I have used for years on my 486, and for
which I bought an adaptor to use on the Cabriolet while I
had it...and, yes, bought an adaptor so the keyboard that
came with Cab, which I tried to never use, was usable on the
486 for that period.)

3. Get a suitable external box from "PC Power & Cooling", which
apparently would result in a quieter external box than most.
(I'm intending to put the UDB not far from my wife's office,
so I don't want to add unnecessary noise to the system.)

4. Put one 2.0GB 3" half-height SCSI drive in box (I already have
one such drive; I might try to acquire another for it someday.)

5. Put a Plextor 12Plex CD-ROM in box.

6. Put a Yamaha CDR-400 CD-R in box.

This would become the Alpha I use for background testing and
benchmarking of GNU Fortran (g77), plus things like doing backups
(to CD-R -- I've never gotten the Sony optical drive I bought
for my 486 to work consistently under Linux, and CD-R is now
basically affordable enough as backup, plus the other benefits
it'll have down the road) and "official" system installs from
CD-ROM.

(Whereas, I still have high hopes of getting a higher-end Alpha,
thanks to David's and others' help, to use as my primary g77
development machine, which would have experimental/development
versions of software like gcc, gdb, and so on installed.)

I'm hoping somebody has comments or offers of help on any of
the above items. I'm not much of a hardware person, and I just
spent what felt like a very painful 8 hours or so on the 'net
browsing for info on the CD-R, CD-ROM (which actually didn't
take that long), the Northgate OmniKey/101 (which took *very*
long -- no FAQs for buying keyboards around that I could see!?),
the enclosure (also not bad, thanks to Eric Smith's enthusiastic
Web page, as recommended by people on this list), and so on.

For example, if anybody knows where I could buy one, or maybe
even two or three, OmniKey/101's (the kind with the DIP switches,
and of course the key caps intact and removable, the better to
properly place Ctrl :) in new or good condition, that would be
*very* helpful. I'm hardly in any mood to buy Microsoft keyboards,
for reasons that would be even more obvious if you could watch me
type once I get going in Emacs (some people claim I reach 100wpm,
but probably not more than 80 in real life, but stuff like Ctrl-A,
Ctrl-E, and so on are very much part of my touch-typing repetoire,
and there's no way I'm going to let MS train that outta me!).

And, any vendors here willing to offer prices or info on the
RAM upgrade, please contact me as well, since I know I don't
want to figure that out myself. (I can probably do the install
myself, but that's a big challenge as someone who is mechanically
declined.)

In any case, I doubt the replies will be of much general interest,
so replying just to me, not to the list, is probably appropriate!

Again, thanks in advance! I've already gotten so much great info
from this list, I can't help but "go to the well" again.

tq vm, (burley)

P.S. I blew a chance to investigate a very "interesting" bug
afflicting g77 on Digital UNIX that I might have been able to
reproduce under Linux on the Cabriolet, before I returned it,
had I been sufficiently alert. So, I'm looking forward to
outfitting the UDB sufficiently to at least make more progress
on g77, which, otherwise, seems to be doing quite well in
alpha testing. But I'm not planning on doing much, if any,
work on g77 while I get these systems/network issues worked out.
