Ross A. Knepper wrote:
>
> > > > % gcc -Dint=long "-Dunsigned=unsigned long"
>
> this would break unsigned char or unsigned short, however ...
> i dont know if a long char is even legal :)

*ulp*. You're right.

You might be able to eliminate "-Dunsigned=unsigned long", but
then you'd miss those cases where someone uses "unsigned" as a
synonym for "unsigned int". You *could* take your chances, or
you *could* say "-Dunsigned=blurfl" to point out all the places
in the code where "unsigned" is used. Fix those to explicitly
say char/short/int/long, then recompile with just "-Dint=long"
