axp@tifrbng.res.in (Avinash Shenoy) writes:
> What I did :
> - Used the RH 4.1 distribution, avanti.img, ramdisk, milo.
> - successfully installed RH4.1(used fdisk, not minlabel,with params 64heads,

You cannot use fdisk with SRM console, because the later doesn't
understand the partitions fdisk creates. You have to partition your
disk using 'minlabel', or the BSD option from fdisk. Unfortunetly, the
minlabel part of fdisk is _very_ buggy when it comes to entering the
partition parameters. Hints: When entering the beginning of the
partition, use blocks, and add '1'. When entering the partition size,
use the +..MB option, but don't enter the size, but the end of that
partition, e.g. the size+the sizes of all partitions before it.
I hope I recalled that correctly.

It might be easier to install a small linux first system, and then to use
'minlabel' to repartition the disk.

[..]

> aboot:Linux/Alpha SRM bootloader version 0.5
> aboot:swatching to OSF/1 PALcode version 1.46
> aboot:valid disklabel found: 0 partitions
> aboot:booted_dev = `scsi 0 6 0 0 0 0 0', guessing boot_device=`sda2'
> aboot:invalid partition 2
> aboot:mount of partition 2 failed
> Enter kernel name and arguments:
> aboot>
>
> I'm stuck at this point & do not know what to do next.

You'll have to reinstall your system, because swriteboot has
overwritten your fdisk partition table, so you won't be able to boot
your old system again. If you've written your fdisk parameters down,
you could just reenter them with fdisk, quit the installation, and
boot with a disk like before.

Than make backups, use 'minlabel' to repartition you disk, and do the
same as before. Having a bsdlabel style partition on your disk, it
ought to work as you described it.
