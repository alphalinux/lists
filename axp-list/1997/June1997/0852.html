improve things 100%

On Mon, 30 Jun 1997, Paul Wouters wrote:

> After trying a lot of mpeg3 players (almost all using x86 optimized assembly
> code) I found mpg123 (version V0.59f)
>
> I made a new entry where I took the gcc flags from the linux entry,
> and the object files from the dec entry and voila. Linux/AXP version
> was made :)
> On my Multia/UDB (166Mhz, 32MB Ram, RedHat 4.1) I can play mp3 if
> I dont touch anything (though I think even then its a little slow). Using
> -2 or -4 didnt improve things at all. Using -0 I can even switch xterms
> without hearing it. (It does depend a bit on the mpeg).
>
> Here's the Makefile entry I used:
>
> linux-axp:
> $(MAKE) CC=gcc LDFLAGS= \
> OBJECTS='decode.o dct64.o' \
> CFLAGS='-DLINUX -Wall -O2 \
> -fomit-frame-pointer -funroll-all-loops \
> -finline-functions -ffast-math' \
> mpg123
>
> I've CC:ed this to the redhat axp list, since I think some people will like
> having an mp3 player for their Linux/Alpha as well.
>
> Thanks Mike and Oliver :)
