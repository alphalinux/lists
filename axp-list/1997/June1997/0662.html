In article <Pine.GSO.3.96.970620165033.8014G-100000@shell1.aimnet.com> you write:
>My questions are 1) is there anything I should check at time of purchase
>to make sure the monitor will work with the UDB? and 2) does TGA support
>SVGA, or should I just be looking for a VGA?

With any VGA or SVGA monitor, the linux console and XFree in 640x480 and
256 colors will work. The SRM console might be bugguy, though. That's why
I use the NT firmware to boot (and this one allows autoboot also)(yes, I
know, shame on me, for using a Microsoft thing).

If you get a multisynch monitor, you may play with your XF86config
and get running some fun video modes. There are 2 MB of video ram in a UDB,
which is sufficient for 1600x1280 in 256 colors.
