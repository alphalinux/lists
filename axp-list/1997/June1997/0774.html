On Wed, 25 Jun 1997, RHS Linux User wrote:
> Recently, I am trying to mount hpfs on UDB. Although
> man pages say vfstype hpfs is OK, but when I try to mount it,
> it says hpfs is not supported by the kernel. Any way to
> get around this?

Is hpfs support loaded? Most things in the kernel are optional; unless you
compile them in (or load them as modules), they won't be available. Do the
command "cat /proc/filesystems" to get a list of filesystems supported by the
currently running kernel. If "hpfs" doesn't show up in the list somewhere,
then you'll need to do some work to load it. You can try "insmod hpfs", but
that may not work depending on your config, the release of RedHat Linux you
have, and the patches you have applied. You can always recompile your kernel
and include support for hpfs in the kernel itself; that may be easier then
fixing broken module support on a particular machine.

For further reading:
/usr/doc/HOWTO/Kernel-HOWTO
/usr/doc/HOWTO/Module-HOWTO
/usr/doc/HOWTO/mini/Kerneld
/usr/src/linux/Documentation/filesystems/hpfs.txt
