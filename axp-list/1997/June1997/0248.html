On Mon, 2 Jun 1997, Alex Krowitz wrote:
> > ... the P166 seems to be twice
> > as fast than the Alpha workstation!!

> Has there been any resolution to this issue? I'm thinking of buying a
> 500 MHz 21164 to run neural network simulations, and I need something
> faster than what Pentiums can give me now.

People point to things like BogoMIPS and SPECint as benchmarks; I really
don't put much stock in such things. The differences across hardware
platforms and OSes are too great to make anything like SPECint be a valid
benchmark. It is comparing apples and oranges.

Now, I point to performance, and the fact that the Alpha architecture is
significantly cleaner and more capable then the Intel. I point to a wider
memory bus, larger and faster memory chips, and a better design. I point to a
64-bit RISC CPU with virtual memory designed into it from the start. I point
to a total of 64 on-chip registers. These things all effect real world
performance in a way most benchmarks don't reflect.

Everything I've seen indicates that your fastest Alphas are a great deal
faster then your fastest Intels. Now, some Alpha motherboards and chips
(notably the UDB) appear to lack a lot, especially in memory bus width and
cache size. Later designs do not have these problems. I think you'll be hard
pressed to find an Intel that outperforms that Alpha under real world
conditions.

But this is all in my certainly not-so-humble opinion. YMMV.
