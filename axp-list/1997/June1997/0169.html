Also, your right to free speech does not give you the right to deny others their
right of free speech, which is exactly what mass mailing the list does, in a
limited sense, so far.

Another good point. The cost you are causing others here is extremely high. As
long as the content on this list was on topic and relatively low bandwidth and
good signal/noise ratio, the members of the list accepted that cost as the
logistical price of free information. However, now you've introduced a cost
which we should not be forced to accept. Now, one could say I could always
unsubscribe from the list to avoid the crap you're sending, but if that were
the case, is that not a denial-of-service by you? Denying me the ability to
read important messages because the high-bandwidth nature of the list would be
putting an inordinate amount of load on my server, which also provides services
to other people. So then, are you denying them service as well, if I keep the
list? And, given that the major users of this server are United Nations, U.S.
government, and educational institutions, are you constituting a national
security issue? All this from one little misunderstanding... Anyhow,
something to think about before you go ranting on about free speech.
