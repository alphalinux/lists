>>>>> "bob" == Bob Bottomley <Robert.A.Bottomley.Jr@cdc.com> writes:

bob> Yes, I got mine working on Friday on my UDB 166 with RH 4.2. I now
bob> have a PC Card modem and can read the SRAM cards from my Zoomer. Here
bob> is what I did:

bob> Get modules working. To do this, you need modutils-2.1.42. You can
bob> find this is the alphabits directory on RedHat. You will probably
bob> never get through to redhat.com and most of the mirror sites do not
bob> seem to mirror alphabits. So try this URL:

bob> ftp://ftp.varesearch.com/pub/mirrors/redhat/alphabits/

bob> Be sure to read the last part of the README about the define required
bob> for an alpha running binutils before 2.7.0.9 (which mine certainly
bob> was).

bob> I installed modutils and then recompiled the kernel with module
bob> support. Believe it or not -- it worked!

bob> I tested it out by configuring floppy support as a module.

You are certainly luckier than I am. I have a stock 4.2. I
uncommented AXP_BROKEN_GAS and compiled modutils-2.1.42. I patched my
kernel to 2.0.30 and applied Alpha-diff 2.0.30.

Top of the kernel config reads

CONFIG_MODULES=y
CONFIG_MODVERSIONS=y
CONFIG_KERNELD=y

Everything compiles OK and the kernel boots fine, but I got memory
violations with depmod. Hand inserting modules also failed.

I got most of the modules and PCMCIA 2.8.23 working under RH 4.0 with
2.0.27. I could mount (read-only) and read my Zoomer formated SRAM,
but popping it back to Zoomer yields "New card, do you want to format
it?" The same thing was happening with my wife's NEC Versa notebook
with much older PCMCIA packages. (DOS couldn't recogized the card at
all ...)
