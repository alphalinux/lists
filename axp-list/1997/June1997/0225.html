> I have seen a couple of posts about people not being able to remotely
> reboot or soft reboot their Linux/Alpha's. However I have not seen any
> responses posted. We are having the same problem. Our Alpha will take
> a 'reboot' or 'shutdown -r now' command and halt all processes and
> unmount drives, but it just hangs at the point where it says 'Milo
> rebooting...' This kind of hampers it usefulness as a remote server.
> If anyone has a fix for this or even a suggestion, please don't
> hesitate to pipe right up.

Sigh...

Yes, it's a known problem for which there's (currently) no solution.

It seems to depend on which platform you're running, as well as version of
MILO. You might experiment a bit with older/newer MILOs, see if one happens
to work for you.

We're working this problem as well as the kernel size problem (2.1.xx kernels
can be a lot bigger than a similarly configured 2.0.xx one). And it looks like
the fix for the latter *mauy* fix the former, as I've observer on a PC164.

We'll announce a new set of MILOs when we get something that looks stable.
