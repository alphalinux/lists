Hello,

During the past week I have unsuccessfully tried to do a network install
(ftp or nfs) of redhat 4.2 Alpha on my new PWS 433a. A mail reply by Jay
Estabrooks to my earlier post suggested to see if my machine answers to ping
requests while it is (seemingly) booting. That is indeed not the case i.e. the
network never actually gets started. In the meantime I have found out two
things that might explain this behaviour:

1) under WNT 4.0, the System Configuration display shows the Ethernet chip
to be a Digital 21143-PB, Rev. 48, embedded
(everywhere else its called a 21142)

2) the latest (alpha-)version of the tulip.c driver, v0.76, has a mod that says
it's meant to bring the 21143 "out of sleep mode" at boot time.
(the miata-kernel in /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/, which i have been using,
has v0.69 linked in)

So maybe the solution would be to get a boot kernel for the miata that has
version 0.76 or later of the tulip driver linked in. Would somebody able to
provide such a kernel, either in the above ftp area or otherwise ? Not having
another Alpha running linux I can't even try to make one myself.
