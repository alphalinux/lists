The method described for booting the AS255 worked for me as well. I
ddin't even had to get anything from gatekeeper, but I had to 'manually' write
the milo floppy (format FAT a:, then cp milo and linload.exe) then booted to
milo and then straight to CD.
I noticed that if something goes wong with booting to the CD, even if
you managed to get back to milo, you'll have to reset the AS255, otherwise
results are unpredictable.
However, befor attempting to install RHL41 onto a Alpha WS, check the
video hardware, in the case of the AS255, if you have a PowerStorm (decchip
21030, aka ZLXP-E2), you're stuck ... the TGA server works only with ZLXP-E1,
the default video board for Multia (aka UDB).
Then, you can develop the right piece of code, or go buy a supported
PCI video card for you AS255.
Good luck ...
