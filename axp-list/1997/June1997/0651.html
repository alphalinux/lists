As I recall, to use a PCMCIA modem under 4.1 (which I did), I had to make
sure that I had the modules and modutils rpms from the updates directory at
redhat. Secondly, I ended up needing to use pcmcia-cs-2.8.23 from
hyper.stanford.edu because later ones wouldn't make right with the kernel
headers, etal that were in RH4.1/2.0.27 kernel. Third, I remember needing to
do a 'strip -x *.o' on the pcmcia modules in lib/modules/2.0.27/pcmcia
before they would load without the infamous insmod bad length error. Oh, and
the Xircom and smc drivers fail at compile time even with 2.8.23 pcmcia set.
I took them out of my makefile since I dont use either one. I didn't try to
find an rpm for pcmcia under 4.1, I just got the sources from the source.

Note that with the stock initial rh4.2 install, insmod wouldn't work right
at all on the pcmcia modules. Replacing the binary with the one from 4.1
worked to solve the problem. I haven't checked the updates tree to see if
the bugaboo has been fixed in 4.2. Note that pcmcia-cs-2.9.5 will compile ok
under 4.2.

All this stuff should have been in the archives someplace, though. (Maybe
there should be a section in the rumored UDB FAQ for using the pc-card slots?)
