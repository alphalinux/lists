>>> HAIMO@wisconsin.cern.ch said:
>
> 1) under WNT 4.0, the System Configuration display shows the Ethernet chip
> to be a Digital 21143-PB, Rev. 48, embedded
> (everywhere else its called a 21142)

Yes, newer MIATAs are coming with a 21143 instead of the original 21142.
The original driver I used to get the 21142 support, tulip.c-069-expoC,
will *not* work with the 21143.

>
> 2) the latest (alpha-)version of the tulip.c driver, v0.76, has a mod that
> says it's meant to bring the 21143 "out of sleep mode" at boot time.
> (the miata-kernel in /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/, which i have been using,
> has v0.69 linked in)

Yes, tulip.c-076 *does* appear to support the 21143; you still need to do
the #define SKBUFF_RX_COPYBREAK 16384 patch to prevent unaligned traps, and
you may need to experiment with a command line option for the driver. Please
refer to my previous posts on this subject to axp-list for detailed info
(I think "ether=" should work as a search key).

There is a more recent MIATA kernel on gatekeeper that does have the newer
tulip driver; I think its called "miata-srm-myri-2030.gz" or some such, and
appeared just this early morning. PLease try that out and see if it works
better for you.
