> Am I missing anything obvious? I never saw a controller with soldered
> termination before,
> looks like a pretty bad design decission to me ?!

having slept and another look on the cards and cables I realize that it's
not that bad as I thought at first:

one NCR card (without PCI slot) only has one 0.5m cable connected
which is first passed to the interanal drive (connector in the middle
of the cable) and then led to the external connector.
here the terminator on the card really is at the end of the bus
and the only need is an external terminator for correct termination.
so, soldered terminator is no real problem and because the cable is
soldered on the card too it wouldn't be possible to replace it
with an active terminator anyway...

the other riser card (with PCI) has two cables connected.
one going to the external connector and 0.15m cable going the
internal disk (which doesn't use 0.1inch pin distance, so I can't use
the same disk for both controllers:(

I always thought about the external boxes about being a "stub" of
the terminated internal bus, which obviously was silly.
the real (hopefully terminated) bus is the external part,
and the internal disk is the "stub" conntected.
while SCSI-2 spec only allow 0.1m stubs, 0.15m might be ok
for a wide range of systems (esp. when using external active terminator
as Jay mentioned (which you should do anyway))

sorry for being that inaccurate. the UDB SCSI stuff doesn't look that bad
to me now that I've slept a bit... ;-)
