>>> Alex Schneider said:
>
> I was wondering whether any flavor of Linux is known to run (or known not
> to run) on the Alpha CPU cards sold by DEC's oem division. Part numbers
> are EBM21-* (21164 at 366 MHz) and EBM23-* (500 MHz). From the specs
> ( http://www.digital.com/info/oem/products/modcomp/mod21164.htm )
> they look a lot like the PC164 boards (selectable 128/256 bit main memory
> path, no SDRAM, IDE and floppy on board, etc.) but they're made to
> plug into a passive PCI/ISA backplane, one processor card per backplane.
> Any info (encouraging or not) would be appreciated.

Sigh...

I'm fairly certain that these CPU daughtercards *are* very similar to
some of our supported existing products, but to the best of my knowledge,
Linux/Alpha doesn't run on them.

I've talked with one of that group's engineers, and his estimation (based on
having done a test port of Linux/Alpha to one of the older 21064A-based
daughtercards) that it's only a few days work, given the appropriate specs
and, of course, access to the hardware... :-)

So, bottom line answer is, Linux/Alpha isn't currently supported on the
DMCC products, but it wouldn't take rocket science to do so.
