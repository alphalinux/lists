Hello Ken

>Something on my alpha have been puzzeling me for a while. I have 128 Mb
>RAM on the machine (PC164, 500 MHz, SCSI), but when booting it only finds
>126 Mb. I have been trying to issue: setenv MEMORY_SIZE 128 in MILO, but
>this doesen't seem to help. Forthermore the machine crashes when I try to
>run larger programs. Kernel: 2.0.28, but the problem also exists with
>other kernels.

1)try running on 1/2 your memory, providing you can. Pull out 4 of the
simms and set the jumper above the ram for 128-bit. May be a bad simm.

2)see if you running the latest and greatest ARC 4.49-7. If not get it.

3)check the Srom U38 should say 23B3.

4)What rev milo are you running? We've found that 2.0.30 is the most stable
kernel and milo to date.

Hope this helps.
