In article <33AD59DF.8FC05590@net-arts.com> you write:
>I own a 233mhz Multia I bought for about $800 new. I added 64 MB of RAM,
>bringing the total to 88, added an external SCSI chasis with a 3GB drive
>and 8X CDROM. Total cost including computer and RedHat disk: $1800. I
>don't kow why this neat little box has been so maligned.

You may try to overclock it with the three jumpers "cpu speed" on the
motherboard. My 233 UDB runs at 300 MHz without a glitch. It seems that
using the support to put it vertical helps in getting rid of the heat.

These are truly good machines, but it is too bad the memory bus is only
at 33 MHz; this is rather limitative with some applications (for instance
Quake).

I do not know why DEC stopped the production of such boxes.
