> cdwrite, Xcdroast, and TCL/TK all seem to have
> problems on the Alpha. They compile but screw up when run. I would guess a
> sizeof(long) problem, as that seems to be everyone's favorite Alpha bug.

This is not "an Alpha bug", this is simply a nasty bug in an orignal
code. I am way too familiar with this from old days of hacking on
Atari ST (where a lot of Unix programs was ported, gcc including,
but where sizeof(int) was not necessarily equal sizeof(pointer) as
well). Sometimes they are easy to fix and sometimes a code writer
knew too well that "all types are four bytes wide". Big sigh!

I do not know anything about cdwrite or Xcdroast, but I can attest
that tcl is indeed full of such bugs.
