At 07:01 PM 6/13/97 GMT, you wrote:
>
>I have booting problems with my new PC164 500Mhz:
>ALmost every time when I power up or push the reset button, the PC164 just
>hangs with all the keyboard lights on! If it boots correctly, then it will
>work.
>
>I have this card:
>- DE450 ethernet
>
>I tried these in different combinations:
>- NCR53C810 card
>- Buslogic BT-956C (not recognize by MILO 2.0.30!)
>- Adaptec AHA-2940 Rev B with latest bios from Adaptec's FTP.
>- Miro Ergo 40SV (S3 968) bios 1.04
>- Matrox Millennium Bios 2.3 (should I try 2.5??)
>- #9 GXE64 Trio Bios 1.02.01
>
>I am in contact with the firm who sold me the machine about this but I would
>like to have a list of video cards and SCSI controllers (Ultra-Wide also) that
>are known to work with a DE450 card in a PC164.

All of those cards have working drivers under Linux/Alpha, AFAIK.

The BusLogic I have no personal experience with; people have been able
to boot from them using MILO, but note that you'll not be able to boot
MILO (from ARC or AlphaBIOS) nor a kernel directly from SRM, using a
BusLogic as your primary SCSI, since those consoles will (probably)
not ever support any BusLogic cards. And I don't recommend FLASHing MILO,
as a general rule.

The 2940UW is known to work under Linux/Alpha, but note that only ARC
and AlphaBIOS know about Adaptecs, not SRM.

I don't believe that the DE450 is your problem, unless, when you remove it,
things start to work, of course... :-)

Have you tried re-seating all the SIMMs and socketed ROMS, etc, that are
on the motherboard? Sometimes these things get loosened up in shipping.

Other than that, I think its a hardware problem with the motherboard... :-(
