>
> I have a situation where someone is tieing up all my network sockets on
> port 110.

This is, normally, a POP server. Are you running pop services?
If not, then shut it down in your /etc/inetd.conf. If you do supply
this service, and your popd is healthy, then this may be a buggy
client. In the latter case you can try to hack your daemon to
open sockets with SO_LINGER and/or enforce timeouts on these sockets.
This assuming that there are no problems in your libraries.
Forcing a client to behave is not very likely, especially if it is
of some "commercial" nature.
Sorry, I do not have more details.
