On Fri, 27 Jun 1997, Nils Faerber wrote:

> OK,
> since I do not know if this is publically known and since some other
> reported problems with the X11 MIT Shared Memory extensions and I had those
> problems too, I just wanted to report that it is now working!
> When applying the axp-diffs-2.0.30 to the vanilla 2.0.30 kernel everything
> works how it should.
> But why are these changes not yet integrated into the 2.0.30 mainstream?
> I had the SHM problem for at least the last 20 releases, because I never
> applied the axp-diffs. I remember someone saying that those diffs would only
> be needed for debugging the kernel with a remote gdb. Obviously this was not
> fully correct.

You are correct that the "for debugging only" is not true. Red Hat
applies a modified version the the axp-diffs and that modification breaks
the SHM extensions. This was just discovered reciently, and we have not
heard from Red Hat yet as to what will be done. They were trying to keep
the header files closer to the way Linus wrote them in the 2.0.x series;
but whatever they have done causes a problem.

The 2.1.13+ kernels do not need axp-diffs. So whenever the 2.1.x series
becomes the next release level we should be fine. I'm currently trying to
get a set of patches that includes the full axp-diffs and the parts of the
new pre-patch-2.0.31 patches that Red Hat includes in 2.0.30-3 so we can
have SHM and the new .31 stuff. When it works I'll post them.

Hope this helped.

-Rick
