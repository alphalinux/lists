Hi everybody,

So far, pledges for $1033, a 1GB drive, and other hardware valued at
~$200 have come in, but I'd say we should be able to get at least
$1300 to reach the goal of the $1500 minimum---keep in mind that Craig
will pitch in about as much money to get a real system, so it's not
like he'll get rich or anything...

Yesterday, my mailbox remained very quiet, so I'd like to ask you
again to consider contributing to this good cause. Remember, every
donation counts!

Also, I realize a lot of people care more for an optimized gcc than
g77. But keep in mind that two things have to come together: before
being able to help somebody with donations of any kind, there needs to
be somebody that is willing to do the work. For g77 this has
happened: Craig is interested in making g77 work well on Linux/Alpha
and with very little effort on our side, we can make sure that he'll
be able to continue to work towards that goal. Besides, since g77
uses the same backend as gcc, it's quite likely that some of his work
will positively affect the gcc backend as well (and that's where all
the Alpha-specific optimizations happen). Also, keep in mind that
we're not paying him in for what he's doing ($1500 for a software
craftsperson of his caliber would probably buy us, oh, let's say,
about 10 hours of programming time, i.e., not exactly a lot...).
Instead helping him get a nice machine is simply a show of
appreciation for what he's done so far.

Well, this mail is getting longer than I wanted it to make, but please
consider making a donation.

Oh, not everybody seemed to like the idea of putting together a web
page with info on the donation. If that's a concern, it's certainly
fine by me to not do that (makes for less work on my side... ;-)

--david
