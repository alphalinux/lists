unmoderated group comp.os.linux.alpha

comp.os.linux.alpha Linux on Digital Alpha machines.

about the proposed group should be directed to the proponent.

RATIONALE: comp.os.linux.alpha

Linux running on Digital Alpha processors is quickly gaining
popularity and is being discussed actively in a number of forums.
This list currently includes a mailing list run by Red Hat Software
and various newsgroup such as comp.sys.dec or
comp.os.linux.sys.development. The amount of interest in this topic
can be demonstrated easily by considering recent traffic statistics
for the Red Hat operated mailing list. For example, in the 22 days
between April 1-22, there were over 950 mails sent to the
axp-list@redhat.com mailing list. Those mails were originated by over
200 different persons. The traffic volume on Usenet is harder to
estimate since there is no specific group on this topic yet, but a
increasing portion of comp.sys.dec traffic is now related to Linux on
Alpha.

The creation of this newsgroup would provide a focused and
vendor-neutral place for discussing issues related to Linux on Alpha.
Since a newsgroup is more easily managed than a high-volume mailing
list, such a group would allow the casual Linux/Alpha user to touch
base and keep track of developments without being inundated by a
flood of email messages (or having to set up email filters).

CHARTER: comp.os.linux.alpha

The comp.os.linux.alpha newsgroup should be used for all discussions
relating to buying, installing, running, maintaining, and developing
Linux on Digital Alpha processor based systems.

The posting of commercial information to this group is permitted so
long as the information is directly of interest to Linux on Alpha
users and provided the postings are kept within reason (no frequent
reposts, exaggerated or misleading claims, etc).

END CHARTER.

IMPORTANT VOTING PROCEDURE NOTES: READ THIS BEFORE VOTING

Standard Guidelines for voting apply. One person, one vote. Votes
must be mailed directly from the voter to the votetaker. Anonymous,
forwarded or proxy votes are not valid. Votes mailed by WWW/HTML/CGI
forms are considered to be anonymous votes.

Vote counting is automated. Failure to follow these directions may
mean that your vote does not get counted. If you do not receive an
acknowledgment of your vote within three days contact the votetaker
about the problem. It's your responsibility to make sure your vote
is registered correctly. Duplicate votes are resolved in favor of
the most recent valid vote. Addresses and votes of all voters will
be published in the final voting results post.

The purpose of a Usenet vote is to determine the genuine interest of
persons who would read a proposed newsgroup. Soliciting votes from
uninterested parties defeats this purpose. Please do not distribute
this CFV. If you must, direct people to the official CFV as posted
to news.announce.newgroups. Distributing pre-marked or otherwise
edited copies of this CFV is generally considered to be vote fraud.
When in doubt, ask the votetaker.
