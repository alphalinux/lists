> Well, I compiled 2.1.42 and modutils-2.1.23 on my UDB. I had to make just
> about everything I could make a module in the kernel a module in order to
> get it to boot. Now, I have another problem.. The kernel boots, kerneld
> starts up, but anytime kerneld tries to launch insmod, I get a memory
> violation in insmod. And yes, I did 'strip -x' on the modules (not doing
> it caused an oops).
>
> Is there a functioning copy of insmod out there anywhere? It's

When this happened to me it was due to the old binutils of Red Hat, not
insmod from modutils-2.1.23. When I upgraded insmod, modules started to
work for me. (After recompilation, naturally)

I have an rpm for the version I installed to make things work. Now, there
are certainly newer versions of binutils, but this one works for me.

http://wopr.campus.luth.se/pub/redhat/RPMS/alpha/binutils-970127-1.alpha.rpm
