Gene Van Sant wrote:
> Has anybody solved this problem?

I haven't solved this problem, but for what it's worth, the solution
does not require you to remove any RAM. Simply

setenv MEMORY_SIZE 64

And all will be happy. I do experience other strange (but probably
explainable) phenomena which are probably not related to this but
nevertheless worth pointing out. MILO goes haywire if I have the
modem turned on (I presume this may be related to the serial console
capabilities of MILO). There also seems to be a problem that when
loading into MILO (with the modem turned off) I get a neverending
number of line-feeds until I press return. This phenomenon repeats
itself after the kernel bootup sequence, until I press return again.
I never had this problem in Digital Unix or NT, so I'm hard-pressed to
dismiss the keyboard (DEC LK411-AA) as faulty.

Hope this is useful info for someone.
