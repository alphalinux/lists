Harald Koenig <koenig@tat.physik.uni-tuebingen.de> wrote:
> looking closely at this riser card, I realize that both the plain NCR
> controller as well as this riser card with additional PCI slot have a
> soldered passive termination resitor pack (yellow DIP 220/330 chip) which
> is directly connected with the SCSI connector, so there is no way to
> disable termination if I want to use both internal and external devices.
>
> Am I missing anything obvious? I never saw a controller with soldered
> termination before,
> looks like a pretty bad design decission to me ?!

I'd have to agree that it's a bad design. They don't terminate the internal
drive, believing that its stub length is short enough to be within spec.
However, in trying to debug my UDB SCSI troubles, I've looked at the signals
on an oscilliscope, and they don't look great.

Adding an external active terminator helps.

I'm thinking about just disconnecting the internal drive, since 540M isn't
enough to do anything useful.
