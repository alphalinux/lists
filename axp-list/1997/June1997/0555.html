OK, you all probably have seen Craig Burley's recent mail announcing
that he has to return his loaned Cabriolet and trade it for a UDB.
The UDB may be a fine machine for certain jobs, but for heavy-duty
work such as g77 development, it surely isn't the right stuff.

Thus, I'd like to start a little fund raiser with the goal of putting
a nice Alpha in Craig's office. This would help ensure that g77 on
Alpha will not just work, but that there is a chance that nice Alpha
optimizations will show up some day. Besides, I really do think Craig
deserves a nice machine that's fun to work with, given all the hard
and good work he put in g77.

To start things, I promise to send a check over $100 to Craig for the
purpose of purchasing an Alpha machine that will ensure the continued
support and development of g77 on Linux/Alpha. The only condition is
that we (the Linux/Alpha community) must be able raise a total of at
least $1500 for me to send in that check. If the echo is so big that
more than $3k are raised, I would propose to divide up the per-head
share so that Craig ends up with a total of $3k (which should be
enough to buy one of the forthcoming 21164pc machines).

Note: I discussed this with Craig a bit and he says that he is not
desperate for machines, but he would definitely _love_ to have a
decent Alpha that he can use as his primary machine. To show he's
serious about this, he offered to pay half the money for a new
machine. Thus, even if we raise only $1500, he should still be able
to get a nice machine.

So, if you, too, think that Craig deserves a decent machine, please
send me mail and tell me how much you're willing to contribute to that
end.

Can we make this happen?

--david
