Richard Henderson wrote:
> A Stab in the Dark -- IPX. It is by nature unaligned and
> fixing it would slow down IP. So your only recourse is to
> edit arch/alpha/kernel/traps.c and turn off the message.

Well, depending on how often the "unaligned trap" message appears,
it may or may not be better to fix the unaligned accesses. Taking
the trap is *extremely* expensive (that's why the message is there;
except for very infrequent cases like fdisk, it almost always pays
to eliminate the unaligned condition).

If the trap occurs once per packet, you *definitely* want to fix it.
