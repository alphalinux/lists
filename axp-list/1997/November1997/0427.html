I have a pair of Multia's I am playing with for a friend, and
one of them has 128MB of ram and absolutely will not make
it through a redhat 4.2 installation without lockup UNLESS
I setenv MEMORY_SIZE 120M. Then it seems to work almost perfect,
compiling the kernel almost always with no errors (and that is with
MAKE=make -j in the makefile! I just looked and it even gets into
swap a little bit, when the load ave was 22)

One time it failed compiling the kernel with a signal 10, IIRC.
Could this be the 'lca: machine check ... Reason: correctable ECC error'
I see in the syslog file? The time looks like it might.

Someone said that I didn't have to do the MEMORY_SIZE thing.
Well I do unless there is something else different? Milo?

My simple benchark against a P133 shows the multia 1/2 as
fast [doing a povray job, lots of floating point].
I thought the Alpha was supposed to be fast at floating point?
Is there a tweak to the povray 3.? code that makes fewer divides
or something?
