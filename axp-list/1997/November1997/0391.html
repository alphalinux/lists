On 15/11/97 at 04:05 PM, Arthur Twarecki <atwarec@zeus.leitch.com> said:
>I don't see any resistors on the SCSI (riser) card in my UDB. Does this
>mean there is no temrmination on the card, or perhaps the 53c8xx SCSI
>controller does termination internally??

As far as I can tell the card is not terminated. It expects the internal
drive (if any) to be terminated, an a terminator to be placed on the
external port. As I don't use an internal drive in my UDB, I've found
that I can get away with terminating only the end of the external chain as
long as I use extremely good quality cables to the external drives (teflon
shielded and all that crap). If I used el cheapo cables from the local
box shifters I started get SCSI resets and arbitration hangs.
