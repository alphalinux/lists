On Wed, 12 Nov 1997, Erik Spaan wrote:

> I did not mean an AT keyboard with 84 keys. I have a normal keyboard with 101
> keys or somewhat like that. Only the connector is a DIN plug, also known as
> an AT style connector?

All the so called extended keyboards (F1 - F12, etc.) that are being
attached to PCs are PS/2 keyboards. PCs use them in AT-compatible mode,
usually, not in the native mode, like DU. The style of the plug is
unimportant; you may always use a converter.

> Other people have told me that this ought to work.

Certainly, what the original writer meant when writing AT keyboards are
incompatible, were supposedly the real AT, 84-key ones.
