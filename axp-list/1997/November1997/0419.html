On Sun, Nov 16, 1997 at 06:58:25PM +0100, Martin Høy wrote:

> I've just got my hands on a Digital Personal Workstation 433a (...)

> clock: unable to get I/O port access
> xf86EnableIOPorts: Failed to set IOPL for I/O

IIRC, a

ln -s Alcor /etc/alpha_systype

should do the trick. Alcor doesn't have to be an existing file, it's just
the name of the symlink that counts. The explanation is that the alpha libc
uses this (a bit unconventional) trick to find out about the system's
architecture if it can't find out by reading /proc/cpuinfo, and that the
Alcor architecture is obviously close enough to the Miata architecture to
make everything work.
