> Sorry, it's a CMD 646, not the (broken) 640, but also, unfortunately, it isn't
> compatible. I asked a long time ago, of the author of the IDE driver, what the
> chances were for a 646 driver, and none was planned, IIRC... :-(
>
> Two things contribute to the continuing lack of decent IDE driver for Alphas:
>
> 1. the only IDE controller chips DEC has used have been either the CMD 646, or
> whatever is included in the SuperIO chips (Intel mostly, but SMC in some)
> that are on the mobos. And apparently, CMD 646 isn't as popular on the
> INTEL mobos as others.
>
> 2. the migration of Alphas from high-end workstations to low-end PC-equivalent
> has caused the SCSI to be brought along and IDE to lag in adoption.
>
> Has anyone thought about looking into one of the *BSD's to see if *they* have
> an appropriate driver for the 646? We've borrowed their SCSI drivers, why not
> IDE as well?
>
> Or perhaps ask CMD themselves, maybe they'd contribute driver source for one
> they did for SCO, or Solaris, or whatever?
