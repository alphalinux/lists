Lev Tarasoff <lev@datoo.ggy.uga.edu> wrote:
.
.
> 1) What experience have people had with : Microway, Aspen, and DCG?
> Are there any other companies that you would recommend that
> actively support Linux?

I have a Microway 533 MHz Screamer and another guy has an older Aspen
Alpine with a 275 MHz processor. Microway has been quite supportive
when I've called them about the system. The Aspen has DEC UNIX and is
a couple of years old. It hasn't been used so much.

> 3)Are commercial fortran 77 packages for Linux worth the extra expense
> as compared to f2c and gcc? If so, which do you recommend? Some past
> posts indicate that f2c is broken on Alpha Linux. Is this true? As the
> primary usage of the workstation will be to compile and run F77 code,
> I need to know that there is a stable and efficient fortran compiler
> for the Alpha.

I got my system with Microway's NDP FORTRAN compiler. It sucks. I got
their 32 bit compiler first, this had a lot of problems and they sent me their
64 bit release. This fixed some problems, but other things did not work.
The documentation lists a lot of options that either do not work or have
been removed. The debugging also either does not work or is bad. The
compiler does not support conversion of big-endian binary data nor the
pointer statement. The code it generates always ran significantly slower
than code generated on DEC's FORTRAN -- I also have a 500MHz Alphastation,
which I compared against. I did not try f2c/gcc because of the problems I
heard about it. I am converting my system to DEC UNIX + DEC FORTRAN. I
also know that NAG has FORTRAN for the alpha, but I have not heard anything
about its performance.

> 4)How stable is Redhat 4.2 for the Alpha? Or should I wait till 5.0 is
> out? And when will that be?

I have had some very annoying problems with Linux on the Alpha and find it
rather buggy -- such as programs bombing with the complaint "Cant get a free
page". We use NIS and this does not work well with the Alpha -- I had to
turn it off. I believe things will get better with the new releases, but
the machine needs to produce, hence the move to DEC UNIX.

DEC has bought Microsoft's FORTRAN compiler and has it for the Alpha.
Microway has said to me that NT with DEC's compiler runs very well, but
they also claim their compiler is good. I don't know what the DEC
compiler costs, but it might be a good solution for a single user. DEC
UNIX licensing and DEC FORTRAN licensing are damned expensive.

I like a lot about Linux, but because of the compiler situation and the
other problems with the Redhat 4.2 on alpha, we have to switch to the
commercial UNIX, so the workstation will cost twice as much, but still
much cheaper than buying a DEC Alphastation.

> Any feedback would be much appreciated.

> Thanks,

> Lev Tarasoff
