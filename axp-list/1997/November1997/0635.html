>
> 1) what are the optimal denisty/block settings for a SCSI DAT drive?

Probably 0 for a blocksize. DATs are usually variable block and this
means that.

>
> tar -xvf /dev/tape

You /dev/tape is a likely a link to /dev/st0, which means "rewind
after done". Use 'tar -xvpf /dev/nst0' (note 'p' and 'n') or make
yourself a link like /dev/ntape, for example, for a non-rewinding tape.
Likely you will have a gap between different tar archives, so
after 'tar -xvpf /dev/nst0' you will have to do that once again,
on an "empty" archive, before the next one will show up.
