On Fri, 7 Nov 1997, Stefan Boresch wrote:

> Just a comment on Alpha Linux / IDE boxes
>
> I have a pc164 based box (500Mhz 21164) with an IDE subsystem only.
> It's solid, but the disk performance is abysmal. So far, I haven't
> found a way to improve that -- so my question to the companies now
> touting low-cost IDE systems is: Any suggestions for me how to
> improve my disk throughput? (My exact system configuration is
> described in several questions from me to this list, i.e. it's
> several times in the archive)
>
> I do not want to restart the IDE vs. SCSI war. The point is that
> good (E)IDE systems on Intel boxes may rival all but the highest
> end (= quite expensive) SCSI systems. My experience is that this
> is not the case for Alpha boxes (mine is a pc164, but there were
> also complaints for the LX164).

I don't understand the motivation to buy such a high end box and then
cripple it with IDE. Perhaps I am perpetuating the IDE/SCSI war a bit
with this, but IDE is *NOT* the way to go for a multi-user system. Don't
get me wrong, other than my servers I have all IDE drives in my computers
and they work wonderfully. The gap in SCSI and IDE performance on
comparable drives isn't much on a single user machine, but it widens
considerably on a multi user server.

If cost is the issue, you can buy NCR PCI SCSI-II controllers for US $50
(perhaps not even needed if you have an on-board controller), and
refurbished/discontinued 2GB 7200 rpm drives for about US $200 to $250
each. Granted, this is slightly more than an IDE setup of comparable size,
but the advantages you would gain would *FAR* outweigh the price
differences.
