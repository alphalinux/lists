Please forgive me a little bit of a rant:

I would personally appreciate it if Red Hat (and for that matter the other
venders) would deliberately make and emphasize the difference between
Linux the operating system developed cooperatively over the Internet and
Linux the operating system packaged by the fine folks at Red Hat in their
promotional literature. I have never found a problem with the way Red Hat
acts - given that many items like rpm are placed in the fund of free
software, just the marketing text. Linux is not a product of, nor the
creation of Red Hat - but the distribution is a product of Red Hat, and
one they can take a great deal of pride in, IMHO.
