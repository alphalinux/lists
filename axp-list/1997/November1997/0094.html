On Wed, 5 Nov 1997, Steven Kokinos wrote:

> >2- Is the NFS the best way for me to have the Unix HD mounted by the NT
> >server. Better yet, What alternatives do I have to meet the task?
>
> This is probably the simplest way to do it. There is also a product
> called Zannet (http://www.zannet.com I think) that allows you to map a
> unix volume to a logical drive on your unix system, similar to the way
> you mount a netware volume. This might work better for you, but since
> your log files are presumably large, you'll need to do testing as to
> speed. For instance, NFS reads are generally fast, but writes are slow,
> so depending on what you are doing you could run into bottlenecks.

Samba is another alternative. But if you use it, be sure to get a new
version, the old ones have a very serious security flaw.
