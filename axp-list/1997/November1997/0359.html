dave,

the 'kernel-compile' benchmark does say little or nothing about how well a
systems or cpu performs.
generally it can be said that cisc code is faster to comile than risc code
(which is an *advantage* of the risc architecture, not a drawback!).

besides that to many other parameters are taken into account like how
many jobs does make launch (make -j 20 boot != make boot) how well does
the kernel work, how fast is the compiler itself etc etc etc

a good over-all benchmark is the spec95 although the intel cpus do look
better at the spec bench than they are in the real word (intel has its own
spec-benchmark-specialist compilers whereas sun, digital and hp have to
compile the spec-bench with them normal 'everydays-work' compilers. also
in real live fast movement and copy of data (slow on x86) is more
important than pure arithmetics (fast on x86).

another good bench is bytemark.
for infos about the benchmark look at www.byte.com. you can also download
the sources there....

my system is an alpha 21164A at 500mHz, PC164 motherboard 128MB
ram(memory bus 'only' 128bit(leaving one memory bank free)) , 1MB
L3 cache. i run the precompiled windows NT bytemark from www.byte.com.

it gives me 7.X ints and 8.X floats, pentium 90 is 1 int and 1 float.
this is fast. faster than SMP x86.

but......
....dont expect linux to run that fast as the benchmarks might promise.
gcc does not produce well optimised alpha code, and the linux system is
not as optimised as the older intel port. and ofcause disk-performance is
very important on how fast an OS seems to run.
