"Maurice W. Hilarius" <harddata@bigfoot.com> wrote:
> On the UDB's I have
> worked on the SCSI is on a small riser card between the internal H.D. and
> the external port. The terminator is a small yellow colored surface-mount
> package on the back end of the riser board. removing it is a pretty
> permanent thing. You now are left with some open circuit connections which
> would have to be jumpered ( the ground lines for the bus). Then you will
> have a "legal" non-terminated end to the bus.

Are you certain that anything would have to be jumpered? I've dealt with
SCSI circuitry quite a bit, and I can't imagine how DEC could have
(mis)wired it such that the resistor pack can't simply be desoldered,
removed, and discarded. There shouldn't be any need for jumpers, because
the ground lines for the bus don't pass through the terminator (though
the PC ground, the SCSI ground lines, and the terminator ground are
certainly all tied together).
