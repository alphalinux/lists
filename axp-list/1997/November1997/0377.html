On Sat, 15 Nov 1997, Armin Ollig wrote:

> the 'kernel-compile' benchmark does say little or nothing about how well a
> systems or cpu performs.

Agreed.

> generally it can be said that cisc code is faster to comile than risc code
> (which is an *advantage* of the risc architecture, not a drawback!).

Why? Being slower to compile surely seems like a drawback to me. Usually
it doesn't matter but why should it be an advantage?

> another good bench is bytemark.

IMHO it's pretty bad und can't be really trusted. Last time I tried to
build it on Linux/Alpha I ran into a bug which caused core-dumps due to
reading uninitialized floating-point-data. Trying to build it on another
system resulted in lots of problems due to inconsistent declarations and
use of library-functions (clock) without error-checking.
Also the dynamic scaling basically prevents me from measuring/checking the
time with a clock.

And a benchmark claimed to be portable shouldn't use #ifdef SOMEOS all
over the place...
