Not that I want to drag out this thread out another week, but I tried to
throw RedHat a bone a couple weeks ago by ordering this RHmember
subscription and one of everything they sold (~$900). Turns out they
wanted a lot from my company before I was cleared to use P.O.'s, including
provide them with 3 P.O. references and a Nerf Ballzooka. :)

I was hoping that it would be easier considering I don't work for a
unknown. Mitsubishi Electronics has ~400 people in the area. I am in a
small engineering design center which only issues P.O.'s to 1 or 2 places.

Our 1997 budget for PC to Solaris connectivity software was $2000. Well
after a $40 RedHat 4.2 CD we have all our engineers on intel/Linux boxes
that work better for us than any other solution regardless of price.

We have automatic P.O.'s set up at a local place (plug for Stay-on-Line in
rtp N.C. just a few miles from RedHat's North American headquarters). So I
just picked up some applixware from them. They don't have a full selection
of RedHat toys, yet. But it would be nicer to give RedHat a bigger slice
of the pie.

RHmember subscriptions don't make sense in most cases. But it would be a
nice way for my company to give RedHat a little payback for providing us
with so much value.
