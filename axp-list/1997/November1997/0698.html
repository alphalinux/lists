On Mon, 24 Nov 1997, Maurice W. Hilarius wrote:

I wrote:
> >on hold with tech support. Their spec sheets say 8025 cylendars, 16
> >heads, and 171 sectors. However, fdisk only allows me to have a maximum
> >of 63 sectors. :(

> Multiply 8025x16x171 to get: 21956400
> Divide this by 64 to get:343068
> Divide this by 32 to get: 10720
> Guess where you can put those numbers?

Something on my UDB was being unruly. I eventually ended up dividing the
number of sectors by three and multiplying the number of heads by three.
This sorta worked, though it left me with 1000+ unused clusters (sectors?)
After rebooting a couple times, resetting the drive, something, it
eventually 'fixed' itself into a configuration that was invalid, and that
I couldn't edit with the fdisk 'x' command. Hmm. Couple more resets,
couple more reboots, switch between ARC and SRM, something, viola! It was
then able to read the SCSI drive properly. The numbers it found did not
coincide with the spec sheet, but it did partition nicely for the first
time, and I ended up with only 137 unused clusters. Every other attempt
ended up with overlapping partitions and other wierd stuff. It actually
mke2fs'd without destroying other partition info! :)

My other problem with the UDB re: the ECC errors after compiling a new
kernel; I shut the machine off and physically powered it back on, and they
went away. Hmm. I've let it sit over the weekend doing nothing and will
see how it is this afternoon. If it looks okay, it'll become a
masquerading firewall (we generate a lot of traffic locally between four
computers, and are annoying the nice people who give us T1 access for $20
per IP address, so we're going to cut it down) and a web server.
Hopefully all will be well, and it'll become a mail server, replacing our
Sparc 5. At this point, I'll attest to the viability of using this
machine as an ISP. =)

I'd also like to publicly thank those who wrote me privately with
suggestions. Finicky little beasts, aren't they?

BTW: It still tells me I only have 32MB even though I have 4 16MB SIMMs.
