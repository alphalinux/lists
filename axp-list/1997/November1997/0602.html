R J Hill-Cottingham wrote:

> > Does it [ Redhat 5.0 ] include g77?

And Erik Troan replied:

> No, because it changes gcc's backend and isn't well
> tested. People have found bugs in gcc which resulted frmo
> g77, and I'm not going to break the widely used C compiler
> for a seldom-used fortran compiler.

This is a sound reason, and basically the same decision that led
Debian onto the path of using g77-0.5.19[.1] (in stead of the - then
curent - g77-0.5.20) in their 1.3.x distributions. However, the
situation is harder for Redhat, because (obviously) they want to
have the same compiler suite for all three (four ?) architectures
they support, and g77-0.5.19 definitely didn't work on Alpha's.

Although g77-0.5.21 (9/9/97) was designed to solve all these
problems, the egcs project (see below) already managed to find
several errors in (patched) backend routines that we - the g77-alpha
group - didn't find in the 6 months leading up to 0.5.21.

> The good news is the egcs group, which is working on
> extending gcc in various ways, incorporates g77 in their
> standard release. Once egcs is released (it's in alpha
> test right now), both egcs and egcs's fortran support
> should end up in the standard Red Hat release.

It really would do good to have a `standard', multi-architecture
Linux distribution to point at when explaining the workings of g77
(often "bug" reports that arrive at g77-bug aren't really bugs, but
things that happen to trigger some behaviour on one architecture,
but not on others - I'm thinking of the ix86'ism -malign-double for
DOUBLE PRECISION variables, for instance).

BTW, for those of you who don't know (and are afraid to ask), the
egcs project home page is at http://www.cygnus.com/egcs.
