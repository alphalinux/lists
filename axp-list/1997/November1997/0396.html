Hello,

I posted recently about my waxing interest in Linux/AXP, and that
I am looking for a cheaper, entry-level system. Can anyone give me a
rough estimate of the expectable performance of a said system, assuming
32M of memory and discounting disk I/O? I know it's apples to oranges,
but, a ball-park equivalent of an x86 P rating is what I'll understand
best, I guess (or Byte UNIX benchmark results, since I have numbers for
various x86 systems).
I've seen the DEC Multia 166Mhz systems being auctioned off on
www.onsale.com. I'd appreciate any more good advice or honest opinions
one whether these things are any good. The site doesn't have much info on
the system (like whether IDE is available, whether or not the CPU is
upgradable, how good the power supply is, etc).
It's humbling trying to break into a new architecture. After
acquiring all sorts of knowledge about Linux/x86, I get to feel like a
newbie all over again. :)

Thanks for any input (and thanks to the kind folks who've already
