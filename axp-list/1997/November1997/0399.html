ok, here is what i have done, and personally reccomend as a good cheap
way to 'break-in' to the architecture...

i purchased an axppci33 board (similar to the board in udb's) with a
166mhz 21066 cpu and installed 256k of 15 ns cache.

its a nice board, it has on-board NCR810 scsi-ii controler and
a crappy (crappy imo) ide controler, so stuff that you would need
to buy would be:

board+cpu 166mhz axppci33 $150

cache 256k 15ns $30

ram two 32 meg true parity simms $220 ($110 for each 32 meg simm)
(8x36 60ns or 70ns)

video supported video card (see
http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux)
*I would reccomend a 4 meg pci
matrox millenium, or even
a millenium 2 if they are well
enough supported by now
*another good card would be a
a 2 or 4 meg S3 based pci card $60 (for the S3)

ether NE2000 clone (pci or isa) or $65
a 21040 (i think that this is the
right number) based card like SMC
Etherpower cards are nice

hard disks 9 gig ultra wide 8ms 7200 rpm $200 (for my 2 gig, i got a good
deal)
just kidding ;)
im using a fast/narrow 2 gig
disk, 5400 rpm, 10ms, works
great, remember linux/axp binaries
will be a little larger in general
than linux/x86 binaries

floppy drive what can i say? $25

case / your choice, my board is baby/AT $70
power supply style

+___
you do the math

from this example you can build yourself a cheap little box and learn
some neat stuff in the process, as for finding the actualy board+cpu
mail me privately...

this is what i would recommend for someone who doesnt want to / cant
spend a lot of money and who wants a box to learn on

as for other systems based on 21064, 21164, or 21164pc, im really not the
guy to ask ;)

also if you have any trouble finding hardware at a price similar to what
i listed here, let me know and i can try to help you out.
