I'm starting a research position at UGA (Athens, Georgia) in January, and
I've been given $5000 in startup funds for purchasing a workstation to
carry out numerical modelling experiments (climate/ice sheet models,
basically non-linear advection-diffusion equations, in other words heavy
floating point and data flow). I would appreciate any recommendations on
how to put together a high-performance workstation on this budget.
Specifically:

1) What experience have people had with : Microway, Aspen, and DCG?
Are there any other companies that you would recommend that
actively support Linux?

2)From posts of the last few days I've come to understand that IDE dma
modes aren't supported on the Alpha chip so that I will likely need
to use a SCSI drive. But would I still be farther ahead going with
IDE and using the money saved to purchase more RAM? (The expandibility
advantage of SCSI's is not important to me, as future expansion would
most likely come in the form of second workstation (assuming I
make it at the grant game)).

3)Are commercial fortran 77 packages for Linux worth the extra expense
as compared to f2c and gcc? If so, which do you recommend? Some past
posts indicate that f2c is broken on Alpha Linux. Is this true? As the
primary usage of the workstation will be to compile and run F77 code,
I need to know that there is a stable and efficient fortran compiler
for the Alpha.

4)How stable is Redhat 4.2 for the Alpha? Or should I wait till 5.0 is
out? And when will that be?

4.5)My remaining questions about stability have just been posted
by someone else. Please pass any individual responses to other
posts concerning STABILITY to me as well.

5) What is the difference between full old-fashioned Workstations, and
these much cheaper Alpha pc workstations? For my budget, is this the best
way to go to get floating point performance?

6) Any comments on my second draft configuration
(eg SCSI controller to use/avoid, Power supply size,...):

* 533 MHz 21164(a) Alpha processor
* Mid-Size Tower & 300W PS
* 2 MB static CACHE
* 128 MB SDRAM Upgradable to 256 MB
* Ultra wide SCSI controller
* 4.3 GB SCSI Hard Drive
* 8X CD-ROM
* 3.5" 1.44 MB Floppy
* 2 Serial, 1 Parallel Port
* 10/100 Mb/s Ethernet card
* 101 Enhanced Keyboard
* Logitech 3-Button Mouse
* LINUX (4.2) installed
*17" monitor
*UPS (A power failure at home that crashed a partition on my Pentium
133 running Linux has taught me the value of having a USP for Linux
systems)

Any feedback would be much appreciated.

Thanks,
