I've got my SRM loaded and am bootp'ing my PC164LX 533Mhz box perfectly
now. Thanks, Jay.

I created a diskette with just LX164SRM.ROM on it, and the first time I
tried to update AlphaBIOS firmware it worked. Subsequent times didn't,
however (I could easily get back to AlphaBIOS from SRM using fwupdate).

What seemed to work best on later attempts was to include fwupdate.exe on
the diskette along with lx164srm.rom then:
1) update AlphaBIOS firmware; This step seems to reboot in the middle of
the process and brings up a version of AlphaBIOS one level down (-2 level
instead of -3). I have no idea where this could be loading from.
2) from the down level AlphaBIOS screen perform the update firmware again
with the same diskette in. This results in buring SRM into flash, and
doesn't work if fwupdate.exe is not on the diskette.

Summary:
You DON'T want fwupdate on the diskette if your AlphaBIOS will load
lx164srm.rom directly.
You DO want fwupdate.exe on the diskette if your AlphaBIOS goes through
the one level down step. (Could this version be coming from the "fail
safe" half of the flash rom?)

===========================================================
bootp wierdness

The bootp image I built using "make bootpfile" would not execute (this
worked perfectly on my PC164 board). I reverted to the procedure given in
the SRM HOWTO and that worked fine (same "make config" for both).

The symptom for the bootpfile failure is that it downloads the bootpfile
from the server and starts executing. At the point where it says "Loading
Kernal" the system hangs.
