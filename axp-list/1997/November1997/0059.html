OK, gang, in preparation for the hypothetical Red Hat 5.0, I've generated
a bunch of new MILOs, based on 2.0.30 plus alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.2a plus
some new platform support.

I'd appreciate any help you could give me in testing them out.

The following are currently on gatekeeper.dec.com at:

/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/Test-Milos-2.0.30+/

cabrio
eb164
eb64p
eb66
eb66p
lx164
miata
noname-udb
pc164
sx164
xl-avanti
xlt-alcor

Please send any feedback, good or bad, ONLY to: Jay.Estabrook@digital.com.
I will keep axp-list updated with the status of particular platforms.

What's New
==========

- Auto-detection of filesystem types (thanks, Erik! :-)

- New PALcode sets for the EV5 and EV56 based platforms.

- Graceful reboot (via "shutdown -r now")

- New platform support: SX164

- Updated ncr53c8xx driver (2.4a) for support of NCR875-based controllers

- More accurate CPU/system information (/proc/cpuinfo): see discussion below

- Platform specific fixes: MIATA fast clock, LX164 slow clock

- Lots of clean-up

What to Look Out For
====================

The change in /proc/cpuinfo reporting results in possible changes in the
"system type" and "system variation" fields. The "type" will more accurately
reflect the platform "family", and the "variation" the specific platform.

For example, Cabriolet will show up as type EB64+ and variation Cabriolet;
PC164 will show up as type EB164 and variation PC164. Similarly for other
"families" such as EB66/EB66+ and ALCOR. All the others should be unchanged.

I don't believe this will affect how the RH 4.2 and prior versions of LIBC
detect the appropriate platform-specific bus IO and memory access mechanisms,
but it is possible; if so, use the "/etc/alpha_systype" backdoor to provide
the necessary information to LIBC for correct operation.

Good luck, and thanks for the help!
