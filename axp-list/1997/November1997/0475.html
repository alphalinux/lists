Bill Broadley <bill@math.UCDavis.edu> writes:
> In any case there are lotsa nice scsi solutions. I want one with
> maximum stability, performance without too high a price.

You might go with something Symbios Logic (nee NCR) based---you can
get ASUS' 875-based Ultra-Wide card for $125, and it's supposed to
perform as well as most anything else.

I'll admit I don't know about compatability with the particular
motherboard you mention, but I do know the driver is well supported in
Linux and is very stable in my UDB.

> My personal favorite is the Buslogic 958, reliable, fast, low cpu
> usage, cheap ($220) etc.

I've used several Buslogic's, and liked them, although they've all
been the older MultiMaster boards. The BusLogic driver is also well
supported, although I don't know about the Alpha-compatability.

> Unfortunately I think you can't use this controller as a boot device
> under the lx motherboards... correct?

Ick. Surely they couldn't get away with such a restriction.

> So do I have any choice besides the adaptec 2940? Not that adaptec
> is bad. Seems a little slower, less stable, doesn't handle bus
> resets as well, more sensitive to external cables etc. Oh and a
> good bit more expensive. ($280 vs $220).

Agreed. You could also check out DPT, which is also more expensive,
but well-supported and fast.
