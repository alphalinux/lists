On Nov 8, 8:26pm, Peter S. Halpern wrote:
> Subject: What can be upgraded on a DEC Alpha Multia VX40A-F2?
> Is there a way to upgrade to 32k colours or better? Possibly with another
> video card?
>

Yes, but space is very tight. It's not so much which cards will work, as a
good sampling of PCI-based cards will work, it's what will physically fit in
the box. I'm satisfied with the built-in, so I never investigated it
thoroughly.
Some guys on this board have experience, and they'll either answer, or
direct you to the archives for this mailing list...

> What is the max external SCSI CD-ROM I can run on this box? The place I
> got it (Starship Computer) says 4x, others say 8x. Is it one of these or
> even higher?

They're on drugs. So long as it's SCSI, the Alpha does not care about it's
speed. I've seen some 24x IDE models. Frankly, it does not matter unless you
are running some game that brings animation & sound over from the CD while it
runs.
For installing Linux or whatever, which is what I do, an old 2x was more than
enough. :-)

>
> Which modems work on these boxes? I would like to get at least 33k, but a
> k56flex would be preferable.

I suppose there's no issue here. Obviously get an external unit. Be aware that
there's special cabling off of the serial port. Dec decided to wire both ports
into a single connector, and you have to make a "Y" cable to go to the two
external devices. Wiring diagrams in the archives.

> Can you put in 60ns memory chips or is the DEC system pretty finicky about
> 70ns chips?

Ho ho ho! Very finicky. It won't mind the faster speed, but it's adamant about
Parity and layout (2x34). Onsale sells the chips sometimes, and they're
certainly not very hard to find -- but they are NOT stock PC memory chips!
