>That sounds like a gross oversimplification. I only have a 21164 but if
>you're right byte-loads will be slower on the 21164a, because they are no
>longer split into several instructions? :-)

>AFAIK current CISC-CPUs do not use microcode for most heavily-used
>instructions - and RISC basically has only those instructions anyway.

You didn't get me right. The Story went about complex CISC instructions.
Either your compiler splits them up (costs time) and the CPU needs overall
less microcode to do the job, or you leave them alone (fast) and the CPU
uses more and complex microcode to execute them "online".

The point is, that different execution times, and in particular the different
instruction fetch times of the higher level CISC instructions, do not go well
with syncronous pipelines.

So it seems to be an advantage if the compilation process takes more time,
for in turn it produces rather uniform machine instructions (to come back
to the original question).

BTW: what "current CISC-CPUs" are you thinking of?

>Of course RISC has advantages.

It depends on what you are doing.
