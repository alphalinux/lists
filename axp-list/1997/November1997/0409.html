On Sat, 15 Nov 1997, Don Kitchen wrote:

> Partition check:
> sda: sda1 sda2 sda3 sda7
>
> So there's obviously a disk there and since digital unix boots, it obviously
> works and the partitions are for real. But even though the linux kernel
> recognizes the disk, once I get into the fdisk program it says the disk has 0
> heads, 0 cylinders, etc, and shows a heading for the partition table but no
> entries.

Well, digital unix uses disklabels whereas fdisk by default uses the silly
msdos partition scheme. No such partitions are set up on your disk, so you
find no partitions.

You can make fdisk use the disklabel (like DU) by typing 'b' as in
b edit bsd disklabel

This should hopefully solve your problem.

Good luck!
