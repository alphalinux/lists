Michael Brennen <mbrennen@fni.com> wrote:

> Great! Any idea what value the old fuse was, in case I need to fix
> this myself later?

I would have guessed that a 250 mA fuse would have been enough. However,
I just looked at the labels on my DEC mouse and Microsoft keyboard, and see
that they are rated at 125 mA and 500 mA respectively. I guess a 750 mA or
1A fuse would be called for.

Anyone that designs a keyboard today that needs over 25 mA should be shot.
There's no reason whatsoever that it should draw that much.

If DEC had designed the UDB properly, they would have used a Raychem
Polyfuse instead of a normal fuse. They are self-resetting, and aren't even
very expensive.
