On Tue, 11 Nov 1997, Michael Tibor wrote:

I (ccurtis@ee.fit.edu) wrote:
> > Firewalling does not work properly on kernel 2.0.30 - I haven't tried
> > 2.0.31 yet. This is on an Intel box, btw, I haven't set up my Alpha
> > firewall yet. I'd recommend you back down to 2.0.29 or try 2.0.31.
>
> Hmmm... what makes you say this? I have both firewall and masquerade
> rules working fine on my UDB at home, as well as an Intel box here at
> work. I've used the Redhat 2.0.30 kernel as well as 2.0.30-pre1 through
> -pre10.

I had a fully functioning masquerading firewall set up as a default route
for a private network with 2.0.29 on an Intel box. Using the same
.config.cache (or whatever the file was called) I downloaded and compiled
2.0.30. The firewall immediately stopped working and displayed a lot of
strange behaviors. I looked around the net a bit and found that 2.0.30
broke firewalling with masquerading - RedHat even sent a notice about it.
One of the -pre kernels I suppose fixed this, but I do now know which one
(-pre3 seems to ring a bell though). They stated they would issue a patch
for the kernel soon. That, in part, was the reason 2.0.x continued to be
developed (2.0.31 is out now) while 2.1.x was being developed. If you'll
notice, RedHat would not for a long time distribute the 2.0.30 kernel.
They shipped 2.0.29 then jumped to I think 2.0.31-pre3. Again, this is on
my Intel (initally) Slackware based box with ipfwadm, not Alpha.

Finding that others had the same problem, I didn't take extensive notes,
and at this point do not recall what specifically did and did not work.
