On 16 Nov 1997, Eric Smith wrote:

> Arthur Twarecki <atwarec@zeus.leitch.com> wrote:
> > I don't see any resistors on the SCSI (riser) card in my UDB. Does this
> > mean there is no temrmination on the card, or perhaps the 53c8xx SCSI
> > controller does termination internally??
>
> J C Lawrence wrote:
> > As far as I can tell the card is not terminated. It expects the internal
> > drive (if any) to be terminated, an a terminator to be placed on the
> > external port.
>
> No. The riser card is *always* terminated, by a little yellow surface-mount
> resistor pack soldered directly to the riser board. The internal drive is
> *not* terminated, and in fact the 2.5 inch SCSI drives that DEC used did not
> even have a place for terminators.

Okay, I located it. But I'm a little surprised as to the location of the
terminators wrt the connectors and the controller.

> A UDB with an internal SCSI drive and no external SCSI peripherals has
> a singly-terminated SCSI bus, which is a no-no. They manage to get away
> with it because the bus is short, but if you look at the SCSI signals on
> an oscilliscope they look nasty. Plugging an external terminator onto the
> connector cleans them up somewhat.

Yuck!

> A UDB with both internal and external SCSI devices, and an external terminator,
> has the correct total of two terminators. However, the cable to the internal
> drive exceeds the SCSI 2 limit on stub length.

Here I assume you're refering to a 2.5" drive. Or perhaps our cabling is
different.

> All in all, I must say that I am very disappointed that DEC didn't design
> the SCSI bus of the UDB properly.

Indeed. Judging from the board layout, the SCSI bus on the riser card
seems to have the following structure.

:::------[]-----:::----# terminators
50-pin '810 50-pin
2mm? .100

So if you don't have any internal device, and you terminate the external
chain, you still end up with a long stub from the .100 connector to the
'810 controller and the smaller (2mm?) connector.

I'm considering taking my HD out into a case. I wonder if it makes sense
to remove the smt terminators and instead put something on the 2mm
connector. This way I'd virtually eliminate all stubs on the bus.
