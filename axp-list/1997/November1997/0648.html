Where you stated:
>
>Hello,
>
> My searching for an Alpha is not finished yet. I want to
>know if there is a limit for Adaptec 2940UW or Intraserver
>Ultra SCSI controller to drive a =>9GB hard disk and how many such
>disks (>=9GB) can be attached to the card. Does this depen upon the
>card or drivers on Linux? Sorry for the ingorance on this.
>
>
Using wide SCSI devices, you should be able to attach up to 7 SCSI narrow
devices (ID 0 to 6) and 8 wide devices (ID 8 to 15). The controller will be
ID 7.
In practice, this can only be done if total cable length is less than 80" (
2 meters) and if good active termination is used.

In my personal experience the SymbiosLogic based cards like th Intraserver
are more reliable on the Alpha. Also issues like setting the termination on
or off on the Adaptec is more problematic. The so-called "auto-termination"
on the Adaptec is less reliable than jumper used on other cards.

In either case thew drivers should be updated and a new kernel built, as
the driver level as of Red Hat 4.2 is quuite old.
I haven't tried it on Red Hat 5.0 yet.
Newer milo (Jay Estabrook posted about 1 1/2 weeks ago) is also very
desirable, and will usually make the system, and SCSI more stable.
