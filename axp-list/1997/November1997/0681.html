With regards to your message at 10:49 PM 11-17-97 +0100, Ronny Ranerup.
Where you stated:
>
>Hi there,
>
>it seems the IDE controller (the CMD646 chip) on the LX won't be
supported for a
>while, so has anyone tried using an external IDE controller on an Alpha?
>
>Especially interesting would be something like the Promise ultra33 or
fasttrak.
>The ultra33 spec says it automagically configures the drives to ultra/dma, so
>could that mean that it isn't necessary to have a special driver for the
onboard
>chipset as the driver just sees it as any IDE controller sitting on the
PCI bus
>and defaults to standard ATA, but with the drive correctly configured for
>maximum transfer rate. Could this work?
>
>Of course, any autoconfiguration-stuff usually means something gets fucked
up
>under Linux...
>
>Anyway, has anybody out there tried an external IDE controller and did the
>performance get anywhere near what it should be (>10Mbytes/sec)?
>
Yes, BUT: All of the PCI based fast IDE controllers I have seen require
drivers to work.

So, while I suspect they will work on your system, are you prepared to
write these drivers?
Besides, by the time you figure in the cost of a good IDE card, why not
just go SCSI?
While better IDe has a decent data rate, it still won't do nice things like
command-queuing.
