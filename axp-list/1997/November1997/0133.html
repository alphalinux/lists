Uncle George wrote:
>
> it seems that the noname, and a linux 486 seem to think that the floating point
> operation
> associated with the following pgm are the same. When it comes to the 164LX, this
> result is always 0 ( ie 0x000000000000000000, the 486/noname have a 0x01 )

If you mean 0x00000001, isn't that an unnormalized floating point
number?? I always thought 0x00000000 would be the smallest number
correctly represented with IEEE single precision floating point
with the implied bit on. Out of curiosity, what sort of application
worries about that sort of number? Why not just switch to double
precision??

No answer, but I do lot's of single precision floating point and
I usually just want the numbers to underflow gracefully to zero.
If the algorithm is unstable with single precision, I normally
switch to double precision.
