Looked at that - u should know that these bits controll whether or not the TRAPPED
FP errors should be returned back to the user as a SIGFPE.
That is all software FP traps. If u dont trap, these bits are meaningless.
They als do not controll the FPCR. on the 21064, float errors are traped to the kern for
its disposition. with the new 21164, u can set the hardware to take care of most traps,
and
report the rite results without the kern ever knowing what happened.
1 of those traps was underflow, and the hardware choice was
either return 0.0, or a denormal. I just got the wrong ( not expected answer ) and wanted
to
know why.
