On Wed, 19 Nov 1997, Murray Stokely wrote:

> On Wed, 19 Nov 1997, Paul J.Y. Lahaie wrote:
> % More like they don't want to kill their CD market by having it
> % available for FTP before. Both the FTP and CD version will most likely be
> % released at the same time. Which makes alot of sense.
>
> It doesn't kill any market, and I've never seen ANY other vendor pull
> this. You can get Slackware, Debian, FreeBSD, etc.. from FTP sites
> about a month before you can buy a CD. Ideally FTP versions should be
> released one week + replication time before a CD is ready so that the
> authors can fix any initial bugs that pop up. (No matter how much
> beta-testing, some little slip-up always gets through).

There is a fundamental difference. SlackWare and Debiand (for example) are
put on an FTP server and then somebody starts making CD's. Here the
organization making the CD's and setting the software on the FTP server
are one and the same. So obviously it may work differently.

But I guess Red Hat 5.0 will be amongst us before anyone can buy a CD.

It won't be a major change from Mustang to Hurricane anyway.
