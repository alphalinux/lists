> On Wed, 19 Nov 1997, nm wrote:
> I think the Linux community is stronger than that, and distribution
> via CDROM only would be an incredibly bad marketing and PR move
> for RedHat. Give em more credit for smarts than that. They stay ahead
> of Debian and Slack because of their marketing acumen, not in spite
> of it. If they did that, there would be a radical shift in the market
> share of the linux distributions.

Quite correct.

> Now, I do think they are smoking dope if they think they will sell many
> RHmember subscriptions at $299.00, that smacks of the Caldera reality
> distortion field. We, my company paid only about $50 more for a full one
> year support contract awhile back. Add it up, Redhat has only had 3 or
> fewer revisions in a given year in the past. Even if you paid full price
> for 3 boxed sets + shipping, AND bought a ball cap, AND figured in a 10%
> discount on the typical amount of Redhat products you bought in a year,
> you would still fall almost $100 short of the $299 membership fee. What
> kind of "deal" is that? Thats alot of money to pay for the convenience of
> getting the dists automatically. Yes, I know there are other unnamed
> perks. But as you can see this is not gonna be for the 'typical' RedHat
> user.

It'll sell some copies to "corporate" folks that won't cut a P.O. for less
than $250.

If it results in some companies starting to believe that Linux is for real for
a mere premium of $100, that's OK by me.

> On the other hand, lest you think I hate RedHat...
> I like RedHat and I'm ordering my 5.0 for $50 (like I did for 3.0.3,
> 4.0, 4.1, and 4.2) And I've got the Motif 2.0, PowerTools, Library,
> Maximum RPM, a couple of ballcaps and a frisbee all bought at Linux
> EXPO 97. So I believe in supporting RedHat, all in all, they have done
> excellent things for the Linux community. Cut them some slack, its
> not like they are the Evil Empire.

I know a couple of people that really *do* consider RedHat to be an "Evil
Empire" because they charge more than $1.99 per CD.

And it all flies in the face of the words of RMS himself who suggests that
people selling free software try to charge *AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE* so as to
improve the funding available for producing more free software. But I digress.

I'll probably upgrade my Intel system once 5.1 comes out, and get the Alpha
version of 5.0 on the "Library" CDs...

I guess my speculation on timing was wrong. I expected Red Hat to wait for
the next release of XFree86 to become available.

Note that that, and fixes to GLIBC, will probably make for a "natural" 5.1
release in about February or March...
