On Wed, 12 Nov 1997, Christopher E. Brown wrote:

> The 486 -> PPro have a limit of either 16 or 32 CPUs MAX (though
> finding motherboards larger than Quad is hard)

The built-in limits of the 486 is one, for the Pentium it's two and
for the PPro it's 4. You can go above that, but the MB has to have much
more logic.

> The PII is limited to 2 CPU by design, and yes it works find, actually
> Linus has among other systems a dual PII.

Nothing is preventing someone from making a 20 processor P-II system.
It's just going to be alot more expensive than if the chip had support for
it built-in.

> The Merced (or whatever the new Slot 2 CPU is to be called) is supposed to
> fix the 512MB memor issue, allow Quad CPU system, etc...

From my understanding of the Intel lit, Slot II is just a different
casing for the P-II that is geared for servers. The next processor is
codenamed Deschutes and it's the P-II that'll run at up to 450MHz and the
Merced is an ia-64 processor (it's native instruction set is not x86 but a
64-bit "EPIC" set)
