If your interested in state of the art compilers for linux running
on the alpha it's time to speak up...

I think I've found the right group within DEC to convince to release
their highly-efficient C, C++, and F77 compilers for Linux on x86
and alpha. One big facator in their decision will probably be an
estimate of how many they can sell.

While I wouldn't use their compilers for ordinary programs, they would
be very handy for anything that you really care about efficiency with.
Right now gcc/g77 isn't very good at all on the alpha, and on the x86
I think that you can often get 10% better performance with the DEC
compilers. I expect the alpha compilers to improve now that they're
being actively worked on by Toon and cygnus, but I could use a good
compiler today.

The cost would be around $595 per node, with the resulting binaries
being able to run on any system without further royalties. Run-time
support may be "as is", that is, they wouldn't extensively test the
libraries, and it would be up to us to find bugs.

If you're interested, please send me your name, email address, and
a guess at the number of licenses you're intested in. I will be
giving this list to Digital. I'll also be posting this appeal to
comp.os.linux.announce or similar newsgroups.
