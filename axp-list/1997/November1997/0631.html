I've got a couple of UDB-233s doing standard duty mail list and ftp
service; they are okay for that, but not for heavy work. The main
reason I've stayed committed to the Intel architecture is that many
binary only products, such as database engines, RealAudio servers,
etc. are provided for linux/x86 only. I would *love* to be able to
get the performance of the Alphas, but no go until I can get native
Alpha versions. IMO em86 is too much of a hack to depend on for
reliable, maintainable, efficient 24x7 servers.
