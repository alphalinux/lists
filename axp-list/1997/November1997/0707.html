'Relatively' affordable Alpha machines are becoming available now in Holland.
I am in the process of selecting a new machine for next year and one of the
possibilities is a Digital Alpha because of the horsepower and because I would
like to get away from Intel as much as possible, if possible.

The choice would be a Digital AlphaPC 164LX board and a 21164PC 533 MHz
processor. However browsing the axp-list archive does not exactly distill much
confidence in the workability of such a solution (for me).
I hope some of you experienced Alpha users will be so kind as to answer the
following questions.

First what do I use a PC for: I use my PC at home, quite intensively, for
statistical analysis, research and simulation for which I have no time at work.
(using the statistical package Stata for Linux). I also have a music hobby (Jazz
midi sequencer). I do not program (except for statistical routines in the Stata
programming languange and some bash futilities). I am not interested in graphics
(except in relation to statistics) or games, networking, servers, etc.
I am quite sophisticated hardware wise and relatively experienced in Linux (now
1.5 years) and use Linux as my only OS at home and at work.

The questions:

1) How stable is the Alpha Linux kernel, does this really approach the Intel
kernel now in stability?

2) How is the hardware compatability: memory, SCSI, disks, zip drives, printers,
and especially soundcards (AWE64?)

3) How about stable drivers for the above mentioned hardware?

4) How is the Digital 164LX mainboard hardware compatability? Can you just plug
in any SCSI controller, disk, soundcard, zipdrive, etc.? How about the BIOS?

5) As I do not want to have to compile my own software for the Alpha (or
(re)compile the kernel if at all possible) it is nice to see more RPM's and
tarballs available. But how about commercial software like Stata, Applixware,
CDE, Netscape, to name the ones I use. Do these run at all comfortably under
EM86, or do they even run at all. Is there software that has problems with EM86?
(Stata for example still uses a.out).

6) Is anybody running Applix or CDE under EM86, please comment?

7) Does EM86 have problems with Linux (RH 5.0 say). Is EM86 just as good as
FX!32 or is it a shabby port?

8) Where do you get EM86, from Digital? Is it expensive, do you buy a license or
software?

9) How do apps under EM86 compare to native 64 bit software (benchmarks?)

10) How about installation of Linux (RH4.x or 5.x). Does this, in general, go
just as smooth as RH on Intel Pentiums or are there peculiarities that really
distinguish the Alpha from the Intel. I would not be interested in a machine
that I would have to spend 1 to say 8 weeks coaxing along just to get Linux
installed and then again maybe another couple of months just to iron out the
kinks and get it running smoothly. I use my machine to work and not to wrestle
with. I don't want especially to be a bleeding edge pioneer (or is the Alpha
past that phase).

11) In the light of 9) above how about MILO. Does MILO have any quirks or
peculiarities that distinguish it from LILO (BIOS, disks, controllers, etc).

12) How about ISP connectivity (modems, ISDN, cable modems, etc?).

I would appreciate it very much if one or more of you Alpha guru's would take
the time to answer one or more of the above questions (as honestly as possible).
