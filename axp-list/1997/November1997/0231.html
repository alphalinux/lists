On Wed, 12 Nov 1997, Rob Zook wrote:

> What do you base this on? I thought the Pentium II was supposed to have
> all the benefits of the MMX Pentium and the Pentium Pros? Data General
> uses _many_ Pentium Pro's in their AV 2000 Enterprise Server. So why
> would the Pentium II loose functionality? I have heard of Quad Pentium
> Pro boards, although I grant you they are hard to find.

This is based on the info released by intel. The PII is a PPro
takeoff, it has a seperate off chip cache, a core very similar to the
PPro, and a different physical package/connector. The PII can only be use
in Sungle or Dual proc configs, the chip itself does not support beyond
dual. Also due to a *feature* in the cache module the PII not only cannot
cache more than 512M, but wraps memory writes above 512M below 512M.


> Besides, I did not think x86/Linux supported SMP with anything but
> Pentium Pros.

x86/Linux SMP will work with

486sx
486dx
Pentium
Pentium/MMX
PPro
PII

The 486 -> PPro have a limit of either 16 or 32 CPUs MAX (though
finding motherboards larger than Quad is hard)

The PII is limited to 2 CPU by design, and yes it works find, actually
Linus has among other systems a dual PII.

The Merced (or whatever the new Slot 2 CPU is to be called) is supposed to
fix the 512MB memor issue, allow Quad CPU system, etc...
