Dear Jim

>I just got a 533Mhz PC164LX, which I want to boot using bootp. I finally
>got SRM working perfectly on my PC164, and am now moving on.
>
>The AlphaBIOS goes through its initialization of memory and SCSI (Adaptec
>2940UW) and then fails the network adapter with 0x5 as the only error code.
> This adapter works fine as soon as linux boots from disk.
>
>I had a problem with SRM because I had to change the ethernet mode to
>"Twisted-pair". Is there something equivalent to this for AlphaBIOS? I
>don't see anything I can adjust.

You mention Alpha BIOS and SRM. Just which one are you using? Digital's SRM
only supports Digital UNIX qualified SCSI controllers and Adaptec is not one
of them. Therefore, it will never boot the hard drive. IntraServer is, but
they have two cards one supports NT/Linux the other supports SRM(cost more).

There is NO SUPPORT AT THIS TIME IN THE ALPHA BIOS FOR ANYTHING TO DO WITH
ETHERNET. Bottom line don't use it because it WILL NOT WORK!

Never should of been installed until it worked, we receive at least two to
three calls on it a week.

Sorry its not better news.
