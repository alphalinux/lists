>>And the last one... Both the internal drive and the card are terminated.
>>You need to remove one of the terminators before starting a chain.

>No you don't , at least if you use an active terminator on the outside bus,
>at the end.
>This way the machine will still run properly if you pull the cable off.

What do you mean? To get things clear:

"active termination" refers to a Boulay-Terminator (2.85V voltage source
drives every signal line through 110 Ohms) or various integrated Chips
e.g. Texas-Instruments (current source while signal is switching).
Maybe you also want to take IBMs "Trung Le Terminator" as active termination.

"outside bus" refers to the part of the SCSI-bus existing between the
SCSI-connector at the back of your computer case and the "SCSI-out" connector
of the last external device which is connected to the bus.

If I got you right, you mean people could leave their internal terminators
alone (TWO terminators) and use active termination at the free end of an
occasionally connected external bus as a THIRD terminator.

I agree that this may work, but it is not legal. I call in question, that
this "hack" continues to work along with "ultra" drives (20 MB/sec narrow).
Anyway, you will considerable reduce ac-impedance on the signal lines and
not every driver will be able to cope with this, as it is beyond the spec.

The legal solution is, to use "automatic" termination at the end of the
internal bus, which connects to the outside world. This kind of terminator
is capable of detecting an external bus and activates or deactivates itself
accordingly.

Maybe this is what you meant by "active" terminator?
