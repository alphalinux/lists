> > Hi all. I have an XLT-300 and thought I would put a buslogic BT958 in it,
> > and a Quantum 2G Ultra-Wide disk.
> >
> As I've been playing with a similar idea, I'd like to ask how one does
> route the new SCSI cable from controller to the drive(s)? At least the
> XLT-300 I got has that huge CPU/cache-card on motherboard, dividing the
> case in two parts: PCI/ISA slots under it and drives, power supply etc.
> on top. Quickly looking it could be possible to put that cable between
> motherboard and CPU card (if I change that Matrox card to a lower slot).
> Is this the way to go? Or is there actually enough space between
> the side panel and CPU assembly for SCSI cable? (In my opinion, it looked
> as if the panel and CPU module were designed to touch each other, but I
> cannot check that right now.)

Well, I went for it, and it worked. The NCR is still enabled, and I can't
get to the Buslogic BIOS yet, though.

I ran the cable(s) underneath the CPU card, and it fits just perfectly.
Be really careful though, as its obviously tight, but I didn't know what
would happen if I ran it over the CPU board.

So, I got both the wide cable for my drive, and the narrow cable for my
CDROM run thru there, but I think there could be a termination problem,
because the RH 4.9 install doesn't detect the CDROM, even on the Buslogic
probe messages..

Now just to figure out how to compile a kernel.. :) Sure is humbling..
