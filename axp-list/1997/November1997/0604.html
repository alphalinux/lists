On Fri, 21 Nov 1997, Roman Martonak wrote:

> I have an extra 2.5 340 MB TOSHIBA SCSI disk, and I wonder whether
> there is a way to connect it externally to my UDB. I have inside my
> UDB a 518 MB 2.5 disk, and it would be nice if I could also make use
> of the other one and gain 340 MB. Does anybody have any experience
> with this ?

I just set something like this up (see my 'help formatting' thread...) on
my UDB. All you have to do (well, what we did) is find an adapter slot
that converts from an internal SCSI connection to an external one. It's a
simple little device that cost around $20.00. You'll then need a SCSI
cable - 6 ft cost $50.00. (huh?) And a case to put it in. We found some
external drive enclosures for about $100 that held one or two disks, so we
went to the local computer shop and bought a PC case with 7 drive bays for
$28.00. It's a bit more bulky than an external case, but who really
cares? We hope to use it like a mini-array (possibly with the md driver)
and support several workstations. We'll just need another adaptor slot
for each workstation we want to support. I'm not sure if you can fit a
2.5" drive in there without another adaptor; we went and got an IBM 3.5"
4.1GB for $350.00 or so, and after letting it warm up, are happy with it.
(It had trouble determining the size automatically at first...)

I still assert that external drives are a rip off...

Have fun,
