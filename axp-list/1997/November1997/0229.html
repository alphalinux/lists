Hello,

> I am running RedHat 4.2 Linux on a UDB (166MHz, 32 MB RAM, 2.1 GB external
> SCSI hard drive, 96 MB swap). When compiling programs with gcc, compiler
> crashes with signal 11 sometimes occur (not often), in an unpredictable way,
> and after typing 'make' again, compilation continues O.K.

I have been bitten by the same sort of problems since I bought my Noname
166 (with which I underwent all possible sorts of hardware problems, from
SCSI termination to _incorrectly pinned CPU fan_ (!) which let its magic
smoke escape). Recently I purchased a (second :-) CPU fan because my
motherboard is installed in a "mini-tower" chassis, and the CPU heatsink
wings are oriented the wrong way : they are only vertical when the
motherboard is horizontal (the fix would be to turn my CPU 1/4 of a turn on
its socket, if you see what I mean, but I fear for the loss of its magic
smoke which is somewhat more valuable than that of a fan).

Anyway. The CPU was running real hot because of the insufficient airflow.
I put this fan on, and never got any more compiler sig11's nor even the kernel
oops I used to get from random utilities (I once got an oops in /bin/true,
can you believe this ?). I would suggest you check the temperature of your
system... The 36-bit ECC facility does not protect the memory path between
the CPU and the cache, and that may be the point where it hurts.
