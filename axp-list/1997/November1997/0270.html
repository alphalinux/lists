These RZ29B-VW drives (DEC's on-platform drives) are jumperless and mounted
in sealed platic cases with built in controllers that should auto-detect
the narrow bus and negotiate SCSI IDs accordingly. These drives are
seagates but use a DEC specific firmware. One other note, it is my
understanding that the current versions of DUnix and OpenVMS do not support
16 bit SCSI IDs so these on platform drives may have some oddities in their
firmware. How this would effect its ability to run in narrow mode I do not
know. You may want to consider switching to a DEC controller such as:
KZPAA-AA (fast narrow), KZPDA-AA (fast wide) or KZPBA-CA (Ultra wide).
Check with DEC, your distributor and the Linux Alpha FAQ for information
regarding support for these cards with your hardware and with Linux.
