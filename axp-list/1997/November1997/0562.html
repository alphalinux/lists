Erik

First, thanks for your courteous and prompt response.

Second, I disagree that Press releases are inappropriate precisely
because they are `read mostly by corporate types.' The `corporate
types' are the ones who think UNIX et al is unimportant and that
Microsoft & major industry players originated most of the stuff they
use. Inasmuch as `corporate types' are the ones who write the checks,
we badly need that they understand how the tools they depend on were
originated and proven out. They need to understand that they have the
most to lose if software patents and proprietariness tie up the
community to the point where we are unable to develop with minimal
restrictions.

I think that this is the point of RMS's incessant harping on `please
give credit and publicly promote the cause of free software'. RMS
comes off as kind of extreme, but most of his positions are well
reasoned - correct or not. I don't think he will -ever- be taken
seriously by `corporate types', but you guys may be in a different
position - inasmuch as you are in this for profit.

Anyway, I think it would be really beneficial if RH can champion the
recognition of the independent s/w development community to corporate
management. It really doesn't make much difference how good GNU tools
and the Linux OS is if I can't get the stuff into my client's shop in
competition against uSoft. This has a ripple effect in that it slows
down Linux ports of important business software - Informix, for
example.

Anyway, thanks for letting me babble.
