>>> "John T. Croteau" said:
>
> Any idea if the PowerStorm 3D and 4D series will be supported in the
> future? Or, should I just go with a 3rd party video card?

I believe the Powerstorm 3D10, which is a VGA of some sort (S3 864/868 or
some such, IIRC), would already be supported by the XFree86 servers.
However, its not one of the more recent or high performance chipsets, so it
might be better to buy a 3rd party card from Diamond or #9 or ATI. Look at
what's supported by the latest XFree86 3.2 release to help make a
choice. You should have no problems with console code support of most
mainstream VGAs, although some with funky BIOS code may be problematic (true
of Mach64 and Millenium cards with early revs of console code/BIOS
emulation).

The higher-end 3Dxx and 4Dxx cards are not currently supported by
XFree86-based servers, and I've not heard even rumors of plans for such,
either in XFree86 or commercial server companies like X-Inside or
Metro-Link.

The 3D30, an 8-plane version aka TGA2, *may* get some kind of support at
some time in the future, but there are no real plans as of the moment.

If you need to dual-boot LINUX with DU, your choices are (admittedly) quite
limited; DU supports only ATI Mach64 of some specific flavor, and S3 Trio64,
in terms of commodity VGAs (both well-supported under LINUX). Also, DEC's
8-plane TGA, aka ZLXp-E1, is also supported under both. If dual-booting
LINUX with NT, your choices are *much* wider.
