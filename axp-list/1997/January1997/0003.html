>When the 2.0 kernel first came out my Pentium machine compiled it in
>about 20 min. Since I spend time compiling programs, I am rather
>disappointed
>that the Alpha doesn't run all that well with gcc. Once the compiler is
>optimized will the Alpha be faster or is the 32/64 bit difference still
>going to be a problem?
>
I'm probably not the best person to ask this question of, but I think the
answer
lies in the speed of the Digital Unix compiler. I think it is reasonable
to
assume that it is pretty well optimized (for the price it ought to be) and
that with the best possible optimization GCC will run at this speed.

I have only read that the DU C compiler is faster, and I have no hard data
on its speed.

Note that the UDB is slow as far al Alpha machines go. More up-to-date
units (read more expensive - but getting cheaper) are far faster due in
large part to 128 bit memory buss structure.

>>
>> I think your 1.8x faster than a P90 is about right. I moved from a P150
>> to the
>> 233Mhz. Udb (clocked up to 266) and I would say it is a bit faster at
>> most things,
>> a lot faster at some (like X and floating point intensive apps) and
>> slower at
>> others (compilation).
>
>What kind of X programs are a lot faster?
>

This is a subjective measure on my part. When I was running an Intel box I
saw a large difference when I went from the XFree X server to the
AcceleratedX
commercial server (this running on a 150 Mhz. Pentium). The (XFree) TGA
server on the
UDB seems at least as fast as the AcceleratedX, and I perceive that
windows in
general seem to "snap" open faster. Graphics programs like XV and XFIG
seem to run
faster as well although this may be due to the fact that I have more RAM
in the
Alpha than I had in the intel (32Mb. in the Intel and 56Mb. in the alpha.
Given the
RAM hungry nature of RISC machines this may not be a factor though)

Again, this is all highly subjective, and I'm sure the many more learned
folks
on the list can give you better info.

In any case, I'm not sorry I bought the UDB. I think it's like when I
bought a
MAC Plus at a pawn shop for $100 - it eventually led to my buying a Power
MAC.
I think my UDB experience will eventually lead me to buy a 21164 machine,
especially
as the prices are coming down fast.

As always, just my $0.02
