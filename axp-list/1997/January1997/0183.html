tomas <tomasb@zero.rainside.sk> writes:
> i've given up installing red hat 2.1 on my jensen and decided
> to get red hat 4.0
> took a look around ftp.redhat.com and found lots of stuff.
> can some1 please tell me what i'm supposed to download to install
> it on my DECpc.

Get down to the 'redhat-4.0/axp' directory. (I checked this is on a
mirror) It should hold a subdir caled 'RedHat', which contains

RPMS . . . . . . . . . . . . . . [Nov 18 17:21]
axp. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . [Oct 4 18:35] 0k
base . . . . . . . . . . . . . . [Nov 18 17:19]
by-group . . . . . . . . . . . . [Nov 18 17:21]
instimage. . . . . . . . . . . . [Nov 18 17:21]
rpmcontents.gz . . . . . . . . . [Oct 4 18:35] 116k
sets . . . . . . . . . . . . . . [Nov 18 17:19]
upgrade. . . . .

As far as I know, you don't need the 'sets' and 'by-group'
directories. They were needed by previous versions of RedHat, and
just havn't been deleted.

You probably don't need 'upgrade', either. You only need the files of
'RPMS' which you wan't to install. Because some files are needed for
the basic install, I suggest you just leave major packages away, of
which you know you won't need 'em.

Back to 'redhat-4.0/axp', get 'images/ramdisk.img',
'images/jensen.img'. Also you might want to get 'kernels/jensen.gz',
to install it with the ARC utils. Use the Jensen-install stuff, which
is _now_ on ftp://ftp.azstarnet.com/linux/jensen/install/ (or similar
path). Don't use former versions, because aboot prior to version 0.5
cannot handle ELF kernels.

Good luck, Martin
