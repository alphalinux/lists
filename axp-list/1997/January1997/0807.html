> I am interested in buying a low-end alpha computer. A friend of mine spent
> $800 for a new UDB last fall. I wonder if there is similar offer available now.

I bought mine for $390 via www.onsale.com (from Starship), prices vary
on an auction-to-auction basis. Note that these have no keyboard, memory
or disk, but they do come with Red Hat 4.0 and a mouse...

You might get a price of $800 on a 233 Mhz model with some memory and
disk from Usenet, this'd be a better deal all round (unless you're on
a really tight budget and already have disks, parity memory etc.)

People seem to strongly advise against using internal disks on the
UDB anyway, for heat/PSU reasons...

I don't think the support from Starship up to much, certainly got more
informative responses from this list than Starship WRT jumper questions
(thanks to all who responded). They do state "hardware support only", but
they couldn't provide that information either...

INHO someone like DCG would provide far superior support WRT linux and
probably the hardware too, but I don't think they're in the business
of selling obsolete hardware?

Cheers,
