> If your modem is external and connected to one of the builtin serial ports,
> then be aware that there was a problem with initialization of the Super IO
> chip of which the serial ports are part; stock 2.0.27 has the fixes
> integrated, but that doesn't do you much good unless you can build a new
> kernel. Microway is aware of this, and was given patches earlier, maybe
> they've made an updated kernel available?

Well, I am finally running 4.0 and kernel 2.0.27 on my PC164, and I still
cannot get dip to talk to the modem properly. I guess the fix for the
initialization of the Super IO chip is still not working properly.
Dip manages to raise DTR on the modem, but it does not dial and I cannot
even quit dip properly (since it is trying to reset the modem or something).
My modem works just fine on my pentium box (as evidenced by this note), and
I am using identical dip commands on both machines. The "reset" command
from dip hangs. For the reference, I have a USR Sportster 28,800 modem.
