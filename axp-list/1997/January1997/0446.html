At 10:07 hrs. 01-16-97 +0100, you wrote:
>>> On a sidenote(I should probably ask this on the SMP list...) has
>>> anybody got linux running on a dual alpha box yet??
>
>>Yes, but it's only using one CPU at this time... :-) :-(
>
>Fascinating ... Does that mean that SMP Linux/Alpha is in the works
>? Looking at the other SMP Linux projects, it seems that a
>hardware multi-processor standard, issued by the manufacturer, is of
>paramount importance to get this job done - is there such a
>standard for Alpha-based systems ?
>
If I may step in here:
I know that there are currently at least 2 different hardware architectures
for SMP Alpha. One of course, being Digital and the other one from Aspen
systems which was developed for their "Twin Peaks" dual CPU board.
We and some others have been invited to work with Aspen on the port to run
Linux on the Twin Peaks, although frankly this is still at the
conversational stage only, as none have yet received a board. The boards
need another generation or two of engineering refinement before they are
ready for release. As far as the time frame of this looks right now, we hope
for a board by the end of April, and with any luck at all should be at the
stage of getting it properly doing scheduling maybe by the end of May or
June. I know a few people with talent are on the list that are contributing
to this project, so it looks fairly confident.

As far as Digital and SMP for Linux, I suspect this, if it comes, will be
mostly through the untiring efforts of folks like Jay and David, hopefully
Linus and some of the others in the community may get into this as well... I
have the impression Linus has his hands fairly full right now on many
things, not the least being an infant and a move across the Atlantic ;>}

Any comments/thoughts on this Jay?
