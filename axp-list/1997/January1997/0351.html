At 12:04 hrs. 01-14-97 -0500, you wrote:
>I am running a alpha machine w/ SCSI 1GB HDD. the verison of the linux I am
>using is Red Hat Linux 4.0 for Alpha. The problem I have is about fdisk
>utility comes with Red Hat. I have 1GB SCSI Hard Drive. and the
>specification of the hdd is
>2700(cylinders),
>9(heads)
>84(sectors).
No harm, do this:
2700 x 9 x 84 = 2041200
2041200/32/64 = 996 ( and a little bit)
go 996c x 32h x 64spt
This will _not_ hurt your disk, unless you do a dd if=/dev/fd0 of=/dev/sda
bs=1024, in which case you will have the worlds first ( or second) $400
floppy disk.. ;^} No, do NOT do this, it _is_ _not_ funny!!!

Have a Linux day. Actually, this is the same as we do in Digital Unix when
we don't have a handy rzsomething to match...
