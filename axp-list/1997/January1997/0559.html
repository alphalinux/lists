On 21 Jan 1997, Eric Smith wrote:

> If they are "Attempted to access non-existent memory.", I would suspect that
> the real problem is that you have very old versions of linload.exe and milo.
> You might try telling milo that you only have 39M of memory, but you're best
> off upgrading.

I have an UDB with four chips of 8 meg RAM. All digital chips (The second
set came from someone who upgraded his own UDB to 16 meg chips). I got a LOT
of ECC errors. I moved the chips into a different order and noticed the
errors got less. I picked the most stable mix of the two sets of ram,
judging by the amount of ECC errors I got. After that it decreased and right
now i dont have any ECC errors anymore. I am not sure wether it was some
bad contact/placed chip(s) or what. But you might try it.

Paul

Ps. A small warning. At some point when mixing chips, the udb got upset and
booted in the SRM console mode instead of the arc loader :)
