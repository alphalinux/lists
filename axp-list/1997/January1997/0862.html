>
> Hi all,
>
> we just got a PC164 system and I did some benchmarks with our Fortran
> programs over here. One of the test-programs is a simple loop
> doing (output of f2c):
> for (i = 2; i <= i__1; ++i) {
> gam += 1. / (doublereal) i;
> }
> Now, the Fortran-program compiled with the Digital Unix (f77) compiler executes
> TWICE as fast as the gcc executable (I found this factor also in other
> programs). I have read that the gcc math libraries are not very fast,
> but I do not understand why this simple loop is that slow with gcc. So my
> questions:
> 1) Any ideas why ? (This is Redhat 4.0).

Well, I don't have your original source code, and I don't have a 21164 to
check it on, but I would guess instruction scheduling is the culprit.
21164 is theoretically capable of 4 instructions per cycle, but they have
to be scheduled properly or no go. Doubtless DEC has put more effort into
this than GNU, and understands the issues and trickies rather more than
GNU folks.
gprof looks neato. You could also use DMT's cycle checker bit o code to
see where the cycles are taken up. (There are timer registers on the
Alpha for this purpose).
