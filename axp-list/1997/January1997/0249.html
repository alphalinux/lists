> Huh. Mine has a SCSI chip on the motherboard.

If you don't believe me, try hooking up an IDE drive to the 44-pin
mini-IDE connector on the motherboard yourself. Compile the kernel with
the nominal IDE driver, and boot, and there you are!

The performance of the Multia IDE is disappointing - apparently it is not
an EIDE and can't do different PIO modes. I only got some 1.3MB/sec
transfer speeds with a Quantum Fireball IDE drive. And it also consumed
100% of the CPU time (not nice).

However, the IDE works, it works well (aside of the bad performance). So,
for a cheap storage-like hard drive, the IDE is a good solution.
