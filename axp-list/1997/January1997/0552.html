Greetings-

Some months ago, I was complaining most frequently to this list regarding my
eb164's inability to cope with memory sizes greater than 64 MB.
This problem has been solved -- at least for me, and I wanted to share my
experience with you all.

Seems that my SROM was a rev. 1.14, labelled DAF2. This is the IC at U12.
I finally recieved a revision 2.1 SROM (which was labelled 2.1, handwritten)
which has completely cleared up all problems associated with my 128MB being
used in it's entirety.

Unfortunately, this SROM also made the ARC flake -- if I attempt to boot the
ARC console, I get dumped to the debug console. This may be a simple upgrade
the ARC in flash issue, I don't know, as I really like the SRM for booting, and
since I happen to have SRM in flash, it's all I use.

Hope this is somewhat illuminating for those eb164 users that are stymied by
constant crashes and an (alt-F7) console filled with garbage memory addresses
and constant complaints about kernel paging requests bombing, and (ick) the
incessant threat of sudden catastrophic crash. Glad it's over for me.
