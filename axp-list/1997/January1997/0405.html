>>> "Ross A. Knepper" said:
>
> i managed to get /dev/audio to make noise. a few more questions:
>
> 1. it is constantly making a sort of hissing noise, whenever the machine
> is running in linux. like static on the radio. any idea whats wrong
> here?

As someone else said, adjust (minimize) "line2", this seems to reduce the
amount of distortion. I've used "aumix" and "xmixer", but the easiest one
to use in script files (for boot-up) is "mixer", which comes as part of the
sound-utils package; last one I used was snd-utils-3.5.tgz or some such,
prolly off of sunsite, IIRC.

> 2. /dev/dsp still does not seem to work. at least, that's what quake
> says.

Um, does it's protection allow user read/write? CHMOD it to 666...

> 3. where could i find something interesting to do with audio, like a
> modplayer or something? or even to play samples. cat > /dev/audio just
> makes garbage noise.

I had some luck a while ago with "vplay", for WAV files and such, which came
with the above-mentioned snd-utils-3.5 package.

Also, for MOD files, "tracker" seems to work well, and look into "timidity"
for playing MIDI files on soundcards without FM synths, ie the MSS on UDB.
You've got to download a ton of GUS sample files, as the program uses them
to synthesize an appropriate waveform and pump it out through the DSP. Works
a treat, and *much* better sounding than FM synth!
