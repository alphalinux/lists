On Tue, 7 Jan 1997, Steven N. Hirsch wrote:

> Harry McKee wrote:
> >
> > I just picked up the RedHat 4.0 CD with a new Multia and tried installing.
> > Everything went well until I tried to boot the kernel off of sda1. After
> > the root drive was mounted, the linux failed with a message of "unable
> > to open a initial console."
>
> It sounds like the install failed to create the necessary device(s) for
> console IO. Boot from the installation diskette and follow the
> instructions through the point where it asks you to specify the location
> of the CD. Then, switch VT's to the second console and mount your Linux
> root partition by hand:
>
> # mount -t ext2 /dev/sda1 /mnt
>
> cd into /mnt/dev and look for tty[0-5] and console. If they are NOT
> there, use mknod to create them, or copy them from the "live"
> subdirectory on the CD.
>
> Steve

Thanks. I switched VT's but could not use the mount command or look into
the CD's subdirectory. For some reason the mount command is not there.
