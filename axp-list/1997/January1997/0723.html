> On Fri, 24 Jan 1997, Steven N. Hirsch wrote:

> > - Whenever the machine is cold-started in the ARC console, it complains
> > about incorrect CMOS information which will prevent me from running NT
> > (not that I'd ever want to), and suggests that I use the Boot setup menu
> > to correct it. Unfortunately, there is absolutely nothing wrong in the
> > boot setup. I've redone it several times, and Linux starts up perfectly!
> > What is causing this time wasting message? How can I rid myself of it?
>
> What is the message, specifically, and what are the attributes of the boot

Well, that's just the problem! It pops up a blue warning box, which
states that anything highlighted in yellow on the menu has erroneous data.
The only selection that is yellow is boot setup, where you configure the
console's choice of boot files.

Unfortunately, once in this mode it fails to show anything in particular
as being wrong! I even went so far as to wipe everything and re-enter
from scratch - same results.

The box always disappears on its own after 10 seconds, or so, and the
system works 100% reliably. It's simply annoying to have banners with
"NT" all over them shoved in my face on every bootup! I guess this is
Microsoft's way of punishing me for daring to use the ARC console without
an accompanying NT installation :-).

I downloaded a different package for firmware updates from Digitals ftp
site. Turns out that the update utilities for the axppci33 do _not_ work
on a UDB (despite statements made in the docs). There is a specific
package called udbupd38.img which finally did the trick.
