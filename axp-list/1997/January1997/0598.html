>>> Harald Koenig said:
>
> is this price without any OS or will a Linux-only box even cheaper ?

This is with NT, the only way the box is currently being sold.

> how much is SDRAM upgrade (128/256/512M) ?
> what's the max. SDRAM size ?

There are 6 DIMM slots, must be filled in pairs (banks). Current DIMMs are
limited by SDRAM densities; typically, 64Mb DIMMs are readily available,
although 128Mb ones are starting to appear. The board specs say, that it
should be possible to put 1Gb DIMMs in (6Gb total), when the chip densities
get there. Is that enough for you? :-)

> > Single chip core logic chipset (PYXIS)
>
> hmm, what about Linux support ?

It's there, with a few "gotchas"; I installed Red Hat 4.0 on one, using a
custom kernel, and even ran XF86_S3 with no problems... :-)

The "gotchas":

1. there's currently no MILO for it; I have patches for running under SRM
console only, at the moment, and SRM/Digital UNIX is not currently being
sold. It's possible that a current MILO might work (PC164?), but there's
no guarantee that it won't take a while to get a working one.

2. the Ethernet chipset is DEC's DC21142, which, AFAIK, is not currently
supported by any of the LINUX drivers. Not so different from the others,
but just enough... :-(

3. the kernel/machine would hang if an IDE disk was plugged in. CMD 646 on
the motherboard, no problems with that on the PC164, so no ideas yet.

We intend to support the DPWa 433/500, aka MIATA, but as yet haven't had
sufficient access to iron out the problems.
