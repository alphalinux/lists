Yash Khemani <khemani@plexstar.com> writes:
> i want to support the redhat effort (and am doing so with my $88 purchase
> of rhl/axp a couple of weeks ago), but i find it difficult to not purchase
> at a significantly lower price. perhaps redhat will consider offering just
> the media less tech support for a lower price...

They do; it's called the Red Hat Archives and sells for around $25. It doesn't
include support or the manual. The current release doesn't include the
Sparc release, but I'm guessing that future releases will.

Even if you want the printed manual (which is less essential now that it
is available online), it is only $20 separately, so if you don't want support
you can get Red Hat/AXP with manual for $45, and you get Red Hat/Intel and
various other archives thrown in as a bonus.

I think the Archives disk contains the complete Red Hat/AXP release, but
if not, hopefully someone from Red Hat can tell us exactly which pieces
are missing. The Red Hat/Intel in the archives is definitely missing
some of the value-added software like Metro-X and Red Baron, but I've never
found that to be much of a limitation, and those programs aren't available
for Red Hat/AXP at all.
