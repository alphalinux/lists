Michael Champigny <champign@cadtls.hlo.dec.com>
> I would like a 4MB card which supports 16M colors at 1280x1024. Has
> anyone got such a card working on the UDB, and if so which XFree driver
> did you use? Performance isn't such a concern (I know the TGA is fast),
> but I need COLORS!

The non-Virge S3 chips are the best supported under XFree on the Alpha.
I recommend the Number Nine Motion 771 w/ 4 MB of VRAM:
http://www.nine.com/products/771/new.html

List price is around $400.

I don't think you'll find a 4M card that can do 1280x1024x24; most cards
need 32bpp to do 24-bit color, because they don't do packing. Even if
you find a card that can do packing, I don't think XFree will support it.

However, the 771 is capable of:
1280x1024x16 up to 110 Hz
1152x864x24 up to 140 Hz
1024x768x24 up to 150 Hz

XFree86 V3.1.2 doesn't automatically detect that this card needs an
inverted clock, and you will get strange artifacts on the right side of the
screen if you don't tell it. From the XFree86 V3.1.2 release notes for
the S3 driver:

Number Nine FX Motion 771

s3RefClk 16
DACspeed 220

This card may require the line:

Invert_VCLK "*" 0

in each Display subsection.

I've heard conflicting reports over whether the Invert_VCLK is needed with
XFree86 V3.2, or whether it is detected automatically.

I'm using mine with a Nokia Multigraph 445Xi, and it's great. Let me know
if you'd like a copy of my XF86Config.
