Aggressive Digital Pricing Dramatically Increases Affordability
of Alpha Computer Line

Workstation and Server Prices Reduced Up To 42 and 47 Percent,
Respectively

New Server Configurations Quadruple Memory Capacity at Minimal Cost

MAYNARD, Mass., Jan. 9 /PRNewswire/ -- Digital Equipment Corp today
reduced prices by as much as 47
percent on its industry- leading 64-bit AlphaServer systems, 42 percent
on its powerful AlphaStation
workstations, and 55 percent on memory options. The pricing reflects
intensified competitive positioning
for DIGITAL's workstation products, and volume manufacturing and
lower-priced memory for its
AlphaServer line.

DIGITAL also introduced new base-level server configurations that
provide as much as four times the
memory of current systems at little or no extra cost.

"Today's aggressive price reductions and new base-level system
configurations enable even more
customers in a broader range of markets to leverage the breakthrough
performance and
price/performance advantages of the Alpha platform," said Harry
Copperman, vice president and general
manager, Systems Business Unit. "They will benefit from the power of
complete 64-bit solutions that
include very-large-memory (VLM) capability, exceptional scalability, and
protection from costly
migration." Pricing highlights include:

-- Midrange AlphaServer 4000 5/300 system (used for line-of-business,

Internet/intranet, LAN, and departmental applications) now priced at

$31,500 with 512 MB of memory, a 47 percent savings. System has

capacity for two 300 MHz Alpha processors and up to 4 GB of 64-bit

VLM64. Similar savings now available on the higher-capacity

AlphaServer 4000 5/400 and 4100 systems.

-- AlphaServer 8200 5/440 system reduced 20 percent to $224,400 with 2
GB

memory. Similarly, flagship AlphaServer 8400 5/440 system is now

$344,400 with 2 GB memory, a 14 percent reduction.

-- Pricing for low-end AlphaServer 1000A 5/333 with 256 MB memory
reduced

12 percent to $13,945.

-- DIGITAL memory now available for as little as $19/megabyte, making it

among the most aggressively priced memory in the industry. AlphaServer

systems are now configured with up to four times previous memory at

little or no increase in price.

-- AlphaStation 600/333, with DIGITAL UNIX, 128 MB memory, 2 GB disk,

21-inch monitor, and PowerStorm 4D40T graphics, priced at $24,995, a

savings of 42 percent.

-- AlphaStation 500/500, the world's fastest desktop workstation, with

DIGITAL UNIX, 128 MB memory, 2 GB disk, 21-inch monitor, and PowerStorm

4D40T graphics, reduced 20 percent to $39,995.

-- AlphaStation 500/333, with DIGITAL UNIX, 128 MB memory, 2 GB disk,
21-

inch monitor, and PowerStorm 4D40T graphics, now priced 20 percent

lower at $21,995.

-- Entry-level AlphaStation 255/300 workstation, with DIGITAL UNIX, 64
MB

memory, 1 GB disk, 17-inch monitor, and entry-level graphics, now

priced at $9,995, a savings of 28 percent.

Server pricing changes are effective worldwide; workstation pricing in
U.S. and Europe.

Larger Memory Systems

"Lower memory prices are fueling the trend toward larger-memory
systems," said Pauline Nist, vice
president of DIGITAL's AlphaServer Business Segment. "Customers are
becoming aware of the dramatic
performance and scaling gains when larger portions of database-intensive
applications are accessed
directly from memory. Very-Large-Memory AlphaServer systems, combined
with 64-bit addressing and
raw speed, give customers a superior, high-performance computing
platform."

The new prices make the AlphaServer 4100 systems -- the first midrange
systems to offer VLM -- 34
percent more affordable than a comparable Sun UltraEnterprise 4000
system. The AlphaServer 1000A
system is now priced to compete head-on with the Compaq 1500. And
DIGITAL's high-quality memory is
now price-competitive with any other memory in the industry.

Desktop Visual Computing Excellence

"Reducing AlphaStation prices is a key element of DIGITAL's continued
drive to provide desktop visual
computing excellence to our customers," said Philippe Ribeyre, vice
president of DIGITAL's Workstation
Business Segment. "Today, we deliver the world's fastest UNIX and
Windows NT 3D workstations at a
fraction of competitors' prices."

As a result of the pricing reductions, an AlphaStation 500/333 delivers
up to 21 percent more
performance at 21 percent less cost than an HP C160L-Vis24, and up to 20
percent better performance
than a comparably priced Sun UltraSPARC 170E.

AlphaServer systems and AlphaStation workstations are based on the
powerful, reliable, and affordable
64-bit Alpha RISC architecture. DIGITAL provides its customers all the
benefits of the company's strategic
partnerships with leading software vendors, and a clear path to the
future without costly and
time-consuming migrations.

Digital Equipment Corporation is a world leader in open client/server
solutions, from personal computing
to integrated worldwide information systems. DIGITAL's scalable Alpha
and Intel platforms, storage,
networking, software and services, together with industry-focused
solutions from business partners, help
organizations compete and win in today's global marketplace.
