Hi

I just wanted to thank all of you who responded to my questions. In summary,
you DO need the /etc/nsswitch.conf file for the machine to come up
properly. I eventually compiled the latest kernel (2.0.27), and - cross
my fingers - the machine seems to be pretty stable so far. The most
noticeable improvement is in the scsi (aic7xxx) behaviour, as I have even
been able to back up my disk to the exabyte tape without kernel errors
(like attempting to free non-kmalloced memory). I hope that the scsi
problems remain a thing of the past, and that we can all get new confidence
on the aic7xxx driver (which is really stable on my pentium box).

So, Microway please take note, and for next time supply your machines with
RedHat 4.0 and kernel 2.0.27 or better. It would save everybody a lot
of grief (even if it makes you learn so many new tricks :-).

Hopefully I will be able to get some work done now!

On another topic: Does anyone have any
comments on the stability of g++ and libg++ on the alpha? This is my
workhorse on the pentium, and I have already encountered some problems in
porting to the alpha, which admittedly may be due to my code (some 200K
lines), but I am not sure.

Daniel
