Not really, Gregory. The new Alpha "A" Workstations have a new chipset and
memory architecture that is quite nice. My comments were not meant to be
derogatory in any sense other than price.
You are quite right with your observation of pricing. Enorex, and many of
the other Alpha vendors (ourselves included of course!) have systems
available right now that deliver performance that is nothing short of
breathtaking.
I had to laugh the other evening. I was watching television, and an ad came
on for the NASDAQ exchange. They were featuring Intel, and one line amused
me greatly. It went something like this: " And by the year 2000 they will
make a processor that does 2 billion instructions per second !"

Funny, I thought that we had that right now with the Alpha 21164 / 500MHz!

Of course Digital may not be a NASDAQ stock... ;-}

All I was trying to point out in my earlier posting is that there has been a
rumour floating around this list that a fully configured system was
available in the "A" series for around $5000. Upon investigation it was my
observation that this base system was somewhat limited in many ways.
