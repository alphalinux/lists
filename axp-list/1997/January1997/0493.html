On Jan 13,  3:59pm, Liam Irish \(UND\) wrote:
> Subject: ah, yes, kernel compilation
>
> I have a Multia 233, standard NCR810 and 500 MEG (Toshiba?) SCSI HArd
> drive.  I've compiled several kernels, but I can't seem to get the scsi
> options right.  A few days back, there was a listing of how to do this, I
> tried it, and it didn't work either.  Currently, I boot the new kernel, it
> goes along fine until it gets to the scsi code, at which point it fails to
> get shared interrupt 11.  Then when it tries to mount the HD, the drive
> changes pitches.  I could use the correct options, and does anyone know
> why it exhibits this strange behaviour?


This usually means you have sound compiled into the kernel... the configuration
program puts the sound at irq 11, which is wrong, wrong, wrong.


The attachment, .soundconf (in your home directory when you run "make config")
has the right sound settings for a UDB. Cheers!
