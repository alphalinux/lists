> Im running RH 4.0 on an UDB with 24+16 Mb Ram and the system is not 100%
> stable. I'm experiencing intermittent errors during compilation of for
> example a kernel. Sometimes gcc exits with an internal error. (2)
>
> Als the SCSI system looks flaky, when I connect a jaz drive to the UDB
> and start some heavy copying the SCSI bus locks up and the system is
> dead

Most likely what you see here are not two separate problems but two
manifestations of the same one. Alphas seem to be quite sensitive to
problems on SCSI bus. Cables **and** termination which work with Intel
based system are often not good enough for Alpha. An active terminator
may help. Good quality and short cables are important. Some controller
drivers, like NCR8xx as opposed to an older "7,8", are possibly better
at handling error conditions but the general principle still applies.

As for compilations - beyond obvious problems when data, or swaped
in pages, do not contain what they were supposed to contain due to
SCSI errors, I/O troubles seem to mess up what is in a memory as well.
