Subj: Whim: anyone for primes?

Okay, I'm off on another wild hare...

There's an effort underway to find all the Mersenne Prime numbers with
an exponent less than 1,345,000 by the end of '97, and below 2,655,000
by the end of 2000.

The effort is spearheaded by some Intel PC geeks (I use the term in the
nicest way -- their site was awarded "Geek Site Of The Day on June 20,
1996! ;^) who want to prove that "the power of hundreds of small
computers like yours can be used to solve seemingly intractable
problems"...

I figure with the 1000+ Alphas associated with this mail list, we might
be able to bump those dates up by, oh, a factor of 4 or more! (Wouldn't
it be great if they had to admit they bettered their dates because of a
bunch of Alphas!!!)

There's code for '95, NT, and UNIX available from a couple of sources.
The stuff for Pentium systems is heavily optimized assembler in all the
right spots. There's an effort underway to create an NT service version
(so you don't have to leave your workstation or server logged in all the
time). There's also plenty of raw source code for a large variety of
other platforms.

Perhaps if some enterprising folks are interested, the code could be
ported to and optimized for Alpha. We (the mail list) could then start
grabbing big blocks of numbers to check and start cranking out prime
numbers like crazy!

Check out "The Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search" page at:
http://www.mersenne.org/prime.htm

Additional "Mersenne Prime Freeware" is available at:
http://fractal.mta.ca/prime/freeware/

Any takers for the porting job? Hey Digit's -- wanna help? Anyone in
the porting centers available? (I'll bet you've got plenty of Alpha NT
boxes with a few spare CPU cycles -- maybe we could wrap this up in a
few weeks!!!)

Sincerely,
Aaron
