You may get lucky sometimes - as the original author did not really
meant "pointer" when s/he wrote that; and conversions **from** integer
**to** pointer can be often ignored. But converting from a pointer
to integer on axp you are loosing half of yours - possibly significant -
bits. This makes wonders to your references and a stack.

most likely caused by somebody following "just ignore the warnings
on integer <-> pointer conversions" principle.
