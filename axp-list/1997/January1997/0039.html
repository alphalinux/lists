On Wed, 1 Jan 1997, Daniel Gruner wrote:

> Well, I thought that after all the good advice I got on this list I
> would be home free with the upgrade to redhat 4.0 on my machine.
> Needless to say, foolish me! I am really annoyed.
>
> The situation is as follows: I finally had a working machine with
> a kernel (2.0.23) that supported my hardware (AHA2940UW), network
> properly working, etc. The machine was flaky, sometimes panicking on
> the scsi controller, and I couldn't use the serial ports (builtin on
> a pc164), so "foolish me! ! !" I made boot floppies and took the plunge
> to upgrade. It all seemed to go smoothly until reboot time, when I got
> a message:
> /lib/modules/st.mod: No such file or directory
> .
> .
> INIT: Entering runlevel:3
> eth0: media is BNC
>
> and the machine was stuck for some 5 or 10 minutes. I thought it was
> locked for good, so I repeated the install, this time making it a "clean"
> install (not an upgrade), and the same thing. Eventually it timed out,
> but it took about 15 minutes, and kept getting stuck when initializing
> almost every service (at least those that had to do with networking
> (rpc.mountd, rpc.nfsd, sendmail, smbd), and I am still waiting for it
> to finish booting...
>
> I did not have these problems when upgrading my pentium box...
>
> Also, I could not use the "stock" boot images from the redhat disk
> (yes, I am registered and all, but redhat is on holiday...) because
> they do not support my scsi controller, so I am using the boot disk
> I made with a recompiled kernel with the proper devices included.
>
> Does anybody have a "simple" pared down kernel image containing drivers
> for the AHA2940UW that I could use to see if this thing boots without
> wanting to do so much stuff? I guess I could also install a super basic
> system, with no networking or anything like that - I would have long
> finished that test...
>
> And this machine doesn't seem to want to continue booting (still stuck at
> smbd, after 20 minutes or so) - hey! now it is trying nmbd!
>
> Well, some way of telling one's boss about how much work we did during the
> Christmas break. Nobody will believe me that I have been at this for about
> 4 days straight (and fortunately I found this mailing list!).
>
> Any comments/suggestions would be appreciated.

Well, I had some problems upgrading my client's EB164. Two things
that helped were setting the memory ("setenv MEMORY_SIZE X" at the MILO
prompt) to 63MB instead of 128, and fixing the network config (I've
forgotten which file, but something was screwy - I'm not sure if thaqt was
my screw up or redhat's). Also the nsswitch.conf helped other things
later.
