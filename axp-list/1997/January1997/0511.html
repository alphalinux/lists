[ Don't do this trick at home ]

With all of the talk about the more stable ncr5380 driver, I decide to dump the
ol' tested and runs (for me) 53c7,8xx drivers. Don't do this while switching
kernels! As I did, putting 2.1.21 on and installing the new driver.

What was interesting was the system would boot, get to mounting the root FS
read-only, then an exception rang out about the swapper.

After several re-configures (IO_MAPPED on, off, forced_syncs on, off) and
having the thing die at exactly the same spot, I was 1) thankful that I kept
2.1.20
on a boot floppy for easy restarts, and 2) beginning to wonder about the whole
deal. I reconfigured 2.1.21 for the 53c7,8 driver, and hey! it crashes at the
same place. (One less variable)

Right about then, I noticed that the new modutils kernel patches were not
applied. So on a lark, I applied them and recompiled. Presto! Working UDB on
2.1.21. It shouldn't be hard, it generally isn't hard, but the (b)leading edge
stuff does introduce variables at the damnedest of times.

(Well of course I compile some of the kernel as modules; gotta test out
modutils out) Still haven't compiled for the BSD driver... That's next.
