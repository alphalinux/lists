I am seriously considering putting a video card in my UDB's PCI slot.
I would like a 4MB card which supports 16M colors at 1280x1024. Has

I like to use oddball resolutions that happen to match the card memory
and/or the actual physical pixel resolution of the monitor. For 16-bit
color and 2MB card, a 1180x880 mode uses the memory nicely and has the
4/3 aspect ratio. Going to 4MB just to get 1280x1024 somehow
disagrees with my parsimonious nature (it only uses 2.5 MB out of 4 MB).

Such shenanigans may look strange, considering that Windows and most
commercial X servers limit resolution choices to 640x480, 800x600,
1024x768 1280x1024 and 1600x1280; however, this is not the case with
XFree (although I seem to remember that the TGA driver again is
limited to those predefined resolutions).

Note that there may be some performance effects related to non-2^n
scanline size, depending on a chipset; the server should then print a
message on startup mentioning 'disabling this-or-that speedup due to ...'.

BTW, the earlier comment about physical pixel resolution refers to
the fact that a slightly sharper image results if the pixel resolution
of the card matches the number of RGB screen pixels: for instance, on
a 17", 0.26 mm/dot monitor with effective screen width of 13" (345 mm)
has 1330 pixels across its face; therefore, it may make sense to use a
resolution of 1330x1000.
