On 29 Jan 1997, Markus Dickebohm wrote:

> I was really surprised as I saw that the old libc is still installed
> on the system:
>
> # rpm -qa | grep libc
> libcaxp-debug-0.43-1
> libcaxp-profile-0.43-1
> glibc-profile-0.960906-1
> libcaxp-0.43-1
> libcaxp-include-0.43-1
> glibc-static-0.960906-1
> glibc-info-0.960906-1
> glibc-0.960906-1
> glibc-devel-0.960906-1

It's amisfeature of RPM. The files don't really use any space as they've
been overwritten but it does look pretty ugly.
>
> rpm --erase libcaxp-0.43-1
> I will certainly lose for example "/usr/lib/libbsd.a" which is a
> part of "glibc-devel-0.960906-1", too.

No you won't -- RPM realizes that glibc-devel still needs it and will R
leave it around. RPM also knows that the libbsd.a isn't the one libcaxp
needs (rpm -qls libcaxp will illustrate this).
