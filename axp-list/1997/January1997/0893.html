Here you go: ( I was just posting the loop, as this is where almost all
the time is spent)

PROGRAM EULER
DOUBLE PRECISION GAM
GAM = 1.0d0
NMAX=100000000
DO I=2,NMAX
GAM=GAM+1.0d0/dble(I)
ENDDO
GAM = GAM - DLOG(dble(NMAX))
WRITE(*,*) GAM
WRITE(*,*) 'Exact: 0,57721566490153286060'
END


> the speed difference might be caused by the fact that at the default
> optimisation level the Digital Fortran compiler unrolls loops,
> whereas g77/gcc won't do that (because this is a CPU architecture
> dependent optimisation).

Tests were done with -O3 on both gcc and f77. I got on a 433 Mhz 21164/Linux

gcc: 12.09 sec.
f77: 6.80 sec.

On a Pentium 133/Linux I get:

f2c+gcc: 40.58
g77 : 39.09
