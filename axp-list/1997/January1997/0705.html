Hi.

Every day, we get the following mail from cron to root:

Date: Fri, 24 Jan 1997 02:41:00 -0500
From: root@ERDOS.DSM.fordham.edu (Cron Daemon)
To: root@ERDOS.DSM.fordham.edu
Subject: Cron <root@erdos> /usr/sbin/tmpwatch 240 /tmp /var/tmp
X-Cron-Env: <SHELL=/bin/bash>
X-Cron-Env: <PATH=/sbin:/bin:/usr/sbin:/usr/bin>
X-Cron-Env: <MAILTO=root>
X-Cron-Env: <HOME=/root>
X-Cron-Env: <LOGNAME=root>

/bin/bash: /usr/sbin/tmpwatch: Permission denied

The cure is probably simple: It turns out that /usr/sbin/tmpwatch is
not executable, so I would only need to do a chmod +x to same.

Before I do this, however: exactly what does tmpwatch do? There
appears to be no manpage entry.

Thanks.
