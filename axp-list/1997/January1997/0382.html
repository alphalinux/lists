>>> Leslie Donaldson said:
> I was wondering what is the fastest and best graphics card to use
> with an Alpha and Linux. :)
>
> I noticed the drivers that are available are for
> Mach 64
> S3
> TGA
>
> I am trying to spec out a card for a new computer system to be bought
> in a few months. So I am looking for a vide card that can do
> 1280x1024x16mill ( At a reasonable speed)

[Note that the only servers I will talk about are XFree86-based ones; at the]
[moment there are no commercial servers available for Linux on Alpha. ]

S3 and Mach64 are tried-and-true, and the new 3.2 release versions seem to
work well at all depths *except* packed 24-bit; that is 8, 16, and 32, and
15 may work as well. Also, the 3.2 server for Mach64 appears to be much more
stable on EV5/EV56-based machines than *any* earlier version.

The TGA server comes in two flavors: a binary-only version based on 3.1.2,
and one from the 3.2 release. The former is much quicker, the latter has
source code available. Unfortunately, both are 8-bit only.

Also in the 3.2 release, although at release time they were not working on
the Alpha, were the S3V server for S3 ViRGE-based boards, and a Matrox
Millennium server. I subsequently ported the Millennium server to the Alpha,
and made a beta version available on gatekeeper.dec.com as:

pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/X11/XF86_SVGA-3.2-test.gz

Note that this server *requires* being run under a LINUX kernel based on
2.0.27 or 2.1.15 or higher. Also, it appears fairly stable at 8, 16, and 32
bit depths, but *not* packed 24-bit. I am working on porting the new version
currently in beta development by XFree86, and will make such available when
its appropriate. The ViRGE server has also been ported to the Alpha, and the
new beta version should also appear when appropriate. The Intel-based
version supports 8, 16, and 32-bit depths, but I'm not certain at this time
whether all that will be available on the beta version for the Alpha.

If your plan is for a Linux-only system, any of the above satisfying your
depth requirement should be fine.

If you plan to multi-boot with Digital UNIX and/or Windows NT, your choices
become much narrower. If this is true, we can discuss later what the limited
options are.
