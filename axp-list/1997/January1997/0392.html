Hi

Just in case anyone is interested in using Qt 1.1 (a neat C++ class
library for the X-Windows system developed by Troll Tech, Norway). -- I've
successfully compiled Qt under Linux/Alpha. However, I had to apply a tiny
patch to get Qt to recognize double clicks. (Can be found at the end of
that email).

However, I've just uploaded a binary distribution to the incoming
directory of "ftp.troll.no". -- Look out for a file named
"qt-1.1-bin-linux-axp.tar.gz" on their FTP server.

If you are unsure what you could use Qt for, just have a look at KDE, the
"Kool Desktop Environment" that is currently under development, but looks
rather promising IMHO. ( http://www.zws.com/kde/ ) KDE has also been the
reason why I've started tinkering around with Qt in the first place.

KDE compiles fine under Linux/Alpha with only a little source code
tweaking and does not crash more often under Linux/Alpha than under
Linux/intel... ;-)

Qt and its sources are freely available for people writing free software
(like under the GPL) BTW.

happy Linuxing! ;-)

Christian

Disclaimer: I'm not related with Troll Tech in any way. ;-)
