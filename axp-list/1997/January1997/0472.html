At 02:21 AM 1/18/97 -0100, you wrote:

>Oh, BTW: Milo still complains about "NCR dead, restarting", but up to now
>Milo has never failed to load the kernel (off the harddisk) and the
>kernel itself does not complain. I've even been able to enable tagged
>command queuing and disconnect/reconnect (and it works)! ;-)

This appears to be some sort of mismatch between the state the ARC console
leaves the NCR810 and the way the MILO/kernel driver expects it. It doesn't
appear to hurt anything, but probably takes a few seconds longer to boot...

>Concerning the DEC-Tulip ethernet adapter I've followed the advise you've
>been giving me and stayed with the "DE4x5" driver which happily
>transferred the 1.5GB NFS data... -- Suppose I'm not missing that much
>when not runnin the "Tulip" driver?

For the DEC platforms with built-in TULIPs (21040/21041/21140) and those
cards made by DEC using those chips (DE434/435/450/500), the DE4X5 driver
is preferred; it was written, debugged, and tuned by a (former) DEC employee
whose job was that driver (among others).

>And BTW: the graphics adapter in this machine is a Diamond Stealth 64 with
>4MB VRAM and S3-986. -- XFree86 3.1.2 works like a charm... ;-)

I think you mean S3-968, and you ought to give XFree86 3.2 servers a try,
they are nearly all a good bit faster than the older ones...

I'm glad you solved the SCSI problem!
