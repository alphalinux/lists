>I have a DEC UDB running RedHat 4.0 and am having some trouble.
>
>1. I am unable to use a modem with the system.
> I am not using the serial splitter since I only need 1 port.
>
> ppp and minicom communicate qith the modem to some degree but
> are unable to dial and I am not able to type in modem AT commands
> from minicom and get any response from the modem.
>
> The modem is configured and running properly. I tested it on a
> Pentium system under windows and Linux and it worked great.

Although the manual states that you can connect your modem with a normal
straight-through 25-pin serial cable to the port I have found this to not
be the case.

I'm using a Motorola Bitsurfr and connecting it with a straight through
cable causes all sorts of problems. Connecting it to one leg of the "Y"
splitter cable solved all of the problems.

This may be unique to the Bitsurfr and its ilk because they are
synchronous devices and the signals on the "unused" pins might have
screwed up the works, a "normal" modem might not be bothered by sinals on
these pins.

The only easy way to test this would be to connect a "Y" cable but I
think DEC wants around $75 for this (I made my own from the docs in the
manual).

Another approach might be to simply disconnect the conductors that are
not used for port 1, these being identified by the "_2" suffix in the
manual - they would be pins:

11, 12, 13, 14, 16, 19, 23 and 25

You could un-solder, cut or otherwise disable them, or use a breakout box
to open them.

Anyway, just something to try that MIGHT help.

Can't help you with your SAMBA problem, I use NFS to connect to my '95
machine, sorry.
