>
> The box was quite happily running RH 3.0.3 (with about 1 crash once a
> week under heavy load).

I would call that rather flaky. :-)

> 1) Is there a known Problem with EB164+NCR53C810+"new" NCR driver?

Not that I am aware of. I was using using this driver on EB164
machine with 433 MHz chip which was accidentally overclocked to 500 MHz.
This should not work at all. :-)

Curiously enough it was misbehaving with #9 S3 video card in it. It was
totally locking up when I tried to run 3.2 X server, but IO was also
affected - even if not to that extent :-). Switching to a "regular"
Diamond Stealth not only made X happy, but also substantially improved
everything. There are some weird interactions there which I do not
understand (conflicts on PCI bus?). X 3.1.2 worked with #9 - to a degree.

> 2) Does upgrading the kernel (stock RH 2.0.18 -> 2.0.27) have any
> chance to fix the problem?

Stock RH 2.0.18 uses an old "7,8" driver. Recompiling a kernel was
one of my first actions after RH 4.0 was installed. It was
an "interesting" exercise but I do not know how to aportion a blame
between a wrong crystal, an "old" NCR driver and a video card.

> 3) Is there a newer version of the NCR driver available somewhere on
> the Net that might do the trick?

There is, but before you will go to a "bleeding edge" maybe you should
try 2.0.28 with its released "new" NCR driver.

> Concerning the other hardware: The box also has one of those "DEC Tulip"
> Ethernet cards.

Tulip drivers is a story on its own. Before you decide to use them
read carefuly what you will find about them in Documentation subdir
in kernel sources, in drivers/net/README.de4x5 and in net archives.
You may want to try DE4x5 driver instead. It may even work. :-)

> Milo (2.0.18) also gives me the infamous "NCR dead"
> message every now and then.

Alphas seem to be much more sensitive on a quality of cabling and
termination on SCSI bus than Intel boxes. Checking that connections
are tight for sure will not harm you. :-)
