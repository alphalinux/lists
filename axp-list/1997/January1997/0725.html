"Andrew Williams" <awilliam@nortel.ca> wrote:
> Has anyone tried putting a 28.8 (or similar) PCMCIA modem into
> a UDB ??
> Q1: Were you successfull? (if no then ignore the rest of these)

Yes, with Red Hat/AXP 3.0.3

> Q2: How much effort was involved?

Not much after I found that one of the packages installed a bogus
/usr/lib/libbfd.h. After that was fixed everything built cleanly and
worked without causing core dumps.

> Q3: Are you satisfied with the end result?

Yes.

> Q4: Would you recommend the PCMCIA route versus an external
> modem?

Depends entirely on your objective. An external is generally cheaper.

> Q5: Can you summarize the steps involved?

For Red Hat 3.0.3:

1. Fix the libbfd.h thing. They have it in two different RPMs, and one
is broken. The correct one in the binutils RPM.

2. Build the PCMCIA package as per its instructions.

For Red Hat 4.0 it is much more of a pain in the posterior, because 4.0/Alpha
doesn't come with module support, and the PCMCIA package won't build properly.
Info has been posted to this list previously about how to solve both problems,
but I haven't tried it as 2.0.18 doesn't work reliably for me anyhow.

Maybe if we're lucky the next release of Red Hat/Alpha will include module and
PCMCIA support.

Cheers,
Eric
