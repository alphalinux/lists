Until I started upgrading my UDB from 3.0.3 to 4.0 today, I had a
functioning system (isn't that right Brian?).

A bit of background to set the scene.

I have the 2 CD-ROM 4.0 Alpha Linux set from Redhat and started the upgrade
today. Made the linload and milo disk. Milo was copied from the CD-ROM.
Used rawrite to copy \images\noname.img and \images\ramdisk.img to two
diskettes. Then I shutdown my operational 3.0.3 system.

I rebooted the system, got to the console and changed the boot option from
the hard disk to a floppy. Rebooted from the floppy and got to the RedHat
installation prompts that ask me if i have a color monitor and from what
media I want to install, and whether I want to upgrade from 3.0.3 or do a
fresh install. I chose to do an upgrade from a local CD-ROM and started the
installation process. For the next half hour, various files were copied
from the CD-ROM onto my HD. Once all of the files were copied i got a
message like (don't recall it EXACTLY and can't back to it!) "put your boot
floppy in and press return." Did it and for about an hour waited. Nothing
happened and all I had was a blue screen with a bit of verbage at the top
and bottom of the page.

Not a big deal, I thought, I'll just start over with Milo from the floppy
and do a fresh install vice the upgrade. Wrong!

Here is the problem as it currently stands.

I get to the console and boot from the floppy. Tells me this Milo is built
for 2.0.18. I quess this is correct. I'm assuming Redhat put compatible
Milo and Kernels on the CD-ROM. At the milo prompt, I place the boot floppy
in and boot it. Milo responds with VFS: Disk change detected on device 2/0
and loads the noname.img (I get the ###'s and various info about hardware,
etc.).

Next i get the prompt
VFS: insert root floppy and press enter.

I do this and a) get a kernel unaligned trap error message (can post if
needed), b) get a transaction block size=512 message and then
c) Kernel panic:VFS:unable to mount root fs on 02:00

at that point, everything is frozen and I have to cycle the power.
This whole process has happened at least 5 times - I can't get any farther.

I've re-rawritten the noname.img and ramdisk.img several times to no avail.

Now, not only do I not have my 3.0.3, I can't even get past the floppies to
do another install.

Suffice it to say I would appreciate ANY help.

TIA
