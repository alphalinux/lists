"Brian E.W. Wood" <beww@intac.com> wrote:
> You need a "crossover" cable to directly connect two 100-base-T (or
> 10-base-T) network cards as opposed to the "straight-through cable"
> normally used to connect one of these cards to a hub.
>
> If you are making the cable yourself using the "flat" type wire and
> modular connectors the crossover is accomplished by flipping over one of
> the connectors before crimping it on so that the wire order is reversed.

Unfortunately this won't work for two reasons:

1) The wire order is *not* the reverse. The 1-2 and 3-6 pairs need to
be exchanged. If you just put a twist in the cable, 1-2 will be swapped
with 7-8, and 3-6 will just be flipped to 6-3.

2) 100-base-T will not work with flat cable. In *needs* twisted pairs.
Even 10-base-T needs twisted pairs, although very short lengths of
flat cable may work in some cases (with reduced reliability).
