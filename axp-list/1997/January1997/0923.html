I was looking at caldera's webpage & noticed some security schtuff that
people may want to keep in mind if they use their boxen in a production
environment.
Some of this may not apply to 4.0 but it will be a reminder for those who
still run 3.0.3, and for those who are putting together 4.1

I'm not sure what version of Perl is going into the next version of
RedHat Linux, or even what was in 4.0, but 3.0.3 has a SUID security bug
that looks like a Bad Thing.

http://www.caldera.com/tech-ref/cnd-1.0/security/SA-96.02.html
Gives some details & a pointer to a patch

Actually, since Caldera is a Red Hat based setup;
http://www.caldera.com/tech-ref/cnd-1.0/security/
Gives a nice & general list of known vulnerabilities.

A more arcane potential for hackers on your system is exploiting a race
condition on /bin/(u)mount
Get rid of the SUID bit; chmod 755 /bin/mount /bin/umount

There is also a problem with sendmail; but sendmail is always a problem
(I have to figure out how to use procmail as client; might be a better
choice for default rpm).

The crontab may also be a bad thing as it comes configured.
