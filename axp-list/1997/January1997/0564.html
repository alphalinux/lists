About:
>
> > mt -f /dev/st<whatever fits> setblk 0 ?
>
> I don't understand. Is this command necessary with _all_ tape drives on
> the alpha, or only tape drives attached to the 53c8xx driver?

Neither. This is needed by drives (hardware) which use variable
block size. Typically DATs. Some software/hardware/tape combination
may work without that, some may not. It may not apply in your situation
but this is the most popular failure mode I have seen.

> Wouldn't it be better to make the driver set this up itself instead of
> having to do it manually?

It does seem to reset itself to defaults every time you unload a tape.
Make an alias. As for making drivers smarter - that's not me. :-)
Check status with mt to see what computer thinks about your tape.
