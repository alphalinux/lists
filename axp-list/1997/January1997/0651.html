On 22 Jan 97 at 22:20, axp-list@redhat.com wrote:

> On Thu, 16 Jan 1997 16:03:04 -0500 Shane Anderson wrote:
> >
> > You've all seen the messages from quite a few people complaining that
> > the /usr partition is busy at shutdown and cannot be umounted... I
> > have that same problem and am curious if there's a know solution.
> > I've shutdown to single user and killed all the processes by hand,
> > but it still shows up as busy when I 'halt' so I'm guessing that it's
> > somewheres in the scripts that it runs/uses something from /usr/bin
> > or /usr/*something*... Any solutions?
>
> We have the same problem as well with Red Hat 4.0 on a PC164 Alpha
> board, where the /usr partition (hda5 in our case) does not unmount
> and consequently a check is forced at every boot up, which is actually
> time consuming and annoying. Does have any idea why the system might
> think there is something using /usr or why the system cannot kill all
> processes sucessfully at shutdown?

Well, here's the solutions I've seen so far:

>From Matthew X. Economou: (which works great for me)

The fix is to do the following:

cp /usr/lib/libnss_{db,dns,files}-1.93.so /lib
ldconfig -v
rm -f /usr/lib/libnss*

And the latest one:

Remount the partition as read-only during shutdown. I imagine this
sets the clean bin (so there's no fsck on bootup) but it doesn't
seem very clean to me since it prolly still gives the /usr
partition busy error.

Anyways, moving the libnss* libs from /usr/lib to /lib works great
for me. I now happily reboot without waiting for a rather lengthy
fsck... :-) Thanks Matt!
