Well, rpms for glibc 1.99 are available in the the Redhat devel tree, that is
probably the easiest way to upgrade, but it is not for the faint of heart. You
must replace several other packages. There is a previous e-mail on this list
detailing the procedure to take, I beleive it is titled Redhat 4.0 NIS HOWTO.
You can skip the step of intalling glibd-nis-1.99.tar.gz and the yp packages.
I would search the axp-list archive at www.redhat.com.

Anyway, it worked for me, although it is *not* recommended, and is at your own
risk to do so. You may end up with a seriously broken system.

Just a question, why do you need a development kernel? If you are upgrading
from 2.0.18, then I would stick with 2.0.27 unless you are prepared for other
things not working. For instance, there are changes to the network code that
require a different ordering of arguments to ifconfig that will break the
network scripts in RedHat. Unless you are prepared to hack away at your
system, I would stay with the release kernels.
