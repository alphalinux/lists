On Wed, 15 Jan 1997, Ross A. Knepper wrote:

>
>
> hi again

> i managed to get /dev/audio to make noise. a few more questions:
>
> 1. it is constantly making a sort of hissing noise, whenever the machine
> is running in linux. like static on the radio. any idea whats wrong
> here?

I'm experiencing that problem too. Here is how to avoid it:

1) start "aumix" (at the console) or "xmixer" (when running X) and turn
off the input channel labeled "Line2".

> 2. /dev/dsp still does not seem to work. at least, that's what quake
> says.

AFAIK, for some reason quake also needs read access to "/dev/dsp". So do
the following:

+) log in as root
+) chmod a+rw /dev/dsp

But keep in mind that this is a security hazard. (If you are in a network
and a microphone is connected to your UDB someone could use that to
overhear you...)

> 3. where could i find something interesting to do with audio, like a
> modplayer or something? or even to play samples. cat > /dev/audio just
> makes garbage noise.

Most of the things you need come with RH 4.0 AFAIK. There is "tracker", a
MOD player (I think it was part of 3.0.3, so it is probably also included
in 4.0), wavplay (plays all these .wav files ;-) -- it needs a small patch
to work properly. If you'd like to have that patch, I could email it to
you). -- For more details you could have a look at the Sound Playing
Howto. -- If you have installed the respective packet, you can find it
under

/usr/doc/HOWTO/Sound-Playing-HOWTO.gz

on your machine.

hope this helps
