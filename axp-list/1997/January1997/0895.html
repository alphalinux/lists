On Tue, 28 Jan 1997, Gavin L Webb wrote:

> I got the UDB Service Manual as Jay said. He also pointed out
> that DEC ships the machines set to 1024x768.
>
> So I opened up my UDB to set it to 800x600 @ 60Hz. The only
> labelled jumper is W2.
>
> Next to the VRAM and VDAC, just above the Ethernet address ROM is a block
> of
> 4 jumpers, which I assumed must be W3-W6 the SRM console video mode
> jumpers. So I decided to test my UDB on a Sony monitor that can handle the
> various TGA modes to check what the current settings were. As Jay said it
> was
> set to the VESA 1024x768 mode at 70Hz, the monitor confirmed this.
>
> Now according to the manual VESA 1024x768 has W3, W4 and W6 installed. Yet
> only two of the four jumpers were set. I added a third jumper and got
> 1024x864
> which should be two jumpers.
>
> Have I got a non-standard UDB? Or am I fiddling with the wrong jumpers?
> Is there a motherboard schematic available?

I'm glad someone else brought this point up! While I found the Digital
Service manual (and similar information on a friendly Web site) quite
informative, I cannot find a single pictorial denoting:

- Jumper identification and pin numbering
- SIMM socket bank identification

Basically, it doesn't help to know that the Digital 12MB simms can go
only in Bank 0 and 1 if one has no idea which sockets are 0 and 1 :-).

Can someone from Digital please provide a map?

Steve
