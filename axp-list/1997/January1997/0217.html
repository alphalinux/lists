Statically linked binaries are easy. You don't have to do anything.
They just run. Actually you probably have to compile the kernel with
a.out support but this is probably done by default.

Dynamically linked binaries also 'just run' but you have to have all
the Digital Unix shared libs and dynamic loader installed. This
basically means copying /sbin/loader and whatever shared libs are needed
into /lib or /usr/lib.

Some Digital Unix binaries look for file that are no on linux machines,
for example /etc/svc.conf. You will need to copy these files also.

Someone recently posted a list of the files necessary for getting
netscape to work.

To do all this you need a Digital Unix license for the machine.
