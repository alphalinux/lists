My development machine arrived thursday, another UDB (multia) with 48 megs
of ram, and WIN NT installed on the first two partitions. Thats when the
problems began. I repartitioned the drive, and erased the previous
version of Redhat (very early not very nice, not a lot of disc space)
that was running on the system. The machine had originally
6 partitions, I cut that down to 4,

Partition number
1 30 megs FAT
2 340 megs NTFS (NT files system, seen as os2/HPfs by linux setup)
3 100 megs Linux Swap
4 540 megs Linux native

I ran the boot and root disks. Did the install. Everything seems to go
OK, it sees the partitions correctly and labels the linux partition
sda3 and sda4. It runs through the process, installs, and then hangs
on the last screen (hit enter to reboot) At this point you need to
physically shut off the machine and restart it. On restart, it loads
milo, but never finishes booting, only displays the following messages

Partition check
sda : sda1 sda2 <sda5>

error failed to open
device 0x0804 result=-6

WHAT??? There is no sda5. I'm a bit baffled as it seems to "see" the
partitions correctly in the "install" program, but not after booting.

Win NT sees the partitions as they should be (2 unkown partitions of the
correct size)
