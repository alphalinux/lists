Some of you folks have been talking about MILO lately and it started
me thinking about what MILO is and why it's that way. I'm confused.
:)

It seems to me that MILO is in all practicality a 'bootable' image a
lot like the kernel. So why not just make the kernel 'bootable' by
linload.exe like MILO is? I certainly agree the MILO is slick in
that you can boot from EXT2, ISO9660, etc. filesystems, but since
we're stuck with that stupid FAT for booting anyway, wouldn't it have
been a lot easier to just put kernels there and use a linload.exe
like program to boot it?

BTW: I'm not complaining. The MILO 'shell' is handy since it give
you opportunity to change boot params and do dirs and so forth...

Just another 2 cents worth...
