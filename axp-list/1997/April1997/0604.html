I had the same problem, the image of the milo floppy doesn't work.
Format a DOS floppy, then manually copy linload and milo onto it from
the Milo dirctory. This will work.
When you'll get the Milo prompt, you'll be able to boot stright onto
the CD, that means the Milo floppy is the only floppy you'll have to
generate and use.
milo> boot -t iso9660 scd0:/kernels/noname.gz root=/dev/scd0
The floppy unit is seen as a 2.88M unit, simply don't care about it.
