Ross A. Knepper wrote:
>
> hmmm ... well i found a pretty major bug, but im not sure if its
> em86 or netscrape. em86 will run simple static programs just fine.
> a.out doesnt work even though i copied over the a.out libs and
> reran ldconfig86. maybe that's not supposed to work?

No, it's not supposed to work 8-) EM86 only supports ELF executables.
I could add a.out support if folks feel there's a burning need for
it, but seeing as much of the world is migrating to ELF I figured
I'd target that first...

So: do folks think I should add a.out support as well?

> anyway, the real bug is when i run netscape. 3.0 just gives me a
> signal 7, while 4.0b3 forks dozens of copies of itself, bringing
> even a PC164 366MHz to a crawl.

Hmmm... I've never had this happen. You may have a shared library
somewhere that confuses em86. Try blowing away /usr/x86 and
doing a clean reinstall of em86-bin.tar.gz and em86-libs.tar.gz

If the problems persist, let me know.
