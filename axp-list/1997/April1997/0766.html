Hi,

A few weeks ago I asked whether anyone had a modified tga.c with a
higher horizontal scan rate to work with a big fixed-frequency
workstation-type monitor. I didn't get any positive replies, but some
other people were interested as well.

Anyway, I spent today fiddling with tga.c, and I have a slightly
modified version that supports 1024x768 at either 60 or 70 Hz, depending
on which PLLbits line you uncomment. Default is 60 Hz.

This works fine on my monitor at both frequencies. If you want to try
it out, please give me some feedback on how well it works with your setup.

Right now, it gives you an 80x25 screen in the top left-hand corner of
the monitor.

Also, X doesn't restore the TGA to the right frequency when it exits.
Since I'm using the binary-only 3.1.2, this may be a problem.

Anyway, hope this helps. You can get it at
http://fizz.phys.dal.ca/~pchapman/tga.c and it replaces
/usr/src/linux/drivers/char/tga.c.

Disclaimer: I disclaim all responsibility for anything nasty this file
might do to your computer. Use at your own risk. I have tested it with
my monitor and it works okay, but make sure your monitor will support the
above resolutions and refresh rates before using this!
