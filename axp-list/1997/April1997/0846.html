Well I seem to have a bit of a problem. Recently somebody was 'playing' around
with the ARC console on my udb (VX40B-F2) and now I don't get any video. My
monitor reports that there is no signal. The monitor works faultlessly on
my PC.

Prior to this problem the system was happily running Linux and X, so I have
managed to install the O/S previously.

>From looking around it appears that this could be symptomatic of screwed
firmware - and after reading the doco on the udb firmware upgrade from DEC
it seemed possible.

Following the instructions I created the FSM boot disk and the UDB firmware
upgrade disk as instructed, and switched the system into FSM boot mode (by
moving the little jumper under the drives.)

When booting the system does boot off the floppy - however it is hard to
see what is happening as there is still no video.

Short of having blown up the TGA video (How ??) I am unsure of what the problem
is. I will be plugging a terminal into the serial port to see if any messages
are sent there but am looking for any suggestions.

Any ideas ?
