On Wed, 23 Apr 1997, Martin Radford wrote:

> With all these questions coming up about Digital Unix, does anyone
> know how much a DU licence actually costs? In particular can any one
> indicate an approximate price for:
>
> (a) the cheapest licence that would provide all the libraries
> required to run (say) DU Netscape under Linux.
>
> (b) a two-user licence including compiler, networking, etc.
>
> I'm assuming of course that things like the C-compiler come extra, and
> are not in the basic cost. Does anyone know at least the pricing in
> the UK and US?

Just a thought, but has anyone at Digital entertained the idea of a special
run time licence pack of Digital Unix for Linux/Alpha. This would be a subset
of DU consisting of just the dynamic libraries and loader neccessary to run
Digital Unix binaries under Linux. This would be commercial, perhaps retailing
in the $100-$200 range, and would be offered with minimal support.

I realize that from Digital's perspective, this could be view as a loss of
a Digital Unix sale, and Linux is probably perceived as a minicule part
of the market. However, it would be some $$$ in Digital's pocket for
people who are using DEC hardware for Linux who can never afford the cost
of a DU license.
