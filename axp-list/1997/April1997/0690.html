From: SurferBoy <surfer@mercury.flipag.net>
I remember reading an article on how to customize your xdm login screen
using XBanner. When I went to the Alpha page it had the penguins on it
and that reminded me. Can anyone tell me where to find XBanner for Linux?

Xbanner is a relatively tame application, and it should compile from sources
(you don't need to find an Alpha executable).

Let me mention that if all you want is a nice root background made out of a gif,
you don't need xbanner---just stick stuff into /usr/X11R6/lib/X11/xdm/Xsetup_0.
You need Xbanner if you want fancy text and such.

The following is a working example: it displays a picture of Elvis and says
"Thank you, Thank you very much" on the login screen (don't ask why).

#!/bin/sh
# $XConsortium: Xsetup_0,v 1.3 93/09/28 14:30:31 gildea Exp $
cat /usr/local/lib/sounds/ethanks.au > /dev/audio &
xconsole -geometry 480x130-0-0 -daemon -notify -verbose -fn fixed -exitOnFail
xv -root /usr/local/lib/images/bigelvis.jpg -quit
