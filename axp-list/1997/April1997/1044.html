>>> Thomas Peter Burg said:
>
> I'm having trouble initialising the integrated network-device on a PWS433a.

Not surprisingly, as I did also... :-)

> I took the most recent miata-kernel (19 Apr, I think) from gatekeeper,
> which tells on startup:
>
> Apr 23 18:37:11 localhost kernel: tulip.c:v0.73 4/16/97 becker@cesdis.gsfc.
> nasa.gov

Yes, the latest MIATA kernel on gatekeeper, miata-kernel-2029-complete.gz,
*does* have a working "tulip.c" driver for the builtin 21142, thanks to
Donald Becker. However, because of the large number of ways the 21142 can be
configured to connect to networks, you need to specify in a bootline option,
how it is really connected. Here's what Donald sent me:

> Built-in -- put the following line in /etc/lilo.conf and re-run /sbin/lilo
> append = "ether=0,0,1,eth0"
>
> The media types are
>
> 0 10baseT (with autoselect)
> 1 10base2
> 2 AUI
> 3 100baseTx
> 4 10baseT-FD
> 5 100baseTx-FD
> 6 100baseT4
> 7 100baseFx
> 8 100baseFx-FD
> 9 MII 10baseT
> 10 MII 10baseT-FD
> 11 MII
> 12 10baseT (no autoselect), v0.69 and later only
> 13 MII 100baseTx
> 14 MII 100baseTx-FD
> 15 MII 100baseT4

So, for example, on a 10Mbit network with a BNC connector, I specified
at the end of the MILO "boot" line:

ether=0,0,1,eth0

where the first two parameters should be zero, the third is the value from
the media type table above.

I have no idea what most of the above options really are, or how they differ
from each other, but there they are... :-)

Good luck!
