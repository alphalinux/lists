On Mon, 7 Apr 1997, Gert van der Knokke wrote:

> The CPU can do 2 instructions per cycle, the newer generation EV4 (?)
> does 4 instructions per cycle (at 500 MHz !) The UDB can't be pushed
> further than 233 MHz (with some risk 266 ) and supports only the EV2
> CPU's

The UDB (21066) is an EV4. It is dual-issue
the 21064 is EV45. It is also dual-issue, but faster, and more
streamlined.
the 21164 is EV5. It is quad-issue. FAST
the 21164A and PC are EV 56. Quad-issue and more streamlined and with
MMX-like instruction subsets.
