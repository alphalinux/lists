Digital Alpha systems typically range around 8.41 spec95 int base to
9.78 for 333Mhz 21164chipset. Don't bother to compare the float. Obviously
Dec is the best for
it, let just say Intel did not bother to design their floating unit well.
If floating point is important don't bother to go with Intel x86 or
PowerPCs.
Besides, I don't think it is a fair comparision. If you want float
and need double precision go Alpha or other RISC (other than PowerPC,
which is a single precision machine) otherwise, intel is good. Choose
your machine by your applications.

Notice: On all the Intel benchmark there is no difference between base
and none base. I have no idea why, but it is true. Performance varies
from systems to systems. For alpha, Cache is very important. So watch the
cache size. SDRAM,EDO ram makes a difference as well (well slight.) Happy
hunting for spec.
