Due to negligence at 03:45 PM 4/7/97 -0400, Hallow let this roam freely
into my Inbox of Terror:
|>I'm thinking of purchasing a UDB system, or perhaps another AXP
|>system. I'll soon have about 1,500. to spend (US that is).

You might want to consider getting a PC164 or similar, altho I'm not sure
if a decent one can be had for your price range.

|>1) Is there any place other than annex in the UK still selling
|>fairly complete UDB systems (perhaps in the US?).

I don't know how complete they are anymore, but I think www.onsale.com is
still auctioning off a few from Starship (some in farms, no less).

|>2) I've heard conflicting reports as to performance. I've heard
|>everything from P5-133 type to P6-200 type performance for the
|>21066 166Mhz. How comparable is it really to x86, and how would
|>the 21066A 233 do, and would it be worth the price jump?

With ~50M in my 166MHz UDB, and a nice external SCSI drive, I'd say that
it's about on par with a P100 (maybe a P120 in some areas). Don't expect
blinding speed with these guys, but they are nice little toy boxen :)

Generally, I've only seen the price jump a little bit from 166->233 (a few
hundred $US, maybe). If I was going to buy another one, I'd go for the 233
instead of the 166 that I got :)

|>3) Withe the 2MB TGA card, what color depths are supported at 1024x768?

The UDB's TGA only supports 8bpp, regardless of resolution. Yeah, I know
that sucks :) The UDB can fit a select assortment of video cards in its
sole PCI slot, check the AXP FAQ for an exact list.
