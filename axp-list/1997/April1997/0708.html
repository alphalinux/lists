>>> Gabriel TAMURA said:
>
> Has anyone solved the problem with X in the AlphaStation 255 with S3
> Trio64 (2 Mb vram) and 32 MB (or 64MB, I have tested on both
> configurations) ?

This is probably because none who do the XFree86 work *has* an AS255 with
a Trio64 card in it.

It really isn't because no one is sensitive to your problem, just that
no one can (easily) help.

> I am considering now to change that card for a Trident TGUI 9440AGi PCI,
> which is supported in the RedHat 4.1/Intel XF86_SVGA binary rpm but not in
> the RH4.1/Alpha, so it looks like I will have to recompile it in the
> Alpha. Has anyone done something like that ?

I wouldn't choose that one; it hasn't (yet?) been ported to run under
Linux/Alpha.

Actually, you might be better off atempting to find and fix the Trio64
problem than to attempt porting the 9440 support, as the S3 server has
been (for most chipsets) successfully ported to the Alpha.

> Why RedHat does not include
> the VGA16 server in the RH4.1/Alpha and the SVGA server only supports
> mgaxxxx (matrox millenium) ?

Probably because the VGA16 code hasn't been made to work on the Alpha. As
possibly the one who looked furthest into this, I can say that without a
*lot* of work, it will never run. This is due to idiosyncrasies of the basic
4-bit VGA hardware and the "rather interesting" way that the Alphas have to
deal with 8 and 16 bit accesses to bus (framebuffer) memory.

The Millennium support was ported to the Alphas because many of the boxes
DEC and its third party resellers put together are equipped with them; it's
a popular and nicely performing board that helps to sell the system. If we
were seeing Alpha boxes commonly equipped with the Tridents, I'm sure they
would be supported by now.
