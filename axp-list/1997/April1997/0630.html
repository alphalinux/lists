At 01:47 hrs. 04-12-97 -0400, you wrote:
> I have a 2940UW, which is auto-terminating, and defaults to ID7 hooked
into
> a seagate UW which is not terminated and is on ID4, which is followed by
> another UW seagate at the end of my chain, which is teminated and set as
> ID0. As for a cdrom, I don't have one in this system.
>
>Could the hardware-savvy people explain how does the auto-termination
>work? It simply looks to see if certain lines are "low" or "high" at
startup. i.e.: is there a device there. Sometimes it can guess right. Often
it fails. Words of advice: Set it manually, or turn it off and use real
terminators. Active ones, preferrably.

>Moreover, would active termination work if you have wide bus with
>narrow devices? What if you terminated the narrow part only--would it
>autoterminate half of the bus?
It tries to guess. See previous comments.
>
>The relevance to axp-list: many problems people seem to be having
>result from SCSI configuration problems. Is there a lucid explanation
>available somewhere?

Sure. Figure out what needs to terminated, and where. Install proper
termination. Don't assume that because a device is either auto-terminating
or has a termination jumper option that it's termination works properly.
Test, see for yourself, don't trust auto-termination, use real active
terminators where you can. Remember that wide SCSI devices have a low and a
high termiantion byte. Go placidly in life amidst the confusion. etc... ;-}
