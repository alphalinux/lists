On Mon, 7 Apr 1997, Hallow wrote:

> I'm thinking of purchasing a UDB system, or perhaps another AXP
> system. I'll soon have about 1,500. to spend (US that is).

You won't need NEARLY that much for a UDB (at least in the US)

> 1) Is there any place other than annex in the UK still selling
> fairly complete UDB systems (perhaps in the US?).

www.onsale.com has VX40's for sale... though without HD, monitor,
keyboard, or ram.

http://www.itsinfonet.com/unix.htm is also selling 233 MHz Multias (VX42)

OR... I've got a VX40 I need to get rid of as well... 24M ram, 1.0GB
drive, etc....

> 2) I've heard conflicting reports as to performance. I've heard
> everything from P5-133 type to P6-200 type performance for the
> 21066 166Mhz. How comparable is it really to x86, and how would
> the 21066A 233 do, and would it be worth the price jump?

I'd go with the 233 personally... that'd be a VX42... And the performance
range all depends on what apps you're running... processor intense,
cached programs fly. Other things are SLOW. compiling is slow. (but
that's due to RISC compiling, not necessarily the processor)

> 3) Withe the 2MB TGA card, what color depths are supported at 1024x768?

The TGA card is almost definitely 8bpp all the way through. again, a vx42
MAY have a 16bpp on the MB (yes, tga is on the MB), but don't get your
hopes up.
