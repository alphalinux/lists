>>> T. S. Horsnell said:
>
> 3. I've seen references to UDB boards with 2Mb cache. (The PC164 only
> has 1Mb). Can someone tell me more? So far, my 3 test jobs show
> a 2 to 3 speedup over the 2100 (433MHz EV5 for my 21164 as against
> 275MHz EV4 for the 2100) but I realise cache sizes can be a significant
> factor in this and would like as big a 3rd-level cache as possible..

The 233MHz versions of the UDB came with a cache SIMM slot; AFAIK, the
largest cache SIMM was 1Mb. The biggest bottleneck on the UDB was the CPU
and not the cache size; don't bother considering the UDB as an option.

PC164's *can* be built with 2Mb soldered onto the MB, but few are, for cost
reasons mostly. I think perhaps Microway (microway.com) build them with 2Mb,
perhaps others if you special order.
