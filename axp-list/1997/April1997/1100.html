I tried to upgrade my version of Mesa from version 2.1 to 2.2.

I have encountered a number of problems:

/usr/src/linux-2.0.27/include/asm/posix_types.h:29: warning: ANSI C does not
support `long long'

which I think I fixed by getting rid of the extra long.

The mail archive told me what to do about readpix.c causing a fatal
error.

The .h files in /usr/X11R6/include/X11 had an include in them looking
for files in /usr/X11R6/include/X11/X11, and there wasn't any
/usr/X11R6/include/X11/X11.

I tried to fix this by creating the needed directory and copying all
the files being looked for into it.

All the source seemed to compile after that, but when I got to the
demos and book directories, I got messages saying that links had
not been done, and a lot of non-executable executables.

It is true that I am relatively un-initiated into the mysteries of
Linux, and DEC, so if you ask me:
"Did you do such and such..."
Probably I did not since I have never heard of such a thing.

All upgrading was done by way of the RH 4.1 cd. Thanks for any
advice or directions.
