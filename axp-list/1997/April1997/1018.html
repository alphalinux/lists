Answers to your problems mostly have to do with the fact that you have:
a) Probably chosen the wrong video card, and
b) Don't have as many "colour bit planes" as X apps tend to expect to get.

If you're using the built in card, you should select the 'TGA' X server. (It
is there amongst the options).

Unfortunately, it is limited to 8 bit planes, which will result in somewhat
grainy results. If fvwm95-2 is allocating lots of colors to different things
at start-up, you may have nothing left for (say) xv to work with.

I ran "AfterStep" on my S3V (x86)-based system *once,* and found that the
"cool icons" chewed up the colour map completely, meaning that Netscape then
displayed in black and white.

You should expect that xv will be somewhat grainy on the UDB due to limited
numbers of colours.

There is a web page or two out there dealing with fvwm95 configuration; I'd
suggest doing an AltaVista search on that. I may have a reference on one of
my web pages; I don't much use fvwm95 and so haven't kept much info on that.
