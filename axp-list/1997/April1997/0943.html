>license to somehow obtain the libraries WITHOUT installing Digital UNIX? I
>have A Digital UNIX 3.2C and a 4.0A License, but since the Linux UFS

Well, Chris, here's the 2 problems:

1: How do I know that you ( or anyone else, for that matter) _REALLY_ have
the necessary license. This is the verification problem.

2: If I send them to you, howmany more "me too's" will I have to deal with?
In other words, if we could solve problem #1, I could probably post them
somewhere to automate the process. As I don't want to receive the Wrath of
Digital ( hmmm... snappy title for a movie?) I don't think I ( or any other
sane/cautious person) will be doing this real soon now... ;-D
This is the time/me too/automation problem.

I strongly suggest sending Netscape some email and REAL mail ( you know, on
"paper") asking for a Linux Alpha Xfree port...

That is the only _good_ solution I can suggest.

Lastly: You can afford a Digital UNIX license, but can't afford a measly
500MB drive to install it on???
