On Mon, Apr 07, 1997 at 03:45:29PM -0400, Hallow wrote:
> I'm thinking of purchasing a UDB system, or perhaps another AXP
> system. I'll soon have about 1,500. to spend (US that is).
> The main questions I have are as follows:
> 1) Is there any place other than annex in the UK still selling
> fairly complete UDB systems (perhaps in the US?).

Try Carlson Computers, carlson@cts.com. I've bought five machines from
them, and never had any problem (except a DOA power supply, which they
promptly replaced).

> 2) I've heard conflicting reports as to performance. I've heard
> everything from P5-133 type to P6-200 type performance for the
> 21066 166Mhz. How comparable is it really to x86, and how would
> the 21066A 233 do, and would it be worth the price jump?

It all definitely depends on RAM. NT4 with 24mb is slow as
dogshit, but Linux/AXP is fine with 24mb, even with X. Some of the
office machines are 48mb boxen, and NT4 on them is fine. Performance
is between a P75 and P100 for the 166 machines; I've heard around P120-P133
speed for the 233s. Basically, the more RAM you add, the faster it will
be.

> 3) Withe the 2MB TGA card, what color depths are supported at 1024x768?

I've never gone beyond 256 colors, but then I've never needed more than
that...

> Any advice, comments, or suggestions would be appreciated.

Carlson has the VX40A-F3s, which come *without* external SCSI port, but
I just (about an hour ago) got through adding one to mine with about
$40 worth of adapter and 50pin ribbon cable. I just completely bypassed
the internal HD, and went straight from the motherboard to the back panel.
Gonna add a 1gig FH 5.25 drive in a DEC RZ55 case, which is the same exact
form factor as the UDB, just about twice as tall. Should work and look
rather nice.
