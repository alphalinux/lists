At 01:50 hrs. 04-17-96 -0400, you wrote:
>
>I finally got Redhat/AXP installed well. But there seems to be one
>irritating problem left--my mouse doesn't work. I plugged in a serial
>port to "J35" on the motherboard and plugged my serial mouse in to that,
>then power cycled. Once I got into MILO, while my mouse was plugged in
>every time I hit "enter" on the keyboard the character sequence "0> 0>"
>would be repeated for a couple of lines, and MILO would complain about not
>recognizing my command. When I removed the serial mouse, the junk went
>away. I assume that to mean that information is in fact getting through
>on the serial port.
>
>When I got into Linux, I saw that tty00 and tty01 existed, but neither X
>nor gpm appear to be seeing the mouse. I linked /dev/mouse to everything
>I could think of, from cua0 to cua3 and from ttyS0 to ttyS3, but nothing
>works. Strange behavior, eh? X is rather unusable in this state,
>unfortunately.
>
>One more thing--if I link /dev/mouse to /dev/cua0 or /dev/ttyS0, X doesn't
>complain (although the cursor doesn't move), but when I link it to cua1 or
>anything else, X starts but it complains:
>
>Warning: /dev/mouse unable to get status of mouse fd (Input/output error)
>
>Thanks again,
>Ryan Rafferty

Uh, Ryan. You need a PS/2 mouse. Not a serial mouse with a PS/2 adapter.
Not a Microsoft or compatible mouse.
There is a difference.
Logitech Mouseman Combo works well. The one with the PS/2 connector on the
cord.
