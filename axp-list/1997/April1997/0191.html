World moves very fast - I dont think that even DEC can track down these
MICRON chips anymore. I think the 256k micron is still avail.

The 1 meg cache from ISSI is rated at 15ns access time. The pci133 has a
jmper for either 15ns, or 20ns. The 15ns setting failes - cache errors
and the such. The 20ns board setting seems to work more favorably (
Although i am sending back my board because of a hard cache error that
causes NT to barf ). They say that they will look/fix it. BUT this board
always had some sort of cache problem(s) ( The board I bought from dec
wouldn't accept/recognise any cache, the other board - with 1 meg cache
- failed intermittenly :((((( ).
Besides there seems to be some doubt if the 1meg chips are worthwhile -
not enough performence umph - stick with 256k cache if u can - lots more
parts suppliers. You could even take them from outdated/busted 486
boards.
The guy mourice hilarious seems to have/willing to sell 1meg cache for
your board - he's been around, fairly knowledgeable. Probably will
replace broken/non-working cache more readily than the salesman at a
local electronics shop - u bought it, u fix it. And at $195 for 11
chips, is more reasonable that what I paid for mine!!!!!
