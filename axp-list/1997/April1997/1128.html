>Maurice,

>Thanks for clearing those various issues up for me. When I inquired about
>purchasing a copy of the "media" I was referring to a transfer of the
>licensing as well. I am still unsure as to the legalities of transferring
>copies of such a license, but that's beside the point. I'm not so much
>interested in buying a new machine (although that complete AXPpci33 box

>OSF1 or DU that will run on what I've got right now *sigh* but I'm not so

Again, to summarize:
There is no real legal or legitimate way to accomplish your goal.
Digital UNIX is not sold for the UDB.
Licenses have the attribute of being "attached" to a CPU.
In theory, you could buy a CPU on a "noname" board ( AXPpci33 Part #
EBP2x-CZ) and then move the CPU into the UDB. However, you would sacrifice
your Digital UNIX support in doing so.
