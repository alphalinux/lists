Greetings,

I've been working at a new Linux/Alpha system for about three weeks. Of
particular trouble is porting certain OpenGL (Mesa)-based apps that I've
written for Intel and SGI systems. I consistently get floating-point
exception errors that are making me exceedingly unhappy.

Even when trying to compile Mesa from scratch, you can't do it. At least,
not on a fresh RedHat installation. After upgrading gcc and glibc to the
current RedHat releases, I still have to compile certain file without
optimization, and I had to "downgrade" the include files in
/usr/include/sys to those of an old Slackware distribution before
successfully compiling Mesa.

And I still am unable to compile Glut and other OpenGL-based apps all for
similar reasons, but none of which I can remember right now.

There's a question I'm trying to ask here: What do I need to do to create
a develpment environment that works *consistently*. Right now, some
things compile, but most don't, and it's a gawdawful frustrating and
unhappy thing.

Any input is much appreciated!
