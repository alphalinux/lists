I was being polite in my previous postings concerning Parasoft. I did not go
into previous conversations with the salesperson. In this posting I will be
very blunt.

When I first contacted Parasoft, I ask if they had a version for Linux/Alpha
I was sent e-mail telling me to download the version for SunOS 4. I have that
e-mail if anyone wants a copy. As an alternative I downloaded the Linux/Intel
version.

Yesterday in the telephone calls from Parasoft, the second caller was rude,
and very personal in his attacks. There was no interest in helping the
possible client, he was interested in only one thing, NO NEGATIVE POSTS,
to the net. Based on his attitude alone I would not deal with Parasoft.

The evaluation software requires a password, and license from Parasoft to
run, and has an expiration date. I downloaded the software late Friday, and
started it running on Friday evening. My original posting was made on Saturday.
I was up all day Easter Sunday, and most of yesterday. I did explain this
to the first caller, and I did state that I was going to try and get some rest.

The last call from Parasoft came late in the day, and I told that caller
that even if I started a new evaluation that the program would not finish before
Insure++ expired. (April 1, 1997). He did not extend the expiration date.

I was able to evaluate Insure++ for basically 3-4 days.

The evaluation still stands, Insure++ itself is unacceptably slow.
The improvements in speed only came after compiling all source files
except one with gcc, and that one with insight. The application was
linked with insight. Even with only one source file being "analyzed"
the speed improvement was only minor.

Parasoft as a company is rude, and interested only in "NO NEGATIVE" postings
to the net.

>
> While it might have been bad timing and perhaps they didn't use perfect
> etiquette, I can only conclude that they certainly are going the extra
> distance in trying to satisfy their (potential) customers.

Again their interest was not in 'satisfying a pontential customer', their
were interested in only stopping "NEGATIVE POSTING".

>
> I can only wish that a few other companies would be this concerned with the
> needs of users and devote this kind of effort in trying to help...

Any company who is willing to be polite, and helpful to a potential client
with an evaluation is welcomed, but not Parasoft. Their attitude was not
one of help, but rudeness, accusations, and asking if I bothered to read the
online manuals. I find their attitude insulting.

Even since the accident where I broke my shoulder, when one of my clients

consultants handled their concerns in a polite manner, or one of my children
have handled it.
