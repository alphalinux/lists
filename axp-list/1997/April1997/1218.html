>>> Andreas Ulrich Trottmann said:
>
> My syslog is full of "unaligned traps". According to
> grep "unaligned trap" /var/log/messages | wc, I have had now way over
> 20'000 unaligned trap errors during the last two days. The total uptime
> during these days was about ten hours.

If you are using the kernel I put out on gatekeeper with the 21142 support,
then I believe that most of the unaligned traps are probably due to the
tulip.c driver. Donald Becker, the author of that driver, said:

>> Most hardware/drivers can align received frames on arbitrary byte
>> boundaries. The Tulip hardware can only align receive frames on 4 byte
>> boundaries, so the 14 byte header misaligns the IP header. There are
>> comments about this in the driver.
>>
>> The only way around this to always copy the rx frame to align the Rx buffer.
>> You can do this in the current driver by setting the COPYBREAK value to less
>> than the minimum packet size. Could you do that and let me know what
>> happens?

Good luck with getting rid of those unligned traps...
