On Wed, 23 Apr 1997, Martin Radford wrote:
> With all these questions coming up about Digital Unix, does anyone
> know how much a DU licence actually costs?

Our AlphaStation running Linux used to run VMS. The reason it's running
Linux instead of DU is that a DU license was going to cost us around $1300,
and that's with whatever educational discount UNH gets because we have so many
DEC machines. I think they also factored in the old VMS license (don't know
for sure, I didn't make the phone call). But we told DEC what we thought of
their license fee, and bought RedHat for $100. :)

> (a) the cheapest licence that would provide all the libraries
> required to run (say) DU Netscape under Linux.

Hmmm, that would need their OSF-BASE license, which is basically the license
to use DU. I don't know if you would need any more license paks, though.
OSF-USER gives you logins to your DU machine, but as it would be running
Linux, you could live with 0 logins to said DU machine. :-)
