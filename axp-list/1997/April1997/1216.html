Randall Shutt wrote:
>
> Yes, I just had a question. When I attempt to use zmail under em86, it
> starts up just fine, but as soon as I try to open my inbox, it claims it cannot
> lock my mail spool file, then claims that it cannot open my inbox due to an
> invalid argument. I have checked the spool permissions against what I have on
> my intel that I run zmail on constantly (like now). They are identical, as are
> the permissions and suid bits on the zmail bin itself. Any takers??

Just curious; do you have the same problem if you run zmail as root?

I just realized, to my horror, that I did not implement suid/sgid
for em86 executables; I stole the kernel startup code from
binfmt_script.c,
which does not support suid/sgid.

Try it as root and let me know what happens...

> Also, when I attempt to use raplayer-ELF-i386, I get unsupported ioctl
> notices.. Anyone have any better luck? This is on a 2.0.29 UDB w/ 96 Megs (if
> that matters). Thanks in advance.

That's a known problem... someone out there was making noises about
adding the appropriate ioctl support. If he doesn't, we will get
around to it *eventually*, but if you want it now... well, that's
why I made the runtime sources available 8-)
