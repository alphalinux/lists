After reading the umpteenth question on pppd...

This is just a suggestion, but you may want to check out some of the
low priced router/firewall products that allow you to experiment on your
Alpha, i.e. upgrade, without worrying about killing your dial-up network
connectivity.

One example is WinGate.

Another is my company's product at:

http://www.iproute.com

It runs on a 386 or better PC (a 386 will easily handle anything from
28.8kbps Analaog up to 128kbps ISDN). Our software understands PPP and
will dial the ISP on demand, supports dynamic IP, Network Address
Translation (multiple machines with just one IP = Linux ipalias), Virtual Private
Networks (Triple and Standard DES encryption), RADIUS, SNMP, etc. It works
with most Ethernet cards and can bridge between token ring and Ethernet.
It uses Stateful Examination packet authentication.

It fits on a floppy and only needs one meg of RAM.

You can download an eval from the above site.

A five IP version costs $195.00

It will help to lessen the pain at upgrade time.

I apologize if the self-advertisement offends anyone.
