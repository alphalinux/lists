On Thu, 10 Apr 1997, Steven Kokinos wrote:

> I've been working on glibc-2.0.2 as well. First I installed
> binutils-2.7.0.9, with the patch (I believe came from Richard Henderson).
> This seemed to go ok. The I installed the new binutils, after adjusting
> the installdir to be /usr, to match the current directory. This also
> seemed to be ok. Next, I went to recompile gccc-2.7.2.1, so libgcc.a
> would be rebuilt against the new binutils. This is where I have run into
> problems (using the gcc source rpm):
>
> cc -c -DIN_GCC -O -I. -I. -I./config ./genattr.c
> cc -c -DIN_GCC -O -I. -I. -I./config rtl.c
> cc -DIN_GCC -O -o genattr \
> genattr.o rtl.o ` case "obstack.o" in ?*) echo obstack.o ;; esac ` ` case
> "cc"@"" in "cc"@?*) echo ;; esac ` ` case "" in ?*) echo ;; esac `
> ./genattr ./config/alpha/alpha.md > tmp-attr.h
> make: *** [stamp-attr] Error 132
> Bad exit status

I think you needed to rebuild crt0.o and libgcc.a _before_ you tried to
compile anything. That might seem like an impossible task, but I think
it's possible - try saying "make libgcc.a" in the gcc source directory.

Until then, no binaries you've built will run. You may be able to get
precompiled versions, but I'm not sure where.
