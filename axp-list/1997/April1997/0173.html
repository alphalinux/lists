On Thu, 3 Apr 1997, CHIH-HSIANG HENRY YAO wrote:

> I am thinking of moving to Digital Alpha systems. Here is a few questions:
>
> 1. How often could you port a Intel application software without much
> problems?

That depends, but for the most part it is straight forward. Some other
people have more experience and might know better, but I have compiled
several packages "out of the box" from source.

> 2. Berkeley SPICE for Alpha precompiled?

The redhat4.1 supposedly has this but it is version 2.something which came
out in the mid eighties the current version is like 3.07g?? and it came
out like 3 or 4 years ago. Anyway I tried to run the one that came with
4.1 and it gave me a bunch of wierd errors implying that the binary did
not work. Not to dis RH but this struck me as pretty sloppy to distribute
an ancient app that did not even work. Basically no.

> 3. Magic for Alpha precompiled?

Dunno, but I doubt it.

> 4. Netscape for Alpha?

No. People are running the intel version with em86 but its a bit sketchy.

> 5. Knews or good news reader for Alpha?

I don't know about knews but tin and trn are availible.

> 6. how hard is it to install NT 4.0 with Linux simutaneously?

>From what I hear, very easy.

> 7. Motif library precompiled for Alpha?

I believe so. Somebody set me straight if I'm wrong. It costs money so I
don't have it.
