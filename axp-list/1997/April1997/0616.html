>>> CHIH-HSIANG HENRY YAO said:
> I know this is in the FAQ, but I still don't quite understand the
> driver list (namely X-Windows)
>
> 1. Does Linux/Alpha uses XFree86 port?

Yes, it does; in fact, that's all that's available at the moment.

> 2. Does all the driver written in the list of XFree86 will work under alpha
> or only a specific driver? I am just confused on this.

No, all the drivers do *not* work under Alpha, as most have not been ported
to Alpha; the biggest problem is the Intel assembly code that most contain.

The following servers are available from XFree86 for running under
Linux/Alpha:

S3
S3V (ViRGE)
Mach64
I128
P9000
TGA
SVGA (supports only Matrox Millennium at this time)
Mono (generic)

> 3. Also, in the FAQ, it said the sound is only supported on Microsoft
> Windows Sound System compatible... Huh? So things like Sound Blaster,
> Sound Scape doesn't work? What is the precise chipset/brand that is
> supported? Only UDB sound card?

No, this is not true, either; I've happily used the SoundBlaster, PAS16,
and GUS drivers with great success. Others I can't vouch for, but I expect
most would "just work", as Hannu took great care when porting to Alpha.

> P.S. I am thinking of purchasing 21164PC using 164SX motherboard in the
> future (not release yet until the summer.) Will that work with Linux?
> Basically how is the compitablity for Alpha systems.

Future boards from Digital Semiconductor *will* be supported as soon as we
get specs and hardware to test on.
