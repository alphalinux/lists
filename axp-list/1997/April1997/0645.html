Sorry for the confusion, I am not the one who was offering this machine
for sale. I was simply pointing out that the price was not totally
unreasonable, as another poster seemed to think that it was.

As they came from DEC, the 166 UDBs had two 8Mb. and two 4Mb. SIMMs for a
total of 24Mb. A lot of them have been sold with no RAM at all however,
and the $400 I mentioned is usually for that configuration (making this
an even better deal). Obviously the only way to get 64Mb. of RAM is with
4 16Mb. or 2 32 Mb. SIMMs. Interestingly the 233Mhz. UDB variants came
with 2 12Mb. SIMMs, which must be in the first 2 slots or they will be
recognized as only 8Mb. each. This has the advantage of leaving two slots
empty for expansion but the downside is that it is nearly impossible to
get rid of 12Mb. units if you replace them.

The real question here is whether "For Sale" items belong on this list at
all. I tend to think that they do not, in spite of the fact that many
readers here may be interested in purchasing a system. The
misc.forsale.computers.workstations newsgroup often has listings for UDBs
as well as other Alpha systems, and the traffic on that group is low
enough that it should not be a burden for anyone looking to buy to peruse
it now and again.
