On Sun, 27 Apr 1997, Maurice W. Hilarius wrote:

> First of all, the ownership of media does not constitute a license for
> Digital UNIX.

That's true, but in the U.S., ownership of the media gives you the right
to run the software without the license. In order to limit you solely to
the terms of the license, the licensor must transfer physical possession
of the media to the recipient and still retain ownership of the media.

All that being said, the latter is probably exactly what Digital does,
meaning you still need the license. (And if you buy an old copy, if the
seller didn't own the copy, per the Digital agreement, he has no ownership
to pass on to you, so you're really just buying the right to possess, not
the title to the media. Confused yet?)
