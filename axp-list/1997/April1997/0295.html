Jonah Giacalone wrote:
>
> Could someone explain to me how a 233mhz 64bit system compares with only
> a P90? Aren't they both PCI? Where are all those Hz going? Is it a
> cisc/risc thing? What would allow the UDB to reach more of it's
> potential? More cache? If this is really the case, why would anyone want
> to go with the Alpha other than for a reasonably asy port to faster
> Alpha platforms?
>
> Jonah
>

Well, I've been playing around with an UDB here (166 MHz)
and Linux is not very fast on it... at least not as fast as my P100.
(same amount of mem 40 Mb)

But I've been trying some small assembler routines and the CPU itself is
very fast..
A loop consisting of 3 instructions (two adds and a branch) executes at
52.6 million loops per second...

The CPU can do 2 instructions per cycle, the newer generation EV4 (?)
does 4 instructions per cycle (at 500 MHz !) The UDB can't be pushed
further than 233 MHz (with some risk 266 ) and supports only the EV2
CPU's

I also have been peeking at the code which GCC generates and it has much
overhead, optimization is not yet up to standard I guess...

I don't know if there are plans to optimize the compiler further but it
could mean that Linux on the Alpha can be much faster than it is now.
