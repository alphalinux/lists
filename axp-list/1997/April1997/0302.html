Michael Alan Dorman wrote:

> I believe that the 166 is _realistically_ equivalent to slightly more
> than a 60Mhz Pentium. The 233 would probably be about a 90.

It's a *little* better than that 8-) When I had a UDB for home
Linux/Alpha development, I found it roughly kept pace with my
100MHz Pentium. I'd call it a P-90 to be conservative. The
233MHz UDB would be, I guess, about the same as a P-120. No,
the performance does *not* scale with the clock speed, because on
the 21066 chips used for the UDB the bottleneck is in the memory
access.
