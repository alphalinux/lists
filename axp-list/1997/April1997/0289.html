I just purchased one 166Mhz with 324 scsi 24 megs RAM. In my opinion it the
equivalent of about a pentium 75 running NT and a little slower than a 90 MHZ
running Linux.

The main problem I have with it is that I can build a kernel on my P90 in
about 12 minutes or slightly less. It take an hour to build a kernel redhat 4.1
on my UDB. I know this could be related to the compiler, but it real world.

Benchmarks says it should be a better than a 90 but real world under NT 4.0 and
linux it's not.

It this is a machine you're getting to play with as many of us have I would suggest
not paying more than $400 for it. There are quite a few of them available. If I
ever get modules to work then I plan to use mine for dialup BBS. I not knocking
the machine, it's a neat little package, but don't expect it to be as fast as a
PR166 intel machine ( at least it doesn't run NT OR LINUX AS WELL ).
