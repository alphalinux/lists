I've asked this question three times over the last month and the same
noncommital answer comes each time. Until proven otherwise (ie: someone
saying "Yeah, I'm running it!") assume it doesn't exist yet. I'd also
suspect that RedHat providing JDK in their version 4.1 for Intel and NOT
for axp is a good sign.

Sadly, since I'm more of a Linux-end-user (read: bottom-feeder) I don't
know what's involved in writing a Java VM. I would think a good VM
would require some hardware-specific (machine/assembly) code, thus
making the Linux/Intel VM not easily portable. However, I have little
grounds to speculate.

I would ***LOVE*** for someone better informed to comment at this
point...
