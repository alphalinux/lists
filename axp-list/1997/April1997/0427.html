On Wed, 9 Apr 1997, Anderson, Greg wrote:

[IDE cable backwards?]
> It's keyed, if I remember correctly.

Some are, and some aren't. There are three different methods of keying
those IDC connectors, and not every menufacturer implements every one.

I don't _think_ the connector on the UDB mainboard is keyed at all,
actually. I'll pull my UDB apart later on and check. Even if it is, you
still need a keyed cable _and_ a keyed drive to be safe.

And, sadly, even then you aren't guaranteed to win, because I've seen at
least one instance of a cable being made up wrongly so that it was
impossible to plug it in the _correct_ way round (duh...).

If in doubt, it's always safest to trace pin 1 (which ought always to be
marked in some way) and make sure it corresponds on every device. Oh, and
it can also be the case that cables don't have every pin connected, so
even if you connect pin 1 on the drive to pin 1 on the motherboard by
putting the cable in wrong at both ends, it may not work.

It's a minefield out there...
