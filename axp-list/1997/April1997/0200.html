On Apr 4, Scott Manley wrote:
> I'm curious as to what I need to compile into the Kernel to make full use of
> the MSS hardware found on the UDB, other than the MSS specific stuff is there
> anything else wich I should be adding?

I have some kernel patches for the AlphaStation's MSS board; some or all of
them may probably be of use for UDB as well. I have no UDB to test them.
Feedback is always welcome.

The patches reside at ftp://genie.ucd.ie/pub/alpha/kernel. Here is a
short description:

sound-alpha.diff - small Makefile patch to allow quick sound
module rebuild by typing make in drivers/sound
(not really useful unless you change and
rebuild the driver every ten minutes)

sound-mssdma2.diff - this patch allows to specify second DMA
channel for MSS (and thus enable full duplex)

sound-ad1848mute.diff - fixes the driver's inablility to mute channels

sound-fulldup.diff - fixes a generic bug that prevents full duplex
operation on any ad1848-based soundcard

The patches are for 2.0.x (tested on 27-29). Sound-mssdma2.diff is
incorporated into 2.1.x line.
