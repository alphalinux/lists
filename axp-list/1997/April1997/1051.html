On Thu, 24 Apr 1997, Brian G. Emmett wrote:

> I recently finished the installation of RH4.1 on two UDB's and have
> noticed that:
>
> - I can only anonymous ftp to them, I can't ftp as a local user
> - I can't get rlogin to stop requiring passwords, and $HOME/.rhosts seems
> to have no effect in that regard
> - I can't rsh to the machine AT ALL. I simply get a permission denied
> error
>
> Now, am I to assume that this has something to do with PAM? Because all
> the old tricks that worked on my Alpha running dUNIX don't seem to work
> anymore. /etc/hosts.equiv doesn't work. $HOME/.rhosts doesn't work.
>
> So I guess I am asking which authentication services need to be disabled
> to allow FTP/rlogin/rsh to work the way I want to, and how do I use
> PAM (pamconfig?) to disable them?
>
> There are no man pages from what I can tell.

First of all. make sure you have the latest updates of everything
installed. I 'fixed' the rsh problem by changing the line regarding
rhosts.

# rsh authorization
rsh auth required /lib/security/pam_securetty.so
#rsh auth required /lib/security/pam_rhosts_auth.so
rsh auth sufficient /lib/security/pam_rhosts_auth.so
rsh auth required /lib/security/pam_nologin.so
rsh account required /lib/security/pam_unix_acct.so
rsh session required /lib/security/pam_unix_session.so

That's about all I know. One of these days I'll read all the
documentation I can find on PAM.
