Ken Seefried wrote:

> Most Intel boxes limited themselves to 256KB or 512KB because the designers of the support chipset (where L2 cache is implemented) have done the research to determine that in most cases there is little benefit to providing a bigger one. There is no inherent reason why an Intel chip couldn't have 1MB, or 100MB, of cache, other than the fact that in most cases it's a waste of money.

It wasn't too long ago when swap/pageing devices were disk drives, and
70ns memory was expensive to have. I dont think that anyone wants to
swap on a disk anymore, opting to buy the (extra 64meg of) memory
instead. One could always prove that X amount of u'r pgm in memory is
all that u need, and the rest can stay on the page/swap device ( most of
the time ). Oh yes, Cost/benefits are a matter of perception.

I have not yet seen any Alpha Docs on these cache/benefit studies ( I
haven't seen any on the Intel either, but haven't really looked ). I
suspect that they dont exist. Digital always seemed to have
Non-disclosure agreements about performence tests, but never provided
any ( at their road shows).
