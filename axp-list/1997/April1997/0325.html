Having read a few posts on this issue, I think it is fair to say
that the vendor is angry with DEC, not you - what this exchange
will probably result in is:

You will probably look harder before you commit to purchase
a new system (not a bad thing - IMHO, system get cheaper daily!)

The vendor will probably maintain a very minimal DEC
inventory, due to the drastic price volatility (probably
a good thing, when the prices do eventually drop he can
clean-up, instead of cleaning around his overpriced inventory).

DEC will wonder why vendors are ordering fewer systems and the
systems they do order have tight deadlines (probably a bad thing
for DEC, since they will be the ones that suffer from their
pricing policies, not their independent hardware vendors)

I hope this maillist does not turn into a "slam the independent
hardware vendors" tool - I looked very hard before purchasing my UDB,
and when I saw lesser machines being "auctioned" for less money, I
didn't
get mad at my supplier (DGC) - I paid a fair price for a complete, turn-
key system, and the few times I needed telephone support they were
available and were very helpful.

The machine I got (UDB 233/266 w/512K cache, 500 Meg HD with
24 Meg RAM and Red Hat Linux installed, including Keyboard, mouse, and
the hardware reference manual) was expensive by todays standards, but
for when I bought it it was (and reamins to me) a good deal.

Ya pays yer money, and ya takes yer chances...
