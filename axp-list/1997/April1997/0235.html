Patrick Kessen wrote:
>
> I guess you're not using a 166Mhz Multia with 32Mb RAM then?
>
> Is the 166Mhz Multia enough for a reasonable or good performing
> x86 binary? Do I need to add some RAM?

Actually, I *did* test em86 on exactly this configuration.
Performance there was, in my opinion, just barely tolerable.
This is one of the things that will likely improve using
native shared libraries...

No, you don't need to add RAM... the "memory footprint" of EM86
isn't that huge, and em86 is much more CPU-bound than memory-bound
on a UDB...

I'll admit, the machine on which I use em86+netscape as my default
browser *is* a 500MHz PC164 with 128Mb of memory, but I keep a
UDB in my office just to remind me to pay attention to the low
end too 8-)
