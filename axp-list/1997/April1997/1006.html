Vik Bajaj wrote:
>
> Is the development of em86 going to occur in a series of
> major beta releases, or rather incremental adjustments? I ask
> out of impatience to get Intel audio programs running on
> my alpha :)

Incremental adjustments, when I feel things have gotten sufficiently
stable. Lately I've had everything in pieces on the floor because
I've been working on supporting calls to native shared library
routines. I'm on my third redesign of the interface sequence now,
and I *think* this one is a keeper 8-)

[this hasn't been easy because I've had to juggle the conflicting
goals of (a) making the interface fast & efficient, (b) making it
relatively easy for programmers to use, (c) making it flexible
enough to deal with the different calling mechanisms between
Intel and Alpha, and (d) implementing some information-passing
hooks between the Intel and Alpha environments (in 99% of the
cases the Intel app doesn't even know there's an alpha environment
around it, but this 1% is the exception to the rule).]

As for implementing audio, I thought somebody on the net had asked
about doing it... that's what I make the runtime sources available
for 8-) All that needs to be done is to implement the sound
ioctl's. I'll probably get around to this *eventually*, but if you
need this right away it's not going to happen...
