>>> Daryll Strauss said:
>
> PYXIS is the chipset in the DIGITAL Personal Workstation. I don't think
> I've ever seen that name in any of the Linux documentation. Is this
> another name for one of the standard motherboards? Will it work with
> Milo and 2.1.x kernels?

PYXIS is the "core logic" chipset found in PWSa 433/500 (aka MIATA) and
the new PC164LX from Digital Semiconductor.

There are a MILO and kernel plus patches for the MIATA out on gatekeeper
now, in pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/. The patches are against [2.0.29 plus
the axp-diffs-2.0.21]. The only piece missing from that kernel is Ethernet
support for the builtin on the MIATA, but I have a test kernel that *does*
support it, thanks to Donald Becker. I'll be putting that kernel out there
tomorrow, I hope.
