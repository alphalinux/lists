Hello,
After some consideration, I have decided to go with an Alpha based
workstation to replace my current (aged) Linux/ix86 system. From what I
have seen, Enorex Microsystems, Inc. appears to have the most reasonable
prices on Alpha hardware. Using the online price quote program, I have
put together a configuration which both meets my budget and requirements
(heavy stats/numerical analysis/OpenGL based modelling):
(http://www.enorex.com/qu7.htm)
- 433Mhz 21164(a?) cpu (PC164 mainboard presumably)
- 128Mb ram
- 2.5Gb EIDE hd
- Matrox Millenium graphics card (w/4mb wram)
- 17" .25 monitor
- 3Com (3c509?) XL 10/100 PCI NIC
- misc. parts (keyboard, etc.)
I would first like to know if anyone here either owns or has used
an Enorex-built workstation under Linux/AXP, and what their impression
(performance/uptime) was. Second; although this machine certainly will be
used for 'real work' (whatever that is <g>), I am hoping to purchase an
SB32(AWE) for playing my various MIDI files and some MP3's I encoded at work..
Will the SB32 work in an Alpha system, or are there timing issues (as with the
GUS+Jensen) which prevent proper operation? - I realize that both external
tools (isapnp) as well as recent kernel releases support plug-n-pray cards,
but want to make certain before I purchase anything.
And last, but not least: Enorex apparently 'burns in' their
workstations for at least 72 hours before shipping, presumably with NT/axp
installed. Although I am a full blooded Linux user, it would be interesting
to stack up the 433Mhz AXP against some of the PPro machines at work on their
own turf: NT. Will NT and Linux/AXP happily coexist on their respective
slices (such as a 1Gb/1.5Gb split)? Perhaps this is not an issue on the
Alpha platform (remember, current x86 user here :-)

Thank you for your time and any answers you can offer,
Daniel
