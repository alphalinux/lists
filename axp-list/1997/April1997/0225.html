In response to a number of emails I've gotten, both in private and on
the list, I figure I'll tell everyone the following:

(1) When applying the trapargs-patch-2.0 to the kernel,
you *may* get two rejected hunks (hunks 5 and 6 on entry.S).
These hunks are patches against the kgdb support in axp-diffs.
If you did not previously apply axp-diffs to your kernel,
it is safe to ignore this error.

(2) In order to run Adobe Acrobat Reader, you must make
the following change to the /usr/local/Acrobat3/bin/acroread
script:

310,311c310,311
< LD_LIBRARY_PATH="`prepend
"$ACRO_INSTALL_DIR/$ACRO_CONFIG/lib:$ACRO_INSTALL_DIR/$ACRO_CONFIG/lib"
"$LD_LIBRARY_PATH"`"
< export LD_LIBRARY_PATH

---
>     X86_LD_LIBRARY_PATH="`prepend "$ACRO_INSTALL_DIR/$ACRO_CONFIG/lib:$ACRO_INSTALL_DIR/$ACRO_CONFIG/lib" "$X86_LD_LIBRARY_PATH"`"
>     export X86_LD_LIBRARY_PATH


	The reason for this change is that, in order to avoid conflicts
	with the Linux/Alpha shared libraries, all environment
	variables of the form LD_... must be changed to X86_LD_...
	to take effect in emulated programs.
