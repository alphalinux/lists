I had the EXACT same problem. I had a 166UDB with a plain RedHat 4.0
installation and I upgraded the provided glibc to glibc-0_961212-4, as
well as rpm-2_3_2-1. After I got past the eth0 hang, I had the exact same
problem at the login prompt. I dare say it's not a problem with XF86, but
glibc compatibility.

I posted a question about this 2 weeks ago, twice I might add, and never
got a response. Good luck getting one.

Anyway, my solution involved nuking the old system and I'll get around to
reinstalling RH4.1 sometime in the future. At least the UDB isn't a
production machine, then I'd be in trouble, but atleast I've got my trusty
old dUNIX box.
