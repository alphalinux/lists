Hi All,
As I promised earlier, I recompiled POV-Ray using gcc instead of cc
and ran it on the machine (AS 500/500) again. The results look good
for gcc. It is slightly slower, but not by a substantial factor. Here
are the results for both compilers:

time ./unix/povray +a0.3 +w640 +h480 +iKALEID5.POV +v

Digital Unix cc:
Time For Parse: 0 hours 0 minutes 2.0 seconds (2 seconds)
Time For Trace: 1 hours 45 minutes 57.0 seconds (6357 seconds)
Total Time: 1 hours 45 minutes 59.0 seconds (6359 seconds)
6333.781u 0.627s 1:45:58.95 99.6% 0+37k 189+408io 5pf+0w

Digital Unix gcc:
Time For Parse: 0 hours 0 minutes 2.0 seconds (2 seconds)
Time For Trace: 2 hours 10 minutes 34.0 seconds (7834 seconds)
Total Time: 2 hours 10 minutes 36.0 seconds (7836 seconds)
7210.081u 8.836s 2:10:36.39 92.1% 0+38k 173+424io 0pf+0w

I was doing some text editing on the machine, which is why its not running
at ~100% like the cc binary is. If you do some math with the 92% cpu
usage, it looks like 10 minutes of wall time is due to other processes
running, thus we can guess that the run would have taken 2 hours 0 minutes
if I was not doing anything else on the machine. That would make it about
15 minutes slower than cc, which is not too bad for a 2 hour run. I will
rerun it again tonight when the machine is idle to make sure this is correct.

Thanks,

Jim
