I had this same problem myself, and I eventually got around it by
using my friends account an a DEC UNIX 3.2 machine, and ftp'ing the
files :) Perhaps not completely legal, but I do own a DEC UNIX
license, for versions 3.2 and 4.0A, so I'm sure it's not that bad.

The thing is the DEC UNIX CD's are formatted, DEC UFS, which,
unfortunately, is not compliant with the Solaris/AIX/etc UFS
filesystem included in the linux kernel. You'll have to modify the
UFS drivers to mount the DEC UNIX CD's :)

best of luck.
