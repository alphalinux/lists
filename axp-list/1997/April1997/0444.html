On Thu, 10 Apr 1997, Nicholas Alexander Bastin wrote:

> but ipfwadm breaks on the last necessary line in the HOW-TO. I enter:
>
> ipfwadm -F -p deny
>
> first, and it does this just fine returning me to the prompt. Then I do:
>
> ipfwadm -F -a m -S 192.168.1.0/24 -D 0.0.0.0/0 (yes, I'm aware of
> the potential security problem here...)
>
> and the computer returns:
>
> ipfwadm: setsockopt failed: Invalid argument

Well, I don't know if its Linux/AXP -based problem but have you tried
to add -P parameter?

I also enter

/sbin/ipfwadm -F -p deny

first and then I just type

/sbin/ipfwadm -F -a masquerade -P tcp -S 192.168.1.0/24 -D 0.0.0.0/0
/sbin/ipfwadm -F -a masquerade -P udp -S 192.168.1.0/24 -D 0.0.0.0/0

and everything works fine.
