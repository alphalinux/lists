I've heard a lot about ARC epoch (1980), but absolutely nothing
concerning Digital UNIX' one. The fact is that when the clock is set to
1997 under Digital UNIX 4.0A, it shows 2045 under Linux. RTC year
register read through /dev/port contains 45; BINARY bit is set, so
that's not just a silly BCD problem. Does anybody know what's going on
and why this has been neither addressed in the `clock' program nor discussed
in this list before? I suppose many people boot Linux via SRM (which
agrees with D.Unix about the RTC) - aren't they experiencing the same
problem? Or they just don't care about what SRM thinks the time is?
The whole thing looks like there is another, different, epoch, started
in 1952 (what a strange starting year - isn't it by any chance the year
Digital Equipment Corporation was born?).

Any comments?

Thanks
