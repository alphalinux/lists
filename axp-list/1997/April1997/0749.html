Due to negligence at 03:06 PM 4/16/97 -0500, Anthony Pardini let this roam
freely into my Inbox of Terror:
|>
|>
|>On Almost every piece of software I try to compile on my alpha running
|>redhat 4.1 with the applicable updates, I get warning cast form pointer
|>to integer of different size.

This is about the most common error around in C. On x86's pointers and
integers are the same size, on 64-bit archs like the Alpha, they're not.
It's a fairly easy fix, as well :)

|>kernel: netstat(5524): unalighned trap at 000001555556ba89c:
0000000011ffff904 2d 10

This is another coding fubar. Alphas want natural alignment; programs that
don't have this are said to not be '64-bit clean'. It's nothing serious,
but it does cause a performance hit.
