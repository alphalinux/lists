>Unfortunately, no, not yet. Although the developers are working on the

g77-0.5.20 has support for Alphas in general. However, it seems
nobody using a Linux/ELF system was available to alpha-test
version 0.5.20 (and it seems ECOFF has some bad bugs in it anyway?),
so getting 0.5.20 to work on a "modern" Linux/Alpha system might
not be easy.

0.5.21 is scheduled to fix this problem, plus a variety of other
bugs introduced in 0.5.20 that afflict most or all ports.

However, 0.5.21 is going to be delayed due to my having to return the
DEC Alpha I'd been loaned to do the g77 work -- fortunately, a
"replacement gift" of a UDB is being made by Craftwork, the company
that originally did the legwork to get the Cabriolet loaner to me
a year ago (but they can't afford the price they're being told
they have to pay if I/they don't return the Cabriolet, and neither
can I -- apparently year-old-plus Cabriolet's are very valuable!).

So, thanks to Craftwork, I had a Cabriolet to work on for a year (and
wish I'd been able to make better use of it, instead of spending much
of that time doing other work to fund my own g77 work), and will
soon have a UDB to continue doing g77/Alpha work.

Once I get an external box added to the UDB (which hasn't arrived
yet) to contain a 2GB disk I have (and had planned to add to the
Cabriolet, now glad I didn't get around to it), I'll be able to
resume Alpha-related work on g77, assuming the UDB has the performance
necessary for compiler development.

And, I suspect the UDB will have a Linux/ELF system on it, instead
of Linux/ECOFF, which means I can work on getting g77 to support ELF.

As usual, for ongoing g77 info, watch:

ftp://gnu.ai.mit.edu/g77.plan

tq vm, (burley)
