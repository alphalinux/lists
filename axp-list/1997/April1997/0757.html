>I suspect cache size is important to all class of computers. The more
>cached memory that can be fetched at the speed of the processor will do
>wonders for any application. So Why do most intel boxes limit themselfs
>to 256k/512k - I have not seem a 1meg/2/4/8 cache at all on the intel
>boxes - oh well.

Cache size is very important, but only to a point. Without going into a bunch of detail, there is a point of diminishing returns with cache size for the most common application profiles. System designers have to determine the point at which the cost of adding cache memory (which is relatively expensive) outweighs the benefits to performance for the majority of users.

Most Intel boxes limited themselves to 256KB or 512KB because the designers of the support chipset (where L2 cache is implemented) have done the research to determine that in most cases there is little benefit to providing a bigger one. There is no inherent reason why an Intel chip couldn't have 1MB, or 100MB, of cache, other than the fact that in most cases it's a waste of money.

Different processors, or different application profiles on the same processor, have different thresholds for this cost/benefit point.
