My suggestion is to check out xemacs. emacs in general is a big memory
hog and you may have a few xemacs zombies laying around going nuts. I saw
one emacs process on a machine here at school not quit when the user
logged out and it went crazy. Before it was finally killed it had been
using 48% of the cpu time and 32meg all that it was alotted under digital
unix.
