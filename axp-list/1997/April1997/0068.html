On Wed, 2 Apr 1997, Dave Riesz wrote:

>
> When I try to compile Mesa (v2.2) on an Alpha, I get the following errors
> when the makefile gets to "readpix.c":
>
> (make linux-alpha)
> gcc -c -I../include -O2 -pedantic -L/usr/X11R6/lib readpix.c
> gcc: Internal compiler error: program cc1 got fatal signal 6
>
> (make linux-elf)
> gcc -c -I../include -O2 -ansi -pedantic -fPIC -funroll-loops -ffast-math
> -DSHM readpix.c
> gcc: Internal compiler error: program cc1 got fatal signal 6

This sounds like a bad SIMM.

Take a look at <http://www.bitwizard.nl/sig11/>

To quote briefly from the page:

<begin quote>
QUESTION

My kernel compile crashes with

gcc: Internal compiler error: program cc1 got fatal signal 11

What is wrong with the compiler?
Which version of the compiler do I need?
Is there something wrong with the kernel?

ANSWER

Most likely there is nothing wrong with your installation, your compiler or
kernel. It very likely has something to do with your hardware. There are a
variety of subsystems that can be wrong, and there is a variety of ways to
fix it. Read on, and you'll find out more.
<end quote>
