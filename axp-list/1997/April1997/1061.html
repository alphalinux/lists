Well, its not exactly the same board; yours has a 21140 chip on it, the
MIATA has a builtin 21142.

If the 21140 is on a DEC-made board, ie the DE500, you could try the
de4x5 driver instead.

The unaligned traps are because, in Donald Becker's words:

> Most hardware/drivers can align received frames on arbitrary byte
> boundaries. The Tulip hardware can only align receive frames on 4 byte
> boundaries, so the 14 byte header misaligns the IP header. There are
> comments about this in the driver.
>
> The only way around this to always copy the rx frame to align the Rx buffer.
> You can do this in the current driver by setting the COPYBREAK value to less
> than the minimum packet size. Could you do that and let me know what
> happens?
>
> There must be a better solution, especially considering that both chips are
> from DEC. Someone must have considered this problem...

Unfortunately, there is no "better" solution; if you look at the de4x5
driver, it uses the "copy" method described above, to get rid of the
unaligned accesses at the cost of some performance.
