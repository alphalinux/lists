On Tue, 22 Apr 1997, Ben Schumacher wrote:
> Alright, quick question here. Please tell me that Linux is able to handle
> a RAID controller(not sure make or model). The company where I work is
> looking to get a powerful Alpha machine to setup as a news server, however,
> they are planning to go with DEC Unix or Windows NT because they know these
> systems will be able to cope with a system running 63 gigs through a RAID-1
> controller. However, I don't want to have to deal with(or mantain) either
> of these two operating systems, especially since I love RedHat on our other
> machines(6 i386's and 1 sparc).
>
> So if somebody could tell me yes, Linux is RAID capable(hardware, I know it
> has software RAID built in) then I would be very happy and I would make
> sure my company does not give more money to Gates.

Someone here who I trust was saying that a local ISP was using the
filesystem striping capability in the linux kernel. He was saying that
the performance increse was linear to the number of disks. So that may
save you a wack of money in terms of raid controllers if you just use
the kernel features.
