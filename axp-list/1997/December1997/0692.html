>
> I installed glibc-2.0.5c-11.alpha.rpm from the errata directory. Now my
> system is fubar. The installation of the RPM failed

"failed" really means what? Such small details are important!

> and after that, the system was unusable.

If this is like it was on 4.2, and most likely it is but I am guessing,
then one of rpms messed up links in your /usr/lib/ directory.
If I remember correctly this was /usr/lib/libtk* which was left
with a link pointing to nowhere. This was causing an exception
in 'ldconfig' (a bug, but that's life until somebody will fix it)
leaving an empty /etc/ld.so.cache. After such sequence of events
your system will behave very poorly indeed.

A cure for the above is to restore link sanity in /usr/lib and to rerun
ldconfig - this time without bombing out.

> When I tried to reboot, I got:

In such situation a very bad move.

> init: error in loading shared libraries

This would support my conjecture. ldconfig runs in a startup sequence
with results as above and you are goner.

> libc.so.6.1: cannot open shared object file: No such file or directory.

Indeed; an empty /etc/ld.so.cache is consulted resulting in the above.

> What I need help with is after I boot from the install floppy and select an
> upgrade, I can switch to a console, but how do I do the RPM commands?

You don't! Mount your disk read/write, go to usr/lib there (not
/usr/lib!), fix links and try to reboot. With fixed links ldconfig
will rebuild /etc/ld.so.cache and everything will be happy.
