| 1. WRT the QLogic, could you check the boot logs and see what IRQ is
| assigned to the QLogic? Also, which slot have you installed that card
| into, a 32-bit or 64-bit PCI slot?

It is installed in a 32-bit slot (it's a 32 bit card), but I think it
is detected strange. The milo/kernel from rh4.2 detects it at irq 36
but milo/kernel from rh5.0 detects it at irq 0, and makes it work way
to slow. Under 4.2, hdparm -t says 7.8mb/s, under 5.0: 0.8Mb/s. The
examples here are with the original milo/kernels that came with the
respective distributions.

RedHat 4.2:
qlogicisp : new isp1020 revision ID (5)
scsi0 : QLogic ISP1020 SCSI on PCI bus 1 device 8 irq 36 base 0xc000

RedHat 5.0:
qlogicisp : new isp1020 revision ID (5)
qlogicisp : unexpected interrupt on line 0
scsi0 : QLogic ISP1020 SCSI on PCI bus 1 device 8 irq 0 base 0x9000

| 2. did you see my previous posting WRT the "media options" you can specify
| with a bootline option to the tulip.c driver? Try specifically setting
| any that seem appropriate to the hardware connection you have, and see
| if it works better. version 083 and beyond of that driver work best and
| without any Alpha-related patches. A number of people have had to use
| this technique, as the autoselection is not bulletproof...

If i specify ether=0,1,eth0 (bnc connector) to the tulip-driver, the
card is not detected at all by either of the milo/kernels. I got
another suggestion to try the de4x5 driver instead, but that driver
does not detect the card, do you know what boot-line options I could
pass it? My card (builtin) is a eth0: Digital DS21142/3 Tulip at
0x8800, 00 00 f8 75 77 f6, IRQ 24 (reported by the tulip-driver with
no options). I have a bnc-connector plugged to the motherboard.
