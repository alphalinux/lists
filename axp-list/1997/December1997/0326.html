With regards to your message at 02:00 PM 12-09-97 +0200, Mika Kortelainen.
Where you stated:
>> what of the media. can i take a tape from on drive and stuff it into
>> another. are all compression schemes the same for all manufacturers? or is
>> this just another hellish issue!
>>
>Without compression, yes (I wrote with my WangDAT, and I was able to read
>those tapes using some HP and IBM DAT's at work). But not with compression.
>Although I'm not sure if DDS-DC (used by that WangDAT) is supposed to be
>compatible (I think newer DDS-2 drives have another compression method,
>but I don't know if that's common to every manufacturer).
It gets worse. The compression for DDS-1 is USUALLY compatible. BUT, most
newer drives either use DDS-2, or even 3. And, frankly, as far as I have
seen, they all have different ideas what to do in compression mode. If you
really need to make tapes transportable the only reliable way is to turn
off all compression. Same applies, but to lesser degree to 8mm
Exabyte-style tape. There at least the mechansim is always the same Sony
hardware. DLT, however, seems to be free of this problem, albeit with a
higher price tag. Still, you get what you pay for.
