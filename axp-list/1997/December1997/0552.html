> "The 21264 is a four-way out-of-order-issue microprocessor
> that performs dynamic scheduling, register renaming, and
> speculative execution."
> >>>>>>>>>>>>^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
>
> I've heard of `speculative execution' in the stock market, but
> ... what is this in a microprocessor?

It means the 21264 will execute instructions that is doesn't KNOW
will need to be executed. It's just assuming previous guess on
which way a branch will go is right. This helps prevent the
latency associated with waiting for calculations that branches
depend on to finish.

Hopefully your right and execution continues (you speculated correctly),
but sometimes you don't and all the speculative instructions after
the point you guessed wrong are thrown away, machine state restored,
and resumed in the correct direction.
