Hey,

a short while ago there was discussion about problems with 2.0.32
and em86. I haven't seen any solutions here yet, so I thought
I post what I found out.

First, I had a pure 2.0.32 kernel with 2.0.30 patches (from
gatekeeper, under em86/v0.2) and em86 did work (after fixing obvious
conflicts manually). Those with problems apparently had pure 2.0.32 with
2.0.32 patches (under Kernels at gatekeeper). So, a compared those source
trees and there's one significant difference between them:

in the pure include/asm-alpha/a.out.h and also with patch-2.0.32
there is the following define:
#define STACK_TOP (0x00120000000UL)

but with 2.0.30 patches it says:
#define STACK_TOP ((current->personality & ADDR_MAX_32BIT) ? 0x100000000UL : \
((current->personality&ADDR_MAX_31BIT) ? 0x80000000UL : 0x00120000000UL))

When I changed it to the latter, em86 with 2.0.32 and 2.0.32 patches started
to work (no reports about "Your kernel does not support 31-bit addressing")
anymore. And I have another report which supports this solution, too.

If this is the right fix, it perhaps could be included in the next 2.0.32
patches?
