With regards to your message at 07:08 PM 12-04-97 -0800, Bill Broadley.
Where you stated:
>Whats the story on EIDE performance on the Alpha LX motherboards?
>
>Net vendors seem to be selling EIDE linux boxes now.
>
>Is the performance still a fraction of what you expect on an X86? I.e.
>1-2 mb/sec instead of 4-6 MB/sec?
The IDE on any of the DEC or Samsung or other variants we have seen is NOT
Ultra33 DMA. MEaning they are quite slow. Even more problematic is the CPU
usuage when accessing drives through this interface. Usage runs at 70% to
90% on normal HD or CDROM accesses.
>
>If so I guess I'll spend the extra $500 or so for a 4 gb ultra/wide vs
>a 6.4 gb EIDE.
A very good idea.
>
>Out of curiosity does the SX board change anything?
The SX has a slightly slower RAM access than the LX, and the chip on it has
no 96K L2 cache. This makes a smaller CPU die, and a lower cost, by about
$565.
Under NT the performance differences appear to be slower than what shows up
under Linux. Of course UNIX will NOT run on an SX.

If your budget is tight, consider the SX. If you can afford the difference
I suggest an LX is the better deal. Note also that RAM for the LX being
faster, is also slightly more expensive.
