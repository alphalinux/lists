Kudos to Jon Hall for fighting with DEC management to get a personal
license for DEC UNIX. I've been a Linux user on Intel and now Alpha and Sparc,
as well as FreeBSD and support Solaris Sparc, SGI IRIX and DEC UNIX. I got
the SCO "Free" Openserver and UnixWare CDs from them to see what they were
like. They are a great idea. You get the developer's kit as well as a bunch
of other trial packages. Jon's suggestion is on the money, but I think $100
for the personal license maybe too high. SCO charges you $19 for the media
for each product (i.e. UnixWare + packages was $19 and SCO Openserver +
packages was $19 + shipping) and the licensing is free from their web site.
