With regards to your message at 04:44 PM 12-11-97 -0600, Rob Zook. Where
you stated:
>At 11:20 PM 12/11/97 +0100, you wrote:
>>
>>Hello,
>>
>>I have a multia 233 with 24 Mb of RAM running linux and NT.
>>Do you think it's worth to buy some SIMM's (I mean, does it really improve
>>the performance, that aren't fairly good compared to pentium that are
>>said to be equivalent)
>
>Well, I don't know if upgrading improves the performance as much on a
>233Mhz Multia, but on my 166Mhz going from 16Mb to 32Mb caused a
>rather dramatic improvement in performance in Linux. I think that's
>simply a UNIX "feature". UNIX relies heavily context switching and
>the higher the memory the faster the swaping will be, in general.
>
Further, the UDB has a rather slow disk subsystem, and the effect of adding
RAM is to provide adequate disk buffers. This too can make a substantial
performance difference.
Finally, with the slow disk subsystem , keeping the machine out of swap can
also help.
