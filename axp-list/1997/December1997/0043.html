On Mon, 1 Dec 1997, Carlos Carvalho wrote:

> > 14. reboot with /vmlinux-2.0.30-my-very-own.gz.
> Sorry, how do I do this? I'm new to linux-alpha... All the other steps
> I understand.

when you have compiled your new kernel, you get this one in the [source
path]/arch/alpha/boot/vmlinux.gz. If you want to be safe, rename this, put
it in the / with your current kernel. When booting, specify your new
kernel instead.

Or, simply replace the current with the new one. But, then, make sure you
have a good and well-working boot disk.. ;-)
