>>> David Goldstein said:
>
> I do NOT know what the patches provided for 2.0.30 do so I am not sure
> whether to attempt to apply them.

Among the most important things supplied in those patches is support for
Alpha platforms that are not included in the pristine 2.0.30 sources.

> Are there patches for 2.0.32?

Not yet, but Real Soon Now (tm)... ;-}

> Are the patches (axp-diff etc...) already incorporated?

In general, no, although there are some small bugfixes that are in 2.0.32 now.

> Primarily I would like to know
> if it is safe for me to boot it up (even if it isn't a stable kernel... I
> understood that 2.0.31 had crashed some harddrives because of filesystem
> enhancements)...

First, AFAIK, stability of 2.0.32 is much better than 2.0.31, and the
disk trashing was fixed.

If you have a machine that is supported in 2.0.32, then you should be safe
using it without any patches.

If you have a newer machine not supported in there, or have a hardware
configuration that needs some of the "adjustments" put into the patches,
then you'll need to wait. As soon as I've had a bit more of a chance to
test them on various machines, I'll make them available and announce it
to this list.
