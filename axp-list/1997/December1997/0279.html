I have recently purchased an Alpha:
LX 566 MHz Motherboard (21164A CPU),
4 PCI/2 ISA, 2 Serial/1Parallel Port,
2 MB 9ns SRAM Cache,
128 MB SDRAM,
ITI4140UE Ultra Wide SCSI-3 Controller with 10/100Mbps Ethernet RJ45,
4.5 GB UW SCSI Hard Drive 7200 RPM (Seagate),
Matrox Millenium II 4MB PCI Video Card,
12X Toshiba SCSI CD-ROM Drive,
but as I currently eagerley await it's arrival, I realize that I know NOTHING
about this architecture! I have over 13 years experience with the Intel
architecture, and somehow until just now failed to realize how different these
would be! I am quite eager to experiment with this, however, I was hoping
someone here could help to give me an idea of what I'm in for. In particular
my initial question is what distribution, if any I should use. I have seen and
browsed through the axp kernel patches, are these included in "Alpha"
disributions? My experience with linux is long and enjoyed, and I currently use
the RedHat distribution, currently version 5.0 which I have upgraded to 2.0.32
kernel distribution. First, is RedHat the way to go with Alpha, and if so, will
I be able to upgrade to 2.0.32, or will I be restricted to 2.0.31 or below? I
also have experience installing systems without distribution, and would be
willing to do this with this Alpha, if that is the best method of installation.
Any help anyone could provide me about distributions in particular, but of
course I would be most appreciative of ANY help one could provide to help "warn"
me of any problems I may be running into, would be most welcome. Thank you for
your help, I look forward to working with you in the future as I explore this
wonderful technology.
