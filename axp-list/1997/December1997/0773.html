On Tue, 23 Dec 1997 Jay.Estabrook@digital.com wrote:

>
> >>> "Thomas M. Langley" said:
> >
> > (The XLT.img will not boot even though I have a "true XL as defined by
> > Digital").
>
> Uh, XLT stands for XL-Turbo, not XL-True... :-\
>
> XLT is the EV5-based box (300, 366, and 433 MHz, IIRC).
>
> XL is EV4 based (233 and 266 MHz).
>
I understand the 'turbo' designation. Digital seems to like it a bit eh? I
call our big boys at the bank 8200's and 8400's, the FE calls them Turbo
Lasers. Go figure.
> > When I issue:
> > b fd0:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 load_ramdisk=1 prompt_ramdisk=1
> > at the MILO prompt, everything seems to go just fine until:
> >
> > VFS: Insert root floppy disk to be loaded into ramdisk and press ENTER
> > RAMDISK: Couldn't find a valid ramdisk starting at 0.
>
> At this point, I'd have to guess that your XL really is a "true" XL, and
> that you used the AVANTI kernel.
>
This is where I got the 'true' stuff. I read message from someone a few
days ago that said there was a difference between a 'true' XL and an
Avanti based system. I tried the XLT kernel because there was no XL kernel
and I had a cold machine.
> The biggest difference between a "true" XL and an AVANTI is that the floppy
> drive (on the XL) cannot DMA to all of CPU memory (the way it is done on
> AVANTI), and therefore requires some special work-arounds to make it
> function, thereby necessitating a separate MILO and kernel.
>
> Try to find a "true" XL kernel (use of your old one is OK so long as it has
> all the drivers and such required by the RH install), and I think you'll
> have better luck.
>
This is exactly what I did. I used the 4.2 XL.IMG and the 5.0 ramdisk.img.
This allowed me to install 5.0. I had to have the installer copy the 5.0
AVANTI.IMG to the hard disk for the first boot up, then I gen'd a new
kernel with the CPU type of XL. All is well now, but RedHat should have
included the XL.IMG file this time also.
