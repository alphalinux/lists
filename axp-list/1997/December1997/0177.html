>
> Whats the story on EIDE performance on the Alpha LX motherboards?

So far they they do not really work worth to mention. Until there
will be IDE DMA support in chipsets available on Alpha boards then
there is not even worth to bother with this. LX does not need to
apply. Some future models of boards are supposed to have it. Then
we will see. If IDE will be killing caches on Alpha (it is much more
CPU intensive than SCSI and Alpha is a RISC chip) then DMA will be also
of a little help, but this remains to be seen.

> Net vendors seem to be selling EIDE linux boxes now.

Marketing. This is aimed at these uniformed suckers who are going
to run on this NT anyway and really do not care about performance
issues. These buyers will rather "save" by putting a Volkswagen
engine into a Porsche; mostly they are not going to use these boxes
themselves but some other poor sods in their organizations.

> Is the performance still a fraction of what you expect on an X86? I.e.
> 1-2 mb/sec instead of 4-6 MB/sec?

This is the smallest problem. Complaints that a machine runs bigger
calculations at 1/3 of an expected speed is.

> If so I guess I'll spend the extra $500 or so for a 4 gb ultra/wide vs
> a 6.4 gb EIDE.

Yes, you will be well advised.

> Out of curiosity does the SX board change anything?

It does. It is definitely much cheaper and is also much slower
(depending on what you are doing it could be 40%). A nominal clock
rate of a chip is only a small part of the whole story. OTOH - if
you know what you are buying and what for this can be quite adequate
solution for your needs.
