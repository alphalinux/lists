Getting into the spirit of the season a little early (not to mention
getting axp-list off my back ;-), I've put together a few gifts for
public consumption:

A new set of alpha-patches based on 2.0.30, which includes SX164 support,
and preliminary support (untested and perhaps not working) for the
Deskstation RUFFIAN (aka RPX164-2, but soon to be aka AlphaPC164-UX or BX).
These patches may also fix the "blank text" problem some folks have had
when using a high-end Powerstorm graphics card. Also included in here are
most of the "ap-0.2-fixes" patches, like RTC epoch, EM86 trap fix, and sound.

A new set of MILO sources based on 2.0.30, which includes some new EBSDK
PALcode for certain platforms (EB164/PC164/LX164/SX164). There are no binaries
available at this time, though the ones on gatekeeper in Test-Milos-2.0.30+
are very close to what you can build with these sources. I will make a more
comprehensive release of MILO at a later time, with proper binaries and updates
to documentation for things that changed (very minor).

And last but not least, a first cut at porting the full alpha-patches over to
the 2.0.32 sources. The port was done with the latest alpha-patches (above) as
the starting point, and it has been quite stable for me, but, as always, YMMV.

So, that said, the following files are now available at:

gatekeeper.dec.com:/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Kernels/

1. alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.4.gz

Apply these, as before, to *pristine* 2.0.30 sources.

2. milo-sources-2.0.30-0.4.tgz

Full source tree, including support for LX164 and SX164.
Use with alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.4.gz is HIGHLY RECOMMENDED!!!

3. alpha-patches-2.0.32-0.1.gz

Apply these to *pristine* 2.0.32 sources.

Application of the patches should follow previous practice, which is
well-noted in the archives of axp-list... ;-}

Also note that I've made no attempt to see whether "xconfig" or "menuconfig"
will work correctly after the patches are applied; "make config", on the
other hand, is *well* tested.

Good luck, and as before, PLEASE report any problems to me.
