Harvey J. Stein wrote:

> Does anyone know what the best optimization flags to use with egcs
> would be?
>
> I've tried:
>
> -O9
> -O9 -funroll-loops -finline-functions -ffast-math -fomit-frame-pointer -mcpu=21164a -static
> -O9 -funroll-all-loops -finline-functions -ffast-math -fomit-frame-pointer -mcpu=21164 -static -fexpensive-optimiztions -fschedule-insns -fschedule-insns2 -frerun-cse-after-loop
> -O9 -funroll-all-loops -finline-functions -ffast-math -fomit-frame-pointer -mcpu=21164 -static -fexpensive-optimizations -fschedule-insns -fschedule-insns2 -frerun-cse-after-loop
> -O3 -ffast-math -fomit-frame-pointer -mcpu=21164 -static -fexpensive-optimizations -funroll-loops
>
> I assumed -O9 was the same as -O3, but then switched back to -O3 just
> in case this isn't true. And, btw, I found all of the above to be
> slower than gcc 2.7.2.1 with -O2. The first was close enough that the
> difference could be considered noise, but the others were
> significantly slower (except for the last which I haven't finished
> testing yet.)
>
> Also, the -mcpu=21164a failed in compilation with lots of complaints
> about the $at register being used in a noat environment again.
