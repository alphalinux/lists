Did you consult the webpages concerning the tulip.c driver, at:

http://cesdis.gsfc.nasa.gov/linux/drivers/tulip.html

If so, you'll note that there is a "media options" table, whereby you can
explicitly tell the driver with a boot line option *EXACTLY* what media it
should expect, rather than letting it try to autosense. You add to the end
of your boot line something like:

ether=0,n,eth0

where the "n" is chosen from the table of options (just snipped from the site):

0 10baseT (with autoselect)
1 10base2
2 AUI
3 100baseTx
4 10baseT-FD
5 100baseTx-FD
6 100baseT4
7 100baseFx
8 100baseFx-FD
9 MII 10baseT
10 MII 10baseT-FD
11 MII
12 10baseT (no autoselect), v0.69 and later only
13 MII 100baseTx
14 MII 100baseTx-FD
15 MII 100baseT4

This was necessary for me to do when using a BNC connection and 21142; I think
I used "ether=0,2,eth0".

Good luck.
