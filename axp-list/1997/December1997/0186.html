Ronny Ranerup wrote:
> bill@math.UCDavis.edu:
> >Whats the story on EIDE performance on the Alpha LX motherboards?
> >Is the performance still a fraction of what you expect on an X86? I.e.
> >1-2 mb/sec instead of 4-6 MB/sec?
>
> Yes, expect 1-2 instead of the 6-10 you get on a good X86/disk combo.

This is only a small part of the whole story. An interaction with
the whole system seems to be much more important than raw numbers.

> I began tinkering in the IDE-interface on the LX, but didn't get anywhere.

Until you will see Alpha boards with DMA support for IDE it is not
even worth to bother to try IMO. Once this will happen then we will
see.

> If you only could get at the registers of the CMD chipset I don't
> think it would be hard to enable the performance goodies,

Nope. Not with currently available board designs.

> but until then, lousy IDE performance...

Even if you would get a stellar IDE performance if your instruction
caches are gone as a result of a disk access then your system, as a
whole, will suffer badly (unless you are not using CPU at all, I guess :-)
