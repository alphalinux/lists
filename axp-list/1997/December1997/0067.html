> compiled in the lowlevel driver (AWE32) but it isn't found. Pnpdump
> report tells that Wavetable's ioport should be 0x620 but that's reserved
> on XLT. According to Creative's WWW page, also 0xa?0 and 0xe?0
> are possible and I tried some addresses (e.g. 0x0a00, 0x0a20, 0xe00,
> 0x0e20) but I'm still waveless.

Great, it appears Creative's WWW page is a bit misleading.
Actually that wavetable part needs THREE i/o ports and pnpdump
finds just the first one. I was pointed to this by a reply and it
was said in AWE FAQ very clearly, apparently many linux/i386
owners have had the same problem, too.

I chose ioports 0xa00, 0xa20, and 0xe20, and immediately everything
started to work. At first I was a bit worried because it reported
0K as the amount of memory, but after I loaded a sound bank, it
changed to 512K (which is the right value). Now I only need to
find a cable to connect it my amplifier instead of these crappy
headphones.

Thanks for anyone who helped!
