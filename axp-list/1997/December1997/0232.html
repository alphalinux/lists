With regards to your message at 12:07 PM 12-06-97 -0500, Craig Burley.
Where you stated:
>>According to what we have heard from DEC so far, it's supposed to be pretty
>>solid. Of course so were the denials of the Intel deal...
>
>Heh...well, I don't recall exactly what was written here, but
>when I talked to my friend (who works for Digital, for awhile
>as one of its spokemen) after the article, he was very careful
>about saying "we are not saying it is true, we are not saying
>it is false". And, it turned out to be false, that is, I thought
>it was saying Digital was selling *Alpha* to Intel as part of a
>deal, and that didn't happen. (Well, not yet. ;-) Personally,
>I'm *much* happier that only Intel's *chip-making* expertise is
>getting its hands on Digital's facilities, than if (as I gathered
>was the claim of the article) Intel's *CPU-designing* expertise
>got its hands on Digital's Alpha architecture.

But they did. One of the biggest motivating factors for Intel to do the
deal was to get out from the lawsuits filed against it for stealing Digital
architecture for Pentium class CPUs. I suspect that some of the technology
is going to make Merced a lot easier too.
The biggest gain for Intel was the way that t (inevitably) handled by the
Wintel press. If you read the big readership magazines, html news sites,
etc. ( PC Mag, PC World, and all the rest of the Ziff Davis Windows empire,
for example) you get one clear message:

Alpha is dead. Digital lost. Intel won.

Frankly, I think Intel won too. DEC got $700 MILLION dollars for
Semiconductor. Did they re-invest it into more R&D? All I have heard of is
the handful of people going to TOEM division, and the 2000 or so going to
work for Intel.

Semiconductor was the division that brought us the affordable motherboards
and CPUs that most of us run Linux on. We now see the next generation of
boards that are NT only, basically. OK, Linux will run on them, but not
UNIX. 64 bit PCI slots? Gone. SRM? Doubtful at best. We should thank our
lucky stars that we still seem to have Jay++, Jon, Jim and others still
involved. Apparently Dave Rusling is now an Intelite, I hear.

Enough doom and gloom. At least Samsung is running with the ball, and with
more Linux enthusiasm than we ever saw before. Their literature lists 2
"software options" for the Alpha: NT and Linux. This is, I believe, a
first. I think it is the first major hardware maker to EVER recognise and
list Linux as an operating system, not just as a passing note of curiousity.

And the UX and BX boards are going to make a certain impact on the Alpha
market.
Remeber that posting this week from the lady looking for ISA ethernet and
video for an LX system? That's a vendor building a machine, but finding
that 4 PCI slots aren't enough.
Ruffian (UX & BX) will certainly help that problem. I also know of a
third-party manufacturer about to release an UltraDMA33 EIDE 64 bit PCI
card for the Alpha boards. In the words of their Vice-President, and the
man responsible for marketing "Now that some serious players ( Samsung) are
involved in marketing the Alpha, we believe that the market will become
sizable enough to make this board profitable. We also believe that we will
derive enough revenue fron the Linux Alpha market to make releasing drivers
for Linux worthwhile"
Now THAT is progress!
At COMDEX I saw quite a few 64 bit PCI cards. What do we think they are
designed for? Alpha! Yes, there are also Sun boards with PCI and 64 bit,
but they are VERY few.
Every single one of those 64 bit PCI board makers I spoke to at COMDEX are
releasing with, or plan to release Linux drivers for them in the next 3
months.

</soapbox>
