"James R. Shipman" <JimS@ibm.net> writes:

>
> At 04:04 AM 12/16/97 -0500, you wrote:
> >Far and away the largest stability problem with my Linux/Alpha box is
> >errors of the following form:
> > Unable to handle kernel paging request at virtual address <foo>
> >followed by an Oops (usually from a daemon, but occassionally from a
> >user process).
>
> I see this problem too on my PC164LX, and there is NO WAY that it is a disk
> failure!

The 2.0.x, x<=30 had very poor memory management which would lead to
Oopses & "unable to handle kernel paging request"s. They tried to fix
this in 2.0.31, & tried to fix the bugs in the fixes in 2.0.32. I
think they were fairly successful.

How much RAM do you have in your system? What are you running?

In any case, with 2.0.30, and probably with earlier kernels, one can
adjust some of the swap parameters to reduce the problem somewhat. I
sometimes do:

echo 20 3 1 3 10 2 2 4 32 4 16384 16384 >>/proc/sys/vm/swapctl

on my intel box to keep it from going into convulsions when running
netscape with 20 independent windows.

You can also try increasing the numbers you see when catting
/proc/sys/vm/freepages.

Or, you can try upgrading to 2.0.32, but then you might not be able to
apply the patches for shared memory & em86, but I don't know which
parts of them made it into 2.0.32.
