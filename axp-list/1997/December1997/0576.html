I know this has been asked a couple of times in the past, but I have
yet to see a satisfactory (or any, really) answer. So, I implore
again: Oh great kernel gurus, knowledgeable in the ways of the virtual
memory system and the mysteries of the Alpha architecture: I beg of
you, dispense a fragment of your omniscience upon us lowly users...

Far and away the largest stability problem with my Linux/Alpha box is
errors of the following form:
Unable to handle kernel paging request at virtual address <foo>
followed by an Oops (usually from a daemon, but occassionally from a
user process). Usually the system is toast shortly thereafter. VC
switching usually still works and any open shells accept keystrokes,
but any attempt to fork just hangs (i.e. `exec'ing a prog at the shell
level succeeds, but just calling a prog hangs the shell
irrecoverably). Quick downward spiral as I attempt to `exec su'
and try to get the system down cleanly before I have no more available
shells to exec from.

Of course, sometimes it's even less nice and the whole thing just
hangs hard shortly after the paging request failure.

I've seen this behavior in a number of kernels up to and including
2.0.28 (including a couple of devel kernels), and up through RedHat
4.2. I've tried a variety of differnt kernel compile params, all to
no success.

So... Does anyone have an intuition as to what's going on? Is it
something I have configured wrong (I'll gladly post/send my full
system HW/SW configs and full text of the error/oops log messages if
it would be of help), or is it a kernel bug? Does anyone have _any_
idea how to fix it? I'd really appreciate any feedback anyone has on
this. If this isn't the right forum for this question, please point
me to the right one and I'll forward this on to them.

Thanks much for any assistance.
