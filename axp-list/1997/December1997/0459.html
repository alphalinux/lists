>>> Dave Gilbert said:
> Uncle George wrote:
> >
> > why would bogo migs be time 2 clock speed. doesn't the 21164 issue 4
> > instructions at a time, and my bogomips would be 4 * clock ? k pasa?
>
> Remember that Bogomips are bogus; any direct comparison between BOGOmips
> is only valid on the same architecture. And the BOGOmips/clock ratio is
> different for each architecture and won't be directly related to the
> number of instructions it can run at once; mainly because there isn't
> anything useful in the BOGOmips loops to execute.

Well, *I* thought it was interesting regardless of its "bogosity"... ;-}

The reason I thought so, was because on *all* previous Alpha CPUs, the
BogoMIPS was *very* closely related to the MHz at which the processor was
running, no matter which CPU (EV4/LCA4/EV45/LCA45/EV5/EV56/PCA56).

To have this one show up at (roughly) *twice* the MHz made me sit up and
take notice... :-) :-)

Now, as to *why* it might be twice what is expected, the Product Brief says:

"The 21264 is a four-way out-of-order-issue microprocessor
that performs dynamic scheduling, register renaming, and
speculative execution."

So, its, uh, probably because of one (or more) of those things... ;-}
