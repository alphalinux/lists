On Sun, Dec 14, 1997 at 02:35:43PM +0200, Harvey J. Stein wrote:
> [ Lots of options ]

All of the -f bits you mention are implied by -O3 except for -ffast-math
and -funroll-loops; -O2 excludes -finline-functions. See

info gcc "invoking" "optimize"

for all of this spelled out.

Personally I've almost always found they slow things down a bit.
Just plain -O2 seems to work best at present.

> Also, the -mcpu=21164a failed in compilation with lots of complaints
> about the $at register being used in a noat environment again.

You need a newer binutils for that. The one from RH5 should be
just fine, but note that you can't just drop that into RH4.
