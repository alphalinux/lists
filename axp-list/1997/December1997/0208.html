> On Sat, 6 Dec 1997, Andreas Johansson wrote:

> > IIRC, all you have to do is recompile the ppp srpm.
>
> ?? Where do I do that?

Ah. I'm assuming you have compilers and stuff.

Go get the srpm via ftp (e.g. ftp://ftp.funet.fi/pub/Linux/mirrors/redhat/
redhat/redhat-5.0/SRPMS/ppp-2.2.0f-5.src.rpm).

At your shell prompt (as root) do:

rpm -i ppp-2.2.0f-5.src.rpm

This unpacks the sources for ppp in /usr/src/redhat.
Then do:

rpm -ba /usr/src/redhat/SPECS/ppp-2.2.0f.spec

This rebuilds the ppp-package. When it's ready (assuming no problem in
compiling) you can find your compiled rpms in /usr/src/redhat/RPMS/alpha.
You should rpm -i all produced rpm or else they won't show up in your rpm
database even though they are present on your disk.

After you are done you should clean up a little in your /usr/src/redhat/*
dirs. All compilation and unpacking is done there.
