> On Thu, 11 Dec 1997, BELABBAS Mohamed Ali wrote:
> > I have a multia 233 with 24 Mb of RAM running linux and NT.
> > Do you think it's worth to buy some SIMM's (I mean, does it really improve
> > the performance, that aren't fairly good compared to pentium that are
> > said to be equivalent)
>
> I went from 32 to 64 MB in my 166 MHz UDB and noticed a considerable
> improvement.
>
> Depending on what you'll be using it for, you might even consider 96 or
> 128 MB.

My question would be whether 'tis better to add 64MB to the UDB, or 128MB
to the Cyrix 150+ server... 'Twould be about the same price...

I would guess from the results I'm getting that a move from 24MB to something
*somewhat* higher would reap great rewards. Jumping to 48MB would probably
be rather beneficial. Further substantial increases in RAM would likely have
lesser results.

Back in about 1994, the "big" performance gain with Linux typically
came with moving from 8MB to 12MB. Moving on to 24MB would provide
better performance, but not nearly so much improvement as that first
4MB of memory...
