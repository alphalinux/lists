Is the card in your 486 a PCI?

You should be able to find the 70-nano true-parity simms that DEC specifies. Get 64M - the Multia needs all the RAM it can get (and then some if you run mondo-dog NT on the poor thing).

IDE drives show up in the arc menu as "multi(0)disk(0)rdisk(0)partition(1)". The SCSI disks are "scsi(0)disk(0)...
My Multia _warm_ boots fine off the IDE _after_, I said _AFTER_, it cold boots from the SCSI drive. Since the IDE is the default boot device, I get a boot menu error message on a cold boot, unless I select my alternate SCSI boot selection (I have a backup copy of the boot and os partitions on the SCSI drive).

Once it cold boots off the SCSI disk, I can warm boot from the IDE. I would really appreciate someone explaining what's up with that?!? Other than that little problem, the Multia is really reliable. Plus it takes some of the load off our central heating and doubles as a toaster!!
