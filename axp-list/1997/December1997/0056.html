I have CD's with RedHat 4.0. I installed it on my alpha and was happy as a
clam until I wanted NCFTP. Being that the CDs were just internet archives
which I had gotten for my intel machine along with a real RedHat-4.0 cd
from RedHat. I got my alpha abd found I had linux already on CD! When I
downloaded the closest release I could find RH4.2 of NCFTP I found that it
needed a newer glibc as well. This seemed risky but since I had just
installed the system I figured it wouldn't be to hard to reinstall. Well
turns out I blewup login capablility with the new glibc... Not being able
to login is real fun :)... I finaly found a site with the right ncftp
version. It works fine. My question is how I avoid this in the future.
RH4.0 is hard to find (and old). I don't really want to get new cds yet (I
am going to buy "real" RH5.0). When I upgrade is there an easy way to get
everything I need for the upgrade? Can I get the RH5.0 cd and have it
upgrade the whole system? Can I just use RPM to figure out what I will
need to replace?
