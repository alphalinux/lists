>
> Here's a trick that should help with this.
> When NT does a disk partition on SCSI, it likes to do a 64 x 32 x number of
> cylinders layout.

Two points:
- The subject suggests that the original poster is using IDE and not SCSI.
Maybe things apply in the same way. I am not sure.

- NT seems to "like" to do things as described if its partition(s), at
least of a FAT type, are small enough so some kind of a geometry
translation scheme is NOT triggered (probably below 512 Meg for a
partition).

> Translate what Linux fdisk is using, and stick to this.

IIRC fdisk from NT does not give you opportunity to specify disk
geometry. Why shoult it? It is "user friendly". So probably you
should start from the other end. Not giving NT a pretext to employ
a geometry translation is likely a wise thing to do.

If you already partitioned your disk and there appear to be conflicts how
partition table is seen, and you **really** do not want to repartition
now, I would strongly suggest of keeping a very good backups (which is a
wise policy in any case). You never know when BOTH sides will decide when
some particular piece of a disk real estate belongs to them. Most likely
you will end up with Linux superblock, and few other things, gone.
This will be a pretty good incentive to repartition cleanly. :-)
