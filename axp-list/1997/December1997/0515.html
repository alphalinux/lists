>I still feel it is the
>developers responsibility to create a viable product before asking for
>money. In other words, perform the work required and then ask for the
>dough.

To how many of the original authors of gcc, GNU Emacs, Linux, GNU
tar, gzip, g77 ;-), and so on have you sent donations as thank-yous
for the work you've already done (and benefitted from)?

In case *that* seems cranky, I'll say that, for me, the answer
is, basically, none of them. Partly because I didn't start using
*any* of that software until I was a year or two into writing g77,
and continue to make almost no use of free software for purposes
other than working on g77. But I expect that to change, and intend
to start donating to people who got this stuff working *as well as*
pledging/donating to those offering to start work on new stuff I
think would be worthwhile (even if I can afford only a little bit).

But I'll tell you for a surety the kinds of comments I often hear
as an author of a significant free-software package:

"I'd contribute to your g77 work, but it doesn't yet do the
things I need it to do."

"I'd contribute to your g77 work, but it already does just what
I want it to do."

So perhaps you can understand why I appreciate those (precious few,
you know who you are ;-) who "Just Do It" (contribute) rather than
offering excuses as to why they don't.
