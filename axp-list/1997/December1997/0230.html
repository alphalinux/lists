With regards to your message at 03:52 AM 12-06-97 -0800, Bill Broadley.
Where you stated:
>Thanks for all the input all. Generally seems like if your using alot
>of disk i/o your better off with scsi (much more so then with an x86 box).
No, it's the same on X86. Go look at the processor loading when you are
doing full-time reads on a CDROM.

>People report good overall performance with EIDE mainly because Linux
>does a decent job of using memory as a disk cache, when theres enough
>ram/not disk intensive jobs.
Yup. One can't be too thin, too rich, or have enough RAM!

>Apparently the LX motherboard was either designed with a poor eide chip,
>or doesn't take full advantage. Doesn't seem fixable.
Somewhat. It (and all the other Alpha boards) don't have UltraDMA or any of
the other new fancy EIDE interfaces. But then again, even with those IDE is
the "Windows" of the disk I/O world ( a large waste of a good system) The
only reason for IDE is price. If we're doing this on a $999 PAckard Bell, I
can see the justification. On a 600MHz Alpha? You've gotta be joking.
Certain comments about Volkswagen parts in Ferraris come to mind. ;=}

>
>Thanks for all the input.
>
>Anyone know if the SX is similiarly low performing in the area of EIDE?
>Seems like EIDE would be more important on the more price sensitive
>SX configurations. Any ideas, experiences etc?
Same chips basically. Nobady at DEC Semi apparently believed that anyone
serious would use IDE. Why they even put it on the boards at all is a bit
of a mystery to me.. The real estate could have been used for onboard
ethernet for example, at about the same cost.

Obviously my $.02 worth. </soapbox>
