Hi!

Well, I ordered it with Linux from Starship (via Onsale.com), but I put NT 4 Server on it first and never got around to installing Linux. I'll probably add Linux now that I've put a 2nd external SCSI on. The arc menu makes multi-booting a piece o' cake.

I added an internal 2.5" IDE drive by adjusting the SCSI ribbon cable with a file and a knife. Just trim off the wires past 44 and file down the cable end so it will fit on the motherboard's IDE connector .

The Multia's IDE chipset autosensed everything about the drive. The only problem I have is it won't cold-boot off the IDE, but it will warm boot after it cold boots from the SCSI?! Hacking the Linux IDE driver sounds like it's not worth the effort. However, I did get the EtherExpress to work on a Compaq laptop with a very trivial hack on eexpress.c, so give it a try! A good place to start would be drivers/block/ide.c

Anyway, everything works fine on the Multia under NT, including PCMCIA. I plan on trying a larger IDE (>512MB), a PCI video card, and the dual boot for Linux over the holidays. Cross your fingers.
