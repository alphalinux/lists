No, I think it's RH who didn't test their alpha release.

They simply copied their intel crap on the alpha release/CD and sold it.

This shows up clearly when you try to do an ftp install and have no recog-
nised network card in your machine: the install tries to insmod the net-
work card module and complains about not finding /bin/insmod which is proof
enough for the incompetence of that person doing the alpha dist: RH simply
don't support modules on alpha.

You can't even install/upgrade single modules because rpm -U complains about
(null) archives and coredumps although the files are there, not empty and
rpm -qlp lists all the files expected in them and after converting them to
cpio's can be extracted. Switching to the new rpm from the CD doesn't help
(independent of g/libc issues since rpm is *statically* linked).

So my solution is: get all the sources, compile them yourself and put RH on
your 'banned' list. I won't buy anything from them again since they put me
off for the third time now (Just *my* decision). I'll try the Debian alpha
as soon as it is available, can't be worse.

Since RH5 was released Dec 1. and I bought my CD on Dec 24 and there is no
better version available from RH I think all those people that downloaded
RH5 for free and tried it have silently dropped it and are using RH4.2.
I am still using a RH4.1 system with some packages and kernel upgrades and
even that install couldn't be done with the original CD but only by a net-
work install from my i386 machine. Now even that is impossible.

I'm sorry that I can't help you. I think we're on our own now.
