On Fri, 19 Dec 1997, Stephen Zedalis wrote:

> Yep, several packages have not been upgraded to later versions, guess it
> was all the hoopla about going to glibc. Its pervasive in several items,
> for instance why is Debian running bash 2.0 and Redhat 1.14? I find that
> often I have to roll my own version, getting some stuff from Debian, some
> from RH, and others still yet from the software site/author directly.

Actually, most of RedHat's transition-to-version-upgrade problems are
related to not only glibc conversion but also to the fact that they are
one group of people converting the software over. I am working on the
Debian Alpha port (heavily) and have found that the advantage of having
different maintainers for different packages is huge when it comes to
porting a large amount of packages over. For instance, instead of one
person porting 100 packages, Debian has 50 people porting 100 packages,
making the transition to glibc easier, but also more "updated"
version-wise.
