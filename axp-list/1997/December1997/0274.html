>Why does everyone want to run IDE on good hardware? IDE is by it's design);
>bad hardware and consumes valuable resources from your system.
>Even some dutch magazine was finally getting the picture and advised NOT
>to use IDE on servers or other high performance hardware.
>So even if some Alpha system DO have IDE (or EIDE) interfaces I would
>recommend you do NOT use them.

Really a sad religious war, this SCSI vs. IDE stuff. Let's just say
that before you dismiss IDE, try it! We've done exactly that and found
out that SCSI (for us) would be money wasted. And let me very much
doubt that you will notice any big difference between the interfaces
unless you got a lot of disks connected to a fileserver. As usual it
depends on what you do, how you do it, when you do it and how the
stars are aligned... ;)

So really, save your hard earned cash and start with IDE and a good
drive, (Seagate Medalist Pro highly recomended) and if you feel that
the performance isn't as stellar as you need it to be, take a few days
(if you can spare them, you lucky sod) and hack the IDE-driver, you
will do yourself, and the other (tens of) millions of people who stick
with IDE, a favour. If you can't spare the time, but have the money,
why not try SCSI. Might be better, then again it might not...

To summarize, Your Mileage May Vary and There Is More Than One Way To
Do It (TIMTOWTDI).
