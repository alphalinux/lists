On Thu, 11 Dec 1997, Kai Schuetz wrote:

> > Does anyone know off-hand what the latest version of the g++ compiler and library are? We're getting problems with the -fhandle-exceptions flag on.
>
> because gcc doesn't fully support exeptions, but im not sure
> if egcs does (but give it a try anyway)

According to documentation, egcs does support exceptions while g++
doesn't (on the Alpha at least). A slight word of warning regarding egcs,
however. A few of us have been testing various configurations of egcs to
see if we can get consistent results despite the configs, but haven't been
too successful. For instance, a "vanilla" configuration (just typing
"configure") gave the least amount of testing failures using the standard
compiler testing suite (included with the full egcs dist). Using
--enable-threads didn't particularly do anything better or worse than
vanilla, but --enable-shared caused a large amount of failures on the
Alphas we've tested them on (two UDBs and a PC164). Also, enabling the
new Haifa instruction scheduler caused at least one of the egcs
compilations to be totally unexecutable (according to the docs, it works
on "some targets" but doesn't list which...it's possible that the Alpha
Haifa instruction scheduler is either not finished or is irrelevant).

On the good front, Fortran-77 support seems pretty good :)
