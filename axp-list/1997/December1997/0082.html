David,

First off, you can take my opinions at face value here, as they are just
that, opinions.

>I do NOT know what the patches provided for 2.0.30 do so I am not sure
>whether to attempt to apply them. Are there patches for 2.0.32? Are the
>patches (axp-diff etc...) already incorporated? I do not wish to actually
>run this kernel until I am a little more confident that a clean unpatched
>source tree is not in some way a danger to my system.:)

The 2.0.30 patches mainly ensure compatibility with libc 1.99, which the
stock headers are incompatible with. However, they aren't essential for
day to day operation if you aren't compiling anything. The other
important bug fix, is the trap handling bug, as this could potentially
crash your system.

>Any one have any comment on this situation? Primarily I would like to know
>if it is safe for me to boot it up (even if it isn't a stable kernel... I
>understood that 2.0.31 had crashed some harddrives because of filesystem
>enhancements)...

I'm not sure - has anyone had any experience with 2.0.32 here? I have
looked at it extensively on Intel, and haven't seen any problems. 2.0.32
came pretty quickly after 2.0.31, and consists mainly of bug fixes to
solve the fs problems in 2.0.31, and a few other instability issues.
