On Thu, 11 Dec 1997, Michal Jaegermann wrote:

> For the last few days I am running Alpha on egcs-1.0 compiled kernel
> and so far so good. :-) This was a "vanilla" configuration.

I was wondering about this. I've heard that kernels compiled on earlier
snapshots of egcs were "iffy", but I've also heard a few Alpha folks claim
that their egcs kernels were solid. I'll have to run a few tests that way
too.

> I would not claim that egcs does not have problems and outright bugs,
> so a caution is well advised, but it seems to be much more solid than
> a quoted sentence would imply.

Agreed. I'm working with on the Debian port over to the Alpha and have
heavily suggested that we switch totally to egcs before our next "stable"
release, but I'm not sure how well that will be received. I just find
that egcs is more "Alpha supportive and optimised" than the hacked gcc
that we have now.

> It fares better on a Fortran test suite than a "standard" g77. Also,
> on a plus side, a debugger support, i.e. gdb, seems to be improved.

I haven't tried gdb with it yet, but that was coming soon. Then again,
I'm hoping that gdb is updated soon to account for the bfd changes that
have happened since the last official gdb release (since libbfd sources
are inclued with gdb and have to be used for building it).

Chris
