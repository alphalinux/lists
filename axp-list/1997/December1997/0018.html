On Mon, 1 Dec 1997, nm wrote:

> well i would think that if redhat and other vendors were taking serious
> hits from companies like cheapbytes, that they would persue to rectify
> the situation... even though the bulk (if not all) of the software is
> gpl'd redhat could still license their scripts, rpm system and any other
> components of value that they have added and thus possibly prevent
> people from selling 'copies' of their ftp site...

Which would not contribute back to the community in the spirit in which
the originals were done. Some people have different ethics, but I don't
think that companies like RedHat should stoop to that level, and hopefully
there are enough consumers like me to keep them in business.

> i do not see any reason that is stopping redhat from doing this...
> i think that the lack of attempt to prevent 'cheapbytes'-like
> organizations only serves to encourage this behavior.

One might also argue that they are encouraging other companies to
follow in their stead and contribute back to the community.

Companies like RedHat made people like me eat their words when it came to
the commercialization of Linux. Caldera has also made significant
contributions back into the source tree. There are other companies which
profit in cooperation with the community instead of because of it.

Not putting iron doors on houses could be said to encourage kick burgling,
that still doesn't make the theif right, or someone I'd buy a TV from.
