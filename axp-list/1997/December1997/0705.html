On Fri, 19 Dec 1997, Nils Haberland wrote:
[...]
> > Two weeks ago we installed RH5.0 on an XL and at first I was confident
> > that these network-storms caused by ypbind had ended. This morning,
> > however, the load on the machine was skyrocketing again and ypbind going
> > mad. Unfortunately, our NIS-server (a dual-P) was locked up by this, as it
> > had been the case with RH4.2 a couple of times before. This makes RH5.0
> > completely unusable for us.
>
> as I have already mentioned in this mailing-list, is this ypbind problem not a
> problem of RH, but of the alpha kernels. The ypbind-slave only starts with
> 2.0.x kernel this ping-flooding, which results a hangup of the ypservers.
> The only solution we have found by now is to use a 2.1.x kernel.

But why do our RH4.1-Multias run fine without hanging the server? They
have a 2.0.27-Kernel.

> It's a problem with select as a strace-session with the ypbind(slave) shows.
> The timeout-time was not reseted to its original value.
>
> Someone told me that the default "personality" of udpclnt seems to be changed
> in the 2.1.x k. It must be a small task to set this in the 2.0.x k on the
> default-values of the 2.1.x.

Hmm, maybe I'll give the 2.1.x a try... Is it hard to get it compiled? Are
there any recent 2.1-kernels which run well on Alphas or others that were
rather broken?
