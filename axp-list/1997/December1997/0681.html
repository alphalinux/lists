Just a comment on the hardware, I have not had direct dealing with ASPEN.
We have four Aspen Durango II based machines (motherboard @ 533, 128
Mb/9Gb SCSI, 100Mbit net, S3 graphics, 19" screen, price about $6200 a
piece) assembled by a local swedish company. We have just received
them a week ago. One is now installed with RH 5, another woth DEC Unix (we
specifically asked for DEC compatibility). We will soon decide on what to
use for production (comput. chemistry). Since we have a DEC UNIX site
license and Linux is almost free, software pricing is not an issue for us.
Anyway I have a good impression of the hardware, installing the software
is rather cumbersome for both DEC Unix and Linux.

Also: they advertise a machine with DUAL 21264 chips to be delivered in
may: let's keep them to it.
