I can suggest an excellent resource explaining the difference:

http://www.paranoia.com/~filipg/LINK/F_SCSI6.html#SCSI_005

Basically, the SCSI bus needs to see a specific terminating voltage for
proper termination. A Passive terminator derives this voltage from a
line providing TERM power using a simple, two resistor, passive circuit to
supply termination voltage on each line. TERM power is supplied by one device
in the SCSI chain (the adapter or a drive). Unfortunately, TERM power voltage
will drop over the length of the line, so that the resultant TERM power voltage
at the end of the line is below the optimum termination voltage.

An Active terminator provides voltage regulation of TERM power at the
terminator, so that the optimum termination voltage can be applied on each
line regardless of line length and other factors.
