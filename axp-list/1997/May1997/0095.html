"Brian E.W. Wood" <beww@intac.com> wrote:
> So the consensus seems to be that the DAT drive should work. My
> experience has also been that most scsi problems are related to cables
> and/or terminations.
>
> In this case all of the cables are high-quality (Amphenol) and I have a
> good active terminator at the end. The only change I am making is to
> replace an existing scsi tape drive (Conner) with the DAT unit - all
> cabling and termination remains the same.

Contrary to what others have suggested here, termination issues aren't
necessarily solved just be sticking an active terminator at the end of
the bus.

Someone else previously wrote about SCSI terminators:
> passive does not require power.
> active does require power.

Which is hogwash. Both active and passive terminators need power, and
plenty of it. Passive terminators actually require *more* power than active;
they use about 0.8 W when the bus is quiescent. Both kinds of terminators
draw substantial amounts of surge current during operation. Often people
have their SCSI chain configured such that only one device is supplying
terminator power. If you look at the power at the far end of the SCSI bus,
after it has passed through several feet of small-gauge wire, it is doing
really ugly, unpleasant things.

The difference between active and passive is that a passive terminator will
directly pass these ugly, unpleasant looking waveforms on to the data and
control lines of the bus, causing all sorts of mayhem. An active terminator
has a voltage regulator that attempts to maintain proper termination voltage
at all times. But if the input power is really bad, the regulator isn't
sufficient.

You have to make sure that the device at the end of the chain
(i.e., your DAT drive) is configured to supply terminator power to the
bus, so that your terminator has a good low-impedance power source.

Note that it is not easy to tell active and passive terminators apart without
taking electrical measurements. Some people have made the claim that
terminators with LEDs are active, but there are passive terminators with
LEDs. Even the markings on the terminators have been known to be wrong.
