>In cases like this, the issue/problem is almost always termination. Just
>having a terminator in the right spots doesn't necessarily ensure a good
>termination scenario on a SCSI bus. Replacing the passive terminator on the
>far end of the device chain with an active one usually clears these matters
>up pretty quickly.

So the consensus seems to be that the DAT drive should work. My
experience has also been that most scsi problems are related to cables
and/or terminations.

In this case all of the cables are high-quality (Amphenol) and I have a
good active terminator at the end. The only change I am making is to
replace an existing scsi tape drive (Conner) with the DAT unit - all
cabling and termination remains the same.

I'll try other configurations, I know scsi can be a little flaky at
times. Since nobody has pointed out any reason why the DAT should not
work I assume my troubles are "local".
