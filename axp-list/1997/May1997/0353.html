>I just installed NT on my UDB. I also just got a USR Sportster, however
>when I plug the modem into the serial port on the UDB the Send and Recieve
>data lights are constantly lit. I realize this probably has to do with the
>fact that there are actually two serial ports(correct?). Anyways, my
>question is do I need to get the special cable that Starship and others
>sell, or is there another fix.

The UDB manual says that you can use a straight-through 25-pin cable to
access the first Com port. I found this to not be the case when I
connected a Motorola Bitsurfr, I would up having to use a "Y" cable,
which I made. I always assumed that this was because the Bitsurfr is
capable of synchronous serial communication, and I figured that the stuff
for the second (async) port was screwing up the clock lines or something.

Now that you mention your problem with an async modem I question my
conclusion.

The "Y" cable is probably the best solution, although you could always
just cut the conductors for the un-needed pins :-)
