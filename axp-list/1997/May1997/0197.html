On Tue, 6 May 1997, Jon 'maddog' Hall, USG Senior Leader wrote:

[snip]

> Java will help, but I think it will be a long time before CAD
> applications and databases are written in Java.

[snip]

Not as long as you might think.... Transitioning CAD and database
applications to a three-tier achitecture means that Java (or something
similar) will play an important role at the desktop application
presentation layer fairly soon --and that scalable, high-performance,
high-availability servers are critical for the middle and back-end layers.

An idiot-proof client connected to high-octane, multi-layered servers is
the likely future of mainstream computing. How Windows, Java, Unix, and
Linux all fit into this future is very much an open question.
