On Thu, 8 May 1997, Miguel Barreiro Paz wrote:

> I got the source for PVM 3.3.11 and compiled it on an UDB with
> only minor glitches ....

I'm curious: who else is using PVM, and for what, exactly?
I ask because I originally joined this list looking for a
fast raytracing platform. A number of listmembers ran
benchmark tests, whose results I haven't tabulated because
they're inconclusive: there are problems with complex POVRay
images on an Alpha. Listmember Terry Ridder and his son
Pieter found the bugs after a lot of work, and fixed them,
so this shouldn't be an issue in the future.

Meanwhile, though, I've settled on a small cluster of Intel
boxes as the most cost effective solution to my POV
problems. The power-per-dollar equation comes out much
better at the low end than the high end. For a fast POV
system, it's cheaper to hook a bunch of Volkswagens together
than to buy a Porsche. Scalability's nice, too, and I've
learned a heck of a lot about tuning a network. (This is my
first Linux network.) Preliminary results are great. Okay,
so I still want a Porsche; but all in good time.

So here I am, the first guy on the block with a PVM cluster,
an excellent solution to my POV problems. The system has
already justified itself financially, doing the one thing
for which I built it. ("Justified itself financially,"
right. Total cost so far: three NICs and a hub.) But now I
start to wonder, what else can I do with it? I don't hear
much about software that can exploit PVM. Do I correctly
assume that one's expected to write it for one's self? Or
are there apps out there I could be playing with while my
system's not busy rendering graphics?

Anybody know?
