At 07:52 PM 5/11/97 +0100, Matt Hamilton wrote:
>Hello All,
> I have just seen a great deal on a clone of a Diamond Stealth 3D 2000.
>According to the info it is functionally identical to the original and has
>a S3 Virage 64-bit GUI & Multimedia Accelerator.
>
> Do you think this would work in a UDB? I have read that the S3 chipset
>is supported and the best one to go for, but I'm not sure how much
>difference it would make being a clone.
>
>TIA
>-Matt

Actually, Matt, I wouldn't get that particular card. First of all, the S3
Virge was meant to be a 2D/3D accelerator, but from the reviews I've read,
it has mediocre 2D acceleration and pathetic 3D acceleration.

As for its compatablity in a UDB, I don't know. I actually haven't work
with UDB's enough to know what will and won't work in them. However, if
you are wondering about which video card you could get, that would work
well, I would suggest the #9 Motion 771, or the Diamond Stealth Series
3000, both of these cards are based on the S3 968 chipset and have
excellent 2D accel., however, they have no 3D accel., but to be honest, on
an UDB running Linux, or NT for that matter, I doubt very much that you are
going to be looking to run Heavy Metal(Mechwarrior 3) in true color, with
full texture mapping.

Anyways, I think I've carried on enough, but I hope something I've said
here made since.
