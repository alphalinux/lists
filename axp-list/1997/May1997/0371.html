On Sat, 10 May 1997, T. S. Horsnell wrote:

> Regarding cost-effectiveness of Alpha versus Pentium, we have a
> PPro system with 128Mb and 4Gb SCSI, cost us nearly 2K pounds.
> The similiarly-configured PC164/500MHz cost 3.5K pounds and
> goes approx 5 x faster for much of our fp-intensive code.

True enough. I wasn't speaking about the cost effectiveness
of Alpha versus Intel, but of low end versus high. IOW with
either platform, a processor half as fast as the fastest
available will cost much less than half the price. You get
a diminishing return on your dollar as you get closer to the
high end.

That said, I certainly won't argue with you about the
relative fp speed of Alpha vs Intel. Which one gives the
most performance per cost, I don't know, but cost issues
aside I'm aware that the Alpha is a better chip for what I
want to do.

As I said, all in good time.
