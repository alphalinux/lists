look on your chip/socket. if its like mine, it says "LCA233", meaning the
chip is rated for 233 MHz. that does not mean you are limited to that
speed, only that results for higher speeds cannot be guarenteed. i
overclocked my udb233 to 300 MHz. it worked for about two months, and
then apparently died a horrible fried death. it wont even turn on
anymore. i dont suggest anyone overclock a udb to 300 MHz. but then, of
course, ymmv.
