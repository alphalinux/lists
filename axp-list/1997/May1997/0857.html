> Hi,

> I just bought a PC164 500Mhz machine and I have problems:
> I installed Red Hat 4.1 from the CD without problem.
> I then recompiled the kernel (2.0.30) with the drivers I want and when I
> reboot, I get this:
>
> INIT: Entering runlevel:3
> eth0:enabling BNC port
> CIA machine check NOT expected
>
> and it halts!

Get your vendor involved now if it is still under warranty. The CIA
machine check is sort of a generic hardware failure. In all the cases
that I have seen, I think it has been a memory related problem. You
probably want to start by replacing the memory.

I have seen some memory that has worked quite fine under one version
of an OS and then die on the next version. A little while back I had
one machine that had 64MB in it. I was quite happy running WinNT 3.51
and 4.0beta2. As soon as you tried to install WinNT 4.0 (the released
version) it would die with the blue screen of death. Replace the
memory, and it is working under all versions of WinNT and Linux now.

You may also want to contact the support staff in the DEC
Semiconductor Group. I beleive that their number is 508-568-7474.
They may not be able to provide direct assistance, but they may have
some ideas as to what else to check.

Good luck....
