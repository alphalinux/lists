Hi All,

I recently downloaded 4.2 off of the devel tree, and found that, coupled
with upgrading to glibc2.0.3 solved a number of problems. However, one
thing I cannot figure out is that 4.2, or the new pam, or something seems
to do strange things to groups. For instance, if I create a new user,
then su to the user, or not. I create a file in the user's homedir, and
attempt to chgrp username I get, 'chgrp: invalid group name 'username''.
I have verified that the group was properly entered into /etc/group, and
the gid is the same as the uid, which makes it easier for checking.
Also, the chown command works fine, and chown username: doesn't seem to
complain either, though it could just be skipping the groups part.

Overall there are a couple of workarounds for this, but it is a real pain
to not have groups functioning normally. Any help is appreciated.
