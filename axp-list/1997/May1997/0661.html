In a fit of pioneering spirit (read: glutton for punishment), I downloaded
the 4.2/axp set (including the elm & lpr updates). Installed from scratch to
my UDB. Noted the following items:

1. During install, starred all the package line items, including the one
marked "Everything". Nonetheless, the install complained about "XF86-SETUP"
not having been selected and being needed to satisfy dependencies.

2. The "Howto-translations" rpm is noted as a 31 Meg rpm. As it appears to
be the largest rpm in the whole set, one wonders if its size could be
reduced somewhat. (Granted when you select everything, you get what you're
given.)

3. Once the install completed, the reboot produced the message "cannot open
/lib/modules/2.0.30/modules.dep"

4. The message "YPBINDPROC_DOMAIN: No server for domain: (none)" came up
about six times.

5. The usual (for post 4.0) short stall after amd was more than 1.5 minutes.
The "starting sendmail:" message came up but never generated a "sendmail" ack.
After another minute, the "starting gpm mouse services:" message came up.
After 3 minutes, no gpm ack (or any other noticable functioning of the
computer).

6. Assuming machine was hung (just like with 4.1) I did the 3-finger salute.
The switching to run-level 6 message was printed but no further actions taken.

7. After rebooting from backup partition, the /var/log/messages didn't give
me any indication that an error occurred. It also gave no mention of
sendmail or gpm which I took to mean they failed to start.

#ifdef SOAP_BOX_MODE_ON

This does not bode well for RedHat's Quality Assurance. While I don't mind
being a beta-tester, I want to know that a-priori. I PURCHASED the Official
RH4.1 set only to find out it WOULDN'T EVEN BOOT out of the box. Most people
(including myself) raked RH over the coals for that. Yet, it appears they
may (repeat may) have done it again. Fool me once, shame on you. Fool me
twice, shame on me. Since the Official sets normally contain the initial
release (only), I would advise against buying either 4.1 or 4.2. Get the
CheapBytes version if you need a CD or download it for free (since it
appears at this point to be worth every penny).
