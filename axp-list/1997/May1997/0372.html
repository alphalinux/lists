On 10-May-17 Heikki Virkkunen wrote:
|>May 9 03:15:00 ksylitol kernel: Reason: correctable ECC error (short
|>frame, retryable, dc_stat=0):pn Correctable read error to b-cache
|>occurred at address 779948

This means that the memory subsystem detected an error (see, ECC is useful
for something ;), and that it was fixable. Of course, there was a
performance hit while it went back and fixed the error. I used to get these
*all* the time; someone suggested opening the UDB up and reseating the
memory. However, in my case, it turned out that the 8M SIMMs that shipped
with it were bad, and I eventually ended up replacing them with two 16M
SIMMs. YMMV,
