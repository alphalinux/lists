Robin Stephenson wrote:

<snip>

> I've got a 233 MHz UDB overclocked to 266 MHz, with the small drive &
> no expansion cards. It's standing on the little vertical plastic
> thing. Should I really be worried about heat buildup? Is there
> anything reasonably straightforward that I can do to improve the
> cooling of the CPU?

I have run my UDB overclocked to 266 with slim HD and no PCI card
laying flat on my desk (not in vertical stand) for up to 10 days at
a stretch without problems.

Is this the best way to go, probably not, but I feel OK with the
heat so far, and I am *fairly* confident that putting the machine
in it's vertical stand will only improve the situation.

IMHO - I think the 3 1/2" drives and/or PCI cards put a severe
restriction on airflow inside UDB. They are fine as shipped.
