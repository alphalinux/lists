I too have just bought a UDB, unfortunately I haven't got my hands on it
yet as it is still the other side of the atlantic :( I currently have a
P166+ (cyrix) running FreeBSD and Xfree. Although I have heard all these
warnings of how slow the UDB/Multia 166's are I still decided to go ahead.
I thought about it and came to the conclusion I don't really use all the
horsepower of my system all that much anyway. My main use of it is
ghostscript, LaTeX, xfig, xdvi and similar applications. Also for
telnet/ftp/netscape etc. I don't really do any number crunching on it. I
do do some C programming, but that is just stuff for university
assignments, so isn't normally that long and compile time isn't really
that significant.
My main question is how quick it *appears* to be? How responsive are
they? (I know I will find out in about 2 months time when I pick it up -
but I'm curious). I have heard of a TGA X server written by DEC for Linux
that uses proprietry code... does it appear much faster than Xfree?
Also how quick is ghostscript? I would imagine it does a lot of floating
point stuff?

I just like the look of them :)
