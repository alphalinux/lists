Christopher B. Browne wrote:
> > 3. real resilience in the SCSI subsystem - something I miss greatly... :-(
>
> This is actually the part that with my experiences I have to take issue with.
> All of the (far too many) times that I saw OSF/1 hang, it was due to problems
> within the SCSI subsystems.

I will have to second this notion! My PC/Linux KT-958 Buslogic
SCSI system ROCKS! It hasn't had a HICCUP! (even after my office
mate accidentally dislodged a cable during a HD write...yeah, in
a hurry I forgot to secure the plug...now it's practically welded :)

Whereas, on our ALPHAStation 500/233, if there are more than two
external SCSI devices (on very short cables and very long cables
and even on a couple medium size cables), the system will hang
if I write to tape and read from CDROM. Bogus! (And yes, this
reproducible problem was given a typical MICROSOFT response of
"i'm sorry, we don't see that problem here so therefore it must
not be a bug or a problem")

My additional two-bits on the "resilence" issue.
