Philip Blundell wrote:

> On Sat, 10 May 1997, Matt Hamilton wrote:
>
> > Sorry to bug people about this, and thanks to those that answer
> my last
> > note, but does the UDB *HAVE* to have parity SIMMS?
>
> Yes. More, it has to have "true parity" 36-bit SIMMs - "logic
> parity"
> ones won't work.
>
> The parity bits are used for an ECC code that allows the logic in
> the UDB
> to correct all one-bit errors and detect all two-bit errors.
>
> p.

I feel it's worth adding a comment here. If you're purchasing RAM from
a vendor, make sure that they understand that "logic parity" will not
work in your computer. Simply asking for parity SIMMs can get you
something unusable. Usually these memory modules come at a slightly
premium price: like $20-40 more per module depending on how much memory
you're buying. (You ARE getting at least 64M for the UBD, right?)
