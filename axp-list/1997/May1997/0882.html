Rick Windham wrote:
>
> This is a very large security hole (IMHO) requiring me to
> physically lock up all Linux boxes behind lock and key.

If you think that this is only a property of Linux then you are
deluding yourself. It is possibly true, though, that more people
know how to boot Linux than something else.

If you need that kind of security, then yes, your boxes should
be kept under lock and key, and console keyboards as well - Linux
or no Linux. In some cases somebody with a screwdriver may simply
"borrow" your hard drive and mount it on another machine. Simple,
effective, and no amount of password hacking will protect you from
that.

> Is there a way or patch to make the single ask for the password?

You can make kernel to disregard 'single' argument (look at the sources),
but then you should also worry about 'init=/bin/sh' and disconnect
floppies.
