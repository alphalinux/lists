Hi,

I've been trying again to use an old streamer (Archive Viper 150,
SCSI-1 ) on an UDB, and got little further...

As before, Multia 233, kernel 2.0.27 and 2.0.30, nothing on the
SCSI bus except the internal Toshiba HD and the external streamer. It's
detected on boot, but any access to the tape hangs. With the 53c7,8xx
driver the whole SCSI driver hangs (ie: no more disk accesses until hard
reboot) no matter what the settings are. With the BSD derived driver,
setting the most conservative settings (no command-tagged queuing, no
disconnects, force asyn...) the SCSI driver hangs for a few seconds,
spits an error and everything resumes after aborting the tape access. The
following is on the logs:

May 31 13:17:59 galahad kernel: ncr53c810-0: restart (ncr dead ?).
May 31 13:17:59 galahad kernel: st0: Error with sense data: extra data not
valid Current error st09:00: sense key Unit Attention
May 31 13:17:59 galahad kernel: ncr53c810-0-<target 0, lun 0>: asynchronous.
May 31 13:18:12 galahad kernel: ncr53c810-0: restart (ncr dead ?).
May 31 13:18:12 galahad kernel: st0: Error with sense data: extra data not
valid Current error st09:00: sense key Unit Attention
May 31 13:18:12 galahad kernel: ncr53c810-0-<target 0, lun 0>: asynchronous.

And on stderr:
[root@galahad /root]# cat /dev/st0
cat: /dev/st0: Input/output error

I've double-checked cables and termination, now including active
terminators. The old venerable streamer works flawlessly on intel-based
Linux boxes and Suns.

Any ideas other than purchasing an external Zip drive? :)

Regards,

Miguel
