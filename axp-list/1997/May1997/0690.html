I seem to have had a really easy upgrade. Painless as a matter of fact.
I was upgradinga my 166Mhz UDB running Redhat 4.0. AFter hearing the horror
stories about 4.1 I had intentionally waited for 4.2. I did the ftp install
and practically few problems.

I didn't select install everything. I actually went through and selected
mostly everything by hand. I didn't get any dependency errors so I guess I
selected right.

I got the message "cannot open /lib/modules/2.0.30/modules.dep" when I
rebooted but it doesn't seem to affect the performance of my machines. I
haven't bothered to go look into it yet.

I'm not using YP at all so I didn't get any of the YP related problems
that others have reported. I didn't get any hangs at sendmail or amd or
anything like others have reported in the past.

I'm very happy with the RedHat 4.2. Lots of new packages and some needed
updates for others. I am disppointed that Grail hangs (the grail home page
talks about a patch to tcl for 64bit machines to fix this). Also, the lpr
bug is annoying but since there is an update already its not too bad.
