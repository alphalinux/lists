In response to Jay Estabrook...

Re:Things DU has that Linux doesn't:
1) C2 security

This may actually no longer be the case. The NSA site that lists all
evaluated systems does *not* list Digital UNIX or "OSF/1" as being rated,
whether at the C2 or B1 levels (I've got an O'Reilly book on computer security
that claims "OSF/1" has been evaluated at both levels).

This may mean that:
a) Digital hasn't pursued classification lately, and that there is no longer a
certified platform, or
b) The NSA is not listing things correctly.

If it's b), then perhaps Digital should contact some people at NIST...
Several VMS versions are listed with C2, and there's a version of Ultrix at
B1...

See <http://www.radium.ncsc.mil/tpep/epl/epl-by-class.html> for more
details...

2) AdvFS
3) Support for HA hardware
4) Better SMP
5)clustering

> 3. real resilience in the SCSI subsystem - something I miss greatly... :-(

This is actually the part that with my experiences I have to take issue with.
All of the (far too many) times that I saw OSF/1 hang, it was due to problems
within the SCSI subsystems.
