At 10:34 PM 5/22/97 -0400, you wrote:
>I seem to have had a really easy upgrade. Painless as a matter of fact.
>I was upgradinga my 166Mhz UDB running Redhat 4.0. AFter hearing the horror
>stories about 4.1 I had intentionally waited for 4.2. I did the ftp install
>and practically few problems.
>

Operative word above is "upgrade". I, foolish sort that I am, like to "start
fresh" with a new release (to make sure all the libs and such match each
other and the binaries) and customize from there. Hence, the complete
install. (One feature they added to their install that I like is the option
to check for bad spots during the formatting of swap space. I was never was
quite sure whether it did that in previous releases or not.)

Evidently, there is a significant difference between "upgrade" and "install"
options in RedHat's sw. I suspect they do most (if not all) of their testing
for situtations like yours (since most people like to upgrade in place).
Sadly, they seem not to have proof tested a complete install (as they
evidently didn't do with 4.1 either).
