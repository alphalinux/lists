>I've got a 233 MHz UDB overclocked to 266 MHz, with the small drive &
>no expansion cards. It's standing on the little vertical plastic
>thing. Should I really be worried about heat buildup? Is there
>anything reasonably straightforward that I can do to improve the

I can't guarantee that it will die from this, but the fact is that the
temperatures reached at 266MHz far exceed the guidelines in the AXPpci33
manual. So> Welll, the AXPpci33 is the same chipset on an AT style board,
andis essentially the same machine in different packaging. They clearly
state figures for CPU heat and airflow. Without a CPU fan the chip exceeds
the recommended maximum temperature by at least 20 degrees C at 266MHz.
With that case there is no really easy way to do this, but it bears
considering. Perhaps a fan in a small cardboard tube on top of the unit
whrere the air comes out? You will need to get 12V from the machine for
this, but you may get that from the unused connector on the SCSI riser inside.
