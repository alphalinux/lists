>> The alpha Multia's were the greatest Desktop computers..for workstations
>> until DEC scrapped them in production. The Motherboards that come with
>> cpu's with a speed of 233 MHz or greater can be upgraded to the latest
>> chips availible from Digital. The 166 however, is not upgradeable. The
>> new firmware, released early this year from a site in Europe supposedly
>> fixed the bottleneck memory issue problem. I have 20 166's and 400
>> 233's which half of those were upped to 400 MHz which fly with DEC Unix
>> Xwindows. NICE.
>
>Is this true. What firmware update is he talking about??
>Has anyone else heard this, I thought the clock speed was
>set by jumpers on the Motherboard?? Has anyone else done
>this with any success??

I just called Digital and without some type of part number I left to believe
its not true. The only way it can be done is if they made a new
21066-400Mhz or its a motherboard replacement. The above leads me to
believe this is not the case.

At one time the VX42 model was fitted to run a 300Mhz 21066-LCA but it never
happen. This is also why some people that have the VX42 models are able to
clock them up from 233Mhz to 266Mhz and the real daring have tried 300Mhz.
Here again the supporting logic is not there to go above 300Mhz.

One final note, I get asked this at least twice a week. Any Digital Alpha
product has a specific Alpha CPU ie:21066, 21064, 21164, and 21164a. These
CPUs CANNOT be swapped out and installed from one motherboard to another.
Bottom line the supporting logic is just not there.

However, if you purchased a Digital Alpha XL, or a Deskstation Raptor they
made motherboard with engine cards that are interchangeable.

Bottom line contact the source and get a part number and put this issue to rest.
