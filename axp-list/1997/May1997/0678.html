> I am planning on buying a Multia to run Linux on, but I am wondering if
> the Multia is still a good option to buy. Currently I run Linux on my
> P133. I already know that a DEC 21066 at 166Mhz is not really a speed
> demon.
> If I buy a Multia it would be solely for the fun of running Linux on a
> Alpha.
>
> Maybe you can give me some advice or share your experencies met Linux on
> a Multia. The firm which sells the machines in Holland asks about Hfl
> 995 ex. VAT thats roughly $500 US. That' is for the 166Mhz version.

I bought a 166Mhz Multia for pretty much exactly the same reason. I've
thoroughly enjoyed having it. They are still in general a pretty good buy
if you don't get them bare bones like many places are selling them. If you
can get one with the 2 12MB DEC simms and an internal 2.5" 850 HD then you
can be up and running in now time with a pretty spiffy machine. Especially
at the price you quoted above. But if you have to buy the memory, and an
hard drive then it starts to get a little pricey. Especially if you want to
need > 256 color and have to put in a good video card.
