At 02:33 hrs. 05-07-97 -0700, you wrote:
>>I just got off the phone (14:33)with the Digital Multia product manager.
>>Bottom line is the Multia is a dead and unsupported product...no upgrades
>>available.
>>
>>Therefore, the 400Mhz system is a myth.
>
>Sorry, I don't get the logic here in this statement. Most of us who bought
>the Multia at discounted prices new beforehand that it was discontinued.
>However, there are many of us who bought Intel based Motherboards and
>CPUs with ZIF sockets with the hope of upgrading later. I know I can
>run the Multia at 266 MHZ, and can be clocked at 300MHZ. I can also
>remove the CPU(it is in a ZIF socket).
>
>Now tell me how that means the 400MHZ system is a myth. So far I don't
>think your providing any new information.
Tom. There is _NO_, I repeat: _NO_ way to set a Multia/UDB to run the CPU
at 400 MHz.
It can run at 300 ( And will probably cook the CPU)
It can run at 266 ( And will also probably cook the CPU unless you improve
the cooling on the box)
It runs at 233, as DEC and God designed it to do.

The 166 variants can not, do not, will not, run at anything other than 166MHz.
