On Mon, 5 May 1997, Mark Hamstra wrote:

>
> OTOH, there is at least one lustrous port (MicroStation) that is
> languishing because very few Linux users are asking for commercial
> licensing --and this is the *only* thing preventing this robust,
> full-featured professional CAD product from being available to
> non-academic Linux users.
>
> Much as I hate to admit it, the nay-ayers are often right presently: Linux
> does not make commercial sense for many applications. Of course, I'd be
> delighted to be proven wrong :-)
>

Yes, and the problem is that CAD is somewhat of a specialized and relatively
low volume market compared with office application suites and even databases.
Particularily expensive (by PC standards) CAD packages like MicroStation.

How many people in the A&E world even *know* that there is a Linux port? I
have seen a brief mention in MicroStation World, but nothing else. The
demand really has to come from *this* community, rather than the Linux
community per se, since only they have the resources to commit to purchasing
in the kind of quantities necessary to justify Bentley's support. Now if it
were available, I would consider purchasing a copy personally, but the $3000+
price tag is a little expensive for personal CAD, even though I am a CAD
professional (AutoCAD and Intergraph EMS).

I doubt that Linux has made enough of an impact in architecture and
engineering firms to justify the numbers it would take to release a
commercial port, in spite of the better technical performance of the Linux
platform over NT. Perhaps when the academic users of MicroStation move into
the corporate world, there will be heightened awareness. Unfortunately, the
trend I see for CAD and Engineering applications is towards WindowsNT as the
platform. Products such as ProEngineer are seeing a high demand for their NT
port in just the first year of its availability, over the traditional Unix
workstations. Companies like AutoDesk and Intergraph are abandoning Unix for
NT. It is the customers that drive these companies, and a significant number
are requesting and *purchasing* NT ports of CAD software.

Sorry for being so negative on this. I think Bentley is great in their
support of the Linux port in the academic suite, and I hope that the
situation improves towards releasing MicroStation commercially.
