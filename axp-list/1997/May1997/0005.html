Henry,
I have tinkered around with i386, Alpha and SPARC Red Hat Linux
machines, and I have found them to be VERY consistent. I have also
found the Users Guide to be very good, there is mre that could be added
to it (IMHO), but what is in there is pretty good (again, IMHO).

I have also found that the Aeleen Frisch (Sorry, I don't have
that funny AE character) Essential System Adminstration 2nd edition
to have very good coverage of linux in general. Even though she writes
only for i386 systems, since Red Hat (And a cast of thousands in the
Linux community) have worked so hard to make the environments so similar
her book is very useful.

Good luck,

Ken
khansen@njcc.com

PS - the easiest X windows configuration I ever did was on the SPARC
port of Linux - the hardware choices are so limited that it is almost
completely installed by default - you simply need to know what
framebuffer your machine has and it is done! Try that on UDB or an
i386!
