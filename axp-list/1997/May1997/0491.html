>I'll bet that you have an external modem on ttyS0 (COM1), and it's turned
>on when this happens to you.
>
>This occurs because MILO spews everything it prints to the screen (and then
>some) out the serial port. Your modem thinks that these are commands, and
>doesn't understand the output, so it sends an error back to MILO, which
>thinks that you're typing at it, ad nauseam.
>
>To fix: Buy a serial splitter cable, or build one yourself. there's a
>local place that can build the cables for $30 (Or so they told me on the
>phone; I didn't actually buy from them.) Or turn off your modem when you
>reboot your machine.

Possible, but I would think that the modem would pretty much react the
same way each time. I have a modem (multi-Tech) on cua0 and it has never
had a problem with the boot data sent to that port.

If you really didn't need the second port and wanted to save the cost of
a "Y" cable (I've seen them for $19) you could simply cut the conductors
associated with the second port (these would be 11,12,13,14,16,19,23 and
25). This would still not solve the problem of a modem reacting adversely
to boot data.

Booting with the modem off will definitely eliminate this as a problem
though.
