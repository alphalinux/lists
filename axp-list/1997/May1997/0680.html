On Fri, 23 May 1997, Kramer, G.T.M. wrote:

> I am planning on buying a Multia to run Linux on, but I am wondering if
> the Multia is still a good option to buy. Currently I run Linux on my
> P133. I already know that a DEC 21066 at 166Mhz is not really a speed
> demon.
> If I buy a Multia it would be solely for the fun of running Linux on a
> Alpha.

I am not sure if a Multia 166 is worth all the pain though. In that case
you are probably better of with a P133. In the beginning it's fun to patch
everything to work, but when you want some application to just urn, and you
can't get it to work because the source generates more warnings then it has
lines of code, you'll rethink it all :) Then again, I _do_ like my Multia :)

> Maybe you can give me some advice or share your experencies met Linux on
> a Multia. The firm which sells the machines in Holland asks about Hfl
> 995 ex. VAT thats roughly $500 US. That' is for the 166Mhz version.

It's still a lot cheaper compared to a similar Intel, since it has everything
built in already. (Try buying an Intel 133, with ethernet, soundcard, scsi,
etc for 995). Though I do recommend to add some ram to it right away. If
you're taking about the company "ReQuest" in Holland, they ship their Multia
with the standard 16Mb ram, which is REALLY not even close to being enough
to run X with Netscape :) I am still annoyed at 32MB.

Btw. you might want to try one of the ReQuest offers made through the Dutch
Unix Users group. It's a bit cheaper then getting it directly from them.
