On May 1, jestabro@amt.tay1.dec.com wrote:
>
> User memory mapped to PCI bus DENSE space *MUST* be accessed on natural
> boundaries of 4 (int) and 8 (long) bytes *ONLY*, otherwise you get unaligned
> access traps (harmless except to performance) at least, bus hangs at worst.
>

Actually this is described in the Core Logic chipset specification,
rather than in the Alpha Architecture manual. But nevertheless, the
question is: are hangs really possible given that low level things like
PALcode and kernel interrupt routines are properly set up? I always
considered such hangs to be major architecture/implementation design
flaws: exceptions should be raised instead. PDP-11 (unlike, say, Intel
8088/*86) was *very* resistant to the events that could potentially
cause lockups.

And, since the non-extended Alpha architecture does not even provide
instructions capable of memory access other than in naturally aligned
4 or 8 byte words (so that CPU cannot issue unaligned or less than 4
byte granular memory access requests), is it at all possible to abuse
the DENSE space?
