Quoth Robin Stephenson:
>
> I've got a 233 MHz UDB overclocked to 266 MHz, with the small drive &
> no expansion cards. It's standing on the little vertical plastic
> thing. Should I really be worried about heat buildup? Is there
> anything reasonably straightforward that I can do to improve the
> cooling of the CPU?

I'm doing the same thing that you are with the exception of having added
a video card to the mix. I've had uptimes of 100+ days with zero heat
problems. Suggestions: 1) make sure that wherever it is placed has good
ventilation, 2) never cover up the vents, 3) keep it out of the light
(the stuff that comes from the BBR, not the overheads), and 4) make sure
that the room it lives in stays at or under ~76 degrees F.

From what I understand, the fan is variable speed and will make more
noise the hotter it gets. In my experience, the fan spins at a fairly
high rate when the machine is first turned on and will settle into a
normal speed once a stable temperature is established.
