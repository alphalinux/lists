>>> josh said:
> yes... the colors used for the --color ls flag are stored in
> /etc/DIR_COLORS. you can also create a ~/.dir_colors file to override
> the system defaults.

Yes, that's *which* color *when*, but for changing the actual RGBs for
the colors, put something like the following in your .Xdefaults:

nxterm*VT100*color0: black
nxterm*VT100*color1: red
nxterm*VT100*color2: green
nxterm*VT100*color3: yellow
nxterm*VT100*color4: blue
nxterm*VT100*color5: magenta
nxterm*VT100*color6: cyan
nxterm*VT100*color7: white
nxterm*VT100*color8: black
nxterm*VT100*color9: red
nxterm*VT100*color10: green
nxterm*VT100*color11: yellow
nxterm*VT100*color12: blue
nxterm*VT100*color13: DodgerBlue1
nxterm*VT100*color14: cyan
nxterm*VT100*color15: white
