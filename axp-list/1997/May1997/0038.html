Say, thanks for your great responses. I wanted to post summary ASAP.

+++++++++++++++++++++++

Wouldn't it be easier and _faster_ to just network them both together?
But if ye insist on serial connections, read the PPP-HOWTO. Ye'll need
to
set up one as a ppp server and the other as ppp client.

++++++++++++++++++++++

i'm not sure i understand what you mean by y cables.. serial connections
are point to point. you can only have half as many connections as you
do
serial ports :) so you have 3 serial ports, you can only have 1
connection, 2 computers talking to each other :)

but if you get that working (null modem cables switch some of the pins
around.. transmit, receive, the flow control ones.. anything that is
directional) you'll just be able to do slip or ppp over the link. slip
makes more sense if there isn't alot of noise on the cable. or you
could
do plip, making a link over the parralel ports, it can do 100K/sec, i
think.. which is much more than the serial ports. i think 115kbps is
the
highest they go, but i could be wrong. sun serial ports can be clocked
up
to 2Mbps, but i'd be suprised if the udb could go that high.

any of that babbling useful? :)

-- zach

++++++++++++++++++++++

These are UDB's, i.e. you have ethernet interfaces already. Why you
will not just add a piece of a coax, with terminators on both ends,
and blast away? Simpler, faster, more effective and likely cheaper.

Even if you would have buy two cheap ethernet cards (you don't) this
would be likely a better solution anyway.

Michal

++++++++++++++++++++

WHy serial connect. Used IP between the two. All you need is a couple
of RJ45 cable and
a multiport. Probably cost you about $30 dollars. Or even cheaper just
one cable with the wires
twisted. Twisting the wire is a bit more complicated. I can't remember
off the top my head
but a couple of the wires need to twisted if you go directly between the
two. If you
need it I look it up, reply, otherwise bit the bullet, get a 4 or 5 port
hub.

Reply to me if you need help with the twist.

++++++++++++++++++++

Thank you all very much. I think I'll use the ppp/ppp-client, to
connect from NT (ip gateway thru ethernet) to Linux via serial port.

Scott
