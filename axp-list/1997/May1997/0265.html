On Wed, 7 May 1997, thomas.e.mccune wrote:

> >The 21064 and 21164 chips will NOT fit in a 21066 socket! It is the 21164
> >that has been rated for 300+ MHz. THAT's what we mean by the 400MHz
> >Multia is a myth. A 21164 chip will NOT fit in a Multia.

> Jim Paradis wrote:
> >There *IS* no such animal. There can *never* be such an animal,
> >because Digital has *NEVER* released a 21066 chip that runs at
> >more than 233MHz, and we never will.
>
> Have you actually looked at the socket in the VX42B to see if the 21164
> would NOT fit?

Here is a quote from the linux-AXP-faq at
www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/alpha-sys-4.html

>Now, the 21164 solves both these problems: it achieve much higher system
>bus bandwidths (despite having the same number of signal pins - yes, I
>know it's got about twice as many pins as a 21064, but all those extra
>ones are power and ground! (yes, really!!)) and it has write-back caches.
>The only remaining problem is the answer to the question "how much does
>it cost?"

I'm tellin ya... a 21164 is NOT gonna fit into a UDB! Nor is the
supporting chipset correct, either.

Another quote:

>DS sells two CPU support chipsets. The 2107x chipset (aka APECS) is a
>21064(A) support chiset. The 2117x chipset (aka ALCOR) is a 21164 support
>chipset. There will also be 2117xA chipset (aka ALCOR 2) as a 21164A
>support chipset.

The 21066 doesn't have a support chipset, because it's all built into the
CPU. Therefore, not e'en a 21064 will work in a UDB.

> Perhaps it was just wishful thinking to believe the story about putting
> in a newer CPU and running the clock at 300MHZ. Why would DEC put the CPU
> in a socket anyway? Aborted plans I presume.

That's more like it.
