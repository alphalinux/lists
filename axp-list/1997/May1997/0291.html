On Thu, 8 May 1997, Rick Windham wrote:

>
> On Thu, 8 May 1997, Ximenes wrote:
>
> > I don't know if there's an actual jumper or something that can be set, but I
> > believe that the kernel is configured to look for/use a VGA card before it
> > looks for/uses a TGA card.
>
> Couldn't it recognize both boards and then you just start X for the
> display you have the monitor hooked up to? Or hook up two monitors? Or
> can the box handle two display adaptors? Some Intel motherboards
> automatically disable the on-board video if they detect a PCI board on the
> buss.

I do not believe this is true, at least not in my case. I *had* to
*explicitely disable* TGA support in the kernel for Linux to use the VGA
adaptor. Otherwise, the Multia would boot recognizing and using the VGA
adaptor, but as soon as the kernel loaded, the screen would blank. If I
swapped the monitor cable over to the TGA port, I would have video.

BTW, no jumpers are needed.

I do not believe you can have both ports active at the same time. You *may*
be able to do this with a multi-headed server like for XIGraphics (Intel
only, however), but I think that the kernel only can recognize one adapter.
