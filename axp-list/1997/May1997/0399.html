Sigh...

You've found a limitation in MILO, WRT the size of kernel it can boot.

The 2.1.36 kernel now does some funky stuff with init code and data,
returning the pages they are on after boot-up is complete (I get 24-40Kb
back, depending on kernel options).

In order to do this cleanly, I assume that new text-init and data-init areas
are defined, which cause a bit more fragmentation and additional pages when
the kernel is loaded. This may make the kernel size larger thanb MILO can
handle; its current limit seems to be somewhere short of 2Mb uncompressed.

So, while waiting for new MILOs that solve this problem, you could try
re-configuring your kernel, removing any extra drivers/options not needed,
and check whether it will work via the command "size vmlinux"; "hex" size
less than 0x1f0000 will prolly work, larger prolly not.

I have no experience as to whether the use of modules will help the
situation or not, though one might expect they could...
