"Jon 'maddog' Hall, USG Senior Leader" <hall@zk3.dec.com> writes:
> The first topic (although readers of this list might say "Red Hat,
> of course) is one that I have dealt with since the beginning. I did
> not want to discourage any distribution by having Digital ship one
> particular distribution.

And as the person who's been bootstrapping Debian/AXP for the last
couple of months, I certainly appreciate that nonpartisanship. It
would have been discouraging---though I probably would have done it
anyways.

> Debian people keep criticizing me because Digital does not mention
> Debian enough (they will probably find Digital mentioning it more
> when there is an Alpha Debian distribution ready and stable).

Which is entirely reasonable.

BTW, if anyone in the Debian camp complains to you again, please ask
that individual to get in touch with me, mdorman@calder.med.miami.edu,
and I can explain why Digital talking about Debian/AXP before it is
done would be a bad idea.

> And before you say that Digital should "encourage" these vendors to
> port, I will tell you that 15 years of commercial operating system
> development and deployment shows that only works in a very few
> cases. The only thing that works (and works well) is that the
> application vendor CUSTOMERS demand a Linux port, then support that
> demand with buying Linux copies. Other methods of doing this lead
> do disaster over time. Trust me, I have the whip marks on my back
> over this one.

One need only look at the times IBM tried to do this to get OS/2 ports
of Windows apps (too many to count) or early ports of, say, 1-2-3 to
the Mac to see specific examples. If the product ever shipped, it was
usually a lackluster port, languished for a short period of time, and
disappeared from the public consciousness.

Mike.
