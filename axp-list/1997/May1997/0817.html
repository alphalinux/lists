> I am having a problem with RH lpr,lpc and all the other programs that deals
> with the lp command. It tells me that the 'lp' command is invalid. How do i
> fix this problem so I am print?

There is no "lp" command under Linux. lp is the printing command for systems
using the SysV printing system. All the "free" UNICes use the Berkeley LPD
system.

What could be missing is:
a) An appropriate /etc/printcap entry for your desired printer;
b) Kernel support for the parallel port (sometimes called /dev/lp; more often
/dev/lp0)

Email your /etc/printcap file; that should be useful for diagnosing the
problem.
