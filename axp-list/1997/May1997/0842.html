Hello, it's me again.

Thanks to this mailinglist, I could finally run Quake and look at the demo.
I hear the sounds, I see the pictures, great. Then I want to play myself.
This always leads to a memory violation, and a core is dumped. This happens
just after the "PackFile: ./id1/pak0.pak : maps/start.bsp". strace show
nothing between the last read() (which seems correct) and the SIGSEGV.

For those who may want to know: there is indeed a bug in the kernel headers
in the 2.0.30 stocks sources; in include/linux/ipc.h things are defined as
ushort, where they should be __kernel_uid_t. To correct it, I applied the
axp-diffs-2.0.30 file from azstarnet to the kernel. The bug seems to have
been corrected in recent 2.1.x kernels. However, the correction only allowed
me to go to the next frustration.

Now I have a bug which is not in XFree, not in the kernel, nowhere except in
the alpha-ELF-xquake-1.06 binary, whom sources I have not, of course, and I
am stuck. I know it works for some people. It does not for me.
I find this unfair.

Anybody has a solution ?
