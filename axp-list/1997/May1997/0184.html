On Mon, 5 May 1997, Paul B. Brown wrote:
> Am I missing something here?? Why does RH 4.1 want to know head/
> cylinder/sector counts for a SCSI drive? Isn't that stuff only needed at
> the local controller level (located on the drive itself)??

Exactly. FDISK thinks it needs CHS info because it was built for a PC,
which needs CHS info because, well, it's a PC, and PCs do goofy things at
times. :)

> If so, how can I get past this need that fdisk has for wanting to know
> H/C/S counts??

Lie to it. Seriously. Just make up convenient numbers for your partition
tables. This would break things like DOS and Windows, but I don't see those
running on your Alpha anytime soon. :-) This is explained in much more
detail towards the end of the SCSI-HOWTO file.
