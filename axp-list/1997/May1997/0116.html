>You have to make sure that the device at the end of the chain
>(i.e., your DAT drive) is configured to supply terminator power to the
>bus, so that your terminator has a good low-impedance power source.
>
Actually I have a scanner at the end of the chain, and all 5 of the
external devices on the chain are configured to supply term power, as
does the UDB itself. Even if the DAT drive (second unit off the UDB) was
not providing power I think that there is sufficient DC available at the
end.

>Note that it is not easy to tell active and passive terminators apart without
>taking electrical measurements. Some people have made the claim that
>terminators with LEDs are active, but there are passive terminators with
>LEDs. Even the markings on the terminators have been known to be wrong.

This point is well taken. I once asked somebody how to tell an active
terminator from a passive one and received the answer "by price",
probably as good as any other method :-)

But seriously, I initially had a LOT of trouble with my SCSI chain. I
looked at the scsi HOWTO and it said that if you were using SCSI-II or
external devices be sure to use high-quality cables that are all the same
and an active terminator. I took this advice and everything worked just
great.

I think the "all the same" is important too, as different cables have
different characteristic impedances which can cause reflections etc.
Active terminators can help reduce this problem but it is better to
eliminate it in the first place.

An idea that a lot of folks have trouble understanding - follow the HOWTO
and it works!

What a concept!

In any case, sorry to veer off-topic. Perhaps we should all adjourn to
alt.scsi.terminators.advocacy?
