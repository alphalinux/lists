On 5 May 1997, Michael Alan Dorman wrote:

'dog: And before you say that Digital should "encourage" these vendors to
'dog: port, I will tell you that 15 years of commercial operating system
'dog: development and deployment shows that only works in a very few
'dog: cases. The only thing that works (and works well) is that the
'dog: application vendor CUSTOMERS demand a Linux port, then support that
'dog: demand with buying Linux copies. Other methods of doing this lead
'dog: do disaster over time. Trust me, I have the whip marks on my back
'dog: over this one.

> One need only look at the times IBM tried to do this to get OS/2 ports
> of Windows apps (too many to count) or early ports of, say, 1-2-3 to
> the Mac to see specific examples. If the product ever shipped, it was
> usually a lackluster port, languished for a short period of time, and
> disappeared from the public consciousness.

OTOH, there is at least one lustrous port (MicroStation) that is
languishing because very few Linux users are asking for commercial
licensing --and this is the *only* thing preventing this robust,
full-featured professional CAD product from being available to
non-academic Linux users.

Much as I hate to admit it, the nay-ayers are often right presently: Linux
does not make commercial sense for many applications. Of course, I'd be
delighted to be proven wrong :-)
