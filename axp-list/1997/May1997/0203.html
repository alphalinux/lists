Tim Miller wrote:
>
> Michal Jaegermann wrote:
> > Eh? "Lower end" Alphas do not differ very much in cost from similar
> > Intel boxes and memory will get you at the same rate. A processor
> > power does not seem to be an issue with an intended application but
> > memory and I/O does.
>
> Since you brought this "lower end" issue up, where does one find
> "lower end" alphas that are comparable in cost to P166's?

Some examples:

PC164 motherboards are being "dumped" for $129/each by several vendors,
and fully configured 166 Mhz UDB/Multias are going for $295 w/ 32 Meg
RAM and 340 internal HD (w/keyboard & mouse) - just add monitor.

OTOH - I recently purchased a P166 w/16 Meg RAM, PCI video, floppy,
keyboard & mouse for $475 - add a NIC for $25 - 50, and more RAM at
$5/Meg (16 Meg for $80), and a HD ($225 for 2 Gig?) and you are good
to go.

If it were my decision, I would probably go with the X86 platform,
since the costs are "similar" and you don't need to jump through
too many hoops to get X configured.

Just my .02 worth.
