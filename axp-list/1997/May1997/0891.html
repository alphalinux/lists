It is possible that you see one of manifestations of a hotly discussed
nowadays, on linux-kernel list, bug somewhere in a buffer management.
Something goes haywire under a heavy load and a machine dies from
a memory starvation with tons of a free virtual memory around.
Earlier kernels were not immune from that but 2.0.30 did something
to make this scenario more likely. Things are still not clear.

It is possible to greatly reduce likelyhood of something like that
to happen by bumping up numbers reported by "freepages". As 'root'
see what shows when you do 'cat /proc/sys/vm/freepages' and 'echo'
back to 'freepages' a similar triplet, but with numbers doubled (or
maybe increased even more). It should substantially help if this
your problem - even if this a workaround and not a fix.
