On Tue, 27 May 1997, Keith Baker wrote:

> I love my alpha but I still have not sucsesfully compiled a kernel. I
> tried taking the stock 2.0.18 kernel axp rpm and header axp rpm. Am I
> correct in asuming since they are the axp rpm files that I do not need to
> apply the diffs?

I thought the same thing until I read the 4.1 Red Hat User Guide. The
philosophy of the RPM's are this:
1) To provide pristine source.
2) To provide all patches used to produce the binary files.
3) To provide the procedure to create the binary files.
So, it's pristine source, then check the .spec file to see the order of
the patches. (rpm -bi continues to refuse to work for me) Then apply the
patches to create the source they used to make the RPM. And vola! :-)
