On Mon, 5 May 1997, Tim Miller wrote:
> I will have to second this notion! My PC/Linux KT-958 Buslogic
> SCSI system ROCKS! It hasn't had a HICCUP!

...snip...
> Whereas, on our ALPHAStation 500/233, if there are more than two
> external SCSI devices (on very short cables and very long cables
> and even on a couple medium size cables), the system will hang
> if I write to tape and read from CDROM.
...snip...

To add to the comments about DU's stability with reference to Linux...

My experience was exactly the opposite of Tim's. I have a Microway Screamer
system with an NCR53c810 SCSI controller. I had problems getting Linux/Alpha
(RH4.0) to run stably at all, while DU ran with absolute stability since I
bought the machine. I fiddled endlessly with the SCSI setup for the NCR810
under Linux as I thought originally it was a SCSI driver problem, although this
did not cure the problem. Finally, with some help from Jay Estabrook at DEC
and Terry Klaila at Microway I swapped the cache static rams and I got Linux
2.0.28 to run stably (figure that one out!).

Although Linux finally ran on this machine, (judged by compiling the kernel
several times back to back while doing other work) it was not so reliable with
regard to some SCSI devices. Although I did not have any disk or CDROM errors
I could not read back a large (400+Mb) backup off my Exabyte 8200 without some
kind of SCSI error. In desperation, without moving the cables, I rebooted to
DU and read the backup tape just fine, several times in a row. My impression
is that the DU SCSI subsystem is rock solid, I wish Linux the SCSI drivers were
as stable.

Just another two cents...
