> Has anyone else had this problem? I have a UDB. And this seems
>random, sometimes if I power off the machine and come back to it it fails.
>Then if I leave if for a while it might or might not fail again.

UDBs are known to be prone to thermal problems, and your "unless I turn
off the machine and come back to it..." is a classic symptom of this sort
of trouble.

First, the usual stuff about clear, cool air around the unit etc...

Then, you're not running anything "extra" in the case are you? The Power
supply is NOT capable of, for example, a second hard drive.

Next, I would pull the main-board out of the unit and check that
everything is seated properly, RAM, SCSI riser, P/S cables, any chips in
sockets etc.

After re-assembly, check all external cables, serial, scsi, net, power
etc. Make sure you have a good active terminator on any external scsi
chain.

Make sure the heads on the floppy drive are clean, if you have a boot
choice to boot from a floppy. I believe that MILO will check that the
disk exists even if you do not select this option.

If you are over-clocking a VX-42 take it back to 233Mhz. (this is VERY
unlikely to be the problem as I have never heard of running a VX-42 at
266 causing trouble but I thought I'd mention it).

I realize all of this is just good general troubleshooting, but often
problems are simple.
