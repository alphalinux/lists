Britt Bolen wrote:
>
> I know this is more of a general unix question... but how would i reboot
> from telnet when my UDB's keyboard/mouse freeze on me? logging in a then
> doing an su root doesn't let me access shutdown

This is because how the $PATH variable is preserved when doing su.
As I understand it when you issue su command you just change the
uid (and gid?) but all environmental variables stays unchanged, i.e.
you are not logging in as root, just changing the uid. This means that
/etc/profile and ~/.bash_profile are not sourced when you change
identities with su.

Either add /sbin/ to the path of your alternate identity or just give
the absolute path /sbin/shutdown -r now
