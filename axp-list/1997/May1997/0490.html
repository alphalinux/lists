Once upon a time, Rick Windham said:
>
> Hello,
> I have a boot option to boot MILO with no params. Nine times out
> of ten it works perfectly, then suddenly out of the blue it will print a
> whole bunch of "O> O>" and refuse to take input.

I'll bet that you have an external modem on ttyS0 (COM1), and it's turned
on when this happens to you.

This occurs because MILO spews everything it prints to the screen (and then
some) out the serial port. Your modem thinks that these are commands, and
doesn't understand the output, so it sends an error back to MILO, which
thinks that you're typing at it, ad nauseam.

To fix: Buy a serial splitter cable, or build one yourself. there's a
local place that can build the cables for $30 (Or so they told me on the
phone; I didn't actually buy from them.) Or turn off your modem when you
reboot your machine.
