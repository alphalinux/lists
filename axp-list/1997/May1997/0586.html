Steven Kokinos <steve@webdevelop.com> writes:

> I've been recompiling a number of apps for the new glibc, which
> seems to be a lot more stable than the previous versions. At any
> rate, I am continually running into errors with signal.h, and
> select.h being broken.

What do you mean when you say "broken"?

The only problem I'm aware of is a conflict between select.h and
asm/posix_types.h.

To fix this, edit asm/posix_types.h (this is a kernel header file),
and add an #if defined(__GLIBC__) block around the declaration of the
FD_* macros, etc. Enclose the section already #ifdef'd for gcc.
