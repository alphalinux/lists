On Sun, 5 Oct 1997, Paul Holcomb wrote:

>
> I just bought a 166Mhz Multia system with the intention of putting a PCI
> video card in so that I could have 16 bit color.
>
> There were a few snags with the Redhat install, but for the most part, it
> went quite well. I then read up on the archives for this list and all the
> other information I could find; everyone seems to have no trouble using
> PCI video cards. The Diamond Stealth 64 DRAM was even noted to work quite
> well.
>
> However, here is what happens when I try to use the machine with the card
> installed. First, no video comes out of the new card at all. Hooked up to
> a power-saving monitor, no video signal is present. On the TGA, the
> normal SRM screen that prints the memory check before loading ARC is visible.
> However, it never gets past the "loading ARC firmware." message.
>
> I tried an ATI Mach64 card, I tried changing the resolution on the TGA card
> with the jumpers, and even tried using serial to see if there was any further
> information. The only life out of the PCI video card at all is after
> the SRM starts to load ARC. At this point, if you press the shift key, the
> monitor attached to the Stealth card clicks off power-saving and *sometimes*
> has a steady cursor.
>
> Most of the messages on this archive involved problems with a new VGA card
> with Linux. I can't even get there. Does anyone have any suggestions? They
> would be _very_ appreciated.
>
> FYI, the ARC firmware is version 4.33 (August 1995). I believe this is the
> latest.
>
I am using 4.49 on an XL266. I don't know if your system is differnet,
but you can download from ftp.digital.com/Digital/info/semiconductor/
literature/dsc-library.html#alpha
