Behold: on that day, 29 of September in the year 1997,
Michael W. Chalkley spake unto me, saying:

> Hello:
>
> I just purchased a used 275 Mhz Alpha with a Cabriolet Motherboard. It only
> has 32 Meg of Ram (4 of 8 slots populated). I have a couple of questions:
>
> 1. I know it is not the fastest system available but could I use it
> as both a news and web server?

I would guess easily. This is not exactly a _slow_ box we're talking
about here. Depending on your demands, I would guess that drive
access and net bandwidth would be limiting factors _way_ before the
CPU would. And memory, of course (but that's true for any box).

> 2. Should I add another 4 simms to take it to 64 Meg or should I
> swap it all out and take it to 128? Can I add 64 to the current 32?

I own such a box and have it running fine on the 32+64=96Mb config.

Also, cache is important. Mine is configured at 2Mb 8ns cache -- I
would upgrade that too if you have less than 1Mb.

> 3. What type of memory do I need. What are the average prices?

FULL PARITY! You can get it from many memory dealers, but they often
don't believe you when you say FULL PARITY. NOT logical parity.
Emphasize this to them. NOT LOGICAL PARITY. IT WILL NOT WORK! AT
ALL! Not even just a little bit. Don't let them even suggest
shipping anything except full parity. And nothing marginal either --
this machine hits its memory hard (much harder than your typical
WinTel box, so the dealer might not be used to having to live up to
real standards) and flakey memory will give you endless headaches. As
you can guess, I had to send back some memory -- 3 separate lots, to
be specific -- for these kinds of reasons... Works great with good
memory, though.

Also, this box predates the recent memory revolutions. Doesn't
support EDO or anything more modern.

And I have no clue about prices -- haven't been in the market for
memory for too long.

> 4. What is the most stable version of RedHat I should run?

I've been running 2.0.28 just fine for quite a while now. I hear that
2.0.30 works well too, but when I tried it I had some problems with
the IPX protocol drivers. YMMV.

Your other questions are beyond my experience. Sorry.

Good luck, and enjoy!
