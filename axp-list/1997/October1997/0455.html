Dear Harvey

> > >We have two 533mhz alphas with 164LX motherboards, purchased from
> > >Aspen Systems. One of them has the following strange behaviour.
> > >
> > >After it's been unplugged it can't be turned on again just by hitting
> > >the power switch. To get it to start, I have to open it up & short
> > >out the J1 pins (the soft power connector next to the power connector
> > >on the motherboard).
> > >
> > >Aspen said it's a problem they've seen before and that they've asked
> > >Digital for a fix.
> > >
> > >Does anyone know anything about such behavior? Are there any fixes?
> > >Do I need to add another switch to the machine so I can avoid popping
> > >the cover every time it's unplugged?
> >
> > My first thought is the power switch is bad. If you can manually restart it
> > then the switch might be the problem. Try replacing it first.
> >
> > We shipped over 400 and haven't seen that one. My guess is the soft power
> > circuit has a problem, if its not the switch. Only soultion then is replace
> > the motherboard.
>
>The problem is that they connected a toggle to the power supply
>but left the soft power connector unconnected.

I believe you just told me what the problem is. If I understand you
correctly you have a hard power supply on your motherboard. The hard power
is a supply with a black thick cord running from the switch to the power
supply. If thats what you have then you need to replace it with a soft
power supply.

The LX motherboard has that soft power jumper that you mentioned J1 SFT
power, this goes to the power switch on your front panel. According to
EMACS and DEC thats all your suppose to use on that motherboard, soft power.
That could be why I haven't seen this one before, we only use soft power.
