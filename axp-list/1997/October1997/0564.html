> > I believe this is the correct reading of the news. The cost of the
> > Hudson plant can now be amortised over a larger production run. Moreover,
> > we don't know for example the Digital didn't have appropriate yields on
> > alphas, maybe something Intel could fix.
>
> I cannot resist this. Yields are highly secret within chip manufacturers and
> so I cannot tell you what they are (nor can I talk about this deal). However,
> I _can_ tell you catagorically that Digital's FAB 6 plant (where all the
> Alphas were and will be made) has excellent yields. The relationship with
> Intel will not "fix" any Digital manufacturing technologies. The suggestion
> is insulting to a great bunch of engineers and technical folks.
>

Guess I can't either. I don't work for Digital but I have worked designing
and fabing CPUs. I don't think Digital has a yield problem, rather an
anti-yield problem. We have bought and worked with every generation of DEC
CPU and have never! seen a CPU that would not run way beyond its
spec. This is in contrast to Intel who has often resorted to selling CPUs
that are speced at different voltage ratios in order to meet their
yield quotas, and any one who has a dual CPU intel board knows that it's
suggested that the two CPUs even come from the same batch.

Perhaps DEC can help intel.
