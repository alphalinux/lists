On Wed, 15 Oct 1997, Alpha RedHat wrote:

> I was suprised to find out that my Alpha XL 233 w/48meg is slower than my
> P-200. (Drat.. Drat... Drat... Thought these alpha's were supost to be
> fast)

They are, really, if you compare apples to apples and not apples to
oranges :P If you estimate your performance increase by comparing the
MHz, then you will be disappointed in the Alpha, but if you realise the
differences in the architectures and understand the organisational
differences in the two chips, then you will have a better understanding of
why the two don't compare MHz-to-MHz. I, personally, see much better
performance in RISC-oriented operations (any instructions that can easily
be broken down into RISC instructions) than in CISC-oriented operations.
Although the Pentiums may seem faster in most operations, keep in mind
that the Alpha can move twice as much data at once if the operations are
optimised properly. The Linux-Alpha distributions right now are not
mature enough to do so to perfection. I'm guessing that we should see
better performance as more code is specifically written with Alphas in
mind.

Oh, one more thing...check your /proc/cpuinfo and see how many unaligned
accesses have been corrected since you last rebooted. If it's more than
0, then that could account for at least some of the performance hit that
you've been seeing. Any time the Alpha has to correct for awful coding
like that, it has to generate 3-4x as many instructions to handle the
problem as it would if the code had been properly written with 64-bit
processors in mind.
