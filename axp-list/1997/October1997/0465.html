Craig Ruff <cruff@niwot.scd.ucar.edu> writes:
> I've been getting duplicate messages for at least a week, and have been
> unable to contact the list maintainer or the redhat postmaster to figure
> out what is going on. Anyone else getting two of each message sent to
> axp-list?

We determined in the past, that some people get sometimes duplicate
message, often even more then 2 copies. This happend to me a lot, and
by looking at the log files, I see it still happens sometimes. Maybe
its due to bad line quality, e.g. the list server believes the mail
hasn't been correctly delivered, though it had.

Anyway, in case you use procmail (which is standard in RH-4.x I believe),
you can easily filter out additional copies by adding this on top off
.procmailrc:

:0 Wh: msgid.lock
| formail -D 8192 msgid.cache

If you're scared that this might delete important messages, you may
restrict this to messages originating from this list.

I am using this for month now, with no problems. It also helps to
remove duplicate SPAM messages.

OTOH, if you get exactly two copies of each mail, it is more likely
that you're subscribed twice, maybe with two different e-mail
addresses being routed to your account. Check the 'Received:'
headers.
