I think people are getting a little bit emotional, here. It is actually
quite common in the industry to farm out foundry work, as that has low
margins compared to license fees.

If I read it correctly, DEC has *not* sold the alpha, but is paying Intel
to build them. Am I wrong? Also, with the cross-patent agreement DEC may
be able to legally use some Intel tech. to further improve the alpha (and
likewise the other way with the ia-64.)

Give it a few years, and keep buying those alphas (and Linux, of course)
and this whole mess may come out for the best.

Incidentally, although Intel is pretty darn irritating, the real enemy
is MicroSoft:-)
