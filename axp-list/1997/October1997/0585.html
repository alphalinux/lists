>>> Stefan Schroepfer said:
>
> pc164trm lists jumper configurations for CPU clock speeds of
> 366 MHz to 500 MHz (corresponding to clock ratios of 11 to 15)
> and says that all other combinations are reserved.
> Does anybody know anything about these reserved settings?

Yes, they are *REALLY* reserved!

EV56's existed for a long time (before the MIATA and LX were sold) that
would go over 500MHz, but the PC164 motherboard/core logic CANNOT handle
anything over 500MHz reliably.

> Is there a possibility to set higher clock ratios (for faster
> CPUs) or even lower clock ratios (see next question below)?

DO IT AT YOUR OWN RISK!!!

> pc164trm says a 500 MHz CPU clock corresponds to a ratio of 15.
> So I suspect that memory bus is at 33 MHz :-(. Is that right?
> Or is 'ratio' relative to PCI bus frequency?

Yes, the system clock is a ratio of the CPU clock, so when you put in
a faster crystal, you have to adjust the ratio, or take the chance of
over/under-clocking the system/PCI bus/etc, with the chance of very weird
behavior.
