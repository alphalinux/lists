My theory is that any kind of group that has a serious dilemma will split
off into two or more smaller groups (look at organized religion - and yes
there are MANY similarities between love of an OS and religion). Since the
BSD projects are management by committee, you will have as many groups as
you have cliqs with different opinions. Linux has avoided that by having
ONE person where the "buck stops" in the person of Linus Torvalds. Not
everyone will agree with Linus, but the project stays sound and coherent.
The committee model that BSD seems to favor and tout as superior has (my
opinion) resulted in an unfortunate dilution of the BSD effort. If all
the stellar programmers that are working on the various flavors of BSD
could ever get together, they could REALLY give other OS's a run for their
money.

</SOAPBOX>
