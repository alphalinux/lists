I don't quite qualify as a guru, but I will comment anyway...

The UDB systems have an apparent slowdown for two reasons: inadequate RAM
and inadequate cache. We could argue that Digital should have known
better, but that would be pointless. At least the RAM situation is
cureable by carefully throwing money at the system 8-).

If your 533 MHz system has a large cache and plenty of RAM, you are going
to scream. For computationally intensive tasks (Fortran's traditional
program), my feeling would be that the pure 64 bit wide environment of the
alpha family will give you more initial power than the mixed 32/64 of the
Intel family.
