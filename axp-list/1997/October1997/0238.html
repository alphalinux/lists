On Wed, 15 Oct 1997, Atif Ahmad Khan wrote:

> I just installed Linux RedHat 4.2 on my newly acquired UDB (Alpha).
> Its got the 166MHz processor in it. I installed 32MB of memory in it
> and a 2GB Fujitsu SCSI 5400RPM external hard drive.
> This machine is, to put it humbly, slow as hell.

What kind of performance did you expect from it? Just curious. The UDB
is pretty much the "bottom of the barrel" when it comes to Alphas since
it's memory access is horribly limited and slow compared to the more
upscale Alphas. You probably would've been better off with a PC164
motherboard and a 466MHz Alpha to really soar performance-wise.

> I was hoping to replace
> a Pentium 75 (Linux) machine with it. But the Pentium machine with less
> memory and a slow 3600RPM IDE drive is screaming fast compared to this Alpha.

Ah, ok. Guess I should've read that sentence to get an idea of what
you're comparing it to. I also have a UDB, but with a pretty stock config
(24MB RAM, Jaz drive, and internal 540MB hard drive) and know your pain as
far as most performance issues go, BUT I do have to say that my floating
point operations are much better than my Pentium 75 at home. I guess it
all depends on what you're doing with it. To be able to maybe give you
better performance hints, I would have to know what you are doing on the
UDB that makes your impression of it so poor.

Keep in mind that gcc isn't totally mature on the Alpha platform right
now, and therefore, doesn't necessarily generate the most optimised code
at times. This becomes evident when comparing the same code on the same
machine running under Digital UNIX vs Linux. Strides are being made to
correct this, but I don't have a time schedule from "the powers that be"
yet.

> I am using the stock kernel that came with RedHat because I can't get anything
> else to compile right for me.
> Does anybody know what the problem could be? Has anybody put up one of the newer
> versions of compiled kernel and modules for UDB that I can download to experiment
> with?

In all fairness, I don't run RedHat on my UDB (I'm a Debian person and am
working with the group to port over our distribution to the Alpha), BUT I
do know that I have several kernels compiled and running on my UDB. In
fact, I just compiled a mostly modularised 2.1.57 kernel today without a
problem. You probably could get slightly better performance out of a
"hand-rolled" kernel, FYI. Be sure to get the appropriate AXP patches for
it prior to trying to compile it under RedHat, btw...

> While I am at it, asking for help, can anybody email me their XF86Config
> file for UDB?

Wish I could. I don't run X on my machine due to memory limitations and
general lack of interest. I find that I can do just about everything with
a bunch of VT's and some ingenuity :)
