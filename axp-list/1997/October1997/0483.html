I believe this is the correct reading of the news. The cost of the
Hudson plant can now be amortised over a larger production run. Moreover,
we don't know for example the Digital didn't have appropriate yields on
alphas, maybe something Intel could fix. BTW if this were the real problem
then Loaral would have been a better partner (forgetting about the
litigation. Even though the stock analysts seem to think the deal was
good for Digital, in the long term it makes Digital more dependent on
Microsoft/Intel and therefore less in control of their destiny.

While I'm on the soapbox, let me say to you starry eyed ones, "learning
about 64 bit computing is a pretty empty phrase. Furthermore, there are
some serious design flaws in the alpha architecture (Of course, Intel
architecture is a giant leap backwards. Why they chose an accumulator
architecture with the 8086, I can only assume that they had read nothing
of the literature)
