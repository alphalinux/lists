Here's what I've found out so far:

Config:
UDB with 2 SCSI disks, 3 partitions each-
/dev/sda1 - DOS
/dev/sda2 - swap
/dev/sda3 - RH4.2 installation
/dev/sdb1 - DOS
/dev/sdb2 - swap
/dev/sdb3 - RH4.1 installation
--sda is internal, sdb is external jaz disk

ok. Using the ramdisk.img out of mustang dir, it asks for what type of
media the installation packages are on. I pick hard drive. The system shows
me the sda1,sda3,sdb1,sdb3 list and asks where the Redhat/RPMS and
RedHat/base is. I pick /dev/sdb3 (which, not coincidentally, is where the
RedHat dir sits).
It then scans the packages and appears to find them find. I answered
upgrade to the install/upgrade question so it then finds both the RH4.2
(/dev/sda3) and RH4.1 (/dev/sdb3) partitions. I select the second
(/dev/sdb3) as the root partition. It then gives me the error: 'mount:
device not configured'

I did an fsck on the jaz (/dev/sdb3) and it came up clean. However, I
switched to VC3 and looked at the messages. All is normal up to the point
after it mounts /dev/sda3 and /dev/sdb3 to tell me those are my choices of
partition to upgrade. (I checked VC3 at several points during the process
trying to follow what things were being mounted and such). The last things
in the message log go like:

* mkswap failed to get size of device /tmp/sdc2
* mount /tmp/sdc3 on /mnt

(note that there is no /dev/sdc2 or /dev/sdc3. Not sure where it's getting
this device name from but it doesn't exist on the system, in the /proc/scsi
list, or anywhere else.)

Perhaps this is why mount says device not configured?
