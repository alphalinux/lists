>>> Matti Aarnio said:
> ...
> > 1.) What's the best tulip.c driver for the Alpha (PC164)? 0.79 works okay
> > but gives a lot of unaligned traps (the machine never hangs though,
> > it's just annoying...)
>
> 0.81 has a copy alignment threshold raised to 1.5 kB, which
> handles this case. But pray tell me (whoever can), why the
> driver can't assume the packet on Ethernet to be IP, and align
> the input buffers with that in mind -- optimize the common case..

And I've heard that 082 always sets that threshold for Alpha, thus
guaranteeing the copy/align will happen, *without* patch... :-)

> It may, of course, be that the buffer must start at even 32 bit
> boundary, and thus receiving 6+6+2 bytes prefix causes IP header
> to have offset of 2 bytes :-( (DestMAC+SrcMAC,pkttype)
> (Reading 21142 databook would tell, but I pass that now.)

BINGO! Yes, it seems that the TULIP, on Alphas, anyway, can only DMA to
32-bit boundaries, thus the *packets* come out aligned, but not the headers.
