From: Computerworld Daily <cwflash_mail@computerworld.com>
The Oct. 27, 1997, edition of Computerworld's daily E-mail.
Contents in this edition include:
* It's official: Intel and Digital settle their lawsuit
________________________________________________________
Digital Equipment and Intel said they will settle their
legal disputes with an agreement that includes the sale of
Digital's semiconductor manufacturing operations to Intel
for $700 million. At a news conference, company officials
said the agreement is contingent on approval from government
agencies.
http://www2.computerworld.com/home/online9697.nsf/All/971027digital19962
____________________________________________________________

A brief overview of this article has DEC continuing to design the Alpha
and future generations of the Alpha, but the fabs are being sold to Intel.
It settles the dispute in that it sells Alpha 'technology' to Intel.
However, it still looks like a pretty pathetic deal for DEC to me.
