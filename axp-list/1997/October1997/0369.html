> I would like to move it to the external one. My question is whether it is
> enough just to partition the new disk and copy the whole linux filesystem
> onto it, or whether the symbol /dev/sda of the internal disk is coded somewhere
> in the configuration files or yet somewhere else (apart from the obvious
> /etc/fstab) ? What I would like to avoid is to have to install Linux again
> on the external disk.

That should work fine, I did this several times that way.

Of course you should test the new system before deleting the old
disk. Remember, when you copied the stuff, the new disk might be
/dev/sdb. Once you remove your first disk, this becomes /dev/sda
again. So boot your new system, edit /dev/fdisk, remove the other
disk, and reboot.

If you switch to different SCSI-IDs, you might have to
adjust your boot loader settings as well.
