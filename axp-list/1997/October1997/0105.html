It's time again to fiddle with video cards on my UDB in the hopes of
getting 16+ bitplanes and higher resolution than the TGA provides.
There's a 4MB S3V card sitting on my desk doing nothing as I've had no
joy with it yet.

Saturday I tried out a Cirrus-based card; the machine booted, and
everything was "happy" up to the point of trying to run XF86_SVGA,
at which point I determined that the Alpha X server only supports
S3V, Matrox Millenium, Mystique, and Millenium II.

The story I hear is that of the Matrox cards, only the (now discontinued)
Millenium is *not* in "beta" state. Is this verifiable? In any case,
the Matrox cards are rather expensive.

The next option is to look into Mach64-based cards. I'll be looking this
evening at a "surplus" place or two; there's nothing being sold new with
"Mach 64" chips; what *is* for sale is the newer "3D Rage" chipsets.
Has anybody had success with "3D Rage" cards?

Is it conceivable that I need to be using a newer kernel than 2.0.27
in order to make an S3V work? I well know of the no_pci_disconnect...
