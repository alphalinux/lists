Jay.Estabrook@digital.com wrote:
> But, the real story is that the EBSDK PALcode was present *long* before
> Linux/Alpha was a gleam in a Maddog's eye... :-)

So if I understand you correctly, in the days before Linux/Alpha, there wasn't
any OS that would boot on the EBSDK PALcode? How did they verify that the
PALcode did anything useful?

> The EBSDK PALcode is a cousin to the OSF PALcode that is part of the SRM
> console, but is *far* from identical. The same PAL entry points are mostly
> there, but other details, like interrupt delivery, differ *greatly*. OSF
> PALcode "cooks" the interrupt; EBSDK PALcode lets the higher level (non-PAL
> code) handle the dirty details of interrupt summary/mask registers, etc.

I don't want to malign all the great work that people have done on EBSDK,
Milo, and the Linux Kernel, but wouldn't having the PALcode "cook" the
interrupts be a Good Thing (tm)? Wasn't it part of the purpose of PALcode
to hide some of the goriest detail of the hardware, so that the OS kernel
didn't need to contain as much platform-specific code?

I know that the PALcode (and console) can't and shouldn't try to hide
*all* of the platform-specific differences, but it would sure be nice to
have platform-specific Milo and a generic kernel.

Cheers,
Eric
