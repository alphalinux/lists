Jan Gryko:
> I am really scared by several postings to this list about
> very low speed of Alpha systems. I am seriously considering buying
> Alpha 533 MHz system to run large Fortran programs and after
> reading todays postings I think that maybe Pentium Pro 266 will be
> a safer investment? Any comments?

Well first of all, don't confuse the UDB with a new 533MHz. UDB = old, 533 = new, ok?

As for the new Alphas, floating point really scream commpared to a lowly PPRO. I think
I saw numbers of about 7-9 times faster fp-aritm compared to a PPRO, not sure though.
Long integers arithmetic is also fast, dhrystone for example is almost four
times faster than a PPRO 200. Byte manipulation suck, as bad as PPRO, at least in gcc.
Should improve with newer versions of gcc. Don't know about your Fortran.
