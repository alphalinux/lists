> I am really scared by several postings to this list about
> very low speed of Alpha systems. I am seriously considering buying
> Alpha 533 MHz system to run large Fortran programs and after

Keep in mind that the machines being discussed are a particular
"unusual" variant called the Multia/UDB.

Street price for 'em seems to be between $350 and $500 USD, depending
on memory/RAM configuration.

They were designed as nice little "network computer" boxes in the
days before Oracle decided that such things wouldn't have disks in 'em.

The UDBs are severely hampered by poor memory bandwidth that results
from insufficient cache as well (apparently) as the CPU not having the
best "connections to RAM." (No doubt others can describe this in the
appropriate terms.)

There is no question of them being "high-powered supercomputer engines."
There is no reason for your system to have the performance bottlenecks
that they do.

If you get an Alpha/533, you should most certainly consider how to
configure the box. These days, any of the Alpha VARS should have some
reasonable clue on how to make sure that the box isn't configured to
have *inherent* bottlenecks.
