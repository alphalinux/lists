Hi,

Normally I would not respond to such drivel, but since the Wall Street Journal
has chosen to say something about it, here goes.

Yes, Digital is in a suit against Intel.

Things are discussed in settlement actions. They should not EVER be discussed
outside those settlement discussions. No reputable negotiator would ever
do that.

In the past there have been rumors and rumors of rumors about various companies
buying Digital, about Digital doing this, Digital doing that, and all have
been for naught. Even when formal agreements are made, papers signed, etc.
things don't always work out (can you say "ACE"?)

So, as the statement below says, don't think its happening until the Fat Lady
Sings.....

You can sign me:

TIRED OF BEING ASKED ABOUT IT IN NASHUA

*****THIS MESSAGE IS FROM TOM SIEKMAN*****


There are stories in today's press regarding the potential settlement
of the patent infringement suit we filed against Intel in May 1997.
This speculation is disruptive to both our employees and our customers.

There are no agreements between the two companies that resolve these
issues.

Let me be very clear, that if any agreement were reached on behalf of
Digital Equipment Corporation in this matter:

o It would provide a fair resolution of our patent infringement suit.
o It would reaffirm DIGITAL Alpha as the 64-bit architecture of choice.
o It would recognize the continuing contribution of DIGITAL's people.
o It would recognize the success and growing importance of Digital's x86
businesses for mobile, client and server computing.

The stories you read today will likely continue until either the
conclusion of the litigation or any settlement. Please be assured that,
when there are facts to report, they will be communicated promptly and
directly to you.

In the meantime, it is crucial that employees not speculate or comment
about any aspect of the Intel litigation, including the potential for
settlement. Any external inquiries should be referred to Dan Kaferle at
DTN 223-2195.
