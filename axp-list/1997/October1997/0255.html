>> I am really scared by several postings to this list about
>> very low speed of Alpha systems. I am seriously considering buying
>> Alpha 533 MHz system to run large Fortran programs and after

>They were designed as nice little "network computer" boxes in the
>days before Oracle decided that such things wouldn't have disks in 'em.

As I've said before, Digital produced a really neat network computer with the
Multia and just as the idea caught on, discontinued it....

I've got both a UDB and an XL-300 along with a 75Mhz 586 all running Linux.
Certainly the UDB is faster than the 586 for the sort of things I use it
for (mainly file serving and providing linux support at work). On the other
hand the XL-300 really flies and is at least as responsive as 300Mhz
Digital Unix workstations I get to play with.

As everyone has said, the UDB is cheap for a reason (in fact at least two -
CPU and memory subsystem) but it's still more than usable as long as you
don't expect it to be a PPro200 equivalent.
