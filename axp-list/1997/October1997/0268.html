Jan Gryko wrote

I am really scared by several postings to this list about
very low speed of Alpha systems. I am seriously considering buying
Alpha 533 MHz system to run large Fortran programs and after
reading todays postings I think that maybe Pentium Pro 266 will be
a safer investment? Any comments?

Don't let yourself be swayed by either the bright eyed enthusiasts ("a
$500 supercomputer") or by boat-anchor doomsayers.

The 'rule of a thumb' is that Alpha is 30% slower that the same-clock Intel
(because AXP instructions tend to do less per cycle). The Multia that
has been discussed here adds another penalty, because it has slow memory-CPU
interface. Most people agree that a 166 MHz Multia is about on par with
a 90-100MHz Pentium. At the same time, it is much better than that on FP
code.

A 533 MHz motherboard will use more advanced CPU/chipset that will not
have the above memory penalty, so that it should be equivalent on
integer code to, say, a 400 MHz Pentium, and it should need a 600 MHz
Pentium to match its FP performance---if everything goes well
(i.e. your FP code is not trapping doing underflows etc., is
cache/TLB/paging friendly, the compiler didn't mess up, etc).
The bottom line is that you have to run your benchmark and see;
most likely Alpha will win, but no guarantees.

Unfortunately, at present, Linux on Alpha still needs more hands-on
treatment than on Intel platform. Debugging and profiling still has
occasional problems, compilers and libraries have uneven performance
especially on FP, there are sometimes kernel/MILO issues that take
people by surprise. If you are new to Linux, I'd say begin with a
Pentium. Having said that, many people have flawlessly performing
AXP systems (myself included).
