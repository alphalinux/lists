I searched axp-list for the word patch, and read all the messages from
1997 (took a *long* time...), but didn't seem to find the patch,
except for some stuff on ftp://genie.ucd.ie/pub/alpha/kernel/, in
particular ftp://genie.ucd.ie/pub/alpha/kernel/my_patches/traps.diff.
Is this the patch you're refering to?

In any case, I unpacked a pristine kernel 2.0.30. I patched it with
ftp://ftp.digital.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/em86/v0.2/kernel-patches/alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.2a.gz.
I configured & compiled it & was able to boot successfully, and things
seemed to be running ok.

However, a) the trap problem remained, and b) I started having header
trouble when compiling things. In particular, including
<sys/resource.h> seemed to give lots of problems.

At this point, even after scouring axp-list & the
axp-kernel-compiling-howto (which seems somewhat out of date, btw),
I'm still having trouble understanding what libs & kernels & patches
are compatible.

I presumably am running glibc2 - rpm reports I'm using glibc-0.961212-5
and glibc-devel-0.961212-5 (rh 4.2). So,

1. Are these compatible with an unpatched kernel 2.0.30? What
about 2.0.31? What about the 2.1 series?
2. Do I need alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.2a.gz, or do I just need it for
em86? Is it compatible with the above setup?
3. alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.2a.gz is pretty new, so I had assumed it
included all well known bug fixes. I take it this isn't the
case? If not, is it going to get fixed?
4. My /usr/include header files seem to include things from
<linux/...> and <asm/...>. I thought someone mentioned that
glibc2 no longer depends on kernel header files. What's the
story here?
5. Should I upgrade/change my glibc? Is there an appropriate RPM
or should I build it from scratch? Will doing so break
anything?

I'm going to see what happens when running an unpatched 2.0.30 &
2.0.31 kernel next.
