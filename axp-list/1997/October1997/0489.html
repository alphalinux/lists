Dear Alpha lovers

>I think people are getting a little bit emotional, here. It is actually
>quite common in the industry to farm out foundry work, as that has low
>margins compared to license fees.
>
>If I read it correctly, DEC has *not* sold the alpha, but is paying Intel
>to build them. Am I wrong? Also, with the cross-patent agreement DEC may
>be able to legally use some Intel tech. to further improve the alpha (and
>likewise the other way with the ia-64.)

This is correct, the Alpha design team is still at Digital and will still
release new processors. However, the Hudson plant never ran to full
capacity so dumping it may have been the right choice. Did you know AMD
processors were built there? From what I'm told they were doing whatever
they could to keep the plant functioning 100% but never did.

Please also note Digital inked the deal so Intel would build the Alpha for
10 years and a lot can happen in 10 years. The Alpha alliance with Samsung
and Mitsubishi should also produce Alpha clones and I'm willing to bet at a
much lower cost.

One final note the 21264 is still due to be released in Q2 of 98.

>Give it a few years, and keep buying those alphas (and Linux, of course)
>and this whole mess may come out for the best.
>
>Incidentally, although Intel is pretty darn irritating, the real enemy
>is MicroSoft:-)

100% agreement!
