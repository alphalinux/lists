Yes, wouldn't it be? :-)

But, the real story is that the EBSDK PALcode was present *long* before
Linux/Alpha was a gleam in a Maddog's eye... :-)

Its intention, like the (early) Evaluation Boards (EB64/EB66), was to provide
a technology display and starting point for potential customers of Alpha chips
who wished to clone or roll their own motherboard products.

SRM consoles were available for the EBs, and often the clones were close
enough to qualify for running it.

The EBSDK PALcode is a cousin to the OSF PALcode that is part of the SRM
console, but is *far* from identical. The same PAL entry points are mostly
there, but other details, like interrupt delivery, differ *greatly*. OSF
PALcode "cooks" the interrupt; EBSDK PALcode lets the higher level (non-PAL
code) handle the dirty details of interrupt summary/mask registers, etc.

> If not, where *does* an OEM get PALcode to use for DU?

OEM's have typically contracted DEC to do an SRM console for them, if they
actually want to run DU on their own design. Deskstation is the only clone
maker that I know of that really has rolled their own firmware, and they only
did it for NT (prolly with some help from DEC at some point and to some
extent). Note that EBSDK PALcode and a MILO have been adapted for use on
their RUFFIAN platform to run LINUX... :-) :-)
