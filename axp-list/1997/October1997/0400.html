John Ross Hunt wrote:
>
> Here's an easier way.
...
> find / -depth -xdev -print | cpio -padvVmB /mnt/disk2

With this caveat the the above will not work if your current
installation is using more than one partition (it can be fixed).
'-xdev' is a stumbling block and you **really** do not want
to skip it.

Otherwise the line above has one advantage over 'cp -a' - you do
not have to worry about creating 'proc' on your target directory.
Easier? I will not argue as this is a matter of taste. :-)
But the line above is practically equivalent to 'cp -ax / /mnt/disk2'
and the same limitations do apply. I did not want to complicate
matters. :-) More "trouble" if your target disk has multiple
partitions and their numbers on both disks are not necessarily the same.

John is right that on a Unix installation with a less capable 'cp' one
has to do something like the above. For example (root partition only):
tar -clf - / | (cd /mnt/disk2 ; tar -xpf - )
which can be a bit simpler and safer with GNU tar. Depending on what you
have on hands there are few other similar methods. Take your pick. :-)

Summary: a totally "fool-proof and easy" method of cloning your whole
installation on another disk does not exist. You will have to think
a bit about what you are doing and with what.
