On Sat, 11 Oct 1997, Dave Wreski wrote:

>
> Hi all. I just bought a UDB and it hasn't even arrived yet. It has no
> memory or hard disk on board.
>
> I'm interested in experiences on where to get true-parity memory for this
> machine, as well as a 4G drive for it..

I've been getting mine at The Chip Merchant:
www.thechipmerchant.com

> If I specify 'true-parity' to the salesman, will I get what I need? I
> don't think there is much 'logical-parity' memory out there still, is
> there?

The Chip Merchant includes their part numbers in their list of simms and
cpus which is very helpful in specifying what I want to the salesperson on
the phone.

> Also, are most people using external cases for their hard disks? I found
> one at APS that looks pretty good..

I got a nice 4 bay case from Dalco Electronics. I still haven't found
anyone else that can beat their prices. And, you guessed it--they have a
web site too: www.dalco.com

The hard drive I'm using is a 3 GB Seagate full-height 5.25" behemoth I
got from www.onsale.com. The thing takes up two of the four bays in the
case, but that's my only gripe about it.

So far this combination has been working beautifully.
