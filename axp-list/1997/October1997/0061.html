>>> Uncle George said:
>
> I get the impression that unaligned (quadword ) fetches have been fixed
> so that the kern will always attempt to fix the alignment prob (silently).

Well, not just quadword, longword (4 bytes) as well.

And not totally silently; look at the code, it typically logs 4 messages
out of every 5 seconds worth of unaligned traps.

> I have been trying to figure a run-away PC, and have been able to trace
> it to a ldq a1,0(t1) where t1=0x12049123c. apparently (the kern tries)
> fixing this coding error, does not work as expected, and (apparently )
> plugs 0(t1) -1 into the pc.

Are you using alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.1 in your kernel? If so, I think there
was a patch to fix this problem a while ago; it involved fixing a bug in the
trap handling introduced by the EM86 patches. Look around in the axp-list
archives, see if you can find it.

> 1) has unaligned error messages been turned off on the axp ( kern 2.0.30)
> I occasionally did get them - but now I haven't seen them lately

No, I don't think they've been turned off, they just don't all get logged
(see above; code is in arch/alpha/kernels/traps.c:do_entUnaUser()).
