Roman Martonak:
>I would like to move it to the external one. My question is whether it is
>enough just to partition the new disk and copy the whole linux filesystem
>onto it, or whether the symbol /dev/sda of the internal disk is coded somewhere

I don't see any problems, I do this all the time on Intel boxes. A 'cp -axv' excluding
things like /proc and amd-mounted filesystems works for me. Absolutely easiest way
to set up a new Linux. The only trouble is that after the cp, I have to mount the new drive as
primary master, boot from floppy with the new drive as root and then running LILO,
but of course this procedure is a little bit different on Alphas and SCSI...

By the way, running a big 'cp' like that on my Alpha is a sure way to kill the system,
I have to run the cp on another system with the Alpha NFS-mounted. Something is sure messed
up in NFS or even more likely the IDE-driver on a LX.

We had a small discussion on the CMD chipset (actually more like questions without answers)
before, and I (or collegues) have found that the CMD chipset isn't even recognized by the
drivers, even though it should be. The IDE-driver works anyway as it defaults to standard ATA
interface, but that means that nice (essential) things like DMA cannot be configured.
Don't know if this has something to do with the fact that the clock goes a couple of
times faster when the drive settings are altered with 'hdparm', but perhaps not
at all unlikely. Unfortunately I don't have time for some invigorating Linux hacking
right now, so if someone would like to delve in to this problem, please do.
The IDE speed on the LX is very disappointing, a couple of times slower than
Intel boxes... Depressing... Of course this had nothing to do with copying of Linux
drives, just thought I should mention it anyway.
