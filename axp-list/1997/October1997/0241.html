On Wed, 15 Oct 1997, Atif Ahmad Khan wrote:

> I just installed Linux RedHat 4.2 on my newly acquired UDB (Alpha).
> Its got the 166MHz processor in it. I installed 32MB of memory in it
> and a 2GB Fujitsu SCSI 5400RPM external hard drive.
>
> This machine is, to put it humbly, slow as hell. I was hoping to replace
> a Pentium 75 (Linux) machine with it. But the Pentium machine with less
> memory and a slow 3600RPM IDE drive is screaming fast compared to this Alpha.
> I am using the stock kernel that came with RedHat because I can't get anything
> else to compile right for me.

To add my two cents to this disscussion... I have a UDB 166Mhz
running Linux/Alpha and while it is not screaming fast, it is of very
decent speed. It can run database queries (Mini-SQL) on very large
database (>5000 rows) faster than my P100 or my P133 (laptop). You mention
you only have 32mb or RAM... I think there might be your problem. Over the
past couple of months I have seen lots of complaints on the speed of the
UDB, and every time, guess what? They all had 32mb or less of RAM. Mine on
the other hand has 64mb of RAM and just moves along great. My theory is
that because RISC binaries are larger, more RAM is needed to run these
program than on the Intel and such CISC platforms. So while 32mb (or less)
may allow your Intel box to run just fine, the same amount of ram hurts
the performace of your Alpha. This is of course just my view of things,
but I am quite happy with the performace of my Alpha, especially on I/O
intensive applications, such as databases. Yes, it is not a P Pro 200 or
what all, but still a respectable machine.
As for the XF86 configuration, I don't use my UDB for X any more,
but I will see if I can dig up my old configuration for it and email it to
you.
Hope this helps!
