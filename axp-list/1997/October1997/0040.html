I guess, just guess, the alias information is stored in your machine or some
machine in your local network. (they act like "DNS"). If someone from outside try
to use that alias, they first need access into your local network looking for your
alias's "DNS". Usually, your local network's private DNS it is not transparent to
the public. In the case that
your local network's gateway has that information, the outsider may get the correct
IP of the alias.
