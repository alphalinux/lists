There is a difference between constructing chips and designing them.

And Digital Semiconductors is *not* the only company that has been fabricating
Alpha chips.

More importantly, it *appears* that DS was not able to keep the foundries busy
constructing chips. It very well may be better to get the better part of a
billion dollars for something that isn't as obviously a valuable asset as some
people think.

It may very well be a better deal for Digital *as well as its customers* to
have Intel as yet another chip source.

Now, if only we could actually see some of these purported $300
StrongARM-based NC boxes that purportedly are running Linux...
