True, farming out fabs is good for consumers, especially when there is
competition, such as Intel/AMD/Cyrix, or all the companies making
embedded MIPS variants.

However, in DEC/Intel case, the assurances of unfettered Alpha
development sound hollow: why would Intel choose to tolerate the
_only_ serious challenger to their future IA-32/IA-64 products? With
the present deal, Intel will have DEC under their heel: "Sorry, our
Hudson facility is sooo busy, we can only make 15 of your 21364 wafers
this month".

I am afraid Palmer made a decision to give up on Alpha.
