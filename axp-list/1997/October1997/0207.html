> Does anyone have staroffice running on Linux/AXP? My requirements
> are for a general purpose word processor/spreadsheet or office suite
> for under $100 that runs relatively well. I've tried LyX and other
> similar WYSIWYG TeX programs, but they are sufficiently strange that
> my users have problems. I'd appreciate any suggestions; I'm
> willing to try Applixware with em86, but I doubt that this will be
> stable (is anyone running this well?), so I'd prefer something that
> compiles natively.

The "official" release of Star Office is targetted at OpenLinux,
which is an x86-only platform. And according to all documentation
I can find at Star Division, StarOffice is indicated to require
OpenLinux.

It's probable that it'll run on other x86 Linuxes (although I
haven't had success on my machine running Red Hat...).

But I'm not aware that Star Division has created an Alpha version
of StarOffice.

You may be able to run StarOffice atop em86; that should be no
less stable than Applix atop em86...
