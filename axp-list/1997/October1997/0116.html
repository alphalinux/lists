Heikki Virkkunen <tesno@ellu.vaala.fi> said...

> What kind of problems did you have? I had 24M of the original ECC memory,
> and i bought 2x8M 36bit 70ns mem more, and that was when mem errors becan
> to occur. First i had about 2 months ECC correctable cache errors, and
> finally, the machine jammed. I removed those 8M chips, and the machine
> booted normally. But when i gave those chips to my friend who send them to
> manufacturer and they came back with message that said that there was no
> errors on chips, i didnt know what to do. Those 2 SIMM slots are broke or
> then there's something wrong wiht compability :) Please, let me know if
> there's something i should do...

I had purchased a pair of 4x36 SIMMS about 5 or 6 months ago. I had
nothing but problems with them. I'd get correctable and uncorrectable
memory errors all the time. Since I didn't have a lot of time to mess
with it over the summer, I just left it like it was for the time being.

Recently I bought 2 new 4X36 60ns. I replaced the error prone SIMMs and
haven't had a problem since.

The cool thing is that I took the error prone ones out and put them in my
Pentium Pro 200Mhz machine. I was very skeptical about this, but I
haven't had a single problem with the RAM yet. It seems amazing to me,
but the AXP must just really beat on memory a lot harder than these Intel
boxes.

I hope this helps. The memory probablly isn't bad, just not GREAT like
you need it to be.

I searched for a long time for an answer to the same question and never
found an answer, so I finally just shelled out the money for new RAM. I
think that's what you'll have to do, unless you can return the old stuff
still.

Good luck,
