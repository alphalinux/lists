> MY QUESTION is does anyone have any "real" experince that says that
> these machines are reliable, and what they can handle as far as users?
> (preferably running pine or similar). I'm assuming that since I can most
> likely fit 50 users before I even hit swap, the network may be what slows
> me down. Also with 500MHz! yikes!

PC164's with 128MB of RAM can be ***EXTREMELY*** flakey. We had ours
running smoothly for about three months, after about three months of
constant work. After upgrading to redhat 4.2 the machine became so
unstable that we had to move to an intel box.

These machines are great, fast, handle lots of users, etc. but have a huge
learning curve in terms of adjusting to the alphaisms, getting them
stable, etc. You might have great luck, or you might not. I would
definitely suggest that if you buy this machine today, *do not* expect to
be using it in any sort of production environment for at least three
months.

Also, if you do go ahead, DCG is a great place to buy from, they are very
knowledgable and usually have the latest patches.
