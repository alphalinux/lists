> Is there any way to stop a process from consuming the
> whole of remaining swap

Type, in bash, 'ulimit -a' and 'help ulimit'.

> has gone and there is nowhere to swapout some user-mem
> in order to swap-in the system process. The machine then
> hangs.

Yes, when your memory is gone, then it is gone - real or virtual -
and your computer cannot do much about that. If you really need
more swap space you may add more swap partitions; on a new disk,
for example. A default is eight swap partition total, but this
can be simply raised by editing source and recompiling your kernel.
