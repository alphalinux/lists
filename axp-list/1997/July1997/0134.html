This is about the 101st time this has been rehashed on this list (thank
god comp.os.linux.alpha will be up soon!), but the common view is that the
UDB/AXPpci33 performance is equal to a lower-end pentium. For various
applications, the 21066-based systems can perform as well as a pentium 133
or as poorly as a pentium 75. Adding cache certainly makes a huge
difference. But even on my uncached AXPpci33 at 166mhz with 32megs of
RAM, the box is faster than my pentium 60, for what it's worth.

Ryan Rafferty
