At 05:16 PM 7/26/97 -0700, you wrote:
>> Is there kernel support for the Adaptec 2940UW SCSI controller in
>> Redhat 4.2 Alpha?
>
>is there support for any adaptec controllers under alpha?

Oh, yes, the 1740 (EISA) works nicely on JENSEN and SABLE, and the 2940UW
seems to be very popular for the majority of Alpha platforms supported.

>i recently bought a 1522b to hook up to my pc64-based system,
>thinking "it's an adaptec controller, of course it'll work"
>but haven't had much luck with it yet. (it's on the NT/Alpha
>supported controllers list that microsoft puts out, but i haven't
>had much luck installing NT with it either.)

AFAIK, this should be usable under NT, you may have to hunt for a driver.

Under LINUX, however, you're (currently) out of luck. I had one of those
1520 cards sitting around, and decided to look into porting the driver.
It's a TRUE ISA card, as far as I can tell, and can only generate addresses
in the range 0-16Mb, which, with the way the Alphas allow access to host
memory from the bus, is a *very* bad fit. Could be done, would require
kernel changes, not just driver ones, didn't seem worth it...

Use it for NT, if you can; if you need cheap, PC64 (CABRIO) supports IDE.
