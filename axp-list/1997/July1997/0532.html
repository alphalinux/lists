Hi,

I'm going to be working on a quantum chemical package, written in F77
wrapped in C++. I can't use g77 because it doesn't support Cray-style
POINTERs, and also the performance is important.

One option I have is Microway's NDP Fortran. The problem there is that
their C compiler won't do C++, and apparently NDP-compiled modules may not
link easily with GNU-compiled ones.

I understand that some development kernels will run Digital Unix binaries,
but that that's illegal unless you have a license. Can I buy just enough
of Digital Unix to use their compilers under Linux? If so, would this be
very expensive?

Alternatively, is there any chance of getting Digital to port their
compiler tools to Linux?

Cheers,

Nikhil.
