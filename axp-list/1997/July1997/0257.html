On Wed, Jul 09, 1997 at 02:35:13PM +0800, Lo Sheng wrote:

> I understand Miata comes with ESS sound card built-in. However, digging
> through all the documentations that come with the machine, I am unable to
> find the settings for IRQ, DMA, etc. Trying to reseach to local DEC
> support is harder than trying to find life on Mars.

I never tried to reach DEC support, so I can't comment on this :-)

However, when you install ESS support for Windows NT (using the supplied
"Per Wkst Dr NTA RX23 1/3 ESS Audio Driver" disk), IRQ and DMA settings
are displayed. The install instructions in the Miata manual say "accept
the defaults", and this works.

For me, the following settings are working under Linux:

#define CONFIG_SB
#define SBC_BASE 0x220
#define SBC_IRQ 5
#define SBC_DMA 1
#define SB_DMA2 5
#define SB_MPU_BASE 0x330

If this doesn't work for you, you will have to deal with WinNT to find
out.
