At 07:20 hrs. 07-03-97 +0300, you wrote:
>Hello,
>
>I have the above combination - PC164, 128Mb of memory, NCR825 based scsi
>controller plus Trident vga board & 3com ethernet, and I'm running
>Redhat 4.2 with 2.1.23 kernel in it.
>The problem I'm having is that occasionally the machine just locks
>up. I'd guess that it might have something to do with the upper part of
the memory
>& the scsi controller.(?)
>I'm now considering to try different scsi board - like Adaptec2940.
>So, any opinions would that help? (Or should I play with the kernel instead?)
>I'd hate to lose memory & wide bus...
I doubt it is the SCSI card causing the problem. Bus or drivers maybe.
Usual stuff applies: use good active termination, short cable length,
disconnect temporarily unneccesary devices ( CD, Tape, 2nd drive, etc.) and
test some more. Adaptec cards work, but they are rarely a solution.
Old NCR 53c7,8xx drivers are also often a problem. The newer NCR53c8xx
driver is better.
