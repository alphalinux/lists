You are by no means annoying me. Feel free to ask questions to me at any
time. My UDB has been a very nice usable machine. I am a phd student at Ga
Tech and I do research in high perforance computing. My UDB has 40MB of RAM
in it and 1 gig external SCSI hard drive. I use the machine to do my phd
research code development. Its performance is not incredibly stellar. About
the sme speed as a 100Mhz Pentium 100 but I feel that I can get a
reasonable amount of work done on it. I have access to much faster machines
but for coding, debugging, and such its a very reasonable platform. The
more ram you have the better since Linux does really good caching with the
extra ram that you have. I noticed that when i went from 16MB from 40 that
my speed improved dramatically.

> What im wondering most is - how difficult is it to set up, how does
> the alpha 166 processor seem to rate against a pentium , and just how
> good is the linux port? I have a copy of redhat alpha 4.2 .

Linux Redhat 4.2 on the UDB is incredible. I did an install with absolutely
no problems (although I did it over the network). I set up X windows with
no trouble as well and had over half of my 1 gig disk still available once
I installed *everything* that was on the cd-rom. The X windows is pretty
swift but won't do more than 8bpp withouth sticking another video card in
the box.

All in all, I'd say its worth investing in it. The only thing that it uses
that your PC can't use is the parity memory.
