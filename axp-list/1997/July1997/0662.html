Alvin Starr wrote:
On Tue, 29 Jul 1997, David Mosberger-Tang wrote:
> I for one am no longer
> subscribing to axp-list and would much prefer receiving such important
> information on comp.os.linux.alpha.

After 10 years of using news and watching it be reduced to a site for
smut and ad's for more smut. To the point where now a full news feed is
4GB a day. I was more then just a little overjoyed with the adevent of
mail-lists as a way of supporting communities of like interests.

Oh, no, not another mailing list vs. news discussion :^)

However, because it bears on Linux/Alpha issues, I will comment
briefly. Once one has a reasonably managed feed, and a decent news
reader (I use Emacs/Gnus), news are much less distracting:

- I almost never see newsgroup spam---my KILL files take care of that;
I get more spam mail than spam news

- I read news when I want, not, like e-mail, when it is sent

- news are automatically cathegorized, by those who know best (?),
i.e. authors; no need for artificial intelligence and procmail rules

- my newsreader threads the messages (some e-mail progs do that, too)

I certainly don't propose to close axp-list, but I prefer to read it
via news. Ah, I almost forgot mention that deja-gnu indexes all news
(on a kick-ass Linux server cluster, no less), while axp-mail list
indexing at RedHat is so-so.
