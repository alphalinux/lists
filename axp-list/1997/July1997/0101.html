>>> Ryan said:
>
> Your disappointment with the TGA's performance surprises me, since most
> people I've spoken to say that the TGA is extremely peppy, though limited
> to 8bpp. Are you sure something isn't configured incorrectly?

He's using the latest XFree86 XF86_TGA, which has (little or) no
acceleration.

Using the older but much quicker (and perhaps buggier) Xtga that's available
on gatekeeper.dec.com:pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/X11, might help, but most of his
disappointment is in regards to no multiple Modelines and no virtual screens.

The TGA is *not* a VGA; its ancestors were the high-end framebuffers of a few
years ago on UNIX workstations, and its design reflects that: it absolutely
*screams* doing many memory-intensive operations, but its video capabilities
remain rooted in the older display technology, ie fixed frequency monitors.
