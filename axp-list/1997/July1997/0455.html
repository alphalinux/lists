I've no useful experience with Linux/Alpha as an NFS server, but as an
NFS client (RH4.1) to DEC UNIX servers, with rsize=wsize=8192 and using
an FDDI link I get:

Sustained NFS-read rates of 3Mb/sec and sustained NFS-write rates
of 0.1 Mb/sec. Compared to 3.5Mb/sec and 0.5 Mb/sec between a
DU client and DU server. Using rcp, the achievable read/write rates
are about 8mb/sec and 6Mb/sec respectively. rsize=wsize=8192 is
*essential* here; 1024 is completely useless. Would that it
could be raised to 32K or more :-)

The systems seem stable in the Linux-client/DU-server mode
(I have 8 PC164 clients to 2 DU servers) although I have had
one curious unreproduceable problem during Linux shutdown
at the point where the system tries to dismount remote-mounted
disks, at which point the client somehow jammed the entire network
(which promptly recovered as soon as we unplugged it).
It seems you cant beat an attached disk, and I now have scratch
disks on each client so that users can stage heavy I/O files
to these at the start and end of a job using rcp.
