On Tue, 29 Jul 1997, Paul N. wrote:

> swap should have no "intrinsic" limits [...]

According to the MKSWAP(8) manpage, there are minimum and maximum sizes
for a swap area - apparently because the swap area bitmap and a
signature are held in a single page. The manpage has formulae for
calculating the values of these, assuming you know the page-size of the
mac;hine in question: for a Pentium, with 4Mb pages, the maximum is about
127Mb.

I don't know if there's any limit on the number of swap areas, though -
perhaps the answer is to have more than one swap partition.
