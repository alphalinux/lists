hello;

this may not be the answer you are looking for but here is what i
use for backups.

i gave up on dat drives, i have three dead dat drives in a cabinet in my office.
even cleaning them regularly did not seem to help extend their life.

i purchased a cd-rom burner, downloaded xcdroast, and wrote several shell
scripts. all files are copied over to an nfs-mounted harddrive. when the
drive is nearly full, it is unmounted xcdroast makes an iso-9660 image of
the harddrive and burners a cd.

epoch backups are manual, since i am copying entire harddrives or portions
thereof.

print out a cd-label, put it on the cd, and file away.

advantage of this is that any cd-player can read the cd. this is
not true of dat drives. i could nver make a tape on the archive unit
and read it on the hp unit, but i could make a tape on hp unit which
i could read on the archive. go figure.

other benefits are:

1. nearly everyone has a cd-player, not everyone has a dat drive.
2. in a pinch i can access the files using a macintosh or god forbid
a microsloth virii.
3. long term storage far greater with the cd than a dat.
4. i can make a copy of the cd using a cd-player and the cd-burner,
to make a dat tape copy i either need two dat drives, or enough harddrive
space to read the dat tape to so that i cat write it to a different tape.
5. i can listen to audio cd's on the cd-burner when not burning backup cd's ;-)
6. i can get to a file without having to read the first 4gb of tape. ;-)
we have all been there right, you are looking for a file, and read in the
first tape of a multiple volume backup and discover the file is one the last
tape. i find changing dat cartridges boring. ;-)
7. cd-rom burner is cheaper than dat drives. hp cd burner internal are around
$350.00 street price in the chicago metro area.
8. the cd's that get burned incorrectly are great for other uses.
hint: please do not let your children see you doing this. if you have a SO
do not let them see you doing this either. ;-)
put the cd in a microwave and set the time for 10 seconds, powerlevel about
80 percent, turn out the room lights and hit start/cook/whatever and watch
the light show. you can also use the aol cd's that you receive in the mail
for this also. ;-)
warning do not set the time for more than 10 seconds, the smell of burning
polycarbonate is really aweful. ;-)
try that with a dat cartridge.

i have been asked to cleanup the files, and programs and post them to
on of the linux sites, but i have been occupied just trying to get the one arm
working again. i can truthfully say that i am short-handed. ;-)
