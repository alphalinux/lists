At 05:44 hrs. 07-17-97 +0100, you wrote:
>
>We have an alpha 21164 and I always thougt it was PC164 so we used to
>compile the kernel with PC164 selected.
>
>The question apears when I looked at /proc/cpuinfo and I saw it says the
>system was an EB164, which is an other option int the kernel configuration
>scripts.
>
>Any solution for this?
>
The PC164 replies EB164 in this, even though it ISN'T one.
Look at the board. If it's a smallish ATX board it's a PC164.
If it's a huge square board with 2 SIMM like cache modules at the top right
corner it's an EB164.
OK?
