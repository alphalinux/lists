Well, there are liars, damn liars, and benchmark writers. There is not clear
agreement as to the rating of the UDB. Some compare it to a P75, others to a
P150. The higher number would come from using it for numerically intense
calculations. Its performance is fairly severely hampered by the fact that it
only has 256K of cache. If it had 1MB of cache (and it simply can't),
performance would be *much* better.

That being said, it's not a bad little machine.

A spare monitor is, of course, usable with another system, so there's no
downside to getting that. If you want to put more disk on it, you will have
to consider putting that in an external case, as the power supply in the UDB
*WILL NOT* support two drives.

I had a copy of one of the cheap-o CD's with Red Hat 4.0, and had no success
installing that. I picked up a copy of 4.1 at a computer show, and found that
it installed fairly cleanly.

There is a web page at DCG Computers that explains in *complete* detail how to
install RH version 3.0.3. Installing 4.2 should be similar enough that the
3.0.3 instructions will at least be useful.

I have a link to the DCG page as well as various other resources at:
<http://www.hex.net/~cbbrowne/alpha.html>. Hopefully it will help answer many
of your questions.
