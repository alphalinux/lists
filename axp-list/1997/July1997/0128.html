On Thu, 3 Jul 1997, Greg Lindahl wrote:

> > I tried both your suggestions. Same problem: Milo can deal with the
> > partition table only until I use mke2fs to create the filesystem. After
> > that occurs, it reports "unknown partition table".
>
> Maybe mke2fs is overwriting your partition table?

I thought that might be it, but a quick before and after comparison
indicates no..

> I am currently trying to install Redhat 4.2 on a new Durango PC164,
> and I'm having fits getting fdisk to generate a valid partition table.
> If I type in non-overlapping numbers in the usual fashion, "v"
> says they overlap. mke2fs runs but silently screws something up,
> and the resulting filesystem can't be mounted.

Ok, that is happening to me when I accept the view of the disk geometry
shown in the bsd label option. Finally, in disgust, I connected the drive
to an X86 linux box and partitioned it there.

When transferred back to the Alpha, it still wasn't recognized by Milo,
but the geometry was completely different. No problems with overlapping.

I've officially given up on convincing Milo to look on sdb for the kernel.
My workaround is to leave a small extended partition at the end of
/dev/sda (after WinNT ends). This is setup as ext2 and will hold only the
kernel images. When they come up from Milo, they _are_ able to see the
second disk - so I'm off and running.

Life is too short to beat my head against the wall on this bug.
Undoubtably it's a subtle Milo problem..

Steve
