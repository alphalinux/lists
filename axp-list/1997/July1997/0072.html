Hello,

DeskStation is not yet shipping Ruffians; however, we are taking orders
www.deskstation.com for more info. <<sales pitch off>>

NT is already ported and running along nicely at 633MHz
(that's the fastest DEC has given us, but we'll go faster).
Except for puking on crossing-cache-line-boundaries (which many graphics
cards cause) the Pyxis chipset makes for insanely fast systems.
2M L3 and SDRAM helps too!

DeskStation has hired someone (me!!) to port Linux to the Ruffian.
Yes, we have our own firmware. Believe me, it's top notch.
The firmware is ARCS, so all we had to do was write an ARCS app
to load MILO and the micropal. no problemo.

Which brings us to the current state of Linux on a Ruffian.
MILO hangs when trying to initialize the interrupts for MILO
(mostly because I haven't made one of those IRQ tables yet).
Don't be discouraged, MILO is actually a significant subset
of the kernel.

Our goal is to have Linux flying by the time the systems ship.
Integrating the Ruffian additions to Linus' source tree
will be a tad longer. Until then, DeskStation will preinstall
Linux on request, and serve up some support RPMS for compatibility.

kernel hacking-ly yours,

!!Dean
ps: Thanks Alex for giving me a proper window to spill the beans,
I've been anxious.
