On Fri, 4 Jul 1997, fb wrote:

> well i was all reved up to get a UDB but i just heard some very
> disappointing news from a friend. according to him the perf is terrible
> (he didnt give me any specs or an idea of what is terrible)
>
> so i am here asking if anyone that has a UDB 166 or 233 would care to
> comment on the performance and suitibility for particular tasks

I have a UDB 166 (read they only have 32-bit memory access) with 32MB of
RAM and the machine runs well. If I run WindowMaker and lots of windows
the swapping goes through the ceiling. It takes me about a hour and 10
minutes to compile the kernel. Someone mentioned to me that should drop
to 40 minutes when I add the extra 32MB of RAM.

My friend uses this same box with 64MB as a Web Server under NT and he
says it's very fast (unlike the comments that it should be a P100 or P90
in speed range).

The 233Mhz's aparently have 64-bit memory access and should be
considerably faster.

I use this box for a Masquerading Firewall and workstation at the same
time and it seems fine. Of course I'm putting in a different video card
for now to get more than 8 bit planes, and in the not to distant future
will buy a much faster Alpha and convert this to a Firewall/Mail/Web
server.

(IMHO)
