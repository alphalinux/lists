Harald Koenig wrote:
> On Jul 3, Dean Hall wrote:
> > Except for puking on crossing-cache-line-boundaries (which many graphics
> ^^^^^^
> > cards cause) the Pyxis chipset makes for insanely fast systems.
> > 2M L3 and SDRAM helps too!
>
> what happens when "puking" ? and how to avoid such accesses or other
> method to avoid the problems ?

It is a limitation of Pyxis that it cannot satisfy page requests which
cross cache line boundaries. Our systems (used to) hang when this
occured.
We have an in-house hardware solution.
(sorry, can't give details)

I should also specify that I meant many top-of-the-line graphics cards
(like the Oxygen), since our customers desire such peripherals.

> could it be that there are similar problems other chipsets too (e.g. AS255)
> which have lockup problems e.g. with S3 card when just trying to
> access the frame buffer (copy to/from video memory with
> only aligned accesses!) ???

Quite possible. However, I am not familiar with that chip
(or many others for that matter).

We did hardware traces of the PCI transactions to find this feature.
Confirming this for other cards would not be a fun week's work.
