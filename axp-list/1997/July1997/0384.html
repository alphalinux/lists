Dear Jay, dear Dan

Thanks again for your efforts helping me with my Linux installation
on a Miata. Finally it works now, but I do not fully understand the
reason why. A particular feature of my system is an Adaptec 2940AU
PCI-SCSI card.

Let me just summarize the problems I had:

* From the beginning Milo would get stuck after some messages from
the aic7xxx driver ("external RAM detected" etc...). In one out
of four or five times there would be an error about an unexpected
machine check from the PYXIS routine. And then it would continue
booting.

* After booting no SCSI could be seen, with no version of the aic7xxx
driver. The kernel would hang for several minutes before continuing
to boot.

* Another problem, I had - although it seemed to me to be unrelated
to the SCSI problem - was, that I could not get X working. I assumed
that I just always had the wrong setup. After fixing the problem with
the link for /etc/alpha_systype, trying to start X would blank the
screen and then hang the system.

When I almost had given up and were thinking of getting another card,
I tried one last thing: I changed the card from PCI slot 1 to slot 2.
Note, that Windows NT works fine with the card in slot 1. So I did
not have big hopes, that this would help. But in fact: surprise,
surprise! Everthing was working afterwards, not only SCSI was seen
and could be accessed, but also the X server was now working properly.
Also MILO is now working properly.

Apparently there are some interferences between the graphics card (a
Matrox Millennium) and the 2940AU which disappeared, when changing the
PCI slot, although they seem not to disturb Windows NT.

I forward this mail also to the axp-list just in case some other
desperate person has similar problems.

Yours,
Lars
