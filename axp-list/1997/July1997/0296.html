How much data was logging?

syslog, by default does a fsync() for every entry of every file. This
will easily drive the load up on a small machine under moderate load.
Our central syslog machines have a '-' prepended to the target filename
in syslog.conf, making use of your system buffers. A lot less load.
Check the manpage...

I must mention the one drawback... you will lose any data that isn't
written to disk before a crash. (obviously) But for remote logging,
etc. that is usually a non-issue. Since you can do it on a per-file
basis, this lessens the problem, as well.

Just an idea.
