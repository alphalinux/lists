Nikhil Nair wrote:
>
> Hi,
>
> I'm going to be working on a quantum chemical package, written in F77
> wrapped in C++. I can't use g77 because it doesn't support Cray-style
> POINTERs, and also the performance is important.
>
> One option I have is Microway's NDP Fortran. The problem there is that
> their C compiler won't do C++, and apparently NDP-compiled modules may not
> link easily with GNU-compiled ones.
>

I have been informed that NAG will release a version of their f90
compiler for alpha. This compiler uses the gcc backend (I think it's
actually something like f2c) so this would permit you to link with
gcc code. But we don't know about performance on the alpha yet ...

>
> Alternatively, is there any chance of getting Digital to port their
> compiler tools to Linux?
>
> Cheers,
>
> Nikhil.
>

The answer of Digital to my request about this point was a clear NO.
Think about it: Digital ports it's dxml (the library) and the compilers
to Linux - who would use DU ?
