On 17 Jul, Terry L. Ridder wrote:
> i gave up on dat drives, i have three dead dat drives in a cabinet in my office.
> even cleaning them regularly did not seem to help extend their life.

I made the same experiences - when they work, they are great. Low media
cost, fast access, and moderately safe data storage. However, it seems
you should never depend on a single tape - read problems happen from
time to time.

The problem is that the drives tend to fail after 1-2 years (the one I
had here made 3-4 years, but this seems to be non-typical). The
problems range from deadjusted tracking to defects in the electronics.

> 3. long term storage far greater with the cd than a dat.

As long as the CDs are stored under the right conditions.

> 6. i can get to a file without having to read the first 4gb of tape. ;-)

Um - one of the advantages of DAT is that you can get to that file in
30 seconds max, and read it right away. I have a Tandberg 1.2GB QIC
drive, which is great for backups (and those beasts tend to be very
stable), but they don't have this quick access.
