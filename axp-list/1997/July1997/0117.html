In article <33BC8F6B.8D4339A4@chibacity.com> you write:
>it is my understanding that when a 3.5" hard drive is used in a UDB the
>PCI slot is inaccessable?
>
>can anyone shed some light on this?

This is perfectly true. I could see the inside of a UDB with an internal
3.5" hard drive, and the hard drive definetely takes the place of the PCI
slot. There is no room left.

Although...

I heard some rumors about the possibility of getting rid of the floppy drive
and the 2.5" hard drive, which may free enough room for a hard drive AND a
PCI card... but these are rumors. Anyway, keeping the floppy drive is a good
idea, when one wants to boot its UDB and the hard drive is crashed/empty.
