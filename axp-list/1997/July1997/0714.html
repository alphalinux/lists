I have been following this list for a while, and reading the newsgroups
(comp.os.linux.* and linux.*), and checking out WWW pages, but I have 2
questions: (question 2 below may be easier than #1..)

First: We are looking at moving up from a 486/100 w/ 32MB RAM,
two 1 GIG IDE drives, and Linux 1.2.13 .
This system will handle 25 logged in users running pine (yes I know, a
memory hog). This machine is our EMail server for the entire college
( >700 students, couple hundred faculty/staff accounts) and WWW server
(average of MAYBE 2-3 hits a minute.) We are looking to upgrade, and DEC
ALpha seems an afforable, powerful way to go. We are looking at/can
afford a DCG Computers Inc. DEC Alpha PC164 500 MHz 128MB/RAM, two-2GIG UW
SCSI-3 drives(8ms/7200RPM), CDRom, 4GIG DDS SCSI tape, and 10/100 EtherCard,
pre-installed with 64-bit RedHat Linux (i assume 4.2).

MY QUESTION is does anyone have any "real" experince that says that
these machines are reliable, and what they can handle as far as users?
(preferably running pine or similar). I'm assuming that since I can most
likely fit 50 users before I even hit swap, the network may be what slows
me down. Also with 500MHz! yikes!

-------- Question #2
My EASSIER second question is about /etc/passwd on the Alpha versus the
Intel 486, can I just copy the /etc/passwd file from one machine to the
other (assuming Shells and Home dirs are copied)? I'm mostly worried
about the de-crypting of passwords.

Thank you in advance of your help!
