> I have a Redhat4.2 / Kernel 2.0.30 Noritake system (with some patches
> courtesy of Jay++, thanks muchly) and it will run along quite happily for
> about three days, and then something dies in teh scsi driver and it
> refuses to write to any of the drives. This is not the bug. (although I
> welcome donated suggestions on this one.. it is a NCR810, and we are using

This may sound silly and obvious, but....
I have a UDB at home that occasionally does this. After tearing out some
hair over it, I discovered that the scsi cable would just BARELY come
disconnected due to the way the machine was positioned on my desktop.
Since then, I've moved it to a different location, made sure the cables
have lots of slack, and bingo, no more sudden scsi deaths.
