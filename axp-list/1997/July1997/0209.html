I've been contemplating whether or not to put this out, and I think
axp-linux people should at least be aware.

Three of the things I've been excited about recently are (a) the Alpha
chip (b) Linux and (c) Java. Needless to say I'd been following the
Java porting progress reports at www.blackdown.org for regularly. For a
long time it sounded like the axp port was just behind the Intel port
and that they would soon be released. Then mention of axp disappeared.

I sent a polite note to the person working on the port (I can't find his
name now anywhere) asking what had happened, and he said something to
the effect that Digital had taken back the Alpha computer he/they'd been
working on, so without a computer I shouldn't hold my breath waiting for
him to do the Alpha port. (I'm paraphrasing here.)

Anyway, I don't know what politics are involved here, and it almost
seemed inappropriate to retell this to everyone, but maybe we should all
be aware that there is absolutely no progress being made on the
axp-linux Java port right now, and not because of lack of willingness.
