No, no, no! It had nothing to do with using a pickle as a battery. It
was using a pickle as a light source. See Digital Western Research Laboratory
Technical Note TN-31: Characterization of Organic Illumination Systems.

http://www.research.digital.com/wrl/techreports/abstracts/TN-13.html

"Maurice W. Hilarius" <maurice@ellpspace.math.ualberta.ca> wrote:
> DEC acronyms always mean _something_.

Of course, AXP and EV are *not* acronyms. Acronyms are pronounced as words,
like radar or snafu. I can't pronounce axp very well, and noone would know
what I was talking about if I said "ev".

The AXP thing was beaten to death on UseNet back when the 21064 was introduced.
The official story was that AXP was added by marketing because they didn't
think they could trademark just the word "Alpha", and that AXP didn't stand
for anything. Which doesn't necessarily meant that it wasn't "Almost eXactly
Prism" internally.
