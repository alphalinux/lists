After searching through this list's archive, I know that it's possible to
use a DAT tape drive with Red Hat Linux on the Alpha. I've had no luck
with the drive I have but I can't say for sure if it's the drive, SCSI
termination or what.

I'm to the point where I am willing to buy a new DAT drive but I want
something that works, reliably. If anyone out there is successfully using
a DAT drive with version 2.0.27 of the kernel I'd very much appreciate it
if you could share with me...

o The make and model of the drive.

o How you compiled your kernel (which SCSI driver and the options used
for that driver). Or, you could e-mail me your .config file and I'll
take it from there.

o Commands other than "mt -f /dev/nst0 setblk 0" you needed to issue for
the drive to work.

Also, are you using tar, dump or some other software to do backups/restores?

Thanks.

Chuck
