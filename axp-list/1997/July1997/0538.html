I have noticed that many new UDB/Multia owners have been asking questions
concerning where they can find 2.5" scsi hard drives to be used internall
on their machines. I was also in this category when I first acquired my UDB
but wasn't able to find any information that was satisfactory. I settled
for an external scsi drive and that has been working flawlessly since.

However, I never gave up on it and recently started looking again. I had
some questions that I wanted to ask to the list populace in the hopes that
I can get more concrete answers this time around. To be quite frank, i'm
not interested in responses that say "why do you want to use an internal
drive?". One of the reasons I bought the UDB was its extremely small and
compact size. :-)

1) I know that the machine has room for a 3.5" hard drive in it but the
connector on the motherboard is for a 2.5" scsi drive. Are there converters
cables/devices that can be purchased to do this? If so then from who and
how much?

2) Many folks have said that 3.5" drives produce too much heat for the UDB
and will run the risk of overheating your machine. I wanted to know what
the heat differences were on average between 2.5" drives and 3.5" drives.
Also, are the speed differences that much different between 2.5" and 3.5"
drives? Also, does anyone have an actual 3.5" scsi hard drive in their UDB?

3) Searching www.dejanews.com with "scsi AND 2.5 AND drive" gave several
results and I have found some folks selling apple drives that are actually
IDE drives with a scsi converter on them. They report to work fine in apple
powerbooks but have some trouble if you try to partition them. I was
wondering if anyone had used one of these (in a UDB or not)? Also, are
these converters available available for free. I know that there are
external cases that turn scsi hard drives into IDE backpack type hard drives
so going the other way seems reasonable as well.

4) I know that the UDB has a PCI slot. I as wondering if it was possible to
buy a pci EIDE controller card and drive a 2.5" eide drive off of that. It
might require an adapter cable from the card to the drive but that would
allow me to access the much cheaper 2.5" EIDE hard drives and get decent
performance as opposed to using the old IDE controller that is on the UDB
motherboard.

Any answers to these questions would be greatly appreciated.
