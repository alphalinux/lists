OK, so you've got an Aspen Alpine, 275MHz, 2MB of cache. Same chipset as a
DEC PC64+ or a Cabriolet.
It has onboard NCR53C810 SCSI. It doesn't use Flash ROM for firmware,
unlike the DEC variant. You should probably get newer firmware PROMs.
Current version is 4.5-5 for SRM, and something else for ARC ( I forget).
Contact Kyle at Aspen Tech support at:
kyleg@aspsys.com to arrange to get new PROM chip ( it's socketed).
Now, you have a pretty decent system.
Make sure your root disk and CDROM are on the onboard NCR SCSI for install.
The QLogic is probably on a PCI card delivering wide SCSI. It can work too,
but is more touchy, driver-wise. I don't think you want to play with any
jumpers. These boards work fine in stock condition. I suspect the QLogic is
confusing things. Lose it for now, until you have the machine up, then
worry later abouting adding it back in if you believe you actually need the
second channel.

Hope this helps.
