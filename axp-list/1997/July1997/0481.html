In article <1.5.4.16.19970719174931.08275d7a@clintondale.com> you write:
>Although
>SCSI is traditionally faster than IDE, does this hold with 2.5" drives?
>From the specs both drives have similar av. seek times (about 13ms).

The IDE limitation is due to the controller architecture, not really
the disk... although IDE disks often have a cheaper electronic with less
cache. I guess that on a 2.5", this is not really true, and that SCSI
and IDE drives have almost the same possibilities; but the IDE ones are sold
in higher numbers, which explains their low price.

Due to the relatively slow mechanic of 2.5" drives, I think you may not
notice the difference under normal working conditions, such as those you
state below:

>The machine is going to be used as a personal workstation, doing mainly C,
>Latex, Postscript, telnet, Netscape type stuff. No heavy number crunching
>or disk IO.

Nevertheless, I think a few more megabytes of memory would significantly
reduce the disk IO. Especially when running Netscape. Remember that alpha
binaries are significantly bigger than x86 or sparc ones.

>So what do you think? Is the SCSI drive going to be significantly faster
>than the IDE drive for my requirements? Will I fit RedHat 4.1 comfortably
>into 800Mb with room for 100Mb worth of data files etc? If I need to I may
>invest in an external Zip drive for archiving work on.

I installed a RedHat 4.1 (btw, RedHat 4.2 is out) on my 500Mb drive, so I
guess you may have a fairly complete installation on a 800Mb drive, with
100Mb free. You may want to avoid the Zip solution, as a Zip is a rather
slow disk.

To sum up: buy a 1 Gb IDE drive instead of a 2 Gb (or a 800 Mb SCSI), and
buy also two 8 Mb 36 bits SIMM modules (I assume you have the 233 MHz UDB
version, the one that comes with two 12 Mb SIMMs and two free slots).
Consider also that this station has not been designed to be moved all
around, so you may shorten the harddrive (or floppy drive) life time
by crossing the Atlantic two often with it.
