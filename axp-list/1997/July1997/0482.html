>Due to the relatively slow mechanic of 2.5" drives, I think you may not
>notice the difference under normal working conditions, such as those you
>state below:

That is what I was thinking.

>Nevertheless, I think a few more megabytes of memory would significantly
>reduce the disk IO. Especially when running Netscape. Remember that alpha
>binaries are significantly bigger than x86 or sparc ones.

I do not have any memory yet in this machine, so I am going to buy 2 x 32Mb
ECC SIMMS to go in it. That should give me ample memory.

>I installed a RedHat 4.1 (btw, RedHat 4.2 is out) on my 500Mb drive, so I
>guess you may have a fairly complete installation on a 800Mb drive, with
>100Mb free. You may want to avoid the Zip solution, as a Zip is a rather
>slow disk.

I know zip's are slow, but I would only be using it as secondary storage,
ie. for archiving stuff, and is much cheaper than a SCSI tape and nicer in
the fact I can choose to either put a filesystem on it or tar to it raw.

>To sum up: buy a 1 Gb IDE drive instead of a 2 Gb (or a 800 Mb SCSI), and
>buy also two 8 Mb 36 bits SIMM modules (I assume you have the 233 MHz UDB
>version, the one that comes with two 12 Mb SIMMs and two free slots).

I would buy a 2Gb disk if I were to go for IDE, just because it would give
me a bit more room, and 2Gb IDE drives are fairly cheap now.

>Consider also that this station has not been designed to be moved all
>around, so you may shorten the harddrive (or floppy drive) life time
>by crossing the Atlantic two often with it.

I won't be travelling around with it too much, maybe just twice or three
times a year, but it would be hand luggage on flights anyway.

A question about cables... I know the IDE drives use an all in one power and
data cable that is standard for laptops... do laptop drives normally come
with this cable or not? Also what sort of cabling does a 2.5" SCSI drive use?
