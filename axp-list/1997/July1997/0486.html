> So what do you think? Is the SCSI drive going to be significantly faster
> than the IDE drive for my requirements? Will I fit RedHat 4.1 comfortably
> into 800Mb with room for 100Mb worth of data files etc? If I need to I may
> invest in an external Zip drive for archiving work on.

For the 166Mhz UDB, the IDE interface is *NOT* enhanced and therefore
severely lacks in performance. I have heard reports that copying a large
file (> 10MB) can take as much 90% of the cpu. You can check the axp-list
archives for the details.

I am currently running a 166Mhz UDB with an external 1gig scsi hard drive
and an external scsi zip. They both work like a charm and I can get about
30Mbs/minute on the zip. Its great for archiving data since i can just
mount the partition and copy data to it. Much better than having to read
off a streaming device. OF course it only holds 100MB but since you are
only planning on having a 100MB user partition then its the perfect size :-)

A completel installation of RedHat 4.2 will *NOT* fit comfortabley on a
500MB drive unless you cut out some of the software. The redhat
distribution comes with *TONS* of stuff and it can easily take up 400MB.
And trust me. If you add any other softawre like XEmacs and/or StarOffice
(running under em86 of course) then you are going to quickly go over the
400MB limit. You can amke it though but you are going to have to leave out
software.

So its a hard decision. If you can afford the money, go for scsi for
performance reasons. If you can't afford the scsi then go with the IDE but
expect performance to suck for any large I/O type jobs.
