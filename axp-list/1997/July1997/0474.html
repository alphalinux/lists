On Fri, 18 Jul 1997, Michael Champigny wrote:

> Yes, the axp-diffs, when applied to pristine sources, do fix the shared
> memory bug. I still don't understand why Red Hat doesn't include this
> patch in their kernel RPM. I've mailed them, but as is typical, no
> reply. I guess they figure a broken XFree86 is acceptable to users.

The answer I've gotten is that Erik wanted to keep the header files more
in line with what Linus "intended." The axp-diffs break many of the
headers up into <header>.h and <header>bits.h. I'm assuming this is only
for the 2.0.x kernels because the new header format is carried into the
2.1.x kernels that do not need axp-diffs.
