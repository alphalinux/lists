I suspect that I will purchase an Alpha system based on DEC's Alpa PC 164
motherboard and expect to have an internal Ultra Wide SCSI disk and an
internal CD ROM.

I assume that the SCSI controller will have internal connectors for both
wide and narrow SCSI devices. If I were to use both of those connectors
then I would be precluded from attaching any external SCSI devices. If I
were to use an ATAPI internal CD ROM instead of a SCSI CD ROM then I would
be able to use the external SCSI connector (assuming no other internal
narrow SCSI devices).

Could someone confirm to me that the PC 164 board has an onboard connector
that could be used in a Linux system to provide CD ROM access with a
generic ATAPI CD ROM?

I'd like to avoid the need to purchase two SCSI controllers.
