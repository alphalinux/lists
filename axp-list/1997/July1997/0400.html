My guess is that you are probably right to a degree.
DEC acronyms always mean _something_.

For example, the DEC part number for an EB164 (EBP40-xx) The EBP in these,
and most Digital Semiconductor and TOEM Division source boards stands for
"Evaluation Board Product".
Semi probably only wanted to sell chipsets, but were forced to do "demo" or
evaluation boards. They hoped other companies would do the actual boards
themselves. DEC Semi still posts all the board mask info and stuff like
that. It sometimes worked, but in many cases the other manufacturers looked
at the cost of setting up for their own production and decided there was
little saving if any , so keep buying whole boards from Semi.
