Hi,

we ARE using the MD-driver with a RAID0-Stripeset of 3 Fujitsu
M2949S-512 (8682 MB each) for our anonymous FTP-filearea. The disks
are connected to a Multia (UDB 166MHz), actively terminated SCSI.
The Kernelversion is 2.0.27 (RH 4.0), 96 MB Memory. We also had some
errors like Kernel-Oopes and "OS-specific PAL bugcheck".
According to the discussion concering the problems with MEM > 64MB
on PC 164 I gave it a try and sat "set MEMORY-SIZE 95" before booting
the kernel, and since then noticed no more kernel memory faults or panics.
I don't know if that helps, but without reducing the Memorysize by 1 MB
I did not even succeeded to boot the installation kernel of RH 4.2 on
another Multia with 80 MB of RAM. From this machine I remember messages like
yours painfully.
So my guess is
1st) Either you also have the problem with the last 1 MB from Milo for
more than 64 MB of RAM ( I don't know whether you boot via SRM or ARC)
2nd) Your SCSI-bus may be too long or needs to be terminated actively.
3rd) The additional disk has still termination enabled so that the SCSI-bus
is over-terminated (I saw funny effects from this already).
