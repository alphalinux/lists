This answer is PCI specific, not limited to alphas. Further
followups and replies to private mail, I think.

joe@neuro.uni-oldenburg.de said:
> AFAIK, on most Intel boxes, *writing to* an external PCI card is
> fast, ie burst mode *is* supported with a max. thruput 132 MB/s. But
> *reading from* that same card is not possible in burst mode.

joe@neuro.uni-oldenburg.de said:
> Is the burst mode read *from* external cards supported by Alpha
> machines, specifically on PC164s?

This is not really a matter for the CPU but the motherboard chipset.
I know that the DEC PCI-to-PCI bridges do a good job of combining reads
and going transparent when the two busses get in sync. I have not really
studied your particular question because the hardware we design always
has a DMA controller on it and writes the data to the host memory.

Hell, we get good transfer rates this way even on a Packard Hell/pentium.

If you really must do bursting reads of your card you need to make sure
the device memory region is flagged as *prefetchable memory*. Prefetching
generates bursts pretty easily. Although it is legit to burst reads to
non-prefetchable memory, it's not likely to happen in general.

You really really REALLY want your card to do bus mastering writes.
