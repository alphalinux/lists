Hmm, Trio64 seems to be a bit flakey on some Alpha platforms; try adding
the following line to the "Device" Section of etc/X11/XF86Config:

Option "no_pci_disconnect"

It may help keep the Xserver from locking up the machine.

> finally a summary question:
> we are wondering if all of our trouble stems from the use of this
> particular ncr controller. we would not have any problem approaching

NCR810 is by *far* the most common controller on the Alphas; at least, the
vast majority of older ones came with it as standard. Newer ones seem to be
equipped with different ones, like Adaptec 2940UW, or QLogic 1020ISP, or
one of the Intraserver Technology's NCR875-based boards.

And yes, there have been some problems with the NCR810; on the Alpha, it
seems that SCSI termination is of utmost importance.

I noticed that the IBM disk is an Ultra Wide; the NCR810 can only support
Fast and Narrow, so its a bit unusual to see such a disk on that controller.
If this was for economic reasons (ultra wide controllers are *much* more
expensive than the older and slower narrow ones), I can understand; but it
occurs to me that there must be an adapter to convert from SCSI-1 controller
cable to the SCSI-2 connector of the disk drive, which may be a possible
source for your problem.

If possible, see if you can find a narrow drive, and see if it behaves better.
If you want to keep the UW drive, you may want/need to invest in a more modern
controller to go with it.

Good luck.
