2) f2c causes spurious floating point errors during translation
This happens with f2c provided by Redhat 4.2, or with a self-compiled
current version of f2c.
A workaround for this problem is to catch the SIGFPE signal,
floating point errors.
.....

3) `clock' command shifts system time by 20 years
After booting, the system date is shifted by 20 years (to the year 2017)
time, day of month and month are O.K. in most cases.
Sometimes even time and date are totally wrong.
This seems to be caused by a bug in the `clock' command.

Your clock problems have to do with the fact that your hardware clock
can keep the time in several ways: NT-compatible or not, and GMT or
local time. You have to tell 'clock' how to interpret the hardware value; I
think the NT bit is handled by -a, and GMT by -u.

As to fortran exceptions, at least some of them are due to the fact
that the default exception mode on Intel (and mamy other CPUs) is
'silent' i.e. the FPU stores a Inf/NaN result and quietly proceeds
with the calculation; on alpha the default exception mode is to call
exception handler.
