Hi,

I know I'm a bit late, but it might be of interest for anybody...

> > I'd like to upgrade my Jensen Board with 16MB SIMMs. I was told, that
> > it might be a problem to use modern SIMMs, because they often use a 2k
> > or 4k refresh scheme, whilst some motherboards aren't able to support
> > those.
I have run at least 6 different sorts of 16 MB SIMMs in my Jensen so far,
the newest ones were from last year AFAIK, without any problems. And
recently I tried brand new 32 MB SIMMs, also worked without problems,
even together with four of the 16 MB SIMMs, resulting in a total of 192MB
RAM...

> > kind of RAM is supported. BTW, the specs I found online say that 4, 16
> > and 32 MB SIMMs are supported, but I know at least one guy, who has been
> > able to use 8 MB SIMMs, too.
Yes, my Jensen originally also had four 8 MB SIMMs...

> 8MB (dual-RAS aka doublesided) seem to work
8 MB and 32 MB are always doublesided, aren't they?

> However, I just tried to use 4* 8MB EDO (60ns) 36 bit SIMMs.
Huh, I didn't know the Jensen supports EDO SIMMs?

> Now I am not sure if the Jensen palcode/kernel *do* parity checking ...
At least mine requires RAM with Parity. AFAIK it's not a normal parity
check but something like this ECC thing newer intel-boards can do, too.
That's why you need /real/ parity and not the so-called "electronic"
fake-parity that was found on the market two years(?) ago...
