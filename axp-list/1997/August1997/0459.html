>Pardon my lack of knowledge. I've just completed install RH and NT on my
>recently purchased UDB. After much difficulty I've got both OS's installed
>and booting. I cannot seem to get RH to communicate with my Bitsurfr Pro (at
>the milo prompt I get a bunch of >0's, while I am able to boot NT and dial
>out with out any problems, I cannot say the same for Linux-Alpha. This is

This is because MILO (by default) echoes to COM1 (/dev/ttyS0) for debugging
purposes. You can get around this by getting a Y-splitter (hard to find, last
I checked) so that you can use the UDB's two serial ports, or unplugging your
modem until after MILO has booted the system.

>one of my problems. The other being, I can't seem to get sound out of the
>NT side, any suggestions on both of these problems would be appreciated.

The UDB uses a Microsoft Sound System compatible chip, so NT ought to just
-love- it ;)
