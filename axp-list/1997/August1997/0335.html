In linux.redhat.axp you write:

| Well, the card I've been having trouble with has been in and used
| successfully by an linux/intel box, for whatever that's worth.

| Anyway, I got jays-milo-2.0.30-06aug.tar.gz from gatekeeper[1]. After
| compiling and installing it, MILO ran without any complaints, but the
| machine still got wedged at the same place. Interestingly, it now
| appears to be variable whether it reports initialising the BusLogic
| driver before going into vapour lock. That is, it appears to hang
| either immediately after reporting `FDC 0 is a post-1991 82077' or
| immediately after the BusLogic driver message which (sometimes)
| follows it.

Well, it's not a problem I had, then. I can describe what I did, for
what it's worth:

- remove the offending card so the system can boot
- configure a kernel with no extra drivers, just the hardware you
actually have
- build a milo with that kernel
- replace the offending card
- see if that works, if not put printk("Got here\n") all over the place
until you find out where it hangs, then figure out why (more printk's),
then fix it.

It is a pain, to be sure, but you can get there from here.
