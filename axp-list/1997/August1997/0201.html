>>> Sheer Panic said:
> If you find out what it is, please tell me.. I spent many hours trying to
> figure this out on a alpha news server, which is configured with MD drives
> and a IntraServer card, and does this whenever it is fscked, always.
> Finally I gave up, decided I'd try again in a month or two when some new
> patches came out..

What version of the NCR53C8XX driver were you using? It is critical to
have at least up to rev 1.18i of that driver, and perhaps even as far as
the 2.4 version, it seems. Stock 2.0.30 contains 1.12d which is not up to
the task of handling the NCR875, and the alpha-patches don't update any
driver, IIRC.

Note that pre-patch-2.0.31-2 and -3 contain patches for that driver up to
(at least) 1.18i, which is what I've been building MILOs with recently.
You can look for them at www.linuxhq.com, and then drop first them, then
the alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.1, on top of stock 2.0.30.

Also, have you been in touch with Intraserver? They are *very* helpful,
and may know of a solution. Talk with Kevin Mocklin (mocklin@intraserver.com)
and see if he can help.

Also, if you've ever had any problems with disks during MILO operation, be
aware that the newest MILOs in the Kernels/ directory on gatekeeper may
solve those problems as well (we found a *bad* timing problem recently).

Good luck.
