>>> "Jim. Shipman" said:
>
> What do I need to remote boot linux on a PC164 board w/DE500-AA NIC. I
> won't have video, SCSI, floppy, or anything else on the board.
>
> Where do I find the info needed to set this up?

Only SRM console offers BOOTP support (at the moment; AlphaBIOS is being
enhanced for sometime in the future), so for starters, you'll need to boot
from there.

The current most recent alpha-patches-2.0.30-0.1 have support for building
BOOTP images; just do:

make dep
make boot
make bootpfile

and grab "bootpfile" from the arch/alpha/boot/ directory.

Note that this should work fine on the PC164, but some of the other platforms
may be problematic at the moment; LX164 and MIATA have successfully been
booted this way, but SABLE needs some additional patches, and I've never tried
it on MIKASA/NORITAKE, nor most of the older boxes.

Good luck; let me know of any problems.
