On 1 Aug, Martin Ostermann wrote:
> I'd like to upgrade my Jensen Board with 16MB SIMMs. I was told, that
> it might be a problem to use modern SIMMs, because they often use a 2k
> or 4k refresh scheme, whilst some motherboards aren't able to support
> those.

Sorry, I have no data on these.

> Because the Jensen is an old design, I wonder if somebody knows which
> kind of RAM is supported. BTW, the specs I found online say that 4, 16
> and 32 MB SIMMs are supported, but I know at least one guy, who has been
> able to use 8 MB SIMMs, too.

8MB (dual-RAS aka doublesided) seem to work - I also remember that
Juergen Blume had 4 of these in his Jensen.

However, I just tried to use 4* 8MB EDO (60ns) 36 bit SIMMs. The
console found them fine, Linux booted and ran, but I got filesystem
corruptions which made the disk unusable after a short time - without
any notices of parity errors etc..

I had single changed characters during kernel compiles ('/' -> '#'),
and then later fsck got quite unhappy ...

Now I am not sure if the Jensen palcode/kernel *do* parity checking ...
I will have to cross-check using NT. It would be a waste not to use
these SIMMs, as they dont work in nortmal PCs.
