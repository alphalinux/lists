Thanks to everyone for all of your help in getting some rescue
disks made for my UDB. Especially for the Zip drive idea. I now have a zip
disk with two complete Linux systems on it, one for Linux/Intel and one
for Linux/Alpha, plus a handful of floppy disks for booting. As for the
UDB, I used the sneaky method for getting ARC to boot milo off of a
floppy. Put milo on a DOS floppy, named it fwupdate.exe and then choose
the update firmware option in the ARC loader. This works quite well and
quickly for booting milo, and eventually Linux from a floppy. Then once
the kernel is loaded, it finds the Zip disk and off we go.
As for the fdisk trouble.... I never figured it out. I just went
back to the source tree I had been using for kernels, and a rescue kernel
built from that works just fine. It is just plain 2.0.30 with the latest
alpha diff before 2.0.30 diff applied. Apparently the 2.0.30 alpha diff
broke a couple of things. Maybe I accidently grabbed a early copy of it.
Where are the offical archives of the alpha diffs? So I can make sure I
get the right one and the most recent one. Thanks.
