On Wed, 20 Aug 1997, Ed wrote:

> My Linux box will detect the EtherExpress 16, and it will detect the
> NE2000 if they are the ONLY cards in the system.
>
> If I put the EE16 and the NE2000 in the machine at the same time, Linux
> will only detect the NE2000.

Okay, I feel a bit stupid for wasting your time with this, but I did find
the answer to my question.

There is a somewhat obscure reference to this issue in the Ethernet-Howto
that talks about multiple net adapters.

Here's the quote that helped me solve the problem.

"The second way (not recommended) is to edit the file Space.c and replace
the 0xffe0 entry for the i/o address with a zero.
The 0xffe0 entry tells it not to probe for that device -- replacing it
with a zero will enable autoprobing for that device."

I edited the Space.c file and replaced the 0xffe0 entry (on the eth1
line) with a 0. This caused eth1 to be autodetected just like eth0 was.
Now both cards are autodetected. I assume that doing the same for
eth[2..n] would likewise enable autodetection for those cards, but I
don't need more than 2 cards.

After that, I re-built my kernel with NE2000 and EtherExpress 16 support,
and now both cards are autodetected with no problems.

Sorry that I wasted your time and ended up answering my own question, but
I hope my finding has helped someone. Oh, it already has. Me!

Thanks,
Ed
