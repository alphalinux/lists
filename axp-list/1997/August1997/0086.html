With regards to your message at 11:01 AM 08-05-97 +0200, Stefan Schroepfer.
Where you stated:
>Maurice W. Hilarius wrote:
>>
>> With regards to your message at 06:27 hrs. 08-03-97 +0200, Stefan
>> Schroepfer. Where you stated:
>> >
>> >(...)
>> >
>> >Is there a trick to run a PC164 board with a normal fan with only
>> >two connectors? Connecting a normal fan and ignoring the FAN_CONN_L
>> >pin a PC164 board doesn't run.
>>
>> That is not something to mess with. This connection causes the CPU
>> to be halted if the fan fails. Without this you would likely destroy
>> your CPU and board, so I do _NOT_ suggest cheating on this!
>
>I know. But I'm looking for a temporary solution until I got
>the original fan.
You need to supply +5V to the middle pin of the fan connector on the
motherboard. The outside pins are ground and +12V supply.
A connection from a 5V wire for disks will work. These are (normally) red
wires. I'll leave the connector to your imagination and skills...
