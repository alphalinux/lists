The procedure for getting the pcmcia stuff to work depends on what
configuration you have. You had to jump thru one set of hoops for RH4.1,
another for 4.2. For 4.2, I had to do the following:

1. Replace the insmod binary from the stock 4.2. I haven't checked the
updates recently but the original insmod would not load modules properly. I
replaced it with the insmod binary from 4.1

2. Dowload pcmcia-cs-2.9.x (2.9.7 is working, finally) from hyper.standford.edu

3. [Get the pristine 2.0.30 kernel sources (from your favorite kernel
source) with the 0.1 axp-patches (from gatekeeper)] Be sure to enable
modules and set version information on the modules. Go at least through make
dep to generate headers that pcmcia-cs needs.

4. Apply the following patch (with thanks to Robert.A.Bottomley.Jr@cdc.com)
to k_compat.h in <pcmcia_directory>/include/pcmcia:

-#define get_user(x, ptr) __get_user(x, ptr)
+#define get_user(x, ptr) ((x) = __get_user((ptr), sizeof(*(ptr))))

5. Make the pcmcia modules as described in the docs. Turn pcmcia on in
/etc/sysconfig/pcmcia and off you go.

Procedure with RH4.1 was different but at least as complicated.
