"RLM" == Robert L Millner <rmillner@cv3.cv.nrao.edu> writes:
RLM> I keep bashing my nose into which is a difference between what is in the
RLM> linux headers and the source for milo and can't get it to compile.

...which is due to a difference between a plain 2.0.30 + pre-31.6 and
the RedHat supplied kernel. The axp-diffs-2.0.30 patch has the needed
definition. If you are building your own 2.0.30 from the source dist,
you may want to still have a look at some of the patches supplied with
RedHat.

There seems to be a difference of opinion in the stock kernel and some
of the patches as to what should be declared an int and a long. On an
intel box, short=2 bits, int=4 and long=4 (according to sizeof()). On
an alpha running D.U. or Linux its 2,4,8 so it makes a difference.
