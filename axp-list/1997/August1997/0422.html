Assunto: RE: Internal modem in DEC PC Axp 150 (alias Jensen)

A> Did you disable the COM1 and COM2 on the motherboard. How does one do that?

Actually, I don't know whether one /can/ do that.

A> I know how to do that with BIOS but on Alpha, without ECU?

You really should try to get an ECU; you'll find one on Gatekeeper.dec.com, for
example. According to my experiences, any version will work. Be careful with
your firmware; version 1.7 seems to work best; the most recent, 1.9 (AFAIK), is
at least incompatible with the ELSA Winner 1000 graphics board in EISA mode,
old versions are (according to DEC) incompatible with Windows NT 3.5 and 4.0, I
didn't try them with Linux.

A> Should I use COM3 and COM4. In that case I guess I would have to share
A> interrupt with COM1 or COM2.

No, I don't think so; as far as I remember, the internal ports use standard
addresses, but not standard interrupts. You can definitely choose any interrupt
your fifo serial card or internal modem supports, as far as it isn't used by
another card or the system board.

A> Hm, that's no good solution either, because the internal serial ports
A> are 16450 compatible, not 16550, which means they don't supply a
A> buffer, so connections above 9600 may get huge error rates.

I also think the error rate will most likely be much greater than that of a
buffered serial port. I tried using a 14400 bps modem with a 16450 and got much
better results with any of two different 16550. The cps rates were much better
and almost no more lost carriers occured.

>> Maybe this is due to the fact I didn't run the ECU configuration (I don't
>> know where to get the software from!)
A> Hmm, I guess that's unlikely. You probably got an ISA card, and the
A> ECU just makes sure, that you don't use the same resource for two
A> cards. If you do a careful setup, and you do know the resources of the
A> cards in use, you don't need the ECU for ISA cards.

That seems to be true, my Jensen worked properly with incorrectly set up ECU.
As far as I remember, I had changed my ISA network adapter's settings without
changing also the ECU settings without any problems.

>> Too bad it is so difficult to find **any** documentation on this machine.

Difficult? It's almost impossible... DEC obviously wants us to purchase more
expensive machines rather than using prototypes.

Viele Gruesse, Christoph
