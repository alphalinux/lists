Alex Krowitz wrote:
>I'd like to buy an alpha to use as a networked computation server, and
>don't need a monitor (except during linux installation, of
>course). Someone mentioned that I might have trouble booting or
>running without the video card.
>
>Does anyone know whether, after installation, I can run without video
>card and monitor? Thanks for your help.

A few observations on serial consoles:
The PC164 and 164LX BIOS uses com1 as the console if no keyboard/video
is installed. A VT-220 is needed for the 164LX, a VT-100 works OK for the PC164.
The LX uses AlphaBIOS and prompts for function keys to be pressed which are
not mapped into the terminals keyboard. Thus far I've found F2 = CTL-B and
F10 = CTL-U.

I haven't tried Linux without video yet.
