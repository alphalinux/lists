On Aug 18, Jerry Feldman wrote:

> This was discussed a bit earlier. I have an A/B switch which has the
> capability to switch monitor, mouse, and keybord between 2 systems. On my
> UDB, the monitor switches fine, but Linux does not recognize the scan codes.
> However, the keyboard works fine with the ARC console.
>
> I am using a keyboard with an AT connector and AT to PS/2 adaptor. That
> keyboard also works fine if it is plugged into the keyboard slot via the
> adaptor.
>
> My first question is: has anyone successfully used a switchbox to share a
> keyboard and/or mouse between 2 systems (in this case Intel/Alpha)?
>
> I am not concerned about the mouse at this time.

I have my Intel PC, a XL266 and UDB166 connected to my old PC style keyboard,
crt and mouse via a A/B/C/D switch (using PS/2 adapters for XL and UDB).

I have to make sure that the keyboard is connected to the AXP boxes whenever
they (re-) boot. after booting I can switch without problems.

only my old logitech mouse needs reinitialisation after switching because
it looses it's baud rate settings etc. (I'm using 9600 baud, not the default 1200
at mouse power-up).
