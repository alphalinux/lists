"Henry M. Pierce" <hmp@InfoMagic.COM> wrote:
> As an FYI, I like to burden each Attorney General of the state in
> which the spam originates. California has a law on the books
> which prohibits the practice and so far I have gotten a positive
> response from Washington State AG. There is also a federal law
> that might be applied to such crap as well that prohibits the electronic
> distribution of "junk". It was written for fax machines, but to
> my none-JD'd eyes might be broad enough to apply to electronic mail
> as well.
This is unworkable. All the SPAMers need to do is to move off shore if all the states have tight regulations. How many California SPAMers have been indicted or prosecuted. Also, until we get IPV6 implemented, the SPAMers can easily spoof addresses, to make it difficult to track them. The main difference between Internet SPAM and FAX SPAM is that the cost of sending a FAX increases with distance, but Internet SPAM does not. ALso, when is SPAM legitimate (eg. such as junk mail), and when is it not. Some ISPs currently block SPAM, but there is no definitive way to block all SPAM. Some customers want SPAM blocked, others want to receive it. The bottom line is that anti-SPAM legislation is doomed for failure, and would possibly even drive legitimate businesses elsewhere.
