>I seem to recall reading somewhere about an Alpha-based Tadpole. I think
>I ignored the rest of the item when I saw the price :-)

I had a look at a Tadpole earlier this week at a Digital demo. Neat looking
toy but hardly a cost effective way of running linux. I would have thought
that unless you must have a portable alpha there are better/much cheaper
solutions. If you just want a portable linux box, a Pentium lap top is a
much better buy. I guess the only reason Tadpole sell the alpha laptop is
for those people who need a portable OpenVMS/Digital Unix system.

If you need a portable Linux/Axp box for demos, why not buy a UDB. I know
it's not a laptop, but it's about $15K cheaper (in Australia).

>I use an old RDI "BriteLite", a Sparc based "laptop" (actually it is an
