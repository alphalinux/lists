David Wild writes:
> All,
> I have something strange happening with ip aliasing that I can't figure out.
> I've tried everything and have resolved it must be something in the kernel.
>
> I assign an IP address to my alpha, say XXX.yyy.zzz.80, then alias 81 82 and
> 83. From the outside world, I can ping, telnet to, ftp to, httpd to any of t
he
> IP address with no problem. I have a nameserver running on the machine and I

> can only use the 80 (real ethernet) address in resolv.conf on other machines
on
> the same network that don't have named running. If I try any of the alias add
res
> s it hang and will not resolv.

It can't reach the port, it's trying the wire instead of the local
address.

route add x.x.x.81 eth0:0
route add x.x.x.82 eth0:1
...

This sometimes happens on intel as well... not sure why it just works for
you. An explicit route is the proper way, however.

I just do both in a "alias-up" script.
