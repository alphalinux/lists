>>These are correct as far as the VGA chip that is onboard, but that chip is
>>really intended/used *ONLY* for text-mode console work, and may not even be
>>usable to run X on (either electrically, or due to lack of enough memory).
>>
>>Note that I *have* run the XF86_Mono server on the Cirrus on the 4D20
>>successfully, but it claimed there was only 64K of memory available to it...
>>
>>Now, as to the *real* accelerator on these boards, it's a chip made for DEC
>>by IBM, and the identifying numbers (common to a 3D30 and 4D20) seem to be:
>>
>> 21-43826-01
>> 26H3574
>>
>>The 3D30 is equivalent (though much faster) than the original 8-plane TGA.
>>It has 2 Mb of VRAM on board, with a Bt485 RAMDAC good to 170MHz, so it can
>>do 1600x1200 to perhaps 70Hz.
>>
>>The 4D20 is equivalent (again much faster) than the original 24-plane+Zbuf
TGA.
>>It has 16 Mb of VRAM, and an IBM RGB561 Palette DAC, and is also capable,
>>AFAIK,
>>of 1600x1200 at reasonable refresh rate.
