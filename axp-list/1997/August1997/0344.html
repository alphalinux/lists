On Sat, Aug 16, 1997 at 07:03:15AM +0200, Ray wrote:
> the newest ones were from last year AFAIK, without any problems. And
> recently I tried brand new 32 MB SIMMs, also worked without problems,

Good to know.

> > 8MB (dual-RAS aka doublesided) seem to work
> 8 MB and 32 MB are always doublesided, aren't they?

No. I definitely know singlesided 8MB PS/2 SIMMs, although these are rare.

> > However, I just tried to use 4* 8MB EDO (60ns) 36 bit SIMMs.
> Huh, I didn't know the Jensen supports EDO SIMMs?

Me neither - but as long as it does not try to do interleaving *between* the
banks (which it does not, IMHO), then the EDOs should work just like normal
FPM (and at the same speed).

> > Now I am not sure if the Jensen palcode/kernel *do* parity checking ...
> At least mine requires RAM with Parity. AFAIK it's not a normal parity
> check but something like this ECC thing newer intel-boards can do, too.
> That's why you need /real/ parity and not the so-called "electronic"
> fake-parity that was found on the market two years(?) ago...

No. The Jensen only uses 1 bit of parity per 32 data bits (the other 3
parity bits on the SIMM socket are simply not connected). It does need a
real parity bit there, but the board definitely can't do ECC - it woule need
a lot more ECC bits than one per 32.

Now I know that NT *does* real parity chacking (it crashed quite early when
experimenting with parity-add-on schemes for non-parity SIMMs), but I am not
sure about the linux kernel.
