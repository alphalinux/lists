I know that some of you folks actually can't spare cpu cycles, but for
those who can,
there is (and has been) a contest to crack the RC5 encryption using
distributed computing.

Information about one group doing so is at http://rc5.distributed.net/
They have basicly provided clients a proxy servers, and are offering 1000
dollars (of the fully 10000)
to the group that finds the key.

I have set up my Alpha PC164 (500mhz) to play for the team
linux@linuxnet.org (http://www.baycis.com/~nickkral/linux/rc5.html), but I
don't think that is so relevant
as that they do keep information about the kind of machines cracking at
this. To have a few more Alphas on board might be a nice way of showing
just what kind of computing power these pack.

Mine is doing a modest job of 376755.38 keys per second but it is pretty
idle right now.

The clients are automatically niced so they should basicly run in your idle
cycles.
