Use ECC or error correcting (also called true parity) RAM. It will be
marked as _X36. The _X32 memory won't work. Neither will EDO. Be aware
that many vendors sell logical parity in place of true parity memory, but
this won't work either. Logical parity fools intel machines that are set
up for ECC into thinking that they have ECC memory, but this doesn't work
for alpha's. You may use memory that is rated faster than that specified,
just don't use slower. 60 nsec or faster is ok, 80 nsec or slower is not.
Just be sure that all simms are of the same speed.

Six months ago I paid ~$40 for the 256K cache, the 1 meg cache was over
$200 so I didn't get it. Check Kingston or Insight on the web for the
cache and RAM.
Also the cache was a pain to install, the sockets were very tight. Beg,
borrow, or steal a decent chip insertion tool and hopefully you won't break
a pin or two as I did :-(

Good luck.
