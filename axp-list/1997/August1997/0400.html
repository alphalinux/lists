We use a 4 GByte EIDE harddisk in our PC164/21164 based system.

The throughput of the EIDE disk is quite slow (approx 1.5 MByte/sec).
With `/sbin/hdparm -c 1 -m 16 /dev/hdc' (32 bit mode, multicount=16),
we get a much better performance of approx 7 MByte/sec.

According to the FAQ, one should also use the hdparm-flag `-u 1'
(unmask IRQ on), to avoid the system time running slow (this is the case
on our system).
Unfortunately, running `hdparm -u 1 -c 1 -m 16' slows the EIDE disk down to
approx. 1.7 MByte/sec :-(

Is there a possibility to get both, a fast disk throughput and a
accurate system time?

Is it possible to enable DMA or PIO mode on PC164 based systems?
(the IDE chipset on the PC164 is CMD646, but the kernel supports only
CMD640)

Is it secure to use `cron' (maybe every 5 to 10 minutes) to synchronize
the system time with CMOS time? (the differences in time are in the order of
several seconds per minute, if there is intensive hard disk access)

Thank you very much,

Stefan
