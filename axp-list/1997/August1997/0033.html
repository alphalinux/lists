With regards to your message at 06:27 hrs. 08-03-97 +0200, Stefan
Schroepfer. Where you stated:
>/* Not a linux related question */
>
>Did anyone of you replace the CPU fan of a PC164 board? Mine was
>vibrating strongly and was very noisy before I destroyed it with
>a screwdriver while attempting to diminish the vibrations.
>
>Are there higher quality fans with the extra 'FAN_CONN_L' pin
>available (60x60x15mm)?
I am surprised you ask this. The implication I draw is that you feel this
is a poor quality fan. In fact these are quite a nice quality and expensive
ball-bearing unit.
I suggest that you might have redeived a defective one, but replacing it
with the same kind is advisable.
>
>Is there a trick to run a PC164 board with a normal fan with only
>two connectors? Connecting a normal fan and ignoring the FAN_CONN_L
>pin a PC164 board doesn't run.

That is not something to mess with. This connection causes the CPU to be
halted if the fan fails. Without this you would likely destroy your CPU and
board, so I do _NOT_ suggest cheating on this!

>
>Sorry for this off-topic question but my alpha is dead without
>a CPU fan.

Understood. I advise buying a replacement fan of the same type from your
dealer or DEC.

Cheers,
Maurice
