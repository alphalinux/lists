On Thu, 14 Aug 1997, Yadong Li wrote:
> And we were trying to mount a disk of a DEC UNIX 4.0 host via NFS,
> we edited the /etc/exports file and tried to mount the disk onto the
> Linux-alpha machine.

> It didn't work, then I found out and convinced our system administrator
> to issue 'killall -HUP /usr/sbin/rpc.mountd' and
> 'killall -HUP /usr/sbin/rpc.nfsd' on the server machine.

Digital UNIX does not use a HUP signal to tell the NFS server daemons to
reread /etc/exports, instead you use the 'showmount -e' command to do that.
Sending a kill signal to the daemons did just that -- kill them. You in
essence yanked the rug out from under part of the NFS susbsystem. It's no
wonder you had problems after that. :)
