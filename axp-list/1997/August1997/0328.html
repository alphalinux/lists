Heaping shovel-fuls of gratitude to the folks who helped me get my
AlphaStation back in order. I had fiddled with some firmware settings
under the (mistaken) impression that this particular machine had Digital
UNIX pre-installed (is it true these License PAKs are worth their weight in
gold? ;) Anyway...up and running with Linux now, much happier.

I ran into another problem shortly after that. The "XL" MILO images didn't
seem to work on this system as they should (AS 200 4/100, upgraded to 233
MHz*, and with #9FX Vision 300 card installed). It would either hang or
reboot at the "Switching to PALcode..." message, I eventually got it to
work by replacing the milo file with one found at:

ftp://gatekeeper.dec.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Miniloader/v2.0/2.0.22/XL-avanti/

* About this upgrade thing: the replacement CPU appears to be a 275 MHz
unit...I'm wondering if this can be run at 266 MHz with a suitable crystal,
or if something in the AS200 design intrinsically limits the maximum speed
to 233 MHz. Any ideas? And if it's possible, where exactly would one find
the appropriate crystal?

A couple of suggestions for...whoever takes care of these things: maybe
it's obvious to the more seasoned users, but it's only hinted at in a
couple of sentences in an appendix that MILO can be installed in a small
DOS partition rather than requiring a boot floppy every time. Also, I was
a bit confused by the /images and /milo/images directories at first, what
with the common filenames within and such...granted, nothing a little
RTFM'ing wasn't able to clear up, but perhaps there could be some different
naming convention for boot and MILO images to make this more apparent and
to eliminate the need to rename one or the other when trying to download
both into a common directory.

Finally...if anyone has built their own kernel on a system with a similar
configuration, I would be interested in seeing the settings you used, as I
have been unable to build one that works so far. I _think_ maybe it's
having a conniption over the second Ethernet card I added (PCI, Realtek
chipset, NE2000), complaining about it being in "nonexistent slot #0"
(though the original kernel from the Avanti disk doesn't complain)...but
there are many other fiddly bits and settings that I'm unsure of,
particularly among the SCSI low-level drivers (which fails to even compile,
BTW, unless I select "Omit FlashPoint support"). Oh yes, and I was
wondering whether any of the sound support settings are viable on this
system.

Thanks much for any pointers!

-- Phil
