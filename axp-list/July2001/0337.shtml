> what to flag and not?

To prevent a flood, the rate of "unaligned trap" messages is limited
to 4 messages in 5 ms.
Good place to look for unaligned traps is /proc/cpuinfo. There are
counts for kernel/user unaligned accesses and address of the
instruction caused last trap.

> > So, a little more about the problem you saw, and the machine you are
> > running on, please...
>
> I am running this on 2 systems a UP1100(last to show the problem) and a
> noname.

Note that unaligned "short int" accesses are special. In the code
compiled for ev4/ev5 (i.e. without byte/word extensions) they do not
generate unaligned traps and hence cannot be fixed by kernel. So if
the short int falls into the adjacent quadwords (addr & 7 == 7),
you'll have silent data corruption.
