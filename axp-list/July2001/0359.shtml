On Fri, 27 Jul 2001, Gregor Maier wrote:

> I would say that DU / Tru64 is definitly faster than Linux on the alpha
> platform because (IMHO)

OTOH, all of the Unix(TM) platforms have suffered from slow
featuritis and adding of overhead to support the very-high end
platforms to the point that they are now dog-slow in the general
case.

Linux has had the advantage of being under the tight direction of
/one/ very brilliant kernel guru, Linus, hence not ever having had to
succumb to marketing kernel cruft. Linux is very very light compared
to the commercial Unix kernels.

Eg, look how slow Solaris is on low to medium end hardware (whether
uSPARC or i386). Linux / BSD are an order of magnitude faster for
context-switches. (another case is IRIX 6.5 Vs 6.2 Vs 5.3 Vs linux.)

Although, IME, {OSF/1,DU,Tru64} seems to have fared better in
resisting kernel bloat than solaris, i'll bet Linux is still faster
than DU for most kernel intensive applications².

For compute bound stuff, yes GCC¹ isn't as fast as Compaq CC - but
hey CCC is also available for Linux!
