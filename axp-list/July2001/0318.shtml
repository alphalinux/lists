>>>From a Win98 client I can't see the server or shared folders when browsing
> the network (the same computer used to configure with swat).
> (I don't logon the client's to the server. Use it as a workgroup).
>
> Swat tells me that SMB and NMB is "not running".
> When starting/restarting from swat, it still says "not running".
>
> I then try to "service smb restart" on the server.
> It says
> Stopping smb [FAILED]
> Stopping nmb [OK]
> Starting smb [OK]
> Starting nmb [OK]
>
> If I try the service restart again, stopping smb fails again.
>
> Still no visible server/shared folders on the network.
> Running \\64.32.16.254 (ip-adress of the server) from the win98-client
> does not work either.
>
> Could it be som wrong configuration in smb.conf that cause the service to
> fail ?
> Do you know of other possibilities ?

The error at stopping a service can be misleading. AFAIK most services issue a
[OK] at startup although there was an error and the service isn't running. When
you try to stop the service the kill fails because there's no process.

The chances are quite good that you smb.conf isn't working and that the server
doesn't start at all.

Look at /var/log/message and the samba log files for error messages. You can
also try to do ps and look if the smbd / nmbd is running.
