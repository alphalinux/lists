> From: "Tim Tuck" <tim.tuck@penrith.net>
> Date: Tue, 24 Jul 2001 10:38:44 +1000
>
> I'm not sure if this has been asked and answered but if you have an internal
> DDS2 drive installed that will cause boot failure of the distro's!
>
> Our 1000 wouldn't even boot the Compaq jumpstart CD until we pulled the scsi
> cable out of the back of the tape unit.
>
Depending upon your configuration, the XP1000 normally has only 1 SCSI
card, which supports the internal drives. If you attach a device to the
external connector on the card, it had better be with a very short cable or
you will blow the buss lengths. The old Alphaserver 1000A had this same
problem/configuration. I suspect that the card is also not configured to
auto terminate.
