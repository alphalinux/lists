On Tue, Jul 17, 2001 at 02:31:03PM +0200, J.A. Gutierrez wrote:
>
> So, the questions are:
>
> - Is it possible to build a milo from sources which will be
> able to load 2.4.x no-CONFIG_ALPHA_LEGACY_START_ADDRESS kernels?

No, AFAIK (somebody correct me if I am mistaken). But it is possible,
and actually quite trivial when you are updating, to reconfigure
and build yourself a kernel with CONFIG_ALPHA_LEGACY_START_ADDRESS=yes.
You may put it on a boot floppy or you may not even bother and
boot for an update into a ramdisk using a kernel from your hard drive.
Just type a boot command at Milo prompt. Loading a ramdisk from
a floppy is likely the easiest.

> - There is another way to upgrade a Ruffian host from 7.0
> (kernel 2.2.19) to 7.1?

See above. Also using boot/ramdisk pair of floppies from 7.0 with CDs
from 7.1 most likely works. Can't say for sure, as I have no way to
try, but somebody with a hardware might. Another option which probably
works (between 7.0 and 7.1, not - say - 6.2 to 7.1) is to do that
"manually" with 'rpm -Fvh *.rpm' in a directory with binary rpms. This
may require few passes of "save error messages, use them to resolve
missing dependencies, try again" type and other minor corrections
afterwards.

There are other hack options but they require more creativity and
digging for low level details; but after all installer is not likely
to be smarter than you are. :-)

> - Will dri/"NoAccel" "off" work for a "Matrox|MGA G200" with
> the kernel/X11 included on 7.1?

AFAIK acceleration with this hardware does not work anyway so I am not
sure what the problem is.
