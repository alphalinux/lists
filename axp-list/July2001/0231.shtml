On Fri, Jul 20, 2001 at 03:09:24PM -0600, Craig Tierney wrote:
>
> The code looks to be under GPL v2. I don't know why it isn't integrated
> into the kernel.

The most likely reason in that case is that nobody ever bothered to
submit it. The driver should also have a maintainer/owner.

> It doesn't require any patches to the rest of the kernel.

Drivers of that sort which really affect only users of a particular
piece of hardware are accepted all the time even in "stable" series of
kernels. But nobody is actively seeking "what else can we add here" so
somebody with an itch has to show some initiative. :-)

> As far as getting my patch back to Qlogic, I probably have been too lazy.

A driver which works only on one architecture will likely be rejected
in that form right away. It is a maintaner job to assure that a given
driver has at least a fighting chance everywhere. There are also
matters of conforming to a style, documentation, keeping global
variables from colliding with anything else and other housekeeping
chores of that sort. It does not mean that s/he has to do all that work
but this will be a clearing and a coordination point for all eventual
patches.

There are some old drivers without a real maintainer. As long as they
are "quiet" and do not create troubles then fine. From time to time
somebody has to clean them up a bit and some actually go broken for
quite prolonged periods. If nobody gets hands dirty that means that
nobody cares.

> I should probably do it anyway.

Pretty good way to get your name on MAINTAINERS list. :-)
