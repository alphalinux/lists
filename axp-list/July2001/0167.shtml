On Wed, Jul 18, 2001 at 12:59:44PM +0200, kjetil.bugge@borregaard.com wrote:
>
> I have recently installed Red Hat 7.1 on a AlphaServer 1000.
> Got it up to console, but when starting X-windows, it crash (system stops
> responding)) when the start-picture for GNOME pops up.
> Have to press the reset-button.
>
> I have configured the video-card (Diamond Stealh 64 DRAM - S3 Trio64 chip
> - 2MB) with Xconfigurator. It probed itself up.
>
> If I use the internal mainboard-video (Cirrus Logic chip) and removed the
> PCI-card, I only see a BIG OK-button when testing the configuration in
> Xconfigurator.
>
> 1. What cause the PCI-video-card to stop the system ?

It may be a probelm similar to what we've seen before on AVANTI-class
machines (ie EV4/APECS) with Trio64 cards, especially if your AS1000
is a 4/xxx model.

Try adding:

        Option "no_pci_disconnect"

to the Device Section of your XF86COnfig-4 file, and see if it helps.

> 2. What cause the internal card to size everything up ?

There's really no Alpha support ever been ported for the builtin Cirrus,
AFAIK.
