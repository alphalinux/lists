Eric,

No prob, its not particularly special: PC100 ECC (or
in your case, non-ECC is also supported) unbuffered
3.3 volt CAS2/CAS3 SDRAM. This is a Pyxis 21174
chipset requirement, even though the Pyxis chipset
locks the frequency of the system bus to a 66MHz
frequency and you COULD use PC66 memory as a result.

However, PC100 memory (go to http://www.crucial.com
and buy memory from the "Intel - 440BX" selections)
and you'll get the PC100 memory selections for
everything up to 256MB modules (the largest supported
on the low-end Pyxis based machines, including SX, LX
and the Samsung/Deskstation UX/BX). You'll note these
are MUCH cheaper and higher quality parts (much newer
part numbers in a volume part) speced well above the
"tested" PC66 memory you'll find under the "Digital -
Personal Workstation" group (these are Pyxis based
machines with board very similar to the LX/SX boards,
though PWS has 2 more DIMM sockets and two PCI buses).

I have 2 LX boxes, both using Crucial 256MB modules of
the PC100 CAS2 (7ns) type. They have performed very
well despite repeated overclocking experiments,
including jacking up the Pyxis bus successfully to
75MHz (but never to 83.5, unfortunately, that will
require more significant soldering work).

Good luck,

--Kirk
