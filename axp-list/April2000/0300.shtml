>
> Ok, I've read a whole slew of stuff about these Tulip
> cards and having to compile the driver into the
> kernel.

I do not know what you are reading but this is a bunk.

> I'm ready to just forget it and buy another

Tulips are in general fine (although some variants have their
quirks). Most network cards I happen to deal with are tulips.
Like this one (straight from a 'dmesgs' after a module was loaded
on Alpha):

p.c:v0.91g-ppc 7/16/99 becker@cesdis.gsfc.nasa.gov
eth0: Digital DS21143 Tulip rev 48 at 0x8000, 00:00:F0:51:02:52, IRQ 44.
eth0: EEPROM default media type Autosense.
eth0: Index #0 - Media 10baseT (#0) described by a 21142 Serial PHY (2) block.
eth0: Index #1 - Media 10baseT-FD (#4) described by a 21142 Serial PHY (2) block.
eth0: Index #2 - Media 100baseTx (#3) described by a 21143 SYM PHY (4) block.
eth0: Index #3 - Media 100baseTx-FD (#5) described by a 21143 SYM PHY (4) block.

> The final reading on my own situation is that the
> system detected the card and added the appropriate
> line to the conf.modules file, but apparently, this is
> for naught, as people around here seem to believe that
> it won't run from there.

Some believe in strange things. So?

> It is not starting. Typing
> modprobe eth0 comes back with an error about not being
> able to open a dependencies file called modules.dep.

Oh, at last something a bit more substantial that "does not work".
This has **nothing** to do with tulip. What happens if you are running
'depmod -a'. Does this fail? Why, i.e. error messages? 'depmod -a'
should run in your startup files while you are booting.
