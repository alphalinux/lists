On Sat, Apr 08, 2000 at 02:35:25PM -0700, B. James Phillippe wrote:
>
> Anybody still making these? If so, what are the recommendations?

It's possible that Tadpole (UK) would still make one if someone offered
to pay enough (>$10K).

But it's not worth it; AlphaBook1 was based on LCA45 @ 233MHz (same as
top-end UDB), but typically it could run only at 116 MHz (or even 57 MHz)
because of heat. Can you say ssllloooooowwwww.... :-\

Also, *very* heavy (metal case), and a NiMH battery that was down to
70% when finished booting (from a full charge)... ;-}

On the other hand, it had a nice TFT display (10.4" IIRC) and PCMCIA
slots that worked well.

Much better option would be a DS10L "Slate"... ;-}
