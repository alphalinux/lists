Essential fixes, including a fix to a long standing bug in skbuf
handling (this locks up NIC under a heavy traffic and affects likely
all drivers from Donald Becker), were done in 2.2.15pre series.
The current 2.2.15pre19 is for all purposes the same as 2.2.15
but it is still waiting for "non possum" from Linus so it is "pre".

A "2.2.14" kernel from Red Hat 6.2 distribution is really roughly
2.2.15pre11 (IIRC) plus updates to RAID support plus some other patches
which were included, or subsumed, by later kernels from 2.2.15pre
series. This kernel may also include an updated tulip driver but I did
not check precisely which version is really that.

You will find in the latest kernels 'de4x5', 'old_tulip' and 'tulip'
drivers. There are various tulip cards which will work with all three
of those (I have such card, for example) or with only two or maybe with
only one. This depends on differences in transmitters (an maybe also in
an autonegotiation sequence); various manufacturers did assorted
"improvements" to a design one time or another. 'old_tulip' driver is
supposed to be redundant in the current 2.2.15pre, and is included only
as a safety measure, but it may turn out that this precaution is vital
to somebody with some particular model of card. Time will tell. :-)

I know that this may be confusing but "Tulip" is really a sizeable
family of cards with varying characteristics with quite a bit of
this stuff not really ever published as technical specs. If you think
that with other NICs the situation is better you may be for a surprise. :-)
