Just a small run down on things to worry about when using SRM.

1. As Maurice mentioned, you need a SCSI controller that SRM
   recognizes. Typically these are QlogicISP, Symbios 53c810
   thru 53c875. Some of the 53c895/896 may also work but I've
   had troubles with them. Probably a brand issue though. I
   like the Tekram DC390F with a 53c875 chipset. It's cheap and
   works.

2. When you partition, a couple catches. First, use BSD labels,
   then, do not start your partitions at the first cylinder and
   do not allocate the last cylinder. At least on my systems
   that causes wrap around and destroys my boot block.

3. Use swriteboot to put the proper things in the boot block
   to allow the HD to boot.

Thats about it I think. If anyone thinks of something else,
specific to SRM, add it to the list.

Also, you can mount your boot floppy and put any kernel you
want on it. It's just an EXT2 file system on a floppy. I
don't think you should use the one you mentioned earlier.
Look in /boot for a more suitable kernel. It will need to
be compressed to fit on a floppy though.
