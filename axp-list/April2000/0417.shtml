On Thu, Apr 27, 2000 at 07:20:54PM +0200, Stefan Schroepfer wrote:
> "Hoover, Tony" wrote:
> >
> > The larger problem of your hard drives not using the ULTRA speeds is because
> > you have non-ULTRA devices on the same SCSI bus. ULTRA SCSI uses low
> > voltage differential ( LVD ) signaling to communicate to the devices. If
>
> It seems like UW and U2W get confused here. UW SCSI is *not*
> LVD and on a single UW bus non-Ultra and non-Wide devices
> can happily live together with UW devices, all at their
> individual maximum transfer rate.
>
> I don't know the tech specs of the DigitalScape 885, but I
> can't see where the original sender mentioned that he expects
> U2W operation.

Yes, You're right. I don't have any devices (drives or adapter) with LVD
thing. All my drives are Single End (with 68 pin connectors attached to a 68
pin connector on the card).
Also, the CD drives uses 50 pin connector connected to the 50 pin connector on
the card.

My HD are U2W (well, that's what I understant from IBM docs) but I use them
(without any converter) as UW drives.

Well, the DigitalScape 885 uses a Sym53C885 chipset.

I just make a simple test : I disconnect my CD drives. And Linux still shows
that my HD are detected as FAST-Wide.

I don't understand since the card detects the drives as UltraWide.

adapter doc isn't helpful.
