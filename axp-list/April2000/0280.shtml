Well, the whole day gone, nothing worked. I have
installed the OS about 5 times now and every time it
goes just fine, and it proceeds to fail on reboot.

This last time, I went back and used fdisk and nuked
all the partitions (even though the prior install,
where I opted for Auto Partitioning, had put them
there it appeared), and I made sure to start the
partitions on cylinder 2 for both drives.

The whole business about getting the filenames at the
end of the install was a nightmare. I pressed ATL-F2
and CTRL-ALT-F2 and it got me to bash# where I did the
cat command and wrote down what was there. However,
they did not tell you in the manual how to get back to
the other screen, they just say, get back. Needless to
say, the same keys didn't work. I tried to type EXIT
at the bash prompt and that just said LOGOUT and it
hung. Finally, the manual also fails to tell you how
to compose a boot command based on the file names you
just wrote down in the prior step. Here is what I
wrote down:

0:1/vmlinuz-2.2.14-6.0 root=/dev/sdb1

I had such high hopes for getting this going. Seems
like the installation is well-done, except for the
fact that I couldn't get it done in a whole day!!
