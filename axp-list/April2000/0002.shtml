Dave Gilbert wrote:
>
> On Fri, 31 Mar 2000, W Bauske wrote:
>
> > I'd like to switch to a 2.3.xx kernel on my UP2K
> > and am wondering if anyone is running one, hopefully
> > it's reasonably stable.
>
> This message comes to you from 2.3.99-pre1 on my LX and it seems happy.
>

That's good. I was thinking about the pre 2.4 series
as the one to use.

> > I've installed a Promise
> > UATA66 PCI card in it and am looking at installing
> > several large UATA66 drives. I've got 3 40GB Maxtors
> > and a 26GB Quantum for tests. One thing I noticed is
> > the Promise card doesn't work on hose 1. On hose 0,
> > it's recognized and the disks are seen.
>
> I have a Promise 66 card and found it unstable when I first tried it
> around 2.3.2x and haven't tried it since. I also found I was getting a
> higher bandwidth off my 34GB IBM using the CMD646 on the LX board than the
> Promise. Surely that is a driver bug???
> An hdparm -tT on the CMD646 has just given me 12.82MB/sec - I was getting
> about 9MB/s out of the Promise.
>

Even at 9MB/sec, that's sufficient for what I want.
The files will be passed across fast Enet so anything
close to or faster than 100Bt is fine for what I want.

Flakiness worries me. I've noticed that 2.2.14 with
IDE-DMA patches does not handle large IDE disk on the
Promise card correctly. It bails out between 8-9GB on
my tests with FS corruption. My understanding was the
2.3.xx series had better large IDE disk support. Given
an Alpha has more interupts than an x86, I'd like to
put 6-8 large IDE disks on a system for data storage
using add-on ATA66 cards.

With IBM's new 75GB GXP drive, that's up to 600GB. I
see pricing on those at around $600 at the moment. I
figure $5000 for 600GB is a good deal so I really want
this to work. The IBM's are 1 inch high drives so they
are quite dense in terms of how much you can get into
a case.

I'll download/try out the 2.3.99pre series to test with.

Thanks.

Wes
