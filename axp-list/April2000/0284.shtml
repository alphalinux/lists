On 22-Apr-00 Jeff DeMaagd wrote:

> It looks like someone cracked their site twice this week. It makes me
> wonder if there _is_ an admin, there seems to be no effort to take down the
> "I cracked this site, I wus h3r3" messages.

This leads me to not want to bother with even attempting to correct the
article. Given the way the site's set up and how it's administered, who's
going to take the article seriously anyway?

> I couldn't stand reading the article as the grammar was at least as bad as
> some posts to other lists that I am on.

The way I see it, an "article" like that should read as if the author was
submitting it to a magazine for publication. It's a little different than a
mailing list posting or a personal communication, as presumably the author has
actually invested more time than the few minutes it takes to fire off an e-mail
in the preparation of the article.

> I am curious if the writer needs to
> take an ESL course. I'm not joking, and I'm not trying to be offensive.

As someone who used to teach a freshman chemistry lab (and who had to suffer
through grading student lab reporst), I can say I've seen papers that were just
as poorly written from native English speakers. However, because of the nature
of some of the mistakes, I'm inclined to agree with you that the writer's
native language is probably not English.

> The whole page seems to be about intentionally messing up the grammar so
> that the reader has no clue what the point is.

I don't know if it's intentional, but the way the article is written is
certainly a barrier to communication. How can we possibly provide constructive
criticism if we can't figure out what the author is trying to say in the first
place?
