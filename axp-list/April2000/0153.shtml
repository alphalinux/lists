On Mon, Apr 10, 2000 at 11:58:18AM +0100, David Addison wrote:
> When I boot one of my UP2000s I get the following message displayed.
>
> Apr 8 20:20:15 linux3 kernel: Floppy drive(s): fd0 is 2.88M
>
> So is this really a 2.88MB floppy unit ?

Most likely not (see below).

> I've looked at floppy.c in the kernel and there copied the floppy_struct info
> from there into /etc/fdprm and then done;

X86 determines floppy driver density from CMOS, I believe, which
Alpha doesn't have. I also believe, that for whatever reason, early
on in porting LINUX to Alpha someone chose to hardwire the floppy
"detect" to 2.88 rather than 1.44. A bad idea, in retrospect, since
it has led to innumerable instances of these same questions over the
years... ;-}

> Do I need to buy 2.88MB floppy media for this to work ?

Most likely both a 2.88 drive and 2.88 media.
