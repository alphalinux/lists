> Why trash it? I'm using a multia for that purpose myself. Works just
> fine. Wouldn't wanna try any massive compute-bound stuff on it,
> though.

Cost of ownership, and the hassle factor. I have a house full of Intels with
 full commonality of parts, operating systems, and software. I only have
the one Alpha. Every time it's time for an OS update, it means buying two
versions. The memory is unique to that box (compared to the rest of my
inventory), the SCSI harddrives are more expensive (compared to their IDE
brethren), etc., etc., etc.. I have no realy need for a One-Of box, nor any
specific need for an Alpha.

It's really become more hassle than it's worth. I can park a much less
expensive Intel box in that spot, and it will perform the same function,
equally as well, with less cost, and hassle.

Oh.. on the inventory list, I forgot to mention that it has a SCSI CD/ROM
installed that will go with it.
