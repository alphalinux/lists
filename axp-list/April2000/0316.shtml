> I've got one running in mine (a Lite-On version), and it's working fine.

Perhaps I should try that.
Mine worked fine until I moved from RedHat 5.2 to 6.0, 6.1, and then 6.2,
so I think it is a Linux kernel problem. It's not just the Netgear
cards that cause all of my systems to crash.
I've had the same problem with an SMC tulip card,
a Linksys LNE1000TX Lite-On card,
and a 3C900.

>
> Is it possible that the bus speed jumpers could be incorrectly set?

> perhaps the jumpers are set in
> such a way that the PCI bus is being over/under clocked.

I have one 433 MHz PC164 board with this problem
that is correctly jumpered according to the manual.
The other two are 500 MHz systems. As I understand it,
the correct settings for these boards are to
have no jumpers intalled. At any rate,
other PCI cards, scsi and video, work.
