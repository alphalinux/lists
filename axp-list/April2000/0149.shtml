I don't know if yours actually is a 2.88MB drive, but I wouldn't be surprised. Digital started using them in their alpha systems back in the ev4 days.

Yes, 2.88MB floppy drives require different media to write 2.88MB disks. They are reffered to as ED, 2.88MB formated, 4.0MB unformated disks. You will probably find it difficult to find any. These were created around the time cdroms were becoming popular and before any of the zips. They were expensive. Then the software industry moved to cdroms and there was no driving force to push adoption of hardware.

If you find a source for the disks at a reasonable price, I would be interested.

Calvin.
