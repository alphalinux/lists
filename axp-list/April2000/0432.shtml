We've had Compaq fix at least 50 systems. This is the scoop we got back in
January:

---------
There have been two bugs found in the XP1000's:

1. A problem has been identified with early CPU Boards which can cause a
variety of failures on the board. The part number in question is 5425088-01
(it's the -01 that's important). The problem has been tracked to the
Motorola SRAM's used on the assembly. The fix is a no cost replacement of
the entire assembly (on failure of the original). Compaq has issued a "FCO"
("Field Change Order" I think) for this part. Compaq fieldpersonnel
reportedly know about this FCO. Care must be taken to ensure that the part
number of the replacement assembly is 5425088-03 (it's the-03 that's
important)!

    Three of the failed systems we have seen can be attributed to this
problem.

2. A problem with the Power Supply has just been identified!!! The
power supply vendor (in China) has identified an issue which can lead to
premature failure of the supply. US Immigration requires that all "natural
products" used as shipping containers be fumigated against insects & disease
prior to leaving port. It appears that instead of spraying the empty
containers, they sprayed containers already stacked with power supplies.

It appears that some of the agents used in the spray (chlorine and bromine)
have caused several of the components in the supply to breakdown and fail.
This problem has affected some 1200 supplies!!!

A potentially faulty supply can be identified by its serial number
(formatted as 2P9xxyyyyyy) The supplies affected by this include all
supplies starting with 2P913 (13th week of the 9th month).
--------

Plus an update in Feb:

-----------
the latest out of Compaq is that not all CPU modules are bad!

Now they're saying it's CPU module 54-25088-01 with date codes 911
through 918.
-----------
