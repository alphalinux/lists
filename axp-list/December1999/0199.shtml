Besides which, this old argument of "x is just as fast as Alpha" always
conveniently forgets that a) Alpha was there first, in terms of speed,
and b) Alpha isn't going to stand still, it's been in GHz testing for
some time now. So, who cares what's fastest today? It doesn't mean
a thing, unless it's kept in perspective.

Personally, I don't really give a flip about Intel architecture, either
the x86 or the LONG, LONG, LONG delayed Merced (or whatever it's being
called now). The fact of the matter is, I use Alpha because it runs the
OS's that I use for a living (mostly VMS but also Tru64 UNIX), and I have
no need or desire to run anything else, save for curiosity's sake.
