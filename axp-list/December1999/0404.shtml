--- Jay.Estabrook@compaq.com wrote:
> AFAIK, PWS cannot be upgraded to XP1000, so I
believe the "upgrade to next generation" is wrong,
unless it really means "to a 600MHz 21164 chip".<

In the months before the XP1000 was sold, there was an
offer 'order your PWS and upgrade later!' on DEC's
site. I'm not sure how 'MONET' is in the inside, but
I think the offer may have been something like you
ship them the machine and they put your parts in a new
one, but given the modular nature of the motherboard,
I wouldn't be surprised if they just swapped the
mainboard and the daughtercard.

> Something not mentioned on the auction site and
worth noting: MIATAs don't always come with BCache, in
either original or GL, and its quite an expensive
upgrade. I'd be likely to assume a GL would have
cache, but it might not.<

My system didn't come with a BCache, which was a bit
of a disappointment, as I thought they all did, the
description should have noted it IMO. The cost
depends greatly on where you buy and whether it was a
new, a 'pull' or a reconditioned part. I'm not
exactly sure what mine was, but I bought mine for
about 100$ from GLcomp.com, but it was an obsolete
part number that I was told was for all intents the
same as the current one - probably since sold out.
The 'current' 2MB part was about 250$ - and it used
the same speed and same part numbers for chips, the
4MB part was in the 1000$ neighborhood - ouch!

For CPU intensive stuff, I've noticed a consistent 25%
speed increase on nearly everything I tested, but I
haven't tested the bandwidth performance.
