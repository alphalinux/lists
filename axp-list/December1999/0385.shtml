What is it that's bothering you about the description?

If it's the builtin QLogic, you should be aware that the second generation
of MIATA, known internally as MIATA GL, does have such. And it was never
made obvious from the model numbers, AFAIK, so it all depends on when the
box was made - May 99 surely tags it as a GL...

Or is it the fact it's called a 21164 and not 21164a? You also should know
that (IIRC) we ever sold anything officially labelled a 21164a. We sold
21164 in two speed ranges, where the one going 366 or faster was known
*unofficially* as the A part.

Or is it this:

Slots: (Total 8) PCI 64-bit - 2, PCI 32-bit - 2, 1 Shared PCI/ISA - 2, EISA - 2

I believe this to be wrong; I believe MIATA (inc. GL) have 2 @ 64 and 2 @ 32,
plus 2 ISA, at least one of the ISA and 1 @ 32 PCI being shared/paired.

XP1000 (aka MONET at 500MHz, aka BRISBANE @ 667) has 2 @ 64 and 3 @ 32 plus
only a single ISA, therefore 1 shared/paired.

In addition, XP1000 has 8 DIMM slots, vs the 6 of MIATA.

They do resemble each other quite a lot, both externally and internally,
with the builtin Ethernet and sound, same style cabinet, etc. They *were*
both designed by the Workstation group, so it is understandable.

AFAIK, PWS cannot be upgraded to XP1000, so I believe the "upgrade to next
generation" is wrong, unless it really means "to a 600MHz 21164 chip".

Something not mentioned on the auction site and worth noting: MIATAs don't
always come with BCache, in either original or GL, and its quite an expensive
upgrade. I'd be likely to assume a GL would have cache, but it might not.

Forewarned is fore-armed... ;-}
