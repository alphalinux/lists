Look at the subject line (suggestions for udb) and the mention
of "excessively" wide processors (128 bits) - where did we go
so far off track!

Not to be daft, but what oh what could you *possibly* need
128 bit CPU for on a "general purpose" machine? I mean
take a word processor, it is still slinging around just 26
letters and a couple dozen punctuation marks (oh, and
ten digits), at least here in the US (doesn't everyone speak
english?)...

Now graphics, weather projections, massive spreadsheets
would benefit, I am sure.

I suspect Intel would call that the s*itanium processor, as in
a s**t load of bits! Executables would be enormous (every
instruction would be a minimum of 16 bytes wide)!

Oh well, having said that, I'm sure Compaq will announce
their new 128 bit CPU called (sorry) Beta!

<grin>
