On Thu, 16 Dec 1999, Ivan Gaboli wrote:

> Thank you for your help. I finally have obtained a bootable kernel following your
> instructions.
> Now, I'm 'fighting' against a Kernel Panic (unable to mout VFS root) during boot
> time ;)

Well, seeing as your root filesystem is probably ext2, and unless you have
compiled your own kernel without ext2 support, chances are you are passing
the kernel the wrong parameter as the root filesystem.
 
> I take this chance to post another question. I've connected to my SCSI bus, 2 new
> HD; and now, when I
> boot 'previous but working' kernel (2.2.5), system spends some time doing a "sdb:
> Spinning up disk....".
> I never seen this step before. What does it mean ?

I just means that the SCSI controller never told the disk to start, so
Linux is waiting for it to spin up. It's actually quite common on systems
where the firmware (like SRM) doesn't have a driver for the SCSI
controller.
