On Sat, 11 Dec 1999, Wes Bauske wrote:

> Jaap Schellekens wrote:
> >
> > The qlogic 1041 (this is a HDV adaptor) i have in my XL300 has lost it's
> > resitor packs used for termination. These are eight 10-pin sips. I need to
> > replace them but have no idea what type i need. All that docs from qlogic
> > state about these resistors is: 'save for later use' ;-( Obviously this
> > did not happen...
> >
> > Anybody out there with one of these boards (or a good memory) that knows
> > what type of resistor I need?
> >
>
> You could also just put a normal HVD terminator on card.
> Should be OK.

IMHO, you have to take care to insert them properly. These termination
packs consist of a 330 Ohm resistor to GND and a 220 Ohm resistor to VCC
per line. You have to take care not to insert them reversed. Also there
might be variants with other pin assignments of GND and VCC etc. I'd take
a multimeter and find the GND and VCC pins on the socket and verify that
on the term pack. (Measure from GND to an arbitrary pin should give 330
Ohm and from VCC to the same pin should give 220 Ohm).
