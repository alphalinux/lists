> > - max 2GB memory in 6 slots
> > - 8 slots - 2 64-bit PCI, 2 32-bit PCI, 2 shared, 2 EISA
> What is it that's bothering you about the description?
Mainly those two, and the combination of the specs assumed to be
all in one box.
 
> If it's the builtin QLogic, you should be aware that the second generation
> of MIATA, known internally as MIATA GL, does have such. And it was never
> made obvious from the model numbers, AFAIK, so it all depends on when the
> box was made - May 99 surely tags it as a GL...
That is interesting. I guess the product descriptions for the DPW/DUW
line have not been updated on the website? I see no mention of USB
connectors or integrated Qlogic controllers (though EIDE is mentioned).

> I believe this to be wrong; I believe MIATA (inc. GL) have 2 @ 64 and 2 @ 32,
> plus 2 ISA, at least one of the ISA and 1 @ 32 PCI being shared/paired.
My earlier 500 has one 64, one shared 64, two shared 32, and one 32.

> Something not mentioned on the auction site and worth noting: MIATAs don't
> always come with BCache, in either original or GL, and its quite an expensive
> upgrade. I'd be likely to assume a GL would have cache, but it might not.
Yeah, but tearing out the L2/L3 cache seems all the rage today....
