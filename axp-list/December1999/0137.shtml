Rich Payne wrote:
>
> On Sat, 4 Dec 1999, Andrei A. Dergatchev wrote:
>
> > I may want to tinker with *BSDs, so I'm thinking to give SRM
> > a try.
>
> So, reflash to SRM,

Although one can reflash NVRAMs quite a few times they are not meant for
constant operations of that sort and will wear out eventually. It is
possible to load a firmware from a floppy without really overwriting
NVRAMs if you just experimenting. I was doing that when playing with
SRM on LX, for example. This does not preserve valuse of SRM variables
but the same applies to reflashing.

Of course if you plan a longer stint with one kind of firmware, and
multiple reboots and powerdowns, reflash by all means. You still
likely have a few thousands of cycles. :-)
