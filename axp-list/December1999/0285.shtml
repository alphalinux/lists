> > Smith and Waterman search of Swissprot 37 database using a 277 residue
> > protein sequence.
> >
> > Intel Celeron 400 - 822 seconds == 9 million cell updates/sec
> > Alpha 164LX 533 - 316 seconds == 25 million cell updates/sec
> > Alpha 264EV67 667 - 164 seconds == 46 million cell updates/sec

> Have you tried the latest of the Intels (733 Mhz) in your tests? I would

I don't have access to anything faster than the 400, we stopped buying
Intel boxes a long time ago now and standardised on Alphas. I would
expect the performance to be approximately double that of the 400 celeron
though which still puts it short of the 533LX and way short of the EV67
chips.

But, as I said, for our work we can get the Alpha to perform 64 bit tricks
which puts the performance up by 20x the 400 celeron for a 533LX box.
