I have had success using dhcpd version 3 from ISC. I was able to
configure it to do dhcpd boot from an Intel ethernet card and windows
picked it up, no problems. The trick is getting it to recognize the
dhcp request, then do the bootp.

Try looking at
http://www.tux.org/pub/net/isc/dhcp/DHCPD-history/

I used the dhcpd-3.0b1pl12, but any of the other 3.0 versions should
work fine. No rpm, but it compiled fine right out of the tarball. The
docs you need will depend on your NIC.

Good luck!

- Tereknor
