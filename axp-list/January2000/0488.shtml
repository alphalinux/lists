On Sun, 23 Jan 2000 plr@interlog.com wrote:
> ida:/disk/hda4# LCA machine check: vector=0x630 pc=0x2000000d140 code=0x86
> Reason: correctable ECC error (short frame, retryable, dc_stat=0x0):
> Correctable read error to memory occurred at address 123ea98

it's not that serious. just a warning message that a single bit was in
error, but the partity bits corrected it. if it never happens again, i'd
just ignore it. it could be a sign that your RAM is starting to go. or
(as per the recent discussion here) it could be a sign that you have a bad
electrical contact between the SIMM pins and the slot leads (due to
corrosion, dust, or whatever). or it could be "photon emissions" ;-)

in any case, i wouldnt go on a wild goose chase without it being a
recurring phenomon.

good luck,

 -ross
