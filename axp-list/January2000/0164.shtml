On Tue, 11 Jan 2000, Paul B. Brown wrote:

>
> Old and new DEC/Compaq and SGI hardware are excellent but they were not
> marketed well. They niched themselves instead of going for a wider
> appeal. Intel, with IBM's and Micro$loft's help, became the "chip for
> the corporate world".

I'm not sure I would like to see workstation-class hardware (read: SGI,
DEC/Compaq, Sun) "going for a wider appeal."

Workstation users, by and large, have money to spend -- and the
performance and price tag of these machines reflect that.

Expand into the corporate market, and you have to compete with
IBM, Dell and the like. Doable.

If you want to push further, perhaps into the home market, or especially
into schools .. you have to start making boxes _cheap_ .. real cheap.
Just like we have "generic" shit box Intel motherboards with buggy
chipsets, we'd start seeing that sort of thing coming out of workstation
makers.

> Apple just doesn't seem to get it. Tangerine and Blueberry iMacs? Good
> God! ... Stop with the cute cases and spiffy colors. Greys and tans
> are just fine. Personally, the color of the case is not going to sway
> me one way or another.

iMac's aren't aimed at companies, they are aimed at individuals. Every
case that I own is either airbrushed with funky designs, or covered with
stickers and logos; or both. Corporate beige sucks. And, who the hell
wants a big beige box in their home. Home users need a machine that
doesn't look garish in their living room -- now whether a tangerine
iMac fits the bill or not is a matter of taste -- but I'm not in a
position to judge; like I say stickers and airbrushed madness mark my
machines.

Alone, the look of the machine won't sway me: thats where my girlfriend's
airbrush and talent come in; but the look of a machine would play
a roll if I was on the fence between one or more machines.

Everyone I know who isn't a computer geek who has a computer do one of
two things with it: stick it in an out of the way room, or put it in
one of those folding computer cabinets so you can't see it when not in
use; because computers look stupid.

Most people wouldn't leave a cardboard box filled with books sitting
in their living room; even if they love the books -- put whatever you
like inside the box, and when you look at it, you still want to get it
out of the room! Computers are the same way, and they even LOOK like
cardboard boxes... with a few notable exceptions; the iMac being one.

Personally, I don't like the look of the iMac much -- but I'm still
thinking of picking one up and throwing Yellow Dog Linux on it :)

> Does anyone know what company pumped 200 million dollars into Apple
> recently? Answer: MicroSloth! No prizes for figuring out why! :-)

Hmm.. that is interesting.
