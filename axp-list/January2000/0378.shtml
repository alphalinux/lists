Doing an isacfg for your soundcard in SRM, to my knowledge, makes absolutely
no difference since Linux doesn't use SRM to find out your soundcard settings.
To this day, I have a rock solid system using 2.2.9 and a SB16, with the
driver compiled into the kernel (*not* modules).
You have to either use a DOS machine to set the soundcard to known values,
or monkey with isapnp in linux to get known settings to stick.
Please try kernel 2.2.9 and see if things are more stable. Remember, don't
do sound as modules, and enable persistent DMA in the .config.
