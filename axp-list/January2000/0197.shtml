I used to work in data center operations at a major oil company, and I
once had a meeting with a very intersting man - a Technology Specialist
who was responsible for deciding things like "How do we save all these
documents for 25+ years" without turning a small state into a warehouse
facility? He was concerned with:

    Media life (how long will the tape/CD/optical disk,etc. be usable?)

    Availability of suitable readers (CD ROM drives in 20 years? How
    about a papertape reader for data from the 60's? And those funky
    optical disks from the early 80's?)

    Availability of software to read the media and translate it to current
    machine?

Ask yourself this - how many corporations have warehouses full of
long-term archives stored on reel tapes, yet no longer have suitable drives
to recover the data from? I came to find out from my friend that the
US Gov't. has a "museum" of sorts full of old tape readers for just such
an incident...

Neat stuff, glad it isn't my problem...
