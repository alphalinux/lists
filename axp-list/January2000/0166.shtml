> Hummm . . . maybe but we will have to overcome the high level of inertia
> of X11. I'm not sure that X11 can do what is required to bring a GUI
> Desktop that the novice can use. Remember the UNIX tenets stated in "The
> UNIX Philosophy". UNIX is not easy to use because it can do so many
> things and is very efficient. They technical level of UNIX users is
> higher, by and large, than their Windows/MacOS counterparts. Get the book
> and read it. I think there is a new edition of the book out that has soom
> updates expounding upon more current events.

I agree. After reading this book it suddenly became quite clear what the
differences are between Windows/Mac and *NIX. After I installed RedHat
6.0 on my Cabriolet and Intel boxes I thought to myself.....'I think we
are loosing site of the tenets'. (I did opt for some major bells and
whistles too.)

I can see where development of games, browsers, and other 'goodies' will
benefit the overall in the future. Don't rule them out, keep them as a
choice.

As long as those interested in slim efficient boxen have a choice I
don't have an issue. When it becomes necessary to use KDE or other large
GUI's I probably will take issue. When I have to add 25% more RAM to run
the OS I'll probably take issue.

If we are not careful we'll find ourselves duplicating what MS has done.
A very large inefficient OS.

$0.02 from a hobbyist -with flamesuit on- point of view
