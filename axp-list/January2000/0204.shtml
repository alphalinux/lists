Ed Hall wrote:
>
> This could be a Joliet
> disk (WinNT's extended ISO9660 format) or a disk mounted with a Rock
> Ridge-compatible WinNT filesystem utility, or a Linux utility that
> can write ISO9660 with Joliet extensions.

FYI - 'mkisofs', which runs on many Unix and Linux systems, and is
included in RH 6.1 distribution (not the current version, but still...),
will write, when asked, a CD with both Rock Ridge and Joliet extensions.
It will even hide names like TRANSL.TBL from Joliet not to confuse it
too much.

The catch is that this installation disk is full of symbolic links. It
is hard to avoid them if a size of this image for 6.1 is 646MB and a
full capacity of a CD media is 650MB. NT, and Joliet, have no idea what
such thing as a link can be. This may be not so important as these
links are required when you boot from that CD and you may possibly get
away without them when this is simply a supply of rpm archives to
install. OTOH NT mangles names throwing into names upper and lower
letters on a whim but installer depends on a specific set of file names
- case sensitive. "Stealing" from somewhere a CD reader, even rigged
with a baling wire for an installation time only, may simplify your
life to a great extent.
