> [.....]
> The more disturbing is a general trend in Linux installation utilities
> for better and better hiding tools which you may need when things will
> stray from a prescribed scenario. They are still there but if you do
> not know where to look then you may find out yourself facing greyed out
> both "Next" and "Previous" arrows and now what? This is not a property
> of a particular platform or distribution. Yesterday "Linux Today" had a
> link to a review in which its author was smitten by an installation
> utility from Mandrake 7.0. A comment from a reader pointed out that on
> his machine an attempt to use this installer become a minor nightmare, a
> big headache and a huge screwup. YYMV.

IMHO nobody, except a newbie to Linux/Unix, is *really* pleased by
a GUI-based installation. The other day, I installed NetBSD on my
Alpha and liked their attitude of hiding nothing behind a fancy
installer. If I were to put something on a "todo" wishlist, it would
be an "expert type" installation, which offers partitioning the
bootdrive, put bootstuff om place and dump the minimal system onto
it from an ordinary tarball. For the individual add-on package selection,
a package manager like 'rpm' or 'dpkg' is ok.
I once did a minimal installer for RH.41 doing the above and I am *very*
tempted doing it again for the 6.x series.

Also, we have to face the fact, that Alpha users are a tiny minority
compared to the x86 world. Look at the docs of RedHat 6.1-Alpha
and judge by yourself how much it would make sense to you if it
would be your first time doing an install on an Alpha. With things
being scattered all over places and the general attitude of "shoot first -
RTFM later" ( which I share, honestly :-) you are almost guaranteed
to loose.
I know, there is excellent doc in various places ( SRM howto, Milo Howto,
mailing-lists like this one, www.alphalinux.com ) but somehow most of
the Redhat's, SuSe's and Debian's of this world fail to compile them
into their Docs in a convenient way.

Don't get me wrong, I love Linux and what the Distributions have done
to get it onto your machine - but sometimes I think they are "fixing"
the wrong problem with the GUI stuff.

Just my $ 0.02.
