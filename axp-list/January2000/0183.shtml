> I agree. After reading this book it suddenly became quite clear what
> the differences are between Windows/Mac and *NIX. After I installed
> RedHat 6.0 on my Cabriolet and Intel boxes I thought to myself.....'I
> think we are loosing site of the tenets'. (I did opt for some major
> bells and whistles too.)

I know what you mean. I see that as well but bloatware is going to
happen since Linux has hit the main stream. However, given the way
UNIX is designed, you can strip out the parts you don't want so it's OK.

> I can see where development of games, browsers, and other 'goodies'
> will benefit the overall in the future. Don't rule them out, keep
> them as a choice.

Yep.

> As long as those interested in slim efficient boxes have a choice I
> don't have an issue. When it becomes necessary to use KDE or other
> large GUI's I probably will take issue. When I have to add 25% more
> RAM to run the OS I'll probably take issue.

I agree completely. Once the GUI cannot be separated from the OS, I'll be
looking for another OS myself.

> If we are not careful we'll find ourselves duplicating what MS has
> done. A very large inefficient OS.

I think that it will not happen simply because of the discussion we and
others have about this very issue. I think "The UNIX Philosophy" is
correct and should be adhered to. I believe the core Linux designers
believe it too.

> $0.02 from a hobbyist -with flamesuit on- point of view

HA! No flamethrower here! See ya!
