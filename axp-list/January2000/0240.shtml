Small point for clarity. You may use an LVD device with an non-LVD
controller (or vice versa) -- however, they use completely different
electrical signalling which are *not* compatible. According to the LVD
spec, if *any* device/controller on a bus detects that a single non-LVD
device is present, the whole bus and all devices revert to non-LVD
signalling. So, if you have 5 LVD disks on an LVD controller, adding a
normal SCSI CD-ROM will disable LVD on the whole bus for all devices and
revert to standard SCSI transfer rates, and more importantly the cabling
limitations.

Rotational speed (7200 rpm) is only one factor of a hard disk's overall
performance. More important is the media recording density, which will be
very high on a 50GB device. Also important is the speed and size, and
algorithms used of the onboard cache.
