"Frasnelli, Dan" wrote:

> > Digipaq has maintained for quite some time that systems
> > with more than a few processor do not scale well. IIRC
> > the sweet spot mentioned was 4-6 processors. Above that
> > the increase in performance per processor decreases.
>
> A more reasonable estimate hovers closer to 4 when discussing
> 2.2.x. In my opinion, one or more of these hot-to-trot
> big iron houses (IBM, SGI, Sun) needs to put more actions to
> their words and contribute some useful code in the way of
> scalability. Chances are good none of them will, as
> highly-scalable systems running commercial Un*x implementations
> are their honeypot du jour.
>

<preface>I don't want to start any pissing contests here.</preface>

I don't know about everyone else, but I do know that we (Compaq)
certainly qualify as a "big iron house" and there are quite a few people
here that are working directly on overall performance and scalability.
I've seen Alan Cox mention that IBM has released at least one
scalability-related patch (probably more), and SGI is all over the place
with performance and scalability efforts (don't have personal knowledge
about Sun).

Problem is, we're probably hiding the fact that we're doing this XYZ
performance work on behalf of ABC company, either by accident or not.
While I'm not allowed to talk officially for Compaq (i.e. disclaimer ;)
I can say that there is active work going on - that's why I bothered to
port the lockmetering code from SGI over to Alpha.

So, what I'd say is: Is it happening? Yes. Is it happening fast
enough? Probably not. (MHO, of course). And will it support Wildfire?
Couldn't tell you anyway ;)
