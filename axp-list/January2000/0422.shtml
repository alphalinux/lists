>>> "Hoover, Tony" said:
>
> play with your SCSI IDs. The lowest SCSI ID (hard drive) your host adapter
> finds will be sda, the next will be sdb ...

This is true *only* on the first adapter whose disks get probed.

The original problem is that there are now *two* adapters, and the
non-embedded one is the one whose disks get probed first.

Try adding "pci=reverse" to the bootcommand arguments, but be aware that
this will *only* work if the two adapters are the same make, ie both Adaptec
or both Symbios or both Qlogic. If they are mixed, it is the builtin
ordering of SCSI adapter probing that will be the determining factor, and
given an embedded controller, nearly impossible to override without
modifying the source, IIRC.

The original post doesn't say what make the embedded adapter is, but given
that it is anything except Adaptec, the Adaptec will *always* be probed for
first, because of the builting ordering within the SCSI subsystem code. At
least that's how I remember the ordering from when last I looked; for the
one you are using, examine drivers/scsi/hosts.c and look at the order of
the "makes" in the builtin_scsi_hosts[] array. In 2.2.14 it looks like
the following, for the more common ones found in Alphas:

        BusLogic
        Adaptec
        QLogic
        Symbios (sym53c8xx)
        NCR (ncr53c8xx)

So, if the embedded is NCR, and the added is Adaptec, bad news... :-\

Good luck.
