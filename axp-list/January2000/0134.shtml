"Frasnelli, Dan" wrote:

> > I don't know about everyone else, but I do know that we (Compaq)
> > certainly qualify as a "big iron house" and there are quite a few people
> > here that are working directly on overall performance and scalability.
> > I've seen Alan Cox mention that IBM has released at least one
> > scalability-related patch (probably more), and SGI is all over the place
> > with performance and scalability efforts (don't have personal knowledge
> > about Sun).
>
> Good, I knew there was at least one way to raise a response from the
> peanut gallery.

Hey - I resemble that remark! ;)

> Implementing mpp support (the right way, the first
> time around) is some tricky voodoo, which is why I keep my paws
> out of that corner of the kernel ;-)

Eh, not really voodoo - it just takes a lot of thought, and even more good
design. The more that I work with both Tru64 and Linux, the more I see good
ideas and bad ideas, and good ideas gone bad. Linux is set up pretty well,
but there are still a good sum of areas where we assume that we're running on
a small PC with one user in front of it, or maybe a small workgroup server.
We're getting there, tho'. :)

> Seriously though.. it would be
> nice to see some of the code that make Solaris and Irix scale well
> (can't speak from personal experience with Tru64) across a perverse number
> of processors integrated into Linux. In that respect it would be better
> to have a surplus of available code than a dearth.
>

Yup. Can't say I could agree with anything more than that paragraph.

>
> > So, what I'd say is: Is it happening? Yes. Is it happening fast
> > enough? Probably not. (MHO, of course). And will it support Wildfire?
>
> Its interesting to watch these companies expand the
> population of ./arch/ by porting the kernel to "unique"
> architectures (S/390), but much more interesting to see
> current development for new and upcoming products - as far
> as I know, Compaq stands alone in that regard and should be
> applauded.
>

Glad to hear it (and so are the three "J"s, I'm sure :). Hopefully we'll be
providing some of that surplus of code soon enough...:)
