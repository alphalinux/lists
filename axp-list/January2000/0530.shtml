> Try to kill and restart your gpm daemon. It could be done in your
> local startup after everything else. I do not know if this will
> help but I have seen something like that on some laptops and doing
> that resolves the problem.
>
> There is somewhere a kernel patch which makes that unnecessary but
> I do not remember now where and I think that it relies on some
> x86 BIOS voodoo.
>
> Also running gpm in a "repeater" mode ('MOUSETYPE="PS/2 -R"', or
> similar, in your /etc/sysconfig/mouse) helps sometimes in a case
> of conflicts. But if you will do that set in your XF86Config
> Device "/dev/gpmdata"
> Protocol "MouseSystems"
> as this is the protocol you will get out of the repeater - does not
> matter how your mouse is talking originally.
