Peter Petrakis <ppetrakis@firestorm.tzo.org> writes:

> Ok. Sound is killing my machine on a regular basis.I've tried
> compilng in the sound drivers into the kernel (2.2.14 now) but the
> isapnp tools init "after" sound is started in the kernel so it can't
> find my card. BTW my card is SB64 isa.
>
> Maybe I don't have my card configured correctly in SRM? I ommited
> any reference to DMA when I set it up. besides that I can't think of
> any other reason why my sound card decides to crash my system. I ran
> it for over a year with AlphaBIOS/milo using modules and it was rock
> solid. I switch to SRM and upgrade from 6.0 --> 6.1 and now it kills
> it constantly. Thanks guys.

I had the same problem with a 164LX (SRM, RH6.1, 2.2.13-0.9) and ISA SB16
Vibra. With in minutes of using the sound-card (just loading the
modules was fine) the machine locks up completely, couldn't even ping
it.

Today I installed ALSA 0.4.1i and sofar everything works fine. Playing
mp3s for over two hours now.

I had one or two minor problem installing ALSA. There was a
#include <linux/delay.h>
missing in support/isapnp.c resulting in unresolved symbols in
isapnp.o and the modules got installed in /lib/modules/2.2.9. No big
deal. I haven't checked 0.5.0pre3 yet.
