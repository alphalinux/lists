> You catch a trend here - the *only* reason, the average user
> is buying a faster x86 machine is for games. No one with some
> brains would claim, that you need a 30+ SPECint95 800Mhz
> Athlon to run Word or Excel.

This is true but I see the market changing. the WinTel workstation is
still, and probably will continue to be, the corporate desktop. A WinTel
server will probably be the back office (File and Print) server. However,
Intel is now pushing hard into the mid-tier server area, such as Internet
and enterprise servers. They *are* making a dent. This is why companies
like Compaq with it's Alpha, CGI with is Mips, and Sun with it's
UltraSPARC better think hard and come up with a strategy that can keep the
LinTel market from becoming a threat to their market share.

> True, marketing is a *weird* thing. Wrong, I'm not into it ;-)
> But it sure makes sense demoing such a beast on trade shows.

Marketing is perception. It's a message that tells you how to solve a
problem. You want to look younger, ladies? Use Pond's Cold Cream. You
want to get rid of that dandruff, use Head and Shoulders. Want firm abs,
use the Nordatrack. Want inexpensive servers with a long life, use
LinTel.

How would you sell LinTel to a corporation?
