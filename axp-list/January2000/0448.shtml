Eric Jones wrote:
>
> Okay. I have a pc164 linux box here, with an isa ne2000 card, a sb16
> isa card, and a few other pci cards. In other words, all of my isa slots
> are taken up. I have only one slot left, a pci.
> The problem is this. I want to set up masquerading on this box so when I
> go adsl in a week, the machines on my lan can get out. That means that I
> need to have two ethernet cards in here, but I've only got the one is
> ne2000.
> So. I'm at an empasse. Do I chunk a soundblaster 16 card that works
> flawlessly in this box for a cheap pci sound card and install an old isa
> SMC ethernet card in the slot that I free up, or do I go out and buy a pci
> ethernet card?
> I would prefer to conform to the analogy "if it ain't broke, don't fix
> it."
> My question : what good, _cheap_ (cheap = under $50) pci ethernet cards
> are out there that will work on alphas? I don't need a swiss army card.
> This is just a cheesy home lan that has an ancient mac, this box, and an
> ancient r6k on it.
> Failing that, there are loads of super super cheap sound cards out
> there. This sb16 is about 5 years old. What chipsets on pci sound cards
> work?
>

Buy a Netgear FA310tx and install the latest Tulip driver
from Becker's site and you should be OK. They typically
go for $20-$25 around here. There are other noname type
cards in the same price range but the chipsets vary alot.
I've seen the rtl8139/via-rhine/winbond/MX. Someone else
should know if they work. I believe a via-rhine will but
when I bought the card, the driver was still flakey. The
MX chipsets should work with Tulip. Anyone seen an MX chipset
that doesn't work?
