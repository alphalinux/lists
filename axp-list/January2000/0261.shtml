With regards to your message at 11:03 AM 1/13/00 -0600, Wes Bauske. Where
you stated:
>Rohan Clarke wrote:
> >
> > Seagate has a technology paper on LVD here:
> > http://www.seagate.com/support/kb/disc/ultra2.html
> >
> > No problems here wi/ using up to 36Gb.
> > 50Gb is a lot of data on a single device though...
> > have you pondered maybe using a RAID?
> >
>
>Depending on what you're doing, RAID is probably not
>worth bothering with unless it's HW. The 50GB Seagate
>is capable of around 23MB/sec on it's own for sequential
>reads.
Not to quibble too much, but the 50GB Seagate is a 7200 rpm drive, and can
do sustained read or write at about 16MB/sec.

>Also, the cost per MB is the cheapest I've seen
>for SCSI. I have 2 50GB and 2 47GB Seagates on my XP1000
>for data storage. Works fine. The 47GB run about half the
>speed of the 50GB but then they're full height vs half
>height on the 50GB so the 50GB have about twice the density
>of the 47GB.
>
>Current Pricewatch lists 50GB Seagates at around $850.
>36GB are running $750. The only reason to consider 36GB
>drives would be 10K rpm ones but they're pricey. I'm
>waiting to see what the coming 72GB drives are priced at.

True. The Seagate 50GB is about $21/GB USD$
The 36GB 10,000 rpm drives are about $29/GB or 50% more per GB.

The new IBM 70GB 10K is out in the real world distribution end of January,
at about $24-$25/GB

It is a 1" tall drive, so density is nice.

http://www.storage.ibm.com/hardsoft/diskdrdl/prod/us36lzx72zx.htm
