>>> Matthew Jacob said:
> On Wed, 19 Jan 2000, Jon Brinkmann wrote:
>
> > I'm attempting to determine the characteristics of the onboard IDE
> > interface (a National PC87312) on my EB164. Neither the EB164 manual
> > nor the PC87312 data sheet go into the characteristics of the
> > interface. I think this interface chip was designed back in the days
> > when there was only on flavor of IDE.
>
> It's a CMD646 chip, and while I haven't tried the Maxtor, I did try an
> equivalent IBM with good results.

The IDE on EB164 was *not* a CMD646 chip, it was part of the 87312
(so-called) Super I/O chip.

I believe that the PC164 was the first to have the CMD646, and the LX164 had
it also, but the SX164 does not (it uses the IDE part of the Cypress 693).

You might be able to do more than PIO on the 87312 but I don't know if
anyone ever bothered to do such a driver under LINUX.

There was some recent talk on this list of using a PCI IDE card (Promise?)
to run the UDMA 33/66 disks, but I don't know what the outcome was...
