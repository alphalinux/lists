In fact LVD needs twisted pair a *lot* less because the electrical
signalling method used in LVD is robust and not prone to interference (hence
the long cable limits possible with Ultra2/LVD). Using SCSI cables with
groups of twisted pairs reduces the potential for interference, and
therefore SCSI buses being run at the limit, eg Ultra-SE would benefit the
most from running the highest quality cables available. In my 14 years
worth of experience with SCSI, I have never found better SCSI cables and
terminators than those available at http://www.scsipro.com (Granite
Digital). Their prices may need you to consider a 2nd mortgage as they are
expenisive, but their quality indicates that they are not overpriced.
