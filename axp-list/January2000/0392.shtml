On Wed, 19 Jan 2000 Jay.Estabrook@compaq.com wrote:

>There was some recent talk on this list of using a PCI IDE card (Promise?)
>to run the UDMA 33/66 disks, but I don't know what the outcome was...

FWIW, I tried a Promise Ultra/66 card and IBM 18GB Ultra/66 drive in my
XL300, then in my PC164. Neither machine provided a bios implementation
that the promise card was happy with - the adapter failed to initialize.

As an aside, be forewarned about using Ultra/66 drives with the CMD646. I
installed and booted RH6.1 fine with the native controller on the PC164
(CMD646) and the IBM 18GB drive. However, over time there was data
corruption, and after a few days, a totally unusable system.

I wound up putting the controller and drive in an old Intel SuSE box, and
NFS mounting it. Sigh.
