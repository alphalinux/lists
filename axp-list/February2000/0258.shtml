On Tue, Feb 15, 2000 at 08:18:48AM -0500, Larry Sendlosky wrote:
> I can maybe save you some time. We've done some benchmarking
> of comparable Linux/Intel and Linux/Alpha webservers and the bottom
> line is they're about the same.
>

Larry's right, of course, because webserving is not normally
constrained by processor speed, but by I/O, so in general you need to
investigate faster disks (scsi, raid etc) more than faster processors.
YMMV depending on how slow and processor-intensive any cgis are, and
how many requests need to be served by cgi.

Busmastering I/O such as network cards and scsi cards often make a big
difference. If you don't have these, your processor spends a lot of
time copying data around.

Its probably worth adding that in real usage it depends enormously on
the upstream bandwidth and routing, the % of dynamic content served,
how fast/slow these cgis are, how "sticky" your web clients are, how
your webserver is configured and most importantly of all, how fast
your I/O subsystem is.

Benchmarks don't tend to elucidate this stuff very much in my
experience.
