Actually, isn't this just a numbers game -

Think of it this way - how many Suns are there per each Alpha box?
Is the ratio 10 to 1 or is it more likely to be 100 to 1? That in itself
could emplain the
attitude seen in point #2. Here also note poiint #5 - you have over 1K Sun
boxes.
How many Alpha's?

It's all really quite sad - Digital and now Compaq seem unwilling or
incapable
of effectively marketing the Alpha chip. There is nothing that either of us
can do
about that - it simply is a fact. Linux is that only thing out there that
can save
this chip and its even got problems. The cost differential for a Linux Alpha

system vs. a Linux Intel ( or AMD) is such that it's putting the boxes out of
reach
for anybody but hardcore number crunchers. So, it won't exist in volume
numbers
in the community so fewer developers will work on it so fewer things will run
on it
so - so - so

In the end it spells bad news for the Alpha processor.

Unless something changes, like the ability to put together an Alpha system
for less than 1K, my guess is that it doesn't have a bright future.

My guess is that Alpha's long term future is to server as a great case
study about how good or even great technology is killed by bad
marketing and management.
