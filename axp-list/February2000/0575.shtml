On Mon, 28 Feb 2000, Joseph Ashwood recommended:

} I had a similar problem with a machine a couple years back, after
} several months of searching, I found that the memory had a tiny
} glitch. Try shifting around your memory modules to see if the behavior
} changes, you should be able to move the behavior from the location it
} is now to another program on your system, if this happens then your
} RAM is at fault.

Hmmm...this is interesting. This could be the problem, as when the machin
only had 24MB of RAM, I didn't notice the problem [but then I was rarely
away from the machine]. I recently put two 64MB SIMMs in, so I'll swap
them and see if that helps [the 24MB is out, due to those SIMMs being
70ns, and the 128MB being 60ns].

FWIW, this happens even when the box is at idle [nearly all services
disabled, and not in X].

Could this be sound-related? I know I have a lot of problems due to my
sound being foobar'd, but I haven't found the time to recompile the kernel
just so I can play an mp3 [amazingly, I can hear my CDs, as the built in
sound card has an output that goes directly to the CDR apparently].

Thanks for the tip, Joe - you may have saved me months of research. :)
