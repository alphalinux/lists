> I, for one, came from a recently cited here "hardcode number
> crunching" group who went with Alpha because of its unbeaten
> FP performance. We know programming and axp specifics
> down to axp asm, but don't know the kernel (or other packages
> which need help for that matter) :-(

If you've got Alpha asm experience, I think the projects that could
make the most use of your skills are XFree86 and Mesa. Performance
has been boosted significantly in these projects due to optimized
x86 code. Alpha has the advantage of 64-bits for higher pixel
bandwidth and MVI instructions which are better than most of the
video extension instructions of other architectures. There is a nice
introduction to MVI programming on www.alphalinux.org. That coupled
with a peak at the x86 code in Mesa could get you a long way towards
coding the equivalent for Alpha.
