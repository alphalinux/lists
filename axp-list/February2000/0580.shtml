All;
        I had an unfortunate occurance with xscreensaver that I thought deserved a
warning.

        Tonight, my PC was running unattended, and as always, xscreensaver had
kicked in. I was downstairs watching a movie, and my wife was in the Office
working on her PC. When she turned around and looked at mine, there was
pornographic material on the screen. (I'm very glad my young daughters were
not in the room at the time!!!!

        She asked me about it later, and I had no idea where it came from. A
little investigation turned up the culprit. There is a module to
xscreensaver called "webcollage". What it does is go out onto the web (on
it's own!!!!), and collects "random images" that it finds there, and puts
them on the screen! (does anyone else see the danger here?!?!?).

        I've disabled this particular hack from my system, and I would suggest
that any of you who have a system running in a situation that would be
sensitive to this sort of thing do the same! (do you want your boss walking
by your PC with that on the screen? How about your 8 year old daughter?

        This is putting aside the obvious dangers of having your PC "roam the
internet" unattended, looking for material to put on the screen!!!!!

        I'll forgo writing a pointed letter to the creators of this hydra. They
are on these lists. I hope that they will take heed, and take that hack out
of future releases. I personally don't need something like that!

                Ric Tibbetts
