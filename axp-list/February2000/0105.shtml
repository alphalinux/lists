I have an AS200 that's soon to be physically away from here. I left
the vid card in it, but otherwise it's just on the net w/ no kb and
no monitor. It is ARC-ish. It gripes as you describe at bootup,
and when MILO runs. The boot info still ends up in messages, and
it comes up just fine (you have to pass the boot instructions from
ARC to MILO, but that's what the options field is for). If it acts
like mine, you probably don't even need a console on it. Here at home,
I use a Multia for passing packets (f/w, ppp stuff, etc). It's been
headless since it went in. If it chokes, I power-cycle it. So far,
I haven't needed to drag a monitor/kb to it. The ppp goes through the
2nd serial. A modem on the first one kept it from booting.
HTH,
