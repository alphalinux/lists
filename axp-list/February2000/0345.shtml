"Hoover, Tony" wrote:
>
> Used Alpha hardware is reasonable ( usually, check e-bay ), but I agree, new
> hardware IS out of reach of most users.

My first Intel 386 box cost me $6K 14 years ago. $3K is cheap
for the amount of FP performance you get in an Alpha.

Heck, I thought the 386 was great because it was almost as fast
as the mainframe I was using at work at the time...

I'd say relatively speaking though, compared to x86 HW, you
are correct. It's much cheaper to go with AMD or Intel and
get usable performance. I'd guess my current AMD Athlon is
probably 30-40 times faster than that 386, for maybe $700 in
parts. It's not in the same class as the 21264 though for FP.

Only HP/IBM/Alpha are at the high end of FP performance
at the moment. Alphas are a bargain compared to it's peers.
