Frasnelli, Dan (dfrasnel@alphalinux.org) wrote:

> It sits in his garage collecting termites last I heard.
> Both Alan and Linus rely on others in the community to test pre-patches
> on a variety of platforms.

> Heck, I could have told Compaq that Alan has no need for an Alpha
> before they shipped it. I offered him one in late '98, and
> his response was along the lines of "it would collect dust".

This is sad, that's two systems that could have been sent to some
useful people!

> Of all the BSD's, FreeBSD has the greatest chance of 'surviving'
> in my opinion (no BSD vs. BSD flames, please) and ensuring strong
> Alpha support should be one of our concerns.

I agree completely. OpenBSD's focus is on security. NetBSD's is to
support every possible arch. FreeBSD's is stability and speed. Of these
three FreeBSD has the largest userbase, the most developers, most
applications, and the largest userbase.

I have not checked on the status of FreeBSD on Alpha lately, but last
time I did it was moving along nicely. FreeBSD on Alpha should be very
helpful. Many orginizations use FreeBSD over Linux for the better
Network support (no flames please!), especially ISP's. As with us Linux
users, some FreeBSD users don't care for the x86 arch. Having a full
blown port of FreeBSD to Alpha might give some of those users the needed
insentive to switch.

It would be in Compaq's (and our) best interest to have binairy
compatibility between Tru64 UNIX, Linux, and FreeBSD. Compaq should put
that toword the top of their to do list.

> Beg to differ. I've taken up more than a few bytes explaining simple
> topics in private, and certainly others here have.

I do it all the time, my point was there are certian people who are a
little on the jerk side of things. It's not something specific to this
group, you'll find one everywhere.

> If the penguin64.org site didn't get your blood boiling hot enough,
> visit linux64.org. Its a redirector to a Tru64 advocacy site. Heh.

Well at least it is pro Alpha, unlike penguin64.org!

IMO,
