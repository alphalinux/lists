> Speaking of secure shell, what differences are there between SSH and
> SSH2? Or if you have a url handy explaining this, that'd be ok.

Are you talking about differences in the protocols or the implementations
from Data Fellows? From an application standpoint, ssh2 is designed
to be more modular than ssh1; what most people refer to as 'ssh' is
just one application using libssh in ssh2. I've worked with the
free implementations, the the commercial implementations (1.3.x and 2.0.x)
and everything in between. Let me know exactly what you want to know, and
a better answer can be provided.
