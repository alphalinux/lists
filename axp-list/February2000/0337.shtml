> organized group working on the Linux Alpha kernel. I have absolutely
> no idea who the main contact(s) are for submitting patches or how to
> find patches for the latest kernels in regards to the Alpha platform.
> It always seems to lag behind the Intel code by several versions. In
> this regards, I can see where people would want to throw up their arms

Right. Most of my frustration comes from the mainstream developers
trying to force x86isms into what should be platform-independent
areas of the code; for example, the first few rounds of the
memchr() implementation were hardcoded x86 asm in ./mm/slab.c.
Had to code an axp asm equivalent (stuck in ./arch/alpha/lib/,
 thank you very much) before the C implementation.

As far as people working on the kernel... here's who I know of over the
past year.. not an official list, just what I can think up right now):
Richard Henderson 2.3 fixes
Jay Estabrook 2.3 new arch support
Andrea Arcangeli -aa branch, bigmem, misc. on alpha
Me 2.2.x-ac fixes, -sec branch
A cast of hundreds? Many unattributed bugfixes

Don't send Alpha patches to Linus or Alan Cox. They do not have the
time nor do they care much for the Alpha. Think we discussed this
on the list a couple weeks ago..

> On the other hand, API seems to be trying real hard to get things
> moving. They seem like a likely candidate for throwing a little more
> resources and/or effort into organizing things better. Maybe setting
> up a sourceforge account for Alpha Linux related development would be
> good. Along with encouraging Alpha developers to post their patches
> with a core set of people who maintain the CVS tree.

Yeah, organization is indeed lacking. We - both Alpha Linux Organization
 and the community we represent - need more hands and eyes to help out.
Personally, I am exceedingly busy with work that I don't have time
to code/test kernels/write articles on alphalinux/etc. Today's an
exception.. slow day.
