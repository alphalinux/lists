I've enjoyed reading the lastest couple threads about Linux/Alpha,
Compaq, kernel hacking, etc. I'd like to make a few comments.

First, the only people in Compaq who read this list are engineers.
And, most of these engineers share the some of the same frustrations you do
concerning Compaq and Linux/Alpha. We as engineers can not really do much
about some things that could change things for the better. It's public
knowledge that Compaq uses 'first.last@compaq.com' for mail. If you
want to be heard by somebody who may be able to do something... well
with a small amount of effort you can figure out the rest...... Maybe
you can be the first to tell somebody there is something called an
Alpha and it can run Linux. (I hope it's not that bad).

Concerning us supporting people with hardware:

In the last 6 months or so, my group, not Compaq as whole, have
sent

  1) DS20 to Andrea Arcangeli
  2) DS20 to FreeBSD (forget the person's name)
  3) Alphasystem (not sure of the model) to OpenBSD
  4) 2 DS10s for RtLinux development
  5) Fully loaded DS20 to Monty of MySQL fame
  6) 3 or 4 other DS10s to external partners with whom we are starting
     relationships.

This may not sound like alot, but it is since we payed for these
systems out of our own group budget. (not forecasted expenses). There
have been other seed units sent by other people in Compaq.

We also have access to some Miata class machines for "seed units". If
you'd like to be considered to receive one of these systems (as a
loaner or whatever) feel free to send me your proposals or
justification describing what Linux/Alpha work you are doing or
intend to do, deliverables etc. I'll pass it on and we'll see what
happens.

This isn't really related to the above, but I'm including it anyway.
This in response to Linux/Alpha kernel hacking and who's doing
what,etc.

It appears that Linus takes Alpha specific kernel changes (for 2.3.x)
from Richard Henderson. I know Jay and the others here doing kernel
work forward the patches to Richard. All Linux 2.2.x changes appear
to go to Alan Cox. If you want to contribute and find out what's
going on, the linux-kernel list seems to be the best
place. linux-alpha is kinda neglected and some things do come to this
list. Or use all 3 lists to make sure you get the coverage. Since
the group of people doing the Alpha kernel work has been so small,
there probably hasn't been much need to "play by the rules" if there
are any. I know Andrea recently had some problems because he doesn't
monitor axp-list and Richard had posted some things here - but the
problems were quickly resolved.

That's all for now...
