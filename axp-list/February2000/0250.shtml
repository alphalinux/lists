On Mon, 14 Feb 2000, dennis pantazis wrote:
> Finding module dependencies /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/scsi/ide-scsi.o:
> unresolved
> symbol(s)
> /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/block/ide-tape.o: unresolved symbol(s)
> /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/block/ide-floppy.o: unresolved symbol(s)
> /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/misc/ipx.o: unresolved symbol(s)
> /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/misc/iph5526.o: unresolved symbol(s)
> /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9/misc/appletalk.o: unresolved symbol(s)

you're probably getting this because there are a bunch of leftover modules
from previous compiles of the same kernel. try

rm -r /lib/modules/2.2.13-0.9

and then once more:

make modules_install

> nic old (generic) krn new (noname) krnl
> de4x5 eth0 eth1
> eepro eth1 eth0

it looks like the drivers are getting initialized in a different order
based on whether you compiled noname or generic. if i were you, i'd just
pick one (noname is smaller) and stick with that order.
