With regards to your message at 10:24 AM 2/22/00 -0600, Jason Burrell.
Where you stated:
>A big deal was made when IBM announced their "open" powerpc motherboard
>design. Should/could/would Compaq et al. open up past designs? For
>example, Digital has had design info on the PC164 at
>http://ftp.digital.com/pub/Digital/info/semiconductor/alpha/dsc-software-al
>pha.html
>for a long time. An open, current design would speed the creation of an
>inexpensive Alpha box, plus win good will points from "the community."
>
>-Jason Burrell

While I commend this as a noble thought, I doubt it will help much with
cheap Alphas.

A few years ago Digital Semiconductor encouraged some companies to build
OEM alpha motherboards, and they did so for a while, but they all quit,
citing the facts that this is difficult to build, small runs make for high
costs, and that the Alpha market in general is not big enough.

Samsung has since stepped into the fray, and is CERTAINLY making less
expensive alpha motherboards, but they still will never get them down to
the pricepoint of a Intel or AMD motherboard being made in the hundreds of
thousands, using a chipset that costs under $30 a set.

Case in point:
New FIC motherboard for Athlon: SK-11.
Uses VIA KX133 chipset which costs the Taiwanese manufacturers $29 a set.
Board wholesales for $85 to $90 US.

It has every bell and whistle you can think of. And they _started_
production at 15,000 pieces a WEEK.

Right now there are still some very decent Alpha deals out there. Look at
the developer and student bundle being promoted by API and some of the
Alpha dealers at:
http://www.alpha-processor.com/developers/api_developer_form.asp

$2999 for a full kit 21264/600MHz box is not bad, IMHO.

Tell enough people, maybe they will sell a gazillion of these and prices
will drop even more.

Let's face it, NOBODY can build this stuff for less than Samsung.
