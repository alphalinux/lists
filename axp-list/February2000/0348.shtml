Frasnelli, Dan (dfrasnel@alphalinux.org) wrote:

> Want to run some thoughts and observations by the list:
>
> 1. Sun has - repeatedly - stated that while Linux is a gee-thats-neat
> phenomenon, 100% of Sun's research and marketing dollars will go
> to pushing Solaris.
> 2. In my dealings with engineers at a certain popular distribution house
> (better not say their name for fear of slander and lawsuits that would
> take even my hat. red on, please..), I've found the dominant non-x86
> platform to be ultrasparc; preferred to the point where the employees
> say that Alpha "sucks" and the port is a waste of time.
> 3. Digipaq is going out of its way to support and contribute to the Linux
> community, as it has for 5+ years. They are the only workstation
> vendor to my knowledge which is marketing a 64-bit system specifically
> for Linux hobbyists.
> 4. Does anyone else see a major disparity between the above items?
> On one hand, you have an openly pro-Linux Compaq; on the other, an
> openly anti-Linux Sun. Instead of shaking the open hand of a
> longtime friend and supporter, we've got folks chasing after the
> grinch and fantasizing about Intel vapourware. Something is
> desparately wrong in the perception dept., and my pipe dream is that
> Compaq's marketing and public relations dept. will try to correct it...
> before Alpha goes the way of the Amiga.
> 5. Disclaimer - I have absolutely nothing against Sun. The group I work
> for has over a thousand Sun boxes. When they work, they're the best
> thing since sliced bread.

IMO one of the problems is there is no single orginization responsible
for the kernel, whereas *BSD does. (not wanting to start a Linux vs *BSD
war). Linus was given a DS20 and AFAIK it is going unused (correct me
if you know otherwise). I also believe Alan Cox was given an Alpha, but
he is not very friendly about it (read: ass hole). Instead of throwing
Alphas at people who don't give a shit, why not give them to people who
*want* them? Groups like the Debian Orginization would benifit from
having multiple systems, even if they are just loaners. I think having
more support for the FreeBSD people help too.

In the latest issue of LJ is a full page Compaq Alpha Linux ad. This is
a good sign, I don't remember DEC ever doing any ads like this. Now if
they can keep doing stuff like that I see good things in the future.

While My coding skills are crap (working on it ;-), I would be willing
to help in any way that I can.

Attitude towords newbies is pretty shitty on this list! This HAS TO
change! IMO we need to split up the traffic a little bit. General Alpha
topics should go to axp-software@alphalinux.org or
axp-hardware@alphalinux.org since that is what they were created for.
This way newbies and everyone else can ask questions on THOSE lists and
certian crabby members of THIS list can stay away from newbies.

Another thing that would help is having people provide demo's at LUG's.
This is an area I would help in if I had a spare system to take to the
local LUG meetings.

A lot of people are tired of the x86 architecture. Creating an Alpha
interest in these people should help. Some of these people are moving to
Mac and SPARC systems. Not many are coming over to Alpha. Why? Because
there are no inexpensive developer/hobbiest systems. Three grand for a
DS10 is not acceptable when you can get Ultra SPARCs for less then two
and Mac's for less then one.

More articles relating to Alpha magazines/journals would help a lot.
Even short articles online would be a boon. I personally plan to write
some. If anyone has a subject they would like touched on email me at
rbf@farrer.net and I'll see if I can do it. Just simply copying articles
on sites like linux.com and changing the content to reflect Alphas would
be very helpful!

Getting people at sites like /. interested would be good. BTW, /. ran on
a UDB for the first few years...

All IMO,

Ron Farrer
