Hi !

Franklin Hooker schrieb:
>
> Why can't I use the following technique to copy
> disk partitions?
>
> dump -0 -f - /dev/sdb2 | (cd /mnt/disk; restore yrf -)

I do not know - the command looks o.k. to me.

However, I always prefer "cpio" for such a task:

   cd $SOURCE
   find . -print | cpio -pdmav $DESTINATION

For me, that never failed - and it is not restricted to
doing it on complete file systems. However, it may expand
"sparse files" AFAIK.

Notes:
1) You can easily add a qualification to "find" to restrict
   that to only files (create needed directories implicitly)
   or only directories (create identical, but empty structure)
   or with a "mtime" option or ...
2) You can easily insert a filter ("grep", "fgrep", "sed", ...)
   between "find" and "cpio" to exclude un-wanted files
   (core dumps, backup versions, ...)
3) On some platforms (sorry - no list, but HP-UX may be one),
   "cpio" does not support the simultaneous use of the flags
   "m" (= set modification time of destination to that of source,
       not to time of copy) and
   "a" (= reset access time of source to original value before
       copying).
   In that case, you have to decide which one you want more.
