Thanks for the pointer on 'mtx'. I've written an NT device driver for
supporting Quantum, DEC, HP, Exabyte, STK, Qualstar, Breece Hill,
SpectraLogic, Seagate/Conner/Compaq, Sony, ADIC (and others) and I've found
that the manufacturers have liberal "interpretations" of the medium-changer
SCSI standard. Admittedly, this did make writing the driver a lot more
"fun" and challenging and it exposed the whole medium-changer genre as a
very loose "standard". Usually (but not always) manufacturers have their
own "style" of interface to their own models, so if you have a
driver/program that works well (for example) with a TZ885 then it'll usually
work well with a TZ897. However, the thing to watch out for is OEMers
(notorious of which is Compaq) -- not only do they OEM models from HP, Sony,
DEC (or whatever manufacturer-du-jour that scores them the best deal) --
they tend to "improve" (and believe me, this is an incredible stretch of the
word) the firmware in the device... so it's always "caveat emptor" when
playing with tape-libraries.
