I don't own a Multia/UDB but I can make a couple comments
about your config.

1. You don't have NCR53C8XX configured. Usually, I thought
   Multias used 53c810 chips for SCSI.
2. You've configured QLOGIC_ISP. Do you have a Qlogic card
   in the machine?
3. No IDE support either. So, you must have a Qlogic card
   or your kernel won't find any disks to access.
4. You've configured EISA. Why? Probably not an issue but
   it's unnecessary.
5. For RH, if you intend to install, you need CONFIG_ROMFS_FS
6. Seems you're missing CONFIG_MSDOS_FS and CONFIG_FAT_FS.
   If you intend to look at the partition where milo and
   co. live, you'll need one or the other to mount and look
   at it.
7. I find networking drivers are easier to work with if
   they are modules. You've specified DE4X5 as builtin.
   Maybe modules would be handier. Also, if you use DE4X5,
   build DEC_ELCP/DEC_ELCP_OLD. That appears to be tulip's
   name nowadays. Then use /etc/conf.modules to map in the
   one you want to use. Some cards work only with certain
   drivers so having all 3 handy allows you to experiment.

As I said, you need to describe your HW config in order to
compare it to your .config. The kernel config process is
not automated. You have to specify the correct drivers for
your system or the kernel will hang/crash.

On the other hand, RH does a fair job probing you system
at install to setup your HW in a reasonable fashion so it
is automated to a certain degree. Once you start building
your own kernels, you need knowledge of your HW config.
