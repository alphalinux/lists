Andrei A. Dergatchev wrote:
>
> > You mean that i's not so easy to switch from an AlphaBios Linux installation
> > to a SRM one ?
>
> In one word: no, not easy.
...
>
> For example, you may have to backup all your HDDs and reformat
> them in order to be able to boot from SRM if currently your system
> boots from AlphaBIOS.

Actually it is very easy __provided__ you have different boot
devices, formatted in an appriopriate manner, to feed to respective
consoles. These can be two disks but you can also, for example,
to make SRM to load your kernel from a disk and AlphaBIOS console
may grab one from CD. Once Linux booted it will be happy with
both ways ("BSD disklabel" or "FAT partition table") of disk
partitioning on the same system.

> > Is it possible to switch back to AlphaBios ?

A while ago I was experimenting with a machine which I was toggling
to SRM with a help of a floppy. So switching back to AlphaBios
was as simple as not sticking that floppy in. :-) Sure, booting
from floppies is not what you want to do in normal operations
but this was not the question. :-)
