Man, did I screw this one up. :-( I had a perfectly working
Multia with ARC and now I have a paperweight. Since I had my
other Alpha's working happily under SRM I decided (foolishly)
that I wanted the Multia using SRM as well. In ARC, I switched
into Digital UNIX console (to get the SRM firmware on boot).

When I turned the machine back on I got nothing. Turns out that
this is a half-flash machine and the flash can only hold either
ARC or SRM. No problem, I'll just start up with the Failsafe
Loader. Well, I put the failsafe loader disk in and power cycle.
I also moved the PWR jumper to the FSL jumper.

The disk spins for about 10 seconds and then I get no video (the
monitor just powers down). I get no error beeps or flashing LEDs
on the back. I figure it must be the S3 video card, so I move
the VGA connector to the builtin TGA port. I power cycle and this
time I get a blank screen with a white cursor in the lower left.
But no text and I can't type anything. :-(

I'm guessing it might be the fixed frequency VGA modes. Maybe I
have to set a different video mode with the jumpers. However, I have
a good multi-sync attached so I don't see why this should be a problem.

Another guess it the S3 card. Should I try taking it out completely?
Maybe it is confusing the SRM firmware, although I now have the monitor
connected to the TGA port.

Any clues? I'm dead in the water and at this point just want a working
system back, either ARC or preferably SRM.

P.S. It's not the hardware...it's been running Linux fine all along...it's
just when I switched to SRM that the problems began. The power connectors
and RAM are seated just fine too.

Please help if you can.

/Michael
