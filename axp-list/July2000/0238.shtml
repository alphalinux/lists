This is a test ... have been unable to post so far ...

I am the proud owner of an Alphaserver 2100A (Does not run Linux....)
and an alphastation 500 (could , but has currently Dec Unix 4.0E)

I have heard about cooling problems on Alphastations ....
the usual design of the desktop boxes like mine puts a heavy burden on
the front fan to cool the system to acceptable levels.

The A500 got vey hot inside the case, yet was running OK, but i decided
to replace the front fan with something bigger, just in case...
... as the processor is irreplaceable here in Chile ....

The fan that came out was a NIDEC TA3500 10cm big, which i replaced
(discarding the plastic bracket) with a gigantic Sanyo Denki 12cm fan that
barely fits nut cools the interior with grace .... after 10 houts of
work , one can touch the processor .

The only problem is the noise ... i'll drill some extra holes in the front
plate to try to smoothen the airflow.

I would advise all desktop alpha owners to check the fans carefully... and
replace with something bigger or/and with higher airflow if needed.
