Hi folks.

If you're sick of reading about the 3000/300, hit delete now. I'm going
to ramble a bit. :)

I've been devouring list archives of this list, and the Decstation Linux
list for a while now, and finally have a tiny bit of time on my hands.
What I'm wondering about is the possibilty of getting approximately 1
dozen 3000/300LX machines I have here at work running a decent (read:
Linux) OS.

Now correct me if I'm wrong, please - but as I understand it, the 3000
is not much more than a Decstation with an Alpha processor instead of a
MIPS. Same basic hardware, right?

The Decstation Linux people supposedly have working
keyboards/mice/framebuffers hell, they seem to have most of it going.
The NetBSD folk even have audio working.

So am I dreaming when I think it might be possible to drag the
Turbochannel drivers and other support out of the Decstation patches and
compile for Alpha? Is anyone working on this? I've been crawling over
the web and found the occasional discontinued project, but nothing
current.

It seems to me the pieces are out there, but nobody has put them
together. Anyone want to help with a jigsaw puzzle where we're not sure
where all the pieces are?

Of course, if I've made stupid assumptions, please tell me to stop
wasting my time and go buy Tru64. :)

Thanks,
Mike.
