On Sun, 16 Jul 2000, Danilo Godec wrote:

> However, what is 'raid-level -1' supposed to mean? AFAIK, raid-level can
> be 0 for linear, 1 for mirroring and 5 for RAID5. Since you have four
> disks, I expect RAID5 is what you want and you should carefully consider
> option 'chunk-side' as it is supposed to be imoprtant for performance.

raid-level 0 is striping actually. I was curious about where you got -1
from too though, Wes--I couldn't find it in my copy of the raidtab manpage
from RH 6.2. I may be way wrong so you should probably verify this in the
linux-raid archive first, but I think you just use "raid-level linear" for
linear devices.

> PS: Check out the 'official' howto at
> http://ostenfeld.dk/~jakob/Software-RAID.HOWTO/

FYI, this has now finally been incorporated in with the rest of the howtos
and can be viewed at any LDP mirror.
