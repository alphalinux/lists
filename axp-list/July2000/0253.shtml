There is a new fractal based video compression algorithm out called FIASCO.
I've been taking some POV-Ray based animations that I did awhile ago and
comparing against MPEG compression.

Using 900 frames of 320 x 240 images (~ 200 Meg total of data) and just P
frames for searches gives:

MPEG (mpeg_encode): ~21Meg
FIASCO: ~1.5 Meg

Visually the quality is nearly the same, but the FIASCO algorithm gives
somewhat "softer" images. But, of course I still need to play with the
encoder settings to get the best results. Decompression works great even
on low-end computers. Anyway, why I'm interested in having someone with a 264
try this out is because of the compression runtimes: MPEG ~ 5 mins and
FIASCO ~10 hours on my SX 533MHz.

You can get FIASCO at . . .
http://ulli.linuxave.net/fiasco/

If anyone want to try this give me a yell.
