On Tue, 28 May 1996, Howard Harvey wrote:
>
> Oh happy day, I have just inherited free and gratis no less than
> two DEC alpha boxes. An avowed linux geek, I obviously will want
> to run Linux on the beasts. The first one is a 2000-300 with
> 80Mb of RAM. Tasty.....

That's the Jensen, and Linux will run on that. You may need to open it up and
get a new video card for it, though - you need an S3 card if you want to run
X11, and most Jensens seem to ave the Compaq QVision card in them (linux will
run fine with that card, but you won't be getting any graphics from it).

> The 2nd one is a dual processor (Yep - 2 processors.....) 2100 with
> 512Mb of RAM. My question is this:
>
> Does Linux support multi-processor alpha machines?
> Especially those with ridiculously large memories?

Umm.. 512MB isn't ridiculously large - one of the alpha's at the university
has 512MB and is running linux quite well. So that part is not a problem.

The 2100 and the SMP part will be a problem, though - Linux/alpha doesn't
have SMP support yet. It shouldn't be a major undertaking for somebody who
knows SMP and Linux (the SMP code is reasonably portable and there is x86 and
sparc support for it already), but it's by no means trivial. You're very
welcome to get your hands dirty with it if you want to try..

Linus
