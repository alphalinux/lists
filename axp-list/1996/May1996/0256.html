Stephen Gaudet wrote:

>>In addition, I direct you to the new Digital Alphastation 255/300Mhz
>>workstation. This workstation is available running Digital Unix and with a
>>21064a/DB-275 running at 300Mhz!!!!!!
>>
>>Check it out digital has not released a 21064a-300Mhz part, so what can it
>>be? Answer, a 275 CLOCKED up running 300Mhz!
>>
>>Since your the expert let me know if a new chip has entered the market,
>>because no one at Digital can tell me of such a part exists.

Simply becasue Digital Semiconductor does not sell a 21064A-300 on the open
market, that does not mean that Digital Semiconductor does not grade and test
chips at that speed bin for internal systems groups' use, using the same
thorough testing that is used to bin all the other speeds.

If anything, the existance of a -300 part diminishes your argument for
'overclocking' viz: if the fastest part that DS sell is a -275, then it
might be reasonable to assume that some -275 parts would run faster. BUT, if
you know that DS is binning -300 parts for internal systems groups, that
*reduces* the number of -275 parts that will run reliably at 300MHz...

Neal.
