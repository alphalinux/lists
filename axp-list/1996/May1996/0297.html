Aaron,

> I have been trying for the past few hours to get sound working
>on a UDB (Multia) machine. I am using pre2.0.4 since pre2.0.5 seems to
>break registering rpc. I am trying to use the Microsoft Sound system
>driver with the settings: ioport=0x530, IRQ=9, DMA=3 (all other settings
>I have tried seem to fail in worse ways than these).

These *are* the correct settings for the UDB sound chip... :-)

>It seems that
>I get garbled sound out of /dev/audio and /dev/dsp. /dev/mixer,
>/dev/sequencer, and /dev/midi* give me a no such device message. The
>devices do have the right major/minor numbers.

/dev/{dsp,audio} have worked for a long time, /dev/mixer, I think, has worked
as well.

Do you say "garbled" after listening to the built-in speaker of the UDB? If
so, know that that speaker is *REALLY* terrible; plug a set of headphones or
powered speakers into the headphone jack on the front, and the sound quality
will be *MUCH* improved.

Mixer apps that are know to work are: aumix (text mode console), xmix, and
xmixer.

Now, /dev/sequencer and /dev/midi* will *NEVER* work; the MSS driver does
not support them, not to mention the hardware... :-)

Use "tracker" or (possibly) one of the other MOD players; also, TiMIDIty
(a MIDI to DSP translator) works *very* well, though I haven't reported the
patches I had to make to run on the Alpha (they were trivial, however, so
for now left as an exercise for the reader... :-).

You can also find "vplay" in one of the snd_utils kits, and it works quite
well with .au, .voc, .wav, etc files.

Anyway, let me know if you continue to have problems; you've got the right
setup, just need to work with what *will* work...

--Jay++
