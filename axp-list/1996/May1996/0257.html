>How about this?
>
>What if you really overclock and see when it fails?

When you try a faster crystal the system will either come up or not. If it
does not come up than you have a processor that simply will not run at
faster speeds.

>But first,
>
>1) is there a problem with burning out the CPU?

There is if you do not have supplied sufficient cooling. Some of the older
Digital Alpha systems only had a heat sink, no fan!

Also, on the Digital Cabriolet, a.k.a. PC64 and the EB164 there is a circut
that supplies the fan with power and kills power to the motherboard if the
fan stops spinning. This can be verified by pulling the fan power cable.

>By the way, Microway basically told me that it is OK to overclock slightly.
I
>bought a 300mhz machine from them and they said I could overclock it to 333
>without problem. They did not supply that crystal, however. I should really
>try that one of these days...

Main rule in overclocking is 10% above intended speed use speed based on the
33mh clock, such as 166, 200, 233, 266, 300, 333, 366, 400.

motherboards and such knows?
