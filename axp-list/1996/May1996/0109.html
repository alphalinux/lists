> nil. Why? Memory and I/O bandwidth, unlike CPUs, cost money. It's
> as simple as that.

Our local DEC rep was out recently showing off a new crop of
servers (nice!) and it was mentioned that, in most of the high-end
packages they've put together recently, memory has accounted for
90% of the cost of the entire machine.

While systems with 2GB RAM are not exactly what is being discussed, I do
think that it is a vivid example in our context here of what should
be emphasized to make things go fast.

> - get as much memory as you can afford---the best CPU won't help
> if you spend all your time waiting for the disk
>
> - if you have a choice between a system with N CPUs or a system
> with one CPU that's N times faster, you always want the latter
> (all else being equal, of course)

Amen, brother. Preach it.
