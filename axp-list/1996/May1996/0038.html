This is how I put together my system (from which I am typing at this
moment). Prices have dropped for disk and memory from when I pieced
together the system.

For now, I am going to save a bit of money and share my NEC 4FG multisync
monitor with my Mac IIsi. Eventually, I'd like to get a 17" monitor.
Price breakdown:

$1170 AXPpci33 (166 MHz 21066 CPU, PS/2 style kbd/mouse IF, NT firmare)
DEC Direct
NOTE: OSF/1 firmware FTPable from DEC. Saves some money
by not ordering OSF/1 verion of AXPpci33 and not
paying license fees.

$ 600 ST31230N Seagate 1 GB SCSI-2 drive
Local firm (Legacy), 1 yr off the shelf swap warr, 5 yr warr.
(I probably could have saved a bit here by going mail order.)

$ 450 ATI Graphics Pro Turbo 4 MB (VRAM) PCI
Up to 1280x1024 at 24 bpp and 75 Hz refresh; accellerated.
PC Zone

$ 320 Case, Keytronics keyboard, 1.44 MB floppy
Local clone builder.
(I probably could have saved a bit here by going mail order,
but I needed to verify the case layout due to SIMM placement
on mother board. I ended up with a mid-tower case that
may (slight chance) require a small mod to the 3.5" drive
bay to clear SIMMs. The mini-tower I have my 532 in wouldn't
work.)

$ 640 16 MB DRAM (two 2Mx36bit 70ns, 72 pin SIMMs)
$ 117 256KB SRAM cache (11 32Kx8bit 20ns DIPs)
Local RAM wheeler/dealer
(I probably could have saved some on the SRAM here by going
mail order.)

$ ?? Misc cables, mouse (TBD), etc

$3300 Approx total

$ 0 Scavenged 210 MB SCSI disk from my decomissioned Mac A/UX.
$ 0 Scavenged Sony CD-ROM.
$ 0 Shared Hayes Accura 144 modem.
$ 0 Shared QMS PS410 laser printer.

I was trying for under $3000, but I wanted a better keyboard and faster disk.
I also decided to go all out on the video board now so I wouldn't have to
put up with 8 bit color anymore and would eventually be able to run 1280x1024.
