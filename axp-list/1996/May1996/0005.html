>>>>> On Wed, 1 May 1996 19:14:46 +0100 (BST), "David (Borris) Fell" <borris@ant.co.uk> said:

Borris> I'm looking for enough documentation to port a couple of
Borris> compilers to the Alpha. When I ported them to the sparc, the
Borris> documentation I used was called the "Sparc architecture
Borris> manual".

You'll want:

Alpha AXP Architecture Reference Manual
Second Edition
Digital Press/Butterworth-Heinemann
DEC order #: EY-T132E-DP
ISBN 1-55558-145-5
Library of Congress: QA76.9.A73A46

Should be available in your favority bookstore. Be sure to get the
*second* edition though, it contains a lot more info than the first
one. In particular it also talks a little about some of the existing
implementations. Architecture is good, but it is always nice to see
things in the context of what actually exists. Notice that the
scheduling rules are not part of the architecture however. You'd need
to get the CPU specific manuals from DEC for those (see Linux/Alpha
FAQ for some pointers: http://www.azstarnet.com/~axplinux/FAQ.html).

Borris> PS - the compilers are Algol68 and Fortran90 compilers, if
Borris> anyone is interested. They probably won't be available for
Borris> free as they have substantial numbers of man years in them
Borris> already.

Ummh, so does gcc, Emacs, the Linux kernel and pretty much any other
significant free software I can think of. (Not that I think anybody
has to apologize for doing commercial stuff on top of GNU Linux...)
