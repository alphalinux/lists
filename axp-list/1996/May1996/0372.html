> This is the intended behavior of all the redhat run mailing lists.
> In general, responses should always go back to the list. Whether you
> guys know it or not, there are tons of "lurkers" on every list. They
> won't post, but they do read everything and get tons of useful information
> from the questions and replies. Every time someone suggests changing
> our setup, those "lurkers" email *us* (the redhat maintainers) asking
> that we *not* change it.

Right - that's what the 'group reply' option in elm and other mailers is
intended for.

By setting Reply-to to the list, you make reply and group-reply do the same,
effectively disabling the reply function.

> Most questions that are asked here need to be answered here. In the
> cases where they are off topic or the author would rather summarize
> responses, the author should set his Reply-to: in his header appropriately.

I do usually reply to the list. However, I do not see the benefit of the
current setup - it makes more work for me if I want to do a single reply to the
author, and it is easy to overlook this and write an inappropriate reply to the
whole list instead of one single person (as it just happened twice to me, with
one german answer and one containing a large uue file).

IMHO, the current behaviour is broken and should be fixed - I do not see any
benefits. If I want to reply to the list, I do - if I do not want to do this, I
see no benefit in making this step more difficult.

Furthermore, many users *expect* their mail reader to work as documented -
which it does not do in this case. The list is already on the from-line, so
everyone who wants to can reply to the list.

> I tend to get *really* annoyed when someone changes the Reply-To
> to point at themselves for no apparent reason. Just doesn't make

If this doesn't change, I will start doing this, too, since I want other people
to be able to reply *easily* to my articles.

> sense...mailing lists exist for *everyone*, not just for each person
> to ask questions and get answers. Even the dumbest of questions

Right. But this does not explain why the current setup is necessary. There are
cases where persolan replys are appropriate, and there are cases where a reply
to the list is best. Why do you try to disable one of these?
