On Wed, 8 May 1996 08:30:20 +0300 (EET DST), Linus Torvalds wrote

> On Tue, 7 May 1996, David Mosberger wrote:
> > It really is time somebody write that paper entitled "Why Low-End
> > Multiprocessing is a Dumb Idea (unless your a nuclear physicist
> > or Intel)".

Sigh; let me quote my reaction to Craig's g77-0.5.8 announcement
back in February 1995:

QUOTE

> 4) Nuclear physicists need something to make up for the
> loss of the supercollider.

AAAAhhhh, Nukes have the name, but we (Meteo types) DO it: burning
CPU cycles in hopelessly optimised FORTRAN, with 5-page long DO
loops and scores of unimaginable and probably misspelled variable
names.

UNQUOTE

And you can be assured: With 4 CPU's, a decent memory system _and_
a parallellising compiler I'll get a close-to-four-times speedup
:-)

> Umm.. I have to speak up for intel here.
>
> The PPro-200 is definitely not low-end, and it squarely
> beats all alpha EV5-300's I've seen on some things (and
> I'm not even counting software availability). The PPro
> usually has a reasonable memory subsystem (the same can't
> be said of the ev5's I've seen), and it has a very
> reasonable price.

Yep; can you say: 4-way interleaved memory ? I know you could :-)

Oh, for those who don't know: Numerical Weather Prediction codes
trample all the memory they get, each and every time step. No use
for caches :-)

[ ... Sound analysis deleted for brevity ... ]

> When compared to the PPro, the alpha has got a few things
> going for it:
> - the "fun" factor. It's more interesting than a PC.
> - the "64 bit" factor. This can be the "sine qua non"
> in some circumstances.
> - the ev5 _is_ actually faster on basic arithmetic floating point
> and many integer operations.

Aaaannnd, it has 32 integer registers. Seriously, without integer
registers, your "floating point intensive" Fortran codes will have
_no_ performance; you _have_ to have them for indexing in all those
arrays. Someone posted a message on c.o.l.m that he saw a 25 %
performance decrease when going from a.out-Linux to ELF-Linux. The
most probable cause is that ELF compilations need (grab) another
integer register, to hold the PIC-offset. That's 8 integer
registers, minus stack pointer, minus frame pointer, minus PIC-base
register, is 5.

> As far as the OS is concerned, the EV5-300 looks like
> it's about 10-20% faster, and that's not a huge margin).

Aaahhh, but nobody's going to buy a machine to just run the OS;
seriously, I've been in the OS business for 7 years before I picked
up a physics job again: As soon as the OS starts to take more than
a few percent of the machine's CPU resources, I get _very_ nervous.

> Don't take me wrong, I love the alpha, but I'd love to
> have a PPro-200 _too_. And the PPro I might actually have
> enough money to buy..

Well, it's certainly true that, to people who are on a tight
budget, I would advise to take a good look at the PPro. The problem
with these machines is that, as soon as you've seen the glossies,
selling PPro-200's to the largest secretary school over here, you'll
almost automatically conclude it's not for you :-)

Toon.
