Linus> But anybody calling the PPro "low end" is seriously deluding
Linus> himself (I can compare numbers between Linux on a EV5-300 and
Linus> Linux on a PPro-200, and they are _close_. As far as the OS

Linus> Don't take me wrong, I love the alpha, but I'd love to have a
Linus> PPro-200 _too_. And the PPro I might actually have enough

It's a pity you took my mail to be P6 vs Alpha. It was just what it
said: about low-end multiprocessing. By that I mean the folks who
take a motherboard designed for a single processor and simply plug in
N CPUs. Thanks to on-chip caches, there is *some* benefit to doing
this, but not for an awful many apps, IMHO (hence the "nuclear
physicist"...).

Maybe you took offense at the "Intel" part of the title. It would
have been better to say "Chip Manufacturer", but lets face it, it
doesn't sound quite as catchy... :-) (And I guess it is Intel that
popularized low-end SMPs.)

I don't think I ever called a P6 "low end". And maybe the 4 CPU
systems that are out there even make sense for a reasonably wide range
of people. But I'd bet that the vast majority of systems out there in
PC-land are hopelessly starved for RAM---I know it's true for all the
machines that are in my home. Not an awfully representative sample,
but it is funny given that I *know* about the importance of RAM etc...
And I won't even get into the issues of I/O bandwidth. In *that* kind
of context, plugging in more CPUs is just silly.

--david
