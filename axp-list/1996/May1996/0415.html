>>>>> On Thu, 30 May 1996 22:56:20 -0400, "Michael K. Johnson" <johnsonm@nigel.vnet.net> said:

Michael> Does everything rebuild correctly so far with glibc? Are
Michael> lots of patches to other packages required? If so, have
Michael> you kept track of the patches?

Can't say everything, since I haven't tried _that_ much yet. But in
general, cleanly written programs compile without any troubles.
Sometimes it's necessary to add a missing #include <errno.h> and
sometimes things need to be compiled with -D_GNU_SOURCE etc., but
other than that, there are few headaches. The only exception so far
was util-linux. It shows that it was never compiled on anything but
Linux. But for that package, I have patches. I expect problems with
the NetKits as well, after that, I think it should be a fairly smooth
ride. And libg++ seems to work fine as well. In fact, I have working
ical and zapem binaries... :-)

In summary: it will take some work to convert an entire distribution
to glibc, but I think it's well worth it.
