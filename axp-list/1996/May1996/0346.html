There are newbies around. I'm a newbie to the Alpha, but am a kernel/etc guy.

My take on DEC compatibility is, if you can/want to buy Linux for AXP, why
would you spend money on a native WP port for OSF/1? That's incongruous,
and, if there are folks out there who want to do (and they aren't pirating
the code :-) ), then we can make an iCBS-like compatibility mode.

Also, let's think this through. If I wanted commercial apps on AXP, and were
a Linux user, wouldn't I actually be better served buying Windows NT and going
that route? I'm talking straight word processors, spreadsheets, etc.

On the other hand, how are SlickEdit, Caldera, et al doing moving their
native port applications?

Actually, I'd like some books on Alpha assembler. Where do you send off for
this type of literature?
