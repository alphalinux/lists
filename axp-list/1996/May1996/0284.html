Actually, if you're adventurous, you can find Alpha-specific patches
for glibc in ftp.azstarnet.com/pub/linux/axp/glibc-*. It's not for
the faint of heart though, since it will require fixing minor bugs in
gcc and gas. I'm working on patches for those, but my primary
goal/hope is that glibc will be used for the forthcoming ELF based
system. I think it makes sense to do the switch together since that
would minimize the amount of confusion.

Also, in way of an update to my earlier mail:

- glibc now supports /etc/hosts.conf
- strptime() is there, too (thanks to Uli Dreppert)
- emacs 19.30 seems to be working quite well when compiled against
glibc

So, yes, things are making progress. If somebody could help with
optimizing libm, I would be very grateful though. It's not something
I'll get to any time soon (though glibc's libm is already in much
better shape than the old one).
