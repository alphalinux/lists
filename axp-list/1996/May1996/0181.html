Hello!

After the latest discussions in this group I made some benchmarks.
Maybe they are interesting for the libc/libm-gurus.

Since I am a chemist, number crunching is of special interest for me.
So here a my results for this little benchmark:

#include <stdio.h>
#include <math.h>
#include <time.h>
main()
{
double x,y[10000];
int i,j;
time_t t;

time(&t);
for (j=0;j<10000;j++)
for (i=0;i<10000;i++)
{
x=11.0+(33.5*i)*(33.5*i);
y[i]=(sin(3.1*i)+cos(5.1*i))*sqrt(x+exp(3.14*log(x+i)));
}
printf("time=%d\n",time(0)-t);
}

Linux 1.3.94 on DEC AlphaStation 200 4/166 : 41 sec
DEC Unix 3.2 on " : 7 sec

The used compilers:
gcc2.7.1 on Linux
gcc2.7.2 on Digital Unix (AFAIK /bin/as is used)
Almost a factor of 6 :-(( . This is much more than reported here before.
