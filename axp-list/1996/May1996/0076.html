Florian Fuchs <captain@sport2.uibk.ac.at> writes:
> We have an Alpha Multia/233 which has 24 MB RAM (two 12 MB RAM chips). We
> bought two 32 MB chips now, however, the Multia wont recognize them. Does
> anybody know why this could be? Are there any jumpers to bet set? Do they
> need parity?

The Multia (and AFAIK all Alpha systems) do require "parity" RAM. In most
cases this means using 36-bit 72-pin SIMMs. On the Multia, UDB, and Noname
the SIMMs must be installed in pairs, as the memory bus is 72 bits wide:
64 data bits plus 8 ECC bits.

So-called "logic parity" SIMMs won't work. The memory really has to be
36 bits wide. It costs more, but ECC is worth it. On a typical PC, a
parity error just halts the machine. With ECC, a single bit error in a word
can be corrected, and the much less likely double bit errors can be detected.

The funny 12MB SIMMs that are supplied with the 233 MHz Multia can only be
used in the sockets they are initially installed in. Don't try to move
them to the other pair of sockets, or to use them in another system.

Some people have been really annoyed by the non-standard 12MB SIMMs, but IMHO
it is a feature. DEC went out of their way to get special SIMMs so that
customers could upgrade the machine from 24 MB without having to throw some
SIMMs away. On the 166 MHz Multia and UDB the 24 MB is supplied as the more
conventional pair of 8MB and poair of 4MB SIMMs.
