Not a chance : I'm a student, and I'm poor like every student :-). But there
are commercial Linux resellers/third party software providers who do it just
fine : RedHat, Caldera...
>
> 2) Is it legal to use Linux as platform for commercial products, or
> will I run into problems regarding copyright and license policies for
> Linux (f.x. GPL)?
GPL won't do any harm to commercial products : the only thing it requires is
that if your software embeds some GPLed code, you bundle or make available for
free its sources or binaries. This is most relevant for statically linked
libraries. There are exceptions to this rule, however : the startup files
needed to compile any C program are not covered by the GPL, which means that on
an x86 platform, an app compiled and dynamically linked with only the GNU libc
is absolutely free of copyright restrictions, wether in source or binary form.
There are some different licenses around, though : the one you may want to look
at is the Artistic License, which covers Perl (but I think the situation is the
same, since RedHat sells Perl code).

On the Alpha platform, things may be different since there are no shared
libraries : but anyway, the worse thing which may happen to you is to be forced
to ship the GNU libc (or other libs) code for free to every customer who asks
for it.
