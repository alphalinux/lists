> Do you say "garbled" after listening to the built-in speaker of the UDB? If
> so, know that that speaker is *REALLY* terrible; plug a set of headphones or
> powered speakers into the headphone jack on the front, and the sound quality
> will be *MUCH* improved.

I am using headphones for the testing. My procedure is:

cat /dev/dsp > test
<talk into the microphone>
cat test > /dev/dsp
<listen>

This is how I have tested sound on my intel machine and it always
worked fine. I hear something that I can barely distinguish from static
that sortof sounds like what I spoke into the mike. I have only tried play
(part of sox) as playback software. I have played Maelstrom and sounds seem
to come out at the wrong speed and with static. The -r (rate) command
doesn't seem to work at all on play. The speed of playback isn't related
to to the clock speed is it? I have a 266Mhz clocked-up UDB.

> Mixer apps that are know to work are: aumix (text mode console), xmix, and
> xmixer.

I have tried to use xmixer and aumix (the ones that come with
RedHat-3.0.3) and they give me messages like "can't read the volume
setting" and they do do something but I can't tell if it is correct. The
overall volume knob doesn't work.

> Use "tracker" or (possibly) one of the other MOD players; also, TiMIDIty
> (a MIDI to DSP translator) works *very* well, though I haven't reported the
> patches I had to make to run on the Alpha (they were trivial, however, so
> for now left as an exercise for the reader... :-).
> You can also find "vplay" in one of the snd_utils kits, and it works quite
> well with .au, .voc, .wav, etc files.

Assuming I get this to work, I want to use this for playback of
MPEG-Audio files (hopefully at 16bit, 44KHz since this was advertised to
work at "cd quality") and, of course, for games. I luckily don't care
about the midi stuff.

Thanks for your help,

Aaron Passey
