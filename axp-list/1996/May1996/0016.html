Hello all!
First let me introduce myself:
My name is Nils Faerber and I'm using Linux nearly from the very beginning
(from 0.98 on). Nearly a half year ago I got the opprtunity to buy an old
Jensen DEC AlphaPC _very_ cheap and couldn't resist.
So far Linux runs just fine on it except for three minor but annoying
problems:
- I cannot read the realtimeclock! It always says 1.1.1970 or 12.31.2016!
- When accessing the serial ports via "echo Hallo > /dev/ttyS0" the machine
crashes completely with endless messages (too fast scrolling to read)
- When heavily accessing the harddisk (e.g. make -j) the system crashes
with something like
SCSI aha174x: asynchronous event ... [and some numbers I cannot remember)

And one question:
- When will dynamic libraries be available? I know that the Sparc-Linux
guys are on their way with elf libraries. It would for sure be a great
step forward for Alpha-Linux to have them (I hate those HUGE binaries
wasting my precious harddisk space).
OK. This should be it for today :)
CU
nils

PS: I use an AlphaPC Jensen, Adaptec 1742A, Elsa Winner 1000, NE2000 Ethernet
