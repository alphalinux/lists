> since "quickstrip" works fine, I can't beleave that it's not possible
> for "strip" to work correctly without a patch for gas or the kernel ?!

Well, "quickstrip" is a quickie utility I wrote way back in the 32-bit
Linux/Alpha days. It's a ten-line program that finds the COFF symbol
table, nukes it in the header, and truncates the file. Period.

"strip", on the other hand, is part of GNU binutils. It does all kinds
of fancy stuff including parsing the image through libbfd, performing
a *transform* from input-image-type to output-image-type (even if the
transform is trivial), selecting *which* symbols to nuke (relocations
stay no matter what, f'rinstance) and creating the output image.

That's why it's possible for one to work and not the other 8-)
