> This is the intended behavior of all the redhat run mailing lists.
> In general, responses should always go back to the list. Whether you
> guys know it or not, there are tons of "lurkers" on every list. They
> won't post, but they do read everything and get tons of useful information
> from the questions and replies. [..]
> Most questions that are asked here need to be answered here.

This is correct, but I don't see how it relates to the Reply-To:
headers. I mean, anybody who's using mailing-lists ought to be knowing
the "follow-up" ( or "group-reply") feature of his mail client. You
can even use it on any other mail, since there will be only one
recipient anyhow.

> In the
> cases where they are off topic or the author would rather summarize
> responses, the author should set his Reply-to: in his header appropriately.
> Our server will not override one that has been set. If the author
> hasn't set one, it is still up to the person responding where to send
> the reply. In general, it should go to the list. If that isn't desired,
> the responder should change it manually.

Argh, what do you think is the reason for almost every mail client
(even Netscape!) to have two different reply methods -- that you cripple
one of them too allow us using cut/copy/paste again?

> I tend to get *really* annoyed when someone changes the Reply-To
> to point at themselves for no apparent reason. Just doesn't make
> sense...mailing lists exist for *everyone*, not just for each person
> to ask questions and get answers.

OK, the most apparent reason for somebody to insert a Reply-To line is
to give people the address(s) he wants the reply send to. This is the
original purpose of the Reply-To field (RFC 822). I use 3 different
accounts, but I don't want to check mail on each of them every day, as
I have to pay phone bills for some of them.

Another point about this is, that I'll use a biff program to notify
me about mails personal addressed to me. I am able to response at
once, if somebody asks me. Other mails from lists get stored were I
can read them as soon I have some time left. I assume that other
people use similar methods to deal with huge lists. Your "feature"
prevents an immediate discussion of issues, but instead delays replies
overnight.

[..] Just doesn't make
> sense...mailing lists exist for *everyone*, not just for each person
> to ask questions and get answers. Even the dumbest of questions
> should be answered *once* on the mailing list. Why? Because those
> responses get archived and it might save someone from asking *again*
> if they can find the answer themselves.

Correct again, but unrelated. Normal behaviour of people that use
mailing lists is to use `group-reply'. By crippling this feature you'll
train people to use `reply-to-author' instead, which will have influence
on other mailing lists, that do it the standard way. BTW, would you
also newsgroups to work this way? Are you missing "me too!!!!" mails
on this list??

> This is the way it works and it isn't likely to change any time soon.
> (Doesn't mean we aren't open to discussion, but this comes up from time
> to time...there are those on the side of the "Reply-To should be
> the author", but there are many more on the other side of the argument.)

Well, please put at least a note about this in the welcome mail of the
list. It will help to avoid people making mistakes, like Michael and
me before.

I won't argue further about this, since the can see at the
number of replies to this, that the list is tired about this
issue. I'll just program my mail filter to delete this line at my
place.

Bye, Martin
