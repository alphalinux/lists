Well, I tried to keep quiet for once, but you pushed the button. If
you don't like MILO, replace it with something on your own. There's
the kernel's raw loader and aboot which can serve to illustrate how
the SRM bootprocess works. Of course, that assumes you don't mind
plugging in an extra MB of RAM that MILO gives you for free. Heck,
there is even an SRM booting howto that gives the fundamentals. All
details are in the second edition of the Alpha arch ref manual and not
to mention the source code. So it's entirely up to you to change
something if you don't like it.

Anybody contributing just a single line of useful free software gets
my deepest respect and gratitude---independent of where they come from
or what their motives for doing so might have been.

And if you had bothered to read the mailing list archives you'd
probably have noticed that the Linux folks from DEC are _extremely_
supportive and enthusiastic about what they're doing. I can say
without any hesitation that the entire time I have spent on
Linux/Alpha so far has been pure fun. And, yes, I too am a student
that turns every dollar/franc twice before spending it.

--david
