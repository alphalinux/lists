<i>&gt; We'd want to install Linux on an Alpha 600 5/266, but we don't know</i><br>
<i>&gt; nearly anything about the Alpha Linux's distribution.</i><br>
<p>
Since you found your way onto the RedHat Linux/AXP mailing list,<br>
you've already located the source of one distribution... :-)<br>
<p>
<p>
<i>&gt; First of all we'd like to know if it's possible to install that</i><br>
<i>&gt; distribution on our machine,</i><br>
<p>
I don't believe it's possible; even though there's EV5 (21164 CPU) support<br>
in the latest LINUX sources, and kernels for the EB164 in the RH 3.0.3<br>
distribution, you'll find it won't work on your box.<br>
<p>
Your box is known as an ALCOR; it has the EV5 CPU, plus PCI and EISA busses.<br>
There's a TGA in there, which does have LINUX console and X support. There's<br>
also a COMBO card as well, with a DEC TULIP Ethernet controller on it and a<br>
pair of QLogic 1020ISP SCSI F/W controllers on it.<br>
<p>
Now, there is a driver for the QLOGIC in LINUX pre-2.0.8, but my Alpha<br>
patches for it aren't in there (yet). The TULIP works quite nicely on<br>
that COMBO card (I've had it in both an XL and an EB164).<br>
<p>
But the biggest problems are:<br>
<p>
1. EISA bus - of all the supported Alpha platforms, only JENSEN has EISA,<br>
and JENSEN is such an odd duck, that doesn't help at all. All the others<br>
are PCI and ISA, and so we've no experience with the PCI/EISA bridge chip<br>
on that MB.<br>
<p>
2. Because of the EISA bridge, I think that our MILO will not run on an<br>
ALCOR. Without MILO, you must depend on SRM, and the kernel's SRM support,<br>
while fine for some platforms, seems woefully deficient for others (I'm<br>
currently playing with a MIKASA (AlphaServer 1000) with PCI/EISA and SRM,<br>
and having a good number of problems).<br>
<p>
So, bottom line is, I think you're (currently) out of luck... :-(<br>
<p>
<p>
<i>&gt; and what's the stability of the SO on it. Where to find the latest</i><br>
<i>&gt; distribution, how to install it, how</i><br>
<i>&gt; to make a backup of the flashrom in case we get in trouble, etc...</i><br>
<p>
Stability, for *supported* platforms, is quite good, now. No shared libraries<br>
(yet), but they're coming...<br>
<p>
Backing up FLASH isn't really needed unless you intend to install MILO<br>
there; but I believe this isn't possible right now, anyway. Or maybe<br>
possible, but not advisable, given that total support for the platform<br>
is not present in LINUX nor MILO...<br>
<p>
If you don't have an Alpha platform that currently runs LINUX, you'd best<br>
wait for someone else to do get the ALCOR going. If such is available to<br>
you, then use that as a build machine, and have a go at the ALCOR... :-)<br>
<p>
