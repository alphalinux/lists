> I just started to install AXP-Linux on my UDB. It went wrong, and my
> D:(FAT) partition crashed. In this way i can't boot the NT , so i can't
> place the milo images on the C: partition. There is no guarantee for the
> SW!!!

If you have any other PC with SCSI interface and one SCSI external
harddisk, you can format & partition it with that PC and put
linload.exe and milo into FAT partition. NTLOADER is not requied if
you don't use NT at all. If the PC is linux machine, I prefer that you
should make 2 more small partition to hold axp-kernel image and
root-image. use "dd" to copy them into the partition.

or, if everything works just well, you don't need to put milo.exe into
your hard-disk to boot linux.

Just write linload.exe and milo onto FAT formatted floppy disk and set
ARC console to boot it. after linux boot, you can put milo into FAT
partition.
