I've heard that the "SRM" firmware used for booting DEC Unix takes
something like ~2MB of RAM, and stays in RAM after the kernel --either the
one from DEC or the Linux one-- got booted.

So (not sure of the formula!): 2 + K + 1.85 + 54.78 = 64

This would mean that with ARC+Milo+Linux dynamical buffer cache,
you could free up to ~3.8MB additional ram for user processes on your system...
not taking into account K ~= kernel size.

However, I am not sure that Linux-Alpha would work on a DEC 600 5/266.
I intent to run Linux on an Digital AlphaStation 400 some months ago, but I
could not get "very positive" informations. For example, I got
conflicting infos about the acronym for the AlphaStation 400 motherboard,
called by some people "avanti", and by others "mustang" !
