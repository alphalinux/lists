On Tue 21 May,
> as long as we don't have to bend Linux or through away great ideas from Linus
> how to do things right and as long as it doesn't take too much effort
> I'd really like to see DEC Unix compatibility as a design goal for
> the *current* Linux/AXP development since we're still at least very close to
> it and trying to get back to some compatibility level in one or two years
> from now without any synchronisation in the mean time may be much more hassle...

I'd very much encourage trying to have some form of compatibility. I have two
specific examples from having owned an Alpha box for only a couple of weeks.

Case 1: to be able to receive executables and objects from a friend who has OSF/1.
He's not able to supply source code, contractually, so if I can't have binary
compatibility, I'm scuppered on this one.

Case 2: I'm looking at porting a couple of compilers on limited resources. Being
able to generate a Linux version and then give my executables to someone with OSF/1,
even if just to persuade them to fund the project further, would be immensly
useful.
