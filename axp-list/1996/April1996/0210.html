Max Gutberlet <MGutberlet@server.net4you.co.at> replied to my message
about Multia/UDB SCSI problems:

> First: The SCSI device HAS automatic termination detection.

As I said before, I've heard this claim from various people (but not from
Digital), and I don't believe it. I've examined the PCI riser card which
contains the SCSI interface, and there is a passive terminator (220/330
ohm surface mount resistor pack) wired directly to the SCSI lines. Even
if there was some way to cut power and ground to the resistor pack, this
would still leave spurious current paths (effectively 220 ohms between any
pair of lines on the bus).

> I have hooked up a Nakachima 7 CD-ROM Changer and it just worked fine.

I too have tried a Nakamichi changer, as well as a Toshiba 3401.

When I used a short SCSI cable (about 18 inch) things worked in a flaky
manner. When I use a 6 foot cable, things fail consistently. I know that the
cables, external terminator, and drives are all OK because they work fine on
either of two different host adapters on my Pentium machine, and on the '825
in the Multia.

Unless someone comes up with a better solutioni, I am going to desolder the
resistor pack from the riser card. When I'm not using external SCSI devices
I can plug an external terminator onto the connector.
