On Sun, 7 Apr 1996 wada@ee.ibaraki.ac.jp wrote:
> >MILO *and* the kernel think *every* floppy is 2.88 since, on Alphas, there's
> >no CMOS to tip them off. It doesn't matter, unless, of course you made a
> >2.88 and expected to be able to read it... :-)
>
> Because my Windows NT does not support 2.88MB floppy, I don't know how to
> make one.

Just add "floppy=0,4,cmos" to the bootparameters, and the kernel will find
the floppy as a normal 1.44MB one.

Or you can use the /dev/fd0H1440 as the device.
