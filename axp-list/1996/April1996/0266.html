> Chris Dale <chrisd@resntl.bhp.com.au> writes:
> > I am attempting to get XF86 working on my multia (UDB) but am at a loss
> > as to what sort of video card is present in this machine. Most of the
> > doc's reference TGA - this means little to me (from a non-pc background).

> TGA is DEC proprietary. It's not compatible with anything else (except
> certain older DEC graphics cards).

It's proprietary in the sense that DEC is the only manufacturer of
21030 based graphics boards. Getting the docs for the chip is not a
problem, though: just call up DEC Semiconductor and ask for the 21030
Reference Manual.

While I'm not a graphics expert by any means, it surely is refreshing
to see a graphics chip that actually makes *sense*. Compared to any
VGA chip, the 21030 is a pure beauty and I think the speed of the Xtga
server shows that they got the interface right.

The only downside is that the 21030 does not support 16bpp mode (and
it obviously won't be able to run any SVGAlib programs either; but
isn't that what X was invented for?). It looks like the 21230
(PowerStorm) does support 16bpp mode, but I don't know how a $750
ZLXp-E1 card compares to a $399 PowerStorm performancewise (you'd
think they saved money somewhere; maybe the low-end PowerStorm uses
DRAM instead of VRAM?).

--david

PS: Since you might know that I'm an MPEG speed freak: a Cabrio with a
TGA is capable of doing RedsNightmare.mpg at 69 frames/second (that's
not under Linux/X11 though).
