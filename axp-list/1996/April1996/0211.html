I had to laugh when I read this, it's so typical! I was in a group of
physicists doing relativity simulations on supercomputers, all in fortran.

This is beginning to get way off the list's topic, but I just have to
point out that Fortran still does give a performance advantage. Ask a
compiler person and they will tell you that many agressive compiler optimizations
cannot be applied to C code, mostly because of C pointer semantics. In
Fortran you can always tell if two active variables share the same memory;
in C it is in general impossible. Recently Hewlett-Packard annouced a 30%
increase in their reported SPEC numbers by improving their compiler performance;
apparently most of it is due to introducing an arbitrary assumption that
the pointers in SPEC code do not alias storage. I think they will get
criticized for that, to say it mildly.

ObAlpha content: the new HP PA-RISC SPEC numbers top even the 400MHz EV5 Alpha.
