Like all manufacturers of chips Digital "bins" parts at a certain
MHz range. You can often get parts which can be over-revved. Whether
it is safe or not is another matter. Also, like all other chip
manufacturers, the Mhz rating climbs during the lifetime of the chip,
partially due to manufacturing techniques and partially due to changes
(eg a die shrink). That's why Digital is producing systems running
the chips faster - 'cos they're binned at that speed...
