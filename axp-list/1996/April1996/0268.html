STOP!!!

21230 != POWERSTORM

The 21230 is a "video codec", and is used on our J-Video board (don't know
the actual product name). It does MPEG decompression, mostly. I don't know
details, but from www.digital.com you can find some info about it, under
the chips subject, or Digital Semiconductor.

Now, I know a bit less about the PowerStorm, but the name refers to an entire
board, not a chip thereon. They come in three flavors, and again, you can
find info via www.digital.com, under the AlphaStation topic. I believe the
3D10 (base model) is 8-bit only, like the low-end TGA, then we go to 3D40
which is the 24-plane equivalent, and finally the 4D20 (I may have the model
numbers all screwed up) with 24-planes plus Z-buffer and ???

Interesting note, that the main chip of the PowerStorm is founded by IBM...

Also, I think it is a more advanced engine based on the TGA design; but there
is some kind of (cheap) Cirrus chip (CL5422?) on there for VGA compatibility.
I think they are all faster than their TGA-equivalents.

I'll see if I can find out more concrete details and let you know.
