Hello

>The best board on the market is the DeskStation Raptor3 Reflex. Two
>models ISA/PCI or 6 slot PCI (comes with 2 Printer/4 Serial/Sound
>insralled), this last card also comes with FAST SCSI-2 AND and FAST WIDE
>SCSI-3 controllers on-board.
>
>The CPU module is replaceable so that you can go from a 366 MHZ to a 466
>MHz just by changing one board. I have used Linux on the DEC EB164
>board but not on the Raptor, so please be sure and find out if their HAL
>will support Linux.

I think this is a stretch to say its the best. I'll give you a few reasons
why. One, Deskstation like to be the first on the market with new product,
not the best, just first. I "use" to sell it so I know. When they first
came out with the Raptor it and wires and a one watt resistor hanging on the
backside of the board...not final product, just first on the market.

Second, when the calls started to come in about problems Deskstation told me
to tell the customer to "work with them" to resolve the problems. Tape
backups didn't work, SCSI-2 I/O problems with the NT event viewer filling up
and I/O performance 20% slower than anything else on the market.

Third, is has a 128-bit data path to cpu, cache and main memory, this is
proven to loose performance from the EB164 DEC design, which has 256-bit.
Also, tests we have conducted show the EB164-366Mhz is faster than
Deskstation's EV56-400.

Finally, Deskstation's system will not run Linux because of its design and
they do not want to support UNIX, this I've been told more than once by
them. They feel NT is the only market.

FYI; Deskstation just purchased some 100-250 EB164 motherboards from
Digital. Wonder why?

Look before you buy!
