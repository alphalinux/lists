First off, they certainly *can* run LINUX; they look identical to AVANTIs,
as MILO and the kernel see them, although the MB is not daughtercard based.

Overclocking is not really necessary; the CPU's on those (100MHz) MUSTANGS
were qualified at 166, I believe. And changing the speed of the CPU is a
matter of jumper(s) on the motherboard.

*HOWEVER*, there is also a PAL on the MB which will only allow it to run
at 100MHz; it's socketed, and thus the units are field upgradeable by the
customer to 166MHz, but you've gotta pay for the PAL, and that may be a bit
more than you're willing to part with, say $1000 or more (I'm not certain
of the number, but could find out if you wish). The PAL isn't that expensive,
its the speed that costs, I guess...

Note that the MUSTANG/AVANTI are EV4/APECS based, unlike the UDB/NONAME
which are based on the LCA chip; EV4/APECS are better performers than the
LCA at the same clock rate by a fair margin.
