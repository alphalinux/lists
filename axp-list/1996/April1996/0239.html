We're getting a very odd random occurance with mail (sendmail) on our UDB.
Sometimes, messages seem to get dropped into the user's spool file at a
slightly wrong offset. The result is a malformed spool file which the
standard "popper" misreads, and the Qualcomm "qpopper" chokes on.

The bug manifests in this way (i.e., the spool file follows this form):
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Ffrom user@blah.blah.blah Mon April 15 12:12:12 1996
<rest of message from user>

rom otheruser@blah.blah.net Mon April 15 13:13:13 1996
<rest second message from otheruser>
-------------------------------------------------------------------

Note the double "F" at the beginning of the file, and the missing "F" in
front of "rom" before the second message.

Has anyone else seen this behavior before? Could it be a seek() problem?
