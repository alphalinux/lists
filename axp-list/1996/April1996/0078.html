Philipp,
I'm biased - I work for Digital and I have an office full of
Alphas. However, that said here is my advice. There are 4 Alpha
systems that you might consider, but which one depends on how much
you have to spend. The prices that I've seen advertised is out
of date by a long shot on these systems.

Either buy a board and plug in an NCR 810 SCSI and one
of Number 9 or Mach 64 PCI graphics cards or buy a UDB (aka Multia).
The UDB is _extremely_ cheap and it will out perform a 100Mhz pentium
but be warned that it doesn't come with a big disk as standard. Of
the boards, you should consider Noname, AlphaPC64 and, the big one,
EB164. The EB164 is expensive (it is however fast). Of the boards
right now I'd say that the AlphaPC64 is a nicely balanced board with
reasonable power for price. ROI sell this board at a good price, so
try calling them. Aspen is selling it's own boards as are Kenetics
with their Platform2000 which I believe is a nice system.

Dave
