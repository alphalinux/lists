My comments are irritating? I should have left off the
name for EV6 altogether. I'm not sure what it will be
called or when it will be announced. However, it is important
to diferentiate ev5 froom ev56 as ev56 gives byte and word
memory access which can be important. There's a reasonably
easy test which I do in hwrpb.c. However it involves some
unexplained instructions that basically probe for the
CPU's features. I would imagine that libc etc would use
/proc/cpuinfo to figure out what I/O map to use.

The comments are irritating!
EV56 is the 21164A, a enhanced version of the 21164, implemented in
a 0.35u process and using a mixed 2V / 3.3V supply scheme.
It's clocked at about 433MHz. Other changes compared to the 21164
are new byte-wide memory access instructions and larger D-cache (?).
It has been announced and there was a short article in Electronic Design,
February 19, 1996.
EV6 must be the 21264 but it has not been announced yet.
