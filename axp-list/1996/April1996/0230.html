On Apr 16, 8:39am, Lance T. Parker wrote:
> Subject: Re: Which Alpha main board to buy?
> The best board on the market is the DeskStation Raptor3 Reflex. Two
> models ISA/PCI or 6 slot PCI (comes with 2 Printer/4 Serial/Sound
> insralled), this last card also comes with FAST SCSI-2 AND and FAST WIDE
> SCSI-3 controllers on-board.
I don't know how much comparable system from DIGITAL cost, but the Raptor/
Reflex systems are really expensive (starting at about 11.000DM for
21064/275 up to 30.000DM for 21164/366) for normal users. If compared
to real workstations (like SGI) they seem to be very cheap... (while they
offer much more performance)

> The CPU module is replaceable so that you can go from a 366 MHZ to a 466
> MHz just by changing one board. I have used Linux on the DEC EB164
> board but not on the Raptor, so please be sure and find out if their HAL
> will support Linux.
On Cebit'96 we talked to some guys of dcp, _the_ distributor of Raptor/Reflex
systems in Europe. They really want to be supported by Linux and are still
working on a special portation on their own, but they search for some
Linux/Alpha developers, too. I am still in contact with them, so it would
be helpful if you send some comments on this to the mailing-list.

> You can reach them at www.dti.com.
In germany it may be better to access http://www.dcp.de/products/Raptor.html.
