Jay,

Thanks for looking into this for me. Both of my units have the same
problem; I think both have the same riser, but I only have one of them
open at the moment to check.

On the component side is etched the number 5022562-01, right under the
fine pitch header, above the PCI connector, and to the right of the '810
chip. At the far right side is a surface mount resistor pack, a yellowish
20-pin SO package, marked 4820P-003-2211/331. This is the terminator.
On the back of the card is etched the same 5022562-01 number that was on
the front, and also the number 5422563.

The cable assembly for the external SCSI connector is marked with a
Digital part number of 17-04124-01 Rev. A01. There is no obvious
indication that the external connector includes any kind of termination;
I would expect to see some kind of switch to sense the mating of a male
connector. But perhaps the connector people are too clever for me.

If the connector does contain automatic termination, the combination
of the connector, the riser, and the Toshiba drive would seem to result
in three terminators on the bus even with no external devices. Or is
the Toshiba unterminated?

Thanks,
Eric
