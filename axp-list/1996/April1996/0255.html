To put it clear: Raptor doesn't run Linux at this moment. At least not stable
enough for the user. I am working for the distributors of these systems here in
Germany and I am in charge of the Linux/Raptor-Project in our company. The main
problem is the different chipset around the CPU. DTI does not use the standard
Digital Sem. Chips and that gives them some advantages like the ability to ship
400MHz 21164a systems from stock at this very moment. But it's quite a load of
work to get the connection between the hardware/chipset and the software to
work properly. In my opinion you can expect to see a Linux running on a 500MHz
Raptor in September/October...
