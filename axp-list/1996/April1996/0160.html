OK, I just tried booting both the latest MILO from:

gatekeeper.dec.com: pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Miniloader/images/XL/milo

with that kernel, as well as the older MILO (milo-1.3.77-27mar1525.xl) from
the pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/X11 directory, and both worked fine.

Are you sure you got the boot line correct?

boot fd0:vmlinux.gz load_ramdisk=1 root=/dev/fd0

This is needed with kernels later than 1.3.48, I believe, as that's when the
RAMdisk activation options changed.

Also, is it possible that there's something strange with the partitions on the
hard disk in that system? Since it was (most likely) running NT, or at least
setup with NT, it may have one of those partition tables with the "extended"
partitions? I'm not convinced of the reliability of the MSDOS partition code
when presented with one of those, so:

if the boot goes back to MILO *before* successfully printing the
"Partition check" messages (ie right after the Ethernet probe messages),
try removing the SCSI disk, and re-init the partition table on another
LINUX box (even an INTEL will work... :-) to *not* use extended partitions.

Good luck, let me know how you get on...
