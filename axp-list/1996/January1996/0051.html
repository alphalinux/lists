According to Nigel Metheringham:
> However I shutdown the system (telinit 0, telinit 6 - halt and reboot
> to the BSD people!), it always shuts down and effectively hangs. I
> have to power cycle to get the system to reboot.
>
> I had a look at the OEM manual and played with the reset jumpers,
> without success - still the only way I have of restarting the system
> is to cycle the power.
>
> So:-
> 1. Is there something that can be done to make the system
> be rebootable?

Yes. Some kernel hacks is needed. NetBSD/Alpha [shame on me] was able
to do this. It dropped me back to the srm (not really reseted
I think). If you did reboot it automatically loaded a kernel again, else
it stopped at the prompt. Maybe we could 'look' a little at this code
to speed up the support for our beloved linux/alpha...

>
> 2. Has someone got a working reset button, and could they
> publish the jumper settings and wiring, please.

Uhm.. I just looked in the manual, flipped a jumper and connected a reset-
switch to a connector on the motherboard. Voila!
