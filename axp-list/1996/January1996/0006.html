On Thu, 4 Jan 1996, Fred Roy wrote:

> My question comes after a recent mail exchange on the way to rebuild a
> kernel from the RedHat distribution.
> I am also attempting to rebuild a Linux/Alpha kernel for the first time,
> here from the BLADE_0.3 distribution. The Makefile in arch/alpha/boot
> fails because the `quickstrip' command is not found. Should I use the
> BLADE_0.3 /usr/bin/strip instead ??

No! That will result in a unbootable kernel, unless you're using a very
recent binutils. If you bought a Red Hat CD, quickstrip was included. If
you installed the Beta or downloaded it, you may be missing it.

In either case, ftp the file quickstrip-1.0-1.axp.rpm from ftp.redhat.com
in the /pub/redhat-2.1/axp-beta/RedHat/RPMS directory. After you get
it, do "rpm -ivh quickstrip-1.0-1.axp.rpm".
