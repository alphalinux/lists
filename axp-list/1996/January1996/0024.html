I'd suspect that libg++ is compatible with the GNU libc, which is a good
reason to move to GNU libc.

> Talking of libc: is there now a concensus of moving to libg? It
> sounds like a fine idea to me, but on the other hand I think it would
> be a bad idea to do a move before there is a reasonable chance that
> the x86 world will move to that library as well.

I'd like to make the move. I'm not sure the Intel world will ever change
to it, but I strongly suspect the SPARC/Alpha/PowerPC ports will eventually
get to GNU libc (the SPARC folks are already working with it). GNU libc
has binary compatibility (on the shared lib level) with Linux libc 5.x as
a goal though, so the Linux world might split on the libc issue (though I
hope not).
