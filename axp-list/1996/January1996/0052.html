> The first problem is board specific. There are two jumpers, one that
> should be wired to the reset button and the second that can be wired
> to a lockable switch _or_ just jumpered. The second jumper enables
> the reset button to work. Having said that, I've not got my Noname

I have the reset pins on my noname connected to the button labeled as
such on the front of my case, as such it works fine. (except for its
exposed position, ive accidently hit it once ! ).

The board was delivered with the reset = HALT, not reset = hardware
reset.
One day I might wire the reset through the keyswitch so I dont accidently
reset the system again...

Ive found the redhat reboot command doesnt work at all for me. I copied my
inittab and rc files off my Slackware based x86 system, and they are sublty
incompatable with the way RedHat setup init and friends to behave a sys V
init.
Specificly, reboot doesnt kill any running programs, the only programs
that die are the ones started by init, as a result the umount always fails.
I havent been annoyed enough yet to grab the source and set them to work
the slackware way.
