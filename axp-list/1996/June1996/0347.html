At 05:18 PM 6/25/96 -0400, you wrote:
>> Could somebody *please* prove me wrong and tell me where to get hardware
>> docs?
>Speaking of which, does anybody know the pinout of the splitter cable for
>getting at the two serial ports? The UDB brings both serial ports out to one
>DB-25; I'm guessing that you can get at the first one with a normal serial
>cable and only need the splitter if you want at the second port. Anybody know
>for sure if that's true?

A *REAL* DB-25 supports 2 serial cables innately. It depends upon the
device and what the cable does. I suspect most cables are just wired for
hte first set of tx and rx pins.... *NOTE*: the following is from a book I
have. I assume no liability for this data. If it should be wrong, or if
DEC didn't adhere to standards, the use of this information is your implied
consent to my irresponsibility and *YOUR* own responsibility for your hardware.
====================================
1 Frame Ground
2 Transmit Data
3 Receive Data
4 RTS
5 CTS
6 DSR
7 signal ground
8 DCD
9 +v dc test voltage
0 -v dc test voltage
11 QM (Equalizer mode? What's that?)
12 2nd DCD
13 2nd CTS
14 2nd TxD
15 transmitter clock
16 2nd RxD
17 receiver clock
18 not used
19 2nd RTS
20 DTR
21 signal quality detect
22 ring indicator
23 data rate selector
24 (tc) external transmitter clock output
25 not used
======================================
