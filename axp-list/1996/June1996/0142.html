I just wanted to take the time to thank all of the people who
have helped me, tolerated my silly questions, patiently answered them,
and made it possible for me to install Linux on my alpha. By the way,
David, I guess this note counts as a confirmation that Linux runs on
the Aspen Timberline (EB164) with a #9 S3 764X based card and an
ISP1020. :-)

I use this machine for both Digital Unix and Linux, and there
is a rather big headache involved with switching between the two. I
blew away the ARC console and replaced it with MILO (Out of the 16 ROM
blocks, 13 were taken up by Debug, ARC, SRM, I decided, perhaps
mistakenly, that ARC could go). It's trivial to switch from MILO to
SRM. However, when I want to switch back from SRM, I set OS_TYPE to
NT (Debug doesn't appear to be an option), fail into debug, attach a
dumb term and bootopt #2 (MILO). This is somewhat of a pain, since I
don't like having a dumb term sit next to my machine and taking up
valuable desk real estate. If someone has a more elegant solution,
I'm all ears (err.. eyes).

Another interesting inconvience is that MILO does not seem to
save the environmental variables. I'm assuming that this may be
related to the fact that I did not use the "environment" command while
running fmu.gz. I wanted to know if doing so would be in any way
detrimental to the SRM and if this will fix my problem, allowing me to
save the environmental variables.

Again, thanks for the help and kudos to all of the people that
made AXP/Linux (and RedHat) what it is today.

Roman
