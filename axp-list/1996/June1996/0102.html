Note that there are two styles of motherboard. One uses the AT-style
keyboard connector, and the other has PS/1-style keyboard and mouse
connectors. Makes a difference if you already have a keyboard, plus if
you have an AT cabinet, you'll have to enlarge the keyboard opening into an
oval to allow access to the mouse port.

Also, I'd ask anyone selling me the board to also supply the technical
documentation for it.
