To those keeping tabs on such things, I am pleased to announce
that this evening gcc, the binutils, and gdb were successfully
bootstrapped with shared libraries.

It will still be a little bit before things are ready for the
general public, but things are moving much better than expected.

You may also be interested in some enticing numbers:

bash$ size /usr/bin/gdb ./gdb
text data bss dec hex filename
1087376 363024 130220 1580620 181e4c /usr/bin/gdb
645316 336914 114864 1097094 10bd86 ./gdb

bash$ ldd ./gdb
libbfd.so.2.6.2 => ../lib/libbfd.so.2.6.2 (0x0000015555556000)
libreadline.so => ../lib/libreadline.so (0x00000155556b2000)
libopcodes.so.2.6.2 => ../lib/libopcodes.so.2.6.2 (0x00000155557de000)
libtermcap.so.1 => ../lib/libtermcap.so.1 (0x00000155558ee000)
libc.so => ../lib/libc.so (0x00000155559f6000)

bash$ ls -l /bin/pwd ./pwd # stripped
-rwxr-xr-x 1 richard richard 4856 Jun 10 03:01 ./pwd
-rwxr-xr-x 1 root root 90112 Apr 5 11:05 /bin/pwd
