> whats the difference between the UDB and the Multia??

The Multia comes with NT, which needs to be replaced to make it into a nice
computer. The UDB comes with an older release of Linux, which needs to be
replaced to make it into a nice computer.

I personally suspect that Digital has realized that no sane person wants
anything to do with NT, and that they are in danger of ending up with a
zillion units of shrinkwrapped NT and nothing to do with them short of
landfill. So they're shoving one along with every UDB that goes out the door.
That probably won't help; expect to see shrinkwrapped NTs expoxied to magazine
covers, and generally following AOL disks through the assorted giveaway
channels.

BTW, from personal experience I can assure everyone that the so-called "easy
WebServer" makes a dandy UDB. You can replace the NT without ever once giving
it a chance to boot --- I know I did:-).
