>
> >> What Operating Systems can the UDB run? I know NT 4.0 runs on it (running
> >> now) and know Linux runs on it. What about OpenVMS and OSF/1?
> >
> >Officially, the UDB only runs Linux :-)
>
> :-)) yes of course
> >
> >And the Multia only officially runs NT 3.5 or 3.51. I've heard of people
> >running Digital Unix (formerly OSF/1), but it is unsupported. I've never
> >heard of anyone trying OpenVMS, and can't imagine why anyone would want to.
>
> I just read a few day ago in vmsnet.alpha, or comp.sys.dec from a DEC man, that
> it is possible to run OVMS on Multias.

It is an option to change to the Digital Unix or OpenVMS loaders on the ARC
console.

The DEC engineer who fixed my VX40E-F3 (Digital Easy Web Server,
converted to Red Hat Linux) tried to change the boot options to Digital
Unix, 'cuz that's what he thought I was running.

Realizing the blasphemous implications of what I am about to say <g>, I'd be
curious as to how much better the Digital compilers are, compared to GCC &
G77.

As an aside to anyone thinking of buying something UDB-ish, one benefit
of owning such a machine is the wonderful onsite repair service Digital
provides; they actually dispatched an engineer to my house to fix my lone
gadget. If Digital could muster a sales & marketing staff to match their
repair staff, WIntel products would wither and die. As it were, I found
out about inexpensive Alpha systems through the proverbial grapevine, and
actually bought from a 3rd party. Why is it that Digital's homepage on
UDB type gizmos quote a suggested retail price of $1200 to $1500, while
their Digital Direct page quotes a retail price of $3000 to $4000?!

-Scott
