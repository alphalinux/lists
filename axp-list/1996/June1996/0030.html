>> I rate the 166Mhz Intel to be superior to a AXPpci33 166Mhz or
>> AXPpci33+ 233Mhz and by far cheaper ;)
>
>Steve Gaudet at DCG says he figures a 166Mhz. UDB to be about equal
>to a 75 Mhz. Pentium (correct me if I'm wrong).

Our UDB does do X faster (thanks for the TGA support, Jay!) than
anything I've seen on an Intel, but I haven't seen anything better
than a #9GXE64Pro. The actual feel is about that of a P-75 when
using it. That's with both machines having 32M. (Note that there
is a HUGE limitation on the UDB TGA: 8 bits is the max color...other
TGA boards can do 32 bit color, but our XFree server can't yet).

>Compiles seem a little faster, this may well be due to faster disk I/O
>(fast-scsi-2 as opposed to IDE on the pentium) and more RAM (56 MB
>on the UDB as opposed to 48MB on the i586)

No, it is the CPU/cache.

>Code is larger due to no shared libraries (yet), not a problem if you
>have a lot of disk space and RAM.
>
>All in all I think the 266 Mhx UDB is a great system, but once you add
>in the large external Hard drive, an external tape drive, an external
>CDROM and some extra RAM the $3K machine starts pushing $5K :-)

Well, RAM is cheap these days. Also, I believe that one of the
Seagate Medalist 1.0G SCSI drives *will* fit in that little bay.
I don't think Seagate advertises them as small, but they definately
are. Anyone got one they could test?

The biggest deal with the UDB is that it's memory starved with only
256K of cache. I think that's *all* you can put on the 166, but the
233 can have 512k (right?) and that makes a hell of a difference.

Me, I'm still looking for a good deal on a Jensen board....<sigh>
