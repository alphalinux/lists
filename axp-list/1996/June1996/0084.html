Just for my own information, how big is the .lita section on that final
executable? (Use objdump -h to find out.)

The new ELF tools limit you to a single 64KB section rather than support
multiple 64KB subsections like ECOFF. To compensate for this, they also
eliminate the replication that exists in the ECOFF tables.

I've collected some statistics from some of the programs on my system:

Program ECOFF ELF static ELF shlib

pwd 0xd50 n/a 0x88
as 0x6fb0 0x4398 0x28b8
gdb 0x17120 0xd7e0 0xb9e0
cc1plus 0x17360 0xd460 0xcd78
xf86_s3 0x19040 n/a n/a

As you can see, some of these big 'uns are right on the line of being
too big -- I'm hoping f771 isn't too much bigger than this, because
that will force development to move in a direction I'd rather not just
yet. Err, well, let's just say that I want to go there, but it is a
hard enough problem that I don't want to feel that it is a requirement.
