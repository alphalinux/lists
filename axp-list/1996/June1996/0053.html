Well, libg++ would require some hacking to get it to work with the
current libc. Once glibc is in place, libg++ isn't much of a headache
any more. Unfortunately, libg++ temporarily does not build on top of
glibc, but that should be fixed real soon (one or two days). Anyhow,
by the time a glibc based distribution is available, it's a pretty
sure bet libg++ will be there too. I think it's not unrealistic to
expect that distributors will soon start to use glibc instead of the
old libc, but that's about as specific as I can be... :-)
