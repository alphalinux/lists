Well, as this is undoubtly because their mailing list is adding an
unusual "Reply-To:" header (we had that before), Red Hat should be
aware that this is hard to avoid. In case somebody else is interested,
you may solve this problem by using 'procmail' as mail filter and by
adding these lines to '.procmailrc':

:0:
* ^(from|to|cc|sender):.*axp-list@redhat.com
| grep -v "^Reply-To: axp-list@redhat.com$" >>$MAIL

This eliminates the "Reply-To:" header, and your mail client will
behave as expected.

Bye, Martin

PS.: I don't want to be counterproductive on Red Hat's mailing list
policies. I think people who are aware enough to add procmail to
their mail system will probably be able to properly handle their mail
client as well.
