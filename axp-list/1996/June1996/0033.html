> >Does the off-chip cache run at core speed or bus speed? I'd heard 21164 based
> >machines ran the level-3 cache (I think 21164's have 96kb L2 on the die itself
> >?) at
> >full core speed. Is that true? What about 21066/21064's? Do they have L2 cache

>
> Experts? I don't know on this one...
> Yes, the 21164's 96kb of L2 cache is on the die, the 21066 and 21064 only have 8kb I-cache and
8kb of D-cache on the die. The 21064A has 16bk I-cache and 16kb D-cache. The level-3 cache on
the 21164 is standard 10 nanosecond cache. I believe the oem 21164 mother board will support L3
cache as fast as 6 nanoseconds (if you can find it).

You should be able to get a 233Mhz UDB with monitor, keyboard and 32MB of extra RAM for around
$3,000 U.S. The same setup in 166 Mhz for less than $2,000. If the VRC15 from DEC is too pricey
use the Sampo AlphaScan 15MX, it is IDENTICAL to the VRC15 (apparently DEC oem's them).
Any Digital authorized distributor can give you this pricing (I work for one) but I don't want to
turn this into an advertisement.
