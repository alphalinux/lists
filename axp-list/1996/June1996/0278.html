A warning about overclocking Alphas...

After some discussion on the net about overclocking Alpha chips I tried several
higher speed clocks on my 300Mhz 21164 Screamer board. The system apparently
worked fine at 333Mhz and has been operating there for several weeks without
problem. Until now...

The other day I spoke to Steve Fried at Microway and he wanted a comparison
benchmark speed for dlinpack (double precision linpack) under f77 (DEC
compiler). He is just about done tuning his NDP compiler and is getting
comparable code speed to the DEC f77 (this from him, I don't have the new
version of his compiler yet). He mentioned problems running the dlinpack
benchmark that caused him to lower the clock speed from 333Mhz to 300Mhz on his
test machine (with a 300Mhz CPU chip). Well, I compiled and ran the benchmark
and my Alpha crashed!

This machine, overclocked from 300Mhz to 333Mhz, has run large crunch jobs of
all sorts for weeks without error and without crashing, AND runs the DEC
diagnostic tests without problems. The dlinpack benchmark (with matrix sizes
upped to 400x400 to make timing easier) apparently stresses the system,
probably the memory system, more than anything else I have run and exposes this
fault. Note that the system runs the dlinpack benchmark fine if the clock
speed is lowered to 300Mhz to match the chip rated speed.

For reference, since Steve Fried found this problem he no longer recommends
overclocking Alphas...
