On Tue, 25 Jun 1996, Bennett Todd wrote:
>
> > There is no fmu.gz for Milo on the Multia because strictly speaking I
> > don't know if it will work. Whilst the Multia _is_ a Noname clone
> > and it _should_ work, I'm not prepared to tell folks to do this
> > without a backup plan.
>
> So, if I understand correctly, the currently-recommended procedure is to keep
> a floppy in the drive and stay with ARC->Linload->Milo->Linux boot?

Umm.. You don't need a floppy: you can create a msdos filesystem on a disk
(make it a small one - just enough for linload+milo, maybe two versions of
them for easier testing etc). Then you have it all on the harddisk - at least
that is how we do it at the university (not on a UDB machine, it's a eb64+,
but I can't imagine that it would be any different in this case). Very
simple, and speeds up booting quite a bit..

> "... without a backup plan" --- what kind of backup plan do you mean? I think
> I'm fatally confused here. Now I'm getting nervous --- I wouldn't want my
> adorable little UDB to catch a case of confusion from me:-).

Without knowing anything about the UDB, I suspect the problem is that it has
no "fallback" bootup method. So if something goes wrong with the flash,
you're screwed. With the development motherboards (at least the Cabriolet),
you can set a jumper to boot the thing into a known state (a _awkward_ known
state, but still..).

The ARC console booting is actually pretty practical, as long as you keep
linload and milo on the harddisk.
