> After building a new kernel on a jensen, do I just copy the new
> gziped kernel over the old one or do I have to use swriteboot ? I am not
> using aboot (couldn't get that to work :). I also am having the problem

If you don't use aboot, you have to get the new kernel on the right
place, that is the begining of the disk. Also, I recall you have to
compile it with `make srmboot`. The usual gziped image isn't bootable
by the SRM firmware. (It's a long time ago that I haven't used aboot,
so maybe I am wrong here.)

I think Michael Schwingen put a precompiled aboot-image on
ftp.digital.com. You might want to use that one to install a recent
kernel, and then you might be able to get a recent version of aboot
working.

Good luck, Martin
