On Sun, 30 Jun 1996, Tim Barthel wrote:

> Brian E.W. Wood wrote:
> >
> > On Sun, 30 Jun 1996 krathis@fast.net wrote:
> > > I'm probably going to buy one of the DEC alpha 166mhz (jr's) and before I do,
>
> Okay, now I'm confused. I was planning on purchasing a Lynx 166 from
> Nekotech. They tell me it is a DEC multia. Is this they same as the
> UDB? The two sound very similar, but we all know that's a big
> difference from being the same. I'm not sure which VX40 it is, is
> anyone familiar with the Lynx?

As far as I know (and anyone smarter please correct me) the only
difference between a Multia and a UDB is that a Multia comes with
Windows NT bundled/installed. I have never heard of a "Lynx" but
a VX-40 is a Multia/UDB with a hardwired 166 Mhz. 21066 and a fixed
256kb. cache.

>
> > I have the 233 version and I think it is a very decent machine. The
> > packaging is great. The performance of the 166 mhz. unit would be
> > somewhere between that of a 75 Mhz. pentium and a 100Mhz one.
>
> Why so slow? I would expect the 166Mhz 64 bit machine would be much
> faster than the 100Mhz 32 bit machine?

RISC architecture is "reduced" instruction set computing. It can therefore
take more instructions to perform a specific task than a "CISC" (complex
instruction set computing) processor. Steve Gaudet at DCG told me he figured
a 166 Mhz. alpha to be about equivalent to a 75 Mhz. Pentium. I have seen
other reports which seem to think it is a lot faster than that. I guess
it mainly depends on what you are doing - alpha chips seem to "like"
doing floating point stuff better than the intel chips. Remember that
equivalent binaries are larger for the alpha and therefore require more
dosk I/O etc. to load and run.

Again, if anyone has better info than me (which probably includes most
everybody) please feel free to jump in here and correct me.

Anyway - just my $0.02.
