I was just talking to someone who told me that digital unix (OSF/1)
binaries would run under Linux/AXP.

I understand that statically linked binaries run because Linux and OSF
are compatible on the syscall level (that excludes OSF binaries making
DEC-proprietary calls, which, however, aren't used by many codes).

However, recently, I have heard rumors that this may not always be the
case in the future; the 'source' wasn't very specific. My impression
was that LInux/Alpha developers were committed to compatibility with
OSF; is that the case?

The matter is important to me, because we will have an application
where we need high floating point speed. Since gcc generates
not-so-fast FP code for now, I was hoping that I would be able to
compile on OSF and copy statically linked executable to Linux.

Since I have OSF and DEC compiler licence, I understand that it
is legally OK. Would anyone offer their opinion on this topic?
