Well, not always true...

You can't boot an OLD kernel with a real new MILO, as these MILO's have the
new SCSI driver, which may negotiate for synchronous transfers, and the OLD
kernel with the old SCSI driver does *NOT* re-negotiate to asynchronous, so
it tries to talk to the disks ASYNCH, when the disks are expecting SYNCH.
Not Good (tm).

>
> > Does the linload image need to
> > match the kernal release?
>
> No.

Well, that's true, but...

The newer linload does a much better job of passing on memory size to MILO
from the ARC console, so you'll use all you've got. It prolly does other
things better, or at least *should*, as it *is* 10K bigger... :-)

If this IPX/samba stuff uses serial ports, you may be in trouble. We've
discovered that the serial ports seem to work *very* strangely right now on
the UDB, and are nearly unusable. Got us baffled, as the UDB looks just
like a NONAME with builtin stuff. And it USED to work; I had PPP working
nicely at 38400 a couple of months ago.

Stay tuned, we're actively working this...
