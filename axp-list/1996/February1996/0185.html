On Tue, 20 Feb 1996, Richard Henderson wrote:

> On inquiry, they indicated that DEC disables
> something at the factory for the NT system, but
> that Linux would work anyway. They could not
> tell me exactly what was being disabled, or why,
> or what effects it had on the chip.

The machine will run Linux *fine*. The favorite change they have is the
amount of flash RAM they place in the machines, which makes it impossible to
use the SRM console Digital Unix and VMS need. They have another cripple
I've heard about (though I'm not sure they've done this), which disables
an instruction that is only used by the SRM console.

Most Linux users use Milo (and maybe ARC) so this isn't an issue at all. Buy
the cheaper machine.
