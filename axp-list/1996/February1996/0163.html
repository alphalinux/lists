According to Erik Troan:
> > * Several bins have the "wrong" permission.. They are like 775 (root.root),
> > which isn't a "problem", but I really prefer all the bins to be 755..
>
> This is a result of our user/group scheme. It's correct if you look at it
> from that point of view.

Well, as I said, its not a security problem or anything. But if you do a ls -l
in say like /usr/bin, 39 bins has 775 root.root, and 426 has 755 root.root ...
And that why I used the "" around wrong... Its just think it would be more
consistent if they all had the same permissions.. And I'm well aware of RH
using the user/group scheme, and I think its a good idea. But I think that
the 775 (664) files should be the files in the users homedir.. The files in
the systems dir have nothing to gain of being 775, ofcourse they have nothing
to loose either... I just prefer that most systembins haveing as "low"
permissions as possible.. imho..

>> * the (t)csh scripts are still wrong with the stuff depending on $status.
>
> Shoot... I meant to fix this.

Is an easy solution.. But like with most shellprogrammings things, I'm sure
others think they have solutions that are 'cleaner' (ofcourse, only crazy
dudez programs in *csh..)

> > * Wrong permissions on /var/lock/emacs (I think..), shouldn't it be 1777
