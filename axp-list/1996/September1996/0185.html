Fude Yao <fyao@everest.micro.umass.edu> wrote:
> My friend has problems with ftp, rlogin and telnet. He's got a UDB
> with 166mHz alpha, E-mail is working fine; ftp, rlogin and telnet works fine
> from his machine to other machine. But ftp, rlogin and telnet deosn't work
> from other machine to his machine. What would the problem be?

The problem is tcpd.

If you're not paranoid about security, you can just change the lines in
/etc/inetd.conf. For example, change

ftp stream tcp nowait root /usr/sbin/tcpd in.ftpd -l -a

to:

ftp stream tcp nowait root /usr/sbin/in.ftpd in.ftpd -l -a

Alternatively, you can read the man page for tcpd and try to figure out how
to make it do the right thing. I gave up on it.
