Andreas Kostyrka <andreas@create.ag.or.at> wrote:
>I've bought recently a DEC Multia 233MHz, the funny thing about this is,
>that in the console it displays a 166MHz CPU :(

And later followed up with:
> Oh, by the way: the processor is in a socket, and ``233'' is written on
> it by hand, *wondering*

If it really is a VX42 (check the label), the jumpers might be set wrong.
http://www.brouhaha.com/~eric/computers/udb.html

Also, check the part number of the CPU. A 233 MHz part is 21066-AB. If the
suffix is -AA, it's an 166 MHz LCA4. If it's -BB, it's a 166 MHz LCA45.
You might be able to overclock the -BB part to 233 (or even 266), though I
don't recommend it.

If the part isn't a 21066-AB, and you were sold the unit as a 233 MHz box,
you were ripped off.

Cheers,
Eric
