I have seen yesterday something suspiciously like this, but this was
2.0.14 on a Cabriolet board and otherwise Red Hat 3.0.3, i.e. no shared
libraries at all.

I was able to watch how things evolved in a span of many hours, as I
happened to be logged on remotely but was unable to do anything.

No violent happenings, no oopses or other visible traces (syslogd
possibly went away early). Just various daemons quietly going away.
Initially I was able to stop and restart inetd services, and this gave
me a short window in which it was possible to get another login, but
soon telnetd went away once again and could not be revived. ftp worked
**from** an affected machine for many hours yet, but no way to ftp to
it.

When I noticed that something weird is starting I tried 'shutdown -r'.
It was going through initial messages but that was it. Repeated
attempts did not have any real effect.

> (The system usually continues to limp along, but you get panic messages for
> anything that causes a child to be inherited by the non-existent init. At
> least that's how I seem to remember it).

There was no panic messages. Everything was quiet but with time more
and more daemons were turning into zombies, at least according to ps.
Eventually, after some six hours or so, most likely init itself died
and the whole machine wedged solid to be revived only with a power
switch and a prolonged fsck (some incoming mail ended in /lost+found).

I am not 100% sure that this is exactly the same, but if it is it is
not limited to 2.0.17 and shared libraries.

Keep on hackin'
