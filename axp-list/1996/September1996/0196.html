>>> Oleg Shein #2 said:
>
> given:
> RedHat 3.0.3 (non-ELF), kernels (from 2.0.12 to 2.0.17),
> Mikasa (alphaserver 1000), PCI video cards (Diamond Stealth
> 3240 Video VRAM 2MB 968-based with 3026 clock chip or
> usual S3 Trio-64V+ 2MB, it doesn't depend), XFree86 3.1.2-7 and 3.1.2F .
>
> I've tried to create XF86Config by myself, using Xconfigurator
> and xf86config. X fails with the folowing output strings:
>
> Fatal server error:
> xf86EnableIOPorts: Failed to set IOPL for I/O
> ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
> what does it mean?

What it means is, the LIBC in the servers doesn't know what kind of platform
it's running on (cat /proc/cpuinfo *should* show "Mikasa", however); LIBC
has a table of known platforms, and "Mikasa" evidently doesn;t appear in it.

Fake it out by:

ln -s Avanti /etc/alpha_io_system

This will make LIBC think its running on an Avanti; that's OK, the MIKASA
is an EV4-based box too.
