Nigel Sale <nms@fgel.demon.co.uk> wrote:

>I take your point about the limitations of UDBs, but gcc was used for
>both sets of timings. Are you saying that gcc on the Alpha is very bad
>with floating point operations ?

Not exactly. You observed the following times:

UDB/233 Pentium/166
Integer 35 sec. 101 sec.
Float 30 sec. 20 sec.

If you look solely at the UDB numbers, you'll see that the integer
performance is slightly slower than the floating point performance.
But for the Pentium, there is a huge disparity between the integer
and floating point code. Without seeing the instruction sequences,
I would attribute this to the effective lack of freely available
integer registers on the Pentium. Which means that there is a
lot of memory traffic happening to deal with loop overhead.
In the case of Alpha, there are sufficient registers available
to permit loop indices/counters to reside in registers, which
precludes memory traffic for loop control, which in turn removes
the possibility of cache misses impacting the performance.
