> No it doesn't. rlogin opens a pty (allowing /dev/tty to work), but rsh just
> connects the socket to stdin/stdout/stderr w/o going through a pty.

upps, I've got it wrong. rshd doesn't open /dev/tty, but the Redhat
rshd register /dev/tty as tty for PAM. From rshd.c:

(void) pam_set_item (pamh, PAM_RUSER, remuser);
(void) pam_set_item (pamh, PAM_RHOST, hostname);
(void) pam_set_item (pamh, PAM_TTY, "tty");
