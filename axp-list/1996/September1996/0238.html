On Wed, 11 Sep 1996, Thomas Bogendoerfer wrote:

> > > if you add tty to your /etc/securetty, it doesn't fail.
> >
> > Have you tried that? With rsh stdin isn't a tty at all, so I wouldn't expect
> > it to ever work.
>
> sure. rsh opens /dev/tty as one of it's first actions.

No it doesn't. rlogin opens a pty (allowing /dev/tty to work), but rsh just
connects the socket to stdin/stdout/stderr w/o going through a pty.

[root@porky djb]# rsh ns tty
not a tty

Note that rsh invokes rlogin if you run it w/o a remote command.
