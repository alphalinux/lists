At 03:41 hrs. 09-04-96 -0400, you wrote:
>
>I'm trying to collect information about the Linux/Alpha market,
>with the intention of trying to make Linux/Alpha much preferred
>over Linux/Intel by virtue of commercial application availability.
>
>If there were a single commercial application that you would request
>to become available on Linux/Alpha, what would it be?
>Are you an end-user, or do you sell Linux/Alpha hardware?
>
>We may be able to make this occur through the use of 'sufficiently
>advanced software technology' (to paraphrase Arthur C. Clarke)
>
4 things:

1) Major Database. Preferably Oracle or Sybase.

2) A real Office Suite. WordDefect or WordPerfect, or maybe talk to IBM
about where they are going with Lotus SmartSuite and AmiPro/WordPro. ( might
be easier than watching them OS/2 it ;-} )Lotus compatible ( no, really,
full compatibility, especially macro compatibility) Personal / small office
database. Soemthing like PAradox / Access / Fox ( although that's about dead
now) Maybe someone could talk to Borland, they need help big-time, I hear.

3) A setup and configuration set that works. No RedHat RPMs are not there
yet. Let's face it, you really have to know your stuff to make an AXP box
work with Linux ( or very lucky, I guess). Something along the lines of the
Win95 installer. Let's face it, 95% of average Joes survive that install.

4) The trivial important stuff that Microsoft just "give away" . Things like
Wordpad, Dial-Up Networking, Paint, some decent FAX tools and manager, and
so on. Yes, I know you can get and compile and install and configure those
things yourself, but let's face it, how many people want to or even feel
like this much of a time investment?

My $.02 worth, anyway...
