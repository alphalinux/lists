>[price from ITS]
>> VX42 (233Mhz) same config $750

I'm wondering about this last comment... I'm definitely not in the
know, but I had the impression that all the UDBs were being dropped,
while this implies that only the VX40 166Mhz unit is being dropped,
but the VX42s are still being supported. Is that true? Am I likely
to be able to, say, get another one of these puppies in 6 months at a
similar price if I so desire?

I have some potential applications for several of these units as small
servers and development machines, but I'm hesitating to get involved
with them heavily for fear that I will get a ways down the road and
find that the only way to stay with alphas involves significantly more
money (and correspondingly higher performance that I'm not sure I need
yet)...

One of the coolest things about these systems as far as I'm concerned
right now is that if I make a commitment to Linux on Alphas, I can
start out with inexpensive medium to low performance machines and move
all the way up to very high performance machines without massive
changes to the systems I build. This true of some other platforms as
well, except that with the alphas the range is larger (lower cost,
lower performance on one end all the way up to higher performance on
the other). If I go over to HP or Sun workstations or something, the
entry level costs are much higher.
