On Thu, 5 Sep 1996 emlowe@fsa.ca wrote:

> I'm having a couple of strange problems with my UDB:
>
> When I run fdisk on /dev/sda (my UDB has the TOSHIBA MK2326FB internal) I get
> the following errors:
>
> %fdisk
>
> Kernel: unaligned trap at fffffc00004145b8: fffffc000004c1d6 28 3
> Kernel: unaligned trap at fffffc00004145bc: fffffc000004c1da 28 2
That's ``normal'':
Alpha can't do unaligned accesses -> they are emulated and that's the
message. Usually seldom used programs (or code) take very long to clean up.
So you can savely ignore them, they just mean a performace penalty, but
the isn't that bad in program like fdisk, isn't it?
