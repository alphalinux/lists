Finally, there are at least _some_ details on the 21264. From various
sources, I gather that it is:

- 8 way superscalar w/out-of-order issue
- 30 SpecInt95, 45 SpecFP95

I'm particularly curious to see what speeds that processor will be
running at. Guessing from the above data, it can't be much lower than
the current mostly-in-order-issue CPUs. Still, for the real details,
one would have to attend the Microprocessor Forum.

If anyone can provide more details (without violating NDA or whatnot,
that is), I'd be interested to hear.

--david

Two of the Alpha talks at MPF:

---
The 21264: A Superscalar Alpha Microprocessor 
with Out-of-Order Execution


Jim Keller, Digital Semiconductor


 Digital will disclose its most advanced microprocessor yet, designed
 to deliver twice the performance of any microprocessor currently
 shipping by combining a high clock frequency with aggressive
 out-of-order execution.

---
Enhancing Motion Video 
Performance in the Alpha Architecture


Pete Bannon, Digital Semiconductor


 Digital will disclose extensions to the Alpha instruction set
 architecture that will be implemented in all future Alpha chips to
 boost motion video performance.
