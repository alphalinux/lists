>>> Robert Allen Barker said:
>
> I am having a bit of trouble installing redhat on a new PC164.
>
> Everything goes well and MILO comes up but whenever I do anything
> that casues milo to detect the SCSI controller it hangs after printing two
> lines, the second line saying that the NCR810 is assigned IRQ16 (which is
> correct and the same as on the EB164). Thats the last thing that happens.
> However, about 1 time in 5 it will get past this point and everything seems
> to work, I can read drives and cd roms, and run programs.
>
> I have tried the obvious such as a different NCR810 (same brand and type),
> different video cards, harddrives, termination schemes, PC164 settings etc.
> Nothing seems to make a difference. I don't think it has to do with
> something on the bus because it hangs as frequently even with the SCSI cable
> disconnected from the 810.
>
> This SCSI card wants to be IRQ16 only so I have it placed in the last 64 bit
> slot, which seems fine on the EB164. I have tried both versions of MILO
> mentioned above. I have tried slowing the PCI bus down as well as the CPU
> with no change.

I don't understand the part about "wants to be IRQ16". As it's a PCI bus
card, it should be able to be any IRQ that the OS wants it to be. It's IRQ16
because you placed it in a specific slot, and the card is configured for a
particular INT[ABCD]. Since the PC164 uses IRQ16 for slot 0 INTA, I assume
that INTA is what the card asserts, and that it's in slot 0.

Have you tried moving the card to the other PCI slots, especially the 32-bit
ones? (From the above description of things you've tried, I expect so). Did
you notice any change in behaviour?
