Paul MJoncas <paul@meganet.net> writes:
> I am looking to buy a used alpha AXP 150(jensen) or 166 to run RedHat
> Linux 3.0.3. I need information on the differences between the two
> systems. Considerations are expandability, speed, known bugs or flaws.

Well, the Jensen ist somewhat faster than a 166MHz UDB or
noname's (I guess that's what you meant) , depending on what you are
doing, this can make a big difference (up to 30%).

However, it's the only Alpha-PC that comes with only an EISA-bus, so
you are limited in terms of supported hardware. This is also
a software issue, because the Jensen lacks the DENSE-memory address
scheme on the bus. This is taken care of by libc for most things like
io-port addresses, but not for memory accesses.

For more information about the Jensen you can check my homepage and
the Jensen section of the Linux/Alpha FAQ.

Cheers, Martin
