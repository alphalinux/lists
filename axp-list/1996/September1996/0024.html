>>>>> On Sat, 31 Aug 1996 20:25:16 +0200, zirzlaff@nienor.IN-Berlin.DE (Torsten Zirzlaff) said:

Torsten> Just a last question, has anyone build a new POVRay? I
Torsten> was today ashamed by a P90 due a POVRay source and not only
Torsten> barely just a liitle bit more as twice times faster than my
Torsten> Noname 266MHz.

Some background: Torsten is running the following povray command:

povray.x -i skyvase.pov -v1 +ft -x +mb25 +a03.00 +j1.000 +r3 -q9 \
-w640 -H480 -S1 -E480 -k0.000 -mv2.0 -b1000

and with his povray binary, it takes 615 seconds to render the image.
According to his mail, this means that the P90 took on the order of
300 seconds.

While I don't have the exact same binary as Torsten, I tried that
command on the machines I have access to:

Alcor (ev5 333MHz, 4MB 3rd level cache): 62 seconds
Cabriolet (ev4 275MHz, 2MB 2nd level cache): 145 seconds (2.33*62)
Noname (lca 166MHz, 128KB 2nd level cache): 527 seconds (3.63*145)

So the Cabrio is about 3.5 times faster than the Noname and the Alcor
is another 2.3 times faster. Now my Noname is only 166MHz and has a
minimal cache of 128KB and a slow one at that (20ns), so these numbers
are pretty much what I expected (as a rule of thumb, my Noname is
about 4 times slower than the Cabrio).

I'm not sure why my Noname came out faster than Torsten's. I suspect
it may be due to library differences. For one thing, I noticed that
my stats (appended below) do not match exactly with the one Torsten
got.

Anyhow, if somebody wants to run my statically linked ELF binary for
comparison purposes, I'd be glad to make available a minimal tar file
that contains the test case (it's about 500KB in size).
