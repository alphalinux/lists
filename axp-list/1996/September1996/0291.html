>>>>> On Wed, 11 Sep 1996 18:48:22 GMT, David A Rusling <rusling@linux.reo.dec.com> said:

David> Don't Panic! They are what they sound like, unaligned
David> accesses (eg read 32 bits from address 0xXXX2). The kernel
David> is just muttering rather than complaining as it has to fix
David> them up - don't forget that early AXP CPUs cannot do any
David> memory access other than 32 bit aligned ones...

But wouldn't be nice if somebody who actually uses fdisk would take
the time to fix them? I think it's wrong to have new users face those
ugly warnings (and turning the warnings off is decidedly the wrong
thing to do).

In fact, with gcc-2.7.x, you can simply declare the offending
structure as "packed" and aligned to 1 byte and gcc will take care of
the rest (I forgot the exact syntax, it's documented as part of the
__attribute__ discussion and it does work---the generated code isn't
efficient, but that hardly matters for fdisk).

Besides, even the newer Alpha chips don't do unaligned accesses in hw.
