> How can I use the serial console instead of usual vga/keyboard on a NoNmae!??!?
>
> What are the setting of the serial port?? (a or b???)
>
> I want to capture the boot of my linux to catch all the errors which make it

I use a serial console - on serial a. I have a Wyse 50 terminal connected
to com1:, a 9600bps.
Milo spits is diagnostics out on this port. I have compiled the kernel
with serial output. ( Although this is confusing, it says ttyS1, my
Terminal is on ttyS0 - but nothing seems to come out ttyS1 ?).

I then changed /dev/console to the serial device, so I could see init's
output.

When the system boots, I get every thing upto the serial initialization
message, I then lose the trace untill init kicks in, so I dont see
the SCSI initialization. If the system does in fact boot, every thing ends up
in the system log anyway.
Ive got a video card fitted ( which doesnt initialize properly when
no monitor is plugged in ! ) and when ive had major problems I
had to resort to plugging in a monitor.
