It is my understanding also that "NE2000-compatible" ethernet cards are
supported under linux. You are a step ahead of me there, so I can't
swear it's true. The NE2000 card is the Ford of ethernet NICs,
originally made by Novell and Everex(?) and now copied by almost
everyone. I just bought some for $39 US each. They ARE suppported by
Win 95. Like you, I intend to connect my Linux/Alpha system to a Win
95/Win NT network. Since both Win 95 and Win NT support TCP/IP, it is
my guess that that would be the path of least resistance in connecting
the two worlds. Bonne chance, and may the force be with you!
