>However, if the community feels that:
>
> o it is better to get started now with a partial standard, and evolve
> later
> o Linux should be used as a "prototype" for the standard (a use for
> Linux of which I heartily approve)

I would say yes to both, and it sounds as if others feel the same way.

>After all, if Linux can handle both a.out and ELF, how hard can it be to handle
>ELF 0.99 and ELF 1.0? (Don't bother answering that, I *know* that it is not
>*I* that will have to do the magic).

Not too hard. At least in ELF32, there is an entire word devoted to
a version number.

Although personally, I'd be hoping people don't get too attached to
a pre-standard version. That would mean maintenence of cruft. I'd
hope that anyone that helped test and experiment with ELF be willing
to recompile their entire system when the ABI changed.

That's probably a pipe-dream though.
