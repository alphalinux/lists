> I have found, after looking at various benchmarks, that intel based systems generally have higher performance than comparably priced alpha systems. But, I have found that 64bit RISC architecture is actually just as fast in multitasking environments. What I am really interested in knowing is how the 21066 166mhz and 233mhz systems compare to intel systems performing general UNIX tasks.

Might want to hit return every once in awhile.

If your counting edu prices:
A 2.1 GB disk, 32 MB ram, 2 MB dram, ethernet, scsi, video

System cost about: $3500 from a cheapie clone vendor for a p5-133 and
about $3750 from Digital if you get the edu price.

Performance numbers:
25 p5-133 21064-233 Diff
26 specint92 177.00 180.00 1.02
27 specfp92 116.00 210.00 1.81

29 specint95 3.90 3.80 0.97

Looks pretty favorable to the alpha to me. I suspect gcc on alpha
does better then gcc on pentium (relative to the tuned spec compilers),
especially since the alpha has many more registers.

Looks like for $250 you get a really nice architecture and > 50% better
floating point.

Hopefully 3rd party's will get their act together and offer something
similiar, instead of $4k 21064 motherboards... So the non-edu people
can share in the fun.
