Not having worked with compilers and loaders for the past 15 years, I plead
ignorance to the details, but the people I talk to state that the ELF
object code standard has some hardware architectural dependencies that they
are trying to hammer out into a standard before any of the implementations
are done.

However, if the community feels that:

o it is better to get started now with a partial standard, and evolve
later
o Linux should be used as a "prototype" for the standard (a use for
Linux of which I heartily approve)

then I will ask the Digital Engineering group to help locate a copy of the
interim work and make it available to people (assuming that the people
who are working on it give us one)

After all, if Linux can handle both a.out and ELF, how hard can it be to handle
ELF 0.99 and ELF 1.0? (Don't bother answering that, I *know* that it is not
*I* that will have to do the magic).
