Avi,

>I've just spent a hundred dollars or so on what I assume to be a jensen in
>Olivetti clothes. It's got Olivetti R7-600 on the front but digital all
>over the back ;-). It's definately EISA based, and according to the
>Alpha-sys overview that makes it a Jensen.

The EISA must mean JENSEN, only our bigger servers have EISA instead of ISA,
and they always have PCI as well, at least now...

>The unit was stripped; what's left is the Adaptec 1742 SCSI board, the
>Digital networkcard and a compaq Qvision 1280 card.

The 1742 is fine, supported under LINUX for a *long* time, as its what Linus
had first (the Jensen, that is... :-). Ditto for the network card, prolly a
DE422. The QVision is the weak link; no current Xserver from XFree86, and not
much chance for even a generic VGA one to work without *many* changes; JENSEN
is problematic in a number of ways... :-( (see below for more info)

>1. What kind memory do I want and how much of it?

Standard 72-pin SIMMS; I believe you need to fill a bank (4 slots) for it to
work (inserted in alternating slots). And it may be much happier with 36-bit
wide, rather than 32. But I'm not certain the 32's won't work? Anyone know for
sure?

>2. Will any keyboard with a ps/2 style connector do?

Yes, same for mouse, though serial mouse works well.

>3. Will my AST fourpourt work?

Uncertain; if it uses only inb/outb type accesses to IO space registers, then
probably. If it's like the Cyclades driver I looked at, which has BUS memory
space registers, then *NO*, it will need reworking, as access to BUS memory is
not always transparent, but especially not on JENSEN.

>4. will my GUS Ultrasound work?

There's a good chance; the latest rev of sound drivers (Voxware-3.5-beta-10?)
is *very* good, portability-wise; SB16 and PAS16 are known to work out of the
box. However, the GUS is prolly one of the more sophisticated drivers in there,
and may have been tweaked for performance in ways that are less portable than
some of the other drivers. I'd be *real* interested in knowing if it does work,
as I get real tired of FM synthesis, having gotten accustomed to an AWE32 under
WfW on my PC... :-)

>5. The various FAQ's etc. stated that X support was limited to a few
>chipsets. Qvision I know isn't one of them. Are there EISA bases s3 cards?

There was someone on the net in Europe, who claimed to have made mods to the
S3 XFree86 server sources to get it running on a JENSEN. EISA boards are rare,
I think; try to find one of the 928-chipset ISA ones; they can be run with
*no* BUS memory accesses, which may be slower but *will* work on JENSEN, given
appropriate LIBC support for the inb/outb's the server does.

>6. will I be able to run Windows NT and Linux on the same machine?

Should be able to, though currently you'd need to switch between ARC console
(for NT) and SRM console (for LINUX), not to mention wrestling with the ECU
for configuring the devices for NT. Both the QVision and an S3-928 were among
the supported options under NT on the JENSEN.

>And last but not least....
>What do the buttons and the LCD on the front of the unit do and show? ;-)

No clue, I've never seen one of these boxes. Anyone else?

Good luck with it.
