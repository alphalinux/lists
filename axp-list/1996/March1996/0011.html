Hi all,

> I have 11 SRAM chips, Type "GLT710008-15T" which are 128Kx8, 300mil
> wide DIL package, 15 ns types.
> The problem is that the firmware doesn't detect any cache, even
> when I put the jumper to 20ns cache timing.

I solved my problem with the 300mil-wide, 128kx8 SRAM chips (for
a total of 1 MB cache).
It is necessary to use 400mil, 128kx8 parts on the noname board.
The socket allows to put 300mil wide parts, but attention:
PINS 31 AND 32 OF THE 300 MIL, 32 PIN SOCKETS ARE NOT CONNECTED!

Supposed you already have 300mil wide parts or are not able to
purchase 400mil parts there is a workaround. Connect Pins 31 and 32
with the adjacent Pins of the 400mil socket. You may solder short
pieces of wire between the pins. Everything works fine afterwards.

I'm using GLT710008-15T parts. They don't meet the 15ns requirements,
but when I switch the jumper to 20ns they function well.
