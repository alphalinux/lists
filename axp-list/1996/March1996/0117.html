Well, after many trials and tribulations, I finally managed to assemble
my new AlphaPC64 system. Powerd up ok, started the install (BLADE 0.3,
because that's all I have handy at the moment) -- everything was going
great. Rebooted a number of times sucessfully. So I left it installing
software from the CD, and went out to a meeting. When I returned (~2
hours later) nothing. No prompt, no virtual consoles, no drive activity,
nothing. Can't get it's attention at all. Ok, so I power cycle. Still
nothing. This is not making me feel good at this point. Check all the
cards, SIMMS, cables, etc. Everything looks good. Power cycle. Still
nothing -- I'm not even getting a blue screen. Ok. This is looking
seriously bad now. Shutdown. Reseat all the cards, SIMMS, cables. Check
the power supply (yep, it's doing the right thing). Still no go. Check
the debug monitor via COM1. Nope. At this point, I'm stuck. All I can
figure is sudden, early hardware failure. So I implore the great
gurus of the Alpha motherboard. Have you any clues, suggestions, etc?
Is there a mystic incantation that I am missing, or is something just
truly zorched?

Hardware setup is as follows:

Motherboard: AlphaPC64, 275Mhz, 2mb cache
Memory: 32Mb 60ns true parity, 4x8Mb SIMMS
SCSI: NCR
Video: Diamond Stealth 64 VRAM PCI, 2Mb
HD: NEC 1Gb SCSI
CD-ROM: Sony 4x SCSI

Software: BLADE 0.3 with newest MILO and kernel that I could locate
easily at gatekeeper. Don't have the numbers at hand, sorry.

Any help is greatly appreciated. Thanks.
