It depends on what kind of application you will run. For my experience, my
program compiled with gcc in a 166MHz UDB+linux runs 1.7 times faster than in
a P90+Linux+gcc. The commercial compiler for P5 may be faster, but IMHO, my
166MHz UDB is at least worth a P133 for my program.
P.S. My UDB has a 166MHz 21066 CPU, which is much slower than a 233MHz 21064A
CPU. So My experience is: test what you need, don't blindly believe SPEC.
P.S. My program is fp intensive, I have counted the instructions in the core
routine, it has 70% fp instructions and 30% int instructions.
