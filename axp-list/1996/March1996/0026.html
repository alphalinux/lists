Hello

> I have seen some discussion about how to get a MILO booted kernel
>to reboot. Has anyone given any thought to getting an SRM kernel to
>reboot? My gut feeling is that it is probably easier since I believe
>that SRM stays in memory after the kernel is running, but I really do
>not know. Does anyone have any ideas (Jay)?

Might I suggest just running milo.arc and linload.exe loaded into the first
partition on the hard drive. There is a simple procedure once the disk is
partitioned to load the milo.arc and linload.exe into the first 1.44 blocks.
Then when the os is loaded use dd to copy everything on your floppy that
contains milo.arc and linload into that partition on the hard drive. Then
set up your boot options to boot sda2:vmlinux.gz and your off and running.
