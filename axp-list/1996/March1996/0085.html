Hi there!

I've just spent a hundred dollars or so on what I assume to be a jensen in
Olivetti clothes. It's got Olivetti R7-600 on the front but digital all
over the back ;-). It's definately EISA based, and according to the
Alpha-sys overview that makes it a Jensen.

The unit was stripped; what's left is the Adaptec 1742 SCSI board, the
Digital networkcard and a compaq Qvision 1280 card. And of course the
Jenson Motherboard.

My current System has two SCSI harddisks which I plan to transplant. One
1Gb IBM SCSI II unit and a 20Mb connor SCSI i/CCS for swapspace.. Also
there's an apple 300i SCSI II cd-rom unit and a Tandberg streamer.

since I'm a total Alpha weenie I'm at a loss what is needed to complete
the system so that I can leave my 486DX/33 for something faster (I
hope;-))

1. What kind memory do I want and how much of it?
2. Will any keyboard with a ps/2 style connector do?
3. Will my AST fourpourt work?
4. will my GUS Ultrasound work?
5. The various FAQ's etc. stated that X support was limited to a few
chipsets. Qvision I know isn't one of them. Are there EISA bases s3 cards?
6. will I be able to run Windows NT and Linux on the same machine?

And last but not least....
What do the buttons and the LCD on the front of the unit do and show? ;-)

The various things needed for booting/installing etc. seem to be dealt
with in the FAQ's. I'll just try from there...
