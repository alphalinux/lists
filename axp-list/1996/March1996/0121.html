Hi!

I think Digital just have released what I've been waiting for..

AlphaStation 255
* 21064A @233Mhz or @300Mhz
* 1MB cache
* Internal FSCSI2
* Internal Network
* Internal Sound
* 2xPCI,1 ISA, 1PCI/ISA shared
* Normal IO ports (2xSer,1xPar, PS2 Mouse/Keyb)
* Floppy drive 1.44M
* CD-Rom drive
* Nice Case
* NT 3.51 media (Digital Unix/Open VMS versions is also availble, but seems
to be more expensive. A model wo any OS doesnt seem availble at the
moment).
* Digitals normal nice warrantys for hw/sw
* NO gfxcard,memory,disk,monitor,keyboard,mouse

If I understood the mail I've got the price for the 233Mhz version is
only **$1668** :-))

The technical specs in the mail I've got is very few, and the local
Digital salesperson didn't know much about it either.... While waiting
for him to dig up more info, I thought it would be a good idea to ask
you guys ;)

So.. Any1 knows more about this baby, and if there is any chance of
Linux/Alpha supporting its devices? (My concerns are about the the
scsi controller (ncr810?), and the IO stuff. The sound/network
stuff is just bonus, I'm sure I could stand to use my isa ne2k a while
more..)

The 300Mhz seems a bit worse priced, about $3200 I think. For thoose that
want superior perfomance, the newish 500 serie, with 21164 @266Mhz with 2M
cache and more stuff for like $6200 seems also like a damn good deal.. :-)

I suppose this will kill the market for the AlphaPC64 board, aka Cabriolet,
which are like $4k for the motherboard alone, which should be a bit slower
than the 300Mhz version even due to the fact that the Cabriolet has 2M cache
instead of 1M..

Keep up the good work guys!

/Mikael
