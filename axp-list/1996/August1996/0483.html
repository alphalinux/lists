This is a bit of a "chicken and egg" scenario. The disk is a floppy in E2fs
format. If you have an Intel Linux machine available to you, you can read
and write to it. Simply mount it as mount /dev/fd0 /mnt and then you can
copy your new kernel to it with cp what-ever-your-kern-is.gz /mnt/vmlinux.gz .
Please note, that unlike Linux on i386 machines, we call the kernel
vmlinux.gz and not vmlinuz.gz. I don't know why...
