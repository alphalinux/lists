>>> sp0t the w0nder slug said:
>
> I know you guys are probably getting tired of my steady stream of
> questions.. I've resolved all problems with my EB164 and redhat with the
> exception of one.. When I compile a new kernel and try to boot it from
> MILO i see all the hash marks then it says it's jumping to the Linux
> kernel and the long line of -'s, then it just sits there (thats with a
> uncompressed kernel, which by the way came out to 8megs. Is that normal?
> the images that come with redhat are about 600k). With a compressed
> vmlinux it shows the 1 and 3/4 lines of hashmarks then just sits there, I
> dont even get the 'jumping to linux kernel' statement. Milo can boot the
> Images that come with redhat just fine however, off of both the floppy
> drive and the harddrive... Anyone have any idea what i'm doing wrong?
> After make config i do a 'make dep;make clean;make vmlinux;make
> modules;make modules_install' then copy the kernel to /.. Any ideas?

Yup, do "make boot" instead of "make vmlinux", and then look for the stripped
and gzipped vmlinux.gz in arch/alpha/boot. *That's* the one to boot.

However, it should boot OK if not stripped and gzipped; are you sure you didn't
generate a kernel with TGA console instead of the VGA you prolly have? Make
sure to answer the TGA console configuration question "NO" if you have VGA.
It *does* come up defaulting to "YES" because that's what Linus' main machine
has for graphics, you know... :-) :-)
