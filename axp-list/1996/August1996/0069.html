Ah. I forgot about these. You can get get libgcc2 built by
creating empty versions in ./gcc/include. Don't forget to get
rid of them after you install gcc, though.

Though really, you don't need libgcc.a to build the kernel, so
you could get away with skipping that too.

> "make LANGUAGES=c CFLAGS+=-I/usr/local/src/kernel/linux/include/asm-alpha"

This is a bad idea, I think. Better to go with the empty files
than risk including the wrong sorts of files.

> or CFLAGS+=-Dinhibit_libc

This is a good idea. I'd forgotten about __eprint as well.
