Jim,
the firmware includes PALcode - that's how interrupts
are driven. The SRM console is also designed to provide callbacks
to the OS to do stuff like write to the console. In the old
days this was, of course, a thumping great teletype. Milo includes
PALcode (hence the difference in interrupt handling) in itself
which it leaves at 0x200000 (physical), an internal register (PALBase)
points at this and the CPU uses this information to vector events
through the PALcode.
