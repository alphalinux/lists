Thanks for all of your reponses about Linux and the Alpha 21164
(PC164). I've put in my order for a Durango Board. Now it
appears that Linux will run at least fairly well on it, if
not flawlessly. It appears that the uncertainty I was hearing
grew out of a lack of testing and experience with Linux on
the PC164. I've seen no reports about failures so far.
Here are remarks from people at DEC and Alpine that summarize
the information I've received:

David A Rusling <rusling@linux.reo.dec.com> wrote:
> ...if [the PC164] isn't [working with Linux]
> then we'll fix it and I think that we have a pretty good record.
> My problem is that marketing are too busy using the PC164 to let me
> have it for long stress tests. But when I do get my hands on it, it
> boots and runs linux just fine...

Randy Perry [Sales Manager, Aspen Systems Inc.] wrote:
>
> Since Durango is based upon the new 2.5V technology , I think the line > on the newsgroup is that the kernel will have to be redone.
> ...
later:
> Let it be known that if PC164 runs Linux with no problems than Durango > will run it in the same manner with the same performance.
