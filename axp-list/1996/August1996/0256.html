At 07:58 PM 8/8/96 +0200, you wrote:
>>>>>> "DD" == Doug DeJulio <ddj@aisb.org> writes:
>
> DD> Do you mean the compile fails, or the kernel you get won't
> DD> boot?
>
>I don't know what is the problem in respect to UDB, but on my Avanti
>(AS 200 4/166 -- EV4) kernel won't boot.
>
> DD> If the kernel won't boot: The default MSS IRQ is 11. On the
> DD> UDB, that's the IRQ required by the SCSI controller, so using
> DD> IRQ 11 on the MSS will make the system unable to access tis
> DD> SCSI disks. That's one possibility.
>
>Yes, this is quite well known on this mailing list, but what about the
>Avanti? Is sound not supported on this type of machine? Kernel hangs
>at "sound initialization". One hears a noise from the internal
>speakers but nothings happens any more. Only possibility is to reset the
>machine and boot a "sound-free" kernel.

Well, check what IRQs are being used with the "sound-free" kernel; if IRQ 11
is one of the PCI devices, like SCSI or DE435, and you try to use it with the
soundcard, you will have trouble. Try putting the soundcard at IRQ 5, if
possible; that seems to work fine on most platforms I've tried, including
Avanti/XL.

Note that there may be another problem here, which I've sent patches to Linus
for; even if no other card is using the IRQ, one of 9,10,11, or 15 may *NOT*
work with an ISA card. Hopefully the patch will be in 2.0.12 or so. Mail me
if you need more info, ie if IRQ 5 is not usable for some reason.
