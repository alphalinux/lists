Linus wrote:
> If you're _really_ serious about this (very nice), you should probably unroll
> the inner loop a bit (that can help hide load latencies), and you can do
> stuff like pre-fetch with a "ldq_u $31,64($srcreg)" etc.

I've since unrolled the inner loops once, but it seemed to make very
little difference in the timings. I'm not sure I understand the cause
of this. I wouldn't have thought that the memory bug was completely
saturated just with the single loop.

Incidentally, "ldq_u $31, X(R)" is the universal nop -- it doesn't
even get slotted. You have to use a real destination register.
I've been trying to figure out how fetch and fetch_m should be
used; whether it helps or hurts to do a fetch on the source as the
first instruction and letting that work while I figure out if we're
aligned or whatnot. The problem here is fetch talks about 512 byte
aligned blocks.

I'll go back and look at that code with your other suggestions in mind.
