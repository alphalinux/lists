>>> Maurice Hilarius said:
> At 02:07 hrs. 08-11-96 -0700, you wrote:
> >Hi,
> >
> >Does anyone know what the default refresh a UDB spits out? I have a
> >15" CTX multi-synch, and I want to make sure that my UDB is driving
> >it within its normal operating range. The image looks good... but
> >I don't want to fry my monitor in a few weeks.
> >
> It's not written in stane, as the defaults are configurable by jumpers in
> the UDB. Most that I have seen are configured for 1024x768 at 70Hz. Pretty
> safe rate as long as you have 1024 supported by your monitor. This is the
> vESA standard refresh rate.

Ah, yes, this seems to be the normal setting for the UDB from the factory,
although some have been seen at 1280x1024, which may cause problems.

But the resolution set-up by the SRM console *can* be changed, to any one of
the resolutions found in the sample XF86Config file that is part of the TGA
README in gatekeeper.dec.com:pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/X11. I'll try to get some
details about this out tomorrow.

Which reminds me, did I dream it, or did someone volunteer to be the author
of a UDB-related FAQ? If so, would they please get in touch with me, I've a
number of other pieces of info that should become part of the lore...
