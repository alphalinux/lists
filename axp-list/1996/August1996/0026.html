Linus> Umm. This does a load from the destination for the first
Linus> quad-word, even if the source and destination are both
Linus> aligned. That's ok if the destination is cached, but it's
Linus> _horrible_ if the destination isn't cached and we're doing a
Linus> small structure copy of just one or two quadwords.

Another thing to watch out for is write-after-write (WAW) conflicts.
At least on the ev4, this is very slow. Not something that normally
occurs, but if you use the Arch Ref Manual's recommended sequence to
store an unaligned quantity to an _aligned_ address, you do end up
with a WAW conflict (it works correctly, but it's quite slow). It's
not a problem with the memcpy code, but just something to watch out
for. Hmmh, did anybody write that book "Optimizing Alpha/RISC Code"
yet? ;-)
