>>> "Joshua M. Thompson" said:
> OK here's what I've found so far playing with aumix:
>
> No, plugging in headphones doesn't help. In fact the system beep does
> _not_ play through the headphones; only the internal speaker.

It's just like on a PC; there's a set of pins on the MB for attaching a
speaker to, that does NOT involve any sound card. That's where the beep is
generated.

On the UDB, I believe its hardwired that output from the sound codec AND
output from the PC-speaker pins go together into the internal speaker;
only the output from the codec can be diverted to the headphone jack if
one gets plugged into it. The output of the pins cannot, except by surgery,
as you've demonstrated... :-)

>
> With headphones, using aumix, the "pcm" slider controls the volume of the
> headphones, "Line 2" controls the volume of the mic input, and "mix"
> controls mixing of the digitized sound output hardware (ie, /dev/dsp) and
> the microphone input onto the headphone output.

Doesn't the "Line 1" value, when made smaller, reduce the hiss and distortion
you've been hearing? It's done that on all the UDB's I've worked with. At
least, I think it was "Line 1", maybe its "Line 3"? Can't remember, no machine
to try it on... :-(
