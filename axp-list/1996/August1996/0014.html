Heres what I do that seems to work well on new disks. Since fdisk will not
let you use anything above 63 sectors you have to fudge the numbers of heads
and cylinders.

Therefore, at fdisk type "m" for menu, select I believe b for BSD label and
hit return.

This will provide you with a list of cylinders, heads(tracks/cylinder)
sector size. Jot down the info and quit out of this program.

Go back to fdisk type "x" for expert and fillin the information by using

c # cylinders
h # heads
s # sectors

type return to get back to main menu and start your partition table.

Hope this helps.
