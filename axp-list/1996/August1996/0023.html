On Thu, 1 Aug 1996, David C. Tuttle wrote:
> After finishing the install, I tried booting the Linux kernel off the
> hard drive from MILO with "boot sda3:vmlinux.gz" and it loaded the
> kernel OK and finds all the hardware, but then it gets hung on:
>
> Kernel panic: Unable to mount root fs on 08:02
>
> The standalone shell in the install has no trouble mounting /dev/sda3
> (or /dev/sda5 or /dev/sda6), and all the files I expect to see are in
> those partitions. So, why won't it mount the root partition at
> boot time?

The last time this happened to me, it was because I'd done something a little
stupid without investigating enough first -- I'd compiled in the "Microsoft
Sound System" driver, with all the settings left at their defaults. That ate
IRQ 11, and that's what the SCSI controller on the UDB wants. It couldn't
talk to the SCSI controller, so it couldn't mount the root partition.

This may not be your problem, but hopefully someone else can benefit from
being warned about this. (BTW, the MSS needs IRQ9; the other defaults are
fine. I'm sending this from a UDB 233MHz/24M/500M with kernel
2.0.10+axpdiffs.)
