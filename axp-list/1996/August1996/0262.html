Someone (Maurice?) said:
> Sorry to say, many of the CD-ROM drives used on Suns and SGI's use weird
> ROMs, that is to say, non-standard...

To which Michael Schwingen <m.schwingen@stochastik.rwth-aachen.de> replied:
> SUN uses 512-byte sectors. I was able to use a SUN CD-ROM on a PC running
> Linux without problems. I don't know sure what SGI uses, and what the SRM
> or MILO do about differing sector sizes.

Can't speak for SRM, but the sr.c driver in Linux (and presumably in Milo)
ask the drive for the sector size and correctly handle either 512 or 2048
byte sectors.
