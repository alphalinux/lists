On Thu, 1 Aug 1996, Richard Henderson wrote:
>
> Incidentally, "ldq_u $31, X(R)" is the universal nop -- it doesn't
> even get slotted. You have to use a real destination register.

Damn, I have my 21164 books at the university, and I'm at home now. But why
do I have this belief that the behaviour of "ldq_u $31,X(R)" changed with the
EV5, and prefetches from the L2 cache into the L1 cache?

(You can't use a real destination register for prefetching, as that results
in a memory fault if the address isn't mapped).

> I've been trying to figure out how fetch and fetch_m should be
> used; whether it helps or hurts to do a fetch on the source as the
> first instruction and letting that work while I figure out if we're
> aligned or whatnot. The problem here is fetch talks about 512 byte
> aligned blocks.

As far as I know, none of the current alpha machines do anything at all
with fetch/fetch_m - the CPU sends out the address to the memory bus, but
the external logic ignores the fetch cycles.
