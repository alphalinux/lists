On Thu, 8 Aug 1996, David Evans wrote:
> Every time I try to compile a 2.0.11 kernel on my UDB with MSS sound support
> it fails. When I compile without MSS sound support it compiles fine.
> Does anyone have any ideas.

Do you mean the compile fails, or the kernel you get won't boot?

If the kernel won't boot: The default MSS IRQ is 11. On the UDB, that's the
IRQ required by the SCSI controller, so using IRQ 11 on the MSS will make the
system unable to access tis SCSI disks. That's one possibility.

If the compile fails: The MSS appears to have no FM synth. All you can do is
/dev/audio -- trying to enable the OPL/FM synth has made the kernel compile
fail for me.
