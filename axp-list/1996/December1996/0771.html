I have been warned that switching back and forth between and UDB and
another machine will cause the UDB to hang or worse to fry some circuit or

Indeed people have been reporting damaging keyboard interface on the
motherboard by hot plugging/unplugging. Normally, this could be caused
either by static discharge into the input pin or by energizing signal
pins before connecting the power (actually, most modern semiconductor
devices are protected to some degree against both perils by the
clamping diodes on input, and the controller used by DEC seems to be
more sensitive than usual).

I would guess that a properly designed switch box protects the
circuits to some degree (e.g. doesn't let static electricity be picked
up from your sweater, and switches power before passing through the
signals), but of course you don't know that for sure about your box.

In any case, perhaps you could do away with the need for two
keyboards? I simply have windows to, oh, about 10 computers open
constantly on my screen, in several virtual desktops. The only reason
to have dedicated screen/keyboard is if you can'd do otherwise
(i.e. Windows) or if you have to reboot your machine often (again,
Windows :^)
