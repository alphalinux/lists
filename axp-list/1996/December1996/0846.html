At 06:45 PM 12/24/96 -0500, you wrote:
>Slightly off topic, but does anyone think that the recent burst in
>popularity for linux-alpha might boost support for Linux-MIPS?
>Any MIPS buffs out there in Alpha land??

Aye (one MIPS+Alpha Fan here). Most of the MIPS chips are rather pricey,
though, much less systems based upon them... On the other hand, MIPS has
made some barn-burners in the past, just like Digital. I like em all. =)
I don't think that anyone makes a system based upon the MIPS chips anymore,
though, with the exception of SGI. From what I understand, the mips port of
NT is rather dead. Sad.

A lot of Mips architecture chips are going into things like cable modems
now, though. Fun stuff like the new Nintendos that takes quite a bit of
horsepower but not so general purpose.

I really don't know about a boost in the popularity of the port, at least
until someone ports it to the SGI machines. Now THAT might do something
nice for us all........ 4D/30's with lots of ram and a nice screen are
pretty cheap now....... =) =)
