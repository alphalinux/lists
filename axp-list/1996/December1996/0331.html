Dee,

What chris meant by "wimpier" is that the Alpha chip in the UDB runs at
166 Mhz. Compared to a CISC chip like the Intel Pentium, an equivalent
would be a Pentium 60. That's right . . . 60 . . . . not 160 . . . .

Where the UDB shines over the Pentium based systems is its bus speed. The
Intel chips move data 32 bits at a time where the Alpha moves data 64 bits
at a time.

If you want to do some serious number crunching or anything CPU intensive,
you should stick with the Intel chips. If you want to so serious I/O then
use the Alpha. I/O intensive apps would include: ftp, http, archie,
gopher, e-mail, etc . . . . In other words, If you want to offer Internet
services of some kind, the Alpha is a good, inexpensive choice . . . .

Just my option . . . .

Enjoy . . . .
