On Wed, 4 Dec 1996, Stephen J. Gaudet wrote:

> Hello
>
> I have two questions. First, due to PNP not working under Alpha Linux
> anyone know of sound cards that work with Alpha?

I have a Gravis Ultrasound PnP which works very well in isapnp mode.
The driver I am using (which supports the Ultrasound PnP) can be found
at romeo.pf.jcu.cz:/pub/perex/ultra. I had to make a patch for it to be
reliable though. If you are interrested I can make it available via ftp.

> Second, where do I find more information on disk stripping for Alpha under
> Linux?

Well, for software striping, take a look at
/usr/src/linux/drivers/block/README.md in your Linux source tree. It
appears to be working though there is some problem with the buffering in
Linux at the moment I think. I haven't even been able to make one disk
go in it's top speed on my Noname 266MHz let alone two striped partitions.
You might have more luck with a faster machine, but I believe there is a
problem in the Linux kernel though. Disk speed was better around 1.3.50
something I seem to remember.
