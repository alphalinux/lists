I just bought a UDB, too, and just got it running.

On Tue, 17 Dec 1996, Shane Anderson wrote:

> Buy an external SCSI case ($100-$200)

Mine was $50.

> Get a slick 2GB or greater SCSI Drive ($300-$500)

2.1G for $337.

> Buy a SCSI CD-ROM (or share one via NFS) for the RedHat install.

I had an external one that I got cheap about a year ago and still had
laying around for a laptop that I never ended up using, so now it's my UDB
CD-ROM.

> Monitor: ?? UDB does 1024x768 at up to 76Hz (256 color ONLY)

I'm working on that same ?? right now -- Currently, I'm using a borrowed
monochrome 14" VGA 800x600 -- hardly the thrilling experience hoped for.

> The UDB at onsale is comparable to:
> ~Pentium 75

I've heard ~Pentium 60, but have no personal data to back that up.

> minimal case (need 2nd case for drives)

Well... If I was doing it over again, I'd try to get a 2.5" drive to put
inside instead of my 3.5".

> basic 3-button logitech mouse (PS/2 connector)

I got a 2-button mouse with mine.

> So maybe $300-$400 isn't a terrible price for the above, but you
> decide. Maybe a Pentium w/ the above list is a better deal as far as
> avaible software and flexibility...

In retrospect, since I got it as a hobby machine for myself, I think,
doing it over, I'd instead go with a Pentium. THere's plenty of hobby
left without having to deal with porting to 64-bit, and it would be nice
to be able to run Netscape, Java and the like, which I understand are
available for Linux/x86 but not for Linux/Alpha.

On the other hand...

> But then again, the UDB is
> really a slick little box. :)

Exactly.
