This isn't a Linux-on-Alpha question, as my Alpha just arrived today, but
I'm hoping I can get some help anyway.

I just purchased a 166MHz Universal Desktop Box, with no memory or hard
disk. I have RedHat Linux. I picked up 32 meg of memory and a 2G SCSI
disk and am ready to install and get hopefully get the puppy running this
week.

The box, however, came with almost no technical information, which was
kind of a surprise. All I really have is instructions from the vendor on
loading Linux. So I have some various questions.

1. my 32Meg is in the form of two SIMMs. Is there any requirement on
using, for example, the left or right pair of SIMM slots?

2. D I have to worry about any jumper settings, whether for the memory or
anything else? If so, what?

3. Should I even _think_ about getting my 3.5" SCSI disk installed in the
bay, or should I resign myself to running with an external drive only? I
think I read on one of the newsgroups that the power supply was sufficient
for the board itself, but not for an added disk. Is this so? Finally, I
note that the power connectors seem to be of a different type than I'm
accustomed to seeing. I would need to Y-connect off of one of these, I
guess, and add an extender or adaptor with one end being this same type of
connector and another being the standard connector that powers the disk.
Are such connectors (the Y and the adaptor) standard equipment, or should
I expect an arcane search?

4. Most importantly, I guess, is there a source for tech info on the
board? I can't find anything on digital's web site. If it helps, the
numbers "5422555" and "5022554-01 D1" are stenciled on the board.

I hope this wasn't too much or too off-topic for this list. I look
forward to more substantive contributions once I'm up and running.
