Several days ago I sent email requesting info about the use of 32 meg
SIMMs in 166 MHz UDBs. The 166MHz DEC Alpha UDB would not recognize
SIMMs larger than 8 megs.... or so it seemed!

A computer store check the SIMMS; one was OK, one was defective above 8
megs. Lots of boot-up alogrithms exist for determining RAM mem size, and
often look for the first meg that will not return the same value that was
written to it. This would account for why it only recognized 8 megs.
Apparently, it automatically assumes the other chip is of the same value.

In summary, I now have two 32 meg, and two 4 meg SIMMs, for a total of 72
megs, installed and running successfully in the UDB.

Also, there is an Alpha site in Great Brittian that has a great deal of
useful info about the 166 UDBs, including allowable SIMM memory
configuration, jumper configuration, and more. Truely worth keeping a
bookmark on it! My UDB NT 4.0 Beta 2 crashed last nite, and all
bookmarks, including that one, are gone! Cant wait for the 4.0
Workstation, full version to arrive.

Thanks for all the help that was offered. Linux is without a doubt the
best choice in a "freeware" OS.
