>>> Robert Allen Barker said:
>
> The EB164 allows the ARC and SRM firmware to be installed concurrently. I
> have tried the same on the PC164 but they seem to overwrite each other. The
> manual for the PC164 states that the board has 1MB of flash rom but that
> it is divided into two banks, and that writing a 1 or a 0 to the
> FLASH_ADR19 register at IO location 800h selects the upper or lower bank.

That's the *physical* description of the FLASH; it's prolly made up of
2-512K parts, and this just describes what one must do to be able to write
to one or the other.

> Neither the SRM nor the ARC seem to be aware of this, is there a smooth way
> of using this feature the allow both the SRM and ARC to installed at the
> same time?

That's because neither the SRM nor ARC have been programmed to *want* to
share the FLASH; they assume total ownership, of all except the first and
last 64K chunks, the first for Fail-Safe Booter, the last for envvars.

Sorry; it wasn't the programmers' idea to do it that way... :-( :-(
