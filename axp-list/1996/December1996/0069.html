In message <009AC3F8.E81DA237.47@leva.leeds.ac.uk>, John Armstrong writes:
>We are planning to run our Multia/UDB with linux, as a firewall/mailserver.
>What sort of PCI-based ethernet cards would be 'best' as the second card
>for this sort of application? The Ethernet Howto seems a bit sparse when
>it comes to PCI cards (an observation, not a criticism ... 8-)
>
>In two of our Pentiums we have DEC DE450-CA cards (aka Etherworks Turbo PCI
>cards) and propose dropping one of these into the Multia to test it. Any
>one have any ideas/suggestions on that?

I've had good luck with Kingston's PCI Ethernet cards in my PCs, but I
haven't tried them in my UDB yet. Kingston's 10 Mbps cards use DEC's
21041 PCI Ethernet chip, just like DEC and SMC's cards, but they're a
lot cheaper -- NECX is selling them for ~$40 each. I have 5 or 6 of
them so far, and I haven't had any problems with Linux or lesser
operating systems :-).
