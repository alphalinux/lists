At 01:22 AM 12-19-96 -0500, you wrote:
>Ok here is a strange question I have to ask.
>I have a combination monitor/keyboard switch,
>and would like to use it to switch back and forth
>between my AT&T Pentium 75 machine, and my UDB (166)
>Multia.
>
>This is a New switch that has the older style keyboard
>ports on it. I figured add an adapter, and plug them in
>and all is well......
>
> BUT (and there is always a but)
>
>I have been warned that switching back and forth between and UDB and
>another machine will cause the UDB to hang or worse to fry some circuit or
>other. This was explained to us by a "Qualified Alpha Reseller".
>
>OK, what the catch, I'd really like to use the switch and save congestion
>on my desk?? Any suggestions?
>
In my experience the Multia doesn't like "losing" the keyboard. As keyboards
are cheap, maybe a purchase of another one might be the prudent route. As
for the monitor, as long as it is a goos switchbox, it should not be a
problem. One thing to be aware of: Some monitors don't take very kindly to
switching modes a lot, and I have seen them break before from doing too much
of this. Most are fine, though.
