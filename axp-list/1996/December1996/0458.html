Michael Schwingen <m.schwingen@stochastik.rwth-aachen.de> writes:

> > to hang the bus during later access. The bus will also
> > hang during large file transfers.
[..]
> > In addition to all this, I have been unsuccessful in getting the
> > tape to stream, even using large blocking factors and buffering
> > output through dd and running the backup on a completely idle
> > system. This, to me, seems indicative of limitations in the
> > NCR driver...
>
> This can't be - I am using the NCR card in my Intel box for quite some time
> now, and never had such problems, neither with Tandberg QIC, nor with HP DAT
> streamers, so it can't be a general drives fault.

About a year back I switch from Linux/Intel to FreeBSD, because the
NCR driver was so unreliable. It was impossible to do backups with my
Tandberg 3600 QIC streamer. Disconnect/reconnect didn't work, if I
actually used more than one drive. 10B/s asyncroneous transfers also
lead to kernel panics or freezes.

The same hardware setup worked flawlessly with FreeBSD (and OS/2, btw).

In the meantime the FreeBSD driver was ported to Linux, and it is now
an option in the kernel. Be sure you use that one! I believe the old
one is called 53c7,8xx, I am not sure about the new one (Sold my last
Intel box a while back :-))
