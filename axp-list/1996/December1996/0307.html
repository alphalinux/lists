a umul takes X cycles.
a umulh takes Y cycles.

The probability of doing a umul is 100%.
the probability of doing a umulh is somewhat less. Problem is when
must i do the umulh in order to get the overflow of 2 48 bit
multiplys.

1) If I dont know any better, then the simplist would be
umul, followed by the umulh. therfor every multiply is
guarenteed to be X + Y cycles.
2) If I knew that the 48bit mult never overflows, then i can
guarentee the operation to be just X cycle.
3) If I can get an overflow condition, then I can just operate
at X cycles, until an overflow happens where I would need to
use the Y cycles to do the umulh + the # of cycles for the
overflow condition.

It would seem that if the overflow condition happens infrequently
then I'm a winner ( # of operations * Y cycles < #cycles for
overflow condition ).
If overflow happens frequently, then the execution of UMULH is
always warrented.
At what point is the break even point. I's got to no.
