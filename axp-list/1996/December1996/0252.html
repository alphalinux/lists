I just got a UDB, and I have installed RedHat 4.0 on it with no problem.
However, I managed to get ahold of an old single-speed CD-ROM drive that
I wanted to try out. It is a Toshiba SCSI-1 drive. I can only get
errors when I try to read it under Linux. The drive works on my 486
with an Adaptec 1542 (when syncronous and fast SCSI are disabled for
that device in the BIOS). It also worked under NT on a UDB. I get:

Dec 5 19:56:09 adams kernel: Device not ready. Make sure there is a disc in the drive.
Dec 5 19:56:09 adams last message repeated 2 times
Dec 5 19:56:09 adams kernel: CD-ROM I/O error: dev 0b:00, sector 64
Dec 5 19:56:09 adams kernel: isofs_read_super: bread failed, dev 0b:00 iso_blknum 16 block 32

when I try to mount the CD (and yes, there is a CD in the drive). I
have tried multiple cables and active and passive terminators. Is this
just not going to work?

Also, I have a couple of questions:
1) After booting, "free" reports 21760 total memory. Does the kernel
take 2816k? Is that normal?

2) Not so much a question as a bug report: the option for setting the
clock in /etc/rc.d/rc.sysinit looks like

if [ ${CLOCKMODE} = "ARC" ]; then
clock -A -a -u

which screws things up. I found if I take the "-u" off, everything
works okay. Or does it just assume that if you have the ARC firmware
you keep your clock in UTC?

Thanks!
