My UDB running RH 4.0 hangs while booting multi user.

It turns out that the hanging command is

ifconfig lo 127.0.0.1 netmask 255.0.0.0 broadcast 127.255.255.255

I've found out ifconfig is trying to resolve something through the
nameserver. There are several ways to stop ifconfig hanging:

1) remove /etc/resolv.conf *and* the default route

2) have /etc/resolv.conf point to a non-existing or non-reachable
nameserver

3) connect the nameserver to the Internet. The nameserver is a
forwarding-only secondary nameserver that is usually not connected
to its primary nameserver. Yet it can resolve localhost and 127.0.0.1
on its own.

/etc/rc.d/rc3.d/S10network configures "eth0" first and "lo". By changing
this order, I fixed my boot problem.

Still, ifconfig lo 127.0.0.1 hangs. What is it trying to ask the
nameserver ? ifconfig in RH 4.0/i386 does not behave this way.

127.0.0.1 is in my /etc/hosts and /etc/host.conf says order hosts,bind
