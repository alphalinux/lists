On Fri, 6 Dec 1996, Iwao Jack Makino wrote:

> I was wondering if anyone has done Linux-Alpha with Macintosh
> networking, I would like to know how is works.
> I have several options. AppleTalk, EtherTalk, MachTen as unix
> connection through my Macintosh Ethernetcard...
>
> Any suggestion will be appriciated.

I am running "netatalk" which allows my Linux (alpha) drives and/or
directories to be exported to my MAC Plus which is on the same ethernet
as the Alpha machine. This of course requires appletalk support at
the linux end (I am using the appletalk.o module) but requires no
special anything at the MAC end.

It works quite well, and netatalk compiled with very little trouble
on the Alpha box.

Netatalk should also allow the MAC to print to a printer connected
to the Alpha box but I have not got around to trying that yet
(so many tasks and so little time :-))

I have also heard of something called Columbia Appletalk Protocol
but do not know much about it.

I would also be interested in anything which would export a MAC app
as an X app, more or less like Desqview/X does with Win apps but
I have not found any such thing as yet.
