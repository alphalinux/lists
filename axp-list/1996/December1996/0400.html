Since the inside of the Alpha's cabinet is rather small, increasing the
size of the fan is not practical. A faster fan, if available could help.
However, several solutions come to mind:

1. Place a flange around the exhuast area of the Alpha, and attach am
auxilary fan to draw more air out. Obviously, the aux fan must draw more
air than the Alpha's internal fan. (I have successfully retrofitted fans
to "sealed" computer cabinets; it works!)

2. There are solid state devices that cool or heat up when a DC voltage
is applied. Solid state picnic coolers manufactured by Coleman contain
these cooling devices. The All Electronics Corporation, (800)826-5432 did
carry these devices.

3. Lastly, lower the environmental temperature of the Alphas
surroundings, and wear a muffler and sweater.

Happy cooling!

Jim
