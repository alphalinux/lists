A friend of mine just asked me about the UDBs at www.onsale.com and
here's my reply. I invite any comments/corrections/etc. (no flames?)

---


> How tough is to bring one of the little DECs up to speed. Was looking 
> at onsale and they seem to have a bunch. Are they standard Simms? 
> Also, what kind of scsi drive is needed? wide? regular ol scsi-2?


Standard 72-pin 70ns (or faster) PARITY SIMMS.  It uses the parity 
bits to implement error correction...  And the SCSI controller is an 
NCR810 (SCSI-II 10MHz...)  Standard 50-pin variety.  
 
> Also, is there much for them that will work under linux? We are kind 
> of looking for some cheap web servers. Presumably httpd is on the 
> linux cd-rom?


Well, I have RedHat 4.0 up and running on my UDB (from onsale) and it 
appears to have Apache HTTPD, but *browsers* are rather scarce for 
the Alpha...  And it has all the usual UN*X utilites you come to 
expect... even 'xv'.


So if you're thinking of it for a Web Server here's what I think 
you'd probably have to do:


  Buy 64MB of PARITY memory ($400 or less...)
  Buy an external SCSI case ($100-$200)
  Get a slick 2GB or greater SCSI Drive ($300-$500)
  Buy a SCSI CD-ROM (or share one via NFS) for the RedHat install.
  Monitor: ?? UDB does 1024x768 at up to 76Hz (256 color ONLY)
  Keyboard: UDB has a PS/2 style connector


It'll also run Windows NT 4.0, but BIG DEAL...


The UDB at onsale is comparable to:
  ~Pentium 75
  basic 16-bit sound card (no FM synth!)
  basic 2MB 64-bit PCI video DRAM (or 32-bit VRAM)
  PCI ethernet
  PCI SCSI Card
  motherboard w/ 256K cache
  minimal case (need 2nd case for drives)
  basic 3-button logitech mouse (PS/2 connector)
  3.5" floppy drive


So maybe $300-$400 isn't a terrible price for the above, but you 
decide.  Maybe a Pentium w/ the above list is a better deal as far as 
avaible software and flexibility...  But then again, the UDB is 
really a slick little box.  :)
