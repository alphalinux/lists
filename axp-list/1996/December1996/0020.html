On Sun, 1 Dec 1996, Woodstock wrote:

> On Sun, 1 Dec 1996, Miguel Avillez wrote:
>
> > 2. I have new disk and want to mv filesystems to that disk. Say I want to
> > move / from the old disk to the new one. How do I do it?
>
> It'd probably be easiest to boot from disks, and mount both drives, as
> say, /mnt1 and /mnt2. Then just cp -a /mnt1/* /mnt2
> (Or mv)

Another way is to boot normally an do just cp -a all /files except:

- The mount point of the target partition (/mnt for example) and other
mounted devices if any.
- And /proc

After the copy just mkdir /mnt (and other mount points) and mkdir /proc.

> Elsewise, you will end up copying the new drive back onto itself.
> 8< )}
