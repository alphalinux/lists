The article should have read:

> -----
> BOSTON - Digital Equipment said today it cut prices by up to 50 percent on
> microprocessors that compete with Intel's popular Pentium Pro computer chips.

BOSTON Digital Equipment said today it cut prices by up to 50 percent
on microprocessors that should have been competing with Intel's popular
Pentium Pro computer chips.

> "This announcement is part of our continuing strategy to enhance the
> competitiveness of Alpha microprocessors in the Windows NT market," said
> William Johnson, vice president of Digital's semiconductor business segment.

This announcement is due to the company's hemmorhaging, and to maintain
our 2% market share that we have come to be proud of.


> Johnson said the Alpha chips deliver superior performance compared with
> Intel's popular Pentium Pro chips and that with the price cuts, Digital's
> chips would be more attractive to personal computer makers.

Johnson said the Alpha chips deliver superior performance compared with
Intel's popular Pentium Pro chips and that if we had more knowlegible
people in sales and marketing than we VP's, our chips would be more
attractive to personal computer makers.

> The cuts come as demand for chips has strengthened in recent months,
> reflecting higher orders by personal computer makers and more stable prices
> for memory chips.

We don't believe in all the supply-demand, point-of-diminishing return
stuff that they used to expouse in economics class. We rely on stock
worth, and quarter to quarter profits.

> A top Intel official has said that the company's chip factories were running
> full tilt and an industry group said Monday that a key ratio of chip orders
> to shipments rose to the highest level in more than a year in November.
>
> Digital cut its Alpha 21164-300 megahertz (MHz) chip to $395 from $695, the
> 366 MHz chip to $495 from $950 and the 433 MHz to $750 from $1,492. The
> prices are based on 1,000-unit quantities.
