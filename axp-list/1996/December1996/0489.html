On Wed, 11 Dec 1996, Eric Sven Ristad wrote:

> I would like to run big jobs on big multiprocessor AlphaServers.
My understandig is, that Linux/AXP doesn't support MP (yet).
> (After all, isn't that the point of using Alpha instead of p5/p6?)
> Please help me figure out if Linux/Alpha is up to the task:
>
> . Does Linux support the full 64 bit virtual address space?
It seems so. I've mapped 2000 times a 1GB sparse file, and my UDB didn't
give any errors. (My Linux/Intel box would allow me around 2GB mmaped
address space.)
> . What is the maximum RAM that Linux will support?
I'd say, that depends mostly upon the motherboard, right?
> . What is the most swap space that your process can use?
A swapfile can usually (meaning Linux/Intel, I didn't verify this for AXP)
be 127MB or so large. By default, the kernel allows for 8 swap files, see
also /usr/src/linux/include/linux/swap.h.
> . What is the largest file that you can create?
At the moment 2GB, but this is currently been worked on, I believe.
> . Does the kernal fully support multiprocessing?
SMP is supported for Linux/Intel (and Linux/Sparc), but not for AXP :(
>
> Please don't speculate. Only reply if you are very confident in your
OK, most of the above is speculation or common knowledge, but at least the
mmap() stuff I tried, so I can assure you, that Linux/AXP is really able
to use a address space larger than 4GB :)
