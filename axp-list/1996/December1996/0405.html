Aspen durango (PC164) w NCR810 SCSI
Redhat 4.0 kernel 2.0.18v6

I'm experiencing lockups, particularly with tape and
Zip drive access on the SCSI bus. The tape is a T4000s
TR-4 drive (SCSI-2) and of course Iomega Zip. Messing
around with the block size on the tape (using mt) is sure
to hang the bus during later access. The bus will also
hang during large file transfers.

Both of these devices result in a statement:
"target n rejected SDTR"
during boot, which I take to mean that they comply properly with the
SCSI standard in this regard.

Lowering the bus speed to 5MHz to improve reliability
is really not acceptable, as I want Fast SCSI-2 for the SCSI hard
drives on the bus.

In addition to all this, I have been unsuccessful in getting the
tape to stream, even using large blocking factors and buffering
output through dd and running the backup on a completely idle
system. This, to me, seems indicative of limitations in the
NCR driver...

Are there more recent kernels in which a significant improvement
in the stability and performance of the NCR810 driver has occurred?

I am considering placing these two devices on another SCSI
card, connected to the ISA bus. What are the limitations there?
Does the kernel handle the 16MByte limit for DMA gracefully, or
should I disable ISA DMA on the (ultrastor 14f) SCSI card?

Thanks
Johan
