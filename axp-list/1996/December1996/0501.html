>
> I guess the Alpha systems store the date differently internally.

Sigh! This is really not Alpha but ARC console - which is meant for
NT. Accordingly to docs (I do not remember where, but I know that
I did not invent that myself :-) one is supposed to have such line
CLOCKMODE="ARC"
in /etc/sysconfig/clock - IF booting from ARC and not SRM.

> According to clock(8), there is a "-A" option to tell Linux that your
> clock is a UDB clock.

Not exactly. This is what I see on the manpage:

-A Indicates that the clock is in the ARC Console's
time

I have seen Alpha shifting time on a reboot but by some hours and
not by 20 years. :-)

> RedHat 4.0 asks you if your system stores the date as local,...

I did fall in the same trap initially. "LOCAL" in this context seems
to mean something totally different.
