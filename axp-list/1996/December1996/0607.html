I've followed your mail for few weeks now.
Finally, I got 2.1.14 to compile and boot. After all, it was
not obvious. First, you don't need to upgrade glibc: I tried it
and got burnt pretty bad. I wish Redhat had given any instruction
on how to do it.
Once you get the 2.1.14 source, symlink it to /usr/src/linux.
Yes, /usr/include/linux, asm and scsi are all symlinked to
/usr/src/linux/include...
When you do a make dep, you'll get a timespecbit.h not found error.
Just do a touch /usr/src/linux/include/linux/timespecbit.h.
When you are doing a make boot, additional errors will surface.
They all have something to do with include files. I just touch them
all. One of the files is smp_lock.h and smp.h in
/usr/src/linux/include/asm. Since I don't have a SMP system, I think
it is safe just to touch those files. After which, you should be
able to contiue to do make boot.

IMPORTANT, when you do a make config, make sure you answer NO to
TGA.

My solution is not optimal. When I compile hclnsfd, I had to hack
the sys/timevalbit.h file. Hopefully, RPM version of glibc will
come out soon.
