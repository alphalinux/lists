> On Fri, 13 Dec 1996, Woodstock wrote:
>
> > [ARC] is usually the default on UDB... and _encouraged_ for booting linux.
> . ..
> > Right. ARC and SRM are usually in the Flash rom... and Milo usually
> > resides on a small MS-DOS partition (in the ARC case). Though milo _can_
> > be put in flash rom, too.
>
> Thanks. Is it correct to state that the preferred environment to run
> Linux is 1) have both SRM and ARC both installed, 2) then use ARC to boot
> MILO and then Linux? (I take it MILO is _not_ optional, right?)
>

The following combinations work (although not on all Alpha systems):

flash->milo->linux
ARC->milo->linux
SRM->milo->linux
SRM->aboot->linux

It is true to say that some Alpha systems will never get MILO ported to them but one of
the primary reasons for MILO was that we charge for the SRM (usually the cost of a
Digital Unix license) and so being able to either flash MILO or boot MILO via ARC was
to bring down the costs of a Linux box. Additionally, MILO gives you total source code
control over everything from the SROM upwards (and on some systems you can have that too)
and includes device drivers and file systems that ARC and SRM will *never* have.
