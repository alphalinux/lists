On Thu, 19 Dec 1996, Aaron wrote:

> Ok here is a strange question I have to ask.
> I have a combination monitor/keyboard switch,
> and would like to use it to switch back and forth
> between my AT&T Pentium 75 machine, and my UDB (166)
> Multia.
>
> This is a New switch that has the older style keyboard
> ports on it. I figured add an adapter, and plug them in
> and all is well......
>
> BUT (and there is always a but)
>
> I have been warned that switching back and forth between and UDB and
> another machine will cause the UDB to hang or worse to fry some circuit or
> other. This was explained to us by a "Qualified Alpha Reseller".
>
> OK, what the catch, I'd really like to use the switch and save congestion
> on my desk?? Any suggestions?

It depends on what switch you have. The really good ones (like the
"servswitch" from Black Box) fake out the unselected computers and
make them think that a keyboard is still connected. This way they
will also boot up without a "keyboard error".

The cheaper switches simply leave the unselected machine unconnected
to anything. This would almost certainly cause problems with the UDB.
I know for a fact that it is quite possible to blow up the UDB
keyboard port by hot plugging or unplugging the keyboard cable and
a cheap switch might well do the same thing.

Unfortunately, the high-quality switches cost about as much as a UDB
is going for these days :-(

Cheap switches also have frequency response problems. They are OK
for text mode but if you are running high-resolution graphics you
will get noticable smearing and ghosting.

As always you get what you pay for.
