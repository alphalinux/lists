Michael Champigny <champign@cadosf.hlo.dec.com> writes:
> Kill as many background processes as is feasible to give the processor
> less to do, then run a minimal window manager such as twm.

Um, as I understand it, twm is far from light-weight in anything but
features. I seem to remember that the original fvwm was among the
lighter-weight wms.

It is, however, academic, since the _best_ solution is to not use a
window manager at all. From the console do:

xinit ./xquake

or, my preference:

xinit ./xf86quake

Now you might have to take this with a grain of salt---I've only done
it under linux/x86; my alpha should be arriving in a couple of weeks.

I suspect it'll work, though.
