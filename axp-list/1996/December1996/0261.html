Every time when your source is "helpful" enough to redefine something
from system header files you comment out this redefinition.

A possible very rare exception (I have seen that) is when your sources
use #define with the same name as in system headers but with a totally
unrelated meaning (or something like that). Then you have to edit
your sources and change the name in question.

Whatever happens you DO NOT edit your system header files. One reason
is that your libraries, with which you will be linking, expect that.
