Hi,

I know that the sound in Alpha is not ready for primetime, but I have to
ask this.

Symptoms:
I am able to play audio to the audio device. Games like quake and
Maelstrom seem to be able to use the MSS audio compatible chip
"correctly". When I try to record I am not able to get anything recorded
except noise. I could not find any mixer that uses the /dev/mixer device
correctly (they all seem to asume SB og Gravis) so I wrote my own crude
mixer just so that I am able to vary the record/playback volumes.
When adjusting the volumes for LINE1 LINE2 LINE3 and IGAIN in a certain
way I can get every thing from "tic tic.." sound to a high frequency
sinusoidal sound (feedback loop) but without any external feedback.
(These are not recorded sounds, but just sounds in the speaker)
This happens both with the sounddrivers in kernel 2.0.18 and the
latest one in kernel 2.1.13.

Possible reasons for malfunctioning:
There are two main possible reasons for this not to be working.
1) The sound driver is not compatible with MSS in alpha.
2) I was moving my alpha around the other day. As I grabed the box with
my hands a static discharge went from my fingers to one of the external
audio connectors. So it is possible that I have destroyed the audio
subsystem of the computer.

Reason for posting:
Could someone give my some feedback if the above symptoms are "normal",
i.e. that with a high probability me audio chip is allright. Or if some
one could say that it is possible to record audio, and this anoying
feedback is not "normal". Then i can assume that my audio subsystem is
broken and can stop to think about it 8-).

Thanks,
K.D.

P.S. Anyone using Gravis Plug and Play cards with alpha systems and
AXP/Linux?
