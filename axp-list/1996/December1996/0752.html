Vik Bajaj wrote:
>
> | I have been warned that switching back and forth between and UDB and
> | another machine will cause the UDB to hang or worse to fry some circuit
> | or other. This was explained to us by a "Qualified Alpha Reseller".
>
> Yes, __PLEASE__ do not use a keyboard switch with the UDB!
>
> I looked at this; the only solution I could find was a nice digital
> switch that keeps a constant load across all inputs. Price? $480.
>
> --Vik

The UDB likes to perform a hard reset of the keyboard's internal
controller by cycling power off/on under logic control after the CPU is
powered up. Most switchboxes maintain DC power to the keyboard by a
"wire-OR" of diodes from all computers connected to it, with the intent
that the keyboard will stay powered up regardless of selector setting.
Unfortunately, the UDB firmware reports an initialization failure if it
cannot cycle things itself.

I just make sure to pull the other keyboard cables before booting the
Alpha, then reconnect them after it's up. This has caused absolutely no
problems to date. I doubt that use of a switchbox will harm the machine
in any way, but YMMV, so don't tell them I told you to do it :-)).

Anyone from Digital have a comment on this?
