>Digital Unix, by far. Linux is still a "baby" OS when it comes to the
>Alpha archetecture. It's only been out for a year or so now. GCC's

"Steep" is kind of a relative term, however. As compared to "free" it is an
expensive OS. As compared to, for example, NT server, it's not too bad a
price on this CPU. A 2 user licence for DUNIX, as you called it, costs $695
for a 233MHz UDB or noname. The only catch is that you can't order it for a
UDB, as it's "unsupported". Good news is that all you have to do is tell DEC
it's a "noname" (aka EB22CZ) bboard, and they will be happy to sell a license...
This is legal, BTW.
