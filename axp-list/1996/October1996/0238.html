>>> "Oleg Shein #2" said:
>
> I need RZ28N parameters (number of heads, cylinders and sectors) or
> Digital Unix disktab entry for bsd style partitioning. A also appreciate
> such information about Seagate Barracuda ST15150N drive.
> finding on seagate and digital www's revealed nothing...
>
> fdisk offers to use 62 sec. 1019 cyl and 65 heads for ST32430N and
> 62 sec. 1017 cyl. and 133 heads for ST15150N, which I think to be wrong.
> I'm going to install aboot, so I guess I really need true parameters for
> fdisk bsd style or minlabel partitioning.

If you're going to install "aboot", you *MUST* use minlabel (well, you
could use the BSD option in fdisk, but why bother? :-); in fact,
minlabel ought to find and give you the right parameters, too. I've never
seen minlabel fail to find the drive geometry.

> ps: should I compile kernel with UFS and BSD style disklabel to support
> such partitions?

No need; UFS is a FileSystem, could actually appear under a DOS partition
table (I think), but that'd be strange... :-)

LINUX kernels *always* understand both BSD/minlabel style and DOS/fdisk
style partitions tables. Sometimes it even gets into trouble because of it.
