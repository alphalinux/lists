Hello All, this one goes out to Jay Estabrook and Stephen Gaudet, and
anyone else who might know anything about TGA and the Multia.

According to the programming information[0] for the DECchip 21030, the
device supports 1600x1280x8bpp/75Hz (non-interlaced) in 2 megabytes of
VRAM. Also, at lower resolutions higher color depths are supported,
e.g. it says that (again with 2 MB VRAM) 1024x768 can do 16bpp (64K
colors?), 800x600 can do 24bpp (16M colors?), and 640x480 can do
32bpp (??? colors?).

Given the above information, does that mean my Multia can do 32bpp at
640x480? (Does that mean I can do 12bpp at 1280x1024? The reference
manual doesn't say.) I was under the impression that the Zx1 (or
whatever it's called) could only do 8bpp in all modes, and that
1280x1024 was its maximum resolution. Can someone explain? Does it
have something to do with the RAMDAC (e.g. fig. 1-2 in the manual
shows a "typical 8bpp configuration" that uses a Bt485 RAMDAC, which
has something called a "color lookup table" in it)?[1]

Also, the manual says that there's this 32bpp mode for the chip. Ok,
the most I've ever heard of is 24bpp, so what's those extra 8 bits do?

Also, supposedly there's a VGA feature connector. Anyone know where
that is on the UDB motherboard? (I seems to have misplaced my
hardware manual...*sigh*.)

[0] For reference, I'm getting this information[2] from _DECchip 21030
PCI Graphics Accelerator: Reference Manual_, Digital order number
EC-N0683-72, Sept. '94.

[1] Hey! If I solder on a better RAMDAC, would that mean I can have
higher color depths? Whee!![3]

[2] Why didn't anyone tell me that writing fscking device drivers is a
royal pain in the ass?[3]

[3] At least I'll finally be able to post to alt.hackers. :)
