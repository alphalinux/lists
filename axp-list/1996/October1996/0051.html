I'm attaching a Conner 8000 series SCSI DAT tape to my UDB. I've used the
drive on PC type equipment with no problems. I've compiled 2.0.20w/axp-diffs
to include the NCR8xx driver, with disconnects, with memory mapped IO, and
no forcing of synchronous IO. Also included SCSI tape support in kernel.
When I go to do a tar, it startts to do the file list it's working on and
then I get a 'read error' message followed by dozens of lines of error
reporting (verbose SCSI reporting turned on in the kernel). The 'taper'
utility has the same problem in that it starts to write to the drive then
gets hung up.

I seem to remember reading in the Conner manual that when first initializing
a blank tape, the drive waits until the first write command is issued, then
goes off and does something to the tape that might take a few seconds. It
appears that the SCSI driver doesn't like that a little bit. I can't think
of a way to initialize the tape since the software I have on the PC side
runs under DOS/Windows and formats the tape in its own way (probably not in
a way compatable with linux/tar).

Is there already a driver or command line option thru mt that can let me use
this DAT tape on my UDB (since I really would rather do that than trying to
back up the UDB via a PC)?

Thanks.
