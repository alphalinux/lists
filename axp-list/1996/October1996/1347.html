> I believe the Sparc standard is being used, therefore any Sparc A/B Cable should work. I would appreciate it if someone could confirm this, I've misplaced mine...

Actually the "rarely used" pins that are used are in fact part of the
RS-232 specification, which specifies two ports on the standard DB-25
connector. Assuming that the SPARC adheres to the official standard
then its cable should work as well.

This standard is for asynchronous ports. Although modems will work
when connected directly to a two-port connector you may (read will)
run into trouble if you connect a synchronous device.

For example my Motorola Bitsurfr will NOT work when connected
directly to the UDB port but works just fine (asynchronously)
when connect to either port of a "Y" cable.
