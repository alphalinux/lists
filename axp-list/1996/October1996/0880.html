Przemek Klosowski wrote:
>
> Does it seem plausible to people to compile the computational kernels
> of their code (ya know, the 10% of the lines that make up 90% of the
> CPU time) on DEC Unix, generating assembler code, and then
> assembling and linking it under Linux?

Even then You would need DEC's optimized BLAS (DXML)
(Basic Linear Algebra Subroutines)
for matrix routines. The DEC BLAS runs many
times faster than the public domain BLAS compiled
on an Alpha, even when the KAP optimizer is used
to unroll, set cashe size, etc.
Profiling often shows that most time is spent
in BLAS routines where programs are matrix intense.

We are talking about a two to four fold differences in speed,
just enough to give the Pentium Pro a competitive
advantage over the the Alpha for Linux Number Crunching.
If it doesn't appear that gcc and the libraries will
improve significantly soon, or that DEC will offer
individual software components for Alhpa/Linux,
there doesn't seem to be much reason for buying
an Alpha as a Linux platform.
