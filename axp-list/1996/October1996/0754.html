Alright,

I found two unused alphas in our storage room. They had all of the Digital
UNIX CDs, Manuals and Microsoft NT 3.51 for Alpha CDs (they were preloaded).
I spooled the /shlib and /bin directories on the UNIX box to a 4mm DAT tape.
I then blew away the NT and loaded RedHat 3.0.3 for Alpha. I also blew away
the Digital UNIX box as we have new versions of the OS to load. I loaded
Digital-UNIX-Netscape-3.0 from Netscape and the following files from the
tape on to the clean "former-NT" box:

/bin/loader

Then I tried to run Netscape.

By trial and error I determined the following files are needed:

/usr/shlib/libXm.so
/usr/shlib/libXmu.so
/usr/shlib/libXt.so
/usr/shlib/libX11.so
/usr/shlib/libdnet_stub.so
/usr/shlib/libm.so
/usr/shlib/libc.so
/usr/shlib/libXext.so

After I loaded them it ran like a charm.

So, I blew RedHat away, reloaded NT and put the other box on the workbench
for the UNIX techs to upgrade.

I know that,

/usr/shlib/libXmu.so
/usr/shlib/libXt.so
/usr/shlib/libX11.so
/usr/shlib/libXext.so

are all currently available under my Alpha Motif 2.0 distribution. What
about the others?

/usr/shlib/libXm.so
/usr/shlib/libdnet_stub.so
/usr/shlib/libm.so
/usr/shlib/libc.so

Can they be duplicated?

Also, I still don't know whether there is a Public Domain or GPL version of
/bin/loader. Maybe there is a work-around?

Any suggestions?

Mike

Maybe it would be simpler to convince Netscape to do a Linux/Alpha version ;-)
