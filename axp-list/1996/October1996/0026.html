There is one issue with f2c, as distributed on Red Hat 3.0.3 CD, which
I do not recall beeing raised.

f2c, in its headers, defines type 'integer', to be used by Fortran, as
'long'. This is probably an effect of making f2c to work on MS-DOS
machines. This is carried to alpha and has this nasty effect that
integer on it, and other types which are predicated on it, are 8 bytes
wide whereas everywhere else this number is four.

I have no idea it this affects a performance one way or another,
although it might, but - as a side-effect - integer*4 and real*4 on
axp are not. The later cannot be classified in any other way as
a bug and affects behaviour of "dusty decks". Also related f2c
and fort77 flags do not behave as documented.

f2c can likely be fixed, but object files compiled with an "old f2c"
and "corrected f2c" will be not compatible after required changes.
