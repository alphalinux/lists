> Has anyone tried any of the following drives with their UDB's??
> Toshiba MK-1924FBV 540MB
> Toshiba MK-1926FBV 815MB

The 1924 is what DEC used for the VX42-F2:

> cat /proc/scsi/scsi
Attached devices:
Host: scsi0 Channel: 00 Id: 00 Lun: 00
Vendor: TOSHIBA Model: MK1924FBV Rev: 802A
Type: Direct-Access ANSI SCSI revision: 02

I was trying to find a 2G drive to replace it with, but they only seem
to ba available in IDE :-( I think that's Apple's fault for switching to
IDE in the PowerBook line.

The performance of 2.5 inch drives is so poor that I think I'm going to stick
with an external 3.5.

At this time, I have colocated at my ISP:
DEC UDB
Iomega Jaz drive (to be replaced by a 4G Seagate Haw soon)
NetBlazer LS ISDN router
Tonecommander NT1
10-base-T hub
APC SmartUPS 700
various power bricks
lots of cables

As you point out, the small form factor of the UDB becomse somewhat irrelevant
when there's so much stuff hanging off of it. I might as well just use a
PC with an internal ISDN card. Then I'd only need two boxes, the PC and the
UPS.
