When I built the my latest kernel, I made sure that when I gave the
specification for the
NCR53c7,8 chip in my UBD, I turned on the Fast SCSII-II swithes, and the
asynchronous
mode. I think I have detected a bit of a speedup just from doing that,
although I have not
had time to test it with any benchmarks. Anyway, once I turned on the Fast
SCSI-II, I receive
this addition tidbit that gives me at a least a warm-fuzzy that the
controller is in the right
mode.

scsi0 : NCR53c{7,8}xx (rel 17)
scsi : 1 host.
scsi0 : target 4 accepting period 100ns offset 8 10.00MHz FAST SCSI-II
scsi0 : setting target 4 to period 100ns offset 8 10.00MHz FAST SCSI-II
Vendor: HP Model: C3325A Rev: 5293
Type: Direct-Access ANSI SCSI revision: 02
Detected scsi disk sda at scsi0, channel 0, id 4, lun 0
scsi : detected 1 SCSI disk total.
SCSI device sda: hdwr sector= 512 bytes. Sectors= 4238836 [2069 MB] [2.1 GB]
