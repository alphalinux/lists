>>> Marc Singer said:
> >
> > I was wondering if UDB's were midi capable, meaning they could play
> > midi files provided that the kernel was built with support.
> > Feedback would be appreciated,
> > Eric.
>
> I successfully compiled the MSS (Microsoft Sound System) driver on a
> 486 with the MSS hardware installed. There is OPL support on that
> board, so I'd guess that the MSS compatible hardware in the UDBs does
> support Yamaha OPL3. I DID have much problems compiling the driver as
> it did not include the proper sequencer sources in the makefile. Once
> I hacked the Makefile and configuration header, it compiled and booted
> properly. Note that I have not tried to play anything with the
> device. The kernel reports that it found the OPL3 port.
>
> Sound initialization started
> <MSS audio codec (AD1848)> at 0x534 irq 11 dma 3,3
> <Yamaha OPL3 FM> at 0x388
> Sound initialization complete

Yes, that looks as if it found it; have you tried using it?

"playmidi -f" will play a MIDI file using the FM synth (if present). It won't
sound very good, but it'll work if there.

I found "timidity" to be *much* more satifying, although more expensive in
terms of disk space (*lots* of sample files) and CPU, although the latter is
hardly noticeable in most instances, I think.

Were the compilation problems for UDB or INTEL? Did you specify both MSS and
OPL3 when you configured sound? There *used* to be problems with that, but I
thought they'd gone away... ???
