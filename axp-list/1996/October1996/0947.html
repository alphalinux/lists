If Jay said there was no termination inside the UDB, he was mistaken. But
I think what he actually said was that the 2.5 inch *drive* had no
termination.

The UDB has exactly one internal SCSI terminator soldered on the PCI riser.
It can't be disabled. If you added two external terminators you bus is
over-terminated, which can cause problems and possibly damage the line
drivers of SCSI chips. You should only add one external terminator at the
very end of the SCSI bus.

The internal disk in the UDB is not terminated, and can't be. Digital
claims that they can get away with this due to the short stub length. When
I was having problems I looked at some of the SCSI signals on an
oscilliscope and they didn't look too bad.
