Hi,

In "Microprocessor Report" volume 10, number 9 (july 8, 1996) the author
states that to obtain maximum performance for the new 500-Mhz device about
4MB of external cache is required. Today, if I'm not wrong, you only get
1MB onboard cache on the PC164 motherboard. The older EB164 supports 2MB,
4MB or 8MB cache modules, but only on a 21164 running max 333MHz.

Is there any rumor of a new motherboard from Digital Semiconductor
based on the 21164A supporting more cache?
