>>> Noah Lee Gibbs said:
> Has anybody gotten any variety of GUS to work with Linux/AXP? I
> haven't tried it yet, and may not have time to do so, but I was curious
> if anybody had and knew it was possible...

Actually, yes; I got an ACE to work (as well as it could :-). Didn't have
to do anything to the drivers, if I remember correctly. It worked quite well
with "playmidi -g", except I kept running out of onboard RAM space, as
the nice piano sample I was using was *quite* large; piano sounded good,
but there were other samples that were unable to be loaded.

BTW, it doesn't matter whether you configure the driver for GUS MAX or not;
it complains that it found one when you *didn't*, and that it didn't find
one when you *did*; but it happily worked either way for me... :-) :-)

I didn't try any of the more "advanced" GUS-related apps/tools/etc.

Also, beware of Plug 'n Play cards; an SB16 PnP I tried was totally unseen
by the probe code. Other cards may be better, once setup by DOS/Win/NT or
whatever (my SB16 wasn't), so your mileage may vary.
