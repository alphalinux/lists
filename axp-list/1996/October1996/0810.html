Jim Nance wrote:
> >...
> > the ECOFF version of netscape (there - now you know why I really care about
> > this :-).
>
> I suspect this is why most people are interested in Digital Unix compatable
> shared libs.

There are some of us who would like to do real number crunching
on an Alpa Linux machine. The current status of optimization
for Alpha Linux in gcc, g77, and the LAPACK/BLAS libraries puts
AXP Linux at a disadvantage. It appears that a 200 MHz
Pentium pro can outperform a 500 MHz 21164A (PC164) using Linux/f77/g77
at a fraction of the cost. DEC has to realize that there is a market
for their software components here.

> What I would really like to talk about though is Digitals plans for ELF,
> ....
> I would like to cc Jon Hall, but I have lost his email address :-(

Jon Hall monitors this list and has responded to my previous query about
the legality of running DEC Alpha binaries on Linux, but it appears
that there are obstacles to these ideas elsewhere at DEC. Let me say
this
again: I would be willing to pay for DEC software that makes my PC164
go faster. I just don't want to pay for all of DEC Unix again.
Since It appears that Linux is now the most widely used Unix
system, I think there are many others in academic computing
who would also make this a profitable market for DEC.
Jon, are you listening?
