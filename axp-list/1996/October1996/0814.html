> On Sat, 19 Oct 1996, David A Rusling wrote:
> > It sounds like MILO is not getting interrupts anymore. Normally this should

> The WNT ARC firmware sees the disk just fine when my external drive is NOT

> On the other hand, if I disconnect the external drive entirely from the

You say that you have "switched around" the cabling. I'm not sure
what you mean by that but the symptoms you cite are pretty classic
for cabling and/or termination problems.

I had some similar troubles which seemed to change when I re-cabled
but never entirely went away till I followed the advice of the SCSI-HOWTO
which suggests that when using fast scsi devices or external cabling you
use an active terminator. I obtained one of these and my SCSI problems
went away (well, I still have rare problems but I think they are
related to using the 53c7,8xx driver. I plan to switch to a newer
kernel with the 53c8xx driver soon).

I have also found that cheap cables cause more trouble than good
ones. The better ones have a cable with an outside diameter of close
to 1/2 inch while the cheaper ones use a thinner wire. If you are
using multiple external cables it is best to have them all be the
same type to minimize impedance mis-matches.

Anyway, that's what my experience has taught me.
