OK. Good. Now, if this is the case, then the SpecInt/SpecFP numbers
are not very good indicators of relative performance characteristics.
And if this is so, I'm wondering what is causing the discrepency. I
know that 64bittedness is one factor we cannot adequately test until
GNU/Linux goes 64 bit--I think GNU is ready and the kernel isn't.
But, I've been under the impression that the compiler is at fault (and
fixable). Are the subsystems comparable? SCSI controller, SCSI disk,
cache, and so on. Does a kernel compile take the same time on the two
machines?

Why do I care so much? Well, I am finding that my Multi 166 is about
70% as fast as my P5/166 and this is with the slow hard drive and
21066 CPU. I bought a couple of UDBs to hack on Alpha with the
impression that they'd be pretty fast for the $. If I can squeeze a
little more from them, I'll be in hog heaven.
