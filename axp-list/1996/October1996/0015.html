On Tue, 1 Oct 1996, Hunter A. Ware wrote:

>
> That brings me to my question. With the exception of neat little
> tricks like kernel debugging and vga patch etc... Do the diff patches
> actually do anything that I need to worry about with the 2.+ kernels?
>

They don't (IMHO) seem to be absolutely necessary, although they do
occasionally fix things (32/64-bit code differences, etc.) that are NICE
to have fixed, but I'm not sure that most of the changes aren't more of a
cosmetic thing. We've got ~20 machines running 24/7/365, most of which
are Alphas with kernels built from the stock sources, and to be honest,
they are MORE stable than our Intel boxen. Didn't think that was
possible, but there it is. If you're having trouble building kernels
after applying the AXP diff, try compiling from the pristine source tree.

Anyone more knowledgable than I (and this includes most of you), feel
free to correct me on the AXP-diff front if I'm wrong. Flames will be
read, considered carefully, and then forgotten forever.
