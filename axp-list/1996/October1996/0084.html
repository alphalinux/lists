<maurice@ellpspace.math.ualberta.ca> wrote:
>At 09:48 hrs. 10-01-96 -0500, you wrote:
>>I'm attaching a Conner 8000 series SCSI DAT tape to my UDB.
><snip>
>
>>Is there already a driver or command line option thru mt that can let me use
>>this DAT tape on my UDB (since I really would rather do that than trying to
>>back up the UDB via a PC)?
>
>Add this to /etc/rc.d/rc.local to make DAT work correctly:
>
>(sleep 30; mt 2>/dev/null -f /dev/nst0 setblk 0) &
>
>Key command here being 'mt -f /dev/nst0 setblk 0'. I don't know if you
>will need to do the sleep before it, but most tape drives won't listen to
>such commands until it initialized (which takes some time).
