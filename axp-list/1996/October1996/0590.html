On Wed, 16 Oct 1996, Allan C. Anderson wrote:

> After having read some of the posts here on performance and the
> alpha architecture, I am thinking that 256K of cache is not enough.
>
> Has anyone upgraded to say 512K or 1M, and if so:
>
> What kind of cache memory is needed?
>
> How much is it?
>
> Is it difficult to install?
>
> and the real question: Did you notice a speedup running linux?

Assuming that you are talking about a VX-40 type 166Mhz UDB it is
my understanding that the cache (as well as the CPU) are permanently
soldered to the motherboard and can't be upgraded unless you have
surface-mount equipment at hand.

I did look into upgrading the (upgradable) 512kb. cache in my 233Mhz.
vx-42 UDB. Although the manual lists a DEC part number I have not
been able to find anyone who will sell it to me.

I do agree that either flavor of UDB would benefit from a larger
cache though.
