On Tue, 29 Oct 1996, Al Potter wrote:

> -
> Greetings:
>
> I have a UDB (VX42) with the internal 2.5" disk and am, like many, not too
> happy with the disk's performance. I looked up the specs on this disk in the
> Pocket PCRef, 5th Edition and it ain't too shabby, not great, but not terrible
> either:
> 4200 rpm, 13 ms access SCSI-II
>
> I've owned several laptop / notebook computers in the past and many of them
> have their internal disks set up to spool down after a short period of
> activity, usually for purposes of power conservation.
>
> The disk in this unit _sounds_ like it's powering up and down in this power
> saving cycle. Is it possible that Digital shipped the unit this way, possibly
> for purposes of HEAT conservation?
>

Hmmm. I agree that most if not all notebook-type machines have some
sort of capability to spin down the hard drive to conserve power but I
don't think such is inherent in the drive itself but rather external
circuitry which tells the drive to spin down. At least I know this
is true with the small IDE drives, I have no experience with 2.5"
SCSI drives other than the one in my UDB. The boot messages say that
it is a fast-scsi-2 device and that the transfer rate is set at
10Mhz.

I think it unlikely that DEC would put power-down in a device that
is not designed to run on battery power but on the other hand I would
have thought it unlikely that they would dump 8000 UDBs on the market
either.

As a DEC shareholder I would like to see them apply the same sort
of quality to their marketing as they do to their engineering :-)
