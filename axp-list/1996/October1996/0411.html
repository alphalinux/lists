On 13 Oct 96 at 16:30, przemek klosowski wrote:

...
> So people are disappointed that their 166MHz Alpha is slower than 75MHz
> Pentium; sure, if someone paid $8000 for it (like I did 3 years ago), they
> would have a point, but for $600? CDROM is 7 days late? Ouch, I guess
> the world domination will have to wait.
...
> I would concede that the wizards on this list (to which group I
> certainly do NOT belong) may collectively be guilty of creating a
> somehow misleading appearance about AXP Linux. A novice could possibly
> conclude that the AXP release is just as stable as Linux/Intel. As we
> know this is not quite true yet. If you are after stability and ease
> of installation, and don't want to spend time understanding and fixing
> things, buy a Pentium!
...

This touches on a few issues that I worry/wonder about. First is the
performance of a UDB, 166Mhz.. I must admit that Pentium 75
performance is a bit dissapointing, but it's not exactly bad. I
started with Linux on my 386 DX/40 and thought that was cool (except
for kernel compiles, but hey..) BUT, I do wonder if the performance
could be improved with a better optimizing GCC or something? I am
extremely happy to see an ELF distribution from redhat. Anyways, the
$790 I paid for my UDB (and NT 4.0) was certainly worth it--it's a
cute/slick/neat little machine. And I fully intend to get RH 4.0 up
and running on a 3 to 4 GB drive and start writing code on it. (I've
always wanted a dedicated Linux machine all to myself.)

The other reason I bought the UDB is because I read in the Linux
Journal (and on-line as well) that the Alpha platform is just as
stable as the Intel platform. After 'lurking' on this list for the
past week I'm getting the impression this isn't true. But I do see
that this effort is in capable hands. And who knows, maybe I can
contribute! I've never been afraid of tough installs,
tweaking/fixing kernel code, etc... Actually if it just plain works
I feel like I've been cheated out of some 'experience'. But I
certainly wouldn't recommend an Alpha (w/ Linux) to a newbie. A x86
system maybe... *SIGH*

I realize I'm babbling a little here. But I'll conclude with a real
question. Just how impressive is Linux on a 433 Mhz (or higher)
system? And is this sort of thing done? I'd imaging that the cost
of Digital Unix isn't prohibitive when you spend $5000+ on hardware.
(I've got to admit I'm tempted to buy one of these systems though...)

Well, 'nuff said. My gratitude goes out to all the folks working on
this stuff. :-)
