Is there any way to force a package to remove despite dependancies
from other modules?

After I had compiled a few kernels, I noticed that somehow my
makefile had been replaced with an intel one (don't ask me how).
I then decided to remove the kernel sources. So I removed
the kernel-src rpm, which failed on some parts since I had some
compiled object files in there. (It died with "directory not empty").
I then went and manually deleted the kernel directory, not realizing
that 2 packages made up that dir (kernel-headers was in there too).

I then went into the graphical RPM proggy and did a check for what was
missing in kernel-headers. After it crashed (too many entries I guess)
I tried removing kernel-headers, after which it promptly complained
that some other modules depended on it and wouldn't let me remove it.

I then went back a step to remove those, but there were modules
depending on them as well! After I had selected all the modules
and none were depending on them, I started the remove process
and it froze up on me.

Aside from the bugs in the graphical package manager, is there any way
to override the "dependancy safety" or perhaps to just replace
a package without removing and reinstalling?
