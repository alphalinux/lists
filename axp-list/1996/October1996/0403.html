At 03:47 hrs. 10-13-96 -0500, you wrote:
>Maurice,
>
>Thanks for the info. It seems that this should be a simple matter to
>correct. However, it would appear that root disk that I am using does not
>contain minlabel. I went back to the RedHat fpt site and downloaded another
>copy from the redhat-4.0 axp tree. This one does not appear to contain it
>either. Any suggestions on where to get an updated root disk or on how to
>make a new root disk would be appricited.
>
Put the RedHat 3.03 CD in a PC, copy \dosutils\rawrite to somewhere on the
DOS path, then go on the CD to \images\1390.
Put a formatted DOS disk in drive a: .
Do a rawrite -f noname.img -d a: -n.
Once drive a: stops spinning and light goes out it is done.
This will work, as I have used it.
Oh, BTW, in this I am assuming a UDB machine. If other, then choose the
appropriate image in the 1390 directory.
Minlabel ends up on the same point on the path (/usr/bin) on the
installation as fdisk.
