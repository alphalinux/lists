On Wed, 16 Oct 1996, Robert Rxdsten wrote:

> Hi, how is the performance of the gnu f2c and gnu g77 compiler using
> linux/alpha? I was under the impression that you don't get the same
> performance as from a linux/i386 solution. I would like to hear some
> advices from someone who has experience on those compilers.

G77 doesn't quite work yet, apparently (though Craftworks claims to
have it).

f2c (using it by itself and with the various drivers) gives me bad results
with rather simple programs. I was using a simple, well known Runge-Kutta
diff eq solver (rkf45.f at netlib) which works fine on my HP palmtop,
various Suns, OSF/1 boxen and etc, but it won't work on Linux-AXP or
Linux-x86 using f2c. This has nothing to do with math libraries, unless it is
exception handling related, as RK algorithm doesn't use anything more
complicated than /.
