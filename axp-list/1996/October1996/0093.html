Eric Smith wrote:
> Assuming you have real 36-bit SIMMs, not 32-bit or some crappy "logic parity"
> SIMMs, if you get lots of these as you describe it means some of your memory
> is bad. From the address you could determine which pair of SIMMs it is, if
> you knew how (which I don't). The easiest thing to do is to try running Linux
> with only one pair at a time. There are only six combinations of four items
> taken two at a time, so it shouldn't take you too terribly long to track down
> which is bad.
>
> There is a also a somewhat less likely explanation: one of your SIMM sockets
> may have a bad contact or a bad solder joint.
Hmmm... not a bad idea, I should have thought of that! This would
eliminate the possibility of the bad contact/solder joint possibility
also... I'll try this when I get home.
