>>> Matthew X Economou said:
>
> According to the programming information[0] for the DECchip 21030, the
> device supports 1600x1280x8bpp/75Hz (non-interlaced) in 2 megabytes of
> VRAM. Also, at lower resolutions higher color depths are supported,
> e.g. it says that (again with 2 MB VRAM) 1024x768 can do 16bpp (64K
> colors?), 800x600 can do 24bpp (16M colors?), and 640x480 can do
> 32bpp (??? colors?).

That's essentially correct, but I'd say the *CHIP* can support those things,
but you've got to surround it with other components which are also capable
of those things.

> Given the above information, does that mean my Multia can do 32bpp at
> 640x480? (Does that mean I can do 12bpp at 1280x1024? The reference
> manual doesn't say.) I was under the impression that the Zx1 (or
> whatever it's called) could only do 8bpp in all modes, and that
> 1280x1024 was its maximum resolution. Can someone explain? Does it
> have something to do with the RAMDAC (e.g. fig. 1-2 in the manual
> shows a "typical 8bpp configuration" that uses a Bt485 RAMDAC, which
> has something called a "color lookup table" in it)?[1]

OK, here's the breakdown in capabilities:

ZLXp-E1 8bpp ONLY, Bt485 RAMDAC, 2Mb VRAM
ZLXp-E2 24 bpp ONLY, Bt463 RAMDAC, 8Mb VRAM
ZLXp-E3 24 bpp ONLY plus Z-buffer, Bt463 RAMDAC, 16Mb VRAM

The UDB has essentially an -E1 built into the motherboard.

AFAIK, no one ever built a board using the 21030 that would do multiple
depths, and no one ever built a board to do anything but 8-bit or 24-bit.

Actually, maybe a better way to put that is, AFAIK no one ever programmed a
given implementation for more than the depth which was at the center of its
primary design.

> Also, the manual says that there's this 32bpp mode for the chip. Ok,
> the most I've ever heard of is 24bpp, so what's those extra 8 bits do?

So you don't have to do *packed* 24-bit, and can work with the more managable
32 bits per pixel in your code. I'm not even sure that the chip can do 24-bit
packed, it prolly wants 32-bit quantities as much as the CPU... :-)

> Also, supposedly there's a VGA feature connector. Anyone know where
> that is on the UDB motherboard? (I seems to have misplaced my
> hardware manual...*sigh*.)

Sigh yourself... :-)

This was never utilized on *any* implementation using TGA that I know
of; NCR did OEM these chips from DEC, but I don't know what they did with
them...

> [0] For reference, I'm getting this information[2] from _DECchip 21030
> PCI Graphics Accelerator: Reference Manual_, Digital order number
> EC-N0683-72, Sept. '94.

I believe this, or maybe an earlier version, is available for FTP from
(at least) netbsd.org, along with some other Alpha-related specs.

Wait just a *little* bit longer for XFree86 3.2 to get released; there's a
TGA server in there, with sources, that you can happily while your hours
away tweaking to your heart's content... :-)
