With the indulgence of the list, and to correct at least one Linux
documentation article that had the incorrect name for "dd":

When dd(1) was created, it was named "copy and convert", for obvious reasons.
It could copy almost anything, and convert it from one blocksize to another,
one code form to another, etc. on the fly. Then the authors looked for a
"terse" name (UNIX names are never cryptic, only terse), they naturally
thought about "cc". But by that time Dennis had already written the "C"
compiler, and that was "cc". So (being straightforward and terse) they bumped
each letter lexicographically and called it "dd".

awk(1) was named after the three authors who wrote it, Alfred V. Aho, Peter J.
~
Weinberger, and Brian W. Kernighan
~ ~

Mr. Aho went on to write a series of books, not the least of which many of you
have used for compiler theory, the infamous "Dragon Book" (yes, you know which
one I mean), which he wrote with Jeffrey Ullman. But that is another story,
to be saved for the USELINUX conference:

(http://www.usenix.org/ana97/index.html#USELINUX)

and to be told only after the consumption of many beers.
