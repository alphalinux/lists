On Sun, 27 Oct 1996, Kari Davidsson wrote:

> Hi there,
>
> I was just curious, about one ore two things regarding
> overclocking a 166MHz 21066 Alpha processor. I know it is not a good
> idea, but if I overclock to 200MHz are there any posiblility that the
> chip itself will get destroyed? Or will I "just" suffer from a very
> instable system? Actually I turned the CPU to 200MHz last night and
> got the amasing 198 BogoMips 8-), but this was just a quick check
> to verify that the jumpers work 8-) lasted only for few minutes from
> boot to cat /proc/cpuinfo 8-). If I go for it regardless of what
> other people say and destroy the CPU, is it exspensive to get an
> replacemant CPU? Are the 21066 chips still beeing made?
>
> This was just a thought that went through my head last night. Any
> feedback on availablity and price of the 21066 greatly appreciated.

I think it is generally believed that overclocking 10-15% is usually
OK. I have been running my 233Mhz. UDB at 266Mhz. for six months
virtuallly continuously with no problems.

I would NOT overcvlock the 166 to 200 however, since that goes way
beyond the 10-15% rule. Also, since the CPU is soldered onto the
motherboard in the 166Mhz. unit your problem would not be where
to get a new chip if you blow it up but where to get a new motherboard
(that is unless you happen to have surface-mount solder/de-solder
equipment at hand).
