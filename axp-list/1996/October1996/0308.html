> On Thu, 10 Oct 1996, Karl Stenerud wrote:

> > Now that I've installed redhat 4,0, fsck ALWAYS hits when it checks
> > my /usr partition, even though I've shutdown properly.
> > (It works fine with my root fs. i.e. says it's clean and skips over)
> >
> > This wouldn't be a serious problem except for the fact that this
> > partition is 1.5gigs.

> I've noticed the same thing since I moved up to 4.0. I have also noticed
> that when the drives are being umounted during shutdown, the /usr
> partition remains busy and doesn't get umounted properly, which I'm
> assuming is why fcsk gets called on that drive every time.

> I've got a 1GB drive, and it's not a whole lot of fun waiting for the
> entire thing to get checked. It's a lot like waiting the minute it takes
> for sendmail to realize that I'm a private network.

I think you'll find the problem will go away once you copy
/usr/lib/libnss* to /lib. The problem is that bash loads
libnss_files.so (and possibly others) dynamically, so as long as bash
is running, /usr can't be unmounted.

This is _probably_ in the Red Hat Errata, but last the I tried to
access www.redhat.com, I couldn't access it, so I haven't seen the
errata yet.
