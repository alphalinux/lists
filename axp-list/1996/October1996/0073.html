Here's a copy of the Y-cable file from www.annex.co.uk. Since the A port has
the standard terminal pinout, if you only need one serial port to connect to
a modem, say, a simple 25-pin straight-through cable will work fine. Just be
sure the end that connects to the UDB is female, though. If you want to make
your own, get a couple of DB-25 to RJ45 (8 contact) connectors, insert the
pins in the hole numbers shown below, and connect them together with a
length of RJ45-terminated cable and go. Total cost should be less than $10.

Steve O'Neill

-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Universal Desktop Box
Serial Y Cable

All Universal Desktop Boxes are equipped with two 16550A compatible
serial interfaces with full modem control, unfortunately due to the
compact packaging of the UDB there is insufficient space available to
incorporate two serial port sockets on the rear of the system. The
Digital solution to this problem is to provide a single male DB25
socket with two sets of signals present.

The first Port A is presented on the standard RS-232 pins, the second
Port B is presented on pins that are normally unused by the RS-232
standard. A special Y Cable is available from Digital that splits
these signals onto two sockets with standard pinouts. The part number
for this cable is B25X-1F (17-04047-01 A01)

Should you wish to make your own cable, or need other connections the
pinout of the male DB25 socket on the UDB is as follows. Note that pin
7 GND is shared between Port A and Port B. Pins are counted right to
left with pin 1 top right as you look into the connector with pins
pointing out at you, pin 14 is below pin 1
Pin Signal

2 Port A TXD
3 Port A RXD
4 Port A RTS
5 Port A CTS
6 Port A DSR
7 Port A GND
8 Port A DCD
20 Port A DTR
22 Port A RI

7 Port B GND
11 Port B DTR
12 Port B DCD
13 Port B CTS
14 Port B TXD
16 Port B RXD
19 Port B RTS
23 Port B DSR
25 Port B RI
