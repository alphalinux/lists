>>> Andreas Kostyrka said:
>
> I'm having still my problems the serial port:
> - date eventually arrives at the modem and back again, but it takes
> very long :(

Are you perhaps using an *old* kernel, ie 1.3.90 or so? There was a problem
fixed after that, I believe, that could cause such symptoms.

> I examined the modem problem under Linux/AXP again, here are my findings:
> - changing the irq value didn't change the problem that much.
> (difficult to say now, subjectively it was then and now SLOW *grin*)

By slow, do you mean each character takes a long time, or does the input
or output seem to burst, say 10-16 characters at once, then a long delay?

> - cat-ing /proc/interrupts showed, that interrupts are arriving on IRQ
> 4, but rather slowly (minicom default init + quit strings -> 14
> interrupts :( )

The serial port DB-25 on the UDB is really *2* serial ports; if the cable
you hook to the modem passes more than the absolute minimum necessary lines,
the modem may be getting confused by the extra lines that are really COM2.

> - drivers/char/serial.c seems to contain alpha specific stuff, but
> that seems to be for non PCI boxes, right?

Yes, those patches are for JENSEN, whose serial ports are pre-historic, or
nearly so... :-(
