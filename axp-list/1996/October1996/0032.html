It's on azstarnet:

ftp://ftp.azstarnet.com/pub/linux/axp/sys/axp-diffs-2.0.21.gz

As always: if it ain't broke, don't fix it. Get those patches only if
you're sure you need them.

Just to avoid false rumors: no those patches don't change _any_
#define or structure declaration other than for bug fixing. The major
difference is in the way things get declared. Those changes are there
to make it easy to construct POSIX compliant libc's (such as
glibc---it's probably not 100% compliant, but probably as close as you
want it... ;-)

Also, note that 2.1 has the bigfile patches integrated already.
That's good news, but it means it may take me a few days to get
synched up with Linus's sources (due to the amount of changes).
