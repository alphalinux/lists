>What might be interesting is to take small (real small) programs, which
>run faster when compiled with DEC C than gcc, take a look at the assembler,
>and figure out why. Then perhaps we can improve gcc.

I haven't really looked at this yet, but my guess is that the
low-hanging fruit here is likely to involve changing gcc a lot
more than discovering how to generate efficient code for Alphas.
They're RISCs, they have Icaches and Dcaches, and given the current
technology in gcc and GNU ld, there's likely to be a lot of obvious
stuff that gcc needs to do -- "obvious" in the sense that we don't
need to discover what DEC cc does to determine it.

People wanting to know more about this might want to read a recent
300+-line post I just put on comp.lang.fortran. Then again, maybe
not. ;-)
