Bernd,

I'm interested. Couple of questions though.

1. I thought Durango was from Aspen, PC164 from Digital OEM. But the
Linux/AXP FAQ seems to suggest that they are the same. Are they ? Also, the
FAQ has a link to PC164 info which seems to be incorrect.

2. Could you give me some detailed info on these boards ? Number/type of
slots, memory sockets, any on-board SCSI, sound etc., connectors, ..

3. Specs on the cases ? Heat-sink or blower for the CPU included in the
assembly ?

4. Recently there was some discussion on this list about the newer NT
Firmware not being able to load MILO/Linux. Are you sure I can run both Win
NT 4.0 _and_ Linux on these boards ?

5. You mention the SRM-console. That's just the firmware needed for Digital
UNIX, right? I believe, when you buy a copy of DU, a CD with assorted
firmware, plus utilities to load it into the NVRAM, is included. So what
would I need to pay this $220 for ?

6. Prices include shipping & handling ? Any taxes involved ? Can you give a
price in
D-Mark or Guilders ?

Groeten,
Geerten.
