On Sun, 20 Oct 1996, Berndt Josef Wulf wrote:

> You can count me in too, #5, but this is not the way to do it. RedHat
> employs highly paid sale-managers and accountants which should have done
> there home work and not us ;)

We don't have anyone whose highly paid :-(

We have done our homework, and I know that there are 423 people on
axp-list. If every one of them bought Applix, we'd approach breaking
even on the port (and manuals, publicity, stocking charges, boxes,
CDs, etc).

We don't make much money on the Alpha port, we do it because it's fun
and challenging. Applixware is a product produced in a partnership with
Applix and is intended to make money. Too produce Applixware for a
platform is a large job (I never mentioned things like regression testing)
which needs a reasonably sized market. Everyone interested in Linux
should applaud making Applixware available for Intel machines (a market
which is substantially larger then the Alpha market) rather then beating
them up for not providing and Alpha port.
