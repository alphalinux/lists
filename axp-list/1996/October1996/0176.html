At 12:02 AM 10-05-96 -0600, you wrote:
>off topic, i know, but i got no documentation with my multia 166 and i was
>wondering a couple of things that someone out there might be able to help me
>with.
>
>ok, i want to get a 4x cdrom drive for it so i can FINALLY install rh linux
>3.03. the problem is, the cable from the scsi card has only one connector,
>and appears to be "stuck" to the scsi card, like soldered or something. can
>you get an adaptor for it or something, since i want to install the cdrom
>drive and a hard drive someday. also, (now i've got my multia cracked open
>on my lap, aren't they just the easiest little things to open?) on the
>motherboard between the hard drive and the floppy drive, there is a male
>43(?) pin connector type deal. what's that? scsi-ii? also (last item) for
>power to my future scsi devices, can i just adapt from the power cable off
>the scsi card, or what would someone reccomend?

SCSI devices are "daisy-chained" together, off of the one connector. You can
have a total of 7 devices plus the host adapter. SCSI ID of these devices
need to be set to be unique for each device, and the SCSI host adapter in
the system is at ID 7 by default. The last device in the chain needs the
SCSI termination enabled, the rest disabled. Power should come from the
outside enclosure you hold the drives in, and they also should provide their
own termination power. If you look at the jumper options for those drives
this will (proabably) be obvious to accomplish. Of course your dealer that
supplies the CD-ROM and external HD should be able to do all of this for you
and explain it to you.
