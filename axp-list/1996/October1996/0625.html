> So...then...what about the benchmarks that show OSF/1 being faster
> than Linux? I know that once we eliminate the LCA's and the slow hard
> drives and such then Linux ought to be faster. Every day I count on
> it being so.

I dont think that the OSF/1 kernel is faster than the Linux Kernel. I
benchmarked my software the other day, and it seemed to run slightly
faster under Linux. However, the variance in the results was more than the
average difference, and I am only seeing differences of about 5 seconds out
of a 600 second run. But my point is that at least for this software, which
is a fairly large IC CAD package, we are on par with, if not faster than
Digital Unix.

What is true is that the Digital Unix compiler, and especially the Digital
Unix libraries are much better optimized than their Linux counterparts
(I ran the benchmark above using a statically linked Digital Unix binary).
Hopefully this will be easier to fix than optimizing the kernel. I don't
think that anyone has done much with this though. David M-T may have,
but I think most of his libc work has involved porting glibc rather than
optimizing it.

Does anyone have a feel for how the optimization of glibc compares to the
old ECOFF libc? Is sqrt() still slow?
