At 05:15 hrs. 10-19-96 -0400, you wrote:
>Due to negligence at 10:18 AM 10/19/96 PDT, Matthew Jacob let this roam
>freely into my inbox:
>|>Yes, I got this with the the UDB 24MB memory. I reseated the RAM
>|>and things seemed to get slightly better. Then I replaced the RAM
>|>that came with the machine with 80MB or so of purchased RAM and the
>|>problem went away entirely.
>
>Advice noted, thanx :) Unfortunately, I can't quite seem to get the UDB
>open...unscrewed the two back screws, pulled the tray, and it gets stuck
>about 2" out. Is there something that I need to try to disconnect? Lambs to
>sacrifice?
>
You now have 2 choices:
1) You will see the power cables to the motherboard on the right ( as seen
frm the rear) you may disconnect them.
2) Better method: Pull tray out about 1". The undo 2 phillips screws on back
of the grey top cover. Slide cover back about one half inch, lift it off.
This requires disconnecting _NO_ cables. Much better, especially if you
forget which way the cables go in...

While doing this keep the UDB plugged in, but turned off. This will
substantially diminish the chances of doing static electricity damage to
components.
