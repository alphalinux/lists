Just a word of warning: a few of my friends and I all bought UDB's.
One of my friends attached a Courier modem to his UDB using a 25-pin
cable, and it absolutely freaked - somehow his NVRAM in the modem was
toasted, he had to totally reprogram it. I'm using an "AT modem" cable,
which only passes pins 1-8,20,22 and it works fine for me. I suspect
that the extra signals on serial port B were confusing the modem? So,
if you really only want to use one of the ports get the cable that only
passes the appropriate lines. Besides, these cables are usually a bit
thinner (and cheaper too!).
