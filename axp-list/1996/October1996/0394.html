On Sun, 13 Oct 1996, Eric Jones wrote:

> What baffles me is that I would have expected redhat to have tested this
> more extensively. I got this sucker on CD (a week late, at that) and I'm
> steadily becoming more and more dismayed with it.

I thought for a while to just forget it, but I can't get un-upset
about it. The RedHat crew in my opinion is doing wonders with the AXP
distribution, and they respond when people ask for help. Half a year
ago setting up and running Linux on Alpha was a black art and hardware
was exotic. Today, people are buying UDBs for $600 and getting RedHat
4.0 for $0--$99, and I hear complaints? Sheeesh!!

So people are disappointed that their 166MHz Alpha is slower than 75MHz
Pentium; sure, if someone paid $8000 for it (like I did 3 years ago), they
would have a point, but for $600? CDROM is 7 days late? Ouch, I guess
the world domination will have to wait.

Besides, it would be one thing if complaints arrived from Jay
Easterbrook, David Mossberger-Tang or Richard Henderson, Eric
Youngdale or David Rusling, who probably contribute 90% of the answers
on this list. As it is, I haven't seen them complain. Let's not
emulate a peanut gallery.

I would concede that the wizards on this list (to which group I
certainly do NOT belong) may collectively be guilty of creating a
somehow misleading appearance about AXP Linux. A novice could possibly
conclude that the AXP release is just as stable as Linux/Intel. As we
know this is not quite true yet. If you are after stability and ease
of installation, and don't want to spend time understanding and fixing
things, buy a Pentium!

I don't want to be apologetic for the bugs and problems---I hope (and,
based on past performance, expect) that the bugs will be
fixed. Perhaps even some amount of exposure might be helpful to
nudge things along :^). It is just the attitude that I mind.

Three cheers to the AXP wizards!
