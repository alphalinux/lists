Bernie,
in testing my new Milos yesterday, I tried pretty much what you're
doing on a PC164. I shoved in an NCR and Milo booted Linux, no problem.
Moving the interrupt to D from A doesn't make sense: both should work, the
timeout on SCSI test 1 means that you saw no interrupts. The interrupt
routing algorithm is fixed and well understood for PCI on the PC164 (and
different from the EB164). MILO _is_ the firmware and so a firmware
upgrade _should_ make no difference.
