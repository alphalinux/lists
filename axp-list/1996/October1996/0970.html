Just let me know when anyone is sick of reading my posts, but I'm attempting
to pinpoint the problem with these programs. I highly doubt it's a memory
problem, although maybe the dynamic libs are allocating more space. At any
rate, I gdb'd xquake and xpilot, both of which core dump. XPilot terminates
with signal 11 (seg fault) and gdb noted this:
#0 0x120001ddb4 in Paint_item ()

Can anyone explain what that is?

XPilot, like XQuake, dies immediatly upon boot. Both attempt to grab a window;
you can see it starting, but then they bomb.

Interestingly enough, Quake does not generate the core. according to gdb
XF86_TGA does. Quake may not be at fault here, but I have no other
explanation for the crashing, since the TGA server works fine with everything
else. Then again, it may be causing the XPilot crash as well.
