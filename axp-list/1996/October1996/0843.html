I suspect this is why most people are interested in Digital Unix compatable
shared libs. However, before we attempt to rewrite the DU libs, perhaps we
should investigate possibility of getting Digital to make licenses for
these libraries available at a reasonable cost. After all, the only thing
you are going to need the DU libs for is to run commercial software, so
I don't think the idea of paying for them is inherently unlinuxlike.

What I would really like to talk about though is Digitals plans for ELF,
and I must admit that these are really questions since I have no idea
what those plans are. Right now we need the DU libs because there are
DU applications that are not available for Linux. I suspect that in the
future there will be Linux applications that are not available for DU (Word
Perfect comes to mind as a potential example). If this happens I think it
would be benificial for both Linux and Digital Unix if their applications
were interchangable. I know that Jon Hall has talked about making a Linux
environment under Digital Unix, and that by itself would suit the needs of
Digital. However what I would really like to see when Digital moves to ELF
(is that planned), is that the libs be interchangable. The only advantage
I can see in this for Digital is that it might allow them to leverage
some of the work done by the Linux community. The disadvantages I can see
for Digital would be potential incompabilities with existing DU application
source code, and copyright problems. Could anyone from Digital comment
on any of this?

I would like to cc Jon Hall, but I have lost his email address :-( If
someone from Digital reads this, I would appreciate you bouncing him a
copy.
