In my relentless pursuit of making alpha a better Linux platform, I have now
instead of fixing the kernel been fixing "Quake". As you probably know, the
alpha version of quake was (a) ECOFF and (b) had broken sprite support(*) and
you couldn't save and load games reliably with it (well, you could save/load,
but quake would crash soon afterwards).

Anyway, thanks to Dave Taylor and the people at ID, I was able to get a
"quake source license" and try to fix the alpha bugs. And there it is:

ftp://ftp.fs.helsinki.fi/pub/Software/Linux/Kernel/testing
alpha-ELF-xquake.gz

(size 244942, md5sum 79c8b4545d77d6e5f34432aa0fe85c8b)

It's just a binary, and you do need a ELF install to actually make it work
(along with the quake world files etc, of course), because I couldn't be
bothered to make a statically linked ECOFF version (don't even ask for it:
consider this just another reason to upgrade to RedHat 4.0).

I suspect it might be a good idea to mirror the binary somewhere, after
having checked that it actually works. The ftp.cs.helsinki.fi site is just a
few PC's on an ethernet, so don't expect it to be incredibly fast.

I cannot give out sources, don't ask. I can't make ports to other things,
don't ask. But I _can_ fix problems, so if you notice that something doesn't
work, please to try tell me exactly where the problem is, and I can try to
reproduce it and make a fixed binary available.

Oh, and I haven't actually tested this exhaustively, so there might be silly
bugs galore, including new ones introduced by me. It WorksForMe(tm).

Linus

(*) Whatever "sprites" are. I don't have time to actually check what it all
does, I just fixed the bugs, and I never actually checked what the sprites
are _used_ for in the game.
