> Does it seem plausible to people to compile the computational kernels
> of their code (ya know, the 10% of the lines that make up 90% of the
> CPU time) on DEC Unix, generating assembler code, and then
> assembling and linking it under Linux?

I took a look at compiling the kernel with the DEC C compiler once. The
kernel is very gcc specific. You don't see it because most of it is hidden
in macros, but nothing that includes anything out of the asm directory
compiled.

What might be interesting is to take small (real small) programs, which
run faster when compiled with DEC C than gcc, take a look at the assembler,
and figure out why. Then perhaps we can improve gcc.
