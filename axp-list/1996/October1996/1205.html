SuperProbe will *not* work as-is on a Jensen, so don't bother trying.

> I have seen that the Dec4x5 is supported.
>
> But, to get to the point... The DE422 card is on IRQ 5, with a port
> number I don't know of. I've seen in many internet pages and other
> documents that those cards usually reside at 0x200 or 0x300, so I tried
> that too.

The DE422 is *NOT* a DE4x5; unless the DE422 has been jumpered differently
than seems to be standard issue, it ought to work (Linus' first Alpha was
a Jensen... :-).

> Oh? So it's out of the question for me to use that? It's awkward that I
> *can* use it with VMS or OSF/1. It didn't seem to have any problems at
> all. The graphical user interface seemed to be working like it should be
> working under Linux.

Not out of the question, but probably more work than you would like. The
Xservers under VMS and OSF/1 were written by DEC specifically for the
hardware of the QVision *and* the JENSEN. Unfortunately, the JENSEN hardware
is *not* like all our other boxes; it was the first, and some things were not
well thought out in the design, which make it difficult (to say the least)
to port the XFree86 code to the JENSEN. Ask Martin Ostermann,
<ost@comnets.rwth-aachen.de>, who has spent *many* hours getting an S3-based
server to run on the JENSEN (http://www.comnets.rwth-aachen.de/~ost/alpha/)

> Two years ago, the company I work for bought this DEC/Alpha. And now
> they've decided to use it. The card hasn't been swapped though; it's the
> one that Digital put in when they've bought it. I'm pretty amazed that
> it's almost not possible to find any specific info about this card on
> the net.

QVision was a COMPAQ proprietary VGA design; AFAIK no one else used the chips
to build controllers except COMPAQ. It's old technology now, never was very
fast to begin with; there's little-to-no COMPAQ VGA support in XFree86 at all,
as no one seemed (or seems) interested in doing/maintaining it.

The JENSEN must have been bought to run NT, VMS, or OSF/1, as Linux-ALpha
was not available 2 years ago. For running those, 2 years ago, JENSEN was
fine. But time moves on, and some things get left behind...
