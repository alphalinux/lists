I am running a cabriolet system with an NCR825 SCSI controller and 64 meg
of ram on the new RedHat 4.0. The kernel is compiled to support the
NCR-8XX (not the NCR-7,8xx) with the default configutation settings. I
have been unable to get the EXABYTE EXB-2501 tape drive to correctly
function with the system. If I try to verify a backup it gives me a
general SCSI I/O error. If I tell taper to retry it gives me a memory
protection error. Tar just gives me the protection error.

Is this a problem with the SCSI driver or a compatibility with the
EXAByte tape drive? We had similar problems when we were running WIN NT
on the Alphas. EXAByte informed us that we would have to upgrade the
Flash BIOS on the tape drives themselves. Has anyone else had any
experience with EXAByte drives and Alphas?
