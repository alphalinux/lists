At 08:00 hrs. 10-21-96 +0500, you wrote:
>
>>>> "Shane Anderson" said:
>>
>> How does SCSI termination work on the UDB? Does it somehow
>> auto-terminate or is there a jumper? I'm a bit concerned with this.
>> Also, can the SCSI ID of that wimpy little Toshiba (340MB) drive be
>> changed? How? Jumpers? Ugh!
>
>Typically, the internal 2.5-inch drive is not terminated; in fact, it may
>be "unterminatable"... :-) I don't know about the ones with 3.5-inch drives
>in them, but those can usually be terminated if one tries hard enough.
>
>The external port needs *GOOD* termination when a cable is attached, as
>the internal cables are 18-inches long, and are completely unterminated.
>This works because of that short length; when an external cable is attached,
>the extra length *will* cause problems without proper termination.
>
>--Jay++
As a matter of fact, I found that placing an in-line terminator right at the
port, then another at the far end of the SCSI bus made it much happier if
you hang 4 external devices on the UDB (like I did).
