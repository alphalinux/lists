I thought some people might be interested in some performance
comparisons between three different machines.

The machines are:-

a) DEC 3000-800 (200 Mhz 21064) running DEC UNIX
b) DEC Noname (266 Mhz 21066) running DEC UNIX
c) DEC UDB (266 Mhz 21066) running Linux
d) Intel Box (180 Mhz Pentium) running Linux

Two 'real' applications were tested. Our business is Computational Fluid
Dynamics, and the codes are numerically intensive applications that use
floating point applications extensively.

The two codes are essentially the same, one written in C, the other in
Fortran.

On the DEC UNIX machines DEC C and and DEC Fortran are used, on Linux
machines gcc and f2c are used.

The times given below are execution times, so the smaller the number the
faster the machine.

C Results

DEC 3000-800 7.8 secs
DEC Noname 8 secs
DEC UDB 34 secs
Intel 9.7 secs

Fortran Results

DEC 3000-800 11.5 secs
DEC Noname 17 secs
DEC UDB 73 secs
Intel 22.7 secs

Our conclusion has been that Linux/Alpha is not a realistic choice at
the moment, and because DEC UNIX licenses are so expensive we are better
off using Intel Pentiums with Linux.

Any feedback welcome.
