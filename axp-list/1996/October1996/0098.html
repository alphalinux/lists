The problem you are seeeing is from the fact that the UDB spits out it's
starting messages on the serial port, and assumes there is a terminal
atatched there. The solutions to this that I am aware of are 2:
1) Don't turn on the modem until the system is running,
2) Or in your startup files put a complete set of code to set the modem and
write to the NVRAM the desired settings.
The above mentioned cabling should be fine, as you only need about 6 lines
to do a proper interface to the modem inclusing RTS/CTS hardware flow control...
