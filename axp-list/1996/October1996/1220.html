>>> Deryk Barker said:
>
> Questions:
>
> Is it easier to upgrade 3.0.3->4.0 or to do a complete
> install? (Fortunately there is nothing of any great worth on
> the system yet so a complete reinstall is not a great problem)

Do an install, *after* saving any important control files, just to make
sure. The 4.0 stuff is ELF and *much* smaller, so it's really to your
advantage to get rid of as much of the ECOFF stuff as possible.

> Do I need to pick up those axp-diffs? (Still haven't quite
> figured out what they are)

They'll be part of the sources installed, so until you want to build
a later kernel than 2.0.18, you're all set.

> linload.exe - I need that too right?

Always best to make sure you have the latest one of these, to go along
with the latest MILO.

> How about MILO? Is the version on the CD OK fo loading ELF
> kernel? (I know it should be....)

You will definitely need a new MILO for loading the ELF kernel; if there's
no MILOs on the CD, look on gatekeeper.dec.com in:

pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Miniloader/test-images/

>
> Anything else I've forgotten?

Crossing of ones fingers can never hurt... :-)
