>At 02:16 PM 10/6/96 -0400, you wrote:
>>Hello:
>>
>>In the last month I have been completely incapable, for several reasons, to
>>compile a working kernel for my stock UDB 166 Noname 330mg HD 24mg Ram...
>>currently running RedHat 3.03 kernel 1.3.90
>
>I too was unable to get a compile of the out-of-the-box Redhat kernel to
>compile correctly. However, I did donwload the lastest 2.0.20 kernel, the
>entire tree, and build without problems. Make sure you backup your
>/usr/src/linux tree and replace it with the tree in the tar file. I've had
>some <include> problems referencing back to the original tree.
>
>Follow the kernel instructions. Edit the top of the makefile to include
>ARCH = alpha. You may have to delete the line and retype the line. An
>extra space seems to kill make.
>
>Make clean; make dep; make vmlinux. You should be good to go.

Thanks for the tips. I am going to continue to try to compile the new kernel
but I have other problems as well... I think I may have a bad simm and some
corrupted or missing /usr/bin files also contributing to the problem.

So, it is necessary for me to source a pre-compiled kernel for the time being.

>HOWEVER, I have been unable to compress the kernel. Everything works OK
>booting with "boot sda1:vmlinux root=/dev/sda1" but using gzip and booting
with
>the corresponding "boot sda1:vmlinux.gz ..." fails.

Have you tried:

make config; make dep; make clean; make boot
