Well, I actually did it kind of backwards. I set the card to
match the default driver settings of IRQ 14, ADDR 0x240.

> > Linux just doesn't seem to know that either the driver or the card
> > exists. I'd think I should at least get an error message if the driver
> > initialization was crapping out on boot up.

I made an interesting discovery. I tried compiling w/ the
software only option and everything worked fine. The driver reports its
existance between the ps2 mouse initialization and the ramdisk
initialization. Writing to /dev/watchdog gives no error messages, and
typing "cat > /dev/watchdog" and waiting a minute reboots the machine.

> So, did you try to configure it as a MODULE?

No, I tried to compile it into the kernel.

have something to do with the driver not getting linked in for some

Maybe its some kind of Makefile or config problem.
