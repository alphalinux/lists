On Tue, 12 Nov 1996, David Wild wrote:
> Hello,
>
> Would anyone care to comment on Red Hat vs Craftworks Linux
> running on an DEC UDB (166MHz)?

Well, my latest craftworks Linux has yet to come for my UDB, But i do have
the first version on my UDB, and redhat 4.0 on my dads. The Craftworks
linux installs very easily, and the x installer is follproof for the UDB.
RH Linux has more updates sooner, but the new version of craftworks
supports rpm, so that work may nullify that.

The one thing I do like is shadowed passwords works very well, and comes
"built in" to craftworks. Redhat comes with a lot more packages, and
seems to have more free support, at least from the lists.

It seems 6 one way 1/2 dozen the other. I have yet to see which performs
better, until I get the new craftorks.
