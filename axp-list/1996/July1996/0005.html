7. Last but quite important. DEC semiconductor in Hong Kong, who is
tying to support us, told us that older 21164 and 21164NT were
actually the same chip and for our own use just use the NT chip
for UNIX. If we get the PC164 and the NT version of the 21164A
will we be able to run UNIX? I know that's possibly sensitive but
the person we get the info from in Hong Kong will be away for a
while nad we are trying to make some purchasing decisions.

I understand that for marketing reasons DEC eventually will produce
two versions of the CPU: NT/Linux version (which won't run Unix/VMS,
but will be somehow cheaper) and a full-featured version. The
differences have to do with ability to map full 64 bits of virtual
address, or some such.

I understand that at present all chips are full-featured. Still, up to
300MHz all chips are sold in both flavors; at the same time 333 MHz
chips are only available in the more expensive Unix/VMS flavor; that
is why 333MHz machines cost almost $4000 more that a 300MHz NT/Linux
one (which, again, at present is most likely capable of running
Unix/VMS as well).
