There is a bug concerning Gnu malloc/free in libc. Sorry, I haven't
fixed it, I can only offer a workaround. I believe its due to the
fact, that malloc and malloc-930716 havn't been equally ported to 64
bit architectures. They use different data types at some places,
e.g. size_t vs int. The makefiles mix the two versions, malloc/cfree.c
and anything else from malloc-930716. FYI: malloc does statistics,
malloc-930716 not, thus the later is supposed to be slightly
faster.

The bug appears for example in tar. Multi volume tar extracts (tar xMf
/dev/fd0) will loop endlessly in free(), called by assign_string().

Using just the new stuff in malloc, it disappears.

Like I already wrote in my previous mail, memcpy() uses an unoptimized
version. ./sysdeps/alpha/Makefile and ./string/Makefile need to be
fixed.
