Disclaimer: I don't want to sound vehement or anything -- I consider this a
very minor issue. Don't take my arguments too personally. That said:

On Sat, 20 Jul 1996, Bryan W. Headley wrote:
> Especially when you have glint/rpm which stores platform information, and
> could be easily enhanced to install only the "right" binaries.

Again, this doesn't always solve the case of big directory trees
network-mounted on multiple architectures.

Yes, you can use various bin directories and munge the path. However, I've
been on AFS-capable systems for about 10 years now, on which my home direcotry
has followed me from a Sun2 to a Sun3, IBM-RT, Vax, PMax, SunOS box and
Solaris box, so I'm speaking with some experience, and my experience tells me:

Trying to manage the different bin directories with envrionment variables ends
up introducing subtle problems that smack naive users in the nose repeatedly.

There are multiple solutions.

The solution AFS uses is an environment-variable-like mechanism in the
*kernel*. The AFS driver internally converts @sys into the local CPU/OS
version wherever it's the traling part of a path element (eg. .@sys becomes
.sun4c_413).

Another solution is fat binaries. For people who don't like them, you can
provide a utility to strip out whatever's non-native on your machine.
