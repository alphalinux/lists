> Building 32-bit binaries on a 64-bit machine should be no problem
> (haven't tried it myself though). The other way round is calling for
> trouble. Last time I looked into it, the binutils, gdb, nor gcc
> handled going from a smaller to a larger target well. Jim Paradis at
> one point had patches to build Alpha binaries on an x86, but I don't
> think they were ever integrated into the main-sources.

I think you'll find its much better now -- at least with gcc.

Binutils makes sure to use long long on 32-bit hosts when building
a 64-bit BFD.

As for gcc, there are a number of optimizations that are not done on
32-bit hosts (so you get pretty pathetic code), but it does work.
At tege's request, I tested the effects of one of my alpha changes
here on an m68k-apple-aux machine and things seemed to work ok.
