On Mon, 22 Jul 1996, Richard Henderson wrote:
>
> Benjamin C. Goodwin wrote:
> > The elfball doesn't have crypt in it - so I need to build up my own
> > glibc.. I'm getting a bit confused as to where to get what I need to
> > build it.
>
> Crypt is a problem. Roland is trying to figure out what to do about
> that now. He'd had a good plan with an implicitly loaded libcrypt.so,
> but it turns out that it is illegal to export software that even makes
> it easy to drop in an encryption module of foreign origin.

Umm.. The canonical way to solve this is just to admit that "crypt()"
isn't cryptography despite the name, it's a one-way-hash, and as such
doesn't fall even under the braindead US export laws.

Linux/x86 isn't the only case of such an interpretation - I think all
commercial unixes see it this way too these days (they _used_ to have a
special "international" edition 5 years ago, but I think they are sane
now).
