>I tried plugging it into the UDB and booting. I got a couple of test
>patterns at first, and then I seemed to get the colorful snow that one gets
>when one has a messed up XFree86 configuration file. I'm guessing that the
>UDB is immediately trying to go to more than 640x480 resolution.

Most likely; they're normally configured to 1024x768@ 72 HZ, some even
as high as 1280x1024 @ 70Hz... :-(

However, it only stays in the higher resolution briefly, UNLESS the console
is configured to stay in SRM console mode, and NOT go to ARC mode. ARC mode
ignores the jumper settings and does 640x480 @ 60Hz or some such, so it should
work on most monitors. If your UDB does NOT finally go to a good video mode,
I hope the brief fling at a higher resolution hasn't damaged the monitor... :-(

>Is this what's going on, or is it more likely that the monitor is faulty?
>This monitor appears to work just fine with an Intel NT box at 640x480x256.

No, I think you've surmised correctly; hopefully it *still* works correctly.

>If the UDB is trying something outside this monitor's specs, is there a way to
>ask it not to? Perhaps a jumper on the motherboard?

Yes, there *are* jumpers on the MB that you can set to tell the console FW
what resolution you wish to use. I'll try to walk you through finding and
setting them, but you should feel comfortable opening boxes up and groping
around before you start... :-)

Note that I'm doing this with a 166 UDB as the model, but I think the jumpers
are located the same on either version of the MB.

0. Orient the box in your lap or on a table with the front to your left and
back to your right.

1. Open the box (unscrew the knurled knobs on the back) and slowly pull out
the MB/drawer. You'll have to remove a couple of wire connectors for the
power soon after you start pulling it open. They are at the top right of
the MB. Pull up on them to disconnect. REMEMBER THE LOCATIONS, but they
are keyed if you forget.

2. Look at the bottom
of the MB, just to the right of center; there's a block of 8 VRAM memory
chips for the graphics. Look above them and try to find a block of four
(4) jumpers, arranged in a box shape.They are usually set in a
particular way (see below) and are the only set of four (that I can
remember) in a box shape. They should be easily accessible, ie not
under anything else, UNLESS
you have an internal 3.5" SCSI drive in there, and then you'll have to
remove it to get access to the MB and the jumpers.
