No. (glibc-1.92-axp-elf.tar.gz is a prebuilt archive I think).

You do exactly as David's readme says. Get the newest libc-*-alpha-diff.gz
first, like libc-960717-alpha-diff.gz.

Then get (*) newest glibc archive that exists, like libc-960714.tar.gz of
today, and all patches needed to get to the same date as Davids -alpha-diff
patch, ie libc-960714-960715.diff.gz, upto libc-960716-960617.diff.gz.
Apply all the libc-9607* patches first to bring the libc sources upto date
with davids diff. Then apply davids diff. Then fetch the newest crypt
archive (glibc-1.09-crypt.tar.gz as of today), read the README-crypt for
details, and untar it in the libc source tree.

To be on the sure side look for rejects ( find . -name "*.rej"). Erase
all .orig files ( rm -rf `find . -name "*.rej"`), and all empty files
(rm -rf `find . -empty`). You are now prepared to compile, which is a matter
of ./configure , make >log 2>&1 (makes you a nice little log of what
happened during the build), make tests (tries if your newly compiled libc
works ok), make install. Voila!

But like I said, any more questions concerning glibc/elf should be posted
on the mailling list I said at the top of this message.

(*) The libc source/patches are _pre-releases_ and are not for public
ftp. To find the 'secret' ftpsite/path you can mail and ask David MT
(hope you don't mind David ;) for example.

Good luck!
