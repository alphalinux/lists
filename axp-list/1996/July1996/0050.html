> My EB164 has a no name brand NCR810 based scsi controller sitting in the
> last PCI slot away from the cpu. It works fine and the system has NT3.51,
> Nt4.0, Linux, and OSF3.2. When I move the card to any other slot all 4 OSs
> hang when loading. Both the ARC and the SRM firmware work fine, see all the
> SCSI devices and can load and run all the OS loaders from disk. In the case
> of NT3.51 and Linux, both say they are using int 16 on the SCSI bus and give
> messages about SCSI timeouts.

OSF3.2 may be hanging because the kernel expects the controller in a specific
slot; try booting the "genvmunix" kernel, and if it works, you'll need to
reconfigure and build a new kernel.

I don't know about NT or VMS, I thought they were more flexible, somehow... :-(

> I thought int 16 was assigned to the last slot (the one which works) on the
> EB164 motherboard.
>
> Any ideas? Could it just be the card (and/or its bios?)

Yes, IRQ 16 seems to be the one assigned to INTA from the last slot.

It almost seems as if that ncr810 card forces the use of IRQ 16. If so, and
I don't know how this could be, then it could certainly cause problems.

Any chance you'd booted an ARC-based kernel from SRM or vice versa? I've seen
some weird behaviour along these lines when I inadvertently did that... :-(
