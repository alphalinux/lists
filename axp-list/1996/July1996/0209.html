Harald Koenig writes:
>> Crypt is a problem. Roland is trying to figure out what to do about
>> that now. He'd had a good plan with an implicitly loaded libcrypt.so,
>> but it turns out that it is illegal to export software that even makes
>> it easy to drop in an encryption module of foreign origin.
>
>what ??? does this mean that even PAM (pluggable authentication
>modules; http://www.redhat.com/pam if you are curious) isn't allowed ???

No.

First of all, as Linus pointed out, crypt() is a one-way hash function
which is not covered by the insane US ITAR regulations.

Secondly, PAM isn't an interface to encryption. It's an interface to
authentication -- whether those have any encryption in them has nothing
to do with the authentication.

Thirdly, these laws *are* interpreted on an "intent" basis -- if you
name your hook functions "triple_des_encrypt()" and "triple_des_decrypt()"
you might get into trouble, where "random_data_munge()" and
"random_data_unmunge()" might not get you into any trouble at all.

Lastly, there is very little case law defining these rules. Most of
what exists is a set of very inconsistent rulings from the government
agencies controlling export (yes, plural -- there are two of them...).

What it comes down to is that when you ask for permission to export
software, if it doesn't do strong encryption, you just need to avoid
using the magic words or phrases that set off alarms. If they are
alarmed, they will take the route of "better safe than sorry". So
crypt() is described as "a one-way message digest algorithm which
is substantially weaker than the already-export-approved MD4 and MD5
algorithms".

>can it be true that even call interfaces can be a "weapons" ? sigh :-(

Unfortunately, yes. Fortunately, it's not quite as insidious as it
looks at first glance. Almost, but not quite...

Of course, the standard disclaimer applies to this whole message: I'm
not a lawyer, so don't construe this as legal advice. If you want
legal advice, ask a lawyer... (It's really pathetic that we live in
such a litigous society that I need to say that. And what's even more
pathetic is that I can't, in all honesty, blame it all on the lawyers...)
