First of all:
What do you base this belief on???
The boards right now are IDENTICAL, except for the PROMs (which may be
interchanged) The simple point here is that some people are confusing the
fact that the board with a DEC UNIX license costs a lot more than the NT
version with the idea that the board or CPU must be somehow magically better
on the UNIX version. The fact is DEC UNIX is not cheap, especially on a
higher performance Alpha. Yes, I realise it's the same UNIX, from the same
CD installation media, however, it's not the software, it's the license that
has a variable cost. The same license on a lower performance Alpha is MUCH
cheaper. One other thing, licenses are NOT transferable.
Secondly: I suggest you speak to someone from the DEC TOEM channel. The guys
from the other channels are not well informed on this, as those divisions do
not sell motherboards and CPU's. The only channel for these is the TOEM or
embedded products group. Even the various DEC "distributors" actually buy
their boards from the embedded products group, and technically speaking
operate under the rule that they are not supposed to sell raw boards, only
integrated complete systems.

Maurice Hilarius (maurice@ellpspace.math.ualberta.ca) of Hard Data :
I simply want to make sure we clear the air here, for once and for all. The
"old" 21066A chips, Jensens, and "no-Name" Alphas, while interesting , are
not very fast by modern standards. There is an amount of clearance stock
being blown out by Digital right now on some of this old stuff, but, quite
frankly, if people are looking for modern Alpha performance, then the only
real choices are the current Alpha PCI-64 (21064A/275 with 2MB cache) or
Alpha PCI-164 (21164/266 or 300, 2MB cache) boards. The rest are simply
obsolete relics by todays standards. To go to great lengths to get Linux
running on a UDB 166 is silly, as a Pentium 166 offers the same performance,
at a much smaller price in terms of compatibility.
One last note: how cheap are the old 21066A's?? Well, we are selling the
Digital "Easy Web Server" which is a 21066A/166 in a UDB(small enclosure)
with 32MB RAM, 1GB SCSI, Ethernet, 2MB TGA video, NT, Netscape Server, and
all the various accessories for the grand sum of $ 1350 (U.S. Dollars) right
now, while supplies last. keyboard, mouse and monitor are additional. With a
Logitech Mouseman, Fujitsu keyboard, and a Wyse 15" monitor, this will set
you back $1875 (U.S. Dollars). Yes, it's cheap, but be warned, it's just not
exceptionally fast. If an experimental machine is what you want , fine! But
if it's performance you seek, look to the newer stuff.
