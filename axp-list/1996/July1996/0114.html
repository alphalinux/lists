Pardon me excessively if I sound slightly commercial:
UDB's can be purchased from authorized DEC dealers and TOEM VARs, and is
available (usually) in two main variants:
166MHz and 233MHz with internal disk ranging from 340MB to 1GB, and internal
RAM normally in 24MB or 32MB configurations, although there is room for more.

A "typical" UDB as one vendor ( ourselves, Hard Data Ltd.) sells is a 166
with 1GB and 32MB, NT ( Yes it runs Linux great!) and runs monitors from VGA
640x480 up to XGA 1280x1024. One note: internal jumpers have to be set to
lower video mode for VGA only monitors with low refresh rate capability.

UDB is a small ( about 12"x12"x2" ) box with everything internal on
mainboard, CPU, RAM, 2MB TGA video, ethernet, SCSI and comes with OS, but no
keyboard, monitor, or mouse. We normally sell it with a Keytronics 101
keyboard, 15" monitor, and a LogiTech Mouseman Combo 3 button (great for X)
for $ 1850 ( U.S.Dollars ) or
$ 2500 ( Canadian $ ).
Warranty is one year on site, and 3 year return to depot.

This is a "typical" configuration, others are available.
