> Yes? is this a true, that the scsi controller is on the motherboard?
> I've the 1G HDD version, and there is a little PCI expansion card with a
> ncr810 on it. What's that?

That's the only way the UDB comes; the NCR810 is on a small "riser" card,
as you have observed, it's not physically on the MB.

> I just want to reinstall the Multia base SW for some reason. I followed the
> instruction in the manual, and switched to the DEC OSF console at the ARC
> console.
> I powered off and on the machine, but the OSF console didn't appear. The
> ARC console appeared instead of.

Very strange. Did you cycle power at the point where the ARC console said to,
*after* selecting the "Digital UNIX" menu item, and then confirming the choice?
*ALL* UDB's come with an SRM console, which typically loads ARC after it does
some POST diagnostics and all...

> How could i repair my ARC firmware. I read something about failsafe booting
> from floppy. Is this possible to recover a correct firmware from a floppy?

There is a self-installing firmware update utility, but I don't know if the
latest version is available on the net; I'll check and let you know...
