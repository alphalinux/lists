On Thu, Aug 09, 2001 at 01:49:28PM -0500, Aaron Brueck wrote:
>
> I'm booting off a 9G SCSI HDD on the Qlogic controller.

Should be fine, unless there's a mag tape on there as well; that is
known to be somewhat problematic, to say the least, but normally
causes problems during boot (ie you never get far enough to install).

BTW, is the CDROM a SCSI, and on the same bus as the HDD? I've had
some problems with that config on some of the older machines (EV5 or
EV56) with a QLogic SCSI built in (I saw it on an AS500).

If so, is there any chance you could move the CDROM to a separate
controller, something like NCR 810, or perhaps even another QLogic, ie
get it off the same bus as the HDD.

This trick worked well for me, but it is frustrating. I think there's
something not quite right in the standard QLogic driver WRT
negotiation or termination when an old 50-pin CDROM is hung on the
same cable as a 68-pin HDD. And yes, I know it works fine with a 2.2
kernel, and under Tru64... :-\

> Onboard VGA is disabled and added a Matrox Millenium II.

Should also be fine, and much better for graphics.

> Replaced the DEC NIC with a Netgear FA310TX to get full duplex.

OK.

> Added a Mylex DAC960 raid controller

Firmware rev >= 3.0, I hope.

> and a ESS Solo-1 PCI sound card.

Dunno about this one.

The "lspci" looks fine.
