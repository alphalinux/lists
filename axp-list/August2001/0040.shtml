Aaron Gee wrote:

> Having no experience with PCI ATA controller cards what is the "lists
> opinion" on the best/fastest ata100 controller for a PC164 or PC164LX
> machine? I'm afraid that SRM won't recognize the card so haven't gotten
> one. (I have a PC164 BTW)

This is true, I neglected to mention that ATA100 cards will not be spotted
by SRM (well, certainly by none of the ones I've used) and generally it's
been a case of boot off either a scsi or internal IDE disk before letting
the kernel bite down on the ATA100 attached disks. This still doesn't
detract from the original IDE disk being used on the board supplied cypress
IDE. It's meerly a question of 'Humm, do I need gob loads of fast disk'
It's a question you'll have to balance up with your machine chassis's drive
bay facilities, v's cost of disk/controller and actual computing
requirements. In the main, the internal slots will not push much past
7.5Mb/s however thats not terrible performance as pretty much most of what
goes on in the linux box tends to occur in memory.
