>After serveral days we found that the APM and Fansettings in the BIOS
>made very huge differences in the performance. It seemed the box
>started to sleep if nobody was typing, this slowed the Fan down and
>than somthing braked the cpu to 1/10 th of its speed.
>
>Now after fixin the settings it is working ok, but the benchmarks
>still show a variance of 5% . We have no idea why.

I'm strolling through the BIOS now. Do you know what changes in setting
you made, and what you set them to?

>I read the P4 has a thermal sensor in the cpu that can slow it down
>if it gets to hot. Unfortunately there seems to be no was to find out
>when this is taking place. So it's easy, to make complete correct
>benchmark runs were the P4 is solwer then a P3/500

I think that's called the "Speed Step" technology, where high speed FP
operations causes the chip temperature to rise, and at some point, the
CPU needs to slow down so it does not burn itself up.

Also, we found programs compiled under GCC to be pretty slow on the
system, and were told that we need to use Intel's compilers that made
use of the SSE1/2 instruction optimization to achieve full performance.
Unfortunately, the compilers are only in beta phase, and do not seem to
work on the Linux 2.4.2-2 that we're using right now.
