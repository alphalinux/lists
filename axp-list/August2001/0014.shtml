On Sat, 04 Aug 2001 10:31:50 -0600, Maurice Hilarius <maurice@harddata.com>
wrote:
> With regards to your message at 10:11 AM 8/4/01, Helge Kreutzmann. Where
> you stated:
> >
> >We have the fan of the power supply and the fan on the CPU. Currently
> >the case is open to (hopefully) reduce heat congestion inside the box.
> >
> >Do you think it is sensible to add some additional fans ?
> Absolutely!

I'll second that!

> The fan in the power supply is definitely not enough to cool this type
> of machine.
>
> I strongly recommend adding another fan, preferably at the back pulling
> air out of the case but one in the front pushing air can be OK too, as
> long as the airflow path goes over the components.

That may also not be enough. I've found on my Alpha and X86 boxen just
adding a fan in the lower front of the case is needed to pull cool air
from the outside and push it back into the case and then the fan in the
back pulls it out the rest of the way.

On thing to try if you have a chip that needs cooling is to take a
little 486 CPU fan and arrange it to blow air over the item in question.
Depending on where this is in your case, you may have to get tricky with
twisty ties. I have that setup on my PC64 because the cache RAM got
pretty warm after several hours of use. Just by aiming the fan to blow
over the chips, they remained cool to the touch. If that helps, you
might want to look into a permanent solution. For the people that
overclock items in their systems, several companies sell heatsink and
fan combos that are used for video card cooling. Some of those are
small enough and are sold to be used on a motherboard chip.
