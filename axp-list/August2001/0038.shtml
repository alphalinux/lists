Peter,

When Phil said "limited to UDMA2," he meant in speed - not that it
wouldn't work. An ATA33, ATA66, and ATA100
would probably all work, but your speed will be limited to the speed of
the ide controller or driver - whichever is slower.
But it should work fine, so if speed isn't an issue then there's no
problem.

Although I would agree with Phil and go with ATA100 since it's so cheap
and you'll get many more years of use out of
it.

Terry
