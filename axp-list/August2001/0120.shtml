Alan W Marley/AWM/CC01/INEEL/US wrote:
> Hello all;
> I was wondering if anyone new of a clever way to extend the swap partition
> on a PWS500, (RedHat 6.2). I have a secondary disk drive for copying etc.
> I have increased the RAM to 512MB and I am stuck with a 72MB swap file.
> I have 27 of these systems to reconfigure, and would like to solicit the
> expertise of those list members for the quickest and easiest method of
> extending the 72MB swap file to approx. 512MB (maybe).
> Thanks for any responses;
> -Alan

Why not just add another swap parition???

- Use fdisk to partition
- mkswap to initialize it
- addswap to add it
- modify /etc/fstab so it is added on subsequent boots

You can have more than one swap partition. You can also use filespace
in a filesystem as well (not ideal, but an option).
