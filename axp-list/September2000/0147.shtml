I got into alpha powered machines 5 years ago. Then it was a hobby, and
still is to some degree. Loved using MY alphas at school to do some
serious numeric analysis on various problems (School had SLOW SLOW suns).
I now have 5 separate (but older) alphas. Only one of those is still used
in a "production" environment. I play with the rest. (Enough background)

It is very nice to see someone from Compaq/DEC ask some questions and try
and go to bat for us (the alpha linux community). Thank you, from this
alpha user.

Hopefully with some pushing the prices will come down and hobbiest like
myself can pick up some newer equipment. Heck maybe even another
production server or 2.

For my work the price performance break is between 2500 and 3000 USD for
the our small server (Currently AMD athlon Thunderbird 950 MHZ with 1
gig of memory ATA 100 IDE drives RAID) About 5000 USD for our big servers
(Dual PIII with 2 gigs of memory and the biggest fastest SCSI drives we
can find). It's getting closer now for us to be able to justify another
alpha. Just not there yet. We have to overcome peoples fear and
unfamiliarity with the product sometimes also :(

Thanks again for asking Pete.
