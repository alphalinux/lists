> CPU dont matter, as well as gcc. all denorm FP numbers are
> trapped to the kernel,
> as the FPU ( even up to a 21264 ) cant process them :-/

gcc or ccc matters a whole lot. In order to do denorms, you don't just set a
bit in a register, you have to ask the compiler (-mieee for gcc, -fp3 for
ccc) to insert extra trap instructions.

The standard kernel has the appropriate handler routines.

I've used this and it works. gcc's ability to *print* denorms is kind of
interesting, though.
