W Bauske wrote:

> The front side bus is but the memory is standard PC.
> Take a look at streams performance between a 21264 Alpha and an Athlon.
> Athlons are a factor of two or more slower. Even though several Alphas
> use PC memory, they still blow away PIII and Athlon in memory bandwidth
> because their chipset interleaves the DIMM's.

It is pretty much a factor of 2, no more. I can match speed with a 500mhz. 21264 no problem
with a 900 mhz. Thunderbird, specifically using Lightwave. As that is almost 100% of my usage
(3D apps, and Lightwave is almost the only app I can compare similar code on both hardware
platforms. That and SoftImage)

One point that will get the shit flamed out of me for saying, and did at one point, is that
Lightwave was poorly optimized for the Alpha platform. (actually not at all). A lot of people
told me I was full of shit for saying that, even though they were admittedly using VC4 as their
compiler. Also I noticed the difference in performance between the Intel and Alpha platforms
were much wider on renders in SoftImage than they were between platforms on Lightwave.

Also, you can interleave the Dimms on the Athlon as well.

> > Please note that I said Classic Athlon. That has 512k off die cache, running at less than
> > full cpu speed. The same test on the latest Thunderbird processors at 900 mhz. comes out
> > to around 250-260 seconds. A full 40-60 seconds faster than the 21264/500.
> >
>
> Exactly what I said. Your vendor is screwing you in terms
> of Alpha performance. I have 900 Mhz t-birds and they
> are no match for my Alphas on all codes I run. Note that
> is ALL codes. In no instance are Athlons faster than my
> 21264's.

You are still missing the point. You seem to think I am Alpha bashing or something which is far
from accurate. However, the Alpha is in a no win situation. Sure 64 bit is better, and I do not
for a second argue that your points are valid or not. The bottom line is that no matter how
good the Alpha is, I can easily outperform it by simply adding another machine.

The cost factor alone keeps Alpha from being a viable solution, that and lack of software since
my only choices are Linux, Tru64 and VMS.
