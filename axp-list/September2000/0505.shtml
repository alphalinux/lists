On Mon, Sep 25, 2000 at 12:32:25PM -0500, T. Martin wrote:
>

> I have a noname AXPpci33 233 mhz board now its slower than watching grass
> grow.
> The Multia us a noname board with extras right? Is there any performance
> to be gained from going to a Multia, as it is now the AXPpci33 takes over
> 50 hours to do a seti unit (either bin Linux or T64).
> I have 3 DECstations 150mhz (NetBSD) they do a unit in 40 hours.
> So is it worth going to a Multia over the noname?

Nope, not even if it's one of the 233 MHz units; the CPU in yours is
exactly the same as the one in the 233 Multia, ie LCA45. Oh, the
Multia would probably have more cache than the Noname, IIRC, but
unless the app fits, it's not going to help much.

Any EV45 box at 233 would be a good bit faster, like AS200 or AS400 or
Cabrio, and any EV5 or above will smoke it...
