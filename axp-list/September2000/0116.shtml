Alvin Starr wrote:

> > > unfortunately the Athlon is shipping in 1.1 ghz. and AMD is releasing no
> > > less than 5 new iterations of the Athlon processor over the next 5 months,
> > > ranging in speeds from 1.5ghz. up to 2 ghz.

> So Steve are you are you all toasty warm from the flames :)

Doesn't bother me a single bit. I don't make computers my religion, just my job.

> To clarify a point that nobody has made directly. Clock speed is not the
> same as instructions per second. I have not looked at the Athlon so I
> cannot comment on specifics and I have not looked a the insturction timing
> in the Intel products for a while but I think my general points still
> will hold true.

To simplify this, my point was that Alpha no longer had an edge in the production
market. The exceedingly high cost of even the entry level machine compared to the
cost of alternatives do not make good financial sense or practicality.

True the Alpha is faster, but if I can combine in a renderfarm 10 machines for the
cost of a single Alpha, and the results are say 5 times faster than the single
Alpha, then what is the point?

Just FYI here, on the entry level 21264, a single frame render of Lightwave is
around 300 seconds, vs. the older "Classic" Athlon 1ghz. also taking about 300
seconds for the same scene. This is how I derived 5x the speed since I can build
approx. 10 machines for the cost of a single Alpha. However, since the newere
systems do not run NT, I cannot really compare as there is no Linux version of
Lightwave to test on the 667's and up.

regardless of how one wants to try to justify numbers, the bottom line is meeting
a deadline. PERIOD. If 10 Athlons get the job done substantially faster than a
single Athlon for the same price, the financial decision is pretty easy to make.
Being good on paper is great but doesn't mean a lot in the real world.

I don't do databases, spreadsheets, servers etc. Major 3D app vendors don't show
support for Alpha linux.

As for how this started, with someone claiming "Bragging rights return"
well....... that was pretty silly now wasn't it? since the Alpha was already
faster than the current x86 offerings, why make such a foolish statement?
