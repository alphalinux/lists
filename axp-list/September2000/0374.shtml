Hi,

We have a few DeskStation Ruffian (RXP164-2) systems. They have been used
for "production" runs very reliably, so we haven't had any reasons so far
to upgrade any of them from their 2.0.30 kernels.

Recently, one of them died from a (system) disk crash. I've purchased an
identical replacement HD. But we're facing the question what best to do
with this system, and so I'd like to poll people on their experiences and
recommendations whether they'd:

1. Rebuild it the way it was, i.e. "clone" it back from one of the other
systems, to run with the 2.0.30 kernel again? (Any recommendations as
to what would be the best/quickest way to do this?)

2. Use the opportunity to upgrade the system to a newer/newest version of
Linux? Any tips as to what people would recommend, and how best/fastest
to do this?

3. Try to put Tru64 on it? We have one of those $99 Tru64 licenses.
(From what I've heard/read, I don't think that's possible with
this system, but you can always ask...)

Hardware:
RXP164-2 MoBo;
onboard Symbios 53C875 UW SCSI Controller;
onboard Digital 21143 10/100 Ethernet Controller;
Diamond Stealth64 Video 3000 Series (S3?) video adapter (we use the system
exclusively for numerical apps, so video performance is not an issue; we
have video in the system just for bootup).
Firmware: ARCS BIOS 1.3.058

Since the Tru64 part of my question is OT, you can e-mail me privately on
this topic if you want to.

TIA,

Marc
