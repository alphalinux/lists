Champigny, Michael (Michael.Champigny@compaq.com) wrote:

> My guess is the Multia 233Mhz will be faster. They come with a 512k
> cache whereas the AXPpci33 (at the least the one I have) doesn't.
> That will make a difference, though probably not much. If your
> doing anything compute bound I wouldn't be using a Multia. As for
> the CPU, they both use an LCA45 (21066A) so they are on equal footing
> in that respect. I don't know about memory bandwidth.

The AXPpci33 and the Multia use the same chipsets. The AXPpci33 also has
the cache chips in sockets vs the Multia which IIRC has it soldered on.
I hostly don't think you are going to see much performance difference
between similarly configured systems since they are so similar. You need
to remember the design of the 2106* is at least 6 years old. If you want
to do a fair comparison, try and find a x86 system from around 6 years
ago to compare it against. IIRC the AXPpci33 has 128-bit bus if all
banks are filled with identical SIMMs. If cost and performance are an
issue, try getting a 21164-based system. A 21164 will definitely smoke
a 2106* without even trying!

JMHO && YMMV,
Ron
