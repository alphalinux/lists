W Bauske wrote:

> Sure it does. Athlons should perform better than they do.
> At least a 500Mhz 21264 Alpha, capable of 1GFLOP can get pretty
> dang close to that. Athlons are saddled with bad motherboard/
> memory designs. Same goes for PIII.

Athlon boards are based on EV6.

> > Lack of software is the problem. What are you talking about?
> >
>
> Let me refresh your memory:
>
> "Just FYI here, on the entry level 21264, a single frame render of Lightwave is
> around 300 seconds, vs. the older "Classic" Athlon 1ghz. also taking about 300
> seconds for the same scene. "

Please note that I said Classic Athlon. That has 512k off die cache, running at less than
full cpu speed. The same test on the latest Thunderbird processors at 900 mhz. comes out
to around 250-260 seconds. A full 40-60 seconds faster than the 21264/500.

A system with a 21264/500 would cost what? and would it run NT4? at the least I suspect
around a 1k or more than the Athlon, and is going to complete a single frame one minute
slower than the 32bit Athlon. for 24 FPS, that time increases to 24 minutes for one
second of animation, and even more considering that a sequence might be 2, 3, 5 or more
minutes long.

Of course I could go to dual 600's which would reduce the time to like 150-160 This woud
be real good, but at what cost then? If I use Microway as an example, I could buy a 10
unit renderfarm of Athlons for about the same price. So I would still get better
performance and better cost out of Athlon.

The only thing that is going to set the balance right is if the Alpha gets priced
reasonably. Otherwise it is a system for those who have no interest in cost.
