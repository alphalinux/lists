Ivy Wang wrote:
>
> But if I delete package magicdev*, it seems I can't use GNOME X-window any
> more. Right?

Wrong. Why that claim? Because you are getting complaints from rpm
when you are trying 'rpm -e ...'? Then add '--nodeps' flag to this
command. You will likely have to click later on a "Recreate Default
Icons" menu entry if you do not want to be bothered with putting on a
desktop icons for removable devices by yourself; these icons will not
longer pop-out and disappear "magically" (many thanks to all deities!).

The problem with a magicdev is not one or another bug in its
implementation but the whole idea of a constant polling removable
devices is fundamentally demented. "Unix 101". Until you have devices
which interrupt on a media change (possible, but in practical terms they
are not there) this is one big stinking kludge which does not really
buys you anything but poping out and hiding icons. On the top of that
various mechanisms involved are not likely to be designed for a constant
spinning up and probably will start fall apart after a while. Even if
not that then an always present, with 'magicdev' that is, buzz from a
52x speed CD (faster, and problably noisier, will be here soon) is
enough to drive you up the wall.

Now, why this topic shows up on Alpha list?
