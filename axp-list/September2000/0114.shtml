Apparently you have little knowledge or contact with the 3D animation market. IF
you did, my comment would have been quite easy for you to understand. For sake of
clarification, production environment was used to represent the word studio. As
in production studio. This is a place where modelers, animators and other
creative types work producing television shows, commercials and motion pictures.

These types of places used the Alpha fairly heavily at one point in time, where I
work included. While you may not want to hear it, the simple fact is that in this
environment, Alpha is getting dumped real fast by just about everyone. As it was,
there was not a lot of Alpha support there to start with. SoftImage, Lightwave,
Digital Fusion etc. A fairly small but impressive list of vendors.

In the last couple of months I have personally offered assistance to studios who
are migrating away from the Alpha platform due to lack of continued software
support.

While Alpha may be stronger than ever, it is only in the server/database/number
crunching arena. Where 3D Animation is concerned, Alpha has all but left the
building. There are some rumblings from a few vendors about Alpha Linux support,
but this seems to be more rumor than reality.
