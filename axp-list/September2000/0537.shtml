With regards to your message at 10:16 AM 9/26/00, Ron Farrer. Where you stated:
>Champigny, Michael (Michael.Champigny@compaq.com) wrote:
>
> > Actually, the Multia 233Mhz (VX42 board) has a removable cache, though
> > 512K is the largest ever made for it. I don't think the 1M cache was ever
> > released into production.
>
>Perhaps not officially from DEC, but AFAIK they were very common cache
>chips used in 486s. The AXPpci33 also has a lot more expantion room...

The cache chips for the AXPpci33 can be of two types, supplying a total of
either 256K or 1024K of cache total.
The chips to do the 256K are not too hard to find, but the ones needed for
1024 are now virtually unavailable.
I last tried about 2 years ago, and could not find any from anywhere.
I even called various manufacturers who used to make them, such as Micron,
Cyprus, and Samsung, and were told that they no longer make them.
