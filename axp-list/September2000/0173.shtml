On Thu, 7 Sep 2000, Matthew Jacob wrote:

> Uh, you can get a backpanel connector that goes to an outside (narrow) scsi
> bus, or the little PCI connector that allows you to put a beefy scsi
> controller. 175$ is steep. 340MB is not nearly big enough for an alpha.

I'll agree with this. You would be much better served to get a cable that
lets you connect external devices and just buy a bigger external SCSI
drive than spend that kind of money on a tiny drive like that 340MB. I
have a hard time keeping the installation that I have on one of my UDBs
down so that it fits on the 540MB hard drive inside of it. You could
probably install a usable system on a 340MB hard drive, but don't expect
to be able to compile anything on it (since the glibc headers and such
take up quite a bit of space...and I won't even go into gcc/gdb).
