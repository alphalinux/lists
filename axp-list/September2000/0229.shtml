I'm seeking suggestions to solve problems I'm having putting a
SCSI CDROM drive on a UP2000 running Redhat 6.1, 2.2.13-0.9
kernel.

I have a Yamaha CRW8424S CD writer that has worked flawlessly for
months connected to a NCR/Symbios 53c875 PCI card, on the
internal 50-pin connector. (I have an HP 6300 scanner on the
card's external 68-pin connector, through an adapter cable.)

About a week ago, I tried adding a low-end Toshiba CDROM drive to
the same chain as the Yamaha drive. I could mount and see some
data from both drives. However, when I tried to read a serious
amount of data from the Toshiba drive, it would spin down and
report "drive not ready, make sure there's a disc in the drive"
errors after a nondeterministic amount of data, usually 1-4MB.

So, I bought a (more expensive) Kenwood UCR-416-RB drive at
Fry's. Now, the "mount" command for either drive hangs, or I get
messages that /dev/cdrom (symlink to /dev/scd1) has wrong major
or minor number. Both CD drives are seen by SRM,
/proc/scsi/scsi, and /etc/sysconfig/hwconf apparently okay. I
have the termination jumpers correct, and I have tried
configurations with either drive at the end of the chain. I even
tried using 68-50-pin adapters and using the built-in SE
connector on the UP2000, with basically the same symptoms.

One other thing, "lsmod" says sr_mod is uninitialized, even after
I did some random gyration to get the Yamaha drive to mount with
the Kenwood on the chain. This strikes me as odd.

Does anyone have experience with the Kenwood drive? Is the drive
just antisocial?

Does the uninitialized sr_mod mean the driver has problems?

Any other suggestions? (Next, I think I will try putting one
drive on the Symbios controller and the other on the built-in
Adaptec one.)

If the Kenwood doesn't work out, any suggestions for a vendor for
a Plextor drive? (I hear they are good.)

Thanks.
