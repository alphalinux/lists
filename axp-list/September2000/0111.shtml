Mike Foley wrote:
> .You don't have 2MB of SRAM cache on the board(have you priced that?)

Athlons need it too. Supposedly the K7 will support up to 8MB L2 cache.

> .You don't have more than 4-5 layers of etch meaning almost
> any vendor in Taiwan can make it. Cheap.
> Consequently, you don't have greater signalling issues.
> (count the number of pins on EV6 .vs. Athlon) All these
> add to the cost.

Umm... am I mistaken that the K7 and EV6 are bus compatible?

> You're not going to get an EV6 system for $500 unless someone
> starts buying them at volumes like an x86. Sorry. Believe me,
> I wish we could sell them cheaper! I'd love nothing more than
> to see every Linux developer running on Alpha. I wish I could
> figure out how to get even 30% of them to run on Alpha!

Oh, I understand. I think the treshold from workstation to server for
us is around $2k. For a little more we could buy Sun hardware or maybe
some UP1100's. I don't know if we are typical, but we like to have
developer workstations of a similar architecture as our servers, so we
can develop/test/debug locally or remote with fewer surprises.

I was encouraged back when Digital released the PC164SX boards... they
sacrificed some performance for lower cost, but at least they lowered
the barrier to developing software for Alpha. But there hasn't been
anything similar since the SX, not counting discontinued hardware.

Thanks for the info.
