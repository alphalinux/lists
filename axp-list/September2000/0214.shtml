This is the classic old Digital pre-MIPS pricing problem....

The CPU boxes (and memory) list prices are priced so high that they (the
VARS) can give 50% discounts and still make a 20 or 30% profit.

With the MIPS boxes, Digital priced things as commodities, and got slammed
in (especially) the education market by SUN because SUN still offered 50%
discounts, and Digital only offered 10%. It didn't matter that the net
price of a Digital MIPS box was less than the Sun... they faculty were
pissed that they didn't get their 50% discount.

So Alphas goosed the price back up again.

Now somebody like Microway comes along, offers a price that is 20% less
than Compaq, and it looks like a good deal.

In memory, we saw the same thing - Clearpoint was 20% cheaper than Digital,
but kingston was 60% cheaper... ie "street prices" for memory. (Of course
then we went back to boards instead of simms.... )

My XP1000/667 listed at $17444 back in November of 1999. Our VAR knocked
$7,000 off the price. So for $10K I got a 21264A / 256meg / 9gig x 10Krpm
SCSI / 10-100 ethernet / Powerstorm 350 graphics /600 CDrom and a Tru64
license.

I would assume that any reasonable third-party vendor could offer an
equivalent system for half that - $5,000 - if they wanted to.
The only thing "extra" you need to buy is a monitor.
