If your team is purchasing DS10 or DS10L's for $7K, even at the 617MHz
rate, you should consider some one on one time with them...and simply let
them know, it's not Alpha you can't afford....it's them.

The whole notion of the gross expense of Alpha has gone to far. Fact of the
matter is, the newer 10's and 10L's can be had within the $4K range. I
realize at least one purpose served through this group is to push the
price/performance numbers. I just don't believe much progress will ever be
made in this area through the use of inflated references.

Bottom line...software developers port to large numbers of boxes first.
Others port to support the needs of high margin/must have environments. I'm
not sure Alpha will ever have the huge numbers to boast of that x86 can. On
the other hand, todays reality is that Compaq can't build the new 617MHz
systems fast enough to avid paying penalties for late delivery. Given the
number of partners now building the Alpha chip....I'd call this encouraging.

cheers
