Christian Iseli wrote:
>The whole (well, pretty much) of the RAM must be available for one
>process, so only a 64-bit machine (and OS) is adequate.
>[...]
>Machine requirements:
> 1 processor (expensible to more CPUs a +)
> 8 GB RAM (expensibility also a +...)
> ~70-100 GB fast disk
> whatever else is needed to make the above fit together and work.
>
>It will run Linux... :-)

A DS20E can take up to 4 GB and the ES40 takes up to 32 GB (with
current models).

You could look at http://www.novaglobal.com.sg/ for example.
They have a 2 x 833 MHz ES40 with 8 GB of RAM for US $100K.

Are you sure you need the memory as RAM? That doesn't come cheap...
At the moment I'm running a 16.8 GB process on a UP2000. It has only
1 GB of RAM but I wrote the code to be virtual-memory friendly. Lob
in two 9 GB SCSI disks @ $206 for swap space and you're done. =:-)
