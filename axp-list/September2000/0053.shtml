> sorry for the non alpha content, but i'm hoping someone on the list can help

> privately.

As there seem to be general problems with (some ?) Adaptec host adapters
for wide-SCSI ("294xU2W" in my case), it might be of general interest
(even if not directly applicable to your problem).

>
> i have an wintel box with an adaptec 2940uw with a seagate 4.3gb drive, a
> toshiba 40x cdrom and a richoh 2x4 cdrw. after 2+ years of dependable
> service, all of the sudden i am getting page faults and scsi port errors
> constantly when windows is doing something disk intensive. i have run the
> scsi card utility to check the disk for bad clusters and none were reported.
> i have checked all of the cables and they seem fine. termination is via
> jumpers on the tosh cd. i can not think of anything else to try to figure
> out what the problem is. no other changes to the system or software.

A whole series of machines delivered to "my" company all showed the
same symptom: The moment you added a "narrow" SCSI device to the
external port (which is "wide", so you used a "wide-to-narrow"
adapter cable), the whole adapter stopped working - it would
not even list the devices connected, let alone use them and boot.

>
> anyone have any ideas on what to do next? change cables? update scsi bios?
> viking funeral?

Make sure you leave the wide external port and do not touch it.

If you need external narrow devices, either install another
host adapter or install a SCSI slot (internal cable to 50-pin
jack, "Centronics" or "mini") and connect the internal narrow
cable to that slot.

My personal conclusion is not to use that brand.
