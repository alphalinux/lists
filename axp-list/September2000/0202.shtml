Steve Rogers wrote:
>
> W Bauske wrote:
>
> > The front side bus is but the memory is standard PC.
> > Take a look at streams performance between a 21264 Alpha and an Athlon.
> > Athlons are a factor of two or more slower. Even though several Alphas
> > use PC memory, they still blow away PIII and Athlon in memory bandwidth
> > because their chipset interleaves the DIMM's.
>
> It is pretty much a factor of 2, no more. I can match speed with a 500mhz.
> 21264 no problem with a 900 mhz. Thunderbird, specifically using Lightwave.

Don't think that a doubling in clock speed can compensate for halving
memory bandwidth. For a great many apps, memory bandwidth is the single
most important factor, more than clock speed.

I sometimes explain the memory problem as a race between a Corvette and
Ferarri through downtown Chicago, in congested traffic, stop lights and
25 mph speed limits. Whichever wins, it probably has little to do with
horsepower.

> One point that will get the shit flamed out of me for saying, and did
> at one point, is that Lightwave was poorly optimized for the Alpha platform.

Why would you get flamed for that? I said near the beginning of this
thread that many commercial vendors have poor support for non-mainstream
platforms. That much is obvious. (I doubt if anyone from Lightwave
reads this list, anyway, since they have little involvement with Alpha
and none with Linux.)

Clearly the question for you is not "which is better for general purpose
computing, Alpha or Athlon" but rather "which hardware/OS does my
software vendor support". That question is really not on topic for
axp-list.
