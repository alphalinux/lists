"William H. Magill" wrote:

> Fox's Blue Sky Studios July announcement for 512 Alpha's (Renderwall)
>
> http://www.compaq.com/hpc/news/news_bluesky_jul2000.html

"Blue Sky purchased the new computer equipment for use on
"Ice Age", its first computer-animated feature film with
parent company 20th Century Fox Animation."

I wonder how that will be affected by the fact that their parent company Fox
Animation closed it's doors recently.

> Apps:
> http://www.compaq.com/hpc/apps/app_film.html

A total of 4 apps. Not exactly a strong endorsement of a well supported product.

> Alpha does hollywood:
>
> http://www6.compaq.com/alphaserver/products/hollywood/

A single reference with no details. Also to Blue Sky, as in the first reference. So
far, the implication seems that Blue Sky was the only reference Compaq could come
up with for studios still using the Alpha platform.

> Rendering software:
>
> http://www6.compaq.com/alphaserver/products/hollywood/rendering_software.html

Mostly a rehash of one of the previous links provided. Mental Ray, Renderman,
Houdini and yet again, Blue Sky is mentioned. This time for their proprietary in
house software. Presumably not available otherwise.

> Compositing software:
>
> http://www6.compaq.com/alphaserver/products/hollywood/compositing_software.html
>
> While these don't specifially address Linux and lower end of film/video
> production efforts, they do point out Alpha is very much a player in the
> digital production world.

Yet another rehash of one of the previous links provided. This time listing only 2
of the 4 software titles mentioned in the first link.

What would appear to be a plethora of links proving that the Alpha is still a
strong contender is quickly dismissed when one reads the material presented. These
links do little more than repeat the same material over and over. They certainly do
not give much credibility to the Alpha does Hollywood Impression that is being
presented.

Honestly I could find more Amiga links that would make it appear as if Amigas and
Video Toasters were still strong contenders in the content creation arena that was
provided here for Alpha.

To be quite blunt, I would be embarassed to present this list of links as any kind
of evidence. They are far too weak, easily dismissed and pitifully lacking in
content.

As clarification though, those apps listed are all quite exceptional products. All
well worth their respective costs.
