hi,

to give some info about the glide stuff/status:

i have started to port glide to linux/alpha in december last year (if i remember corr).

it never worked on my alpha (alphastation255 / ev45 / apecs / avanti) because /dev/3dfx
(3dfx.o / 3dfx_driver.c) was not working correctly (mapped the pci stuff wrong).
i did not know that, so i stopped working on it.
phillip ezolt had some success - /dev/3dfx worked on his machine - i dont know why.

i corrected the /dev/3dfx error recently (have not worked on glide for 6 months or so).
the new version and a patch is attached (hope that it is allowed to post files to this list).
i sent this to the joseph kain already who put the 3dfx driver into the web.
he said:
"Ok, I'll take a look and should be able to apply them to the CVS
tree soon."

in dec last year i just took the glide3x tree and started porting.
because things changed a lot i grabbed the latest GlideV2 tree recently and
started the port from beginning (i have a voodoo2).
things are going quite well.
yesterday i made the mapping of the pci mem into virt mem of glide work.
the libs still crash, but - to some point - it is possible to debug.
(GlideV2 patch from yesterday is also attached).

i am looking forward to get "spinning things" (mentioned in one mail about this topic)
(the stuff phillip ezolt got to work) work in this month.
but dont hold your breath!
linus torvalds sayed that linux-2.4.0 will be out in dec 1999 ;-)
so it could be that it will take me 1,2,3,4,... months longer too ;-)
(i am just a human who does regular work and who has a lovely girlfriend).

niki w. waibel
