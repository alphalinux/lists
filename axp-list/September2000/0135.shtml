I have a 900+ Mhz Athlon and 533 Mhz 21164A. They are very
comparable in speed, etc. The 264 would simply wipe the floor
with the Athlon. The Athlon's performance vis a vis write-through
L2 cache performance is anemic compared to even a Pentium II !
They got it cheap alright, at performance computing expense.
This was revealed to me in one of the great tools for measuring
performance, seti@home, and the various performance measuring
tools that people have kindly donated. Since then I have run
other benchmarks and the results are all the same. Athlon has
a ways to go to even compete with Pentium III's. You can forget
it when it comes to two generations old Alpha's. They still,
Mhz for Mhz, outperform them all. If the Alpha community
wants developers to develop for the Alpha, then the price
for developers has got to come down. I got my EV56 on the
most amazing insider deal and built my system for about $400.
I don't expect to beat that deal any time soon. I have a computer
with 512 Alpha's in it at work, as well as 21264A workstations,
but of course, all of that work is proprietary.
