This is a problem/issue that Digital/Compaq Alpha marketing, still, simply
does not understand. We in the education community have been arguing this
for years and the result was the reluctant creation of the XP900. There
were no plans for ANY alpha workstation class machine a couple of years ago
-- and the one they agreed to build was going to run VMS only!

People who "grow-up" using Intel or Sun workstations simply never
"graduate" to using Alphas. If for no other reason than Alpha Workstation
choices barely exist, let alone come close to being competitive on a
cost-per-seat basis... and as someone mentioned, forget about applications,
they just aren't there. It hurts any kind of justification efforts
tremedously.

If you've ever heard MadDog's story about getting Linus an Alpha years
ago, you'll understand that this is not a new problem, just an ongoing one.
