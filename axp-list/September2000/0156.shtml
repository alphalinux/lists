> Intel stock was just downgraded, and Athlon processors are rapidly
> replacing Alphas on a widespread basis in the production environment.

Intel's stock was downgraded because Intel had to recall yet another
Processor last week. Their production numbers and projected revenues are in
shambels as a result. (Initial estimates were 2-4 months of re-design to
fix the problem!)

[SUN's Enterprise Servers have had a similar problem, but with the
motherboards, for at least 6 months, but SUN had forced their customers to
sign non-disclosures to get their boxes fixed so the story just started
to leak out last week.]

Intel is really hurting right now on the high end and AMD has moved
dramatically past them in the 32 bit world. And in the 64 bit world, the
IA64 is still a joke.

Itanium performance on real silicon is slower than Itanium emulation on
Alpha EV 5!!! [Don't even think about how much faster the emulation is
on EV6/7 compared to EV5.]

And the last I saw last week -- that sucker still draws 40 Watts!!!
You'll never see that in a laptop or anything that is not kept in a
seriously controlled environment. And we're not even close to a gigherz
in clock speed yet.

The real problem is that Intel has the PR and Marketing machine whereas
Compaq/Alpha (let alone Tru64) simply does not. DEC couldn't market its way
out of a paper bag ("stealth" marketing was the term), and so far, Compaq
has not proven any better for either Alpha or Tru64. VMS, by comparison,
IS staging a strong comeback and THEY may pick-up the Alpha torch as a
result.

The recent stunning sales in the HPC world are despite the general
marketing efforts, not because of them. Wildfire can run rings around
anything else in the marketplace and the HPC technical people and the
"spooks" know that - that's why they are buying them like candy.
But the General Public still believes that a 32 bit Intel box is "as good
as it gets."
