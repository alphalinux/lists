Peter Rival wrote:
> Steve Rogers wrote:
> > unfortunately the Athlon is shipping in 1.1 ghz. and AMD is releasing no
> > less than 5 new iterations of the Athlon processor over the next 5 months,
> > ranging in speeds from 1.5ghz. up to 2 ghz.
>
> Yup, and it's still slower (at some things, _much_ slower) than the Alpha
> even at a given clock speed. Tell me again why Alpha has higher SPECCPU
> scores at 500MHz than Athlon at 1GHz? And has anyone noticed where the extra
> 32 bits on the Athlon chip went? Oh, that's right - it's only a 32 bit
> chip! Doh! So much for my VLM capabilities without needing massive hacks...

Yep. Anyone around here who doesn't yet know the virtues of the Alpha
hasn't been paying much attention. 64-bit, plenty of registers and a
clean ISA. I personally enjoy coding for it.

I think the game for many is really price/performance, not MHz. Athlons
might not have better absolute performance than Alpha, but they are dirt
cheap. I bought my SX three years ago because it didn't cost as much as
2-3 x86 workstations. It wasn't the fastest, but it was measurably
faster than x86 at the time, and it was easy to justify as a development
workstation.

I've been patiently waiting for EV6 to hit the same price point, but I
don't see it happening. They are still well outside the hobbyist budget
and more than what most companies are willing to pay for desktops. It
isn't clear to me whether Athlon succeeds due to volume alone, or if AMD
uses other tricks to cut costs relative to API.

> > It may be considered a return of bragging rights, but it is a hollow
> > vicotry at best.
>
> Okay, if this is a hollow victory, I guess I'll take it. That, and the best
> damned chip money can buy. Oh, and I'll see you at EV7. And if you're not
> an Alpha user and that doesn't scare you, it's only because you don't get
> it. But if you seriously believe your email, that's a good indication that
> you don't get it anyway... Go out, read the product briefs and check the
> benchmark scores. Then join the believers.

I think Steve and others have read the product briefs and seen the
scores. No doubt many are frustrated that the particular application
they need isn't available on Alpha or doesn't perform adequately on
Alpha because commercial software vendors haven't spent the time and
money to optimize their apps on Alpha. Commercial vendors may also not
be willing to ship products tuned specifically for EV6 with such a large
base of EV56 and earlier out there.

The difference with Athlon is they can beat Intel with the same codes.
Nobody writes code specifically for Athlon either, but it doesn't matter
much. (It'll be _very_ interesting to see what kind of industry support
AMD can muster for their 64-bit Sledgehammer.)

-Jeff
