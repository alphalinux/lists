Jay points out that I should be a bit more clear:

1) My PCI bus example refers to a "feature"/bug in the previous
generation Digital PCI chipsets that no one would have ever noticed if
it wasn't for gigabit networking. Jay tells me that the new XP1000
motherboards have been clocked very close to the maximum bandwidth
with 64-bit PCI cards.

2) My PCI bus example was just an issue of "peak verses actual" and
wasn't directly relevant to SCSI disks. A single SCSI controller can't
max out the PCI bus anyway.

These days you can buy RAID systems that can begin to get close to the
PCI bus peak bandwidth, but it's hard. One example with IDE RAID would
be the systems at www.raidzone.com, which have "bonnie" results of 60
megabytes/sec reads, which is 50% of the PCI peak. (They don't have an
AlphaLinux driver, but they do have Intel Linux...)
