On Wed, 17 Mar 1999, Frank C. Brants wrote:

> Just out of curiosity, I compared the /proc/cpuinfo info on an Intel
> P166 box to the same info on my Multia 166.
>
> There was a pretty big difference in the bogomips rating...

> vendor_id : GenuineIntel
> bogomips : 66.76

> cpu : Alpha
> BogoMIPS : 163.05

> Any ideas why?

Yeah, because BogoMIPS means absolutably nothing. It occasionally appears
to be of minor use within an immediate CPU family, but outside of that, it
is utterly useless. It's just a timing loop, man, it has no bearing
whatsoever on actual computational ability. RAM access times will throw
the whole thing off dramatically, along with any number of other settings.

Want a better illustration?

P5 200 MHz; bogomips = 79.87
P5 100 MHz; bogomips = 39.83
        # These seem ok, right?
486 75 MHz; bogomips = 37.48

I have Cyrix 486 clone that clocked in around 37 or so too. But by messing
with various BIOS cache options and RAM waitstates, I could get the
bogomips down to 18 or so; even though nbench would run with better
results.

Bogo == Bogus.
