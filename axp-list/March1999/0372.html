> Is it legal to distributed binaries compiled against a static library whose
> license prevents redistribution?

http://www.unix.digital.com/linux/license.htm

Under clause 4 "restrictions" it say's "You may not rent, lease, or
otherwise transfer the Software. You may not make the Software available
over the internet or similar networking technology. You may not remove
any copyright, trademark, or other proprietary notices from the Software
or the media. You may not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble
the Software, except to the extent COMPAQ cannot prohibit such acts by
law."

This refers to the library and says nothing about staticly linked
binaries.
I can't find anything else in this document that say's otherwise. Under
clause 2
I can argue that this rpm is for testing purposes so I meet that
requirement. I really don't see a problem with it. I'll remove it if
requested to do so but I don't think that'll happen cuz everything is
legal. So try it out :)
