On Tue, 2 Mar 1999, Vignaud Jean-Baptiste wrote:

> I'v noticed this too, but i suspect a ide, or more serious, a irq problem.
> All disk access are very slow and have the behaviour to temporary stop the
> mouse.
> There is long lags sometimes...
>
> Maybe the obtimized IDE driver for SX164 (patch from 2.1.126) will help. I
> haven't tested it since 2.1.126 so i cannot say if it works great on recent
> kernels. see http://flowserv.teco.edu/crew/krebs/linux/cy82c693.html

The patch is now distributed in the beta ide-udma driver for 2.2.x
(http://www.dyer.vanderbilt.edu/server/udma/) and it gives me ~6MB/s vs.
~1.5MB/s (hdparm -t) on my 164sx. Integer performance on a pc164sx/533
should be about the same as a pII/350 (floating point much higher on
anything but linux).
