At 06:50 AM 3/15/99 -0800, Eric Brown wrote:
>I saw a few messages go by about hdparm, so I thought I'd give it a try;
>according to hdparm, my 8 GB SCSI disk only reports 0.5 MB/sec raw read.
>
>This is TERRIBLE! my other Linux machines report > 5MB/sec raw read; the
>Alpha disk lags by a full order of magnitude.
>
>What the *HECK* is going on? What can I do to diagnose this? Might
>it be the cheapo SCSI CD-ROM I have hanging off the Alpha? How do I find
>out if SCSI disconnect/reconnect is actually enabled? (I have it enabled
>in the kernel....) The disk can't be *that* slow; it's fairly recent.

Try disconnecting the CD-ROM. SCSI always negotiates the speed of the
bus to be that of the slowest device. Ergo, if you have a slow SCSI
CDROM (God forbid, it's SCSI-1!!!), everything runs slow.
