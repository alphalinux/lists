It seems to me that a lot of people have been caught by the Intel marketing
scheme. The clock rate for a cpu is just one of several factors that are needed
to know its true benefits. The only time that the clock speed should be used by
itself is when comparing chips of the same design. Comparing a PII 233 with a
P233 is not right because they have different amounts of cache and differences
in architecture (RISC/CISC).

> Alphas come with 2 different generations of CPUs at many different

> previous generation is the 21164 chip. Your PC164LX has this chip, and
> indeed you can buy them at 533, 600, or 667 mhz. Compaq should be able
> to show you benchmarks for various machines; the 21264 is much faster
> than the 21164 at the same clock speed at most tasks.
