On Mon, 15 Mar 1999, Charles Taylor wrote:

> > I bet ice , blackbox, lesstif, and flwm would run well on a UDB. There's
> > nothing wrong with using fvwm2 , just configure it. Check themes.org on
> > how to do it.
>
> I use Afterstep on my (166, 64M) Multia. Just make sure you use low-color
> icons/themes on that built-in TGA card.

Agreed.

I used the 8-bit TGA video on my UDB at 1152x868, and all works well..
you do have to know what you are doing when you configure your theme
though, to keep the color usage reasonable. I've been using X on 8-bit
video machines for awhile, so I have had a lot of practice at this.

In short, xv is your friend. Dither your images to the standard colormap
and you can't go wrong.

I like a bit of eye-candy here and there. I have a background image
(dithered to std. color) and I use Eterm with pixmap backgrounds (also
dithered to the std. map). Everything looks decent, is readable and
easy to see, and nothing (ok, except Gimp) complains about the color map.

Not as nice as my 24-bit console, of course.. where I can just throw
any image I like at it, but it is ok.

I have the exact same config on my UDB as I have on my laptop. fvwm is
my wm; I don't get fancy with all that.. X is just a really snazzy
"screen" for playing with xterms as far as I'm concerned.

Now that I think about it, the fanciest wm that I've ever really run
(aside from setting up a really old pre 1.0 beta of KDE to check it out)
is Afterstep 1.0, which is still on my Pentium.

I'd run AS 1.0 on all my machines, actually.. but I've since lost the
source, and AS 1.0 is long gone, so all I have is the binary tarball
that I put together for myself awhile back out of the scraps in /usr/local

I try to stay close to my roots.. windows/guis are to be distrusted,
and terminals are to be loved (DEC VT100's, of course)
