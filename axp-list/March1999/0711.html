  Ah, name resolving... There are two ways to resolve names - one is to have
a
DNS server on your network. In this case I think you'll need to bother your
lan
techs, 'cause the odds are they already have a DNS server(s) running (unless
LAN is MS-Windows strictlywith WINS server(s)) and have them include an
entry
for your Linux machine. Therefore allowing everyone on your LAN to point to
your Linux box by name.

  Second - is just for you - to have a "hosts" file in your c:\windows
(WIN95)
or C:\winnt\ssytem32\drivers\etc (WINNT) include an entry for your Linux
like
this:

192.168.150.10 alpha.domain.net alpha

 Assuming the right IP address and the right name.
