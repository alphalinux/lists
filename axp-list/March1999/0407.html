I'm gobsmacked.

On a POTS (plain old telephony system) outgoing analog data is passed
through a midpass filter before being sampled (called PCM - pulse code
modulation) and transmitted digitally. At the receiving end the the
exchange reconstructs reconstructed without anything more than a simple
reconstruction filter (i.e. the signal is not 'processed', and should be
analogous to the signal before the PCM filter). Your modem then performs
its DSP wizardry to extract your digital data from this analogue signal. To
do this it will typically preprocess the signal to remove extranious
noises.

In the olden days all communication was symmetrical like this.

However, the new x2 technologies rely on the connectivity provider (no
'service' provision necessary) having a direct digital connection to the
exchange thus not passing throught the analog filter/PCM stages.

You, alas, must go through the filter/PCM stages. Therefore your upload
speed will be half your download speed.
