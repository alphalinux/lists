Bill,

Well, the 166 Mhz that can't be overclocked are roughly equivalent to
90/100 Mhz Intel chips. Perhaps that perform better doing certain tasks
but 90/100 Mhz has ben my experience. Once you go to the VF42 model you
can get the 233 MHz which can be overclocked to greater speeds: 266 and
perhaps 300. Are these the models you are referring to?

For a small, dedicated server, they are perfect. Just setup and forget
until they crash. I'll be usiing one for a small mail/web server and the
other for a firewall.
