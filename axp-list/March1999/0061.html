On Tue, 2 Mar 1999 16:17:11 +0100 (MET), Danilo Godec <danci@kibla.org> said:
> I expected to see my new 533 MHz alpha fly, but kernel compile (ok, not
> really appropriate test - alpha 8 minutes, P II 300 MHz 3.5 minutes with
> about same settings), gzip and gunzip, md5sum on a large file were all a
> little dissapointing to me.

You are doing two entirely different tasks: Once you compile an
x86-Kernel, the other time an Alpha kernel. This is not the same
thing, because the generation and optimization of Alpha code is more
complicated than that of x86 code.

> Was I expecting to much or *should* my alpha feel (and be) faster than
> PII 300 MHz? Can I do something about it?

IIRC the SX isn't that fast. If you look at the SpecInt figures,
you'll probably see only a slight advantage. Now, if you look at
hand-optimized floating point stuff, that's a different story ...
