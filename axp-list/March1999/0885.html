> On Mon, 15 Mar 1999, Charles Taylor wrote:

> > I've updated the Mozilla binaries. I've included the lesstif library
> > that they were built with, so the new tarballs don't require anything
> > "special" at all to be installed on the system. This won't interfere
> > any lesstif RPMs that may be already installed.

> What's different with this version of Mozilla compared to your last one?

The "debug" one is new - it's larger, but prints error messages that
would normally pop up in "subprocess diagnostics" windows (e.g. the
"buttonup" messages) will be written to stdout instead.

The "nodebug" one is essentially similar the old one , so if you have
the old one working, you don't need these.

The reason for the new packaging is that the new (0.88.0) lesstif
doesn't seem as stable with Mozilla as the older one. This way, you
can update lesstif for other programs and Mozilla won't die on you. :)
