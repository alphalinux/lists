My reaction to the page was exactly the opposite - the new pages
actually work in alpha-mozilla, and there's no java crap or borders
that don't work.
I like the way the links are set up. My version of mozilla doesn't
work right for entering strings in search boxes or forms, so the
ability to keep clicking on choices to drill down to what I'm
looking for is a plus.
This doesn't necessarily affect me, but I see lots of questions
in newsgroups, etc. about what linux can do for a corporation, and
what "commercial-quality" software runs under Linux. The redhat._com_
page is a good quick reference to answer these questions.
Note that we're talking about redhat.com, not redhat.org.

I prefer to use FSF, open source, or "free" software - or develop my own -
but Linux is ready for corporate use, and anything that helps business
to see that is good, even if it means commercial software on Linux.
The new RedHat site is better at promoting commercial use of Linux,
and this will be a benefit to those of us who like free quality software.
