Okay, it happened again. This time it was NOT SCSI related, as I though
previously. The system (Noname) was printing a large (~2MB) Postscript
file (via ghostscript 5.50), doing ip masquerading for a Win 95 & Linux
system, and downloading a large file (unknown size, it never got past
14MB). And it just lockedup, no ethernet or no dialup (ping), and no local
console response, just dead. The screen still had ncftp displayed on it,
but no mouse or keyboard movment/response.

I figured, ohh well and powered it off and back on again and did it all
over again. This time I ran top in a nxterm and kept an eye on it.

It froze again, aahhhh...

Looking at the frozen top, it showed about 50% idle cpu, 912K pyhsical
memory free (out of 32MB), and about 19MB free swap (out of 32MB).

There are no messages in the syslog other than the normal gs (ghostscript)
unaligned traps, within a few minutes of the lockup.

Any ideas?

system:
Noname (AXPpci33) 32MB RAM
RH5.2 NO UPDATES
