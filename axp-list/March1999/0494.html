Quick scorecard of the new EV6-based boxes:

    DS20 == DP264 == GOLDRUSH (1 or 2 CPUs on daughtercards, 2 PCI buses)

    XP1000 == MONET (1 CPU, 2 PCI buses)

Kernels based on 2.2.1 and later have DP264 and MONET support; you simply
build a kernel for DP264 or a "generic" kernel, and it should "just work".
Or use the floppy images available on gatekeeper.dec.com:

        /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Images/generic-up-221.img
        /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/Images/generic-smp-221.img

Note that the DP264 kernel/image on the RH 5.2 CD will NOT work on MONET;
it is a much earlier version (2.1.120?) which did not have the necessary
support.

In addition, that old DP264 kernel is broken WRT PCI cards with builtin
PCI-PCI bridges, like the IntraServer cards that have 2 SCSIs plus Ethernet
behind a bridge. If you have such a beast, use the above-mentioned kernel
images from gatekeeper instead.

Indeed; at the time that I did that table, all we had were internal names

FYI, TAKARA is a DEC TOEM board, available with EV4 or EV5 daughtercard, and
from 4 to 13 PCI slots; also called DMCC.

And TSUNAMI is the core logic used by all our current EV6-based products
(except for the high-end TurboLasers; ie 8200 and 8400).
