On Mon, Mar 08, 1999 at 04:47:24PM -0500, Phillip Ezolt wrote:
> If it's broken, let's fix it. How does the complier tell the library
> that the code was compiled with -mieee? What isn't it doing the right
> thing, and what exactly IS the right thing?

It doesn't at the moment, it only tells the preprocessor.

One way for it to be accomplished is for <math.h> to change the
assembly name of the functions when -mieee (or no -mieee, depending
on your view of life). E.g.

#ifdef _IEEE_FP
__extension__ double exp(double) __asm__("_ieee_exp");
#else
double exp(double);
#endif

When it makes a difference whether we change names based on -mieee
or the reverse depends on your philosophy as to whether a user should
be allowed to mix -mieee and -mno-ieee code, and what happens if a
pointer to `exp' gets put into a table.
