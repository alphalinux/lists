Yeah, I agree. It's been awhile since I looked at the effects of
overclocking, but IIRC, the basic relationship is inversely exponential..
the higher you o/c, the less time the machine will work for.. with
the cooling affecting the derivative of the relationship.

I've been running my P5 (rated at 150 MHz) at 200 MHz happily for about
8 months or so. As soon as the price for 200 MHz CPU's dropped, I o/c'd
on the idea that if my proc dies, I can easily buy a new one.. and if it
lasts at 200 MHz long enough so that I simply can't get P5 processors
anymore, then I have gotten more than my money's worth.

Though, when it dies.. I'll be sad to see it go.. It's a classic jalopy
machine, hacked together from the remains of a few 386 and 486's ..
along with a few IDE drives that never did work quite right, and speedy
2x proprietary CDROM drive.. all of which is dumped into a full
tower case and glued together with Linux. Every variant of Windows
crashes pretty hard on it, but Linux works just fine ;)

I'd like to o/c my UDB .. but, that would be a really major chore. Once
I get a new Alpha, I'll o/c the UDB on principle, I guess.
