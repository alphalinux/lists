FYI.

One of my UDBs went south. It would spontaneously reboot either
during the SRM bootstrap, or sometime while the system was running.
It seemed like a power problem so I resigned myself to losing the
box. Before I gave up entirely, I opened the box, opened the power
supply and beheld a substantial mass of cat fur and litter. The cat
would balance on the top of the UDB as it stood vertically.

Today, I got out a volt meter and checked the voltages coming from the
power supply. They're good. I wiggled the connector. Doing so
showed the power light to flicker and the speaker to crackle--note
that the speaker is connected to the motherboard through the power
connector.

So, I delicately pulled the pins from the power connector, lightly
sanded them with 400 grit paper, lightly sanded the connector on the
motherboard, and ha! The machine boots and runs reliably.

The message is that Digital engineers appear to be pretty good at
building cat-litter-proof power supplies. %^)
