> hda: 1.5Mo/s

> This i with no call to hdparm

depends on your disks - find out what features they support and play a bit with
the parameters. the most insteresting is of course the transfer mode (hdparm -X).

for one of my old disks, it's even faster to use pio instead of dma (a fujitsu disk).
this is because the driver also changes the pio timing values.
 
if you have more than one device per channel try using the slower device (e.g.
cdrom) in pio and the faster one in dma mode. for pio mode also try to enable
32bit IO (hdparm -c 1).
