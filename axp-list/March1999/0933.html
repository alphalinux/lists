On Sat, 13 Mar 1999, Oscar Levi wrote:

> So, I delicately pulled the pins from the power connector, lightly
> sanded them with 400 grit paper, lightly sanded the connector on the
> motherboard, and ha! The machine boots and runs reliably.

You really shouldn't use sandpaper on connections as it will remove any
coatings on the copper making them more suseptable to corrosion. You
should use a good quality contact cleaner and your connectors will last a
lot longer. Some contacts are self cleaning but power connectors really
are quite poor quality.
