I withdraw the question. I can see from the Ken Dawg's response to other
questions that this is not the place to bother the "all knowing ones" with
trivialities. It's a shame. I have an electric car based largely on the
helpfulness I found on the EV mailing list. I wonder if the NT communitee
is any friendlier. No wonder software companies are more inclined to
support windows. :-\
        Ok that's enough sarcasm. I know that Mr. Dawg doesn't represent the
linux communitee. I even know where he's coming from. As someone who's
first home computer has 16k of ram, and who remember's the horror of seeing
your program stored on a stack of punch cards that just tipped over and
scattered, I've always known at least a bit more about computers than the
people around me. Those newbie questions can wear a little thin. HOWEVER,
you're not going to get anywhere in life if you're not willing to help
other people out. That's especially true when you find yourself on the
fringes. If you don't help the newbies onto your OS, then they'll just go
elsewhere. Then the software developers will figure it's not worth
supporting your platform. I watched something like that happen to OS/2.
The folks at IBM decided not to support the home market. Too much work.
Too many supid questions. For some reason it never occured to them that
business execs are probably going to want to have the same stuff at work
that they have at home. How many people do you know w/ OS/2 now? (Besides me)

........................................................................

Enough waste of bandwidth. As for the above problem: It's solved... sort
of. I popped the case, and the video card is not an E1 as stated in the
manifest, but an E2. A quick look at supported hardware list at redhat
told me that E1 is the only flavour that's supported. The folks probably
ran out of E1's and figured they were doing someone a favor by dropping
inthe next higher model.

So at the risk of offending Mr. Dawg (or perhaps with the intent of
offending him) I'll ask another silly question. Does anyone know of a fix
that allows support for the E2? If so, how stable is it? Failing that, I
guess I'll have to accept that I'm not done spending money yet.
