Having said that, here are some quick comments on that advert. The
quotation damage isn't mine (well, mostly not mine).

>> > DEC ALPHA MULTIA 166MHZ VX40B-F2 64 mb RAM keyboard (minus 1 key)
>> (IBM
>> > keyboard)

Minus 1 key...?!

>> > mouse Redhat Linux 4.2 and 5.2 cd’s SCSI cable 68 pin to 50 pin
>> > centronics SupraFax Modem 288
>> > w/cable and power supply THIS UNIT MAKES A KILLER SERVER OR
>> > WORKSTATION
>> > UNDER WINDOWS 4.0 WORKSTATION, LINUX OR DEC UNIX!!

It's *not* a killer workstation. The major benefit is that it comes
in a small box, and a very nice-looking, solidly-built small box at
that. Once you start hanging all sorts of stuff off the external
buses it loses that benefit, and I promise you it's *not* all that
fast -- my 266MHz box is approx. the same as a P166 I gather (better
on floating point).

>> > The DEC ALPHA
>> > MULTIA (VX40F-F2) is truly a desktop wonder. Its compact design
>> > integrates the powerful Alpha
>> > CPU, SCSI, network, sound, and graphics subsystems

8-bit colour only with the built-in TGA graphics.

William> single-board
>> > package. This is an awesome Windows NT and Linux workstation
William> and/or
>> > server! All this unit needs to operate is a monitor, and hard
>> > drive. (check your closet) THE DEC ALPHA MULTIA 166 RUNS THE
>> >
>> > FOLLOWING
>> > LANGUAGES: LANGUAGE HARDWARE REQUIREMENTS LINUX 16MB RAM/500
>> > MB HD

Realistically you need at least 64Mb RAM for linux. Get as much as
you can afford. Watch for SIMM plating compatibility -- gold:gold or
tin:tin, don't mix them, and also look out that you get true parity.

>> > INTERFACE - PCI Fast
>> > SCSI2 internal/external interface (NCR 810) FLOPPY - 1.44MB 3.5"

These were the other things I mentioned: looks like you're OK on that
score.

                                [...]
>> > Also included is an internal Sony SCSI CD reader, model number
William> CDU76S,
>> > cable not included.

I don't know where this is supposed to go: inside an external SCSI
box, I assume. There's no drive bay expansion on the UDB, unless you
don't mind losing the internal hard drive & the floppy.

                                [...]

Personally I think you'd be better off saving the money and buying
something better suited to your needs, unless you've got clues and
want to play with 64-bit Linux, in which case the UDB makes a great
box. It also makes a very nice X server, although you'll probably
want a nicer graphics card.
