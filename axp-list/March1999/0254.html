Quoting Jochen Kuepper (jochen@uni-duesseldorf.de):
> What do you think about that. Has anybody else some numbers to compare ?

egcs-1.1.1 is not the big runner. On my Pentium Pro 200 a tested
it with a small wavlet transformation routine. (It's a convolution)

Here are the results:
gcc-2.7.2.1 : 99.68 MFLOPS
egcs-1.1.1 : 54.95 MFLOPS
pgcc-1.1.1 : 97.10 MFLOPS (an egcs version for pentium processors)
egcs-snapshot: 98.81 MFLOPS (egcs-2.93.10 19990228)

Soon after egcs-1.1 was out, i saw a near factor 2 improvement of
runtime in executables created by the snapshots.
These will be in egcs-1.2 (not in egcs-1.1.2).

I don't have my Alpha handy now, but i think the snapshots became
much better there, too.

But: I don't think it's good to use gcc-2.8.1 on Alphas. A lot
fixes/improvements have been made in egcs especially for Alpha.
(Red Hat ships egcs on Alphas, not gcc-2.8.1, i think!)
