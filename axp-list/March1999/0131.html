On Wed, 3 Mar 1999, Dumb Kid wrote:

>I know the uid limit in x86 linux is 65535. And I would
>think the limit in Alpha will be much bigger due to the 64-bit
>arch.

uid >= 2^16 is a feature slated for inclusion starting in the 2.3 series.
There's so much to change, they will also be changing many other
quantities for 64 bit archs., to get it all done at once. (For example,
the kernel will have to keep track of time in nanoseconds, as we are
starting to move into GHz territory clock speeds.)

So you'll have to wait on this one, but do check the archives
(http://www.linuxhq.com/lnxlists/) for discussions of this issue and
possible pointers to people who may be working on patches already.
