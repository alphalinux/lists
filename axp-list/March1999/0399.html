Greg Lindahl wrote:
>
> > If you are experiencing trouble with this, it would be in your best interest
> > to link in either the CPML or the FFM. First, they are MUCH faster than (on
> > some things) than libm, and they both seem to round to zero appropriately.
>
> You seem to be assuming that zero is the "right answer". It is
> if -mieee is not specified. But if -mieee is specified, then there
> should be routines which return a denorm. I'm surprised that CPML
> doesn't have such routines. (?)
>

Zero is the correct answer. If you think a denormal
provides useful algorithmic information, you're kidding
yourself.

Why would Digital/Compaq library routine writers do
something that doesn't make numerical sense? Whether you
push the result to zero after the exponent bits go to zero
or wait until there are no more bits in the mantissa, the results
to whatever equation you're calculating are highly unlikely
to have any significant difference.
