performance improvement. (from 310 MFLOPS to 456 MFLOPS) Quite
an improvement in the scheduling algorithm for 21164's...

Keep in mind the Bogus part of BogoMIPS. It does seem
to indicate though that the 21264 is able to do two instructions
per clock on the timing loop where as the 21164 only does one.

> I'd be more interested in stream numbers:
> http://www.cs.virginia.edu/stream/
>

The streams tests are similar to parts of the benchmark I ran.
All they are is about the simplest loops you can code. Not
very representative of real code. I have seen old vector code
that is like this though I doubt it's in production anymore.
But, they do stress memory if you use large enough arrays and
that I suppose is the point of the tests. Anyway, my tests that
are similar show nowhere near the speed up Compaq quotes on
their WWW site. I suspect the XP1000 has slow memory compared
to the DS20/DP264. Also, it could be gcc/g77 can't do simple
loops well yet and stall the pipeline. On the somewhat more
realistic tests, I see about 700 MFLOPS vs 456 MFLOPS on 21164.
Note the XP1000 is using egcs from RH5.2. I'm hoping egcs 1.1.2
will push that 700 up to high 800's or low 900's, low 900's
being preferred...
