>>> Hirsim{ki Mika said:
>
> My MILO is an LX164 MILO (2.0.30) from RH 5.0 and
> is currently burnt into the flash rom on board.

This is very old, and is the source of your problem (see below).

> Can I create the fmu.gz somehow by myself from a
> 2.0.35 MILO?

Yes, most likely. But you'll either have to build it under RH 4.2
from the 2.0.35-0.2 sources that are already available, or wait for
newer ones (hopefully appearing soon).

> The question still
> remains, why does the 2.2.3 kernel, booting from
> LX164 MILO, think it is running on an EB164, not
> on a LX164 based AlphaPC?

Because the version of MILO you are running from FLASH does *NOT* identify
itself correctly.

One of the things that had to happen in order for a "generic" kernel to be
possible, was that MILO had to *accurately* describe the system it was
running on. Without an accurate description, a generic kernel couldn't
correctly access the hardware on which it was running.

I am certain that the MILOs shipped with 5.2 do this; I think that the MILOs
shipped with 5.1 might have; but I'm *certain* the MILOs shipped with 5.0
DID NOT.
