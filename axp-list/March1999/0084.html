Kerry Boguszewski-Odom wrote:

> Below is a portion of the
> logfile.

Pity that not a bit more of earlier stuff as we do not know
**what** your ppp peer rejected.

>
> Feb 28 10:50:59 Suzi pppd[518]: rcvd [LCP ConfNak id=0xa <magic
> 0xf654978b>]
                                           ^^^^^^^
              Your peer is unhappy and rejected what you tried.

> Feb 28 10:50:59 Suzi pppd[518]: rcvd [LCP ConfNak id=0xa <magic
> 0xf654978b>]
                                           ^^^^^
                                      The same again.
> Feb 28 10:50:59 Suzi pppd[518]: Serial line is looped back.
                                    ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
          This is kind of a "generic" error response, unfortunately,
  and likely means here that you attempt of connection was rejected
  by the other side.

There is really not one method of starting ppp connection and knowing
what the other end wants really to see helps a lot. The fact that
an NT implementation of ppp is buggy does not help if you are trying
to connect to such machine (it first accepts and authenticates
and later rejects). Bumping up a debug level for ppp will give you
more (even much more) information in log files which often helps.
Maybe you simply are trying to use a wrong authentication method?

PPP-HOWTO and pppd manual page have a lot of useful information.
