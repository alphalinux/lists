On Thu, 11 Mar 1999, Larry Snyder wrote:

> Steve Frampton <frampton@alcdsb.on.ca> wrote:
> > Are you guys nuts? Why, just a few years ago Billy told us, "640 K was
> > helluva lot".

> ...and a few years prior TJ Watson said 16K of core would be fine and
> the world market for computing devices was 5.

Yeah, these sorts of quotes always make me laugh.

Of course, in the contexts they were said in, they are pretty accurate..
(Gate's statement less so, as the computing revolution was in pretty good
swing then, but still) no one could have predicted the explosion of
computing.. and certainly the very-early computing pioneers would have
only dreamed of having even a fraction of the storage and memory capacity
that we have today.

The sentiment of Watson's remark is actually on the money.. considering
the prohibitive cost and size of the computer back then, there was indeed
a small market. This small market exists even today .. few places need a
supercomputer.

Indeed.. few places today even need a computational brain; computers are
used more for delivering information than computation.
