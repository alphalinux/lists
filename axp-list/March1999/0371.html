> Is it legal to distributed binaries compiled against a static library whose
> license prevents redistribution?
Chances are good, no. I'd have to spend time deciphering the legalese to
make a reasonable guess, though.
        Here is the patch against mpg123-0.59o (with axp mods)
that I sent Peter. Simple modification of the Makefile, assumes static
libcpml installed under /usr/local/lib/.

Enjoy.

------------------------------------------------------------------
--- pristine/Makefile Sun Jul 12 17:56:03 1998
+++ mpg123-0.59o/Makefile Sun Mar 7 10:30:20 1999
@@ -84,7 +84,7 @@
                CFLAGS='-DLINUX -DOSS -DREAL_IS_FLOAT -Wall -DREAD_MMAP \
                        -fomit-frame-pointer -funroll-loops \
                        -finline-functions -ffast-math \
- -Wall -O3' \
+ -Wall -O3 -mieee' \
                mpg123-make

 linux-ppc:
@@ -269,7 +269,7 @@
        $(CC) $(CFLAGS) $(LDFLAGS) mpg123.o tabinit.o common.o layer1.o \
                layer2.o layer3.o audio.o buffer.o decode_2to1.o equalizer.o \
                decode_4to1.o getlopt.o httpget.o xfermem.o decode_ntom.o \
- $(OBJECTS) -o $(BINNAME) -lm $(AUDIO_LIB)
+ $(OBJECTS) -o $(BINNAME) /usr/local/lib/libcpml.a $(AUDIO_LIB)

 mpg123.exe: mpg123.o common.o $(OBJECTS) decode_2to1.o decode_4to1.o \
        tabinit.o audio.o layer1.o layer2.o layer3.o buffer.o \
