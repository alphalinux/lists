Thatcher Hubbard wrote:
>
> I'm overwhelmed at the amount of people who had suggestions on this one,
> thanks all. After thinking for a minute I decided that I could confirm my
> overheating theory more easily than most others. So I took the top of the
> case off and set it in front of a small box fan. It's loud, and it's
> causing slight jitters on the 19" monitor for my PC right now, but it's been
> up for 18 hours and 53 minutes according to 'top'.
> The box fan is an imperfect solution though, how can I keep this thing
> cool enough to run for long periods of time? I've heard you can replace the
> exhaust fan in the case (there is indeed plenty of room for that) but I'm
> wondering if that would be enough. As for the popping, there seems to be a
> consensus that that is something in the power supply. Could this still be a
> problem, and could it be contributing to the heat problem? (I'm ignorant of
> electronics theory)
> At any rate, thanks again to the list members for turning out to help a
> new Alpha owner.

The fan in a multia is thermostatically controlled. A heat sensor governs the
fan speed. You can see the sensor zip tied to the power supply.
Try fooling it. Stick it into the CPU heat sink and see if the fan doen't then
run full blast. A cheep trick but it may work for a while.

Another trick may be to connect the fan directly to the 12V supply. This
should speed it up a bunch.
