When escaping from the RedHat install, your hard drive is
not necessarily mounted. You might have been editing the
files in the ramdisk filesystem which of course get forgotten
when you reboot. If this is the case, do the same steps you did
before, but use mount(1) to mount your harddisk then edit the
files there.

    Good Luck,
    Brian Beuning

PS. This happened to me over the weekend. My system clock
had been set to 2019 and lots of symbolic links had really bogus
timestamps. I did a global find to locate all these links in the
future and wrote a script to remove and recreate the links. It
turns out after removing the symbolic link to
    /lib/ld-linux.so.2

that almost no programs (like rm, ln, ls) will run anymore.
I did the same stuff you are doing to recover. Aren't the
programs in /sbin supposed to be compiled without shared
libraries to make recovery easier?
