What I've heard so far is suprising to me.
I have had a Multia for a couple of years now and never had any of this
trouble.
But I'm Dutch , and I bought my Multia in Holland (www.request.nl)
These Multia's came from a series that were intended for a firm in
England, but
have never been used. The were all complete though, so they have not
been stripped down.
A friend of mine had one, with a bad MB, but they replaced it under
warranty and the
machine is running beautifully eversince.
I also know someone who bought a Multia already when the weren't dumping
them
(This is about 3 or 4 years ago , he paid about $4000) This Multia is
still running beatifull to.

I think the question is: Who is stripping these boxes, and can't he be a
bit more carefull ?

Jaap

Well, I have two theories.
1. Many of these machines were stripped down in a hurry.
Memory and disk was removed with no clear intenion of ever using
the old box again. One of mine has a missing power-connector pin,
luckily one of the ground pins...
2. Typically, old cardboard boxes of "stuff no-one is going to use"
receive none-to-gentle handling in storage. This would apply to
both stripped down _and_ "complete" mchines.
