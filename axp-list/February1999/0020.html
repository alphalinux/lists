Tommy, King wrote:
>
> I wonder is it now having a offical or some nice people made a RPM?

I never heard of rpm for VNC but last time I tried VNC compiled on
Linux/Alpha without any problems. I do not recall even firing up
an editor to tweak makefiles but maybe I did that. This was a while.

Having something in rpm format is convenient, and your rpm database
registers an installation, but this is NOT a requirement. Besides
a packager could have libraries which you do not have, or a compiler
too aggresive for your model, or someting like that, and source rpm,
or just "PSO" (plain old sources :-) can be actually preferable.

On the top of it recent incidents when a cracker left on server
in Netherlands doctored versions, with trojan horses, of tcp_wrappers
and utils-linux archives should serve as a warning. It is much easier
to pull such stunt with a binary set of rpms than with sources.

> I tried compling the sources, but it give me funny errors and sig 11

This sounds like your hardware has problems and not VNC. Not very
promissing for a machine which is supposed to go into "production".
