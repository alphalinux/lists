On Fri, 12 Feb 1999, Robert Bowles wrote:

> I have a few multia's, and one had such a problem.
> Turned out to be a bad connector...
>
> Take the box apart and unseat/reseat all of the cables,
> making sure they're tight and clean. You may want to clean some
> of the contacts (_carefully_) with a small piece of steel wool
> Also make sure that the scsi/pci-riser card is seating properly.

This would certainly explain spontaneous rebooting, but I am unclear
as to how it could explain "popping" coming from the machine.

In any case, I've seen this sort of thing while browsing the list
archives.

Does anyone have a good theory as to why the Multia seems to be
susceptible to such bad connections? One or two instances of it, and I
tend to just shrug it off, but this sort of thing seems to be a common
problem. Makes me wonder just what sort of gremlins run around underneath
the hood when we turn the lights out.
