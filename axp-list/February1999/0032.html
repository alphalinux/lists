Another reason executables could be getting larger is perhaps the
compiler is getting smarter. Usually, if the compiler finds unaligned
data, it will pad them up to avoid that. If this is the case, and
previous egcs missed to align data certain data structures, then this is
a good thing.

Also, executables for Alpha are naturally larger than x86 executables
since Alpha instructions are ALL 32bits wide, while x86 instructions are
variable. Also any pointers on Alpha are always twice as large as 32bit
machines.
