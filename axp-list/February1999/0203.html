Hey,
        I've got a pretty nice brain teaser of DEC trivia for everyone today.
As mentioned in other messages, I use a PWS 500a. The system had been
sitting in storage for a while when I got it, but was in mint condition;
some papers in the box from Digital are stamped with the date 24 Februrary
1998, so I assume this is a later model 500a. I had the vendor yank the 8
bpp DEC TGA video card and replace the Qlogic ISP with an Adaptec 2940UW, but
other than that it shipped the same as from the plant.
        Anyhow, here is the discussion I have had with another Alpha
enthusiast regarding the 500a: Everything I have read on the system indicates
that the PWS "a" models are designed to run NT (and Linux, of course) and
the PWS "au" models are designed for Digital UNIX and OpenVMS but also can
have ARC/abios. In fact, I have been told numerous times that the "a"
hardware is incompatible with SRM and do not ship with it as an option.
        My system has a badge on the front which clearly reads "500a", yet
the system shipped with SRM as the default power-up firmware. In fact,
the abios setup allows me to choose "AlphaBIOS", "Digital UNIX SRM",
"OpenVMS SRM", and "ARC" as default power-up modes. Were late model
PWS "a" systems shipped with both SRM/ARC to extend the life and usefulness
of the soon-to-be EOL'd PWS series, or had the capability to run both firmware
options been there all along?
        I find it interesting to consider, as some vendors on Usenet have been
known to charge ~$2500 more for used "au" system than an "a" system with the
same options.

Best regards,
Daniel
