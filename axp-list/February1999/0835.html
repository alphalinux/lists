You wangt to change the kernel while it is in use?

I guess you could, but it seems to me to be (at best)
a tricky proposition - your system is running RH5.1,
while it is up and running, you go through and change
all of the OS/kernel files, and they take effect with the
next reboot. Your system would be (at best) considered
unstable between the upgrade and the reboot...

Some OSs allow you to do this, but they tend to be in
cluster arrangements (i.e. two mated systems, bring one
down while the other keeps processing, then bring up the
upgraded system and have it take over, while the other
system "updates itself - HP-UX Service Guard
environments can do this). You can also do this on
a mainframe (MVS, VM, etc.), but those OSs were built
for three nines or better (99.9% uptime).
