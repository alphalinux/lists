Rob,
        Thanks for your patch. I suggested the simple #ifdef __alpha__
solution to Alan Cox earlier today (yesterday? Oy, it's late) - chances
are he'll adopt that in ac3, ac4, or as he said on linux-kernel, "whenever".
The compile just bombed out, but I'll have to examine it later.. kinda
lethargic at the moment. Andrea Arcangeli also has a patch against
2.2.2 that is supposed to fix the semaphore woes:
http://e-mind.com/~andrea/linux.shtml.
        Did anyone else catch Linus' linux-kernel post regarding
the breaking of compilation on non-x86 platforms? His basic philosophy is
that if recursive semaphores were going to be added, it is better to
do it all in one swell foop (grin) and cause major breakage up front
rather than be subtle about it. Can't say I disagree, although I wish
we could have had some warning between pre4 (working) and pre5 (not working).
        While I'm on the subject, 2.2.2-ac2 does not compile because
of changes made in drivers/cdrom/cdrom.c. I'll hack with it at a reasonable
hour later today.
