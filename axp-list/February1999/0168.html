On Wed, 3 Feb 1999, Jason Duerstock wrote:

> a = (int *) a;
>
> dereferences null, thus causing your program to go kerfloom.

a = (int *) a; just means to tell the compiler to start _treating_ a as a
pointer-to-int for that expression in case you wish to do ptr arith on it
or deref it. But he doesn't ever dereference it.

So: a = (int *)a + 1; will increment a's address by sizeof(int) * 1, which
is 4 for Intel, Alpha, etc.

version of your code. Is this what you were asking about?
