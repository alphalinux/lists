On Sun, 31 Jan 1999, Designer 7 wrote:
>I was just wondering what people might recommend...
>
>If one were to build a Alpha machine for use as a graphics machine, what
>components would one use that are currently available on the market??
>
>I assume CPU would be 21264... ideally
>
>But what graphics card? What software is available that would use
>specialized hardware? And just for fun, what monitor?
>
>How would the ideal Alpha based graphics machine (exclude clustering)
>compare with a SGI?

        Depends on if you're considering price or other features, and if you're
talking one of their new NT machines or one of the MIPS machines.
        The new NT machines have a really sophisticated graphics/video setup
built into the motherboard that uses a special memory setup to access up to 980
MB of RAM for texture mapping, etc. It's far and away faster (for 3D stuff,
particularly) than anything on the market that needs to use an AGP or PCI bus.
The machines support either two (the "320") or four (the "540") processors
(Pentium III's) using SGI's own chipset (rather than an Intel version) and
is more efficient (as in: you pretty much double your speed when you double the
processors). The NT machines start at $3300 for the 320 and $5600 for the 540.
The 320 supports IDE drives (built in) and the 540 supports Ultra2 SCSI (built
in). The 540 also supports 2 GB RAM, and I have a feeling that you'd have to
be a pretty sophisticated gamer to run out of speed if you buy the four CPU by
600 Mhz version due out later this year. Digital video will support two video
streams in or out in real time at up to 60 fps. The video system will support
any of their monitors up to and including the 24" Sony Trinitron-based
wide-screen HDTV shaped monitor AND the 17.5" (diagonal) 15" x 9" HDTV-shape
108 dpi widescreen flat active matrix monitor.
        The MIPS-based machines,particularly the OCTANE, offer videoboard
modularity and dual processors. More than...er...adequate. But pricey. Irix is
very easy to use and very stable and optimized for graphics.
        
        The Alphas are good, but don't really compare.

        david
