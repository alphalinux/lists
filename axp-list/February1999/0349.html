On the Utrecht University where I completed my studie in biology we
used a tool called "GRIND" (GReat INtegrator Differential equations)
developed by Rob J. de Boer. It is mainly developed on Irix using the
OpenGL library for 3D graphics, but it also has a plain X11 interface.
It compiled fine on my linux-alpha as well as on several other
platforms.

You can get it from:
    http://www-binf.bio.uu.nl/~rdb/software.html
It is great in Space-phase stuff and if I remeber correct it can
calculate Lyapunov Exponents and a lot of other things. It does not
calculate Correlation Dimensions.
