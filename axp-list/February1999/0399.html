> Hi all,

> I bought a Multia not so long ago off of onsale. After putting 64MB of
> RAM (true parity TI),an external 2GB drive and RedHat 5.2 on it, I found it
> would reboot spontaneously. At first, I believed it to be an aging battery
> and so replaced it. Now, it is still doing the same thing. It just blanks
> and reboots, with some weird, very quiet popping noises from inside the
> case. Times between reboots vary from 5 to 35 minutes. Is this a heat
> related problem? I have the machine in the vertical stand, and it's not
> near any other equipment that produces a great deal of heat. Is my box
> doomed to be unreliable?
>

I have a few multia's, and one had such a problem.
Turned out to be a bad connector...

Take the box apart and unseat/reseat all of the cables,
making sure they're tight and clean. You may want to clean some
of the contacts (_carefully_) with a small piece of steel wool
Also make sure that the scsi/pci-riser card is seating properly.

Other possibilities include
- bad memory (once again, try reseating)
- bad fan
