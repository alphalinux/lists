  Thatcher,

  I went to the Yahoo auction, found a good price for a PC Air Conditioner,
$13.00, took the PCAC apart and took out one fan and reversed the other fan
and then put it in the psuedo PCI slot. The PCI riser card on my UDB has a
mini 4-pin power connector that will accept an adapter from a 5.25" to 3.5"
adapter kit. So.. I plugged the power adapter in, looks just like one for
a PC floppy, and plugged in the PCAC. I also screwed in a CPU fan from a
486 to the hd/floppy cage and pointed it right at the CPU heat sink. These
modification makes it a lot cooler in there, the PCAC now sucks in cool air
from the bottom back and distributes it over the mainboard while the power
supply fan blows the heated air out the top cooling vents.

  The other suggestions about checking all connectors for oxidation,
scorched spots, etc. should also be done.

  David
