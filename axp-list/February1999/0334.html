        While browsing around the net last night, I came across an
interesting package that potentially benefits porting x86 asm-optmized
routines to AlphaLinux. The package, "intel2gas"
<http://hermes.terminal.at/intel2gas>, allows specification of an
assembler's macros, opcodes, etc. through an abstract specification file.
As you probably can tell from the package name, it was originally written
to convert between MASM and GNU as. According to the authors, they have
been successful with everything they threw at it.
        Granted, I do not see this as a panacea to cure all portability
problems, but it has two things going for it: 1) It compiles straight out
of the box under AlphaLinux and 2) If successful, it could improve
performance of packages with x86-optimized routines under AlphaLinux
significantly. Affected programs would probably be various games first
ported to x86 Linux, emulators, certain cipher codes (some AES entries
come to mind), etc.

Anyone interested?
