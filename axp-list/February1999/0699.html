On Tue, Feb 23, 1999 at 12:06:00PM -0800, Stephen Williams wrote:

> I see that ATI All In Wonder PRO/PCI video cards are plausibly priced,
> and am considering shoving one into my alpha PC164. It has the ARC BIOS
> and I run Linux 2.2.x. (There is NT burried somewhere on an IDE disk as
> well, but I guess I won't miss it much if it croaked.)
>
> Are people running these cards in this or similar computers?
> (I'm willing to buy Metro-X, I was thinking of doing that anyhow.)
>
> Limitations?
>
> Thanks.
>

Well.. the problem is that not all video cards agree with the console
firmware (or is it the other way around?).

In fact, I had problems getting my ATI All-In-Wonder Pro to do
ANYTHING on my PCI-64 (cabriolet) box running ARC firmware.

Hardware compatibility with the console firmware seems spotty at
best. And although I haven't tried to plug my All-In-Wonder into my
SX164 yet, I'm guessing that AlphaBIOS will be just as unhappy with it
as ARC was.

Other than that, the card worked fine for 2d under Linux on my old
Intel box. Unfortunately none of the video features work due to ATI's
closed hardware specs, and stubborn stance on releasing those specs.

Although Metro-X does claim to support the ATI AIW, I haven't seen any
information which would indicate that the Metro-X server is capable of
supporting any of the video effects.

So, since only about 1/6th of the card's features are supported, I'd
recommend that you save your time and get a card from a different
manufacturer.
