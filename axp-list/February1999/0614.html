>> Is that memory Parity memory? I believe that the SX (plz correct
>> if I am wrong) takes 72 bit dimms (standard parity).
>> This certainly sounds like a memory incompatability problem to
me..

>i use 2x 64 MB DIMM 100Mhz non-ECC in my PC164SX and they work fine.

My 164SX uses standard 64MB non-parity DIMMs too, and they work. So
non-parity DIMM is fine. In fact, you don't even need the PC100
(100MHz) DRAMs. The manual says so too. You can download a copy of
the manual from the digital/compaq web site.

>> When I power it on, it only gives me six beeps (and a black
screen). Six
>> beeps are supposed to mean 'Checksum error detected when image was
read
>> back from memory'. OK, but what does that mean? What can/should I
do?

Sounds like you got flaky RAM. Hope you bought yours from a reputable
dealer. I bought no-name DIMM dram as well & was lucky to get them to
work (after some trials & tribulation - but mine was supposedly rated
at 100MHz though). With marginal RAMs, you have to make sure that the
RAMs are really seated properly in the sockets & the contacts are
clean. I used good quality SDRAM from Samsung for my second 164SX and
it worked the 1st time round.

Hope this helps.
