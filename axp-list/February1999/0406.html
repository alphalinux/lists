On Fri, 12 Feb 1999, Ross A. Knepper wrote:

> On Thu, 11 Feb 1999, Thatcher Hubbard wrote:
> > I bought a Multia not so long ago off of onsale. After putting 64MB of
> > RAM (true parity TI),an external 2GB drive and RedHat 5.2 on it, I found it
> > would reboot spontaneously. At first, I believed it to be an aging battery
> > and so replaced it. Now, it is still doing the same thing. It just blanks
> > and reboots, with some weird, very quiet popping noises from inside the
> > case. Times between reboots vary from 5 to 35 minutes. Is this a heat
> > related problem? I have the machine in the vertical stand, and it's not
> > near any other equipment that produces a great deal of heat. Is my box
> > doomed to be unreliable?
>
> my udb had this problem a year or two back. when it got so frequent that
> it was totally unusable, i took it in to the digital service center, where
> they told me i had a bad power supply, which they replaced for the
> small(?) fee of $110. since then, it has performed beautifully. it had
> the same symptoms as yours, so i fear you may have the same probem ...

I'll second this diagnoses. You have a dying power supply. That little
popping sound is the core of the transformer being repeatedly energized
and de-energized as the switcher tries to start. My UDB just went the
same way. Rather than try to purchase a new supply, I just bought a
bare-bones UDB from CPU Micromart and slid my existing chassis in.

Up and running again :-).

Steve

p.s. - For the frugal among us, it may well be possible to kludge on a
standard PC power supply. Just be sure to disconnect whatever voltage is
currently trying to exit the pin used by the UDB for the speaker <g>.
