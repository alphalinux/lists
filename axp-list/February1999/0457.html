Many thanks for the information on the KDE RPM's. I'm going to rebuild my
KDE from source, BUT I went to look at the official KDE site (ftp.kde.org)
and found that there were no RPM's there!

It seems to me that the people on this list that are busy making RPM's for
the rest of us ought to upload them to the official "sites". This not
only makes it easier for alpha people to get a RPM of a distribution, but
makes the alpha market look STRONGER. Many people are putting thier time
and hard effort into ALPHA rpm's, and this work needs to be recgonised by
various progects themselves, not just those of us in the alpha community
proper.

Some RPM's that I know are built (and even on redhat) but weren't on the
"official sites' were

                JDK 1.2
                Kaffe
                KDE 1.1
                GIMP (although I understand that was recently taken care of)
                etc.

Of course this is all part of my master plan. The more we publisize
the alpha, the more popular it becomes, the more are produced, the cheaper
they get. Eventually I will no longer be considered a strange geek with a
wierd machine but a computing genius ahead of his time. I might get a
date to boot.
