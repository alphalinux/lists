Howdy All,

        I finally got some RAM for my Multia/UDB and it boots up no problem
(incidently, compaq double-bit parity used in prosignia/proliants (at least
the prosignia 300 and the old proliants) works in multias.. May be a waste
to some, but it worked for me). However, I am now contemplating what
harddrive to use.
        I currently have a Seagate Barracuda (7200 RPM) drive installed. Again,
this may be a waste to some, but hey, this is stuff I got for free...
Anyhow, I'm wondering if the drive will overheat in the multia.. I ran it
for about 2 hours and popped open the case and the drive felt warm to the
touch, but nowhere near as hot as the CPU (damn.. didn't know these things
ran so hot ;) ). Does anyone have experience with a 7200 rpm drive in a
multia?
        If bad things could possibly happen, I may put the barracuda in another
machine. If that happens, I will need a new drive. I don't need anything
huge, since this is going to be a firewall/webserver. I'm thinking about
replacing it with a 1.7 gig IDE laptop drive (they're $89 at
www.centrix-intl.com).. How is the IDE performance on Multias with the new
kernels?

Thanks for any info you can provide.

Mike
