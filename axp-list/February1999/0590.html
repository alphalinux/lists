        With the Mesa fix, FlightGear fell right into place. It built just
fine with:
        1) CFLAGS set to '-mieee -mcpu=ev56 -Wa,-m21164a -pipe -pendantic -g'
           (again, the most important one is probably -mieee here)
        2) -lffm preloading -lm in the Makefiles (the LDFLAGS env. variable
           could probably take care of this)

Peter Petrakis or myself may have an rpm or two of it available shortly, but
performance is terrible (as should be expected for software-only rendering).

On my 500a PWS (500Mhz 21164a, 2M cache) with 128M ram, Permedia2 card at
1024x768@32bpp under XF86 and Mesa linked against libffm and optimzed,
the best I can achieve is 1.0 fps with textures on, 2.0 fps with textures
disabled. Time to convince Darryl Strauss that Glide on AlphaLinux is a
good thing, or plan to wait it out until Metrolink/XiG release their
hardware-accelerated servers.
