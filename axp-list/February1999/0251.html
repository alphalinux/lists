gmake uses a couple different mechanisms to look at
archives, member information in the archive. Could
be Solaris and NetBSD use a different layout for their
date info. As I said, if it really bothers people, they
can edit it out of the source or just change the code
to tolerate time differences they find acceptable. I
seriously doubt a skew less than a minute or so would
cause trouble in all but the most trivial test cases.

The code is however technically accurate, just a little
too picky if you ask me.
