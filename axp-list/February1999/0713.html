Daniel J. Frasnelli wrote:
>
> Did anyone else catch Linus' linux-kernel post regarding
> the breaking of compilation on non-x86 platforms? His basic philosophy is
> that if recursive semaphores were going to be added, it is better to
> do it all in one swell foop (grin) and cause major breakage up front
> rather than be subtle about it.

Daniel,
watch out! Recursive semaphores were *** removed ***. They were added
just in time for 2.2.0, to guard against a problem discovered right
before release , and caused a lot of teeth gnashing, unhappines and
assorted breakage in various drivers. Now the original problem was
solved in another way and "recursive semaphores", which is a strange
concept on its own, are out.

Backing out recent changes is only a "band-aid" solution which should
not stay. This is NOT a desired fix.

  Michal
