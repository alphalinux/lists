> my udb had this problem a year or two back. when it got so frequent that
> it was totally unusable, i took it in to the digital service center, where
> they told me i had a bad power supply, which they replaced for the
> small(?) fee of $110. since then, it has performed beautifully. it had
> the same symptoms as yours, so i fear you may have the same probem ...

Wow! $110? As I told the original poster in private mail, its likely
the power supply. $5 in parts from Radio shack would likely fix it
(dead rectifier?). And Ratshack charges an arm and a leg for parts at
that.. but the parts surely won't cost $110!

Man.. for $110 a pop, if you have 2 or more Multia's with a bad PS it'll
be just about the same price as taking an analog electronics course at a
community college and fixing it yourself!

Of course, they are replacing the whole PS and all.. but still.. its the
principle.

<sigh> .. Things like this make me just want to pull my Heathkit CP/M
brute out of the basement (still works!) and go back to the good 'ole
days.

Then I recall how heavy that thing is, and getting up the stairs out of
the basement would probably kill me. So, I guess the good ole days weren't
very good, but still.. :)
