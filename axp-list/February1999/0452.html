Well, there have been many suggestions about how this problem occurs and
what to do and I can say that I have had the same problem with my multia
and cured it for no cost in the following way. A bit of solder. Turns
out that the crappy AT style connector that brings power to the
motherboard had got a little lose in its old age since I have had this
thing open a few times and the machine started to spontaneously die with a
little pop. It would sometimes run for hours and then die and other times
it wouldn't even make it into MILO before it happened. What I did was to
put a bit of solder on each of the blade connectors from the motherboard
and this made for a much tighter fit of the connector from the power
supply. The machine hasn't died since.
