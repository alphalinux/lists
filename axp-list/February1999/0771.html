At 01:18 PM 2/24/99 +0000, you wrote:
>I'm setting up a multia for school, I think that speed and efficiency
>have improved somewhat, but I might get better improvement on mine if I
>break up the drives (ie. seperate drives for /usr, /home, /root, etc.).
>Because this is used for geologic apps, and many students will use it,
>it will probably help. Are you going to use seperate drives, or just one
>internal? I'm interested in your eventual speed ratings if it's the
>later.

I'm going to probably stick with using the single internal drive. From what
I understand the SCSI controller on the multia is far from perfect. If you
have the free PCI slot w/riser card, you may want to put a new controller
in it. I'm not particularly concerned with speed on my system. I'm mainly
going for something that works, since I've only paid for the multia box
itself, and have the harddrive and RAM from other machines. I'm just hoping
the dang barracuda doesn't overheat and fry itself. I'll let the list know
how it goes once I finish testing.
