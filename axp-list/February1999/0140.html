Carles Perello wrote:

> >Ok.. I just looked at the specs for the 21264... why is it still a
> >.35micron chip? Almost everyone has gone below that.. and towards the
> >.18micron.... can you imagine the kind of heat a 0.35 micron chip can
> >generate at 600Mhz? I don't get it... does Samsung lack the technology?
> >Can they not license it from someone like AMD? Or are they just slow in
> >upgrading their fabs?
>
> 0.25um has just been recently introduced in the product industry and is in the
> so called 'ramp up' phase. 0.18 still needs at least 2 years to be
> there...although everybody is pushing hard...

Hmm. Samsung is already using 0.18 um for RAM (e.g. 1-Gbit DRAM)- in samples.
(http://www.usa.samsungsemi.com/about/press/1gsdramoem98.htm) Seems that
technology is nearing, or perhaps in, 'ramp up' phase...

But also, the 21264 was designed for 0.35 (since it was supposed to be available
last year). There's an argument that goes something like, "If it's the fastest in
the world at 0.35, why not get some extra use of those fabs and make a lot of
money in the process?" I've heard that along these lines, Intel debuts products
like the Pentium at slower speeds than the fastest they're capable of, and waits
to get the yield up in higher-speed chips before releasing them. There's some
profit-taking involved in such scheduling- make money on the chips that don't test
quite so fast before destroying this "low-end" market with faster chips. It seems
like a necessary condition for this to work is to be the performance leader in
your market segment, which Pentium sort of was, and which 21264 definitely is.

I don't know if such thinking plays any part in Samsung/Compaq's decisions. In
one sense, it might make a consumer feel cheated, that we're not getting the best
price/performance as soon as it's ready. On the other hand, the level of
investment necessary to make 21264 happen, especially with all of the delays, had
to be enormous, so such behavior is very understandable.

Let me emphasize again that I have no idea whether any of this is pertinent, and
please correct me if I'm off-base on any of it. The economics involved have
intrigued me for a long time. :-)
