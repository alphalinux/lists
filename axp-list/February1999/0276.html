On Wed, Feb 03, 1999 at 08:36:40AM +0100, Joachim Wesner wrote:
> >Also, I found some benchmarks comparing the 21164 600 mhz running Linux
> >and Digital Unix. The results of the benchmark were that the DU had a FP
> >nearly three times that of Linux. Does that number, if correct, reflect
> >any other performance differences b/w the two OSs?
>
> No, these bad numbers reflect differences in **compiler and math lib** quality,
> partly already fixed. From the numbers I know, often the *SAME* binary even
> runs a few % faster on Linux.

To be more specific about this: The CXX compiler (runs under DEC UN*X) is
better than egcs which is better than plain gcc on the alpha.
With Fortran, the difference is even larger.
And, yes, it's not only the compiler, but also the math library (libm.so).
If you use libffm (fast free math), e.g., your egcs compiled binaries will
perform somewhat better.

You can use statically linked DUX4 binaries, produced by the CXX compiler,
under linux-alpha.
If you have a DUX4 license you don't need, you can copy the DUX4 shared libs
to your linux-alpha box and use dynamically linked DUX4 binaries. I don't
know, if it's possible to install the CXX compiler uner linux-alpha, but it
should.
