Andrei A. Dergatchev wrote:
>
> I found that I'm getting very often this *exactly* order (e-310) when I use
> uninitialised
> arrays.

Ah, that sounds familiar!

> So, it is probably a compiler problem ?

I would not call it that way. :-) It sounds like you are feeding
an unitialized garbage into arithmetic operations. Accidentally this
garbage very often will come out as floating point numbers in e-310 range
(pointer leftovers from old stacks). Compiler does not have to initialize
your arrays to zero except in some well defined circumstances.

If this is really the case then you have to fix your code. It may work
on some other platform, by a pure chance, the way you think that it
should work but it does not make it correct.
