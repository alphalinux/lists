One might also say that people will live a long life, if u - lets say - eat
properly. If u dont then they get flaky. Once they get flakey, then it becomes
a question of what to do.

So its nice to say that the adaptec is reliable - so what. Its when there is an
exception that has to be taken care of THEN it becomes a real problem for the
adaptec aix7xxxx drivers. When this timeout exception does occure, it appears
that thecombination of the driver, and the card never recover enough so that u
can either continue, or graciously shutdown. This particular bug still exists,
no matter what u do to the hardware to minimize exceptions.

this Sort of reminds me of someone else's experience with writing channel
command programs for the IBM ( 14 inch) disk drives. The company originally
bought real ibm disk drives & controllers. Apparently these drives hiccuped
rarely, and the error handler really didn't need to be all that beefy. Later a
compatable albeit cheaper brand X drives/controllers were substituted. The
hiccups occured more frequently, and the software driver was enhanced to take
care of all the exceptions that just never happened with the real stuff.

So, when i was running with 2.0.33, I was a happy hacker. with 2.0.34 those
pesky ( and non-recoverable) timeouts kept ruining my sessions. Hardware didn't
change. Just the OS.

Tried 2.2.0pre9. Seemed like the disk & cdrom was happy. As soon as i accessed
the tape drive, that pesky timeout occured. ( from the log the scsi disk went
first, and then the tape drive ).
Someone replied that the xfer speeds weren't reported, and not calculated for
the slow drives. My cdrom & tape did not negotiate sync xfer speeds, and didnt
report the speeds that was being used. I changed the AlphaBios setup for the
adaptec, and made both try sync negoaiations. Also set termination to bot
high&low ( was set on auto ). On reboot, the cdrom reported a sync xfer rate,
but not the tape drive. BUT i have not had the timeout occure since. I am (
later ) going to undo what i did to see exactly where the prob is.
