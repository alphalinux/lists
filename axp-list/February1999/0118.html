> >I have two questions.
> >
> >1) Does anybody know any tricks for increasing speed on a UDB. (no

For pure CPU horsepower:

Depending on your UDB, you may be able to overclock it. If you have a
soldered-in processor, you can't. Else, you could overclock your existing
processor and/or replace it.

How handy are you with electronics? I have a spare UDB now, so I'm
thinking of looking into the VX40 MB design and seeing if I can't up the
clock rate somehow. A worthless hack, even if I can do it ... it'll
cost me more in my time/effort than a used 233 MHz machine, but buying
gear isn't _nearly_ as fun :)

For a general speed increase/responsiveness:
More/faster RAM will be a big help (RISC needs lots of RAM). A faster
HD, the 340 megger that came with my UDB is painfully slow. My new
HD flies (even though the UDB can't drive it all out).
