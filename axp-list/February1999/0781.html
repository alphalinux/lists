I am not yet an alpha owner/user but I've been lurking on this list for
some weeks to determine the feasability of using a alpha/linux platform.

I'm looking to put together a fairly network intensive application and
have been perusing the source of the various linux NIC drivers.

I was just about set on using the ZNYX 346/348 dual/quad port board
when I found in

src/linux/net/de4x5.c (from 2.2.1)

the following comment:

    I have removed the buffer copies needed for receive on Intels. I cannot
    remove them for Alphas since the Tulip hardware only does longword
    aligned DMA transfers and the Alphas get alignment traps with non
    longword aligned data copies (which makes them really slow). No comment.

I presume that the author meant 32bit for the first longword, and 64bit
for the second longword.

Is this still an issue with tulip-like cards on the Alpha?

Looking at the yellowfin (gigabit) driver it seems that it can both skip
the extra copy and is 64bit aligned by nature of the cards being 64bit
PCI cards. However, the density of ports low (1 per 64bit PCI slot).
