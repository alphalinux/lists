At 04:08 PM 2/11/99 +0200, you wrote:
>Assuming that driver was a module and not directly in the kernel.
>I once tried to compile de4x5 into kernel and only one of my Ethernet
>cards was found. But as a module, they both work fine.

Compile the support into the kernel for the network device. Then in MILO
add a line that looks something like:

ether=irq,base_addr,interface

example: ether=10,0x210,eth0 ether=11,0x300,eth1

On boot, the kernel stops soon as it finds one network device. If there
are two cards of the same family the first card it finds will be the only
card it finds (unless you tell it otherwise).

I also don't think you need to pass the irq and base_address, just a simple
line like ether=eth0 ether=eth1 might work.

Take a look at the Multiple-Ethernet mini-HOWTO, because I am often wrong. :)
