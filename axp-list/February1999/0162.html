Unless my brain has turned to jello lately,

a = (void *) 0;

is the same thing as saying

a = NULL;

and

a = (int *) a;

dereferences null, thus causing your program to go kerfloom.

Perhaps you'd like to explain more clearly what you're trying to do?

Jason
