On Sat, 27 Feb 1999, Bob Taylor wrote:

> I had a thought while reading this post. If you had a floppy with a
> minimal ext2fs / on it and loaded it to ramdisk then chrooted to
> the ramdisk, then you should be able to upgrade (except the kernel).
>
> Do I have a valid thought?

the problem with this theory is that chroot only affects the current
process, so other users who log in will be using the (possibly broken)
system being upgraded. also, init and the assorted daemons won't be
affected.

if you're worried about downtime, your best bet is to upgrade at 4am when
nobody else is using the system. anything else you try is probably more
likely to increase problems rather than decrease them.
