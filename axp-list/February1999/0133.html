On Wed, 03 Feb 1999, Trent Gillespie wrote:
>Within this thread there have been several mentions of overclocking the
>alpha CPU. Has anyone been able to accomplish this (reliably) on any of
>the LX/UX boards, and if so how far?
>

I experimented a bit with running my 533 processor at 600 in an LX board.
Superficially, it seemed to work, however, after about an hour or so, I got Sig
11s when compiling big stuff as the kernel that I never had before. so I wnet
back to 533. With (much) better cooling I assume it will work.

>Also, I found some benchmarks comparing the 21164 600 mhz running Linux
>and Digital Unix. The results of the benchmark were that the DU had a FP
>nearly three times that of Linux. Does that number, if correct, reflect
>any other performance differences b/w the two OSs?

No, these bad numbers reflect differences in **compiler and math lib** quality,
partly already fixed. From the numbers I know, often the *SAME* binary even
runs a few % faster on Linux.
