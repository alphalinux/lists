On Sun, 28 Feb 1999, Michael Tibor wrote:

> On Sat, 27 Feb 1999, B. James Phillippe wrote:
>
> > Hello,
> >
> > Anyone else notice this? I CTRL-ALT-DEL'd my Alpha earlier this evening so
> > I could move it (the monitor was disconnected), and rather than the "beep"
> > from init warning of shutdown followed by the thrashing of the disk to kill
> > processes, the box just instantly cycled! Needless to say, my filesystems
> > weren't too happy about that (and neither was I). Can anyone else confirm
> > this (remount ro or sync first :) I'm running 2.2.1 on an AS200, compiled
> > for Avanti/SRM.
>
> Isn't it init's job to handle that?

It is, assuming the kernel correctly notifies it. However, the kernel may
also opt to simply do a hard cycle of the unit immediately after receiving
ctrl-alt-del from within the keyboard interrupt. Which it does is supposed
to be determined by the last call to reboot(2), which is normally done by
init.
