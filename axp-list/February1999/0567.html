 Actually it's not that bad. Yes, the pins of the USB connector are not con-
nected, but if you look closely, very near (0.5 cm from USB connector) are
pins looking like 4 "V" - those are real USBD0+, USBD0-, USBD1+ and USBD1-
from the Cypress i/o chip. The GROUND and POWER are near by as well. So, ba-
sically you need some resistors, insurance and you have USB. My brother and
I have done it - at least the hardware seems to work. If there will be inte-
rest and provided I will find some time, I can draw the scheme.

 But at the moment (to be precise, I made the last test about a month ago)
it's useless: the USB Open Host Controller Interface Driver has a problem to
find an IRQ for the i/o chip. See my last post to this list for details.
