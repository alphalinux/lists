It is a bit field - by looking at ip.h header but __BYTE_ORDER
has to be equal to one of two things before you will get it.
This should come from some other headers.

> the basic structure of the code is something like:

In situations like that, when you are not sure what compiler is
really getting, try to add '-E' to compilations flags you use,
capture output and check it (collapsing all these blank lines
by filtering it through something like "awk 'NF>0'" often helps
in readability :-). Sometimes things are different than they
look like at the first blush.

The above assumes, of course, that your compiler was not wailing
about redefinitions and the like - what you blissfully ignored.
