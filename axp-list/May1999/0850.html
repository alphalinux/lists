On 24-May-99 Peter Howell Jr wrote:

> When I attempt to su to anything other than root, I get the following
> error.
> shell-init: could not get current directory:

You're doing this from your home directory, probably. What this means is that
the user you su'd to doesn't have permission to read the directory you're in
(in this case, your home directory). Try changing to another directory (one
that's world readable) first.

Example. Here I'm root, in root's home directory:

[root@romulus /root]# su charlet
shell-init: could not get current directory:

charlet can't read root's home directory /root. However,

[root@romulus /root]# cd /
[root@romulus /]# su charlet
[charlet@romulus /]$

Now it works, because charlet can read /
