On Fri, 7 May 1999, Greg Lindahl wrote:

> > DEC blew it and Compaq is not picking up. Two parts of the company,
> > hardware and software, are working against each other. It's amazing.
>
> It's also not true. But there's no point in having a flamewar about it
> on this list; we're trying to be productive here.
>

The way I see it, DEC and Compaq have blown it with Alpha.

>
> They are making their Fortran and C compilers available soon. Please
> give credit where credit is due. This committment was made recently,
> and they haven't even had a chance to not meet it.
>

Linux can run DU binaries now. To run some apps, you need the libraries.
Those have been written. It's all available. It wouldn't take a software
guy half a day to package it and have it on a ftp site. What are they
waiting on? Until I see it, those annoucements are no different than
vaporware. They were made to ride the Linux tide and cover their financial
problems, IMO.

> > For those of us who enjoy the Alpha platform because it is technically
> > superior, it's over.
>
> If it's over, then why are you posting about it?
>

It doesn't make sense for Intel to have 90% of the marketshare with a product
like the Alpha. The reason I'm posting is because I see absolutely no reason
why Compaq can't make the libraries and development environment available
to us now. DEC/Compaq knew about Alpha Linux for a LONG time now.
If Alpha Linux wasn't working until this past Janurary, I would see their
recent annoucement as sincere.

I've used Linux for a long time so waiting for things to work is not new to me.
But, when you have a technically superior platform that's being supported like
someone's redheaded step child, it's just frustrating. There's a reason why
the top two guys at Compaq left... they didn't know what they were doing.

Managers... they can't make the right decisions to get out of a paper bag.
