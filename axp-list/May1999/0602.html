>>> Jeff DeMaagd said:
> On Sun, 16 May 1999, Rich Payne wrote:
>
> > The onboard SCSI on the DS20 and DP264 machines (it's an adaptec 2940 or
> > something like that) is electronically isolated on the current rev of the
> > motherboard. So unfortunatly you can't just enable it :(
>
> What does this mean? Is the chip non functioning? Or some software
> trickery is needed to turn it on? Eventually I'd like to buy a board like
> this, but if there's funny stuff like this happening, I would be looking
> elsewhere. Hopefully you brave people will figure it out before the
> system starts costing a smaller fraction of a compact auto.

The original DP264/DS20 motherboards were designed with the Adaptec 7895 (?)
dual-channel SCSI controller built into the motherboard.

We discovered some problems with using it that has forced us to disable it.
Early motherboards had it active but deprecated, later ones (evidently) have
it more strongly deprecated... ;-}
