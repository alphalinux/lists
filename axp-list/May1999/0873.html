Rui-Tao Dong wrote:
>
> I have a not quite dead (floppyless and diskless) UDB and I am trying
> to make it a dedicated firewall for my cable modem. I need a terminal
> program to talk to serial console.

Mostly anything should work here. Like minicom from your distribution
CD, for example.

> Has anyone got Kermit working?

Oh, sure. As a matter of fact I am using it right now (its telnet
part over ppp).

> I got the (very old) rpm

Just get current sources (7.0.195, I believe) and type 'make linux'.
That is about everything unless you want to change a configuration
a bit (say to switch off a support for Hebrew or Japanese).

Kermit has that kind of licence which prevents putting it in any
kind of a distribution. Get it from
ftp://kermit.columbia.edu/kermit2/test/tar/cku195.tar.gz

> /* Usage link /dev/cua0 */
                  ^^^^^^
            'cua' devices are deprecated for the last 3 years or so.
They will still work with 2.2 kernels but you will get a nastygram
in logs on every open.
