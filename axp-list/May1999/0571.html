On 17 May 1999, Rui-Tao Dong wrote:

> I finally got my warranty replacement notebook drive. It is a Maxtor
> 1.3G slim (E?)IDE drive. I mounted it underneath of the Toshiba 340M
> SCSI. (My floppy died.) The stock 2.0.35.2 kernel from DEC ftp site
> recognized it just fine. After I did hdparm -m 16 /dev/hda, hdparm -t
> /dev/hda gives me about 4.1MB/sec. The original drive reads
> 1.8MB/sec. Is that about normal?

Yep.. that sounds about right to me. I get something like 1.6M/s off
the 340M SCSI drive that shipped with my UDB and 4.7M/s off of the IDE
drive that I put in there. If I don't use hdparm to tweak the drive, I
get like 800k/sec :)

FWIW -- my Multia has the 340M internal SCSI drive in it, a 2.2g IDE
drive, and the floppy. Things are cramped, but I don't have any heat
problems (of course, the IDE drive is used mostly for archival, so it
spins down often).

IDE performance doesn't seem that bad on the Multia. Certainly above
par when compared to the IDE performance of the laptops that I've used;
which I think is a better comparison than to an Intel MB with a Triton
chipset and all.
