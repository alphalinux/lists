[Apologies for the somewhat off-topic post, but this is related to my
Alphastation which serves (among other things) as an FTP site for some
Alpha/Linux files.]

At 4:30PM Eastern today, and lasting until 6PM, someone from
"dialup102-MARK-ITT.ru" (195.161.91.81 and "dialup107-MARK-ITT.ru"
(195.161.91.86) connected to orangesherbert's FTP server 694 times (about 8
times a minute). Since I have a limit of 5 anonymous users allowed on at any
one time, 632 of those requests were rejected. Of the requests that got
through (All 5 available sessions were used at any given time), they all got
varying amounts of one of the precompiled Mozilla binaries available at my site
before disconnecting. I am concerned that, had I not had limits on the number
of connections, this person would have loaded the hell out of my box and
connection.

Question for the experts: Does this look like a denial-of-service attack
attempt, or is the person above using some bastardized version of wget (or a
similar utility) that tries to open a whole bunch of simultaneous connections
to get one file?

(The net result of this event was merely a bunch of extra lines in the logs -
no perceptible slowdown on the Alpha - I was browsing with Mozilla during part
of this. :) )
