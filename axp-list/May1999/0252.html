Albrecht Jacobs wrote:

> What about your SCSI hardware: cables, termination and so on??
[...]
> In another Linux system I got strange messages about bad blocks
> from mke2fs, but: it must be the SCSI hardware, the drive is new and works
> fine in some other Linux system.
> So: check your SCSI hardware!

I have thought about that as well. It's actually rather
interesting/fragile. The tape lives just below a seagate hard drive
that is starting to give me trouble. But the hard drive gets *very* hot
and only at a certain capacity, so I think that it's just flaky (even
though it too is new). The IBM hard drive below the tape is working
fine, as it has been for some years now. I also have a cd-rom burner
that makes the whole subsystem freak out if I move it too close to the
hard drive while it is powered on. It's in an external case that sits
on top of the other devices in another case. As long as there about 1"
of foam padding under the burner, all is well.

It seems unlikely to me that it's a hardware issue with respect to
loading the driver since the code works when not compiled as a module,
but the initial media error that set thing whole thing off may need to
be looked into. Termination, etc, should all be fine - nothing is
terminated except the IBM hard drive sitting at device 0.
