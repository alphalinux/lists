On Mon, 17 May 1999, Chris Wedgwood wrote:

> > At older Laser and TurboLaser multiprocessor systems
> > all CPUs seem to be running at same clock, or one which
> > is in PLL lock to some system wide distributed reference.
>
> Could, in theory, a PLL skip? Thus resulting in smal differences
> after long periods of uptime?

It should be rare, but maybe possible. I didn't ask this question in my
EE clases. But, at least with all the PLLs using the same base clock
should re-synch on every input beat, so even if one was drifting, it'll
get corrected on the next pulse. But then, failures are possible, but
then if it skips, you may be having bigger problems down the road than
'just' clock skew.
