> Ray> Being dutch, I'd like to know what's a 'dutch auction'?
> Ray> Or are you just referring to the place of the happening?
>
> A Dutch Auction is like a game of dare. The price starts high and
> automatically falls over time. The first person to bid gets it at
> whatever the price was when they bid.
> It's a very swift way of selling things with little extrinsic value.
> It will fail for artworks as they have little intrinsic value, but can be
> sold for potentially unlimited ammounts.
> Named after the flower auctions in Holland, which are a sight to behold (in
> particular as two auctions take place simultaniously on the left and right
> of the arena, everyone has two buttons to press!)
>
> Phil

That might be a normal dutch auction but that is not how ebay does
them. Wehn ebay first started you saw alot od dutch auctions but they
have mostly switched to yankee auctions lately. Their version of a
dutch auction has multiple (x) units for sale and the top x bidders
all get to buy at the lowest bid. Say there are 10 motherboards for
sale and ten people bid ranging from 50-500 dollars. At the end of
the auction these ten people all get to buy them at the lowest guys
bid of $50. The difference in the yankee format, if I remember
correctly, is that they all get to pay what they bid at the end.
