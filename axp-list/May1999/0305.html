gatgul@voicenet.com said:
> I for ONE am surprised, anyone else ? BTW may I ask who we is ?
> Digital? Compaq?

There is no more Digital.....there is only Compaq. And it is Compaq that
is porting the compilers, even though the people doing it used to work for
Digital.

jdemaa17@calvin.edu said:
> Will companies be allowed to distribute the output binaries?

Yes, we have always allowed this. The issue of Tru64 Unix has
always been the intellectual property in the libraries. Since the libraries
that the compilers link to are Linux libraries, this is a non-issue.

jdemaa17@calvin.edu said:
> I'd really like to see RedHat and MetroX recompiled using this
> compiler.

Ironically, you might not see the performance that you would think you would
get, particularly inside the kernel. It has been my experience that kernel
programmers often program knowing what the compiler would generate, ergo
an optimizing compiler may or may not generate significant improvements in
speed.

User-level programs are another subject, particularly when the coder is an
"average" coder.

jrc@art-render.com said:
> Out of curiosity, will these compilers use Linux style ELF or retain
> the ECOFF of Tru64?

ELF. When we started the Alpha Linux project, I was fairly animate about
having ELF shared libraries, rather than ECOFF. Intel Linux was ELF, and it
seemed the way the world was going. I figured that we would get more debuggers
and other types of tools if we could at least have the same symbol tables and
linking information that Intel Linux had.

And (of course) when Richard Henderson gave the Alpha Linux project ELF
shared libraries, I was elated......and it is something that I have never,
ever forgotten. It is for this reason that Richard will never, ever have to
pay for a beer in my presence.
