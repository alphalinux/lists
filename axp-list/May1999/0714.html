On Thu, 20 May 1999, Dr . David Alan Gilbert wrote:

> No - you don't need that. Get a recent kernel, turn on PCI-DMA, turn on
> support for which ever controller you have and turn the auto probing for DMA
> on and it should do the trick.

Actually, you do need that (at least on 164SX).

Without the UDMA patch, there IS NO support for the controller I have
(/proc/pci tells me it's Contaq 82C693, but I think it somewhat related to
Cypress 82C693) and the transer rate (measured with hdparm -f -t /dev/hda)
is ~1 MB/sec.

With the patch it grows to just under 6 MB/sec.

Just a side note: An Intel Pentium 233 MMX, Intel AN430TX motherboard and
QUANTUM FIREBALL EL2.5A disk gives me 10.13 MB/sec with hdparm
(2.0.36 kernel).
