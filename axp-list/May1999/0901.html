Generally, when glib et al shafts me, i get a cpp'ed ( preprocessed ) version of the
file/routine that is giving me problems. I then link that routin in with my pgm
& trace or just wait for it to crash.

This would mean:
1) get your current ver of glibc
2) compile all of it - I would not recommend installing, as sometimes thing just dont
work out
3) Touch the file u have problems
4) make 2>&1 | tee compile.log
5) go to that dir where it is compiled in, and redo the gcc/cc/egcs command adding a
-E option.
    a) edit the output file to remove the # line numbers
    b) copy this file to your area.
    c) compile it in.
