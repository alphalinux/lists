At 10:01 20/05/99 +0100, Paul Jakma wrote:
>> That isn't so - it seems like non-Intel processors (including some x86's)
>> do very badly on PIO IDE transfers.
>>
>uhmm.. isn't PIO slow on intel too? is there an architectural reason why an
>intel chip can do PIO faster than, eg, an alpha?

I am not sure - but I've seen similar things on ARM. I'm guessing that
on the Alpha we end up with a loop where each entry in the loop grabs a single
byte/word from the drive and each time it does this it has to slow down,
wiat for the result etc and then carry on.

I suspect on the Intel it does sometype of single instruction buffer copy
and so doesn't have the latency issues - thats just a highly unsubstantiated
guess.

>> >On what kind of intel pc did you get 8-10MB/s, and what kind of
>> >disk?
>>
>> On my Alpha LX board it was giving around 2MB/s with PIO - I got
>> around 6MB/s when I got the DMA going. That is a different IDE chip and
>> needed a particular rune to get the DMA going when building the kernel.
>>
>the lx has the cypress super controller, right? afaik, the ide maintainer
>has an updated test patch with much enhanced support for various ide
>controllers (esp. non-intel ones) including the cypress. might be worth
>trying his patch. Have a look on a linux-kernel archive.

No - you don't need that. Get a recent kernel, turn on PCI-DMA, turn on
support for which ever controller you have and turn the auto probing for DMA
on and it should do the trick.
