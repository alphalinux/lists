>>> James Livingston said:
>
> There have been a number of different SMP
> implementations, starting with the AlphaServer 2100 4/200, but I don't
> believe there have been any that didn't use a common system clock signal.

Yes, this is so, a common clock *to* the CPUs.

And also, each CPU uses a PLL to generate its internal clock from the common
one.

However, because of the nature of PLLs (so I'm told), there can be no
guarantee that the CPUs will operate in lock-step, nor that they will
have cycle-counters that operate at the same frequency. *CLOSE*, but
no guarantee of exactness.
