Hi,

I used a similar (SWXRC230 / Mylex DAC960; 3-ch EISA RAID) under Digital
Unix ,
and had to configure my RAID devices , using a utility that runs from ARC
, like you mentioned.

I had this on a floppy disk , it was called SWXRCMGR
(? Storage Works Raid Controller ManaGeR ?)

Now , after you do this , and compile support for the DAC960 into the
kernel,
the RAID-sets you have created , should be indicated like being single
disks, hangin off
the controller ,

so in your system, there should be 1 disk internal , hanging off the
normal scsi controller,
and 1 disk for every RAID set you've created, hanging off the raid
controller.

(All this is from my Digital Unix / srm / ARC / Hardware knowledge , I
never actually
used this with Linux)

IIRC the SRM firmware actually had support for this hardware raid
controller, making it possible to
have the root disk (and kernel !) on a raid set. Dunno bout linux
though.....
