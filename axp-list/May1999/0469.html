On Fri, 14 May 1999, Chris Price wrote:

> I have a pair of xl300 21164 series machines with on board NCR53c810
> fast scsi-2 controllers. What I did to improve performance on my disk
> subsystem was to put my cdroms, tape backups, and other traditionally
> 'slow' scsi devices on the builtin scsi controller and then went out and
> purchased a QLogic 1040B UW controller for each machine which runs my
> system and user space disks (2xseagate cheetah 9gb). I do however have
> the cheetahs at scsi ID's 0 & 1 on each machine.
>
> I am very pleased with the performance of these machines (especially
> with the 256 bit memory datapath enabled).

256 bit? I thought the XLT has a 128 bit (+ ECC) datapath. Did a miss out
on anything? If so, i would love to know how to enable this.
