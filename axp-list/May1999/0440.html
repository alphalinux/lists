Phil Carmody wrote:
>
> I was under the impression that the priority was in the other order, with a
> asymptote between 7 and 8. So it goes (highest priority) 7 .. 0 15 .. 8.
> The reason for the wraparound/gap being the fact that 8 bit devices can't
> detect devices with ids 8-15 request the bus, so 8-15 must back down.
>
> I can't cite anything to back this up I'm afraid, but I would have assumed
> that the SCSI card would be given the highest priority, and it has Id 7 on
> every SCSI setup I've encountered.
>
> So, put your swap on device with Id 6 etc...

        I stand corrected.

        On a somewhat Alpha related note (simply for discussion -> I am always
looking for critiques/suggestions on how to improve performance);

        I have a pair of xl300 21164 series machines with on board NCR53c810
fast scsi-2 controllers. What I did to improve performance on my disk
subsystem was to put my cdroms, tape backups, and other traditionally
'slow' scsi devices on the builtin scsi controller and then went out and
purchased a QLogic 1040B UW controller for each machine which runs my
system and user space disks (2xseagate cheetah 9gb). I do however have
the cheetahs at scsi ID's 0 & 1 on each machine.

        I am very pleased with the performance of these machines (especially
with the 256 bit memory datapath enabled).

        Comments/suggestions? (other than 'QLogic sukz' or 'Seagate sukz' :) )

        Chris
