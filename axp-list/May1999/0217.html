On Thu, 6 May 1999, Maurice Hilarius wrote:

>
  It might take a while. A board with 2 or 4MB of board cache, and a 64 bit
  CPU, and a 128 or 256 bit memory path uses chips that simply cost more.
  
  The CPUs are not the issue. A 21164A 533MHz CPU is under $400 cost. Yes, it
  actually costs less than a pentium II 450MHz.

cheaper even, i just got a Samsung 21164A for £149 UK pounds
(exclusive of taxes), ie $243, from an official samsung dealer
(compusys). As a comparison: P11 400MHz chips are about £210, P11 450
£355, P111 500 £520.

The motherboard (samsung ux) was £452 -> $737 which is
about the same price as a high-end Intel GX board.

So the Alpha EV5x is definitely competitive with intel in the
high-end, and perhaps even in the high-midrange...
