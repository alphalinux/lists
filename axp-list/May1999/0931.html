We already do a lot of stuff far better than Solaris. Support for
legacy hardware is one (as anyone who's had a sparc IPX "upgraded"
from SunOs to Solaris by their friendly company sysadmin will tell
you). Linux boxes have a long usable life, and dusty old hardware can
be given a new lease of life by porting to linux.

The most important (IMHO) we _don't_ support are:
* Support for very high end hardware
* H/A
* High end filesystems

The introduction of either XFS or ext2+ (ext3?) will address the last
issue. I believe (although I'm not sure why) that some proprietary
linux HA solutions exist. Support for very large memory, very high
numbers of processors etc is going to be considerably more painful.
Alan Cox (for one) has gone on record saying that we should stick to
the small stuff and let people use Irix if they want lots of
processors.

Sean
