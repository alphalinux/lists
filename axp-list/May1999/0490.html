On Sun, May 16, 1999 at 08:42:08AM -0400, Uncle George wrote:
>
> does anyone know why redhat 6.0 is up in the high $70's. Do they
> redistribute any of the profits to the developers of the software they
> publish on their compilation CD ?

Because they are a business, and a good business makes profits.
Also, at $70 for an OS, you're at a fraction of fair market for
anything approaching an equivalent OS solution...
Okay, they didn't author the software, but they did integrate it.

If I had to download, compile, install, test (integrate) all of
the packages that go into any distro, it would cost my company
1000's of dollars of my time at each release.
Add to that any level of tech support (so that other admins can bug
RedHat instead of me sometimes) and you have a real bargain.

How do they spend this money?
They're investing in corporate support, RHCE, metalab, E, Alan Cox...
Things like RHCE and support don't mean alot to me, but they do
to many companies. Even if I never use them, their very existence
adds to the commercial viability of Linux (which does mean alot to me).

RedHat offers the sugar-coating that lets corporate America swallow Linux.
