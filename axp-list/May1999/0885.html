Ok, here is how the jumper setting work.....I figured this out by looking
in the manual. If you consider that for a particular jumper, if it is on,
assign it binary 0, if it is off, assign it binary 1. If you look at the
settings for 466:

IRQ3: 0 (In)
IRQ2: 1 (Out)
IRQ1: 1 (Out)
IRQ0: 1 (Out)

Ok, so its 0111, 533 is 1000, 600 is 1001.....got the pattern? Just count
up in binary, and each step is 66 MHz. 667 -> 1010, 733 -> 1011. 800 ->
1100, etc....
Not that I suggest even trying past 600MHz!!! I have mine running at 667
only because I ending up putting a modified EV6 heatsink on my EV56!!! Heat
was a major concern!!!
