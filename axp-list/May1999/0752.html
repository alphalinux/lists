From: Robert Bowles, rbowles@op.net, Thursday, May 20, 1999 5:37 AM
>
> On Thu, May 20, 1999 at 09:49:58AM +0800, Bernard Chew, Lai Wan wrote:
> >
> > Do the Alpha PC164 motherboards use standard ATX power supplies and
> > enclosures?
>
> Not really...
> If you're talking about PC164LX and PC164SX motherboards,
> (slightly differnt creatures from the older PC164)
> yes you can use a standard ATX power supply and enclosure.
>
> If you're talking about a PC164 (proper), they use a standard
> enclosure and ATX power, but they don't support the ATX "softswitch",
> so there's no way to turn it on. :(
>
> In order to use a modern power supply, you have to short
> the ground to some other pin (I forget which) on the supply
> (or on the board (for the adventurous)). You should be able to
> find this out from the ATX spec.
>
> One vendor (http://www.omnistep.org/~advantag) used to sell
> PC164 motherboards with the proper pins shunted on the board.
> They're currently sold out, but you could ask them for info...
>
> In any event, be careful and double check your work _and_
> anything anyone tells you. Remember, if your machine bursts
> into flames, it is ultimately your responsibility...
>

Make sure what you have is a PC164. Not a PC164LX and not a
PC164SX. Try the supply without making any mods first, if it
does not work it will not do any harm, and if it works you do
not need to do the mod. My board has a wire soldered to the
power connector as shown below. Use at your own risk.

           +12V DCOK GND +5V GND +5V GND +3V +3V
             O O O O O O O O O O
                                       _
                                      / \
             O O O O O O O O O O
            +5V +5V -5V GND GND GND GND -12V +3V
