Peter Howell Jr wrote:
>
> Quick question.
>
> When I attempt to su to anything other than root, I get the following
> error.
>
> shell-init: could not get current directory:

Most likely it cannot get current directory :-) (because you did that
from a directory readable to root but not to an account you try to
"su"). If you do instead 'su - <whomever>' then the resulting process
will inherit the whole environment from <whomever>, including its home
directory, so the problem will solve itself. In nearly all situations
this "su -" is actually a good idea.
