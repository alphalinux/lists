On Thu, 13 May 1999, Dan O'Reilly wrote:

> I've installed RH 6.0 on my Multia, on a primary partition. Now, I would
> like to move it to a completely different disk, and put it either in another
> primary partition, or onto a logical partition in an extended partition.
> Is there some magic for doing this? I have WNT 4.0 in the primary partition
> on the disk I'm moving it to.

Just recursively copy everything. Mount the new disk on /mnt and cp -R
everything except mnt in /. Then go to the new disk and change the stuff
in /etc/fstab to reflect the new root device and such.

I did this out of curiousity the other day when I installed an IDE drive
in my Multia.. then I booted the IDE disk and everything went great.

You'll need to set up your autoboot set up of course.. for me, I just
changed the command line passed to MILO (I use ARC to jumpstart MILO).

Worked like a charm. I expected having more trouble than that.. which is
partly why I did it -- I was bored and figured I may as well cause a few
problems so I can learn something :)
