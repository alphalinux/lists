I just put one of these together, and it is working like a dream, except I
haven't figured out how to do a hands-off way to boot. In effect, I want to
know how to boot directly from the hard disk? I want to remove the floppy
drive from this computer.

This is of course a AlphaBIOS machine. I've read the docs on alphalinux.org,
but none seemed to tell me what to do in this configuration.

Right now I am doing the following:
1) Use the AlphaBIOS OS loader to load linload.exe from the floppy and get MILO
   up and running.
2) Change floppies and do:
MILO> boot fd0:vmlinux.gz root=/dev/hda1

I can probably get this onto one disk and have the boot command passed to MILO
automatically. My objections to this are:
1) Floppies are slower than a snail
2) I don't have many extra floppy drives around, so I want floppyless machines
3) Upgrading is more work...mount the floppy, copy the kernel, wait for it to
   sync, unmount.
4) Only one kernel will fit on a floppy which has the linload data on it.

Don't get me wrong, I don't plan on rebooting often, but I also don't like
imperfections. What are my options? Yes, I will be going SCSI soon.
