DEC blew it and Compaq is not picking up. Two parts of the company,
hardware and software, are working against each other. It's amazing.
One of the best ideas, the Alpha processor, in the industry has stagnated
completely because the companies that have owned the technology is set
on making the little money they can on software instead of unloading the
hardware. What is stopping Compaq from making Alpha optimized development
software available right now? Man, it's pretty clear to me why DEC fell
apart, and it's pretty clear why Compaq is, also. I see the same thing
happening with Sun and Java. You know, all the great mathematicians and
scientists are remembered for the work they did. Very few of them made
money -- the way Bill Gates is making money -- on the incredible work they
did. Yet, they still retain intellectual rights to their work. You don't
see anyone else but Newton and Leibnez being credited for calculus. The
list goes on.

For those of us who enjoy the Alpha platform because it is technically
superior, it's over. I don't see Compaq ever making the right decisions
with the platform. That boat has long sailed. Ugh. Really frustrating.
If you want optimized performance, looks like Merced is the way to go.
At least Intel is helping develop an optimized compiler that will
eventually be freely available to the public for free through gcc.

It's been made clear here that it is easier to rewrite gcc for alpha
than to try to optimize it as is. We are still waiting for Compaq to
make their development environment available to us.
