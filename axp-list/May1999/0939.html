Our smallest server comes with a 3x9 raid 5 array and 512 meg memory and
two processors. I agree 8 processors and 4 gig of swap space may be a
bit high end for linux, but i feel we should eventually work in that
direction.

In my opinion, one of the strongest aspects of Linux (don't flame me
here) is the ability to run on inexpensive hardware. A dual processor
HP server runs for much more than a dual processor pc server. if we can
work with the same hardware as other brands of unix, than customers can
get the benefit of having Unix AND cheap hardware (rather than having
cheap hardware and then the negating M$ tax).

our short term goal should be easy linux on the destop, but we should
not loose sight of the SMP-RAID market.
