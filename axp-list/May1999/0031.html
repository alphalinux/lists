Hello!

> Uh... What is this about 128kbps? Is it too slow to compress, do you
> think the quality is too low?
The audio quality is poor.

> I know audio people that can't tell the difference between 128k and any
> higher rate. I have a hearing impairment, so 128k does just fine.
In this case case good for you but in general 128kbps sound real bad (with
ISO based codecs).

> If it is the speed issue, real-time compression is easy with a new Alpha
> (533 21164A or better on bladeenc).
BladeEnc is not the best choice. As said before, encode is much more
optimized, especially optimized for Alpha, and is available as source. When
compiling encode you can also use Compaq's portable fast math library which
gives another performance gain.
But enyway the resulting audio quality is still not good enough for
128kbps...
