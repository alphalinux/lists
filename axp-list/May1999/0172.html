On Thu, May 06, 1999 at 07:15:01PM +0200, Kai Schmidt wrote:
> I have a little problem with one of my multia's. I got the machine two weeks
> ago, I installed the OS everything was ok, suddenly the machine was
> dead, no
> ping no telnet. After rebooting I was able to connect via telnet but after a
> few keystrokes it turned dead again. Only this time a reboot didn't help.

The same thing happened to my Multia a few weeks ago. One day, I
opened it up to put in a PCI Ethernet card (another DEC Tulip card).
I booted it, it worked just great. I had eth0 and eth1, did the
routing thing I needed it to, and decided I didn't need the card
in there any more. I took the card out. I put the case back on
and plugged the machine back into its normal place in the room.

Two days later it was dead... no video output, no network, no
ping, no keyboard (couldn't use SysRq)--dead. But power supply
was still on (with fan). I rebooted, let it fsck, and it stayed
up for another day or two.

I got really worried, so I took it apart and left the case off
to see if the fan by the CPU heatsink was working. It was. The
CPU was quite cool, but it would still die off after a day or
so of use (usually in the middle of the night).

5 days had passed since it first died, and I decided to unplug it,
slide the guts tray out, reseat every component I could (including
the power supply connector to the board) and put it back together
solidly.

I quickly booted and built a new kernel (I have no idea if the old
kernel contributed to the problems, but I doubt it seeing as
video output went away), 2.2.7pre4 with the Alpha 48-day uptime
patches.

I've had zero problems since. The machine has been up for 7 days.
I think it was the reseat of all the connectors (power especially)
that did it. I should mention that I had this machine for a good
6 months before these problems, and not once did it not work as
expected.
