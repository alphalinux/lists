On Wed, 5 May 1999, Tommy C. King wrote:

> Well it's using BIOS 2.20, even upgraded the BIOS, when at times
> (uncertain), the kernel access the ATLAS-2 harddisk, it RESTARTS the system
> for no reason, and the harddisk is corrupted, because files had not been
> close and some of the data is in buffers.

I've seen my Adaptec do a restart when it got upset at some SCSI problem -
this went away completely when I went upto the 2.2.x kernels - but your
using those already.

I think your taking the right track - the Symbios/NCR cards are much
happier at dealing with problems and stability.

> I am running a mission critical application at a colocationed ISP. I am
> replacing it with a EIDE if I can't get funding....any ideas? I had to see
> 28% CPU usage just to write to disk....(Now it's a cool 7%)...

If you can persuade the DMA to kick in then this might not be bad - a nice
fast EIDE disc will cost you less than an OK SCSI disc.
