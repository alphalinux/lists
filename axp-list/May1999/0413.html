mgraffam@idsi.net wrote:
>
> Just recursively copy everything. Mount the new disk on /mnt and cp -R
> everything except mnt in /.

Careful! 'cp -R' will change time-stamps, expand links and things like
that. You really want 'cp -a' which happens to be the same as 'cp -dpR'.
Actually you may even want 'cp -ax', depending on your layout, although
you will have to repeat that for every file system separately.
You definitely _do not_ want to copy a contents of /proc. :-)

'cp --help' will tell you more even without looking at cp docs;
and there are some other ways to copy full file systems even
if only less capable 'cp' is available ('cpio' is an old stand-by,
for example).
