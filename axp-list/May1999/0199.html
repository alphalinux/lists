Hello all,

I'd like to thank everyone who responded to me and especially
rth@twiddle.net for trying to help figure out why my binutils package was
not performing. I'd like to say that I've successfully now figured it
out! The problem appears to have been a rogue glibc2.1 installation into
/usr/lib. I noticed that there were some glibc2.1 type files in that
directory and removing them has solved the problem.

Specifically what was wrong I cannot say. libbfd.so lives in /usr/lib
instead of /lib; and binutils is the only package that depends on libbfd,
making me think that was the problem. What appears to have happened,
though, is libbfd itself, having lived in /usr/lib, first looked in
/usr/lib for libc.so, despite ldd reporting it looking in /lib for libc.

I believe this to be improper behavior, but I would not consider myself
expert enough to make such a determination. /lib is listed before
/usr/lib in my ld.so.conf file, and my guess is that once /lib had been
exhausted and /usr/lib was searched (for libbfd) it resumed at that point
when trying to resolve libc, rather than restarting at the beginning of
the search tree.

Would anyone care to comment on this assertation? Such a change may want
to be propogated up the tree if it is persisting in glibc 2.1 (which I do
not want to even *think* about trying to install again!) =)

Christopher
