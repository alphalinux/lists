Ok...people have said some slightly less than pleasant things about
the Adaptec cards.

Here's my 5 cents. I think it's heat-related.

Whenever our backup server(PowerCenter, MacOS, running Retrospect
with 2940UW to AIT drive) gets too hot, the card freezes up; MacOS
doesn't crash, and turning off the tape drive+back on again seems to
"reset" the card. This happens very quickly, within about 10-15,
maybe 30 minutes of operation.

Symptom is that the red LED(bus activity indicator) stays on continuously.

Now, DEC alphas don't exactly run cool. In fact, they run really,
really hot. If you had problems with your adaptec card, try leaving
the side access panel off, and aim one of those little
fans(Duracraft, Vornado, or the like) into the interior, particularly
at the PCI cards(you may want to use a larger box fan to keep the
drive bays cool, but I find that even a little Duracraft fan moves a
ton of air in the general vicinity, enough to keep my Barracuda just
a little above room temp.)

I'd be interested to hear if anyone tries this. I haven't heard much
in the way of complains on the AppleshareIP list, probably because
most of the systems being used for ASIP(9500, 8600, 9600, G3, B+W G3)
have a rather large(5 inch) low speed fan that sits right on top of
the PCI cards, moving air between them(an absolute must for high-end
video cards!) Not to mention, PowerPC machines run cooler because,
among other things, the CPU uses much, much less power...
