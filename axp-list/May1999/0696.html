Quick question. Since I can't seem to find a premade ISO image of the
RedHat 6.0/Alpha distribution, I've decided to bite the bullet and
make my own. I started today by downloading the 6.0 alpha tree, and
now I'm to where I need to know how to make the disk image.

I found a "Burning a RedHat CD" mini-HOWTO, that has a section called
"Creating a bootable CD". It says (for a 5.2 distrib) to

   mkisofs --v -r -T -J -V "Red Hat 5.2" -b images/boot.img -c misc/boot/boot.cat -o /tmp/redhat.img .

I'm sure this works for Intel, but does this also produce an Alpha-bootable
CD? Since I'm taking the time to make a CD, I figure I might as well make
it bootable.
