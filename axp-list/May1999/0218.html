On Fri, 7 May 1999, Wes Bauske wrote:

  Actually, it's not a wrong instruction. If a scsi bus is not
  terminated correctly, you get some sort of signal reflection
  which I guess is noise and that causes bad things to happen.
  
  The HW folks could explain it better than I can. But, it is
  necessary to use active termination on both ends of the bus
  to keep the signal clean.
  

There's an excellent article in the Apr98 Digital Technical Journal
about SCSI:

http://www.digital.com/info/DTJR00/

it has some in-depth coverage of hot-swap issues. good read.
  
The physical form of the interface is extremely critical to
minimising the effect of a hot-swap on the bus.
