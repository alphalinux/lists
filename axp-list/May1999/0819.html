On Sun, 23 May 1999, Charles E Taylor IV wrote:

> I went the SVGA to NTSC converter route. Found a cheap converter on
> the net so > I could use the TV to display gqmpeg's interface (this
> uses less CPU than x11amp, and on a multia you need to save all the
> CPU time you possibly can if you want to play mp3s).

Yeah, decoding hi-quality mp3 pushes the Multia right to the wall.

> > Anyone have a Matrox card in their Multia? How is mpeg video playback?
>
> No slot. I'd expect the Multia to be pretty dismal at it, though,
> based on my experience with mine for playing back mpeg audio.

Well, I can decode the VCD's ok on the Multia -- my problem is that the
TGA card isn't quite fast enough for a good looking picture, and of course
it is 8 bit. Going with a 16 or 24 bit color depth will make the Multia
push more data along the bus, but it saves the CPU time used for
dithering each frame -- I would think this would work out ok, but I'm
not sure.
