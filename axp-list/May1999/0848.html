On Mon, 24 May 1999, Peter Howell Jr wrote:

  
  When I attempt to su to anything other than root, I get the following
  error.
  
  shell-init: could not get current directory:
  
  
  I obviously have something set incorrectly, but I don't know what it is.
  I'm running RH5.2 on an alphastation 200

it means what it means. that the shell didn't have permission to
access that directory.

happens if as root you cd to /non/public/accessable/directory
and you then su to a user who isn't allowed to read that directory.
the shell tries to find out it's PWD and can't.

no harm though.
