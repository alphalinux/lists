On Wed, 5 May 1999, Tommy C. King wrote:

> Ask DMA to kick in? How? I compared the thru put of the EIDE and the SCSI,
> it's not that good.

That depends on the motherboard; for an LX its just a matter of asking for
it when you compile the kernel (turn on a lot of options, including the
CMD-646 stuff, PCI-DMA etc).

I get better performance this way than out of my Seagate Barracuda.

> How should I go about to do that, will a RAID-0 help? I have 2 old 3.2 EIDE
> and they had serve me way longer than I can remember :-).

That should be OK - get hold of the MD tools, and build the kernel with MD
support - and its easy to get it going from there.
I'd put the two discs on separate IDE channels.
