>>>>> "Chris" == Chris Price <cprice@its.to> writes:
Chris> I have tape drive (travan, and DAT) in various machines at varying SCSI
Chris> id's in the 4,5,6 range. AFAIK, the scsi bus prioritizes i/o requests
Chris> based on scsi id (please correct me if I am wrong here guys/gals) with 0
Chris> receiving a higher priority than 1, 1 than 2 and so on. So on a mixed
Chris> bus of hard drives and tape units, it is 'better'(but not required) to
Chris> have lower priority system devices such as tapes and cdroms sitting at
Chris> higher numbers (such as 4,5 or 6).

I was under the impression that the priority was in the other order, with a
asymptote between 7 and 8. So it goes (highest priority) 7 .. 0 15 .. 8.
The reason for the wraparound/gap being the fact that 8 bit devices can't
detect devices with ids 8-15 request the bus, so 8-15 must back down.

I can't cite anything to back this up I'm afraid, but I would have assumed
that the SCSI card would be given the highest priority, and it has Id 7 on
every SCSI setup I've encountered.

So, put your swap on device with Id 6 etc...
