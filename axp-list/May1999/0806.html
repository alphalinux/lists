I used to support an application (Telcom, 24x7, etc...) that had 2 Gig
of swap space under Solaris (we had 512 Meg RAM in the server and
4 different SCSI chains, you do the math) - everything was fine until
a Sun Tech. stopped by to look at the system and just laughed! He
had never seen such swap size outside of Wall Street, but they had
truely large RAM...

Bottom line, that is a WHOLE lot of swap, and if you have Gigabytes
of RAM, and are running a truely *staggering* amount of applications
it *could* make sense...
