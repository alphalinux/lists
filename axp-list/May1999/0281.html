Dan O'Reilly wrote:
>
> At 05:40 PM 5/11/99 -0300, ALEXANDRE A. P. SUAIDE wrote:
> >Hello,
> >
> >I have an alpha station 255 (avanti) and I would like to upgrade
> >its RAM. The compaq seller here charge me about US$800 each 64 MB
> >module. It is too expensive. I would like to know if PC memories
> >works fine. If its works, what kind of memory I should buy?
>
> You need 60ns true parity fast-page-mode (FPM) memory 72-pin. I just
> bought 2 32MB modules here in the US for about $150. There are those
> who say 70ns is the correct speed, but I'm running 112mb of 60ns in my
> AS 200 and it's running just fine.

Among those who say this are the sustaining engineering group at Digital
(er, Compaq) who support the Avanti. The Avanti was designed at a time
when 70 nsec. memory was considered fast; I'm not sure, but there may
not even have been any such thing as 60 nsec. DRAM at the time, which
would make it very hard to design a system to work with it :-) Anyway,
the Official Word is that 60 nsec. memory isn't guaranteed to work (nor
guaranteed not to work), so you'll do best to stick with 70 nsec. parts
unless you can afford to experiment.

Dan is right about fast page mode; EDO won't work. Also, you want 36-bit
(true parity), not 32-bit SIMMs. These are installed in pairs, giving a
64-bit datapath with 8 check bits, sufficient to do ECC. IIRC, you can
also use 33-bit SIMMs, if you find any, for longword parity. The only
Alpha I'm aware of that runs with no check bits on the memory is 164SX.
