> Uh... I don't know if this is your problem.. but the
> stuff in RH6.0 is linked with glibc 2.1 ... NOT glibc
> 2.0 which is what RH 5.2 comes with. If you didn't
> upgrade your glibc, then most packages in RH 6.0 won't
> run properly. You have two solutions, recompile the
> SRPMs from RH 6.0 so that they link with the glibc you
> have now.. or upgrade glibc to 2.1. (note, upgrading
> glibc to 2.1 may break other glibc 2.0 programs.. )

You were right, thank you very much. I changed back to glibc 2.0 (and
downgraded all the other rpms that I had installed from RH 6.0) and
everything is working well again. This scares me about updating to RH 6.0! I
have a system that has many packages that were compiled from source and are
not registered in the rpm database (KDE comes to mind as well as all the
kernel related stuff).

Any advice on how to go about to update to RH 6.0? Initially I was thinking
that I could do an as needed package by package update, now doesn't seem
such a good idea anymore ;^) What have you done to upgrade from 5.2?

Thank you,
