> does anyone know why redhat 6.0 is up in the high $70's. Do they
> redistribute any of the profits to the developers of the software they
> publish on their compilation CD ?

They employ a number of developers, and all their code is GPL'd. These
people are paid out of their profits, naturally.

The "Official Red Hat" CD includes phone support; they also sell a CD
and manual without such support for half the price. And, you can just
download RH 6.0 and pay nothing, or buy one of the cheap CD's various
companies have put together (harder to do the latter for Alpha, though).
Or whatever.

> Upgrades to windows (95,98,NT) are in the $90 ( if u look real hard )

Have you priced Windows source code recently? Installation support?
You're comparing apples to oranges. You can get RH 6.0 for free, with
Red Hat's blessing (the GPL), if you have the bandwidth and the time.
Or copy a friends CD. Or clone an existing installation. All legally.
And Red Hat expects most people to do these things.

Red Hat isn't a charity. They're in this to make money. If you have
a problem with that, don't give them yours. You can use their product
anyway, and not pay them. Or use another distro. Or assemble your own.
You can make your own distro and and sell it. Or copy and resell Red
Hat's.

Isn't free software wonderful?
