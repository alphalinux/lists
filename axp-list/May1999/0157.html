Hi,

Another question to field..

I have a recently purchased UX machine, 512MB RAM with RH 5.2, whose
kernel I upgraded to 2.2.6. This machine seems to be randomly and
silently killing peoples' tasks, both CPU-intensive numerical models
and idle xterm sessions. Of course, I haven't been able to reproduce
it, so I'm at a loss to explain the problem. This machine has two
ethernet cards, the builtin one and an Intel Etherexpress 100. There
is a lot of nfs traffic over both cards (some of which seems to cause
the machine to spontaneously reboot on heavy loads, but that's a
different story), but the killing doesn't seem to be related to network
load.

Sitting next to this machine is an LX that has been operating for over
a year with no problems. I initially upgraded to 2.2.x on this
machine, but then backed off to 2.1.132 when 2.2 seemed flaky.
Unfortunately, I can't seem to get 2.1.132 to even compile on the UX.
We need 2.[12].* kernels, because we're using DU 4 binaries (we have
licenses).

Has anyone seen anything like this? Nothing shows up in the logs when
tasks die.
