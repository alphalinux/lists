At 09:49 27/05/99 -0400, you wrote:
>I have a AS200 4/233 that I am running RH5.1 on.
>
>I am trying to install RH6.0 on it. I go to MILO and use the
>command:
>
>boot -t iso9660 scd0:kernels/generic.gz root=/dev/scd0
>
>to boot off of the cdrom. Things work OK though loading the
>image, but then it locks up with the last two lines being:
>
>Freeing unused kernal memory: 168K Freed
>
>Warning: Unable to open an inital console.

Yep - the same thing happened to me; use the boot floppies
isntead - that worked.

Oh - and while I'm here - other bugs in RH6.0 Alpha upgrade:

   1) If you have mounts in your /etc/fstab for places other than
      the root directory (e.g. /discs/deskapps) it fails to mount
      and completely falls over in the upgrade. Just edit your
      fstab to get rid of everything except / and /usr and /var

   2) I think it set the date to 2017 while installing - tsk.
      (yee olde Arc clock setting problem)
