"Ray Kiesbrink (DSN)" wrote:
>
> > Every problem I've had with Adaptec controllers and Linux have been
> > solved by replacing a passive SCSI terminator at the end of my SCSI chain
> > with an active terminator. On at least three different PC systems from
> > 386 to dual Pentium MMX, XT, ISA and PCIbus...
> >
> >
> Maybe you could place a HD at the end of your scsi chain, these provide better (active) termination.
> saves a buck or so :)
>

        HD's IMO do **NOT** provide proper termination. I have had several
experiences with HD's set to provide active termination and having
multiple errors on that bus. All of these problems were fixed by
purchasing an active terminator.

        As far as cost goes, I cannot see why you would suggest that a HD would
save a buck or two. The last SCSI2 active terminator I bought ( an
Amphenol) connector was about ~$25.00 CDN dollars.

        I would assume that a SCSI-3/UW Active terminator would be maybe twice
as much??

        Chris
