> > Okay, the 875 just isn't working out with Red Hat 6.0. It always times
> > out after the scsi bus resets. So how do I enable the onboard scsi?

> Hm, that's a good question. If just connecting the cables to the

>

>
> to see if anything sticks out. But since you boot from the floppy for
> installation, SRM shouldn't get in your way.

The onboard SCSI on the DS20 and DP264 machines (it's an adaptec 2940 or
something like that) is electronically isolated on the current rev of the
motherboard. So unfortunatly you can't just enable it :(

I've run into the same problem with the time outs, and actually put the
disks on an adaptec
2940 to get it installed. You may be able to build your own boot kernel
for this, using the sym driver, I didn'y try because I ran out of time.
