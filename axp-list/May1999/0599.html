>>>>> "Franco" == Franco Tassone <franco@dns.rgn.it> writes:

Franco> Unfortunatelly, time_t in Irix is defined as long (4 bytes), while time_t
Franco> in AlphaLinux is 8 bytes long, though defined as long.
Franco> It seems to me that time/date info stores differently in a time_t
Franco> variable, from SGI to Linux.

This is a red herring. The time stamps have the same numeric value, even if they
are represented differently. The 32 most significant bits of the time_t on
an alpha will be zero (until 4 decades time) and the lowest 32 bits will be
identical. The actual storage of the date in the RTC (real time clock)
hardware on the motherboard is the only place where different values will
be found, but the OS hides that (using standard C structures/types like
time_t).
