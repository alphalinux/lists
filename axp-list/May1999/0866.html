OK, well the kerneld/kmod mechanism (which looks at /etc/conf.modules)
was primarily designed to load modules on demand. If you genuinely
was just to load it at boot, you could do worse than to add "insmod plx"
to your boot scripts.

However, if your module provides a device, you could use something
like this in your /etc/conf.modules

alias char-major-73 plx
...for a char device with major number 73 or "block-major" for a block
device. Then the "plx" module will be loaded when anyone accesses the
device. If you have a daemon that talks to the device and you start
that on boot, the module should be autoloaded.

Good Luck!
