*maybe* Samsung. Definately not AMD.

AMD just licensed the ev6 bus for their processors and chipsets, no other
parts of the archatecture. Plus, they're moving to remove that bus and
just go with an HT (HyperTransport) connect coming straight out of their
CPUs. They're also desperately trying to dump their chipset biz to the 3rd
party vendors to save costs.

Samsung is still doing *some* stuff with Alpha. Most of the UP* series was
designed @ API (who has dumped Alpha entirely now). The only exceptions
are the UP1100 (phasing out if not already phased out), and the UP1500.
The UP1500 may never see a huge light of day because it uses an AMD761
chipset. Unless they can swap it to a Via or another 3rd party vendor's
chipset that emulates the true AMD chipset, they're out of luck. At the
very least if they do this, it means a major re-spin of the board which
will take some time and may not be justified w/ the shortening life of
Alpha.

With CPQ shooting itself in the foot w/ marketing and trying to get the
bullet out w/ a pair of pliers instead of seeing a real doctor, it may
just all be water under the bridge at this point.
