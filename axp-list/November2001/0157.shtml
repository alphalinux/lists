> Don't think so. Have you tried to run 'swriteboot'?
> Can you boot from the CD but switch the -fl root=/dev/hda to root=/dev/sda*
> where sda(1,2,4,5) is the / partition from your install?
>
> >> Redhat defaults to DOS disk labels if there is no previous BSD label on
> >>the disks.
>
> Why is this anyway? They don't support Alphabios/MILO, so it would seem
> logical to assume people are using SRM which requires BSD labels. Or did
> this start with 7.0?

--> when I started messing around back w/ 5.2 on alpha it was this way
too.. back then it made sense to do it though. Typically you were starting
off with a system that had been previously installed w/ Tru64 (DEC UNIX
back then) or VMS. So... it was a quick and dirty way to get the
disklabels correct for people who didn't know as much of what they were
doing. Unfortunately, when 7.0 came out and ONLY supported SRM/BSD they
never changed it. It wouldn't be a bad thing, considering that the only
supported systems are SRM based now, and that if you wanted to try the
whole MILO deal you could just use fdisk natively and swap em to DOS based
partitions in the installer.
