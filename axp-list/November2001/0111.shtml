n1pfc@isobutane.net wrote:
> *maybe* Samsung. Definately not AMD.

Yeah, I know, AMD doesn't have the resources.

> AMD just licensed the ev6 bus for their processors and chipsets,
> no other parts of the archatecture.

But, in addition, I thought Alpha had to find another licensee before
Intel finallly about them out a few years back and AMD was it?

> Plus, they're moving to remove that bus and just go with an
> HT (HyperTransport) connect coming straight out of their
> CPUs.

Yes, I know. But what are they doing inside? I wouldn't be surprised
if the new, integrated memory controller (let alone the cache
controller) is very much based on recent EV designs.

> They're also desperately trying to dump their chipset biz to the 3rd
> party vendors to save costs.

And HyperTransport is an _excellent_ means of doing this. AMD, great
job! They really hit Intel blindside with this approach. SiS and
nVidia already have their technology in use in their chipsets.

> Samsung is still doing *some* stuff with Alpha.

Yeah, I'm still curious. You'd figure that they'd still be able to use
the existing cores and shrink to new feature sizes -- but I know its a
_lot_ more complicated than that. Too bad Intel just didn't buy out
Alpha back in the mid-'90s. I mean, thanx to Digital's FX!32 (and the
corresponding one for Linux), Intel could have probably gotten better
x32 compatibility and performance out of binary translation, combined
with the Alpha's performance thanx to its simplicity.

God I loved Alpha. It was RISC to an "anal-level," just right IMHO.

> Most of the UP* series was designed @ API (who has dumped Alpha
> entirely now).

Yep. Hopefully API Networks will become the "premier" engineering
component for HyperTransport.

> The only exceptions are the UP1100 (phasing out if not already phased
> out), and the UP1500. The UP1500 may never see a huge light of day
> because it uses an AMD761 chipset. Unless they can swap it to a Via or
> another 3rd party vendor's chipset that emulates the true AMD chipset,
> they're out of luck. At the very least if they do this, it means a major
> re-spin of the board which will take some time and may not be justified
> w/ the shortening life of Alpha.

You'd figure they could make a HyperTransport version and then API
Networks could come out with an HT memory controller bridge? I guess
I'm thinking too far "out-of-the-box" on what Samsung can do.

> With CPQ shooting itself in the foot w/ marketing and trying to
> get the bullet out w/ a pair of pliers instead of seeing a real
> doctor, it may just all be water under the bridge at this point.

Yeah. Again, I think we'll miss Alpha.
