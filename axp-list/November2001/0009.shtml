Just getting in from a day on the road..so there may already be a number of
comments regarding who's systems and architecture have been driving Intels'
fab processes.

This really isn't news. Intel has been one of DEC/Compaqs reference
accounts for many...many years. The hardware along with VMS Clustering is a
nearly bullet-proof combination, so it's a perfect combination for
environments that require 99.999% uptime. Seems to me (don't quote me..it's
been many years) that when I first hired on with DEC in 1986, Intel,
Honeywell, HP and others relied on VMS and the VAX line for their HR, MRP
(though not referred to as MRP at that time) and other critical applications.
