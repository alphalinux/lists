But MIPS has always been a niche market. It would be nice to see them

> > (What ever happened to Transmeta/Carusoe? -- $2.60; down from a 52 week

> IIRC they are having quality problems, and are facing strong competition
> in the low-power market, e.g. Intel's Mobile Celeron.

I just purchased a Sony Picturebook with the Crusoe 667MHz. I would take
this against anything from the Intel line for performance and battery life.
Well, actually for performance while on battery power and battery life.

The speedstep Intel processors never seem to mention that you lose 1/3 or
more of your performance running on batteries. From my benchmarks I would
actually say 1/2. And the power usage numbers for the "ultra-low" voltage
processors are on the 500MHz processors. To me it is always the same, Intel
kicks ass on marketing.

I've heard for awhile now people mentioning the Crusoe's quality problems.
But, I have never heard what those problems are. Is this about production
lines or how the processors perform? As far as performance I've been very
happy with my laptop. I'm running RedHat 7.2 and use it as a grade book,
video camera (using quicktime4linux), programming (fractals and stuff), and
as a tool to take from office to office while I'm fixing things. One
"strange" feature is the increase in performance as you do the same thing
over and over.

I change a few lines of source code and then recompile several times a
session. And the first run is usually twice the time of the last runs.
Which also means if I want to encode motion jpeg I need to first do a short
run at 15 frames a second before it will encode smoothly at 20 frames a
second. If I really knew how to code I would the hardware mjpeg stuff from
the video driver into quicktime4linux.

> Also I wouldn't rule out Intel's XScale, which is a capable contender in
> the low-end of the market.

I sure wish that Compaq would have had the brains to keep StrongARM. It seem
to be the choice of new handhelds now. It is amazing how far itzy has come
with the StrongARM Linux handhelds coming out.
