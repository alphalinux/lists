> No, bootpd and bootparamd are two different things. In Red Hat Linux,
> dhcpcd can be configured to serve as the bootp server.
>
> The bootparamd reads /etc/bootparams and provides diskless
> workstations. It can tell NFS rooted clients where to mount their
> filesystems.
