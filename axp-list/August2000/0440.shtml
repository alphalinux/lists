On Tue, 29 Aug 2000, Dave Gilbert wrote:

> > Could you check that this works for you? If the problem is present in
> > Linux/AXP as well, it's a general 64-bit issue. Otherwise it would be
> > a Tru64 specific issue.
>
> Those examples work for me; although you could have a combination of
> colour depth as well (I'm in 24 bit colour (32bpp/8 bit waste).
>
> While those work for me I have seen similar problems; I had a web page
> which one of the icons was corrupt, and when running kfouleggs (a rather
> addictive game) I find that the eggs have strange box like additions where
> I guess it should be masked.

You're right: I was running 16bpp while the problems occured. For testing
I've switched to 24bpp and the problems disappeared and all icons appeared
normally. With 8bpp PseudoColor I get even crashes at the places some of
these icons should show up. So its apparently related to the color depth
on alphas (it obviously works on Intel as there are no reports from Intel
people).

I've reported this to the responsible people at KDE/QT development.
