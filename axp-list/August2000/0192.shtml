I had similar things happening on my AlphaStation 200 4/233 when I upgraded
from the 5.3 firmware CD to the 5.5 firmware CD (ie. the normal looking boot
letters at the top followed by spam and then an init, into an endless loop
of this). At the time, I only had 8MB of RAM. It seems that the newer
firmware needed more than 8MB of RAM, I added 16MB of RAM and I had SRM 5.5
back again (though in the mean time, I had created a BIOS floppy and
adjusted the jumper to read the SRM from that floppy and was able to reflash
with 5.3). (I was running Tru64 UNIX 4.0d at the time, and boy was it slow
with 8MB of RAM. It was swapping onto the disk so much that we started
calling the box "Thunder" because of all of the noise the HD made.)

If you've got a decent amount of RAM installed, then the parity issue might
be your problem.

I found the instructions for creating the SRM floppy on DEC's website and
the jumper change in the owners manual.
