> If you really want SRM and want to keep your SCSI controller then maybe
> you have around an IDE drive (it may be quite small and mount it, say,
> on /boot) which can be used as a boot device? If your SRM will see it
> and other drives will show up once a kernel is running then the above
> should not matter.

I have a PWS433a and do something like this. I've installed SuSE 6.3 on the
SCSI drive and I can boot the SuSE CD-ROM. There's an option on the SuSE
install menu to "boot installed system." Since I seldom reboot this machine,
this is better alternative than buying a new SCSI card.
