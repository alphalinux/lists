I've got an AlphaStation 200 and had some difficulties getting from ARC back
to SRM. What I ended needing to do was to make a firmware floppy
(instructions were on DEC's firmware site). Once I had that, I went to the
"Upgrade Firmware" option of ARC, and when I got to the firmware update
menu, I did a Ctrl-P (it may have been prior to the menu, it was awhile ago
and I can't remember exactly where it was). This brought me to an SRM
prompt. I set "OS_TYPE" back to UNIX and power-cycled. Then I did a `b
dva0` to boot off of the floppy and loaded SRM (update SRM at the prompt).

This was much cleaner than the previous time when I just pulled the battery
off of the motherboard and when I put it back in, I had an SRM (and an error
about my sound device not being in the ISA config).
