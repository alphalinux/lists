> >I have seen 50 MB/s to ext2 filesystems over a single FCAL link,
> for large
> >writes. That's not as good as it should be, but one extra copy isn't the
> >guilty party.
>
> That's pretty good. Is this over the standard UFS on RH Linux?

That's over the Linux filesystem called "ext2". Linux also supports the
filesystem named UFS, but it isn't the standard one that people use.

> >We're using CVFS (CentraVision FileSystem) at FSL, but unfortunately they
> >have yet to send us a full-speed client. On small SGIs, they get
> up to 170
> >MB/s for a single client reading over 2 FCAL links. There's no reason why
> >the AlphaLinux client should be any slower, but the work isn't done yet,
> >alas.
>
> Is this also a multiple access file system? Who makes it, and how is
> its performance?

It is made by MountainGate, which is now owned by ADIC. For info, see
www.centravision.com. It is multiple access, by heterogeneous clients (SGI,
Mac, NT, Linux now; Solaris soon, AIX later.) It's also much more
expensive -- around $2000 for a single CPU client -- than GFS (GPLed).
