Sure, that's a fine attitude. What CVFS gives you today is a proven,
reliable, heterogeneous SAN filesystem. When we got to the install phase at
FSL, GFS's journaling version was 2 months away, and it's still 2 months
away. GFS is only on Linux, and it hasn't received enough real world testing
for me to stare my clients in the eye and promise that it won't eat all
their data once a year.

These qualities may or may not be important to you. And GFS will undoubtedly
be better in 6 months.
