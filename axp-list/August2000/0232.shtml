Hi David,
Your problem might be related to damaged RAM.
A year ago I struggled around with a bad 64MB-DIMM in my PC.
Symptoms were a seg-faulting gcc and fs-corruption. You could test your RAM by compiling for example the kernel multiple times and watch out for signal 11.

Perhaps it's also sth. different then the RAM, but your problem looks extremely hardware-related.

You could try to pull the ram-sticks out and clean the contacts before you plug them in again.

Good luck,
