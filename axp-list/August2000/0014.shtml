Atsuko Crum wrote:
>
> When I switched to SRM and typed "show dev", it displayed the following:
>

With your disk controller you would have to boot either

> dka0.0.0.4.0 DKA0 TOSHIBA CD-ROM MX-5702B 3476
                 ^^^^
                 from CD
> dva0.0.0.0.1 DVA0
                 ^^^^
                 or from floppy
> ewa0.0.0.3.0 EWA0 00-00-F8-75-50-D1
                 ^^^^
                 or from a network
> pqa0.0.0.4.0 PQA0 PCI EIDE
> pqa0.0.1.4.0 PQB0 PCI EIDE

All of these are possible but each has its own sort of pain. Once
your kernel is running it will likely see drives as before but I never
tried something of that sort myself.

If you really want SRM and want to keep your SCSI controller then maybe
you have around an IDE drive (it may be quite small and mount it, say,
on /boot) which can be used as a boot device? If your SRM will see it
and other drives will show up once a kernel is running then the above
should not matter.
