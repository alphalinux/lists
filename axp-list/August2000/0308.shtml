> I have a question about Digital Unix
>
> Must i have a DIGITAL Labeled disk to put the root system on ?
>
> my harddisk has the name in dmesg > linux and SRM
> Vendor: QUANTUM Model: ATLAS IV 9 WLS
>
> cant I install Digital Unix on that disk ?
>

Do you mean physicaly paper label on the outside of the disk...?
        No, you can use almost any drive (ie, those that the SRM
        recognizes). Most of the old problems with Digital branded
        SCSI drives died with Ultrix and SCSI-I. And in fact, I have
        been using StoragWorks drives with MK-Linux (Apple's Linux)
        and the Mac OS for years - they are all manufactured by
        Quantum or Seagate or IBM.

Do you mean the Logical Disk label...?
        Yes, you must have Tru64 Unix (formerly known as Digital Unix,
        formerly known as OSF/1) disk labels and formatting to load boot
        Tru64. That is to say, you can not use Fdisk to format a disk
        and then load DU on it. You must first zero the Fdisk created
        labels using "disklabel." Then create the partitions using
        Disklabel. Then the boot process will write the boot records
        in the correct place on the disk.

        Want to add Linux?
        There are some size rules in the setup papers you need to follow.
        First - don't use AdvFS with Tru64 for any partion you want to
        mount under Linux. (I keep /usr/local in ufs format, and the rest
        in AdvFS.)

        Then take Fdisk and change the "type" which ever partions you want
        to use for Linux. And you can install Linux.

        But by doing all the partitioning with Disklabel under Tru64, and
        then just changing the type with Fdisk all of the linux partitions
        can be mounted under Tru64!
