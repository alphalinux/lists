Foreground:

  tput setf [0-7]

Background:

  tput setf [0-7]

Reset to defaults:

  tput sgr0

Ron West wrote:
> We are porting our applications over from SCO Unix to Linux. Our user
> applications are not written to take advantage of the GUI, but are all Borne
> or Korn shell scripts executing from the command line. One command that
> executes from the .profile is the SCO setcolor command which changes the
> forground and background colors of the user's screen. I (a new Linux admin)
> have looked everywhere for an equivalent command, but have found nothing, no
> referrences to anything other than DIR_COLOR which isn't what we want. Is
> there a way to do this? I simply want to color the screen at the Borne shell
> blue with white characters.
