On Sat, Nov 25, 2000 at 04:17:16PM -0600, W Bauske wrote:
>
> I have a clean 7.0 CD install and it can't find my
> 2.4.test9/10 modules. I pay my $$ for a copy of RH7.0,
> released after the 2.4.testxx series has existed for
> quite a while now and it doesn't work. Why buy a CD
> if it's broken?

If you will look into your Documentation/Changes file in
kernel sources (always a good idea, especially if you plan to hack
with development kernels) you will find there a section with
a title "Current Minimal Requirements". A version of modutils
was raised there fairly recently - quite a bit later then RH
distribution CDs were sent to manufacturing. The lead time on
such things will always lag behind a pace of a kernel development
so this is hardly "broken".

OTOH relatively recently a serious security hole was found in modutils
and Red Hat issued a security update which covers all 6.x and 7
releases. Depending on what you are running on 5.x system you may be
affected by that as well (not if you were not adding stuff on your own)
but then you want to recompile modutils from sources configuring with
2.0 compatibility and possibly other options. As a side-effect this
security update solves also your problems with modules in the latest 2.4
kernels but this was not a primary reason for it.
