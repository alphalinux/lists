On 28-Nov-2000 Matt Wilson wrote:
> On Alpha the maximum swap partition size is 128 GB. You can have both
> swap files (on filesystems) and swap partitions (on block devices).
> Together these are "swap areas". The default maximum number of swap
> is 8.
>
> Matt

Does this mean that I could get 8 * 128 GB = 1 TB of total swap space? (Not
that I really need it, but....) More realistically, does this mean that I can
devote a full 36 GB drive in a single partition to swap? In terms of
efficiency, what would be the optimum allocation of swap to partitions? E.g.,
would it be better to divide up a 36 GB drive into 8 swap partitions, or
perhaps two 18 GB drives into 4 swap partitions each? Or what?
