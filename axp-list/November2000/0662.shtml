Hi!

I had a problem with doing that to change my LX from AlphaBIOS to SRM.
For some reason I got stuck at the firmware update prompt. I tried a
couple of variations of the update command, but couldn't figure out
what to do.

After searching the list archives, I figured out that all I needed to
do was copy the SRM ROM image (LX164srm.rom) to a floppy. The ROM
image is not listed on the HTML page. It's in the download directory
itself. After you have downloaded the image, go into the AlphaBIOS
setup and and then choose the "AlphaBIOS Upgrade" prompt.
The AlphaBIOS will then find the ROM image on floppy and display the
version numbers of your current firmware and the firmware on the floppy.
Tell it it's ok to proceed and it will start the flashing. On my
machine it hung when it tried to reboot after flashing, so I had to do
a hard reset to activate the new firmware.

If you want to go back to AlphaBIOS without reflashing, you can run
AlphaBIOS from floppy by placing a floppy with the AlphaBIOS image
lx164nt.rom in the drive and typing "arc" at the SRM prompt.

Alan
