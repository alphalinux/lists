> The AlphaPC164 requires a hard switched power supply.
> If you don't have one, you must jumper two of the Motherboard
> power connector wires together and manage on/off strictly from
> the power supply switch in the back. (Maybe this has nothing
> to do with your problem, but it might.)

Well, the original power supply had a remote push-on, push-off switch on
the front of the case (similar to AT), and appeared to have hardwired the
softpower circuit to ground (green and black wires hooked together).
Trying to power that up, fans and drives spin up, but nothing on the
keyboard LEDs, no video output, and no serial port output.

I pulled a 400w ps (a Powmax LP-6100D) from a working Athlon server, but
had the same results. Unfortunately when I put the ps back in the Athlon
system, it then did the same thing as the PC164 (fans and drives come up,
but nothing on the keyboard or video.
