IBM drives (least the ones I have) are the same way.

if you do go the 80->68 pin route, you'll need a converter that is spec'd to
U2W or U3W to get them to run at full speed. It's more of an issue of the
signal levels / crosstalk through the adapter than the brand of adapter used
w/ each drive.

I've seen the adapters range from $10-$25 for UW spec'd ones.

SCA connector drives are typically the same price as the 68 pin drive
equivelent of it. So speaking from a purely financial standpoint, you'll
save money per drive if you go w/ 68pin because you won't need the
SCA->68pin adapter (unless you really want hot-pluggability).

Kurt Ludwig.

I think this is right:

68-pin is the normal SCSI (up to Ultra160) connector.

80-pin is the SCA connector, that includes the data plus power (plus SCA
can be hot swapable).

So you can buy either...however, I know at least one brand (Seagate
maybe) that will only work at UltraWide with generic SCA-68pin converters,
but work fine with Seagate converters.
