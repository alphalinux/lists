Ya know.. Normally I wouldn't reply to this clearly off-topic
flame-bait post, but I've read too much of this stuff lately and I'm
starting to think that some people might actually believe these kind
of of things about Red Hat.

First and foremost, if Red Hat were a company like MicroSoft, I (as a
developer) probably wouldn't even be drafting a response to this. At
Red Hat I'd like to think that we're a member of the Community that
makes Free Software/Open Source Software work.

First, you can very easily disable kudzu. Type "chkconfig kudzu off"
and you're done. I'd like to see you disable the hardware
auto-detection in Windows...

The Red Hat Network Daemon will not automatically do anything to your
system at all until you configure it to do something. You are in
complete control over whether or not to use the service. Updates are
still delivered via FTP as they always have, so nothing has changed.

As far as inetd v/s xinetd goes... well, I'd have to say that I've
heard not as many complaints as I have praises of xinetd. But with
Red Hat Linux you're welcome to modify it to use inetd if you so
desire, that's your choice. It's also your choise to use another
distribution if it suits your needs better. Instead, I'd rather have
users submit *detailed* bug reports to bugzilla or participate in
development discussion on redhat-devel-list@redhat.com.

Cheers,
