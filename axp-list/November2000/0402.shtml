On Sun, Nov 19, 2000 at 06:56:07AM -0800, Greg Meno wrote:
>
> I recently purchased a PCI voodoo 3 3000 for use with
> my LX. The card shows it's video BIOS init then SRM
> comes up and a boot dka200 puts me into linux.

Do not try to run this card in an 8-bit depth. Xconfigurator
attempts to probe (don't!) and test in 8-bits. If you will
allow that you are in trouble.

Actually when I tried some voodoo on Alpha, not LX, recently the card
was the happiest in 32-bits (this will be 24-bits in XFree 4 as 32
vanished there). It did work in 16-bits but in places were strange
artifacts. I did not investigate these as I played with this
card literally for some 15 minutes or so.

You can specify your 'DefaultColorDepth' in XF86Config.

> It displays a white screen then a black screen (this
> is with XFree 3.3.6) I press the reset switch and I'm
> back to SRM..

This sounds like a memory probe gone too far. :-)

> At this point I decide to go with XFree 4.0 (bad idea)

Maybe, maybe not. See above.
