With regards to your message at 11:13 AM 11/29/00, s-luppescu@uchicago.edu.
Where you stated:
> > Have you considered using a solid-state disk for this, or possibly a SCSI
> > RAID array?
> > It would give you infinitely better swap performance in either scenario.
>
>A solid-state disk would be prohibitively expensive, but RAID seems like a
>good
>idea. But, ``infinitely better swap performance''? Methinks you exaggerate a
>bit:-}
Yes, the term "infinitely" is a bit laden with exaggeration, I agree.
Probably better by an order of magnitude would be more likely.
Bottom line:
The fastest disk drive on the market is the Seagate Cheetah15K with about
an average real transfer rate (media to heads) of about 30+ MB/sec.
A decent RAID array with striping, but no redundancy is likely to give you
about a sustained rate of between 120MB/sec and 200MB/sec, depending on the
disks and interface types.
Next limiting factor is your PCI bus, which, with a 64 bit, 33MHz SCSI card
is limiting you to about 230MB/sec, maximum theoretical, with around
200MB/sec actual.
6 to 7 times faster than a single disk, and 3 to 6 times faster than a
software RAID or similar striping of swap across multiple disks.

Does that seem fair enough?
