if you want to run SRM, you have to re-do the disk labels on them from DOS
to BSD disk labeling (at least on the boot drive). So if you don't mind at
least partially re-installing the machine, and have hardware that shows up
in SRM (at least for the boot drive), then it's worth it.

it's much simpler to go into SRM and boot, rather than AlphaBIOS, then MILO,
then boot. It's also a lot less to manage for configuration.

the SRM files for the PWS series are at:
http://ftp.digital.com/pub/DEC/Alpha/firmware/readmes/digitalpw.html
