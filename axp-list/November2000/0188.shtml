On Wed, Nov 08, 2000 at 10:27:42AM -0600, Terry L. Ridder wrote:
> richard, would you have any idea, hint, or
> suggestion as to why in the rtlinux rtl.o
> when rdps statement is reached the alpha
> would just lock up? this is on a pc164, the
> alpha with the broken interrupt mask.

Nope, and I really don't believe it either -- rdps consists
of exactly three instructions:

        bis zero, ps, v0
        nop
        hw_rei

which doesn't leave much room for something to go wrong. It
seems much more likely that the culprit is actually swpipl,
and that you've got an interrupt pending.

> also is there any chance that this could be
> caused by a differene between booting with
> milo vs. booting with srm & aboot?

Not really. More likely the difference between working
interrupt hardware and not. Given the games rtlinux plays
with soft interrupt supression, I'd kinda doubt it works
on the pc164.
