On Thu, Nov 16, 2000 at 08:49:51AM +0100, Helge Kreutzmann wrote:
>
> Yesterday evening my LX stopped booting while in SRM. I don't know if
> something special happened, because I switched it on, typed "b dqa" after
> a brief moment and left the machine. When I turned on the Monitor about
> 10 minutes later I saw:
>
> >>>AllocOSMIOB failed
>
> Subsequent resents/reboots only made it up to the initialisation of SRM:
> eb.ea.e9.e8.e7
>
> and no SRM prompt was shown anymore. All external cables seem fine.
> Unfortunately my LX handbook only talks about AlphaBIOS so it doesn't tell
> me excactly what to do now.
>
> First I will of course check all internal cables. The LX handbook talks about
> the failsafe loader.
>
> a) If I switch on with the failsafe loader enabled, according to the handbook
> I will "return" to AlphaBios. Is that correct or is there a way to directly
> go into SRM again ?
>
> b) What could be the reason for this problem ? All disks seem to spin up
> fine, the keyboard is reset and so on. I recently had some SCSI problems
> (added a new disks and got lots of SCSI timeouts. When I disabled
> Tagged Command Queuing it got way better, but recently I had another
> set of timeouts (and some warning about a "phase" unfortunately I do
> not remember the excat phrase) which fortunately went away after
> several minutes (so I did not have to reboot)) could those be causing
> SRM to hang ?

That message, "AllocOSMIOB failed", comes from the Adaptec driver, and
indicates that, for some reason, it failed to alloc a new IOB from its
free list.

So, yes, it seems to related to SCSI, and no, I have no idea what you
should do... :-\

Good luck.
