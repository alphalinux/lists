On Tue, 7 Nov 2000, Thomas Leitner wrote:

>
> On Mon, 6 Nov 2000, Rich Payne wrote:
>
> > There's 3 differnet things being announced here. First is the UP2000+
> > motherboard, it's basically and improved UP2000. The second is the 833MHz,
> > 4MB DDR slotBs (which you can only use in a UP2000+ I might add, please
> > don't put them into a UP2000). There are no 667/750 DDR SlotBs.
>
> Can you elaborate a bit on what's improved in the UP2000+ over the old
> UP2000?

2-64bit PCI buses
4 64-bit PCI slots, 2-32 (vs 2-64, 4-32)
PCI bus ordering change (PCI bus 0 is now slots 0,2,4, PCI 1 is 1,3,5)
Autodetection of SlotB speed/cache (yes, no more jumpers!)
Support for EV68 slot-Bs (like the 833 4MB DDR)
there's also several smaller changes that I've forgotton....

>Is it only the faster CPU?

The CPUs are seperate.

>Will Tru64 still run on it?

Yes, though as before it's not officially supported.
