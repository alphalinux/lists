kwood@atipa.com wrote:

> His concern is that when he runs xinit and then netscape, his machine
> locks up hard. It seems to be a memory problem as he can't telnet in
> and he can't move his mouse (which is usually hardware).

This has happened with my UP1000 from time to time,
when I have experimented with different kernels.
My general suspicions and impressions are that

1. The 2.2.14 and 2.2.16 Alpha kernels from RedHat
both have problems or lack features.

2. The 2.2.16 Alpha kernel from the RedHat updates
directory fares better than 2.2.14, but it lacks ide dma,
a must.

3. The best Kernels for UP1000 are those you roll
yourself. Obtain a stock 2.2.16 or 2.2.17 kernel
from ftp.kernel.org:/pub/linux/kernel/2.2.
Obtain patches from ftp.alphalinux.org/pub/people.
The patch for ide dma, really a must, is in hedrick/ there.
The patch for software raid, not so important, is in ...
arg! I can't recall at the moment.

4. There may be other patches here that you might
want to apply, but there appears to be now documentation
anywhere about these options and the ramifications
of applying or not applying them. It seems that the
only way to find out is to query this list.
(Someone correct me if I'm wrong here.)

5. Gnome in RH 6.1 and maybe 6.2 can cause problems
with some alpha systems. It's forced me to used kde
exclusively, at least for now.
