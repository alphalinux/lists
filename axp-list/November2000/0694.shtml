With regards to your message at 04:00 PM 11/29/00, David Singleton. Where
you stated:

>Be a little wary of associating peak disk performance and swap
>performance. Paging is done in (I think) 8KB pages on Linux Alpha -
>that is not the way to get good IO performance.
Hence my suggestion of a hardware SCSI RAID.
As this then allows the segments to go throught the cache RAM on the RAID
controller, and as the writes are distributed over multiple disks we gain
the benefit of the cache RAM on the disks as well. With this buffering I
suspect that stalls will be kept to a minimum.

>I have done a number of tests hoping to use paging for good ol'
>fashioned job-swapping (like suspend a 5GB job and start another 5GB
>job when you only have 6GB of RAM). The "memory exchange" can take
>HOURS on some systems and will get only a few MB/s on most.
>
>If you really need 40GB you may be much better off writing your own
>out-of-core solver where you roll in and out data in LARGE record
>sizes.
I agree that a software solution is probably for the best!
