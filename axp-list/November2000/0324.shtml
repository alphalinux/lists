SRM was originally designed to boot Tru64 (Digital Unix) and OpenVMS,
whereas AlphaBIOS was designed to boot WindowsNT on alphas.

The hardware support under SRM is much more stringent (mainly due to the
fact that Tru64 and VMS only support certain graphics cards / SCSI / IDE
adapters)

SRM is based off OpenVMS for device naming and general operation.

if you need more hardware support at boot time (like video cards and SCSI
cards to boot from) then AlphaBIOS is a better bet. For most users though,
SRM is actually easier.

there are certain systems that no Milo exists for (so you have to use SRM).
AlphaServer 800's are an example of this.
