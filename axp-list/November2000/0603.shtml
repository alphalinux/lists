Right. What I don't quite get is your urge to install a "new" system. When
you pay support for an OS, it means you receive upgrades. That's the way it
works for Solaris, HPUX, etc. When there is need to change something in your
installation, you usually apply patches specific to your particular problem.
Setting up a new version of the OS always brings some problems that need some
time to iron out.

With free software, it's even better. You grab the necessary piece of source,
compile it, test that it fixes your problem, and install it on all your
machines. Even if you have hundred+ machines, that should not make such a
difference (provided they are all similar). No incompatibilities, nor
unexpected results (Murphy willing... ;-)
