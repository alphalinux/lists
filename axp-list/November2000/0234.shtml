Hi,

I need help to get sound to work on a vanilla XP1000 workstation under
RedHat-6.2 . On the Compaq web site I couldn't find much technical
information about the sound card in the XP1000.

So here's what I did up to now: In SRM, "show config" tells me that
the sound card is on IRQ 5, DMA 1 and 5 (pretty standard I think).
Then I compiled the kernel with the following options turned on:

     CONFIG_SOUND=y
     CONFIG_SOUND_OSS=y
     CONFIG_SOUND_DMAP=y
     CONFIG_SOUND_SB=y
     CONFIG_SB_BASE=220
     CONFIG_SB_IRQ=5
     CONFIG_SB_DMA=1
     CONFIG_SB_DMA2=5
     CONFIG_SB_MPU_BASE=330
     CONFIG_SB_MPU_IRQ=-1

When I boot that kernel "dmesg" says,

     Sound initialization started
     ESS chip ES1887 detected
     <ESS ES1887 AudioDrive (rev 11) (3.01)> at 0x220 irq 5 dma 1,5
     <ESS ES1688> at 0x330 irq 5 dma 0
     Sound initialization complete

So far, it looks good to me.

If /dev/sndstat (same output as /proc/sound) looks good, I cannot
tell:

     OSS/Free:3.8s2++-971130
     Load type: Driver compiled into kernel
     Kernel: Linux 5b.gmd.de 2.2.18pre2aa2 #7 Wed Nov 8 16:25:00 CET 2000 alpha
     Config options: 0
     
     Installed drivers:
     Type 26: MPU-401 (UART)
     Type 2: Sound Blaster
     Type 29: Sound Blaster PnP
     Type 7: SB MPU-401
     
     Card config:
     Sound Blaster at 0x220 irq 5 drq 1,5
     SB MPU-401 at 0x330 irq 5 drq 0
     
     Audio devices:
     0: ESS ES1887 AudioDrive (rev 11) (3.01) (DUPLEX)
     
     Synth devices:
     
     Midi devices:
     0: ESS ES1688
     
     Timers:
     0: System clock
     
     Mixers:
     0: Sound Blaster

/proc/dma says,

     1: SoundBlaster8
     5: SoundBlaster16

/proc/devices says in the character device section,

     14 sound

/proc/interrupts says,

     5: 9 soundblaster

/proc/ioports says,

     0220-022f : soundblaster
     0330-0333 : MPU-401 UART

When I try to play some sound, I cannot hear anything in the
headphones and I get the syslog message,

     kernel: Sound: DMA (output) timed out - IRQ/DRQ config error?

There's no difference in the symptoms, if I unset CONFIG_SOUND_DMAP.
Anybody knows what I did wrong?

Thanks for your help,

       -- Georg
