On Fri, 24 Nov 2000, David Rhodes wrote:

> Apologies for being a clueless newbie...
>
> I am trying to upgrade the version of cdrecord I have on my pws to something
> post-v1.9. I couldn't find an alpha rpm for it so I thought it would be a
> good opportunity to get to grips with rebuilding from a source rpm, so I got
> hold of cdrecord-1.9-2.src.rpm and used rpm with the --rebuild flag to try
> to create an alpha rpm. From reading up on rpm I thought this should appear
> after the rebuild somewhere under /usr/src/redhat/RPMS, but it didn't. I
> got what looks to be the package contents in /BUILD and a tarball plus some
> patches in /SOURCES, but no rpm.

It should be in /usr/src/redhat/RPMS/alpha when it's done. Look at the
output from RPM, it will tell you if it failed, though it's not always
obvious. I've found that sometimes the package will compile just fine, but
in actually packaging the RPM it fails because a pre-requisite is missing.
Read the output carefully and look for the requires line and check that
you have everything installed.

Also, are you sure the build was successful? If I remember correctly
cdrecord can be rather difficult to build.
 
> Now I suppose I could just install it from the tarball, but I would like to
> know where I went wrong in the rebuild process. Can anyone enlighten me?
