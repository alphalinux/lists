On my PC164 systems, using the most recent SRM,
bootdef_dev, boot_file, and boot_osflags
are reset when the system is shut down and power is turned off.
Hmmm...Why should it behave this way when older SRMs
on my DEC 3000 provide a non-volatile bootdef_dev?
The battery is good.

John Jasen wrote:

> On Sat, 11 Nov 2000, Robert Williams wrote:
>
> > The boot variables under SRM, such as boot_dev,
> > appear to be volatile. Once set, they are reset to
> > the defaults when the system is shut down.
> > I've been reading all of the manuals...
> > Is there no way to make these settings persistent
> > across shutdowns?
>
> boot_dev is volatile.
>
> bootdef_dev is not.

Is there something else besides
"set bootdef_dev dqa0<cr>"
that makes it non-volatile?
It doesn't behave like my DEC 3000
SRM.

>
> boot_file and boot_osflags should remain consistent.

Yes. They should....and they do on my Tru64 machine
using a very old SRM.
