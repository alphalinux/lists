> > http://www.phys.ufl.edu/~prescott/linux/alpha/dec3000-port.html

> > than starting from scratch.

> Well, considering that the MIPS port has TC support working on the
> MIPS-based DEC-marked machines, it's quite a bit farther than that

 Craig started his DEC 3000 effort at the time MIPS DECstation machines
were already running fine. TC support is pretty easy for DEC 3000, but
there were problems with hardware initialization and SRM interactions upon
bootstrap, IIRC. So yes, Craig's effort appears to be the most advanced
one known, indeed. You may contact him for details -- last I've heard
from him was this Summer.

> attempt. I've seen boot logs where the bus has been initialised (at least
> on a MIPS). I have a feeling that most of the work will be making sure
> that the code is 64-bit clean and handling the quirks that may come up on
> the Alpha-based machines.

 You'll have to handle TC via the sparse space. It will surely need code
adjustments. The most challenging task will be getting the onboard stuff
working, I'm afraid. At least DEC 3000 is well-documented.
