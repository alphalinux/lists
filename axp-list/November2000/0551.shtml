"Christopher C. Chimelis" wrote:

> On Sat, 25 Nov 2000, Robert Williams wrote:
>
> > Sure some config files are overwritten,
> > but reports of those changes are stored in the update
> > log file, and the old configuration file is saved.
>
> Key word: SOMETIMES. I've run into situations on RH servers that I
> inherited where config files where just overwritten without notice and
> without a backup.

That has never happened to me, as far as I can remember.
(Maybe it did and it wasn't a big deal at the time.)
I update Red Hat systems frequently, both intel and alpha.
I have had problems with updates, particularly
recently upon going to 7.0 on some intel systems, where
I didn't look to see if the video card was supported.
That was my fault. I think updating RH
is easier than updating Tru64 Unix, and certainly easier
than updating or installing Windows. The rpm system
has always been a good idea, in my opinion, even though its implementation
has had growing pains.

I have nothing against Debian. I think it is not good
to pit one Linux/Unix against another. Remember,
the true center of selfishness is in Redmond, WA.

> > The upside of this is that you must pay attention
> > to these changes right away.

> Again, SOMETIMES.

Again, I've not had problems in this way.
There are problems...but we live with them
because they are not as bad as the popular alternative.
