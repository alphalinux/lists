On Sun, Nov 26, 2000 at 07:52:00PM -0500, Rich Payne wrote:
>
> OK, the SYM1010 SCSI chip is new, you must patch the Linux kernel for
> it to see and use the card. I think the patches come from LSI Logic:
> http://www.lsilogic.com/support/support+drivers/scsi/linux.html

Well, yes and no. At least 2.2.18pre and the current 2.4.0-test will
recognize and will support this controller without any other extra
patches. I know because I tried it. There is also even newer 1.7.2
sym53c8xx driver (a drop-in replacement for all 2.2 kernels) on
ftp://ftp.tux.org/tux/roudier/drivers/.

Fragments of dmesg from a boot of 2.4.0-test11 on UP1100 which I posted
Friday were with 1010 card and this can be noticed there. This does not
mean that everything is already peachy. We had problems with LSI (not
Tekram) 1010 based card on UP2000+ in Ultra3 mode (not in slower modes)
but this did not happen on UP1100. There are still tests to be made and
it is not likely that this was a Linux fault.

SRM on these machines had no problem with recognizing this card as well.
This, of course, implies nothing of that for Miata and I have a zero
experience with a Tekram implementation.

> I wouldn't expect MILO to see it either.

One probably has to create a custom MILO with the current sym53c8xx
(NOT ncr53c8xx) driver.
