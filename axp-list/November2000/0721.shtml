Michal Jaegermann wrote:
>
> On Thu, Nov 30, 2000 at 12:38:43PM -0800, Paul Bunn wrote:
>
> > 16MB/sec is good for a 9GB SCSI drive. For more modern IDE drives,
> > you'll be able to realize 30-40 MB/sec sustained transfer rates.
>
> Eh? Care to give specific drive model examples and actual measurements
> from 'hdparm -t'? I am not talking about theoretical limits of
> manufacturer quoted peak media-to-head rates. This is not the same
> thing. I am genuinly interested.
>
> Numbers around 25 MB/sec I have seen myself. With one active device
> on a bus, of course.
>

16MB/sec is good for a typical 9GB SCSI drive I've
used. Lots of them do little more than half that,
particularly if they are 7200rpm.

Same question back to you. What 9GB SCSI drive
have you tested that showed 25MB/sec? I've seen
10k 18GB drives do around 25MB/sec. You have to
typically use higher density drives to get 25MB/sec
or more. A UATA100 drive is capable of the
performance quoted, running 5400rpm.

9GB is a wasted slot though. I'll take an 80GB Maxtor
instead.
