On Fri, Nov 24, 2000 at 06:26:20PM -0600, W Bauske wrote:
>
> I consider xinetd really stupid, but then I
> know I'm used to the way inetd works and that inetd
> is pretty standard for the other systems I use.
> (AIX/Solaris/etc) Being different than the other
> Unices is not a good plan if you ask me, particularly
> if you are interested in the server market. Maybe
> RH doesn't give a damn about servers.

Whatever you may think now if you are seriously into servers then xinetd
is in your future. Various tools do change. Telnet and bind4 were
"standards" for a long time. Not anymore; at least as far as bind4 and
its zone files go. Telnet may come back but not in the form you have
seen it over years but with an encryption, something like SRP
authentication and the like.

Going from inetd to xinetd is in reality pretty simple. Some tools
do lag behind. For example, 'tcpdcheck' and 'tcpdmatch' from
tcp_wrappers do not parse yet an information from xinetd configuration
files (although they do parse more formats of /etc/inetd.conf
then you likely even know that exists :-). Yet another "transition
period". One would think that by now you get used to such things. :-)
