With regards to your message at 10:59 PM 9/11/99 -0400, Dan Morrill. Where
you stated:

>I have an external SCSI ZIP drive that I discovered won't work with my
>exisiting Intraserver SCSI card due to the way I have it configured. (I have
>both internal SCSI ports in use which disables the external port.)
>
>So, I saw an Adaptec 2906 PCI card relatively cheap and figured I could pop
>it in for use solely with the ZIP drive. Before I buy it, though, I figured
>I'd ask here to see if anyone has any experience with this card on Alphae.
>
>My system is an SX164.
A simpler solution:
Get a new internal SCSI cable that is long enough to reach your current
internal drives and end up at the back of your case.
There are adaptors made that plug into a drive connector, and allow
external connection.
Then you can hook up your external devices at the end of the existing
internal SCSI cable.
A cable and adaptor like this will run you about $40 to $50 depending on
the number of connectors needed, etc.
You _will_ need an external terminator to put on the external connector
when you don't have drives attached there, as well.
