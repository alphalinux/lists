OK. My full .config is available at
http://fido.bfriars.ox.ac.uk/~sean/config.2.2.12

...but basically, its a ruffian with a symbios scsi card, four disks,
three of which live in a software raid-5 using the alpha raid-tools.
It has 376 Mb of RAM, and two network cards: and onboard tulip, and a
3c905 as follows:

eth0: Digital DS21142/3 Tulip at 0x8000, 00 00 f0 51 05 d6, IRQ 44.
eth1: 3Com 3c905B Cyclone 100baseTx at 0x9800, 00:50:04:65:a0:d6, IRQ 19

It uses these to act as a router between two subnets here. Kernels
between 2.2.5 and 2.2.11 inclusive used to give random sigsegv's, but
2.2.12 has been brilliant. It had a fair bit of uptime, but I
rebooted it yesterday to try 2.3.17.

While compiling this reply, the load never dropped below 50, and I was
still able to log in, use scp to upload the .config to my webspace,
and do sundry grepping around to get the network card info etc.

Sean
