"Champigny, Michael" wrote:
>
> Hello,
>
> My final plea for help...after almost 3 months of trying to install
> Linux on a Jensen, I'm at the end of my rope.
>
> I will pay $50 cash to the first person to lead me to the first
> RH6.0 installation screen. I need the capability of booting off
> the harddrive. You can either arrange to stop by, mail me a
> floppy image which will work, or talk me through on the phone.

The howto says you'll never get a floppy install to work.

> This is probably a 10 minute job but I've wasted hundreds of
> hours of work on it already. The box is slow as molasses...it's
> the principle of the matter!!!
>

>From looking at the install instructions, I'd say you
better talk to Jim Paradis. Without his utilities you won't
get it to work unless you maybe take out the drive
and connect it to another SRM based Alpha and install
it there. Might be able to get by with just making the
HD bootable from another box. Even then, Jensens look
pretty messed up compared to a PCI based Alpha.

So, it seems your main problem is to get the hard disk
bootable. Also, the method used to make Jensens bootable
is not the same as a newer SRM based system. You have to
create a combination aboot/kernel back to back according to
the howto I read.

Personally, I don't think it's worth the time to mess with
but to each his own.

Paradis utilities:

ftp://ftp.digital.com/pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/JENSEN_INSTALL/arcutils/

Howto install:

http://www.comnets.rwth-aachen.de/~ost/alpha/FAQ-9.html
http://www.comnets.rwth-aachen.de/~ost/alpha/jensen.html

Make sure the firmware is 1.7.
