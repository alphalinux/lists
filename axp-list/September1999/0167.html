Hi Michael!
What kind of harddisk are you using? Aboot (0.5-7) seems to be picky
about disk size (and/or layout?) and such. Maybe using another disk
might help...
Reasoning: I recently switched from a 4.5 gb DCAS to a 9 gb DDRS, while
I was able to boot 0/a on the old disk I get an error message similar to
yours on the new disk (actually IIRC it complains about an CRC error
while loading the kernel , which it does not on the other disk using the
same aboot+kernel-disk). (It does not matter whether I write aboot and
kernel over the first partition or over some spare cylinders. But the
funny thing is aboot runs anyway, it's just the kernel-load that fails.)
Another approach could be to let someone with a running alpha-linux put
a jensen capable kernel on your data partition. (In that case
3/'real-kernel-name.gz' would reference that kernel from aboot. And, as
I offered earlier, I would be pleased if I could 'taylor' you a
2.2.12-kernel according to your requirements.)
By the way, don't you compaq-lads get more recent hardware than
jensen-boards? ;)
