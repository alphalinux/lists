On Thu, 16 Sep 1999, Kurt Ludwig wrote:

> Looking for a new SCSI card for my 164sx 533MHz system
> currently running 2 adaptecs with multiple hard drives on them... (from
> which I've heard and seen is a bad thing on alpha linux)

        Tell me about it! I am limping along with an AHA-2940 in my XLT,
with 6 (3x2GB+3x4GB) drives on it. Accessing four or more drives at once
is assured to get one a SCSI timeout, which means raid is pretty much out.
I had concluded that maybe it was just SCSI limitations that I was
pushing, but maybe also the card quality as well? The chain is within
length specs, and runs only at fast speeds, even though two of the 4GB
drives are ultras. I have a BT-930 I would love to use... but that is
another story (no alpha support for them yet). Also, the onboard NCR is
already busy with a 2GB system disk, three CD drives, a tape drive, and a
CD-R. :)
 
> any ideas for good SCSI cards that don't time out under high load? and that
> may alos be compatible with Digital UNIX?

        I don't need Tru64 compat, just stablity with Linux. From the
other recommendations made, it looks like a Tekram DC-390 (w/53C875
chipset) would be the best, and about $70/each. Am I correct in this? I
plan to use two of them, one for four disks and one for three disks. And
yes, I need the ones with 50pin connectors, the cables to my storage array
are HD50, and I don't want to pay for adapters! I have heard that Tekram
SCSI cards work well with Linux/Alpha, does this include this card
(DC-390)? Thanks.

> -gotta love free power in dorm rooms... computers haven't been turned off
> for 2 weeks now... all 3 of em

        Yea, three weeks for me, and I have eight computers running!
Approximately 750 watts of power drain. :) Of course, I do have a BF-UPS
(think BFG...) for when they decide to play with the breaker box
(maintance or others).
