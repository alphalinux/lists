The problem is that there is no support for the Powerstorm family (based on
the E&R chipset) by Xfree86 (or MetroX) that I could find. The reason it
comes up witht the Cirrus is because they included that chip on the board to
be able to handle some text like the SRM boot and other non-graphic intensive
stuff that needed to be displayed before a driver could be loaded. if you
actually take the card out you can look at the different chips and you will
see this. Presently there is no way (I could only get a b&w server to work
with my powerstorm) to run X windows with this card. However, I have a PWS433
also and I picked up a cheap ($29.00) S3 card (PCI) and it works fine with
Linux. The only limit on that card is your monitor really. If you have more
detailed questions - Jay Estabrook - from Compaq - formerly Digital - reads
this list and he could give you more help. Most of what I am citing in here
comes from him.
Good luck.
