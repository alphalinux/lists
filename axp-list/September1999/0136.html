I tried to get the Dec Unix version of Pro-Engineer running on a pc164,
Red Hat 5.2, kernel 2.2.7, Pro-Engineer version 20, build 9852.

It was a very dificult task. Machine type is referenced through a
"uname -a" in many places, and i had to change several scripts that
called the machine type.

Also, there are a couple ioctrl calls that were allocated inside dec
unix that are missing from linux. they are:

SIOCRPHYSADDR
SIOCGIFFLAGS
SIOCGIFCONF

to fix these, add the following lines to net/core/dev.c:dev_ioctl() and
recompile your kernel.

        if(cmd == 0xc0106914)
                cmd = SIOCGIFCONF;
        if(cmd == 0xc0206911)
                cmd = SIOCGIFFLAGS;
        if(cmd == 0xc01c693e)
                cmd = SIOCRPHYSADDR;

and this is where the fun ended. I then ran into licensing issues,
specifically the error:

ERROR: could not connect to NameServer.

and could not continue.
I was helped tremendously on this project by a person identified only by
"m.", from perth. Even though i was unsuccessful, i am still greatful
for his help.
