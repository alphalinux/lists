On Wed, 8 Sep 1999, Xiaozhou Qiu wrote:

> > If you are *very* careful, you can change the disklabel without
> > reinstalling the system. I did this some time ago, but be *VERY* careful.
>
> The problem is that people who have no idea of BSD-disklabeling probably
> did not reserve a space for aboot at the beginning of the disk.

True. I forgot that. But it should be possible with little changes if your
first partition is a dos partition holding milo and ldmilo/linload. This
one can be deleted safely. You have to be careful to adjust your fstab
afterwards.

> > So it doesn't interpret the OSF disklabel at all? Would be interesting to
> > find out whether it's possible to get SRM loading aboot from a DOS
> > disklabel, too by using some nasty tricks.
>
> Generally you can not get SRM to load aboot from a DOS disklabel, because
> the place to store DOS disklabel overlaps with the address where SRM will
> find the position of the bootloader.

That's about what I found out when I looked at it, too. Would be
interesting to find out though, whether it's possible to fake the
neccessary stuff into a dos disklabel to make srm believe that it can take
the entry address for the boot loader. This would result in a slightly
"defective" dos disklabel but could be sufficient for SRM and Linux to
boot from that disk.
This was just a speculation and I for myself don't believe it's really
possible, but I don't know what parts of the OSF disklabel are
checked/needed by SRM.
