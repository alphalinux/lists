On Thu, 14 Sep 2000, you wrote:
> I am new to the list and just wanted to say thanks for all the great
> information in the archives. With your help I was able to convert two of
> my alpha/nt workstations over to redhat 6.2. I have 6 stations all
> together two are PC164 with 433 Mhz EV56, 128 MBram and the other four are
> 500 mhz / pc164lx w/256 meg ram. I am planning on converting them all and
> then putting them up for sale.

Cool. Those remaining LX's will make great servers. Running Linux that is :-)

> I am having a little trouble running the 3dlabs glint 500 TX board in these
> stations. Currently I have a generic S3 board in them. The glint board
> won't run at anything higher then 800x600 8bit color depth and the xserver
> won't seen ram past 8meg these are 16 meg cards.

These cards a picky... Since X needs to support the chipset AND the ramdac.
The problem may be that ramdac support for that card isnt so great hence
the reduced resolution. Perfect example of this are the Accellgraphics
GLINT 500TX boards. 16MB with a funky IBM ramdac that isnt supported.

Metrolink sells Xservers for linux/alpha , www.metrolink.com . They support
a few 500TX based cards. The cost is cheap but for a few bucks more you
can buy yourself a PCI voodoo 3000 that is fully supported on under X free
and soon to have hardware acceleration.

> Any help with the video would be great, also any idea what a fair price for
> these boxes would be? They have new 4.3 GB 7200 rpm scsi drives and new
> CDROM and floppy drives as well.

With that much ram which alone is $300 bucks I would say anywhere between
$700-$900 would be a good price.

> You would cry if you new what we paid for them back in 1996.

I know what they cost :-) and I know how horrible their 2D performance
is. Honestly I think you're better off just getting a new card.
