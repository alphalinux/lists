Pierrick,

>the ADAPTEC 2100S is a AIC7892 chipset which sound to be supported by the
>SRM console. ( same chipset is using on 29160 board ).

On RAID controllers, the SCSI I/O ASIC is concealed behind the RAID
controllers MPU and often a bridge (which may be integrated into the MPU).
The system will not, by design, be able to detect or communicate with the
SCSI I/O ASIC directly. Additionally, you could have a OEM SCSI controller
with a chip that may otherwise be supported, but SRM and most Tru64/OpenVMS
and even Linux drivers will check for various information in the PCI
identification string, including category, type/level or rev, burst method
and vendor. If the revision, type or vendor doesn't match what's supported
by SRM, you can't boot from it, even if a driver exists for it.

Also, don't even try to use the RAID configuration utilities that may (or
may not) appear during boot as part of the SCSI BIOS emulation startup. If
you cannot locate a console app that either runs from Linux or SRM console,
plan on either building a configuration (on a very low priority machine) and
configuring it on a PC. You'll ultimately need a controller that SRM
supports via a console app or a Linux compatible tool to configure and
manage the RAID.

I've had better success with Mylex DAC960 and have briefly experimented with
an eXtremeRAID 1100 (booting from a Qlogic 1040 controller). Mylex was a
long term OEM for Digital StorageWorks, which gets them preferential
treatment (especially where SRM support is concerned).

Good luck,
