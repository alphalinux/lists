Kai Schmidt wrote:

> I have to disks, the aboot file written from a Suse Intel box with dd
> and 8192 blocks.
Hi Kai!

8192 seems quite a lot for a floppy. If you are writing things to other
medium types, please ignore this mail. But writing an image (image.name)
to the first floppy (fd0) can be accomplished by 'dd if=image.name
of=/dev/fd0 bs=512 count=2880'. Be sure to check the floppy quality, as
around 5% of any given pile of floppies seem to contain bad blocks
(numbers come from own experience and recycling almost 1000 old
floppies).

Good luck
