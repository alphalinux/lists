It sounds like they have different base levels of optimization. I don't
know about gcc but I do know that ccc does not optimize unless you tell it
to. It also inserts basic debugging information (line numbers only I think)
at the default levels.

I don't want to start a war but, if you really want floating point
performance then you should be looking at Fortran. It was designed for
number crunching, C was designed to do a little of everything. For more
performance under ccc, take a look at the man pages. Some of the switches
that might be good are: -fast -intrinsics -inline all -03
