> I'm now having trouble with ethernet. I have a stock XP1000 with it's
> builtin ethernet controller. During the install I'm asked which eth0
> module I want to load. I tried DE4*5 and Tulip.
>
> The DE4*5 just gives me insmod failure messages at boot time, and Tulip
> causes a segmentation fault and stops the boot process!
We were able to boot SuSE 7.1/Kernel 2.4.4 with both modules on two stock
XP1000, no problems there. For the de4x5, we used the following lines in
/etc/modules.conf:

alias eth0 de4x5
options de4x5 args=eth0:fdx

The tulip driver worked as well, but offers just a very poor performance.
Netperf reported a mere 6 MBit out of a 100 MBit connection (de4x5: 68
MBit).

> Again, I don't recall what I did to get this working in 6.4 but I thought
> that DE4xx module worked.
In SuSE 6.4/Kernel 2.2.14 or SuSE 7.1/Kernel 2.2.19, the de4x5 driver is
compiled in and your module definitions probably were just ignored.
Try out Kernel 2.2.19 as supplied on the SuSE CDs, it should work fine.

We reverted back to 2.2.x on our production machines anyway due to some
serious VM problems (significant slowdowns for large file I/O when
bdflush/kupdated get activated).
