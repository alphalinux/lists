> That may be the problem. "install_xx" image is designed to be a
> initrd (initial-ramdisk) as opposed to a normal ramdisk.
> The differences are in short:
>
> ramdisk initrd
> --------- ---------
> kernel starts: init linuxrc
>
> possible to
> re-mount root-dev: no yes
>
> I have modified aboot to be able to load such an initial ramdisk
> together with the kernel, just like LILO or syslinux do this on x86.
> The parameter used is "initrd=PATH", in this case "initrd=/etc/initdisk.gz".
> If the device used appears to be a floppy, aboot prompts to change disks
> between loading the kernel and the initrd.

Please understand that we are not sufficiently versed for those
system operations. So can I assume that I should do

P00>>boot dva0 -file vmlinux.gz -flags "initrd=/etc/initdisk.gz"
