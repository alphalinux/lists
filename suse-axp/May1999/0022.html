> No. The assemlber syntax for gcc/egcs *.S files is pretty much as the
> digital assembler syntax (I´d played a little bit with it and tried
> to build the pentium mmx commands, but I wasn´t successful).

the instructions of course are the same, but are the assembler commands (for
alignment, symbols, ...) also the same??? by the way can you use register
names now in gcc/egcs instead of only numbers? this was what really drove
me nuts. i'm used to their names ax, tx, ... (according to their role
in alpha's function calling) and i'll probably never get use to addressing
them only by numbers.
 
> That´s true. But inline assembler syntax is also different to the
> syntax of other assemblers for Intel achitectures.
no, gcc's syntax to do inline assembler is the same for intel and alpha
and probably all the other machines. the are a few machine dependend
constraints and the have quite different meanings. but the rest is the
same. so the syntax is the same, but of course the instructions are not
the same.
