> Now that Compaq has made their own Alpha-optimised C and C++ compilers
> available, I'm sure more Alpha-users than myself have toyed with the idea of
> building a complete Linux with these tools. I'd guess most of the
> applications and much of the kernel would compile without changes, but
> ofcourse, that's unknown until tested. I know there are assembler parts and
> inline code which probably would not compile, but these might possibly be
> left to Gnu tools.
>
> Has SuSE any plans to try this (or have you already done so)?

This means compiling all required libraries to each and every
program. I've made the ccc my default compiler, but it needs libraries
compiled with ccc as well. I tried to compile kde(1 and 2) and the result
was disappointing..:(

I'm not sure wheter it applies to all libraries, but at least qt must be
compiled with ccc(which I have already done).

The kernel is probably another chapter, it makes full use of gcc-extension
and will most certainly fail to compile correctly, even small parts of it.

Our only hope is to compile ccc-binaries static, which, in theory, will
work wonders. The drawback with that approach is the size of the
binaries...
