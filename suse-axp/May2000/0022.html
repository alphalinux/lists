I am not sure whether you have your Alpha with SuSE-6.3 installed.
You seem to have no problem with your gs. From the responses
I received from the mailing list, it is apparent the SuSE-6.3 for Alpha
has a terrible problem with Ghostview/Ghostscript. Still no good
solution. Yet, not many do seem to care about this. Why is that
so? With all the nice features of SuSE-6.3 compared to RedHat-6.1,
SuSE has this basic trouble. No matter what people say, there are
more RedHat-for-Alpha distributions than the SuSE-for-Alpha one.

This fact makes me quite frustrated. Not many update rpm packages
in the ftp.suse.com, either, compared to the updates for RedHat for
Alpha. My favorite window manager is AfterStep. Its version in the
SuSE distribution is way behind that for RedHat, etc. What about
libjpeg? Even with all the help from this mailing list, I failed to
install SuSE-6.3 on a UP2000 by ftp. When I try to buy a CDROM,
SuSE told me to contact the Korean distribution. Well, the Korean
distributor has never imported a copy of SUSE for Alpha. They
promised to import 6.4 when it comes. So, I have one Alpha with
RedHat and another with SuSE-6.3. What a nonsense!

I am still holding onto SuSE because of yast and nisplus. But
that does not really give me a good excuse for all other troubles.
