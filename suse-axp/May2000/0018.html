On Thu, 11 May 2000, Matthias Schloegel wrote:

> Hi !
>
> Does anybody know what 'kernel stack not valid' means ?
for aboot it usually just means "something with boot_file and/or
boot_osflags is wrong"...
Especially aboot has a major problem with boot_osflags="0,1" or
similar, or boot_file pointing to a non existing file/path.

In very rare cases I think I have seen aboot fail to boot a certain
kernel binary at all, but I guess the error was between my keyboard
and chair... ;)
