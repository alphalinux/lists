Joerg Bruehe <joerg@sql.de> writes:

> Hi to all !
>
> I just was caught by big surprise:
>
> In a directory containing files with small and CAPITAL letters
> (for example, random order: "ABC" "CEF" "ade" "ccc" ),
> the bash expands some patterns from small to capital letters:
> [abc]* -> ade ccc
> [a-c]* -> ABC ade ccc CEF
> IMHO, the latter expansion is wrong because the character range
> from "a" to "c" (small) does not include "A" or "C" (capital).
>
[snip]
> The system is an "Alpha Station 200 4/166" running SuSE 6.1 AXP :
> kernel 2.2.9
> bash 2.02.1(1)-release (alphaev56-unknown-linux-gnu)

bash-1.14.7(1) on alpha-linux works as expected:
ls [abc]* -> ade ccc
ls [a-c]* -> ade ccc

bash-2.00.0(4) on alpha-osf3.2 works as expected.
/bin/sh on alpha-osf3.2 works as expected.
bash-2.03.4(1) on ix86-linux works as expected.

bash-2.03.6(1) on alpha-linux behaves strangely as described.
bash-2.03.6(1) on ix86-linux behaves strangely as described.

looks like a bug in the recent version of bash.
