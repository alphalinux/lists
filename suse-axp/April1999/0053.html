Hello Kris,

I have tried your boot kernel and this one doesn't work either.
As stated in my mail I am not working with a noname box, but with an Alpha
Server 2000 4/200, Sable Architecture.

When I boot your kernel, I get to
"Ok, now booting the kernel ...."
and the machine freezes.

I.m.h.o. there could be two reasons for this :

1. There is a problem with the SUSE aboot kernel. This one detects the
right architecture, but locks the machine when
"Calibrating Delay Loop". I guess that this is the point where the CPU
performance is determined. In case that the machine clock is involved here,
this could be a hint to 2. (see below).
I have successfully installed and booted the RedHat 4.2. This one goes past
that point and boots OK. Now I want to switch to SUSE because I expect a
better support for the Mylex 960 raid controller.

2. People have repeatedly reported problems with the clock settings on
Alpha machines in several newsgroups.
I have a similar problem, i.e. when starting through the arc console I am
every time forced to set date and time.
After the machine has successfully booted, the date is sometimes 1911 and
sometimes 2038 which appears to be very random.
I have asked Compaq/Digital to send a technician and have a look into this.
This does - of course - not explain why the Red Hat kernel works
and the Suse doesn't.

Final question : Is there a support site of SUSE that deals with the Alpha
stuff. On their 'normal website' in the support database I haven't found a
single line although announced at CeBit. I am looking for different
flavours/versions of the aboot kernel for booting through SRM.

I will keep you updated, because I think that more people will face this
problem.

Many thanks and best regards
Thomas Schneider
