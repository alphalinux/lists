Observations
   Your kernel boots a little bit further than the one before and freezes
   machine after detecting the scsi devices. I have found out in the
   meantime that the problem is apparently a general one with the size of
   the kernel. see next
   I have managed to compile different kernels using the SuSE sources
   myself. Depending on the size of the kernel, the boot process stops
   sooner or later. Want to say : The bigger the kernel, the earlier the
   machine freezes. I have also tried to strip and compress manually, but
   never get system up and running.
   Compiling RedHat 5.2 kernels ( I have 5.2, not 4.2, sorry for the wrong
   information before) works without a problem and is booting alright.
   RedHat Kernel version is 2.0.35, where yours is on 2.2.something. In
   general the 2.2.x kernels appear to be much bigger although I limited
   all devices to a minimum.

There must be a general problem of this kernel booting on this machine.
