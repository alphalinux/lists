> I am trying to locate the 6.4-axp-evaluation/beta.iso file. The only
> place where I found it was ftp.alphalinux.org.
That iso was not really meant for public use. More or less I threw things
together for a number of people with specific tests. alphalinux.org spotted
the image and I agreed to publish it there because I wanted quick results
since I was already weeks behind schedule ...
> Shouldn't it be also to be
> found somewhwere on ftp.suse.com? As much as I like the alphalinux.org
> service the connection is quite slow and I would rather use a local

> After the experience with 6.1 and 6.3 I am not going to buy 6.4 before
> trying it, as my jensen is not supported... (I do think that SuSE deserves
> money for their distribution, but it has to be usable to some degree...)
Fully agreed. As for the Jensen: I've included a jensen kernel on CD
and on a special disk image that basically runs, but the de4x5-driver is
broken for eisa-cards and I did not manage to patch it into a usable state.
> While I am at it, why is there no real german ftp-site of suse (mirrors
> are nice but where is the real mccoy?)?
Due to the limited bandwidth of ftp.suse.de, it never really was a full
suse-download site. Some of the german mirrors (gwdg.de for example)
have everything and more ftp.suse.de could ever offer.
> And ftp.suse.com is a bit on the
> x86 side one might complain. For example /pub/suse_update/ points deep
> into intel-land without proper notification to the user or excape routes
> for other platforms (come on, a link ala axp or ppc is not so hard to
> incorporate, given that I mentioned this fact months ago).
Well: pub/suse_update is an historical artefact, that was meant to be
deleted almost two years ago (but we had to keep it because many links
pointed there...). ftp.suse.com/pub/suse/$ARCH/update/$VERSION is
the current hierarchy.
