swriteboot from SuSE works just fine. SuSE may not write the
bootstrap automatically. You may have to do it by hand. The main thing
that has to happen is that the 1st cylinder of the disk is
"expendable". Either by starting the first partition at cylinder 2 or
by creating a 1 or 2 cylinder partition (starting at cylinder 1)
that you don't use. swriteboot will write to cylinder 1 (the start
of the disk).

To be sure you may want to run 'fdisk' by hand and make absolutely
sure you use BSD labels, i.e. enter 'b' at the first prompt.
