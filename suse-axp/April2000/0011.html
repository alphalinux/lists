> Um,
> So I am trying to put SuSE 6.3 on an ES40 with 8 gigs and eight 9gig
> internal disks.
>
> What is the recommended way to get the installer to correctly write
> partitions onto the disks?
> For example.....the yast installer does not seem to want to talk to the disk
> correctly. It keeps complaining about bacd superblocks/or/ too many mounted
> filesyste.
>
> When I run "fdisk" from another tty it has no idea what the correct number
> of cylendars are on the disk.
>
> If i ignore what fdisk is displaying and just do
> 3 through 150
> 151 through 350
> 351 through 800
> 801 through 1106

Is the ES40 a machine with ARC-firmware or with the SRM-console?
As discribed in the SuSE 6.3 Book, chapter 2.1.5, there ar two diferent
ways to make partitions an AXP-computers:

1. ARC firmware: do with Yast

2. SRM-Console need BSD-partitions: change to the 2nd conssole with
[ALT]+F2, start fdisk and change with 'b' to BSD-Partiton-tables, and
work on as described in the book
