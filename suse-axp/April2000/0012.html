Hi Mike,

Sounds like you probably have Tru64 BSD labels on the the disk(s).
These confuse fdisk. I believe the David H. from Linuxcare has
mods to fdisk to grok the "strange" geometry Tru64 disklabel writes
and I have modss to fdisk that lets you 'zap' the labels (and
geometry) and start "fresh". Unfortunately you have neither of these
fixes and you'd have to do some handwaving to get a modified fdisk
into the installation.

The best thing to do is when you are at the partitioning phase
<alt><f2> into the bash shell and clear the labels by hand. eg

dd if=/dev/zero of=/dev/sdx bs=1024K count=4

(I'm pretty sure SuSE has a 'dd' in their install ramdisk)

this will zero the first 4MB of the disk (which will definitely
zap the labels). Once you do this, fdisk will not find a label
and initialize a new one.
