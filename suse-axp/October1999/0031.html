newbie helping newbie! You certainly did a good job with it. I got it to
boot off the harddrive with your help.

>
> See if aboot is installed
> # rpm -q aboot
>
> If it's not installed, you'll need to install it.
> Get to the configuration screen where you get to add/change
> your configuration. See Section 3.12.3 in the gawdawful manual.
> Highlight the 'a' series of software and hit return.
> You may notice that aboot is not selected. Imagine that.
> Select it and hit F10 a couple times to get back to the

> Let 'er rip and aboot should get installed.
> Once you've verified that aboot is installed,
>
> # swriteboot /dev/sda /boot/bootlx
>
> and, assuming you partitioned the drive as specified
>

I had actually previously gone through the steps above since I figured
I needed aboot. So that was not the problem. The problem was that the
saving boot command for SRM:

>>>> b dka0 -fi 2/boot/vmlinuz -fl root=/dev/sda2
>

is nowhere documented in the manual (SuSE, are you listening?). Instead,
the manual tells you to do
>>>boot dkb0 0

which in my case didn't get me anywhere because the boot device
happened to be dka0 (I guess with a little more experience I could have
guessed that). Furthermore I needed to specify the file 2/boot/vmlinuz
even though I had specified this already in the aboot.conf file. While
I don't necessarily mind typing this boot command string in everytime I
reboot my machine (shouldn't happen too often anyway), it would still
be nice to reboot it automatically. The SuSE manual tells you to "set
bootdef_dev dkb0" (which should for me probably be dka0) and to "set
auto_action boot". I suppose I need to issue these commands in the
SRM console? Then the manual continues: "more detailed may be found in
the manual of your AXP machine". Great! I don't have the manual since
this was a donated machine that came without one. Is there a copy of the
manual available somewhere on the web?

I think that SuSE should spend a little more time grep'ing through
their AXP manual and throwing out stuff that is related to Intel and
replacing it with stuff related to alpha. Perhaps, when SuSE AXP 6.3
comes out?
