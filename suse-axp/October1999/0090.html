Let me clarify my remarks about RH6.1 and MILO on EV6. RH6.1 BETA Installer
did not have an fdisk option from the installer, you can still use the Alt-2
console to do your disk partitioning. We have a copy running on our
testdrive program on an ES40. (http://www.testdrive.compaq.com) We have
LOTS of machines with Lots of OS's free and otherwise, including SuSE
6.1(x86 + alpha), RH6.1 (x86 + alpha), RH6.0 (x86 + alpha), TurboLinux (x86
only), Tru64, VMS, etc. And we are waiting on a power upgrade to the lab to
add a 8x Alpha Beowulf cluster.

Yes there is a BETA MILO for EV6 systems, however given the fact that
Compaq/Microsoft have stopped future development on AlphaNT and only
maintenance is being done I would not expect much of a future for MILO.
(Personally I don't like to add BETA on top of BETA, that way I know which
one broke)

As for BSD labeling, here are a few pointers:

1. skip the first track, this is for the health of your partition table
(fdisk warns you) and the aboot loader.
2. DO NOT USE THE LAST TRACK REPORTED BY FDISK. Fdisk reports one more
track than there is, I don't know why but using the last track (247/247)
results in a bad filesystem, and a bad install. There are some examples in
the installation guide (See #3)
3. Read the installation guide we have written:
http://www.unix.digital.com/linux/documentation.htm

(Warning blatant plug)
You may also want to check out http://www.compaq.com/linux, we have a
collection of documentation, kernels, patches, and special deals on Linux
Ready Compaq Alpha Workstations/Servers.

As for the UP2000, you will need to talk to Alpha Processor, Inc
(http://www.alphaprocessor.com/) as that is their product not Compaq's.
