On Fri, 4 Feb 2000, Erik Arneson wrote:
>
> Howdy everybody,
>
> The installation worked great, as I said yesterday, and now I'm in
> the middle of setting up the system the way I like it to be.
> However, I've noticed a couple of very strange lockups, where the
> system simply freezes.
>
> I can't find exactly what's causing it (I haven't looked really
> closely), but the last one happened while starting up YaST from the
> command line. I don't have X11 running on the machine.
>
> Has anybody seen this, or should I dig deeper and try to discover
> what's causing it?
>

Yes, there seems to be a problem with modules on AXP. If you
use modules currently can compile a kernel which compiles your
drivers into the kernel, this may help.

If you need modules for your disk, take take /etc/full_vm.gz as
your kernel, it has most common scsi drivers compiled in.

Once you are running without modules, removing /lib/modules/...
will delete all modules.
