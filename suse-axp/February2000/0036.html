On Wed, 2 Feb 2000, Erik Arneson wrote:

> On 1 Feb 00, Rich Payne <rdp@talisman.alphalinux.org> wrote:
> > The problem is Compaq/Netscape, not Suse. You (we) have to convince them
> > that they don't need to record every download/install of the product.
>
> Compaq's got a good reason for doing it. The more folks they can show
> are using Netscape on Linux/Alpha, the better the case for getting some
> real, live support for it from Netscape.

No, I can't agree with that. First, do you think Linux would
be as successful if they required people to register before downloading?
Second, There's not going to be a Netscape for AlphaLinux and I would say
that anybody starting work on this today would be wasting time. Mozilla is
the next version of NS and that already runs on AlphaLinux.

I've been through this with Q already, the requiremnt of tracking the
downloads is a political one within Compaq, Netscape actually has nothing
to do with this (well, other than it is their product).
