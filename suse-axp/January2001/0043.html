Dear AXP users,

now that I use SuSE 7.0 (kernel 2.2.16) on my "Avanti"
(AS 200 4/166 with TGA graphics card),
I want to inform you about two shortcomings of Milo 2.2 which I noted:

1) During the boot phase, it shows messages like kernel boot messages
   but has erroneous representations for some characters
   (from memory: "r", "k" with accents above; "g", "j" with lower part
   cut; probably more).
   (As SuSE does provide special kernels for the TGA graphics card,
   "DEC 21030 TGA 8-plane" according to the boot messages,
   this card might be the reason - there is no special TGA-Milo AFAIK.)

2) When using "shutdown -r", the machine goes down properly, but then
   remains in that halted (?) state without showing the ARC-Bios
   texts or any other activity.
   I press the reset button to reboot then.

I fell back onto my older Milo 2.0.35, and both problems were gone.
It does boot (and reboot) the 2.2.16 kernel without any problems.

Where could I find information on the advantages of Milo 2.2 over 2.0,
so that I would know which stuff I am losing by my current setting ?

Regards,
