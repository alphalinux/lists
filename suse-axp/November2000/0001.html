All;

I'm trying to install SUSE 6.3 (over RH 6.0) on an 164SX.

I can boot the floppy and load MILO. At this point I can, "MILO> hdb:".
I can then look at my CDROM and see the files on it (this alone took
hours to figure out).

I have tried the following:
1. MILO>boot hdb:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 fake_initrd
---asks for "boot disk"

2. MILO>boot fd0:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 fake_initrd
---asks for "boot disk"

3. MILO>boot hdb:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/hdb fake_initrd
---Kernel panic: try passing init= option to kernel

4. MILO>boot hdb:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/hdb/etc fake_initrd
---Kernel panic: try passing init= option to kernel

5. MILO>boot hdb:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/hdb/etc fake_initrd
load_ramdisk=1
---Kernel panic: try passing init= option to kernel

6. MILO>boot hdb:/etc/vmlinux.gz root=/dev/fd0 fake_initrd load_ramdisk=1
---asks for "boot disk"

----FRUSTRATION VENT-------
I have been using LINUX for 5 years on Intel platforms and I've had many
problems,
however they paled next to Alpha. Suse seems to have a good Alpha list and I
like
Suse installs on Intel, but COMPAQ(DEC) has the most confusing
ARC-AlphaBIOS-SRM-MILO
mess. The documentation seems to be 3 years (1997) out of date. I notice
that the
Suse manual doesn't even mention AlphaBIOS. I spent many-many hours
installing
RedHat 6.0 but X seems all screwed up and there is NO support. When I
compare
performance it seems to run 50-100% fasted for the same clock speed, but
Compaq
doesn't seem to support the Alpha for beans. They are killing their own
product through
neglect and it seems so sad (although DEC did a terrible job as well).

A year ago our company evaluated the Alpha, but it is so poorly supported
and difficult
to use that the better performance wasn't worth the hassle.
