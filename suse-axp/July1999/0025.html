On 26-Jul-99 Dipl. Phys. Thomas Bruns wrote:

> When I tried to recompile the kernel with <make xconfig> the
> SCSI-controller ncr53c7,8xx was ++not++ a valid choice ???
> When I tried <make menuconfig> it was !!!
> But anyway, it didn´t solve the problem. No matter if I tried modules
> or direct kernel-code nothing worked for the /dev/sg-devices !!!

xconfig is known to be very buggy. You should *NOT* use it since the choices
you get seem to depend on the current .config file. menuconfig is a much better
choice, but even that is known to have some bugs left. I guess the only secure
way is using config or editing the .config file manually at the moment.

What SCSI controller do you use? If you have an NCR/SymBIOS Logic 8xx (in spite
of 7xx), you should use the newer BSD derived driver NCR53c8xx or SYM53c8xx.
The ncr53c7,8xx is known to have problems with newer hardware and I think it's
not really supported anymore since the newer drivers are better.
This behaviour is the same for the lxsuse kernels as well as as for all the
others.

> Printer:
> With kernel 2.2.9 from SuSE and aps-filter an <lpr something.ps> put a
> print job in the queue without error, but thats all! After a short time
> the job vanished and the spooling-system was content, I was not !!!
>
> A <lsmod> showed that the correct modules were loaded (lp, parport...)
> but <cat /proc/interrupts> showed no irq for the lp-device.
> A call to <tunelp> revealed an interrupt <-1> for the printer ?!?!?
Linux' standard behaviour is polled communication with the parallel port
instead of an interrupt driven one. So you should not get an interrupt in 2.2.7
either.

> See above, the 2.2.7 Kernel works fine !!!
Hmm... there were major changes in 2.2.8 as well in the lowlevel parport driver
as in the printer device driver. Does anyone know these changes a bit more
detailed? I just had a look at the corresponding kernel sources and it seems
that only drivers/char/lp.c and drivers/misc/parport_pc.c had non-m68k
dependant changes. I'll have a closer look at it tomorrow.
