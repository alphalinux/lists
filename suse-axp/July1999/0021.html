Hi,

over the last weekend I tried to make my cdwriter (TEAC-SCSI) and
printer (cdj500) work on my AlphaPC LX164 under Kernel 2.2.9 from
SuSE 6.1 AXP and failed (almost).

CD-Writer:
After reboot <cat /proc/scsi/scsi> printed a listing of all the
scsi-devices on the bus, but when I tried a <cdrecord -scanbus>
the return was <so such file> although the /dev/sg* devices were
installed. A <lsmod> showed that all necessary modules were loaded.
Another <cat /proc/scsi/scsi> printed <no device connected> ???
But harddisc-read/write and cdrom-reading still worked fine !
So I suspect a driver conflict /dev/sg* vs. /dev/sd* ?!

When I tried to recompile the kernel with <make xconfig> the
SCSI-controller ncr53c7,8xx was ++not++ a valid choice ???
When I tried <make menuconfig> it was !!!
But anyway, it didn´t solve the problem. No matter if I tried modules
or direct kernel-code nothing worked for the /dev/sg-devices !!!

solution:
On last resort I de-installed the <lx-suse> package with the
SuSE-adapted 2.2.9 kernel source and installed the other kernel source
package (you get a linux 2.2.7ac0su directory in /usr/src).
This kernel supplies three different drivers for ncr53c7,8xx and I chose
the third. I think it was <sym53c800> (or similar). That one
worked out of the box :^) !!

Printer:
With kernel 2.2.9 from SuSE and aps-filter an <lpr something.ps> put a
print job in the queue without error, but thats all! After a short time
the job vanished and the spooling-system was content, I was not !!!

A <lsmod> showed that the correct modules were loaded (lp, parport...)
but <cat /proc/interrupts> showed no irq for the lp-device.
A call to <tunelp> revealed an interrupt <-1> for the printer ?!?!?

solution:
See above, the 2.2.7 Kernel works fine !!!

hope this might save other people´s weekends ;^)

        Thomas
