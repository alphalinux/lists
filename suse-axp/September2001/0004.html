No, Suse AXP support hasn't stopped. But as Steve said, it is behind the x86
platform. Suse AXP 7.1 was actually just released a couple months ago. And
since Suse is actively selling and supporting the AXP platform, I think that
pretty well answers your question. But it makes since that since there are
thousands more x86 chips out there than alphas, that the development would be
for x86 first, and ppc/alpha/etc... would come second.

Compaq seems to be doing a wonderful job at supporting linux on alpha. You can
download from Compaq and RedHat a bootable ISO called "Jump Start" that will
prep/partition your alpha workstation/server to prepare it for installing
RedHat, Suse, Debian, TurboLinux, etc... Also, Compaq just mailed me the latest
5.1A version of True64 and it came with 2 sets of Linux portability tools. It
included tons of GNU software and it even came with KDE2, Gnome, and
instructions for converting True64 to use RPM package manager.

Very optimistic...
Terry
