Well for starters why don't you go to
http://ftp.digital.com/pub/DEC/Alpha/firmware/

and get the new firmware?

Secondly I have found that fdisk works just fine on large drives.
I usually use the bsd labels though.
Most often it doesn't know how to deal with all the cylinders and sectors so
you just have to wing it.
Meaning that you start "a" at 2 and go to , say, 45.
Then make "b" start at 46 and go to, say, 110

etc........

It takes a few trys to get it right but it does work.

Hi list,

I just finished upgrading my alphastation 250 4/266 by outfitting it
with a new 18.3 GB SCSI HD from Western Digital. This is in addition to
the old 1GB Quantum HD that the machine came with. Since the WD drive
is an Ultra SCSI-2 I also installed a new SYM8952U PCI-SCSI card. The
Quantum drive obviously had to remain on the on-board SCSI controller.
After boot-up everything worked well, i.e., the system (currently
running SuSE-AXP 6.1 and eagerly awaiting 6.3) recognized the new HD as
/dev/sdb and I was able to partition it with YaST. However, a major
drawback is that fdisk doesn't seem to recognize the full 18.3 GB and
stops at about 16.3 GB. My hunch is that the BIOS of the motherboard
will not allow larger disk sizes to be recognized. This has to be a
card made in 1994 or 1995 when large disks like this for desktops
weren't around. So, here are my questions for the experts:

1. Is the BIOS to be blamed or is there something that can be done with
   fdisk to remedy the situation and allow full access to the 18.3 GB?
2. If my diagnosis is correct and the BIOS is too old, is there an easy
   way to update it for the AS 250 4/266? And if that is so, can you
   give me pointers to such an upgrade?

Thank you very much in advance, Alex.
