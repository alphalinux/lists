Hallo everybody,

I have the same problems with my Ruffion board UX164 (milo and ldmilo.exe
from \cdrom\milo\ruffian):

> Other problem with date / hwclock:
> The system clock as the correct date, but when i boot to linux, the date
> switch to current day/month, but to year 2052 (next epoch) ?
> I can set the correct date and all is wunderful, but after next boot, the
> system is in 2052. :( [apc164ux4; ldmilo from SuSE CD]

The system clock has the correct date and time but the booted Linux switches
to year 2052 or in the case of started xntpd service to year 2068.

If one resets the hardwareclock using 'hwclock',e.g.

      hwclock --set --date="1/20/00 16:47:05"

you will have the correct date and time still after the next reboot. But the
system clock accessible via the ARC firmware is switched to the year 2048.

One more question I have is the following: During the installation process I
had the right date and time. But after next reboot with enabling the xntpd
service and the initial ntpdate I could observe the switch of date and time in
the /var/log/message file:

Jan 19 18:14:02 ptpa5 sshd[212]: log: RSA key generation complete.
Feb 6 21:29:46 ptpa5 ntpdate[214]: step time server 141.30.61.179 offset
506488543.828885 sec
Feb 6 21:29:46 ptpa5 xntpd[217]: ntpd 4.0.98d Fri Nov 26 03:47:12 GMT 1999
(1)

What's the reason for? Setting the date on command line to the correct date
and time and starting ntpdate gives a correct result.

Thanks for help!

Jörg
