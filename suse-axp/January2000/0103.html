I had exactly the same problem: SCSI controller not recognised, but
while installing the software it worked.

I've looked on CD1 and found in /etc/ 4 kernels. I've scanned them
for the string ncr53c and found that the one called vm_full.gz has
this string in it. I copied this kernel to /boot/vmlinux.gz, changed
the boot-line to boot this kernel, and wonder oh wonder, it worked! ;-)

My system (AXPpci33, 21066 processor) now boots and the SCSI controller
is recognised!

SuSE: why is the full-featured kernel not mentioned when you have to

needs (which I chose), one with TGA support and one with dual processor
support (?)... Why not include the full-featured kernel as an extra
option?
