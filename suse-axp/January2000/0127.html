Hi,

My SuSE 6.3 works well except for a few glitches. A major one of
those is that an internal (ISA) modem configured as /dev/ttyS2 or
/dev/ttyS3 crashes Linux - not necessarily immediately, but within
a few minutes.

Do I need a special kernel? The default SuSE kernel recognizes
the device. Also, I've tried alternative ISA IRQs (2 and 5) with no
success. I believe the only way a Linux kernel can "freeze" is when
an interrupt is not acknowledged (and keeps on interrupting).

At first, I believed it to be a problem with ipchains, but the behaviour
is systematic whenever I use the serial port for the modem for a few
minutes.

It's a PC164 with EB firmware. Perhaps a solution is to disable the
existing serial ports and use one of those - I just can't find any info
on how to disable the ports on the motherboard - anyone?
