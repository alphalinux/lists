> i have a strange problem with my XL366 Alpha.
> SuSE Linux 6.4 works fine but only with local users.
> The home filesystem is on this mashine.
> When a user login and the home filesystem is mounted via NFS
> from a Alpha with TRU64 operating system,
> the most of application are not running, e.g. kmail, netscape
> and many other programs from kde.
> But no error message is printed in /var/log/messages or on the
> console window.
> Can anybody help me?

You have mounted /home rw, right? Try starting the nonworking programs
from a xterm and see what it says.

Also, make sure that the UID/GID are the same on Linux as on Tru64, and
that the filesystem is exported rw.

I've used NFS to mount homedirectories, and it's been working great, so
try these things first. Tru is normally very good at NFS, but perhaps it's
not quite compatible with Linux(I heard that 4.0d might not be). I know it
should work, because we use it at our school(http://www.etek.chalmers.se).

There is also the possibility that it's something completely diffrent, but
as said, try these things first...
