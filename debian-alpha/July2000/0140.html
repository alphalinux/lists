On Sat, 22 Jul 2000, Colin Smith wrote:

> Hello to all who may be able to help --
>
> I have a DEC Alphastation AS200 with a standard Number 9 Video card. I

Which Number 9 card? Many were based on S3 chipsets (either Trio64 or
ViRGE-based). If you can, provide the output of 'lspci -v'. That would
help in trying to figure out what type of card it is. If it is S3-based,
the SVGA driver may not be the Xserver that you need.

> Configured drivers:
> SVGA: server for SVGA graphics adaptors (Patchlevel 0):
> mga2064w, mga1064sg, mga2164w, mga2164w AGP, mgag200, mgag100,
> mgag400, s3_virge
 ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
Note that most of the supported cards in the SVGA server are Matrox cards.

> (--) SVGA: PCI: S3 Trio32/64 rev 0, Memory @ 0x04800000

Here's why I suspect an S3 chipset :-) I've got a Trio64, FYI, and have
been down this road before :-) Before trying just the S3 server, though,
let's have a look at that lspci and find out just which (S3 or S3V) is
better for your card).
