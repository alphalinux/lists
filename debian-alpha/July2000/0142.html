> On Thu, Jul 20, 2000 at 01:31:43AM -0400, Jim Crilly wrote:
> >
> > As the topic says I have an AlphaServer 1000A and am trying to
> install
> > Debian on it. A few seconds and screen flickers after it starts
> booting
> > the kernel from the rescue image I get kicked back to SRM with the
> error:
> >
> > halt code = 6
> > double error halt
> > PC = fffffc000031b24c
> >
> > I've tried Debian 2.1 and 2.2 and a few other distributions just to
> be
> > thorough and they all do the same thing. I am new to the Alpha
> > architecture so this maybe not be a Linux specific issue but since
> the
> > box is currently running Tru64 and had NT on it at one point so I
> don't
> > think it's a hardware problem. I have updated the SRM firmware to
> 5.7 and
> > ARC is 4.59 which had no effect.
>
> It's not a hardware problem - 1000A support has been broken for the
> 4/xxx
> models for quite a while in both 2.2.x and 2.4.x kernels.
>
> The good news is that both 2.2.17-pre13 and 2.4.0-test5-pre3 have
> working
> support for 1000A 4/xx and 5/xxx. You'll have to find a way to get an
> updated kernel before trying to install. I have made one available,
> but
> don't know if the configuration is appropriate for a Debian install.
> For anon FTP at gatekeeper.dec.com:
>
> /pub/DEC/Linux-Alpha/TESTING/up-2.2.17-pre12-15jul/
>
> you will find a floppy image that could be tried.
>
> >
> > Basically the hardware in the box is a Mylex DAC960(all the drives
> on on
> > this RAID controller), QLogic ISP10x0(cdrom is on here), DE500-BA
> NIC, S3
> > Trio64/Trio32 PCI Video(on-board LISA video is disabled), 128M
> memory.
>
> All OK except for the DAC960, which prolly has firmware (version <
> 3.x)
> that is not supported by the LINUX DAC960 driver.
